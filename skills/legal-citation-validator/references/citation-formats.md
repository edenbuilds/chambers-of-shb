# Legal Citation Formats - Indian Law

## Statutory Citations

### General Format
```
[Act Name], [Year], Section [Number]([Subsection])([Clause])
```

### Examples by Act Type

**Constitution**:
- Article 14, Part III, Constitution of India
- Articles 14-18 (Right to Equality)
- Article 21, Constitution of India, 1950

**Central Acts**:
- Section 300 of the Indian Penal Code, 1860
- Section 14 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872
- Section 7 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963

**With Subsections**:
- Section 14(1) of the Indian Contract Act, 1872
- Section 14(1)(a) - full clause reference
- Section 14(2) proviso

**Short Form (after first mention)**:
- "Section 14" or "Section 14 ICA" (if context clear)
- "Section 300 IPC"
- Never shorten on first mention

## Case Law Citations

### Primary Reporter Formats

**1. All India Reporter (AIR)** - Most Common
```
[Case Name] v. [Respondent], AIR [Year] [Court] [Page]
```
Examples:
- Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, AIR 1973 SC 1461
- Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India, AIR 1978 SC 597

**2. Supreme Court Reports (SCR)**
```
[Case Name] v. [Respondent], ([Year]) [Volume] SCR [Page]
```
Examples:
- Vishaka v. State of Rajasthan, (1997) 6 SCR 241

**3. Supreme Court Cases (SCC)**
```
[Case Name] v. [Respondent], ([Year]) [Volume] SCC [Page]
```
Examples:
- Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India, (1978) 1 SCC 248

### Multiple Citations
When case reported in multiple reporters:
```
[Case Name] v. [Respondent], AIR [Year] [Court] [Page]; ([Year]) [Vol] SCC [Page]
```
Example:
- Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India, AIR 1978 SC 597; (1978) 1 SCC 248

### High Court Citations
```
[Case Name] v. [Respondent], AIR [Year] [HC Abbreviation] [Page]
```
Examples:
- State v. Navjot Sandhu, AIR 2005 Del 2

## Amendment Citations

**Format**:
```
[Ordinal] Amendment, [Year]
OR
Constitution (Amendment) Act, [Year]
```

**Examples**:
- 42nd Amendment, 1976
- Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976
- 44th Amendment Act, 1978

## Abbreviation Rules

### When to Abbreviate
1. **After First Full Citation**: Always spell out on first mention
2. **Clear Context**: Only abbreviate if unambiguous
3. **Formal Writing**: Minimize abbreviations

### Acceptable Abbreviations
- v. (versus - note the period)
- SC (Supreme Court - no periods)
- IPC (Indian Penal Code)
- CPC (Code of Civil Procedure)
- CrPC (Code of Criminal Procedure)
- ICA (Indian Contract Act)
- SRA (Specific Relief Act)

### Unacceptable Abbreviations
- vs (use "v.")
- vs. (use "v.")
- S.C. (use "SC")
- Sec. (use "Section" in formal writing)
- Art. (use "Article")

## Spacing and Punctuation

### Spacing Rules
- ✅ "AIR 1973 SC 1461" (spaces between each element)
- ❌ "AIR1973SC1461" (no spaces)
- ✅ "Section 14(1)" (no space before parenthesis)
- ❌ "Section 14 (1)" (extra space)

### Comma Usage
- ✅ "Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, AIR 1973 SC 1461"
- Comma before reporter citation
- Comma after year in SCR/SCC format: "(1978) 1 SCC 248"

### Period Usage
- "v." always has period
- Court abbreviations: NO periods (SC not S.C.)
- End of sentence: normal punctuation

---

## Citation Order

When citing multiple authorities in one sentence:

1. **Constitution first**
2. **Statutes second** (chronological by year)
3. **Case law third** (chronological, Supreme Court before High Courts)

**Example**:
```
Article 14 of the Constitution; Section 14 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872;
Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, AIR 1973 SC 1461; Maneka Gandhi v.
Union of India, AIR 1978 SC 597
```

## Special Formats

### Writ Petitions
```
[Petitioner] v. [Respondent], [Year] [Writ Petition No.] [Court]
```
Example:
- Ram Jethmalani v. Union of India, WP (C) 490/2011 (SC)

### Public Interest Litigation (PIL)
```
[PIL Name/Petitioner] v. [Respondent], AIR [Year] SC [Page]
```
Example:
- Vishaka v. State of Rajasthan, AIR 1997 SC 3011 (PIL for sexual harassment guidelines)

### Constitutional Bench Decisions
Indicate bench size in parenthetical:
```
[Case Name], AIR [Year] SC [Page] (13-judge bench)
```
Example:
- Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, AIR 1973 SC 1461 (13-judge bench)

## Citation in Different Contexts

### In Protocols
First mention: Full citation
Subsequent: Short form acceptable

### In Legal Opinions
Every citation must be complete (no abbreviations except standard ones)

### In Judgments (when citing)
Follow Supreme Court Rules, 2013, Order VI, Rule 3

### In Academic Writing
Footnote citations with full details; in-text short forms

---

## Validation Checklist

For each citation, verify:
- [ ] First mention has full Act name/case name
- [ ] Year is included
- [ ] Court abbreviation is standard (for cases)
- [ ] Section/Article number is correct
- [ ] Spacing is proper
- [ ] "v." used (not "vs" or "vs.")
- [ ] Reporter abbreviation is correct (AIR/SCR/SCC)
- [ ] Page number included (for cases)
- [ ] No non-standard abbreviations (Sec., Art., etc.)
