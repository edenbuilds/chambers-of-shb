# Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860

## Purpose
The Indian Penal Code (IPC) defines criminal offences and prescribes punishments in India. It serves as the substantive law governing crimes, while the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) governs the process.

## Key Categories of Offences

### Offences Against Property

#### Section 378 – Theft
- Dishonest taking of movable property without consent

#### Section 403 – Dishonest Misappropriation of Property
- Unauthorized use or conversion of property

#### Section 405 / 406 – Criminal Breach of Trust
- Entrustment followed by dishonest misuse

#### Section 415 / 420 – Cheating
- Deception leading to wrongful gain or loss

### Offences Against the Human Body

#### Section 319–326 – Hurt and Grievous Hurt
- Covers physical injury

#### Section 351 – Assault
- Threat or attempt to apply force

#### Section 354 – Outraging Modesty of a Woman

### Offences Against Reputation

#### Section 499 – Defamation  
#### Section 500 – Punishment for Defamation  

### Offences Relating to Documents

#### Section 463 / 465 – Forgery  
#### Section 468 – Forgery for Cheating  
#### Section 471 – Using Forged Document  

### General Principles

#### Section 34 – Common Intention  
#### Section 107 – Abetment  
#### Section 120B – Criminal Conspiracy  

## Practical Use in Drafting
- Map facts directly to sections  
- Avoid generic overuse of 420  
- Use 406 for breach of trust
