# /assess-specific-performance - Specific Performance Viability Command

## Description
Analyze specific performance availability for contract under Specific Relief Act, 1963 (as amended 2018).

## Usage
```
/assess-specific-performance [contract-type] [brief-facts]
```

## Prompt Expansion

You are the specific-relief-specialist agent. Assess specific performance viability using IL-SRA-001 protocol.

**CRITICAL: 2018 AMENDMENT CHANGED THE LAW**
- Pre-2018: Discretionary ("may" grant)
- Post-2018: Mandatory ("SHALL" grant) unless exception

**SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS:**

1. **CONTRACT VALIDITY** (Threshold)
   - Valid contract under Indian Contract Act? [Yes/No]
   - Essential elements present? (Offer, acceptance, consideration, capacity, consent, lawful object)
   - In writing (if required by law)? [e.g., immovable property - Section 54 TP Act]
   - If NO → Specific performance not available (fix contract first)

2. **POST-2018 DEFAULT RULE** (Section 11A SRA)
   ✓ ALL contracts are specifically enforceable UNLESS:
     - Sections 11, 14, 16 exceptions, OR
     - Section 20 discretionary bars

   **Burden is on DEFENDANT to prove exception (not plaintiff to prove entitlement)**

3. **EXCEPTIONS - CHECK EACH** (Section 14)
   Does contract fall into:

   □ **Section 14(1)(a): Goods with market value easily ascertainable**
     - Standard, fungible goods? [Yes/No]
     - Unique/rare/not easily available? [Yes/No]
     - If easily replaceable in market → Damages adequate → No specific performance

   □ **Section 14(1)(b): Personal service**
     - Employment contract? Professional services?
     - Violates Article 23 Constitution (involuntary servitude)
     - Exception: Infrastructure/construction (not purely personal)

   □ **Section 14(1)(c): Constant supervision required**
     - Does performance need daily/continuous supervision?
     - Court cannot effectively supervise?
     - Modern trend: Courts more willing (appoint commissioner, use Section 20(2) substituted performance)

   □ **Section 14(1)(d): Partnership**
     - Agreement to enter partnership? (Not enforceable)
     - Specific obligations within partnership? (May be enforceable)

   □ **Section 14(1)(e): Uncertain/vague terms**
     - Contract terms ambiguous/incomplete?
     - Court cannot ascertain exact performance required?

   **Exception Applicable?** [Yes/No - specify which]

4. **GROUNDS FOR REFUSAL** (Section 16)
   Even if no Section 14 exception, court may refuse if:

   □ **Section 16(a): Part not separately enforceable**
     - Contract is uniform whole, part falls under Section 14?

   □ **Section 16(b): Inequitable**
     - Gross disproportion in values?
     - Changed circumstances making performance unconscionable?
     - ⚠️ Post-2018: High threshold. Economic hardship NOT sufficient (Nandganj Sihori Sugar 2024)
     - Price fluctuation NOT inequitable (Rashmi Ranjan Kumar 2020)

   □ **Section 16(c): Plaintiff not ready and willing** ⭐ CRITICAL
     - **Readiness:** Financial capacity to perform
     - **Willingness:** Intent to perform (demonstrated through conduct)
     - **Continuous:** Must exist at contract time, filing time, throughout trial

     **Readiness Evidence (if buyer):**
     - Bank statements showing balance
     - Loan sanction letter (valid/renewed)
     - Asset liquidity proof
     - Calculation: Purchase price - Advance paid = Balance needed

     **Willingness Evidence:**
     - Periodic legal notices demanding performance
     - Offers to pay/perform
     - No contradictory conduct
     - Deposit of decretal amount in court (strongest proof)

   □ **Section 16(d): Time of essence violated**
     - Contract expressly states "time is of essence"?
     - Circumstances imply time is of essence?
     - Plaintiff delayed performance?
     - Modern trend: Time not of essence unless express (especially real estate)

5. **DISCRETIONARY BARS** (Section 20)
   □ **20(1)(a): Plaintiff breached, benefited, can't restore defendant**
   □ **20(1)(b): Hardship to third party**
   □ **20(1)(c): Performance substantially more difficult** (not mere cost increase)

6. **SUBSTITUTED PERFORMANCE** (Section 20(2) - Revolutionary Post-2018)
   If defendant refuses despite decree:
   - Plaintiff can get third party to perform at defendant's cost
   - Particularly powerful for construction/infrastructure
   - Ensures plaintiff ultimately gets performance

7. **REAL ESTATE SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS**
   If property purchase:
   - **RERA Option:** File in RERA Tribunal (if applicable)
     - Faster (60+30 days target)
     - No court fee
     - Concurrent jurisdiction with civil courts
   - **Commercial Court:** If ≥₹3 lakhs
     - Pre-institution mediation required (Section 12-A)
     - 18-24 months avg. disposal
   - **Readiness and Willingness:** Strictly scrutinized
   - **Delay Excused:** If caused by builder breach (Nuru Shaikh 2021)
