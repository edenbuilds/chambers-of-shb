You are assisting with preparing a bail application in India.

**Task**: Guide user through bail application preparation and draft bail application.

**Workflow**:

1. **Read Protocol**:
   - Read `/Users/swarochishchekuri/Downloads/ClaudeBrain_test/Protocols/criminal_law/IL-CRIM-BAIL.md` for comprehensive bail law guidance

2. **Gather Information**:
   Ask user (if not provided):
   - **Accused details**: Name, age, address, occupation
   - **Offence**: What is the accused charged with? (IPC/BNS sections)
   - **Date of arrest**: When was accused arrested?
   - **Current status**: Is accused in custody or anticipating arrest?
   - **Charge sheet filed**: Yes/No? If yes, when?
   - **Previous bail applications**: Any earlier bail applications filed and rejected?
   - **Accused's background**: Previous convictions? Employment? Family? Roots in community?
   - **Case strength**: How strong is prosecution's case? Any weaknesses?

3. **Determine Bail Type**:
   
   **Three Types of Bail**:
   
   **A. Anticipatory Bail** (Section 438 CrPC / 482 BNSS):
   - **When**: BEFORE arrest, when person has reason to believe they may be arrested
   - **Who can grant**: Sessions Court or High Court (NOT Magistrate)
   - **Factors**: Nature of accusation, antecedents, likelihood of fleeing
   - **Continues**: Till trial conclusion (Sushila Aggarwal case, 2020)
   
   **B. Regular Bail** (Section 437 CrPC / 483 BNSS):
   - **When**: AFTER arrest
   - **For**: Non-bailable offences
   - **Who can grant**: Magistrate, Sessions Judge, High Court
   - **Restrictions**: Section 437(1) - cannot grant if offence punishable with death/life UNLESS accused is woman, child, or sick
   
   **C. Default Bail** (Section 167(2) CrPC / 187(2) BNSS):
   - **When**: Charge sheet not filed within 60/90 days of arrest
   - **Nature**: INDEFEASIBLE STATUTORY RIGHT (must be granted)
   - **Timeline**: 60 days (offences <10 years); 90 days (death, life, ≥10 years)

4. **Assess Bail Eligibility**:
   
   Based on offence, determine:
   
   **Bailable Offence**:
   - **Right to bail** (Section 436 CrPC / 482 BNSS)
   - Court must grant
   - Only surety/bond amount to decide
   
   **Non-Bailable Offence**:
   - **Discretionary** (Section 437)
   - Court considers: nature of offence, strength of case, antecedents, flight risk
   - **Difficult if**: Murder, rape, terrorism, drugs (NDPS), money laundering (PMLA)
   - **Special restrictions**: NDPS Section 37, PMLA Section 45 (twin conditions - very strict)

## FORMAT 1: ANTICIPATORY BAIL APPLICATION (Section 438 CrPC / 482 BNSS)

```
IN THE COURT OF SESSIONS JUDGE / HIGH COURT OF [STATE]
[District]

Criminal Miscellaneous Application No. ____ of 20___

In the matter of:

[Applicant Name]
S/o [Father's Name]
Aged [Age] years
Residing at [Address]
... Applicant / Petitioner

VERSUS

State represented by [Police Station]
... Respondent

APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 438 CrPC / 482 BNSS
(Anticipatory Bail)

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

1. That the applicant is a law-abiding citizen aged [age] years, residing at [address] and engaged in [occupation].

2. That the applicant has reason to believe that he/she may be arrested in connection with [FIR No. ___ dated ___ registered at Police Station ___ for offence punishable under Section(s) ___ IPC / ___ BNS].

[OR: "That complainant [name] has made false allegations against applicant and there is apprehension that police may arrest applicant."]

3. BRIEF FACTS:
[Narrate facts showing:]
- What is the accusation
- Why accusation is false / weak
- Why applicant apprehends arrest

4. GROUNDS FOR ANTICIPATORY BAIL:

a) **Nature of Accusation**: The accusations are [false/exaggerated/based on misunderstanding]. [Explain why case is weak]

b) **Antecedents of Applicant**: 
   - Applicant has clean record with no previous criminal cases
   - Applicant is [respectful occupation/position]
   - Applicant is permanent resident with deep roots in community
   - [Evidence: Employment letter, property documents, character certificates]

c) **No Flight Risk**:
   - Applicant has family residing at [address] 
   - Applicant has immovable property at [address]
   - Applicant has established business/employment
   - Passport details: [number] - willing to surrender

d) **Cooperation**:
   - Applicant undertakes to cooperate with investigation
   - Applicant will appear as and when required
   - Applicant will not tamper with evidence or influence witnesses

e) **Liberty is Sacrosanct** (Arnab Goswami v. State of Maharashtra, 2020):
   - Liberty is fundamental right under Article 21
   - Bail is rule, jail is exception
   - Anticipatory bail should not be denied mechanically

5. That applicant has not filed any earlier application for anticipatory bail in this matter.

6. That applicant is ready to abide by any conditions this Hon'ble Court may deem fit to impose.

PRAYER:

In the circumstances, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

a) Grant anticipatory bail to the applicant in the above-mentioned case
b) Direct that in event of arrest, applicant be released on bail on executing personal bond of Rs. [amount] with [number] surety/sureties for like amount to the satisfaction of arresting officer
c) Impose such conditions as this Hon'ble Court deems fit
d) Pass any other order as deemed fit in the interest of justice

AND FOR THIS ACT OF KINDNESS, THE APPLICANT SHALL DUTY BOUND FOREVER PRAY.

Date: [Date]
Place: [Place]

[Signature]
[Name of Applicant / Advocate]

ANNEXURES:
1. Copy of FIR (if available)
2. Employment proof / Business registration
3. Property documents
4. Character certificates
5. [Any other supporting documents]
```

## FORMAT 3: DEFAULT BAIL APPLICATION (Section 167(2) CrPC / 187(2) BNSS)

```
IN THE COURT OF [MAGISTRATE / SESSIONS JUDGE]
[District]

Default Bail Application No. ____ of 20___

In the matter of:

[Applicant Name]
S/o [Father's Name]
Presently lodged in [Jail Name]
... Applicant / Accused

VERSUS

State
... Respondent

APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 167(2) CrPC / 187(2) BNSS
(Default Bail / Statutory Bail)

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

1. That the applicant was arrested on [date of arrest] in FIR No. ___ dated ___ registered at Police Station ___ for offence punishable under Section(s) ___ IPC / ___ BNS.

2. That the applicant has been in continuous custody since [date of arrest].

3. CALCULATION OF PERIOD:
   - Date of arrest: [Date]
   - Today's date: [Date]
   - Total period in custody: [Number] days

4. OFFENCE AND APPLICABLE TIMELINE:
   - Offence alleged: Section ___ IPC / ___ BNS
   - Punishable with: [State punishment - if death/life/≥10 years: 90 days; if <10 years: 60 days]
   - **Applicable timeline**: [60 days / 90 days]

5. CHARGE SHEET NOT FILED:
   - Despite expiry of [60/90] days from date of arrest, charge sheet has NOT been filed
   - Period expired on: [Date]

6. INDEFEASIBLE STATUTORY RIGHT:
   - Under Section 167(2) CrPC / 187(2) BNSS, if investigation not completed and charge sheet not filed within [60/90] days, accused is entitled to be released on bail
   - This is an **INDEFEASIBLE STATUTORY RIGHT** (Sanjay Dutt v. State, 1994)
   - Court is **BOUND** to grant bail (not discretionary)
   - Gravity of offence is irrelevant for default bail

7. That applicant is ready to execute personal bond with sureties as required.

PRAYER:

In the circumstances, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

a) Take judicial notice that charge sheet has not been filed within statutory period of [60/90] days
b) Grant DEFAULT BAIL to applicant as a matter of indefeasible statutory right under Section 167(2) CrPC / 187(2) BNSS
c) Release applicant on executing personal bond of Rs. [amount] with [number] surety/sureties
d) Pass any other order deemed fit

AND FOR THIS ACT OF KINDNESS, THE APPLICANT SHALL DUTY BOUND FOREVER PRAY.

Date: [Date]
Place: [Place]

[Signature]
[Name of Applicant / Advocate]

ANNEXURES:
1. Copy of FIR
2. Remand orders (showing dates and custody periods)
3. Calculation sheet of custody period
```

6. **Key Precedents to Cite**:

```
**LANDMARK CASES ON BAIL**:

1. **Arnab Goswami v. State of Maharashtra (2020)**: Liberty is sacrosanct; bail cannot be denied mechanically

2. **Sushila Aggarwal v. State (NCT of Delhi) (2020)**: Anticipatory bail continues till trial conclusion; survives actual arrest

3. **Gurbaksh Singh Sibbia v. State of Punjab (1980)**: Anticipatory bail is constitutionally valid; aid to liberty

4. **Sanjay Dutt v. State (1994)**: Default bail is indefeasible statutory right; must be granted

5. **Gudikanti Narasimhulu v. Public Prosecutor (1978)**: Bail is rule, jail is exception

6. **Dataram Singh v. State of U.P. (2018)**: Sick/infirm persons granted bail even in serious offences (humanitarian grounds)

7. **Arnesh Kumar v. State of Bihar (2014)**: No automatic arrest for offences <7 years
```
