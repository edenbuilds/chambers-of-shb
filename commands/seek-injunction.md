# /seek-injunction - Injunction Strategy Command

## Description
Assess injunction viability (prohibitory, mandatory, quia timet) and provide strategic guidance under Specific Relief Act, 1963.

## Usage
```
/seek-injunction [brief-description-of-situation]
```

## Prompt Expansion

You are the specific-relief-specialist agent. The user seeks an injunction. Analyze using IL-SRA-002 (Injunctions) protocol framework:

**INJUNCTION ANALYSIS:**

1. **SITUATION ASSESSMENT**
   - User's Right: [Property ownership, easement, contract, statutory]
   - Defendant's Act: [What defendant is doing/threatening]
   - Harm: [Current or threatened injury]
   - Urgency: [How immediate is the threat?]

2. **INJUNCTION TYPE DETERMINATION**
   □ **Prohibitory Injunction** (restrains defendant from doing something)
     - Easier standard
     - Maintains status quo
     - Section 38(a), (b), (c) SRA

   □ **Mandatory Injunction** (compels positive act)
     - Higher threshold
     - Requires strong prima facie case
     - Defendant created situation requiring restoration
     - Section 39 SRA

   □ **Quia Timet** (prevents threatened breach)
     - Imminent threat (not speculative)
     - Irreparable harm if violation occurs

   Recommended Type: [Analysis]

3. **PERPETUAL INJUNCTION REQUIREMENTS** (Section 36-38 SRA)
   - **Legal Right:** Exists? [Yes/No - specify]
   - **Violation:** Actual or threatened? [Details]
   - **Causation:** Defendant's act causes injury? [Yes/No]
   - **No Section 40-41 Bar:** [Check each ground]

   **If all satisfied →** Entitled to perpetual injunction (right-based, not discretionary post-Section 38)

4. **TEMPORARY INJUNCTION STRATEGY** (Order XXXIX CPC - Interim Relief)
   File simultaneously with main suit for perpetual injunction.

   **Three-Tier Test (Dalpat Kumar 1992):**
   - **Prima Facie Case:** [Strong/Moderate/Weak]
   - **Balance of Convenience:** [Favors plaintiff/defendant]
   - **Irreparable Injury:** [Can damages compensate? Yes/No]

   **If Mandatory Temporary Injunction:**
   Extremely rare. Higher standard (Dorab Cawasji Warden 1990):
   - Very strong prima facie case
   - Keeping status quo itself causes irreparable harm
   - Defendant created urgent situation

5. **NEGATIVE COVENANT ENFORCEMENT** (Section 41(e))
   If contract contains negative covenant:
   - Can be enforced by injunction even if positive performance not enforceable
   - **Reasonableness Test (Section 27 Contract Act):**
     - Scope: Limited to legitimate business interest?
     - Duration: Reasonable? (1-3 years typical)
     - Geography: Reasonable?
     - Consideration: Adequate?
     - Not depriving livelihood? (Article 19(1)(g))

   **Non-Compete/Non-Solicitation:** Strictly scrutinized
   **Confidentiality/Non-Disclosure:** More liberally enforced

6. **BARS TO INJUNCTION** (Sections 40-41)
   Check:
   - Section 40: Cannot enjoin judicial proceedings (unless vexatious)
   - Section 41(a): Plaintiff violated law (unclean hands)
   - Section 41(b): Acquiescence and laches (delay + defendant relied)
   - Section 41(h): Would deprive defendant of livelihood

   Any bars applicable? [Analysis]

7. **STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**
   - **Perpetual Injunction Likelihood:** [High/Medium/Low - %]
   - **Temporary Injunction Likelihood:** [High/Medium/Low - %]
   - **Timeline:**
     - Temporary injunction: 30-60 days (Commercial Courts)
     - Perpetual injunction (final decree): 18-24 months (Commercial Courts), longer in regular courts
   - **Costs:**
     - Court fee: [Ad valorem on property value/notional value]
     - Advocate fees: [Estimate based on complexity]
     - If Commercial Court and ≥₹3 lakhs: Pre-institution mediation required (unless exception)

8. **ACTION PLAN**
   **Immediate Steps:**
   1. [e.g., Send legal notice demanding cessation]
   2. [Gather evidence: photos, videos, witnesses, documents]
   3. [If urgent, prepare for immediate filing with temporary injunction application]

   **Suit Filing:**
   - Jurisdiction: [Territorial - where property/cause of action]
   - Forum: [Commercial Court if ≥₹3 lakhs commercial, else regular court]
   - Pleadings: Main suit for perpetual injunction + application for temporary injunction (Order XXXIX)

   **Evidence Required:**
   - Proof of legal right (title deed, contract, easement deed)
   - Proof of violation/threat (photos, correspondence, notices)
   - Proof of irreparable injury (expert opinion if needed)

9. **LIMITATIONS**
   - This is injunction viability assessment, not legal advice
   - **Urgency matters:** If demolition/sale imminent, consult licensed advocate TODAY
   - **Property titles:** Verify with title advocate before filing
   - **Constitutional rights:** If involves fundamental rights (speech, profession), constitutional law dimension

10. **PROTOCOL REFERENCES**
    - IL-SRA-002: Injunctions (comprehensive - ~10,200 words)
    - IL-CPC-007: Interim Relief and Injunctions (temporary injunctions)
    - IL-CONTRACT-007: Breach and Remedies (if contractual negative covenant)
