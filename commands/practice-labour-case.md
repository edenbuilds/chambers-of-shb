## Purpose
Simulate realistic labour court proceedings for lawyers to practice arguments, cross-examination, objection handling, and judicial reasoning. Provides AI-powered opposing counsel, judge responses, and case law challenges for comprehensive litigation skills development in wrongful termination, retrenchment, and industrial disputes cases.

## Usage
```
/practice-labour-case <case-scenario> [difficulty: easy/medium/hard]
```

## What This Command Does

When you invoke `/practice-labour-case`, I will create:

1. **Complete Case Simulation:**
   - Realistic fact pattern (wrongful termination, retrenchment, domestic enquiry defect)
   - Worker's statement (examination-in-chief affidavit)
   - Employer's defense (written statement)
   - Documentary evidence (appointment letter, salary slips, termination letter - described)
   - Procedural history (conciliation failed, labour court reference filed)

2. **AI Opposing Counsel:**
   - Aggressive cross-examination questions (to test your client preparation)
   - Strategic objections to your examination
   - Counter-arguments to your submissions
   - Case law citations challenging your position
   - Settlement negotiation tactics

3. **AI Judge (Presiding Officer):**
   - Procedural questions (admissibility, relevance, jurisdiction)
   - Pointed queries on legal principles (burden of proof, domestic enquiry standards)
   - Challenges to your argument logic (flaws, contradictions)
   - Case law testing ("Counsel, have you read *XYZ v. ABC*? How do you distinguish?")
   - Final assessment with judgment draft

4. **Interactive Examination Practice:**
   - **Cross-Examination**: You cross-examine employer's witnesses (AI plays witness)
   - **Re-Examination**: Rehabilitate your client after damaging cross-examination
   - **Objection Handling**: AI judge rules on your objections (sustained/overruled)
   - **Witness Coaching Simulation**: Prepare your client (worker) for cross-examination

5. **Argument Practice Modes:**
   - **Opening Statement**: Frame the case (1-minute pitch to judge)
   - **Evidence Stage Arguments**: Objections, admissibility, procedural motions
   - **Final Arguments**: 10-minute structured closing (3-point framework)
   - **Rebuttal**: Respond to opposing counsel's arguments (spontaneous thinking)

6. **Case Law Challenge Mode:**
   - AI judge cites adverse precedent ("Counsel, in *State Bank of India v. N. Sundara Money*, Supreme Court held...")
   - You must distinguish the case OR accept and argue exception
   - Tests your knowledge of labour law jurisprudence

7. **Difficulty Levels:**
   - **Easy**: Straightforward wrongful termination (no domestic enquiry, clear facts)
   - **Medium**: Procedurally defective enquiry (requires nuanced argument on natural justice)
   - **Hard**: Valid retrenchment challenged (requires creative legal arguments, last-in-first-out violation, mala fide downsizing)

8. **Performance Feedback:**
   - Argument strength assessment (persuasiveness, legal accuracy)
   - Cross-examination effectiveness (question quality, trap-setting)
   - Objection strategy (when to object, when to let it go)
   - Case law application (appropriate citations, distinguishing adverse precedents)
   - Suggested improvements (alternative arguments, better phrasing)

9. **Judgment Simulation:**
   - AI judge delivers reasoned judgment (for/against your client)
   - Analysis of strengths/weaknesses in your presentation
   - Alternative arguments you could have made
   - Appellate prospects assessment (if you lose)

10. **Specializations:**
    - **Wrongful Termination** (no enquiry, defective enquiry)
    - **Retrenchment Compliance** (Section 25F challenges)
    - **Unfair Labor Practices** (Schedule V, ID Act)
    - **Domestic Enquiry Defects** (procedural justice violations)
    - **Statutory Benefits Recovery** (PF, Gratuity, Bonus disputes)

### YOUR ARGUMENT STRUCTURE (10 Minutes)

**POINT 1: BIASED ENQUIRY OFFICER (Fatal Defect)**

> "Your Honour, the domestic enquiry in this case is VITIATED by BIAS. The enquiry officer, Mr. Sharma, was the reporting manager of the Applicant. He was not an independent tribunal. He had supervisory authority over the Applicant, evaluated his performance, and had admitted to work disagreements.
>
> Under the principles of natural justice, *nemo judex in causa sua* - no man shall be a judge in his own cause. The Supreme Court in **Siemens Ltd. v. Union of India (1976)** held that an enquiry officer must be INDEPENDENT and FREE FROM BIAS. Here, the enquiry officer was an INTERESTED PARTY.
>
> Further, Mr. Sharma was the very person who brought the theft complaint to HR. He was BOTH complainant and judge. This is a GROSS violation of natural justice, rendering the enquiry VOID."

---

**POINT 2: NO CROSS-EXAMINATION OF COMPLAINANT (Natural Justice Violation)**

> "Your Honour, the Applicant was denied the right to cross-examine the person who leveled the charge - Mr. Sharma himself. Mr. Sharma claimed security informed him of theft, but the security guard was NEVER examined as a witness. The Applicant could not test the veracity of the complaint.
>
> The Supreme Court in **Mohinder Singh Gill v. Chief Election Commissioner (1978)** held that principles of natural justice require:
> 1. Notice of charges
> 2. Opportunity to be heard
> 3. **Right to cross-examine witnesses**
>
> Here, the RIGHT TO CROSS-EXAMINE was DENIED. This alone is sufficient to invalidate the enquiry."

---

**POINT 3: TERMINATION IS ILLEGAL (Reinstatement Warranted)**

> "Your Honour, when a domestic enquiry is vitiated by bias and procedural defects, the termination based on such enquiry is ILLEGAL. The employer cannot rely on a defective enquiry to justify dismissal.
>
> The Supreme Court in **Bharat Forge Co. Ltd. v. Uttam Manohar Nakate (2005)** held that where an enquiry is fundamentally flawed, the worker is entitled to REINSTATEMENT with FULL BACK WAGES.
>
> In this case, the enquiry is FUNDAMENTALLY FLAWED due to bias. The termination is therefore ILLEGAL and VOID. The Applicant is entitled to:
> 1. Reinstatement with continuity of service
> 2. Back wages from termination date
> 3. Statutory benefits: PF, Gratuity
>
> I pray this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to allow this reference and grant reinstatement with back wages. Thank you, Your Honour."

>
> Here, my learned friend has NOT proven any actual bias. Mr. Sharma conducted the enquiry fairly, examined evidence, and gave the worker an opportunity to defend.
>
> Further, the worker was found guilty based on EVIDENCE - security footage showed him near the store room, and 10 meters of steel wire was found missing. The enquiry officer's finding is based on EVIDENCE, not bias.
>
> As regards cross-examination, the worker could have cross-examined the security guard. The enquiry officer called the security guard as a witness. The worker chose NOT to cross-examine.
>
> I submit this is a case of a worker caught stealing, trying to escape on technicalities. The enquiry was fair, the finding is valid, and the dismissal is justified. I pray the reference be dismissed. Thank you."
