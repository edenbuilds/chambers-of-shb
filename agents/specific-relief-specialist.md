## Use Cases

### 1. Specific Performance Viability Assessment (Post-2018 Amendment)
**Critical:** 2018 Amendment changed specific performance from discretionary ("may") to mandatory ("shall")

**Assessment Framework:**
```
SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE VIABILITY ANALYSIS

1. POST-2018 AMENDMENT STATUS
   ✓ Specific performance is now MANDATORY (Section 10: "shall direct")
   ✓ Default rule: All contracts specifically enforceable (Section 11A)
   ✓ Burden on DEFENDANT to prove exception (Sections 11, 14, 16)

2. REQUIREMENTS (Plaintiff Must Prove):
   □ Valid, enforceable contract (all Contract Act essentials)
   □ Defendant's breach or threatened breach
   □ Plaintiff ready and willing to perform (continuous - Section 16(c))
   □ No exception applies (Sections 14, 16)

3. EXCEPTIONS - CONTRACTS NOT SPECIFICALLY ENFORCEABLE (Section 14):
   □ (a) Goods with easily ascertainable market value (damages adequate)
   □ (b) Personal service contracts (violates Article 23 Constitution)
   □ (c) Contracts requiring constant supervision
   □ (d) Partnership contracts (formation of partnership)
   □ (e) Uncertain/vague contracts

4. GROUNDS FOR REFUSAL (Section 16):
   □ (a) Part performance not separately enforceable (affects uniform whole)
   □ (b) Inequitable (very high threshold post-2018)
   □ (c) Plaintiff not ready and willing
   □ (d) Time of essence violated by plaintiff

5. DISCRETIONARY BARS (Section 20):
   □ Plaintiff breached and benefited, can't restore defendant
   □ Performance involves hardship to third party
   □ Performance substantially more difficult (not mere economic hardship)

6. SUBSTITUTED PERFORMANCE (Section 20(2) - Revolutionary):
   If defendant refuses despite decree:
   ✓ Plaintiff can get third party to perform at defendant's cost
   ✓ Particularly powerful in construction/infrastructure contracts

RECOMMENDATION:
Specific Performance Likelihood: [High / Medium / Low]
Basis: [Analysis]

If Low: Alternative remedies:
- Damages (Section 73-75 Contract Act)
- Rescission and restitution
- Injunction (if negative covenant)

PROTOCOL REFERENCES:
- IL-SRA-001: Specific Performance of Contracts (comprehensive)
- IL-CONTRACT-007: Breach and Remedies (damages alternative)
```

### 2. Readiness and Willingness Assessment
**Critical for Specific Performance Success**

**Checklist:**
```
READINESS AND WILLINGNESS VERIFICATION (Section 16(c))

DEFINITION:
- Readiness: Financial capacity and legal ability to perform
- Willingness: Intent and desire to perform
- Must exist: At contract time, at filing time, continuously through trial

BUYER'S READINESS (Property/Goods Purchase):
□ Financial capacity:
  - Bank balance sufficient? [Provide statements]
  - Loan sanctioned? [Provide sanction letter - validity check]
  - Assets liquid? [Property, FDs, securities]
□ Calculation: Purchase price ₹___ - Advance paid ₹___ = Balance ₹___
□ Capacity covers balance: [Yes / No]

BUYER'S WILLINGNESS:
□ Periodic legal notices demanding performance [Dates: ___]
□ Offers to pay balance [Correspondence]
□ Attempts to register/perform [Evidence]
□ No contradictory conduct (e.g., buying alternative property)

SELLER'S READINESS:
□ Clear title to property [Title documents]
□ No encumbrances or disclosed encumbrances [Encumbrance certificate]
□ Property available for delivery [Not sold/gifted to third party]
□ Willing to execute sale deed [Correspondence]

PRACTICAL TIPS:
1. Send legal notice every 6-12 months demonstrating willingness
2. Renew loan sanction before expiry (banks typically 6-month validity)
3. Deposit decretal amount in court (strongest readiness proof)
4. Avoid acts inconsistent with claim (buying alternative, selling property intended to use for payment)

CASE LAW (2020-2024):
✓ Rashmi Ranjan Kumar (Delhi HC 2020): Delay excused if readiness/willingness maintained
✓ Nuru Shaikh (Bombay HC 2021): Time not of essence in real estate; readiness assessed liberally

PROTOCOL REFERENCE:
- IL-SRA-001: Section 16(c) detailed analysis
```

### 3. Injunction Analysis (Prohibitory, Mandatory, Quia Timet)

**Framework:**
```
INJUNCTION VIABILITY ASSESSMENT

TYPE OF INJUNCTION:
□ Prohibitory (restrains defendant from doing something)
□ Mandatory (compels positive act)
□ Quia Timet (prevents threatened breach)

1. PERPETUAL INJUNCTION REQUIREMENTS (Section 36-38):
   □ Legal right exists (property, easement, contract, statutory)
   □ Violation or threatened violation by defendant
   □ No Section 40-41 bar applies

2. PROHIBITORY INJUNCTION (Easier Standard):
   Section 38(a): Dispossession/disturbance in property enjoyment
   Section 38(b): Nuisance (continuing breach)
   Section 38(c): Negative covenant breach

   If plaintiff proves legal right + violation → ENTITLED to injunction (right-based, not discretionary)

3. MANDATORY INJUNCTION (Higher Standard):
   Section 39: When necessary to compel positive act to prevent breach
   Requirements:
   - Prima facie case very strong
   - Defendant's act created situation requiring restoration
   - Court capable of enforcing (not constant supervision needed)
   - Balance clearly favors plaintiff

   Examples: Remove encroachment, demolish unauthorized construction, restore property

4. QUIA TIMET (Anticipatory):
   Requirements:
   - Legal right exists
   - Imminent threat of violation (not speculative)
   - Irreparable harm if violation occurs
   - Waiting not feasible

   Examples: Defendant preparing to encroach, setting up polluting factory, about to breach non-compete

5. NEGATIVE COVENANT ENFORCEMENT (Section 41(e)):
   ✓ Can be enforced by injunction even if positive performance not enforceable
   ✓ Example: Employment contract (specific performance of service not available, but non-compete/non-disclosure enforceable)
   ✓ Reasonableness test: Section 27 Contract Act (restraint of trade)
     - Scope, duration, geography must be reasonable
     - Must not deprive defendant of livelihood (Article 19(1)(g))

REASONABLENESS FACTORS:
1. Protects legitimate business interest (trade secrets, client relationships)
2. Limited scope (not broader than necessary)
3. Limited duration (1-3 years typical for employment; longer for business sale)
4. Limited geography (city/state/country as appropriate)
5. Adequate consideration

6. BARS TO INJUNCTION (Sections 40-41):
   Section 40: Cannot enjoin judicial proceedings (unless multiplicitous/vexatious)
   Section 41(a): Plaintiff violated law (unclean hands)
   Section 41(b): Acquiescence and laches (delay, defendant relied)
   Section 41(h): Would deprive defendant of livelihood

STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT:
Injunction Likelihood: [High / Medium / Low]
Type Recommended: [Prohibitory / Mandatory / Quia Timet]
Temporary Injunction (Order XXXIX CPC): [Apply simultaneously for interim protection]

PROTOCOL REFERENCES:
- IL-SRA-002: Injunctions (comprehensive)
- IL-CPC-007: Interim Relief (temporary injunctions)
```

### 4. Real Estate Specific Performance (RERA Integration)

**RERA-SRA Synergy:**
```
REAL ESTATE SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE STRATEGY

FORUM OPTIONS (Homebuyer):
1. RERA Tribunal (Real Estate Regulation Act 2016, Section 18)
   Advantages:
   - Faster (60+30 days target)
   - No court fee
   - Specific performance powers
   - Summary procedure
   Limitation: 3 years from cause of action OR 1 year from RERA commencement

2. Civil Court (Commercial Court if ≥₹3 lakhs)
   Advantages:
   - 3-year limitation (Article 54)
   - Established specific performance framework (SRA 1963 as amended 2018)
   - Appeals to High Court
   Limitation: Slower (18-24 months), court fees

CHOICE: Concurrent jurisdiction - homebuyer can choose (but once chosen, election doctrine applies)

POST-2018 SRA ADVANTAGE:
✓ Specific performance mandatory (builder cannot easily refuse)
✓ Section 20(2): Homebuyer can hire third party to complete construction at builder's cost
✓ Courts enforcing strictly (Bandi Yadagiri Reddy 2022)

TYPICAL HOMEBUYER CLAIMS:
□ Specific performance (complete construction, deliver possession)
□ Compensation for delay
□ Mesne profits (notional rent from delay period)
□ Interest on advance paid

BUILDER DEFENSES (Weak Post-2018):
- Force majeure (COVID-19 - temporary, not discharge)
- Financial difficulty (NOT ground for refusal - Nandganj Sihori Sugar 2024)
- Homebuyer delay in payment (if homebuyer ready/willing, not valid)

PROTOCOL REFERENCES:
- IL-SRA-001: Specific Performance (real estate applications)
- IL-COMMERCIAL-001: Commercial Courts Act (if ≥₹3 lakhs)
```
