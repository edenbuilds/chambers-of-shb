# IL-CRIM-KIDNAPPING

**Protocol ID**: IL-CRIM-KIDNAPPING
**Domain**: Criminal Law > Crimes Against Person
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Last Updated**: 2025-12-06
**Statutory Basis**: Indian Penal Code (IPC) Sections 359-369 | Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 Sections 87-95

---

## Overview

**Kidnapping** under Indian criminal law involves unlawfully taking or enticing a person, primarily to protect vulnerable individuals (minors, mentally unsound persons) and preserve lawful guardianship. This protocol covers:

- Two distinct types: Kidnapping from India vs. kidnapping from lawful guardianship
- Age thresholds (16 years for males, 18 years for females)
- Aggravated forms (for murder, ransom, slavery, begging)
- Distinction from abduction
- IPC to BNS transition and landmark cases

---

## Statutory Framework

### IPC (Pre-July 1, 2024)

**Section 359**: Definition of kidnapping (two types)
**Section 360**: Kidnapping from India
**Section 361**: Kidnapping from lawful guardianship
**Sections 363-369**: Specific kidnapping offenses and punishments

### BNS (Post-July 1, 2024)

**Sections 87-95**: Corresponds to IPC kidnapping provisions (definitions and punishments largely unchanged)

---

## Definition of Kidnapping (IPC Section 359)

**Two Distinct Types** (mutually exclusive):

1. **Kidnapping from India** (Section 360)
2. **Kidnapping from Lawful Guardianship** (Section 361)

**Key Distinction**:
- **From India**: Geographical element (taking person out of India)
- **From Lawful Guardianship**: Guardian's control element (taking minor/unsound person from guardian, even within India)

---

## Kidnapping from India (IPC Section 360 / BNS Section 88)

### Statutory Text

**Section 360**: "Whoever conveys any person beyond the limits of India without the consent of that person, or of some person legally authorized to consent on behalf of that person, is said to kidnap that person from India."

### Essential Elements

1. **Conveyance**: Accused conveyed (transported, moved) a person
2. **Beyond India's Limits**: Outside territorial jurisdiction of India
3. **Without Consent**:
   - No consent from person conveyed (if capable of giving consent)
   - OR no consent from person legally authorized (if minor/unsound person)

---

### Element Analysis

#### 1. Conveyance

**Meaning**: Physical transportation, not mere enticement or persuasion

**Examples**:
- Putting person on ship/plane departing India
- Driving person across border (India-Nepal, India-Bangladesh)
- Transporting unconscious person out of India

**Not Conveyance**: Merely sending ticket or persuading person to leave (person must be physically moved by accused).

---

#### 2. Beyond India's Limits

**Territorial Limits**:
- **Land Borders**: International borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar
- **Maritime Borders**: Territorial waters (12 nautical miles from baseline)
- **Airspace**: Overflight of foreign territory in aircraft

**Completed Offense**: As soon as person crosses border (even 1 meter outside India).

**Example**: Accused drives victim from Punjab to Pakistan (crossing Wagah border) → Offense complete at border crossing.

---

#### 3. Without Consent

**Consent from Competent Person**:
- If person is adult (≥18 years) and of sound mind → Person's own consent required
- If person is minor (<18 years) OR unsound mind → Consent from lawful guardian required

**Invalid Consent**:
- Consent obtained by coercion, fraud, or misrepresentation → No valid consent
- Consent from unauthorized person → No valid consent

**Example**: A tells B (adult woman) "We're going to Nepal for tourism." B agrees. A takes B to Nepal and sells B into trafficking → Consent vitiated by fraud → Kidnapping from India.

---

### Punishment (IPC Section 360)

> "Whoever kidnaps any person from India shall be punished with imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine."

**Nature**: Cognizable, non-bailable, triable by Magistrate of First Class.

---

## Kidnapping from Lawful Guardianship (IPC Section 361 / BNS Section 87)

### Statutory Text

**Section 361**: "Whoever takes or entices any minor under **16 years of age** (if male) or **under 18 years of age** (if female), or any person of **unsound mind**, out of the keeping of the lawful guardian of such minor or person of unsound mind, without the consent of such guardian, is said to kidnap such minor or person from lawful guardianship."

---

### Essential Elements

1. **Taking or Enticing**: Physical removal OR persuading to leave
2. **Minor (Age Threshold)**:
   - Male: Under **16 years** (15 years 364 days)
   - Female: Under **18 years** (17 years 364 days)
   - OR Person of unsound mind (any age)
3. **Out of Keeping**: From physical control/care of lawful guardian
4. **Lawful Guardian**: Parent, adoptive parent, court-appointed guardian
5. **Without Consent**: Guardian's consent not obtained

---

### Element Analysis

#### 1. Taking or Enticing

**Taking**:
- **Active**: Physically removing minor/unsound person (e.g., carrying away, dragging)
- **Force Not Required**: Consent of minor is irrelevant (offense complete even if minor goes willingly)

**Enticing**:
- **Passive**: Persuading, inducing, alluring minor to leave guardian's keeping
- **Sweet Words**: "Come with me, I'll buy you ice cream" (no force, but persuasion)

**Landmark Case**: **Thakorlal D. Vadgama v. State of Gujarat (1973)**

**Principle**: "Enticement" does not require deceit or fraudulent inducement; merely persuading or alluring minor to leave suffices.

**Example**: A (25 years) tells B (15-year-old girl) "Let's go watch movie." B agrees and leaves home without parents' knowledge → Enticement (even if no deceit).

---

#### 2. Age Thresholds (Gender Disparity)

| Gender | Age Threshold | Rationale |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| **Male** | Under **16 years** | Presumed capable of self-care after 16 years |
| **Female** | Under **18 years** | Enhanced protection due to societal vulnerability + marriage age (historically 18 years for women) |

**Determining Age**:
- **Best Evidence**: Birth certificate, school records, medical examination (bone ossification test)
- **Burden**: Prosecution must prove minor was below threshold age at time of kidnapping
- **Benefit of Doubt**: If age uncertain, benefit goes to accused

**Example**: Girl's birth certificate shows DOB: 01-Jan-2006. Kidnapped: 15-Dec-2023 (age 17 years 11 months) → Under 18 years → Kidnapping from lawful guardianship.

---

#### 3. Out of Keeping of Lawful Guardian

**Keeping** (Physical Control):
- Minor resides with guardian (parent, adoptive parent)
- Guardian exercises care, custody, and control
- **Not Required**: Guardian need not be physically present at time of taking (e.g., child taken from school during guardian's absence)

**Lawful Guardian**:
- **Natural Guardian**: Father, mother (Guardians and Wards Act 1890)
- **Adoptive Guardian**: Person legally adopted minor
- **Court-Appointed Guardian**: Guardian appointed by court order

**Not Lawful Guardian**:
- Stranger, friend, relative (without legal guardianship)
- School teacher (temporary supervisory role, not legal guardian)

**Example**: A takes 10-year-old child from school playground while parents are at work → Out of keeping (parents are lawful guardians, physical presence not required).

---

#### 4. Without Consent of Guardian

**Consent Required From**:
- Lawful guardian (not minor)
- **Minor's Consent Irrelevant**: Even if minor voluntarily goes with accused, offense complete if guardian did not consent

**Exception** (Section 361 Exception):
> "This section does not extend to the act of any person who in good faith believes himself to be the **father** of an illegitimate child, or who in good faith believes himself to be **entitled to lawful custody** of such minor or person of unsound mind."

**Good Faith Belief**: Accused genuinely believed he had lawful custody (subjective test + reasonableness).

**Example**: Divorced father (without custody rights) takes child from mother's home believing he has custody → Section 361 Exception may apply if belief was in good faith.

---

### Punishment (IPC Section 363 / BNS Section 89)

**Section 363**: Punishment for kidnapping

> "Whoever kidnaps any person from India or from lawful guardianship shall be punished with imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine."

**Nature**: Cognizable, non-bailable, triable by Magistrate of First Class.

---

## Aggravated Forms of Kidnapping

### Kidnapping for Murder (IPC Section 364 / BNS Section 90)

**Elements**:
1. Kidnapping (from India or lawful guardianship)
2. **Intent**: To murder OR with knowledge likely to cause murder

**Punishment**: **Death penalty** OR **life imprisonment** + fine

**Example**: A kidnaps child intending to murder child for ransom non-payment → Section 364.

---

### Kidnapping for Ransom (IPC Section 364A / BNS Section 91)

**Elements**:
1. Kidnapping or abduction
2. **Intent**: To hold person for ransom OR to compel government/person to do/abstain from doing act
3. **Threat**: Death or hurt to kidnapped person if demands not met

**Punishment**: **Death penalty** OR **life imprisonment** + fine

**Landmark Case**: **Sunil Batra v. Delhi Administration (1979)**

**Principle**: Kidnapping for ransom is aggravated form due to economic motive + threat to life. Deterrent punishment required.

---

### Kidnapping for Slavery (IPC Section 367 / BNS Section 93)

**Elements**:
1. Kidnapping or abduction
2. **Intent**: To subject person to slavery OR to compel person to labor against will

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

**Example**: A kidnaps child from village and sells child to factory owner for bonded labor → Section 367.

---

### Kidnapping for Begging (IPC Section 363A / BNS Section 92)

**Elements**:
1. Kidnapping or abduction
2. **Person Kidnapped**: Minor (under 18 years) OR person with unsound mind/disability
3. **Intent**: To employ or use for begging

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

**Example**: A kidnaps 10-year-old child and forces child to beg on streets → Section 363A.

---

## Distinction: Kidnapping vs. Abduction

### Comparative Table

| Aspect | **Kidnapping (IPC 359-361)** | **Abduction (IPC 362)** |
|--------|------------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Definition** | Taking person from India OR from lawful guardianship (minor/unsound) | Forcing, compelling, or deceitfully inducing person to go from any place |
| **Age Limit** | Male < 16 years, Female < 18 years (for lawful guardianship) | **No age limit** (applies to any person, adult or minor) |
| **Consent** | **Minor's consent irrelevant** (only guardian's consent matters) | **Person's consent vitiated** by force/compulsion/deceit |
| **Mens Rea** | Intention to take out of guardian's keeping suffices | Intention to use force/compulsion/deceit required |
| **Standalone Offense?** | YES (punishable under Section 363) | **NO** (must be abduction "in order to" commit another offense - Section 362 not punishable alone) |
| **Example** | 17-year-old girl leaves home willingly with boyfriend (parents unaware) → Kidnapping (girl's consent irrelevant) | Adult woman forced into car and driven away → Abduction (if done to murder/rape, punishable; if no further offense, not punishable) |

---

### Abduction (IPC Section 362)

**Statutory Text**: "Whoever by force compels, or by any deceitful means induces, any person to go from any place, is said to **abduct** that person."

**Essential Elements**:
1. **Force OR Deceit**: Compulsion or fraudulent inducement
2. **Movement**: Person goes from any place (even short distance)
3. **Intent**: To commit further offense (abduction alone not punishable)

**Abduction Must Be Coupled With**:
- **Section 365**: Abduction with intent to secretly/wrongfully confine → 7 years
- **Section 366**: Abduction to compel marriage → 10 years
- **Section 366A**: Procuration of minor girl (under 18 years) for illicit intercourse → 10 years
- **Section 366B**: Importing girl (under 21 years) from foreign country for illicit intercourse → 10 years

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Kidnapping from India (IPC Section 360)

**Scenario**: A, a human trafficker, convinces B (25-year-old woman) to go to Dubai for "job opportunity." A transports B to Dubai by air. Upon arrival, A confiscates B's passport and sells B to brothel owner.

**Analysis**:
- **Conveyance**: A physically transported B from India to Dubai (by air)
- **Beyond India**: Dubai is outside India's territorial limits
- **Without Consent**: B's consent vitiated by fraud ("job opportunity" was false pretense)

**Charge**: **IPC Section 360** (kidnapping from India) + **IPC Section 370** (human trafficking)

**Punishment**: Section 360: 7 years + fine; Section 370: 7 years-life + fine.

---

### Example 2: Kidnapping from Lawful Guardianship (IPC Section 363)

**Scenario**: A (22-year-old male) befriends B (15-year-old girl) on social media. A persuades B to run away from home and elope. B willingly leaves home and meets A. Parents file police complaint.

**Analysis**:
- **Minor**: B is 15 years (under 18 years for females) → Minor
- **Enticement**: A persuaded B to leave home ("run away and elope") → Enticement
- **Out of Keeping**: B left parents' physical control
- **Without Consent**: Parents (lawful guardians) did not consent
- **B's Consent Irrelevant**: Even though B went willingly, her consent does not matter (only parents' consent matters)

**Charge**: **IPC Section 363** (kidnapping from lawful guardianship)

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to 7 years + fine.

**Note**: This is the most common kidnapping scenario in India (elopement/romance cases).

---

### Example 3: Kidnapping for Ransom (IPC Section 364A)

**Scenario**: A and B kidnap C (wealthy businessman's son, age 25 years) from his office parking lot. A and B demand ₹1 crore ransom from C's father, threatening to kill C if ransom not paid within 48 hours.

**Analysis**:
- **Kidnapping**: C is adult, taken by force (abduction, not kidnapping from lawful guardianship)
- **Ransom Demand**: A and B hold C for ₹1 crore ransom
- **Threat**: Death threat if ransom not paid

**Charge**: **IPC Section 364A** (kidnapping for ransom) + **IPC Section 387** (extortion by threat of death)

**Punishment**: Section 364A: **Death penalty OR life imprisonment** + fine (aggravated kidnapping).

---

### Example 4: Kidnapping for Begging (IPC Section 363A)

**Scenario**: A kidnaps 8-year-old child from railway station. A mutilates child's arm (to evoke sympathy) and forces child to beg on streets. Child's begging earnings given to A.

**Analysis**:
- **Minor**: 8 years (under 18 years)
- **Kidnapping**: Taken from lawful guardianship (parents)
- **Intent**: To employ child for begging

**Charge**: **IPC Section 363A** (kidnapping for begging) + **IPC Section 325** (voluntarily causing grievous hurt - arm mutilation) + **IPC Section 370** (human trafficking - exploitation)

**Punishment**: Section 363A: 10 years + fine; Section 325: 7 years + fine; Section 370: 7 years-life + fine.

---

### Example 5: No Kidnapping (Minor's Consent + Guardian's Consent)

**Scenario**: A (18-year-old male) wants to marry B (17-year-old girl). B's parents approve the relationship. A and B leave together to register marriage at court. B's distant relatives (opposed to marriage) file kidnapping complaint against A.

**Analysis**:
- **Minor**: B is 17 years (under 18 years for females)
- **Taking**: A and B left together (enticement/taking present)
- **Lawful Guardians**: B's **parents** (not distant relatives)
- **Consent**: B's **parents consented** to relationship and marriage

**Charge**: **NO kidnapping** (parents' consent present)

**Legal Position**: Parents are lawful guardians; their consent negates kidnapping charge (distant relatives' complaint without merit).

## Landmark Cases

### 1. **Thakorlal D. Vadgama v. State of Gujarat (1973)**

**Facts**: Accused (adult male) persuaded minor girl (under 18 years) to leave her father's home and accompany him. Girl left willingly. No force, no deceit (girl aware she was leaving home).

**Issue**: Does "enticement" (Section 361) require deceitful inducement OR does mere persuasion suffice?

**Holding**: Supreme Court held **mere persuasion = enticement**. Deceitful inducement NOT required. Accused convicted under Section 363.

**Principle**:
- **"Enticement"** = inducing, alluring, persuading (by any means, even truthful persuasion)
- **No Deceit Required**: Even if accused honestly tells minor "Leave home and come with me," it is enticement if minor leaves
- **Minor's Consent Irrelevant**: Minor's willingness does not negate kidnapping if guardian did not consent

**Application**: Elopement cases (boyfriend persuades girlfriend under 18 to leave home) = Kidnapping from lawful guardianship, EVEN if girl goes willingly.

---

### 2. **State of Haryana v. Raja Ram (1973)**

**Facts**: Accused married girl who was allegedly under 18 years. Prosecution charged kidnapping. Defense argued marriage valid under personal law (Hindu Marriage Act allows marriage at 16 years for girls at that time - now amended to 18 years).

**Issue**: Does marriage to minor girl (under 18) negate kidnapping charge?

**Holding**: Supreme Court held marriage does NOT negate kidnapping if girl was under 18 years at time of taking from lawful guardianship.

**Principle**:
- **Criminal Law ≠ Personal Law**: IPC Section 361 protects minors regardless of marriage validity under personal law
- **Age 18 Years**: For females, age 18 is threshold for kidnapping (even if personal law allows marriage earlier)
- **Taking Precedes Marriage**: Kidnapping occurs at time of taking (before marriage); subsequent marriage is separate event

**Application**: Man takes girl under 18 from parents and marries her → Kidnapping charge stands, EVEN if marriage is valid under personal law.

---

### 3. **Sunil Batra v. Delhi Administration (1979)**

**Facts**: Accused kidnapped businessman for ransom, demanding ₹10 lakh. Kept victim confined, threatened to kill if ransom not paid.

**Issue**: Appropriate punishment for kidnapping for ransom under Section 364A.

**Holding**: Supreme Court upheld death penalty conviction, citing economic motive + threat to life as aggravating factors.

**Principle**:
- **Aggravated Kidnapping**: Ransom motive makes kidnapping heinous (economic crime + endangerment of life)
- **Deterrent Punishment**: Death penalty or life imprisonment justified for Section 364A to deter organized crime
- **Collective Conscience**: Kidnapping for ransom shocks society's collective conscience → Warrants severe punishment

## Compliance Checklist: Investigation & Prosecution

### For Investigating Officer

**Immediate Actions**:
- [ ] Record FIR under Section 363/360 (cognizable offense - arrest without warrant allowed)
- [ ] Interview complainant (lawful guardian): Establish guardian-minor relationship, age of minor, circumstances of taking
- [ ] Issue lookout notice (if kidnapping from India - prevent border crossing)
- [ ] Coordinate with border agencies (if kidnapping from India suspected)

**Age Verification**:
- [ ] Obtain birth certificate, school records (establish minor's age)
- [ ] Medical examination (bone ossification test if birth certificate unavailable)
- [ ] Affidavit from parents (stating minor's date of birth)

**Consent Determination**:
- [ ] Interview minor (if recovered): Did minor go willingly? Was there persuasion/enticement?
- [ ] Interview lawful guardian: Did guardian consent to minor's departure?
- [ ] **Key Point**: Even if minor went willingly, offense complete if guardian did not consent

**Accused's Intent**:
- [ ] If aggravated form suspected (murder, ransom, slavery, begging): Gather evidence of intent (ransom demands, confinement location, prior statements)

---

### For Public Prosecutor

**Proving Elements**:
- [ ] **Age**: Prove minor was under threshold age (16 for males, 18 for females) at time of kidnapping (birth certificate, medical evidence)
- [ ] **Lawful Guardian**: Prove complainant was lawful guardian (parent, adoptive parent, court order)
- [ ] **Taking/Enticement**: Prove accused actively took OR persuaded minor to leave (eyewitness, minor's testimony)
- [ ] **Without Consent**: Prove guardian did not consent (guardian's testimony)

**Anticipate Defenses**:
- [ ] **Good Faith Belief** (Section 361 Exception): Accused believed he had lawful custody (e.g., divorced father) → Prove no such belief OR belief not in good faith
- [ ] **Age Doubt**: Accused argues minor was above threshold age → Prove age through birth certificate, school records, medical test
- [ ] **Consent**: Accused argues guardian consented → Prove no consent through guardian's testimony

---

### For Defense Counsel

**Challenge Age Determination**:
- [ ] Obtain independent medical examination (bone ossification test inconclusive for ages 16-18)
- [ ] Challenge birth certificate authenticity (if discrepancies in records)
- [ ] Argue benefit of doubt if age uncertain (e.g., medical test shows 17-18 years, certificate says 17)

**Plead Section 361 Exception**:
- [ ] Prove accused had good faith belief he was entitled to lawful custody (e.g., divorced father without custody rights believed he had rights)
- [ ] Show accused's conduct consistent with parental care (not criminal intent)

**Consent Defense**:
- [ ] Prove lawful guardian actually consented (written consent, witness testimony)
- [ ] Challenge complainant's status as lawful guardian (e.g., relative claiming guardianship without legal basis)

**Minor's Will** (Limited Relevance):
- [ ] Argue minor's willingness + mature judgment (mitigating factor for sentencing, NOT defense to charge)
- [ ] Humanitarian grounds for leniency (if case involves elopement/marriage, not exploitation)

## IPC to BNS Transition

### Section Mapping

| IPC Section | BNS Section | Change |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| IPC 359 | BNS 87 | Definition unchanged (two types: from India, from lawful guardianship) |
| IPC 360 | BNS 88 | Kidnapping from India - punishment unchanged (7 years + fine) |
| IPC 361 | BNS 87 | Kidnapping from lawful guardianship - age thresholds unchanged (male <16, female <18) |
| IPC 363 | BNS 89 | Punishment for kidnapping - unchanged (7 years + fine) |
| IPC 364 | BNS 90 | Kidnapping for murder - punishment unchanged (death OR life + fine) |
| IPC 364A | BNS 91 | Kidnapping for ransom - punishment unchanged (death OR life + fine) |
| IPC 363A | BNS 92 | Kidnapping for begging - punishment unchanged (10 years + fine) |

### Effective Date: July 1, 2024

**Offenses Before July 1, 2024**: Prosecuted under **IPC 359-369**
**Offenses On/After July 1, 2024**: Prosecuted under **BNS 87-95**
