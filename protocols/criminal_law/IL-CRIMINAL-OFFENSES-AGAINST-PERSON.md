## Purpose
This protocol provides comprehensive guidance on offenses against the human body under the Indian Penal Code 1860, covering assault, hurt, grievous hurt, culpable homicide, murder, kidnapping, abduction, wrongful confinement, and related offenses with punishment provisions, key precedents, and defenses.

## Core Framework

### 1. ASSAULT & BATTERY (Section 351-358)

#### 1.1 Assault (Section 351)
```
DEFINITION:
Whoever makes any gesture or preparation intending or knowing it likely to cause
apprehension that he is about to use criminal force to that person

KEY ELEMENTS:
1. Gesture or preparation (raising fist, brandishing weapon, threatening posture)
2. Intention or knowledge (to cause apprehension of force)
3. No actual force applied (threat only, not touching)

EXAMPLES:
✓ A raises fist toward B (assault, even if doesn't strike)
✓ A points gun at B (assault, even if not loaded)
✓ A approaches B with knife in threatening manner (assault)

PUNISHMENT (Section 352):
Imprisonment up to 2 months OR Fine up to ₹200 OR Both

COGNIZABLE: No (police cannot arrest without warrant)
BAILABLE: Yes
COMPOUNDABLE: Yes (victim can settle)
```

#### 1.2 Criminal Force (Section 350)
```
DEFINITION:
Intentionally using force to any person without that person's consent, to cause:
- Injury, fear, or annoyance
- Commission of offense
- Deter from legal right

ELEMENTS:
1. Use of force (actual physical contact)
2. Intentionally
3. Without consent
4. To achieve one of specified purposes

EXAMPLES:
✓ Pushing someone (to injure or annoy)
✓ Snatching object from person's hand
✓ Blocking someone's path (preventing movement)

PUNISHMENT (Section 352):
Imprisonment up to 3 months OR Fine up to ₹500 OR Both

If provocation: Imprisonment 1 month OR Fine ₹200 OR Both
```

### 2. HURT & GRIEVOUS HURT (Section 319-338)

#### 2.1 Simple Hurt (Section 319, 323)
```
DEFINITION (Section 319):
Causing bodily pain, disease, or infirmity to any person

EXAMPLES:
✓ Slapping causing redness/pain
✓ Kicking causing bruises
✓ Pushing causing minor injuries
✓ Causing temporary illness (food poisoning if intentional)

PUNISHMENT (Section 323 - Voluntarily Causing Hurt):
Imprisonment up to 1 year OR Fine up to ₹1,000 OR Both

AGGRAVATED FORMS:
Section 324 (Hurt by dangerous weapon): Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine
Section 325 (Voluntarily causing grievous hurt): Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes (if hurt by dangerous weapon/grievous hurt)
BAILABLE: Yes (simple hurt), No (grievous hurt)
COMPOUNDABLE: Yes (with permission of court)
```

#### 2.2 Grievous Hurt (Section 320)
```
DEFINITION - 8 TYPES OF GRIEVOUS HURT:

1. EMASCULATION: Permanent deprivation of sexual organ power

2. PERMANENT LOSS OF SIGHT (either eye)

3. PERMANENT LOSS OF HEARING (either ear)

4. PRIVATION OF ANY MEMBER OR JOINT:
   - Loss of hand, foot, finger, toe
   - Permanent stiffness/immobility of joint

5. DESTRUCTION/PERMANENT IMPAIRING OF POWERS:
   - Of any member or joint (paralysis of limb)

6. PERMANENT DISFIGURATION OF HEAD OR FACE:
   - Scars, burns, deformities (visible, permanent)

7. FRACTURE OR DISLOCATION OF BONE OR TOOTH:
   - Any bone broken (arm, leg, rib, skull)
   - Tooth knocked out

8. ANY HURT ENDANGERING LIFE:
   - Internal injuries (ruptured organs)
   - Severe bleeding, shock

PUNISHMENT (Section 325):
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine

If by dangerous weapon/means (Section 326):
Imprisonment 10 years to LIFE + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No
COMPOUNDABLE: Yes (with court permission)
```

#### 2.3 Key Case Example
```
CASE: Virsa Singh v. State of Punjab (1958) - Supreme Court

FACTS:
Accused struck victim once with spear (on abdomen)
Victim died from internal injuries (intestines perforated)

ISSUE:
Whether single blow can constitute murder (Section 302) or only culpable homicide?

HELD:
If injury is:
1. Intentionally inflicted
2. Sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death
→ Intention to cause death presumed → MURDER (Section 302)

If injury not sufficient to cause death ordinarily:
→ Check actual intention (if intended death → Murder, else → Culpable homicide not amounting to murder)

In this case: Spear thrust in abdomen = sufficient to cause death ordinarily
→ MURDER (even if single blow, no prior enmity)

PUNISHMENT: Life imprisonment (under Section 302)
```

### 3. CULPABLE HOMICIDE & MURDER (Section 299-304)

#### 3.1 Culpable Homicide (Section 299)
```
DEFINITION:
Act by which death of human being is caused with:
1. Intention of causing death, OR
2. Intention of causing bodily injury likely to cause death, OR
3. Knowledge that act is likely to cause death

GENUS: Culpable homicide is GENUS (broad category)
SPECIES: Murder is SPECIES (specific, aggravated form of culpable homicide)

PRINCIPLE:
All murder is culpable homicide
But all culpable homicide is NOT murder

PUNISHMENT (Section 304 - Culpable Homicide Not Amounting to Murder):
Part 1 (intention/knowledge likely to cause death): Life or imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine
Part 2 (knowledge but no intention): Imprisonment up to 10 years OR Fine OR Both
```

#### 3.2 Murder (Section 300)
```
DEFINITION:
Culpable homicide is MURDER if:

1. INTENTION TO CAUSE DEATH (straightforward)
   Example: A shoots B in head (clear intention)

2. INTENTION TO CAUSE BODILY INJURY SUFFICIENT IN ORDINARY COURSE TO CAUSE DEATH
   Example: A stabs B in heart with knife (such injury ordinarily causes death)

3. INTENTION TO CAUSE BODILY INJURY + KNOWLEDGE THAT DEATH LIKELY
   Example: A strikes B (who has weak heart) knowing B may die

4. KNOWLEDGE THAT ACT IS SO IMMINENTLY DANGEROUS THAT IT MUST IN ALL PROBABILITY CAUSE DEATH
   Example: A sets fire to crowded theatre (knows people will die)
   Committed without excuse (no justification)

PUNISHMENT (Section 302):
Death penalty OR Life imprisonment + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No
COMPOUNDABLE: No
TRIABLE BY: Sessions Court (not Magistrate)
```

#### 3.3 Exceptions to Murder (reduce to Culpable Homicide)
```
SECTION 300 - FIVE EXCEPTIONS (Murder → Culpable Homicide):

EXCEPTION 1: GRAVE & SUDDEN PROVOCATION
If accused, deprived of self-control by sudden provocation, causes death

CONDITIONS:
- Provocation must be grave (serious, not trivial insult)
- Sudden (no time for passion to cool)
- Not self-induced (victim didn't provoke first)

Example: A finds wife in bed with B, immediately kills B (may reduce to culpable homicide)
But: If A waits 2 hours, plans, then kills → Murder (passion cooled, premeditated)

EXCEPTION 2: PRIVATE DEFENSE EXCEEDING POWER
Death caused in good faith exercise of right of private defense, but exceeded lawful bounds

Example: A attacks B with fist, B shoots A dead (excessive force, not proportionate)
→ Culpable homicide (not murder, as some right of defense existed)

EXCEPTION 3: PUBLIC SERVANT EXCEEDING LAWFUL POWERS
Public servant causing death while acting for public good, in good faith, but exceeded powers

Example: Police firing on rioting mob (to disperse), kills person
If good faith + public order → Culpable homicide (not murder)

EXCEPTION 4: SUDDEN FIGHT (WITHOUT PREMEDITATION)
Death in heat of passion upon sudden fight, without premeditation or undue advantage

Conditions:
- Sudden quarrel (not planned)
- Heat of passion (no cooling period)
- No undue advantage taken
- No acting in cruel/unusual manner

Example: Bar brawl, A and B fight, A kills B with beer bottle
If no premeditation, no weapon brought beforehand → Culpable homicide

EXCEPTION 5: CONSENT OF PERSON KILLED (if ≥18 years)
Death caused with consent of person killed, who is ≥18 years, sound mind

Example: Euthanasia (mercy killing with patient's consent)
Still CRIME (culpable homicide), but NOT murder

NOTE: Consent in suicide pact = Murder (Section 309 - abetment of suicide)
```

### 4. KIDNAPPING & ABDUCTION (Section 359-369)

#### 4.1 Kidnapping from India (Section 360)
```
DEFINITION:
Conveying any person beyond limits of India without consent of:
- That person, OR
- Person legally authorized to consent on their behalf

ELEMENTS:
1. Person taken out of India
2. Without consent (own or guardian's)

PUNISHMENT (Section 363):
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No
```

#### 4.2 Kidnapping from Lawful Guardianship (Section 361)
```
DEFINITION:
Taking or enticing any person:
- If MALE: Under 16 years
- If FEMALE: Under 18 years
Out of keeping of lawful guardian, WITHOUT CONSENT of guardian

KEY POINTS:
- Consent of child irrelevant (even if child agrees)
- Consent of lawful guardian required
- Age: Male <16, Female <18

EXAMPLES:
✓ A takes 15-year-old boy from school without parents' consent (kidnapping)
✓ A elopes with 17-year-old girl (even if girl consents, parents didn't) - kidnapping
✓ A entices 14-year-old to leave home (kidnapping from guardianship)

NOT KIDNAPPING:
✗ Taking 19-year-old woman (adult, even without parent consent - may be abduction if force/fraud, not kidnapping)
✗ Father taking own child (lawful guardian, unless court custody order violated)

PUNISHMENT (Section 363):
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine

AGGRAVATED FORMS:
Section 364: Kidnapping for murder → Imprisonment up to LIFE + Fine
Section 364A: Kidnapping for ransom → Death OR Life + Fine
Section 367: Kidnapping with intent to subject person to grievous hurt/slavery → Imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine
```

#### 4.3 Abduction (Section 362)
```
DEFINITION:
Compelling any person by:
- Force, OR
- Inducement (fraud, deception, promise)
To go from any place

DIFFERENCE FROM KIDNAPPING:
- Kidnapping: Age limit (male <16, female <18), consent of guardian issue
- Abduction: NO age limit (even adults), compulsion by force or inducement

ELEMENTS:
1. Compulsion (force OR inducement)
2. Person made to go from place
3. No age bar

EXAMPLES:
✓ A forces 25-year-old woman into car, drives away (abduction)
✓ A deceives 30-year-old man (false job promise), takes to remote location (abduction)

ABDUCTION ALONE NOT PUNISHABLE:
Abduction + ulterior motive = Punishable

Section 363: Abduction from India or lawful guardianship → 7 years
Section 365: Abduction with intent to murder/wrongfully confine → 7 years
Section 366: Abduction to compel marriage → 10 years
Section 366A: Procuration of minor girl (for illicit intercourse) → 10 years

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No (if with ulterior motive)
```

### 5. WRONGFUL CONFINEMENT & RESTRAINT (Section 339-348)

#### 5.1 Wrongful Restraint (Section 339, 341)
```
DEFINITION:
Voluntarily obstructing any person so as to prevent that person from proceeding
in any direction in which that person has right to proceed

EXAMPLES:
✓ Blocking doorway (preventing exit)
✓ Surrounding person (preventing movement)
✓ Parking vehicle blocking another's car (preventing driving away)

PUNISHMENT (Section 341):
Imprisonment up to 1 month OR Fine ₹500 OR Both

COGNIZABLE: No
BAILABLE: Yes
COMPOUNDABLE: Yes
```

#### 5.2 Wrongful Confinement (Section 340, 342)
```
DEFINITION:
Wrongful restraint that prevents person from proceeding beyond certain circumscribing limits

DIFFERENCE FROM WRONGFUL RESTRAINT:
- Restraint: Preventing from proceeding in any direction
- Confinement: Completely restricting within limits (cannot leave at all)

EXAMPLES:
✓ Locking person in room
✓ Surrounding person in circle (cannot escape)
✓ Tying person to chair

PUNISHMENT (Section 342):
Imprisonment up to 1 year OR Fine ₹1,000 OR Both

AGGRAVATED FORMS:
Section 343 (Confinement for 3+ days): Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine OR Both
Section 344 (Confinement for 10+ days): Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine
Section 346 (Confinement in secret): Imprisonment up to 2 years + Fine
Section 348 (Confinement for extorting property): Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes (if for 3+ days)
BAILABLE: Yes (simple), No (aggravated)
COMPOUNDABLE: Yes (with court permission)
```

### 6. CRIMINAL INTIMIDATION & INSULT (Section 503-510)

#### 6.1 Criminal Intimidation (Section 503, 506)
```
DEFINITION (Section 503):
Threatening any person with injury to:
- Person, reputation, or property of that person or someone he is interested in
To cause alarm, OR
To cause that person to do/omit any act

ELEMENTS:
1. Threat (to person, reputation, or property)
2. Intent to cause alarm OR compel action/omission

EXAMPLES:
✓ "I will kill you if you testify" (intimidation to omit act)
✓ "I will damage your shop if you don't pay" (intimidation, extortion)
✓ "I will publish your secret if you don't resign" (intimidation)

PUNISHMENT (Section 506):
PART 1 (simple intimidation): Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine OR Both
PART 2 (threat of death/grievous hurt): Imprisonment up to 7 years OR Fine OR Both

COGNIZABLE: Yes (if threat of death/GH)
BAILABLE: Yes (simple), No (threat of death/GH)
COMPOUNDABLE: No
```

#### 6.2 Insult (Section 504)
```
DEFINITION:
Intentionally insulting, and thereby giving provocation to any person, intending or
knowing it likely that such provocation will cause that person to:
- Break public peace, OR
- Commit offense

ELEMENTS:
1. Intentional insult
2. Provocation given
3. Intent/knowledge that victim may commit offense or breach peace

PUNISHMENT:
Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine OR Both

COGNIZABLE: No
BAILABLE: Yes
COMPOUNDABLE: Yes
```

## Defenses & Exceptions

### 1. PRIVATE DEFENSE (Section 96-106)
```
RIGHT OF PRIVATE DEFENSE OF BODY (Section 97, 100):
Every person has right to defend own body and body of any other person against:
- Offenses affecting human body (assault, hurt, kidnapping, wrongful confinement, rape)

EXTENDS TO CAUSING DEATH (Section 100):
If offense is:
- Assault causing reasonable apprehension of death or grievous hurt
- Assault with intention of committing rape
- Assault to gratify unnatural lust
- Assault to kidnap/abduct
- Acid attack

CONDITIONS:
1. Imminent danger (no time to seek public authority help)
2. Reasonable apprehension (not imaginary)
3. No more harm than necessary (proportionate force)
4. Good faith (genuine belief in danger)

LIMITATIONS (Section 99):
✗ Cannot claim if time to seek public authority protection
✗ Cannot claim if inflicted more harm than necessary
✗ Cannot claim against public servants acting in good faith (even if illegally)

CASE EXAMPLE:
A attacks B with knife (life threat)
B shoots A dead in self-defense
If B had no means to escape, genuine fear of death → Defense valid
If B could have fled, or disabled A without killing → Defense may not cover death, only hurt
```

### 2. CONSENT (Section 87-93)
```
ACT NOT OFFENSE IF DONE WITH CONSENT:

Section 87: Consent to suffer harm (sports injury, medical treatment)
Section 88: Act for benefit of person (surgery with consent)
Section 89: Guardian's consent (minor's medical treatment)
Section 90: Consent NOT valid if: Fear, misconception, intoxication, unsound mind, under 12 years

LIMITATIONS:
✗ Cannot consent to death (murder, even with consent)
✗ Cannot consent to grievous hurt (unless good faith for benefit, e.g., surgery)
✓ Can consent to simple hurt (boxing match, sports)
```

## Landmark Cases

### 1. **K.M. Nanavati v. State of Maharashtra (1962) - Supreme Court**
**Facts**: Naval officer found wife with lover, shot lover dead, claimed grave & sudden provocation.

**Held**: Provocation must be sudden (no time for passion to cool). Here, 2 hours gap between discovery and killing → Murder (not culpable homicide). Exception 1 not applicable.

**Impact**: Tightened "grave & sudden provocation" test. Planning after provocation = Murder.

---

### 2. **Virsa Singh v. State of Punjab (1958) - Supreme Court**
**Facts**: Single spear blow to abdomen causing death.

**Held**: If injury sufficient in ordinary course to cause death + intentionally inflicted → Intention to cause death presumed → MURDER (Section 302).

**Impact**: Established "sufficient in ordinary course of nature" test for murder.

## Conclusion

Offenses against person range from minor assault (₹200 fine) to murder (death penalty). Key distinctions: Assault vs hurt (threat vs actual force), Simple hurt vs grievous hurt (8 types), Culpable homicide vs murder (exceptions reduce murder to CH), Kidnapping vs abduction (age limit vs compulsion), Wrongful restraint vs confinement (obstruction vs complete restriction). Understanding these gradations, defenses (private defense, consent), and landmark precedents is critical for criminal law practice.
