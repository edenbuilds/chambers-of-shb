## Overview

**Robbery** is theft aggravated by the use of violence or putting a person in fear of instant death, hurt, or wrongful restraint, either during the commission of theft or to facilitate the retention of stolen property or to aid in escape.

**Dacoity** is robbery committed by five or more persons acting in concert.

These are among the most serious property offences in Indian criminal law because they combine the elements of property violation with violence or threat of violence against persons.

### Key Characteristics
1. **Robbery = Theft + Violence/Fear**: Robbery is an aggravated form of theft where violence or fear of instant harm is used
2. **Dacoity = Collective Robbery**: Dacoity requires five or more persons acting together
3. **Severe Punishment**: Both offences carry mandatory rigorous imprisonment, with minimum sentences
4. **Instant Harm Requirement**: The fear must be of **instant** death, hurt, or restraint (not future harm)
5. **Non-Bailable**: Both robbery and dacoity are non-bailable, cognizable offences

## Definition of Robbery (IPC Section 390 / BNS Section 309)

### Full Legal Definition

**IPC Section 390**: In all robbery there is either **theft** or **extortion**.

#### Two Types of Robbery

**Type 1: Robbery as Aggravated Theft**

**When theft is robbery**: Theft is robbery if, in order to the committing of the theft, or in committing the theft, or in carrying away or attempting to carry away property obtained by the theft, the offender, for that end:
- Voluntarily causes or attempts to cause to any person **death**, **hurt**, or **wrongful restraint**, OR
- Puts or attempts to put any person in **fear of instant death, hurt, or wrongful restraint**

**Type 2: Robbery as Aggravated Extortion**

**When extortion is robbery**: Extortion is robbery if the offender, at the time of committing the extortion:
- Is in the presence of the person put in fear, AND
- Commits the extortion by putting that person in fear of **instant death, instant hurt, or instant wrongful restraint** to that person or some other person, AND
- Thereby induces the person so put in fear then and there to deliver up the thing extorted

### Essential Ingredients of Robbery

#### For Robbery by Theft (Most Common Type)

1. **There must be theft** (all seven ingredients of IPC Section 378 must be present)
2. **Violence or fear must be used** in one of the following three stages:
   - **Before the theft**: In order to commit the theft
   - **During the theft**: While committing the theft
   - **After the theft**: In carrying away or attempting to carry away the stolen property
3. **The violence or fear must be of instant harm**: Death, hurt, or wrongful restraint must be instant, not future
4. **Purpose of violence/fear**: To facilitate the theft, retention of property, or escape

#### For Robbery by Extortion (Rare Type)

1. **There must be extortion** (IPC Section 383: intentionally putting person in fear to induce delivery of property)
2. **Offender must be in presence** of the victim at the time of committing extortion
3. **Fear must be of instant harm**: Instant death, instant hurt, or instant wrongful restraint
4. **Delivery induced by fear**: Victim delivers property then and there due to fear of instant harm

### Explanation 1 to Section 390: Use of Force
The offender is said to use force to any person if he uses force to that person in order to perpetrate the robbery, and if he uses force to any person in order to take or attempt to take away property, whether or not that person is in possession of the property.

**Example**: A attacks B and causes hurt to B in order to snatch B's bag. A commits robbery even though B may not have been holding the bag at that exact moment (bag was on a table nearby).

## Definition of Dacoity (IPC Section 391 / BNS Section 310)

### Full Legal Definition

**IPC Section 391**: When **five or more persons** conjointly commit or attempt to commit a **robbery**, or where the whole number of persons conjointly committing or attempting to commit a robbery, and persons present and aiding such commission or attempt, amount to five or more, every person so committing, attempting, or aiding, is said to commit **dacoity**.

### Essential Ingredients of Dacoity

1. **Five or more persons**: Minimum of five persons required
2. **Acting conjointly**: Must act in concert or common intention
3. **Committing or attempting robbery**: The act must constitute robbery (theft + violence/fear of instant harm)
4. **Includes aiders**: Persons present and aiding the commission also count toward the total of five

### Key Distinction: Robbery vs. Dacoity

| **Element** | **Robbery** | **Dacoity** |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Number of Offenders** | One or more (no minimum) | Minimum **five** persons |
| **Nature** | Individual or group offence | Collective offence only |
| **Punishment** | Minimum 1 year, up to 10 years RI | Minimum **7 years** RI, may extend to life |
| **Gravity** | Serious offence | More serious (collective violence) |

## Punishment for Robbery

### Section 392 IPC / Section 311 BNS: Simple Robbery
**Punishment**:
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Minimum **1 year**, may extend to **10 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Magistrate of First Class

**Example**: A snatches B's purse from her hand with force. A commits robbery under Section 392.

### Section 393 IPC / Section 312 BNS: Attempt to Commit Robbery
**Punishment**:
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Up to **7 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Magistrate of First Class

**Example**: A attempts to snatch B's chain but B resists and A fails to take the chain. A has attempted robbery.

### Section 394 IPC / Section 313 BNS: Voluntarily Causing Hurt in Committing Robbery
Whoever, in committing robbery, **voluntarily causes hurt**, shall be punished with:
- **Imprisonment for life**, OR
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Up to **10 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Court of Session

**Example**: A snatches B's wallet and in the process punches B in the face, causing injury. A commits robbery with hurt under Section 394.

**Note**: "Hurt" means bodily pain, disease, or infirmity (IPC Section 319). If grievous hurt is caused, Section 397 applies.

## Punishment for Dacoity

### Section 395 IPC / Section 314 BNS: Simple Dacoity
**Punishment**:
- **Imprisonment for life**, OR
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Minimum **7 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Court of Session

**Example**: A gang of six persons robs a bank at gunpoint. All six commit dacoity under Section 395.

### Section 396 IPC / Section 315 BNS: Dacoity with Murder
If any one of **five or more persons**, who are conjointly committing dacoity, **commits murder** in so committing dacoity, **every one of those persons** shall be punished with:
- **Death**, OR
- **Imprisonment for life**, OR
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Up to **10 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Court of Session

**Principle of Joint Liability**: Even if only one member of the gang commits murder, all members of the dacoity gang are liable for murder if the murder was committed in the course of dacoity.

**Example**: A gang of seven persons commits dacoity in a house. One member shoots and kills the owner during the robbery. All seven are liable for dacoity with murder under Section 396, even if only one fired the shot.

**Landmark Case**: *Mohan Singh v. State of Punjab* (1963) - Supreme Court held that all members of a dacoity gang are constructively liable for murder committed by any member during dacoity.

### Section 397 IPC / Section 316 BNS: Robbery or Dacoity with Attempt to Cause Death or Grievous Hurt
If at the time of committing robbery or dacoity, the offender uses any **deadly weapon**, or causes **grievous hurt** to any person, or **attempts to cause death or grievous hurt**, shall be punished with:
- **Imprisonment for life**, OR
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Minimum **7 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Court of Session

**Example**: A gang robs a jewelry shop while armed with guns and knives, and during the robbery, one robber slashes a customer with a knife causing grievous hurt. All are liable under Section 397.

### Section 398 IPC / Section 317 BNS: Attempt to Commit Robbery or Dacoity When Armed with Deadly Weapon
If at the time of **attempting to commit robbery or dacoity**, the offender is **armed with any deadly weapon**, the imprisonment shall be:
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Minimum **7 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Court of Session

**Example**: A gang of five persons, armed with pistols, enters a bank intending to commit dacoity but is apprehended by police before taking any property. They are liable under Section 398 for armed attempt.

## Preparatory Offences Related to Dacoity

### Section 399 IPC / Section 318 BNS: Making Preparation to Commit Dacoity
Whoever makes any preparation for committing dacoity shall be punished with:
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Up to **10 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Magistrate of First Class

**Example**: A group collects weapons, conducts surveillance of a target house, and plans the dacoity. Even if the dacoity is not attempted, preparation alone is punishable.

**Rationale**: Dacoity is so serious that even preparatory acts are criminalized to prevent the commission of the substantive offence.

### Section 400 IPC / Section 319 BNS: Punishment for Belonging to Gang of Dacoits
Whoever, at any time after the passing of this Act, shall belong to a **gang of persons associated for the purpose of habitually committing dacoity**, shall be punished with:
- **Imprisonment for life**, OR
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Up to **10 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Court of Session

**Essential Ingredients**:
1. Gang of persons (association of persons)
2. Purpose: Habitually committing dacoity (regular criminal activity)
3. Accused belongs to such gang

**Example**: A is a member of a notorious gang that has committed 20 dacoities in the past year. Even if A did not participate in every dacoity, his membership in the gang is an offence under Section 400.

### Section 401 IPC / Section 320 BNS: Punishment for Belonging to Gang of Thieves
Whoever, at any time after the passing of this Act, shall belong to any **wandering or other gang of persons associated for the purpose of habitually committing theft or robbery**, and not being a gang of thugs or dacoits, shall be punished with:
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Up to **7 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Magistrate of First Class

**Distinction from Section 400**:
- Section 400: Gang of dacoits (five or more committing robbery)
- Section 401: Gang of thieves or robbers (not necessarily five persons)

### Section 402 IPC / Section 321 BNS: Assembling for Purpose of Committing Dacoity
Whoever, at any time after the passing of this Act, shall be one of **five or more persons assembled** for the purpose of committing dacoity, shall be punished with:
- **Rigorous Imprisonment**: Up to **7 years**, AND
- **Fine**

**Nature**: Cognizable, Non-bailable, Triable by Magistrate of First Class

**Essential Ingredients**:
1. Five or more persons
2. Assembled (gathered together)
3. Purpose: To commit dacoity (specific intent)

**Example**: Five persons assemble at a hideout to plan and execute a dacoity. They are caught by police before leaving for the target. All five are liable under Section 402.

**Distinction from Section 399**:
- Section 399: Making preparation (individual act, any number of persons)
- Section 402: Assembling (collective act, minimum five persons)

## Distinction Between Theft, Extortion, and Robbery

| **Element** | **Theft (378)** | **Extortion (383)** | **Robbery (390)** |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Taking** | Dishonestly taking movable property | Intentionally putting in fear to induce delivery | Theft or extortion + violence/fear of instant harm |
| **Consent** | Without consent | With consent (obtained by fear) | Without consent (theft) OR consent by fear of instant harm (extortion) |
| **Violence/Fear** | No violence or fear | Fear of injury (not necessarily instant) | Violence OR fear of **instant** death/hurt/restraint |
| **Delivery** | Offender takes property | Victim delivers property | Offender takes (theft-robbery) OR victim delivers under instant fear (extortion-robbery) |
| **Punishment** | Up to 3 years (simple) | Up to 3 years (simple) | Minimum 1 year, up to 10 years (RI mandatory) |

### Key Distinction: Extortion vs. Robbery

**Extortion becomes Robbery when**:
1. Offender is in the presence of the victim
2. Fear is of **instant** harm (not future harm)
3. Victim delivers property then and there

**Example 1 (Extortion, not Robbery)**: A sends a threatening letter to B demanding Rs. 10 lakh, threatening to kill B if he does not pay within 7 days. B pays. This is extortion (fear of future harm, offender not present at delivery).

**Example 2 (Robbery by Extortion)**: A points a gun at B and says "Give me your wallet now or I will shoot you." B hands over the wallet. This is robbery (fear of instant death, offender present, immediate delivery).

## Landmark Cases on Robbery and Dacoity

### 1. **Mohan Singh v. State of Punjab** (1963) - Dacoity with Murder
**AIR 1963 SC 174**

**Facts**: A gang of dacoits robbed a house. During the robbery, one member of the gang shot and killed the homeowner. Other members claimed they did not intend murder.

**Held**: The Supreme Court held that under **Section 396 IPC**, if any one member of a dacoity gang commits murder during the commission of dacoity, **all members of the gang are liable for murder**, even if they did not personally commit the act or intend to cause death.

**Principle**: **Constructive liability** for dacoity with murder. Common intention to commit dacoity makes all members vicariously liable for murder committed by any member in furtherance of the common intention.

**Reasoning**: Section 396 is based on the principle that when persons act conjointly to commit dacoity (a violent crime), they assume collective responsibility for all acts done in furtherance of that crime, including murder.

### 2. **Girja Prasad v. State of M.P.** (2007) - Robbery: Instant vs. Future Fear
**AIR 2007 SC 3088**

**Facts**: The accused threatened the victim with consequences if he did not hand over money. The victim, fearing harm, gave money to the accused.

**Held**: For an offence to be robbery (as opposed to extortion), the fear must be of **instant death, hurt, or wrongful restraint**. If the threat is of future harm, it is extortion, not robbery.

**Principle**: The critical distinction between robbery and extortion is the **immediacy of the threat**. Robbery requires instant fear; extortion may involve fear of future harm.

**Test**: Was the victim in fear of harm then and there (instant)? If yes, robbery. Was the fear of harm at some future time? If yes, extortion.

### 3. **Lakshmi Singh v. State of Bihar** (1976) - Robbery: Force Before, During, or After
**AIR 1976 SC 2263**

**Facts**: The accused snatched a purse from the victim and when the victim tried to resist, the accused pushed her, causing her to fall.

**Held**: Robbery can be committed by using force or putting in fear **before, during, or after** the theft. Even if force is used after snatching the property to facilitate escape or retention, it constitutes robbery.

**Principle**: The violence or fear element in robbery can occur at any of three stages:
1. Before: To facilitate the commission of theft
2. During: While committing the theft
3. After: To carry away or retain the stolen property or to escape

### 4. **Shaik Muneer v. State of A.P.** (2007) - Dacoity: Five Persons Requirement
**2007 Cri LJ 3725**

**Facts**: Four persons committed a robbery. The prosecution attempted to charge them with dacoity.

**Held**: Dacoity requires a **minimum of five persons** acting conjointly to commit robbery. If fewer than five persons are involved, the offence is robbery, not dacoity, regardless of the severity of violence.

**Principle**: The numerical requirement of five persons for dacoity is mandatory and cannot be waived. Courts cannot convict for dacoity if the number of offenders is less than five.

**Note**: However, if some offenders are not apprehended or identified, and evidence shows five or more participated, dacoity can still be established.

### 5. **Hari Kishan v. State of Haryana** (1975) - Robbery: Wrongful Restraint Sufficient
**AIR 1975 SC 1491**

**Facts**: The accused blocked the victim's path and demanded money, threatening to harm him if he tried to leave. The victim gave money.

**Held**: Robbery can be committed not only by causing hurt or death but also by **wrongful restraint**. If a person is put in fear of instant wrongful restraint (being prevented from moving) and induced to deliver property, it is robbery.

**Principle**: The fear element in robbery includes fear of instant death, hurt, **or wrongful restraint**. Physical violence is not always necessary; preventing freedom of movement is sufficient.

### 6. **Gurmukh Singh v. State of Haryana** (2009) - Attempt to Commit Robbery
**AIR 2009 SC 1471**

**Facts**: The accused surrounded a vehicle, attempted to snatch valuables, but were chased away before succeeding.

**Held**: For **attempt to commit robbery** (Section 393), it is sufficient if the accused took steps towards committing robbery and had the intention to commit robbery, even if the robbery was not completed due to intervention or resistance.

**Principle**: Attempt requires:
1. Intention to commit the offence
2. Some act done towards its commission (not mere preparation)
3. The act falls short of completion

### 7. **State of Maharashtra v. Daniel David** (1980) - Dacoity: Vicarious Liability
**AIR 1980 SC 990**

**Facts**: A gang of six persons committed dacoity. During the dacoity, one member caused grievous hurt to a victim. The other members claimed they did not participate in causing hurt.

**Held**: In dacoity, all members of the gang are **vicariously liable** for acts done by any member in furtherance of the common intention to commit dacoity. If grievous hurt is caused during dacoity, all members are liable under Section 397.

**Principle**: **Common intention** (Section 34 IPC) applies to dacoity. Each member is liable for the acts of other members done in furtherance of the dacoity.

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Simple Robbery (Snatching with Force)
**Scenario**: Rajesh is walking on a street when two men on a motorcycle approach him. One of them forcefully snatches Rajesh's mobile phone from his hand and they speed away.

**Analysis**:
1. ✅ Theft: Dishonest taking of movable property (mobile phone) without consent
2. ✅ Force used: Snatching with force constitutes violence
3. ✅ During theft: Force used while committing the theft
4. ✅ Instant harm: The act of snatching itself involves immediate force/violence
5. ❌ Not dacoity: Only two persons (less than five)

**Offence**: Robbery under Section 392 IPC / Section 311 BNS
**Punishment**: 1-10 years rigorous imprisonment + fine

### Example 2: Robbery with Hurt
**Scenario**: Three men enter a jewelry shop. One of them points a knife at the shopkeeper and demands jewelry. When the shopkeeper hesitates, another man punches the shopkeeper in the face, causing injury. They take jewelry worth Rs. 5 lakh and flee.

**Analysis**:
1. ✅ Theft: Taking jewelry without consent
2. ✅ Violence and fear: Knife threat (fear of instant hurt/death) + actual hurt (punch)
3. ✅ Hurt caused: Bodily pain/injury to shopkeeper
4. ❌ Not dacoity: Only three persons

**Offence**: Robbery with Voluntarily Causing Hurt under Section 394 IPC / Section 313 BNS
**Punishment**: Imprisonment for life OR up to 10 years rigorous imprisonment + fine

### Example 3: Dacoity (Five Persons)
**Scenario**: A gang of six persons, armed with sticks and knives, robs a bank. They threaten the staff and customers with weapons, tie them up, and take cash worth Rs. 20 lakh.

**Analysis**:
1. ✅ Robbery: Theft + violence/fear of instant harm (threats with weapons)
2. ✅ Five or more persons: Six persons involved
3. ✅ Acting conjointly: All acting with common intention
4. ✅ Armed: Sticks and knives (deadly weapons)

**Offence**: Dacoity under Section 395 IPC / Section 314 BNS
**Alternative charge**: Section 397 (Dacoity with deadly weapon)
**Punishment**: Imprisonment for life OR 7-10 years rigorous imprisonment + fine (Section 395)

### Example 4: Dacoity with Murder
**Scenario**: A gang of seven persons commits dacoity in a farmhouse. The owner tries to resist, and one gang member shoots and kills him. The others did not intend to kill anyone and did not fire any shots.

**Analysis**:
1. ✅ Dacoity: Five or more persons committing robbery
2. ✅ Murder committed: One member killed the owner during the dacoity
3. ✅ All liable: Section 396 imposes vicarious liability on all members

**Offence**: Dacoity with Murder under Section 396 IPC / Section 315 BNS (all seven members liable)
**Punishment**: Death OR imprisonment for life OR up to 10 years rigorous imprisonment + fine

**Principle**: All members of the dacoity gang are liable for murder committed by any one member during the commission of dacoity, even if they did not personally commit or intend the murder.

### Example 5: Extortion (Not Robbery - Future Threat)
**Scenario**: Anil sends an anonymous letter to Vikram demanding Rs. 10 lakh, threatening to kidnap Vikram's daughter if he does not pay within a week. Vikram pays the money through a bank transfer.

**Analysis**:
1. ✅ Fear induced: Vikram is in fear of harm to his daughter
2. ✅ Delivery of property: Vikram delivers money
3. ❌ Not instant fear: Threat is of future harm (within a week), not instant harm
4. ❌ Offender not present: Anonymous letter, offender not in presence during delivery

**Offence**: Extortion under Section 384 IPC / Section 308 BNS (NOT robbery)
**Punishment**: Up to 3 years imprisonment OR fine OR both

**Why not robbery**: Fear is of future harm, not instant harm, and offender is not in the victim's presence at the time of delivery.

### Example 6: Robbery by Extortion (Instant Fear, Presence)
**Scenario**: Four armed men stop Suresh's car on a highway. They point guns at him and say, "Give us all your cash and valuables right now or we will shoot you." Suresh, in fear of instant death, hands over Rs. 50,000 and his watch.

**Analysis**:
1. ✅ Extortion: Suresh delivers property due to fear
2. ✅ Offender in presence: Armed men are physically present
3. ✅ Fear of instant harm: Threat of instant death (guns pointed)
4. ✅ Immediate delivery: Suresh delivers then and there
5. ✅ Robbery by extortion: All conditions for robbery through extortion are met

**Offence**: Robbery under Section 392 IPC / Section 311 BNS
**Punishment**: 1-10 years rigorous imprisonment + fine

### Example 7: Attempt to Commit Dacoity When Armed
**Scenario**: A gang of eight persons, armed with pistols and knives, is caught by police while conducting surveillance of a bank. They have detailed plans, weapons, and getaway vehicles ready but have not yet entered the bank.

**Analysis**:
1. ✅ Five or more persons: Eight persons
2. ✅ Preparation/attempt: Surveillance, weapons, plans indicate attempt
3. ✅ Armed with deadly weapons: Pistols and knives
4. ✅ Intent to commit dacoity: Plans and preparation show intent
5. ❌ No actual dacoity committed: Caught before execution

**Offence**: Attempt to Commit Dacoity When Armed under Section 398 IPC / Section 317 BNS
**Alternative**: Making Preparation to Commit Dacoity (Section 399) or Assembling for Dacoity (Section 402)
**Punishment**: Minimum 7 years rigorous imprisonment + fine (Section 398)

## Defenses to Robbery and Dacoity

### 1. **Absence of Theft**
- If no theft occurred, there can be no robbery (robbery requires theft or extortion as base offence)
- If accused can show property was taken with consent or lawful authority, no robbery

### 2. **No Violence or Fear of Instant Harm**
- If no violence was used and no fear of instant harm was created, the offence may be theft or extortion, not robbery
- Fear must be of **instant** harm; fear of future harm does not constitute robbery

**Example**: If A threatens to harm B tomorrow unless B gives money today, and B gives money, it is extortion, not robbery (no instant fear).

### 3. **Self-Defense or Private Defense**
- If the accused used force in lawful exercise of the right of private defense (IPC Sections 96-106), no robbery
- The force must be proportionate and necessary for defense

**Example**: A attempts to snatch B's bag. B uses reasonable force to protect his property. B is not guilty of assault; A is guilty of attempted robbery.

### 4. **Lack of Common Intention (For Dacoity)**
- For dacoity, there must be common intention among five or more persons
- If accused can show he did not share common intention to commit robbery, he may not be liable for dacoity

**Example**: A is part of a group of six persons, but only three intended to commit robbery while others (including A) were unaware. A may not be liable for dacoity if lack of common intention is proved.

### 5. **Fewer Than Five Persons (For Dacoity)**
- If fewer than five persons committed the offence, it is robbery, not dacoity
- Accused may argue that number of offenders was less than five

### 6. **Alibi**
- If accused can prove he was elsewhere at the time of the offence, he cannot be convicted

### 7. **Mistaken Identity**
- If accused can show that he was wrongly identified as the offender (especially in cases with no direct evidence)

## Procedural Aspects

### Cognizance and Complaint
- **All robbery and dacoity offences**: Cognizable, Non-bailable
- **Triable by**:
  - Magistrate of First Class: Sections 392, 393, 399, 401, 402
  - Court of Session: Sections 394, 395, 396, 397, 398, 400

### Investigation
- Police can arrest without warrant (cognizable offences)
- FIR to be registered immediately
- Investigation includes: identification of accused, recovery of stolen property, recording witness statements, establishing number of offenders (for dacoity), proving violence/fear element

### Trial
- Serious offences (dacoity, robbery with hurt, etc.) tried by Sessions Court
- Burden of proof on prosecution to establish:
  - Theft (all seven ingredients)
  - Violence or fear of instant harm (critical element)
  - Number of offenders (for dacoity: five or more)
  - Common intention (for dacoity)

### Compounding
- **None of the robbery or dacoity offences are compoundable**
- Victim and accused cannot compromise to end the case

### Bail
- **All offences are non-bailable**
- Bail may be granted by Sessions Court or High Court in exceptional circumstances
- Factors considered: nature of offence, evidence, antecedents of accused, likelihood of fleeing

## Key Takeaways

1. **Robbery = Theft + Instant Violence/Fear**: Robbery is an aggravated form of theft where violence or fear of instant harm is used before, during, or after the theft

2. **Instant vs. Future Harm**: The critical distinction between robbery and extortion is the **immediacy** of the threat. Robbery requires instant fear; extortion may involve future harm

3. **Dacoity = Collective Robbery (5+ Persons)**: Dacoity requires a minimum of five persons acting conjointly to commit robbery

4. **Severe Punishment**: Robbery and dacoity carry mandatory rigorous imprisonment with minimum sentences (1 year for robbery, 7 years for dacoity)

5. **Vicarious Liability in Dacoity**: All members of a dacoity gang are liable for acts done by any member in furtherance of the common intention, including murder (Section 396)

6. **Non-Bailable and Non-Compoundable**: All robbery and dacoity offences are non-bailable and non-compoundable, reflecting their seriousness

7. **Preparatory Offences**: The law criminalizes even preparation, assembling, and membership in dacoity gangs (Sections 399-402) to prevent commission of dacoity

8. **Three Stages of Violence**: Violence or fear in robbery can occur before, during, or after the theft

## Conclusion

Robbery and dacoity are among the most serious property offences in Indian criminal law due to their combination of property violation and violence against persons. The law imposes severe penalties, including life imprisonment and death (for dacoity with murder), to deter these crimes and protect public safety.

Understanding the fine distinctions between theft, extortion, and robbery—particularly the requirement of **instant harm** for robbery—is critical for proper charge framing and adjudication. The principle of vicarious liability in dacoity ensures that all participants in collective robbery bear responsibility for the acts of their co-offenders.

With the transition to BNS 2023, the substantive law on robbery and dacoity remains unchanged, ensuring continuity in legal practice and precedent.
