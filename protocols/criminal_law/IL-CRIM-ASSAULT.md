## Overview

**Assault** under Indian criminal law encompasses offenses of causing **hurt** to another person, ranging from minor injuries (simple hurt) to life-threatening injuries (grievous hurt). This protocol provides comprehensive coverage of:

- Definition and classification of hurt (simple vs. grievous)
- Punishment provisions (escalating with severity and weapons used)
- Aggravated forms (dangerous weapons, acid attacks, grievous hurt to extort)
- IPC to BNS transition
- Landmark judicial precedents and medical-legal principles

---

## Statutory Framework

### IPC (Pre-July 1, 2024)

**Sections 319-320**: Definitions (hurt, grievous hurt)
**Sections 321-326**: Simple hurt (punishment varies by circumstances)
**Sections 326A-326B**: Acid attacks (specific grievous hurt provisions)
**Sections 327-338**: Causing hurt to extort, wrongful restraint, etc.

### BNS (Post-July 1, 2024)

**Sections 114-124**: Corresponds to IPC hurt provisions (definition and punishment largely unchanged)

## Definitions

### Hurt (IPC Section 319 / BNS Section 114)

**Statutory Text**: "Whoever causes bodily pain, disease or infirmity to any person is said to cause **hurt**."

**Essential Elements**:
1. **Bodily pain**: Physical discomfort (slap, punch, kick)
2. **Disease**: Illness caused by act (poisoning, infection)
3. **Infirmity**: Temporary or permanent impairment of body function

**Threshold**: ANY bodily pain, disease, or infirmity (no minimum severity requirement for simple hurt)

---

### Grievous Hurt (IPC Section 320 / BNS Section 115)

**Eight Categories** (exhaustive list):

#### 1. **Emasculation**
- Permanent loss of power of any member or joint
- **Examples**: Castration, severing of limb, permanent paralysis of arm/leg

#### 2. **Permanent Privation of Sight (One Eye)**
- Total loss of sight in one eye (permanent blindness in that eye)
- **Medical Test**: Cannot perceive light even with corrective lenses
- **Partial Vision**: If partial vision remains (e.g., blurred vision) → Simple hurt, NOT grievous

#### 3. **Permanent Privation of Hearing (One Ear)**
- Total loss of hearing in one ear (permanent deafness in that eye)
- **Medical Test**: Cannot perceive sound even with hearing aid
- **Partial Hearing**: If partial hearing remains → Simple hurt

#### 4. **Privation of Any Member or Joint**
- Loss of use (not necessarily physical severing) of any member/joint
- **Examples**: Fracture rendering arm unusable, spinal injury paralyzing leg

#### 5. **Destruction or Permanent Impairing of Powers of Any Member or Joint**
- **Destruction**: Complete loss of function
- **Permanent Impairing**: Significant reduction in function (not temporary)
- **Examples**: Mangled hand (cannot grip), knee injury (cannot walk normally)
- **Medical Evidence**: Expert opinion on permanence and degree of impairment

#### 6. **Permanent Disfiguration of Head or Face**
- **Permanent**: Cannot be remedied by surgery/treatment
- **Disfiguration**: Deformity visible to others, affects appearance
- **Head or Face**: Includes scalp, ears, nose, eyes, mouth, chin, forehead
- **Examples**: Deep scar on face, loss of nose/ear, burn marks on face
- **Not Disfiguration**: Scar hidden by hair, temporary swelling, easily removable tattoo

**Landmark Case**: **Virbhadra Singh v. State of Himachal Pradesh (1977)**
- Permanent scar on face (2-inch long, visible) = Grievous hurt
- Court: Disfiguration need not be ugly/repulsive, merely permanent and visible

#### 7. **Fracture or Dislocation of Bone or Tooth**
- **Fracture**: Break in continuity of bone (complete or incomplete fracture)
- **Dislocation**: Displacement of bone from joint
- **Tooth**: Includes any tooth (permanent or temporary)
- **Medical Evidence**: X-ray/CT scan showing fracture/dislocation
- **Examples**: Broken jaw, dislocated shoulder, knocked-out tooth

**Note**: Fracture of any bone (even small bone like finger) = Grievous hurt

#### 8. **Severe Bodily Pain or Danger to Life**
- **Severe bodily pain**: Enduring for 20 days or more (as per Explanation to Section 320)
- **Danger to life**: Injury that could cause death if untreated (e.g., deep stab to abdomen, internal bleeding)

**Explanation to Section 320**:
> "Severe bodily pain" means pain for **20 days** or more.

**Medical Jurisprudence**: Pain duration measured from injury date to healing (not just hospital admission).

## Distinction: Simple Hurt vs. Grievous Hurt

| Aspect | **Simple Hurt (IPC 323)** | **Grievous Hurt (IPC 325)** |
|--------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Definition** | Bodily pain, disease, infirmity NOT falling under 8 categories of Section 320 | Any of 8 categories in Section 320 |
| **Severity** | Minor injuries (bruises, cuts, temporary pain) | Serious injuries (fractures, blindness, disfiguration) |
| **Punishment** | Imprisonment up to **1 year** OR fine ₹1,000 OR both | Imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine |
| **Medical Evidence** | Not mandatory (victim testimony suffices) | **Mandatory** (expert medical opinion on nature of injury) |
| **Cognizance** | Non-cognizable (police cannot arrest without warrant) | **Cognizable** (police can arrest without warrant) |
| **Bail** | Bailable | **Non-bailable** (bail at court's discretion) |

## Punishment Provisions

### Simple Hurt (IPC Section 323 / BNS Section 115)

**Voluntarily Causing Hurt**:

> "Whoever, except in case provided for by Section 334, voluntarily causes hurt, shall be punished with imprisonment up to **1 year** OR fine up to **₹1,000** OR both."

**When Applied**: All hurt cases NOT falling under Section 320 (grievous hurt) categories.

---

### Grievous Hurt (IPC Section 325 / BNS Section 117)

**Voluntarily Causing Grievous Hurt**:

> "Whoever, except in case provided for by Section 335, voluntarily causes grievous hurt, shall be punished with imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine."

**When Applied**: Injury falls under any of 8 categories in Section 320.

---

### Aggravated Forms (Enhanced Punishment)

#### Hurt by Dangerous Weapons or Means (IPC Section 324 / BNS Section 116)

**Dangerous Weapons/Means**:
- Firearms (gun, pistol, rifle)
- Sharp-edged weapons (knife, sword, glass bottle)
- Heated substances (boiling water, molten metal)
- Corrosive substances (acid, alkali)
- Poison
- Fire or explosive

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **3 years** OR fine OR both

**Rationale**: Use of weapon elevates risk, even if injury is simple hurt (not grievous).

---

#### Grievous Hurt by Dangerous Weapons (IPC Section 326 / BNS Section 118)

**Combined Offense**: Grievous hurt + use of dangerous weapon

**Punishment**: **Life imprisonment** OR imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

**Examples**:
- Stabbing with knife causing fracture → Section 326
- Shooting with gun causing emasculation → Section 326

---

#### Acid Attacks (IPC Sections 326A-326B / BNS Sections 124-125)

**Section 326A**: Voluntarily causing grievous hurt by acid

**Elements**:
1. Grievous hurt caused
2. By throwing or administering **acid** (corrosive substance)
3. With intention to cause grievous hurt OR knowledge likely to cause grievous hurt

**Punishment**:
- Imprisonment **NOT less than 10 years** (mandatory minimum), extendable to **life imprisonment**
- Fine (minimum ₹10,000 for victim's medical expenses)

**Acid Defined** (Section 326B): Any substance with acidic or corrosive nature capable of burning/disfiguring human tissue.

---

**Section 326B**: Voluntarily throwing acid (attempt to cause grievous hurt)

**Elements**:
1. Acid thrown or administered
2. With intention to cause grievous hurt OR knowledge likely to cause grievous hurt
3. **Actual grievous hurt NOT required** (attempt suffices)

**Punishment**: Imprisonment **NOT less than 5 years**, extendable to **7 years** + fine

**Rationale**: Deterrent for acid attacks (prevalent crime against women, causing permanent disfiguration).

---

### Hurt to Extort (IPC Sections 327-329 / BNS Sections 119-121)

**Section 327**: Voluntarily causing hurt to extort property/valuable security

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

**Section 328**: Causing hurt by poison, etc., to commit offense

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

**Example**: Accused administers sedative to victim to steal jewelry → Section 328.

---

### Hurt to Wrongfully Confine (IPC Section 330 / BNS Section 122)

**Elements**:
1. Voluntarily causing hurt
2. **For purpose** of extorting confession or information
3. Wrongful confinement involved

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine

**Example**: Police torture suspect to extract confession during illegal detention → Section 330 (applicable to public servants and private persons).

---

### Hurt to Deter from Lawful Act (IPC Section 331-333)

**Section 331**: Hurt to deter public servant from duty

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **3 years** OR fine OR both

**Section 333**: Grievous hurt to deter public servant from duty

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

**Example**: Accused assaults police officer to prevent arrest → Section 331/333 (depending on injury severity).

## Medical-Legal Examination

### Medical Certificate Requirements

**For All Hurt Cases**:
- [ ] Nature of injury (abrasion, laceration, contusion, fracture, burn)
- [ ] Size, shape, location of injury (with diagram/photo)
- [ ] Weapon/object likely used (sharp vs. blunt, size estimation)
- [ ] Age of injury (recent <24 hours, old >24 hours)
- [ ] Simple hurt or grievous hurt classification (per IPC Section 320)

**For Grievous Hurt**:
- [ ] **Which category** of Section 320 applies (fracture, disfiguration, emasculation, etc.)
- [ ] **Permanence** of injury (temporary or permanent impairment)
- [ ] **Duration of pain** (if claiming 20+ days severe bodily pain)
- [ ] Expert opinion (orthopedic for fractures, ophthalmic for eye injuries, ENT for ear injuries)

---

### Pain Duration Measurement (20-Day Rule)

**Section 320 Explanation**: "Severe bodily pain" = pain for **20 days or more**.

**Medical Jurisprudence**:
- Day 1: Date of injury
- Day 20: 20th day from injury (continuous pain, not intermittent)
- Medical records: Daily pain assessment, analgesic prescription, patient complaints

**Landmark Case**: **State of Gujarat v. Shaileshbhai (1988)**

**Holding**: Pain duration must be **continuous** 20 days, NOT cumulative/intermittent.

**Example**:
- Injury causing pain for 10 days, heals, pain recurs 1 month later for 15 days → NOT grievous hurt (not continuous 20 days)
- Injury causing continuous pain for 25 days → Grievous hurt (Section 320 Eighth - severe bodily pain)

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Simple Hurt (IPC Section 323)

**Scenario**: A slaps B during argument. B suffers reddening of cheek (no fracture, no permanent injury). Pain lasts 2 days.

**Analysis**:
- **Hurt**: Bodily pain (slap causes pain) → Section 319 definition satisfied
- **Grievous Hurt Test**: Does injury fall under Section 320 categories? NO (no fracture, no 20-day pain, no disfiguration)
- **Classification**: Simple hurt

**Charge**: **IPC Section 323** (voluntarily causing hurt)

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to 1 year OR fine ₹1,000 OR both.

---

### Example 2: Grievous Hurt (Fracture) - IPC Section 325

**Scenario**: A punches B's jaw during fight. B's jaw fractures (confirmed by X-ray). Jaw wired for 6 weeks.

**Analysis**:
- **Hurt**: Bodily pain + injury (fracture)
- **Grievous Hurt Test**: Fracture of bone (jaw) → Section 320 Seventh category (fracture of bone)
- **Medical Evidence**: X-ray shows mandible fracture

**Charge**: **IPC Section 325** (voluntarily causing grievous hurt)

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to 7 years + fine.

---

### Example 3: Grievous Hurt (Permanent Disfiguration) - IPC Section 325

**Scenario**: A throws acid on B's face. B suffers severe burns, permanent scarring on 40% of face. Medical opinion: Scars permanent, not removable by surgery.

**Analysis**:
- **Hurt**: Bodily injury (acid burns)
- **Grievous Hurt Test**: Permanent disfiguration of face → Section 320 Sixth category
- **Medical Evidence**: Plastic surgeon opinion - permanent scarring, visible disfiguration

**Charge**: **IPC Section 326A** (acid attack causing grievous hurt)

**Punishment**: Imprisonment **not less than 10 years**, extendable to **life** + fine (minimum ₹10,000 for victim's medical expenses).

**Note**: Section 326A has **mandatory minimum 10 years** (no discretion to reduce below 10 years).

---

### Example 4: Hurt by Dangerous Weapon (IPC Section 324)

**Scenario**: A slashes B's arm with broken glass bottle. B suffers deep cut (5 cm laceration) requiring stitches. No fracture, heals in 15 days.

**Analysis**:
- **Hurt**: Bodily injury (deep cut)
- **Grievous Hurt Test**: No fracture, pain < 20 days, no permanent impairment → NOT grievous hurt
- **Weapon**: Broken glass bottle = sharp-edged dangerous weapon (Section 324)

**Charge**: **IPC Section 324** (voluntarily causing hurt by dangerous weapon or means)

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR fine OR both.

**Rationale**: Even though injury is simple hurt (not grievous), use of dangerous weapon elevates punishment from 1 year (Section 323) to 3 years (Section 324).

---

### Example 5: Grievous Hurt by Dangerous Weapon (IPC Section 326)

**Scenario**: A shoots B with pistol. Bullet fractures B's femur (thigh bone). B hospitalized for 3 months, undergoes surgery.

**Analysis**:
- **Hurt**: Bodily injury (gunshot wound)
- **Grievous Hurt Test**: Fracture of bone (femur) → Section 320 Seventh category
- **Weapon**: Pistol = firearm (dangerous weapon per Section 324)
- **Combined Offense**: Grievous hurt + dangerous weapon

**Charge**: **IPC Section 326** (grievous hurt by dangerous weapon or means)

**Punishment**: **Life imprisonment** OR imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine.

---

### Example 6: Severe Bodily Pain (20+ Days) - IPC Section 325

**Scenario**: A beats B with bamboo stick (blunt object) on back. No fracture, no disfiguration, but severe back pain. Medical records: Pain for 25 continuous days, analgesics prescribed daily, patient complaints documented.

**Analysis**:
- **Hurt**: Bodily pain (beating with stick)
- **Grievous Hurt Test**: Severe bodily pain for 20+ days → Section 320 Eighth category
- **Medical Evidence**: Daily medical records showing continuous pain for 25 days

**Charge**: **IPC Section 325** (voluntarily causing grievous hurt)

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to 7 years + fine.

**Note**: Pain must be **continuous** 20 days (not cumulative intermittent pain).

## Landmark Cases

### 1. **Virbhadra Singh v. State of Himachal Pradesh (1977)**

**Facts**: Accused caused permanent scar on victim's face (2-inch long, visible mark). Defense argued scar not "ugly" enough to constitute disfiguration.

**Issue**: What constitutes "permanent disfiguration of head or face" under Section 320 Sixth?

**Holding**: Supreme Court held scar = grievous hurt (permanent disfiguration). **Disfiguration need not be ugly or repulsive**, merely permanent and visible.

**Principle**:
- **Objective Test**: Is disfiguration permanent and visible? (Not subjective test: Is it ugly?)
- **Face Defined**: Includes all parts visible to others (forehead, cheeks, chin, nose, ears)
- **Permanence**: Cannot be removed by ordinary medical treatment

**Application**: Any visible permanent scar on face/head = Grievous hurt (Section 320 Sixth).

---

### 2. **State of Gujarat v. Shaileshbhai (1988)**

**Facts**: Victim suffered pain for 10 days after injury, healed, then pain recurred 1 month later for 15 days. Prosecution argued cumulative 25 days pain = grievous hurt.

**Issue**: Does "severe bodily pain for 20 days" (Section 320 Eighth) mean continuous or cumulative?

**Holding**: Gujarat High Court held pain must be **continuous** 20 days from injury date. Cumulative/intermittent pain NOT sufficient.

**Principle**:
- **Temporal Continuity**: 20 days measured continuously from Day 1 (injury) to Day 20
- **Medical Records**: Daily pain assessment required (not just hospital visits on Day 1 and Day 20)
- **Healing Period**: If pain stops (healing occurred), subsequent recurrence NOT counted

**Application**: Medical certificate must document **continuous daily pain** for 20+ days to satisfy Section 320 Eighth.

---

### 3. **Laxman v. State of Orissa (2002)**

**Facts**: Accused knocked out victim's tooth (permanent tooth) during fight. Defense argued tooth is small bone, not serious injury.

**Issue**: Does fracture/loss of tooth constitute grievous hurt?

**Holding**: Supreme Court held loss of tooth = grievous hurt (Section 320 Seventh - fracture or dislocation of bone or **tooth**).

**Principle**:
- **Tooth Explicitly Included**: Section 320 Seventh says "fracture or dislocation of bone **or tooth**"
- **No Severity Threshold**: ANY tooth (molar, incisor, canine), permanent or temporary
- **Medical Evidence**: Dentist certificate confirming tooth loss/fracture suffices

**Application**: Knocking out even one tooth = Grievous hurt → Section 325 (up to 7 years).

## Compliance Checklist: Investigation & Prosecution

### For Investigating Officer

**Immediate Actions**:
- [ ] Secure medical examination of victim within 24 hours (preferably immediately)
- [ ] Photograph injuries from multiple angles (with scale/ruler for size)
- [ ] Seize weapon used (if available) and preserve as evidence
- [ ] Record victim's statement (pain description, duration, functional impairment)

**Medical Certificate**:
- [ ] Obtain medical certificate from government doctor (preferred) or registered medical practitioner
- [ ] Certificate must specify: (i) Simple hurt or grievous hurt, (ii) If grievous, which Section 320 category, (iii) Weapon likely used, (iv) Age of injury
- [ ] For grievous hurt: Obtain specialist opinion (orthopedic for fractures, plastic surgeon for disfiguration, ophthalmologist for eye injuries)

**Charge Determination**:
- [ ] Section 323 (simple hurt) OR Section 325 (grievous hurt) based on medical certificate
- [ ] If weapon used: Section 324 (simple hurt + weapon) OR Section 326 (grievous hurt + weapon)
- [ ] If acid attack: Section 326A (grievous hurt by acid) OR Section 326B (attempt to cause grievous hurt by acid)
- [ ] Aggravated forms: Section 327-333 (hurt to extort, wrongfully confine, deter public servant)

---

### For Public Prosecutor

**Trial Strategy**:
- [ ] Call medical expert to testify on nature of injury (simple vs. grievous)
- [ ] Produce medical certificate + X-rays/CT scans (for fractures)
- [ ] Establish weapon used (eyewitness testimony + seized weapon)
- [ ] For 20-day pain: Produce daily medical records showing continuous pain
- [ ] For disfiguration: Produce photographs (before-after if available), plastic surgeon opinion on permanence

**Anticipate Defense**:
- [ ] Self-defense (Section 96-106) - Prove force used by accused was excessive OR accused was aggressor
- [ ] Accident (Section 80) - Prove mens rea (intention/knowledge/recklessness)
- [ ] Consent (Section 87-89) - Prove no consent OR consent vitiated by fraud/coercion

---

### For Defense Counsel

**Challenge Medical Evidence**:
- [ ] Cross-examine medical expert: Pain duration (was it continuous 20 days?), permanence of disfiguration (can surgery remove scar?)
- [ ] Obtain independent medical opinion (second opinion on grievous hurt classification)
- [ ] Challenge chain of custody of medical samples (if applicable)

**Plead General Exceptions**:
- [ ] Private defense (Section 96-106): Accused acted to defend self/property against unlawful aggression
- [ ] Accident (Section 80): Injury occurred without criminal intention/knowledge/recklessness
- [ ] Consent (Section 87-89): Victim consented to act (e.g., surgery, sports injury)

**Reduction Strategy**:
- [ ] Argue for simple hurt (Section 323) instead of grievous hurt (Section 325) if medical evidence ambiguous
- [ ] Seek bail (if simple hurt - bailable offense; if grievous hurt - argue weak evidence for bail grant)

## IPC to BNS Transition

### Section Mapping

| IPC Section | BNS Section | Change |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| IPC 319-320 | BNS 114-115 | Definitions unchanged |
| IPC 323 (Simple Hurt) | BNS 115 | Punishment unchanged (1 year OR ₹1,000 fine) |
| IPC 324 (Hurt by Weapon) | BNS 116 | Punishment unchanged (3 years OR fine) |
| IPC 325 (Grievous Hurt) | BNS 117 | Punishment unchanged (7 years + fine) |
| IPC 326 (GH by Weapon) | BNS 118 | Punishment unchanged (life OR 10 years + fine) |
| IPC 326A-326B (Acid) | BNS 124-125 | Punishment unchanged (10 years-life for 326A, 5-7 years for 326B) |

### Effective Date: July 1, 2024

**Offenses Before July 1, 2024**: Prosecuted under **IPC 323-326**
**Offenses On/After July 1, 2024**: Prosecuted under **BNS 115-118, 124-125**
