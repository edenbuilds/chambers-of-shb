## Purpose
Comprehensive guidance on property offenses under IPC 1860: theft, extortion, robbery, dacoity, criminal breach of trust, criminal misappropriation, cheating, mischief, criminal trespass, and house-breaking, with elements, punishments, landmark cases, and defenses.

## Core Framework

### 1. THEFT (Section 378-382)

#### 1.1 Definition & Elements (Section 378)
```
THEFT = Dishonestly taking movable property out of possession of any person without consent

FIVE ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS:

1. DISHONEST INTENTION (at time of taking)
   - Intention to cause wrongful gain to oneself or wrongful loss to another

2. MOVABLE PROPERTY
   - Can be moved (cash, jewelry, vehicles, electronics)
   - NOT immovable (land, buildings - different offense: criminal trespass)

3. TAKING (severance from possession)
   - Actual physical removal from victim's possession
   - Even slight movement sufficient (asportation)

4. OUT OF POSSESSION OF ANOTHER PERSON
   - Property must be in someone's possession (not abandoned)
   - Includes custody/control (servant has custody, master has possession)

5. WITHOUT CONSENT
   - Taking without permission
   - OR consent obtained by fraud/misrepresentation (vitiates consent)

EXAMPLES:
✓ A picks B's wallet from pocket (theft)
✓ Employee takes office laptop home without permission (theft, even if temporary use intended)
✓ A finds B's lost phone, appropriates it (theft if dishonest intent, not if attempts to return)

NOT THEFT:
✗ Taking own property (even if in another's possession temporarily)
✗ Taking abandoned property (no possession)
✗ Taking with honest belief of right (no dishonest intention)
```

#### 1.2 Punishment (Section 379-380)
```
SIMPLE THEFT (Section 379):
Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine OR Both

AGGRAVATED THEFT (Section 380):
Theft in dwelling house, means of transportation, or place of worship:
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine

Examples:
- Theft in home (dwelling house)
- Theft from train, aircraft, vehicle (transportation)
- Theft from temple, mosque, church (place of worship)

THEFT BY CLERK/SERVANT (Section 381):
Of property in possession of master:
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine + Liable to return property

THEFT AFTER PREPARATION FOR CAUSING DEATH/HURT (Section 382):
Imprisonment 10 years + Fine

Example: A breaks into house with weapon (prepared to hurt if caught), steals jewelry
→ Section 382 (more serious than simple theft)

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No (if >₹5,000 value or Section 380-382)
TRIABLE BY: Magistrate
```

#### 1.3 Key Case - Pyare Lal Bhargava v. State of Rajasthan (1963)
```
FACTS: Accused removed timber from forest, claimed bona fide belief it was ownerless.

HELD: If person honestly believes he has right to property (even if mistaken):
→ NO dishonest intention → NOT theft

Test: SUBJECTIVE (did accused honestly believe?) not objective (was belief reasonable?)

IMPACT: Honest belief in right to property = Defense to theft (even if belief wrong)
```

### 2. EXTORTION (Section 383-389)

#### 2.1 Definition (Section 383)
```
EXTORTION = Intentionally putting person in fear of injury (to person, reputation, or property)
and thereby dishonestly inducing person to deliver property or valuable security

ELEMENTS:

1. INTENTIONALLY PUTTING IN FEAR
   - Threat or intimidation (express or implied)
   - Fear of injury to: Person, reputation, or property

2. DISHONESTLY INDUCING
   - Victim delivers property due to fear (causal connection)

3. DELIVERY OF PROPERTY OR VALUABLE SECURITY
   - Victim parts with property voluntarily (though under duress)

DIFFERENCE FROM THEFT:
- Theft: Taking WITHOUT consent (victim unaware or non-consenting)
- Extortion: Victim DELIVERS property (coerced consent)

DIFFERENCE FROM ROBBERY:
- Extortion: Fear of FUTURE injury ("I will harm you tomorrow if you don't pay")
- Robbery: Fear of INSTANT injury ("Give me money now or I'll stab you")

EXAMPLES:
✓ "Pay ₹10 lakh or I'll publish your affair photos" (extortion - reputation injury threat)
✓ "Transfer property or I'll kidnap your child" (extortion - person injury threat)
✓ "Give me ₹5 lakh or I'll burn your shop" (extortion - property injury threat)
```

#### 2.2 Punishment (Section 384-389)
```
SIMPLE EXTORTION (Section 384):
Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine OR Both

EXTORTION BY THREAT OF ACCUSATION (Section 388):
If threat is accusation of offense punishable with death/life imprisonment OR
Attempt to excite hatred/contempt (sodomy, unnatural offense):
Imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine

PUTTING PERSON IN FEAR OF INJURY TO COMMIT EXTORTION (Section 385):
Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine OR Both

EXTORTION BY PUTTING IN FEAR OF DEATH/GRIEVOUS HURT (Section 386):
Imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine

PUTTING/ATTEMPTING TO PUT IN FEAR OF DEATH/GH TO COMMIT EXTORTION (Section 387):
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No (if Section 386-388)
COMPOUNDABLE: No
```

### 3. ROBBERY & DACOITY (Section 390-402)

#### 3.1 Robbery (Section 390)
```
ROBBERY = Theft + Assault/Wrongful Restraint + Instant Fear

DEFINITION:
Theft is robbery if:
1. Offender voluntarily causes or attempts to cause death/hurt/wrongful restraint
   to victim, in order to commit theft, OR
2. Offender commits theft and at time of carrying away property:
   - Causes or attempts to cause death/hurt/wrongful restraint
   - Puts victim in fear of instant death/hurt/wrongful restraint

KEY: Violence or threat of INSTANT harm (not future, like extortion)

EXAMPLES:
✓ A snatches purse from B, pushes B down (robbery - hurt during theft)
✓ A threatens B with knife "Give wallet NOW" (robbery - instant fear)
✓ A punches B unconscious, takes jewelry (robbery - hurt to commit theft)

PUNISHMENT (Section 392):
Rigorous imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine

If HIGHWAY ROBBERY (Section 391 - committed on highway between sunset and sunrise):
Rigorous imprisonment up to 14 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No
NON-COMPOUNDABLE
TRIABLE BY: Sessions Court
```

#### 3.2 Dacoity (Section 391)
```
DACOITY = Robbery by 5+ persons acting jointly

ELEMENTS:
1. Crime of robbery (theft + violence/instant fear)
2. 5 or more persons
3. Acting in concert (common intention)

RATIONALE: Greater danger to society (gang crime, organized, difficult to resist)

PUNISHMENT (Section 395):
Rigorous imprisonment for LIFE OR up to 10 years + Fine

AGGRAVATED DACOITY (Section 396 - Dacoity with Murder):
Death OR Life imprisonment + Fine
(One of most serious IPC offenses)

PUNISHMENT FOR BEING MEMBER OF GANG OF DACOITS (Section 400):
Rigorous imprisonment for LIFE OR up to 10 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No
NON-COMPOUNDABLE
TRIABLE BY: Sessions Court
DEATH PENALTY possible (if murder committed, Section 396)
```

### 4. CRIMINAL BREACH OF TRUST (Section 405-409)

#### 4.1 Definition (Section 405)
```
CRIMINAL BREACH OF TRUST:
Whoever, being entrusted with property or dominion over property:
1. Dishonestly misappropriates or converts to own use, OR
2. Dishonestly uses or disposes of property in violation of direction of law OR
   legal contract touching discharge of trust

ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS:

1. ENTRUSTMENT
   - Property given in trust/confidence (not taken without consent like theft)
   - Relationship: Trustee-beneficiary, agent-principal, employee-employer

2. DOMINION OVER PROPERTY
   - Control, custody, management (not mere servant holding for master)

3. DISHONEST MISAPPROPRIATION/CONVERSION
   - Using property for own benefit (not owner's/principal's)
   - Dishonest intention (wrongful gain or wrongful loss)

4. VIOLATION OF TRUST/LEGAL DIRECTION
   - Breach of obligation to account, return, or apply property as directed

DIFFERENCE FROM THEFT:
- CBT: Property ENTRUSTED (lawful possession initially, later misused)
- Theft: Property TAKEN without consent (unlawful possession from start)

EXAMPLES:
✓ Lawyer receives ₹10L from client for property purchase, uses for own investment (CBT)
✓ Company cashier deposits collections in personal account (CBT)
✓ Trustee of charitable trust uses funds for personal vacation (CBT)
✓ Agent sells goods, pockets sale proceeds (CBT)
```

#### 4.2 Punishment (Section 406-409)
```
SIMPLE CBT (Section 406):
Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine OR Both

CBT BY CARRIER/CLERK/SERVANT (Section 407):
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine
(Higher because relationship of trust/dependency)

CBT BY PUBLIC SERVANT/BANKER/MERCHANT/AGENT (Section 408):
Imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine
(Higher because professional trust, public confidence)

CBT BY PUBLIC SERVANT CONCERNING PROPERTY ENTRUSTED TO HIM (Section 409):
Imprisonment for LIFE OR up to 10 years + Fine
(Highest - public servant misusing official position)

COGNIZABLE: Yes (if value >₹100, which is always the case now)
BAILABLE: No (if Section 408-409)
NON-COMPOUNDABLE (Section 408-409)
```

#### 4.3 Landmark Case - R.S. Nayak v. A.R. Antulay (1984)
```
FACTS: Former Maharashtra CM received donations for charitable trust, allegedly diverted for personal/political use.

HELD: Public servant + entrusted with property in official capacity + dishonest misappropriation
→ Section 409 CBT (most serious form)

PUNISHMENT: Life imprisonment possible (though in this case, convicted but later acquitted on appeal)

IMPACT: Established strict liability for public servants in CBT cases (zero tolerance for corruption)
```

### 5. CRIMINAL MISAPPROPRIATION (Section 403-404)

#### 5.1 Definition (Section 403)
```
CRIMINAL MISAPPROPRIATION:
Dishonestly misappropriating or converting to own use movable property that:
- Came into possession innocently (not entrusted, not stolen)
- Example: Found property, mistaken delivery

DIFFERENCE FROM CBT:
- CBT: Property ENTRUSTED (fiduciary relationship)
- Misappropriation: Property came into possession INNOCENTLY/ACCIDENTALLY

EXAMPLES:
✓ A finds B's lost wallet, keeps it (misappropriation - not theft as found, not CBT as not entrusted)
✓ Bank mistakenly credits ₹10L to A's account, A withdraws and uses (misappropriation)
✓ Courier delivers package to wrong address, recipient keeps it (misappropriation)

PUNISHMENT (Section 403):
Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine OR Both

MISAPPROPRIATION OF PROPERTY POSSESSED BY DECEASED AT DEATH (Section 404):
If dishonestly misappropriating property that was in possession of deceased person at time of death:
Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes (if value >₹100)
BAILABLE: Yes
COMPOUNDABLE: Yes
```

### 6. CHEATING (Section 415-420)

#### 6.1 Definition (Section 415)
```
CHEATING:
Whoever, by deceiving any person:
1. Fraudulently or dishonestly induces person to deliver property, OR
2. Intentionally induces person to do/omit doing anything which he would not do/omit
   if not deceived, and which causes harm/damage

ELEMENTS:

1. DECEPTION
   - False representation of fact (express or implied)
   - Concealment of material fact (if duty to disclose)
   - Any act of trickery/fraud

2. INDUCEMENT
   - Victim acts due to deception (causal link)

3. DELIVERY OF PROPERTY OR ACTION/OMISSION
   - Victim delivers property OR does/omits act

4. FRAUDULENT/DISHONEST INTENTION + HARM
   - Accused intended to deceive for wrongful gain/loss

EXAMPLES:
✓ Selling fake jewelry as genuine gold (deception → victim pays → cheating)
✓ Real estate fraud (selling same plot to multiple buyers - deception)
✓ Ponzi scheme (false promise of high returns - deception → investors deposit money)
✓ Identity theft, credit card fraud (impersonation - deception)
```

#### 6.2 Punishment (Section 417-420)
```
SIMPLE CHEATING (Section 417):
Imprisonment up to 1 year OR Fine OR Both

CHEATING WITH KNOWLEDGE THAT WRONGFUL LOSS MAY RESULT (Section 418):
Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine OR Both

CHEATING BY PERSONATION (Section 419):
Pretending to be another person or representing another person:
Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine OR Both

CHEATING AND DISHONESTLY INDUCING DELIVERY OF PROPERTY (Section 420):
Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine
(Most common, serious form - property loss)

Section 420 is iconic: "420" slang in India for "fraudster/cheat"

COGNIZABLE: Yes (Section 420)
BAILABLE: No (Section 420), Yes (Section 417-419)
NON-COMPOUNDABLE (Section 420)
```

#### 6.3 Modern Cheating - Cyber Frauds
```
COMMON TYPES:

1. PHISHING:
   Fake emails/websites impersonating banks, asking for credentials
   Victim enters password → Account hacked → Money stolen
   Offense: Cheating (Section 420) + IT Act Section 66C (identity theft)

2. ONLINE SHOPPING FRAUD:
   Fake e-commerce sites, payment taken, goods never delivered
   Offense: Cheating (Section 420)

3. LOTTERY/PRIZE SCAM:
   "You won ₹25 lakh lottery, pay ₹50,000 processing fee to claim"
   Victim pays fee, no prize received
   Offense: Cheating (Section 420)

4. ROMANCE SCAM:
   Fake online relationship, request money (medical emergency, visa, etc.)
   Offense: Cheating by personation (Section 419) + Section 420

PUNISHMENT: Section 420 (up to 7 years) + IT Act violations (up to 3 years)
Can be tried cumulatively (10 years total if convicted under both)
```

### 7. MISCHIEF (Section 425-440)

#### 7.1 Definition (Section 425)
```
MISCHIEF:
Whoever, with intent to cause or knowing likely to cause wrongful loss/damage to public or any person:
- Causes destruction of property, OR
- Causes diminution in value/utility of property, OR
- Changes any property in manner that destroys/diminishes value/utility

ELEMENTS:
1. Intention or knowledge (to cause wrongful loss/damage)
2. Destruction, damage, or alteration of property
3. Wrongful loss to owner or public

EXAMPLES:
✓ Slashing car tires (destruction)
✓ Spray-painting graffiti on building (diminution of value)
✓ Releasing water from reservoir (alteration causing loss)
✓ Poisoning crops in field (destruction)
✓ Damaging public road (mischief against public property)

PUNISHMENT (Section 426):
Imprisonment up to 3 months OR Fine OR Both
```

#### 7.2 Aggravated Forms (Section 427-440)
```
MISCHIEF CAUSING DAMAGE ≥₹50 (Section 427):
Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine OR Both

MISCHIEF BY KILLING/MAIMING ANIMAL (Section 428-429):
If animal value ≥₹10: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine OR Both
If animal value ≥₹50 (cattle, etc.): Imprisonment up to 5 years OR Fine OR Both

MISCHIEF BY INJURY TO IRRIGATION WORKS (Section 430):
Imprisonment up to 5 years OR Fine OR Both
(Serious because affects agriculture, livelihoods)

MISCHIEF BY DESTRUCTION/DAMAGE TO PUBLIC ROAD/BRIDGE (Section 431):
Imprisonment up to 5 years OR Fine OR Both

MISCHIEF BY CAUSING INUNDATION/OBSTRUCTION TO PUBLIC DRAINAGE (Section 432):
Imprisonment up to 5 years OR Fine OR Both

MISCHIEF WITH INTENT TO DESTROY/MAKE UNSAFE DECKED VESSEL (Section 433-434):
Imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine

MISCHIEF BY FIRE/EXPLOSIVE WITH INTENT TO DAMAGE (Section 435-436):
House/tent: Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine
Vessel: Imprisonment up to LIFE OR 10 years + Fine

MISCHIEF TO RAILWAY/AIRCRAFT (Section 438-439):
Putting out of order any machinery/equipment with intent to cause accident:
Imprisonment for LIFE OR up to 10 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes (if damage >₹50)
BAILABLE: Depends on section
```

### 8. CRIMINAL TRESPASS & HOUSE-BREAKING (Section 441-462)

#### 8.1 Criminal Trespass (Section 441-447)
```
DEFINITION (Section 441):
Whoever enters into or upon property in possession of another person:
1. With intent to commit offense, OR
2. With intent to intimidate/insult/annoy person in possession, OR
3. Having lawfully entered, unlawfully remains with above intents

ELEMENTS:
1. Entry or remaining on property
2. Property in possession of another
3. Entry/remaining without permission (unlawful)
4. Specific intent (to commit offense, intimidate, insult, annoy)

EXAMPLES:
✓ Entering neighbor's land to steal fruits (intent to commit offense - theft)
✓ Entering ex-partner's home to threaten (intent to intimidate)
✓ Entering rival's shop to create nuisance (intent to annoy)

PUNISHMENT (Section 447):
Imprisonment up to 3 months OR Fine ₹500 OR Both

HOUSE-TRESPASS (Section 442, 448):
If trespass into building/tent used as human dwelling, place of worship, or custody of property:
Imprisonment up to 1 year OR Fine ₹1,000 OR Both

HOUSE-TRESPASS TO COMMIT OFFENSE PUNISHABLE WITH DEATH (Section 449):
Imprisonment for LIFE OR up to 10 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes (if house-trespass or to commit serious offense)
BAILABLE: Yes (simple), No (aggravated)
```

#### 8.2 House-Breaking (Section 445, 454-460)
```
HOUSE-BREAKING:
House-trespass committed by:
- Entering through passage made by accused OR
- Any passage not intended for human entrance, OR
- Entering/exiting in manner that involves breaking (roof, wall, door, window, lock)

Essentially: Unlawful entry into dwelling by breaking/unusual passage

PUNISHMENT:

HOUSE-BREAKING TO COMMIT OFFENSE (Section 454):
Imprisonment up to 2 years + Fine

HOUSE-BREAKING AFTER PREPARATION FOR HURT/ASSAULT (Section 456):
Imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine

LURKING HOUSE-TRESPASS/HOUSE-BREAKING BY NIGHT (Section 457-458):
Lurking house-trespass by night: Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine
House-breaking by night: Imprisonment up to 14 years + Fine
(Higher punishment for night crimes - more fear, difficulty in defense)

HOUSE-BREAKING TO COMMIT OFFENSE PUNISHABLE WITH DEATH/LIFE (Section 459):
Imprisonment for LIFE OR up to 10 years + Fine

HOUSE-BREAKING WITH DEADLY WEAPON (Section 460):
Imprisonment for LIFE OR up to 10 years + Fine

COGNIZABLE: Yes
BAILABLE: No
NON-COMPOUNDABLE
TRIABLE BY: Sessions Court (if serious, e.g., Section 458-460)
```

## Landmark Cases

### 1. **State of Maharashtra v. Mohd. Yakub (1980) - SC**
**Issue**: Can picking pocket be robbery or only theft?

**Held**: If victim unaware at time of taking (pickpocketing) → THEFT
If victim aware and resisted, force used → ROBBERY

Robbery requires immediate violence/threat, not stealth.

---

### 2. **K.N. Mehra v. State of Rajasthan (1957) - SC**
**Issue**: Difference between cheating and breach of contract?

**Held**: Cheating requires fraudulent intention AT TIME OF INDUCEMENT
If contract made honestly, later breached → Civil breach, NOT cheating
If contract made with intention to deceive from start → Cheating (Section 420)

---

## Conclusion

Property offenses constitute bulk of IPC crimes. Key distinctions: Theft vs extortion vs robbery (consent, violence), CBT vs misappropriation (entrustment vs innocent possession), Cheating (deception inducing delivery), Mischief (property damage), Criminal trespass vs house-breaking (entry, breaking). Understanding these gradations critical for charging, defense strategy, and punishment severity (₹500 fine for simple trespass vs life imprisonment for dacoity with murder).
