## Overview and Purpose

### Historic Transformation

On **July 1, 2024**, India replaced the **Indian Penal Code (IPC) 1860** - a 164-year-old colonial-era criminal law - with the **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023**, marking the most significant criminal law reform in independent India's history.

### Purpose of This Protocol

1. **Comprehensive Mapping**: Section-by-section IPC to BNS conversion
2. **Identify Changes**: Material vs procedural changes
3. **Practical Guidance**: Help practitioners, courts, law enforcement adapt
4. **Ensure Continuity**: Clarify which precedents continue to apply
5. **Highlight New Offences**: BNS additions not in IPC

## Overall Changes: Summary

### Material Changes (Substantive Law)

**Limited**: BNS retains **90%+ of IPC offences** with same ingredients

**Key Material Changes**:
1. **Sedition**: IPC Section 124A → BNS Section 152 (re-worded, "acts endangering sovereignty")
2. **Mob Lynching**: New offence (BNS Section 103(2)) - not in IPC
3. **Organized Crime**: Enhanced provisions (BNS Sections 111-115)
4. **Terrorism**: Defined in BNS (Section 113) - not separately in IPC
5. **Snatching**: Now separate offence (BNS Section 304(2)) - was included in theft in IPC
6. **Community Service**: Added as sentencing option (BNS Section 4(5))

### Procedural/Structural Changes

**Extensive**: Section numbering, consolidation, removal of obsolete provisions

**Changes**:
1. **Section Numbers**: Most sections renumbered (e.g., IPC 302 Murder → BNS 103)
2. **Consolidation**: Similar offences merged (e.g., multiple theft sections consolidated)
3. **Removal**: Obsolete offences removed (e.g., Section 309 attempt to suicide - decriminalized)
4. **Simplification**: Verbose language simplified
5. **Examples**: More illustrations added for clarity

### Judicial Precedents

**Continuity**: Supreme Court and High Court judgments interpreting IPC **continue to apply** to corresponding BNS sections (if ingredients unchanged)

## Crimes Against Person: Detailed Mapping

### Murder and Culpable Homicide (IPC Sections 299-311 / BNS Sections 100-106)

| Offence | IPC Section | BNS Section | Punishment (IPC) | Punishment (BNS) | Change |
|---------|-------------|-------------|------------------|------------------|--------|
| **Culpable homicide** | 299 | 100 | - | - | **Definition same** |
| **Murder** | 300 | 101 | Death OR life + fine | Death OR life + fine | **Same** |
| **Murder punishment** | 302 | 103 | Death OR life + fine | Death OR life + fine | **Same** |
| **Culpable homicide not amounting to murder** | 304 | 105 | Part I: life or 10 years + fine; Part II: 10 years or fine or both | Same | **Same** |
| **Causing death by rash/negligent act** | 304A | 106 | 2 years or fine or both | 5 years + fine | **ENHANCED punishment** |
| **Abetment of suicide** | 306 | 104 | 10 years + fine | 10 years + fine | **Same** |
| **Attempt to suicide** | 309 | - | **REMOVED** | - | **DECRIMINALIZED** |
| **Infanticide** | 315 | 91 | 10 years or fine or both | Same | **Moved; same** |

**Key Changes**:
1. **Section 309 IPC (Attempt to Suicide) REMOVED** - humanitarian move; focus on mental health
2. **Section 304A punishment enhanced** - 2 years → 5 years (addressing rash/negligent driving deaths)
3. **Mob lynching** added (BNS Section 103(2)) - not separate in IPC

**BNS Section 103(2) - Mob Lynching (NEW)**:
"If a group of five or more persons commits murder on ground of race, caste, community, sex, place of birth, language, personal belief: punishment is death or life imprisonment and fine ≥ Rs. 1 lakh"

**Significance**: First statutory recognition of mob lynching; mandatory minimum fine

### Hurt and Grievous Hurt (IPC Sections 319-338 / BNS Sections 114-126)

| Offence | IPC | BNS | Punishment | Change |
|---------|-----|-----|------------|--------|
| **Hurt** | 323 | 115 | 1 year or Rs. 1,000 or both | **Same** |
| **Voluntarily causing hurt** | 323 | 115 | 1 year or fine (Rs. 1,000) or both | **Same** |
| **Grievous hurt** | 320 | 116 (definition) | - | **Same** |
| **Voluntarily causing grievous hurt** | 325 | 117 | 7 years + fine | **Same** |
| **Acid attack** | 326A | 124 | 10 years to life + fine | **Same** |

**No material change**: Hurt offences retained with same ingredients

### Sexual Offences (IPC Sections 354-376E / BNS Sections 74-79)

| Offence | IPC | BNS | Punishment | Change |
|---------|-----|-----|------------|--------|
| **Assault/criminal force on woman with intent to outrage modesty** | 354 | 74 | 2 years or fine or both | **Same** |
| **Sexual harassment** | 354A | 75 | 1-3 years | **Same** |
| **Rape** | 375-376 | 63-64 | 7 years to life (can extend to death in aggravated cases) | **Same** |
| **Gang rape** | 376D | 70(2) | 20 years to life + fine | **Same** |
| **Rape of minor <16 years** | 376(2) | 65 | 20 years to life or death | **Same** |

**No material change**: Sexual offences retained; POCSO Act 2012 continues to apply

**Note**: Age of consent remains **18 years** (Exception 2 to Section 375 IPC / Exception 2 to Section 63 BNS)

### Kidnapping and Abduction (IPC Sections 359-369 / BNS Sections 137-143)

| Offence | IPC | BNS | Punishment | Change |
|---------|-----|-----|------------|--------|
| **Kidnapping from India** | 360 | 137 | 7 years + fine | **Same** |
| **Kidnapping from lawful guardianship** | 361 | 138 | 7 years + fine | **Same** |
| **Kidnapping for ransom** | 364A | 140 | Death or life + fine | **Same** |

**No material change**

"Whoever commits theft of any property from any person, by snatching or sudden taking without the person's consent, shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine"

## New Offences in BNS (Not in IPC)

### 1. Mob Lynching (BNS Section 103(2))

**Already covered above**

### 2. Organized Crime (BNS Sections 111-115)

**BNS Section 111**: Organized crime defined
- Continuing unlawful activity
- By individual/member of organized crime syndicate
- By violence, threat, coercion for pecuniary gain

**Punishment**: Death, life imprisonment, or imprisonment ≥5 years + fine ≥Rs. 10 lakh

**IPC Position**: No separate organized crime offence (relied on MCOCA 1999)

**BNS Change**: **Statutory definition and punishment** in main criminal code

### 3. Terrorism (BNS Section 113)

**Definition**: Act with intent to threaten unity, integrity, security of India or strike terror

**Punishment**: Death, life, or imprisonment ≥5 years + fine + forfeiture of property

**IPC Position**: No definition in IPC (relied on UAPA 1967, other special statutes)

**BNS Change**: **First definition of terrorism in main criminal code**

**Significance**: Controversial - overlaps with UAPA; potential for misuse

### 4. Sexual Offences Against Children (BNS Chapter V)

**Enhanced focus**: BNS has detailed chapter; IPC relied more on POCSO Act 2012

**POCSO Act continues**: BNS does not replace POCSO

### 5. Hit and Run (BNS Section 106(2))

**Causing death by rash/negligent driving and fleeing without reporting**:
- Punishment: **10 years + fine**

**IPC Position**: Section 304A had uniform 2-year punishment for all rash/negligent acts causing death

**BNS Change**: **Enhanced punishment for hit-and-run** (10 years vs 5 years for regular rash/negligent death)

**Controversy**: Criticized as harsh on drivers; may deter reporting

## Sentencing Changes

### Community Service (BNS Section 4(5))

**NEW**: Community service added as sentencing option

**When**: For petty offences

**Duration**: As specified by court

**Significance**: Restorative justice; reduce prison overcrowding

**IPC Position**: Not available

### Enhanced Punishments

**Hit-and-Run** (BNS 106(2)): 10 years (IPC 304A: 2 years)

**Rash/Negligent Act Causing Death** (BNS 106): 5 years (IPC 304A: 2 years)

**Acid Attack**: Same (10 years to life)

### Decriminalization

**Attempt to Suicide** (IPC 309): **REMOVED**

**Rationale**: Humanitarian; focus on mental health support, not punishment

## Procedural Sections Removed

### IPC Sections Removed in BNS

1. **Section 309**: Attempt to suicide - **decriminalized**
2. **Section 377**: Unnatural offences - **partially read down by Supreme Court in Navtej Johar (2018)**; consensual adult same-sex relations decriminalized; non-consensual/child offences covered under other sections
3. **Sections 161-171**: Offences by public servants - **covered by PCA 1988**
4. **Obsolete provisions**: Sections relating to slavery, etc. (already redundant)

## Judicial Precedents: Continuity

### General Principle

**IPC-era precedents continue to apply** to corresponding BNS sections **if**:
1. **Ingredients unchanged**: Offence definition same
2. **Punishment same**: Sentencing provisions unchanged

**Example**:
- **IPC Section 302 (Murder)** → **BNS Section 103**
- Ingredients: **Same** (intentionally causing death)
- Punishment: **Same** (death or life + fine)
- **All IPC 302 precedents apply to BNS 103**

### Precedents Requiring Re-Evaluation

**If ingredients or punishment changed**, precedents may need re-interpretation:

**Example 1: Sedition**
- **IPC 124A** → **BNS 152** (re-worded)
- **Kedar Nath Singh v. State of Bihar (1962)** (leading IPC 124A case) may require re-evaluation for BNS 152
- But likely to be persuasive

**Example 2: Rash/Negligent Causing Death**
- **IPC 304A** (2 years) → **BNS 106** (5 years)
- Sentencing precedents may not apply directly
- But mens rea/ingredients precedents continue

**Example 3: Mob Lynching**
- **BNS 103(2)** - NEW offence
- No direct IPC precedents
- Courts will develop new jurisprudence

## Practical Implications for Practitioners

### For Police/Investigating Officers

1. **FIRs**: Cite **BNS sections** (not IPC) for offences post-July 1, 2024
2. **Charge sheets**: Use BNS section numbers
3. **Conversion tool**: Maintain IPC-BNS conversion table
4. **Training**: All officers must be trained on BNS

### For Prosecutors

1. **Framing charges**: Use BNS sections
2. **Arguments**: Cite BNS provisions
3. **Precedents**: Continue citing IPC-era precedents (for corresponding BNS sections)
4. **New offences**: Develop prosecution strategies for mob lynching, organized crime, terrorism (BNS)

### For Defense Lawyers

1. **Charges**: Check if BNS section correctly cited (many errors expected in transition)
2. **Precedents**: Argue continuity of IPC precedents for unchanged offences
3. **New offences**: Challenge vagueness, overbreadth of new provisions (e.g., BNS 152 sedition)
4. **Sentencing**: Argue for community service where applicable

### For Courts

1. **Judgments**: Cite BNS sections (mention IPC equivalent in parenthesis for clarity)
2. **Precedents**: Apply IPC-era precedents to corresponding BNS sections
3. **Interpretation**: Interpret new/changed provisions in line with constitutional principles
4. **Guidance**: Provide clarity on ambiguous new provisions

## Transition Period Challenges

### Common Errors Expected

1. **Wrong section numbers**: Citing IPC sections for post-July 1, 2024 offences
2. **Mixing IPC and BNS**: Charge sheet citing some IPC, some BNS sections
3. **Pending cases**: Confusion on which law applies to cases filed before July 1, 2024

### Pending Cases

**General Rule**: **Law in force at time of offence** applies

**Cases where offence occurred pre-July 1, 2024**:
- **IPC applies** (even if trial post-July 1, 2024)
- Cite IPC sections
- IPC precedents apply

**Cases where offence occurred post-July 1, 2024**:
- **BNS applies**
- Cite BNS sections
- BNS provisions; IPC precedents persuasive if offence unchanged

**Mixed cases** (continuing offence straddling July 1, 2024):
- Law at time of completion applies
- Complex issue; courts will clarify

## Quick Conversion Tips

### Finding BNS Equivalent of IPC Section

**Method 1**: Use **IPC-BNS conversion table** (in annexure or online tools)

**Method 2**: **General Pattern**:
- **Crimes against person** (IPC 299-377): BNS ~100-146
- **Crimes against property** (IPC 378-462): BNS ~303-335
- **Forgery** (IPC 463-489): BNS ~336-351
- **General exceptions** (IPC 76-106): BNS ~6-26

**Method 3**: **Search by offence name** (e.g., "murder") in BNS

### Common Confusions

**IPC 302 (Murder)** ≠ **BNS 302** (Punishment for committing robbery)
- **IPC 302 = BNS 103**

**IPC 420 (Cheating)** ≠ **BNS 420** (Removal, etc., of property-mark)
- **IPC 420 = BNS 318**

**Always use conversion table** - don't assume same section number!

## Conclusion

The **IPC to BNS transition** represents:
- **Historic moment**: Replacing 164-year-old colonial law with Indian-centric code
- **Continuity**: 90%+ offences retained with same ingredients
- **Modernization**: Language simplified, obsolete provisions removed
- **Additions**: Mob lynching, organized crime, terrorism defined
- **Decriminalization**: Attempt to suicide removed
- **Challenges**: Transition period confusion, training needs

**Key Takeaways**:
1. **Most offences unchanged**: Ingredients, punishments same
2. **Section numbers changed**: Use conversion table
3. **IPC precedents continue**: For corresponding BNS sections (if unchanged)
4. **New offences**: Mob lynching (103(2)), organized crime (111-115), terrorism (113), snatching (304(2)), hit-and-run (106(2))
5. **Community service**: New sentencing option
6. **Sedition re-worded**: IPC 124A → BNS 152 (controversy continues)
7. **Attempt to suicide decriminalized**: IPC 309 removed

**Practical Guidance**:
- **Use BNS sections** for post-July 1, 2024 offences
- **Maintain conversion table**
- **Cite IPC precedents** (remain valid)
- **Train all stakeholders** (police, lawyers, judges)
- **Clarify ambiguities** through litigation/legislative amendments

**Ultimate Goal**: **Justice** - the transition should **not disrupt** the delivery of justice; courts must ensure **smooth transition** while safeguarding rights and ensuring accountability.
