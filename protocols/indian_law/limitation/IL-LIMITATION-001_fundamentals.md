## Purpose
Comprehensive coverage of Limitation Act 1963 providing statutory time limits for filing suits, appeals, and applications. Covers fundamental principles (Sections 2-25), key Schedule Articles, exclusion/extension provisions, and application to contracts, property, torts, and procedural matters.

## 1. LEGISLATIVE FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Purpose and Scope

**Limitation Act 1963** (effective January 1, 1964):
- Replaced Limitation Act 1908
- Prescribes **time limits** for filing:
  - **Suits** (civil claims in courts)
  - **Appeals** (from decrees/orders)
  - **Applications** (to courts, tribunals, authorities)

**Purpose** (*Consolidated Engineering Enterprises v. Irrigation Department* 2008 SC):
1. **Legal certainty**: Disputes resolved within reasonable time
2. **Prevent stale claims**: Evidence deteriorates, witnesses disappear over time
3. **Presumption of satisfaction**: Delay creates inference of payment/waiver
4. **Protect defendants**: From indefinite threat of litigation

**Constitutional Basis**: Entry 13 List III (Concurrent List) - Limitation

### 1.2 Application (Section 1)

**Extends to**: Whole of India (except Jammu & Kashmir - until 2019)

**Applies to**:
- Suits in civil courts
- Appeals to civil courts
- Applications to civil courts
- Appeals/applications to tribunals (if Limitation Act not excluded by specific statute)

**Does NOT apply to**:
- Criminal proceedings (Criminal Procedure Code has own limitation)
- Revenue proceedings (unless Limitation Act expressly made applicable)
- Specific statutes with own limitation provisions (Consumer Protection Act, Arbitration Act in some respects)

## 2. FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

### 2.1 When Limitation Begins (Section 3)

**Section 3 - Bar of Limitation**:
"Subject to the provisions contained in sections 4 to 24 (inclusive), every suit instituted, appeal preferred, and application made after the **prescribed period** shall be dismissed, although limitation has not been set up as a defence."

**Effect**:
- Limitation is **substantive law** (not mere procedural technicality)
- Court must dismiss suit/appeal/application if time-barred (even if defendant does not raise objection)
- **Mandatory** dismissal (no discretion except under Section 5 condonation)

### 2.2 Computation of Period (Sections 4-12)

**Section 4 - Exclusion of Time in Legal Proceedings**:
If person prosecuting suit/appeal/application is prosecuting **another civil proceeding bona fide** (relating to same matter in court of first instance or appeal):
- Period during which such proceeding continues **excluded**

**Example**:
- Suit filed in wrong court (lack of jurisdiction) → Dismissed after 1 year
- Time for re-filing (if fresh suit possible): Limitation period **excludes** 1 year spent in wrong court

**Section 5 - Extension of Prescribed Period (Condonation)**:
"Any appeal or any application, other than an application under any of the provisions of Order XXI of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, may be admitted after the prescribed period if the appellant or the applicant satisfies the court that he had **sufficient cause** for not preferring the appeal or making the application within such period."

**Key Points**:
- Applies to **appeals and applications** (NOT suits - no condonation for suits except under special statutes)
- **Sufficient cause**: Liberal interpretation (*N. Balakrishnan v. M. Krishnamurthy* 1998 SC - justice-oriented approach)
- Maximum extension: **NO statutory limit** (unlike CPC appeals with fixed extended period)

**Sufficient Cause Examples**:
- ✅ Bonafide ignorance of order (no knowledge)
- ✅ Serious illness of appellant/lawyer
- ✅ Natural calamity (floods, earthquakes - COVID-19 impact)
- ✅ Postal delay in receiving order
- ❌ Negligence, laches (delay without explanation)
- ❌ Impecuniosity alone (poverty NOT sufficient cause per se)

**Section 12 - Exclusions in Computing Period**:
The following periods **excluded**:
- Day from which period begins (count from next day)
- Time during which court closed (public holiday falling on last day extends deadline to next working day)

**Example - Computation**:
- Limitation period: 90 days
- Decree passed: January 1, 2024
- Start counting: January 2, 2024 (exclude January 1)
- Last day: April 1, 2024
- If April 1 is Sunday (court closed) → Deadline extends to April 2, 2024

**Section 14 - Exclusion of Time in Certain Cases (Prior Litigation)**:
"In computing the period of limitation for any suit, the time during which the plaintiff has been **prosecuting with due diligence another civil proceeding**, whether in a court of first instance or in a court of appeal or revision, against the defendant shall be excluded."

**Requirements**:
1. **Same cause of action**
2. **Same parties** (or their representatives)
3. **Bona fide** prosecution
4. **Due diligence** (not abandoned)

**Example**:
- Suit filed in District Court X (lacks territorial jurisdiction) → Dismissed after 18 months
- Plaintiff files fresh suit in District Court Y (proper jurisdiction)
- Limitation for fresh suit: Original limitation period (3 years) **minus time already elapsed** (minus 18 months if prior suit was bona fide and with due diligence) = 1.5 years available

### 2.3 When Cause of Action Arises (Section 2(b))

**"Cause of Action"** (not defined in Act, judicial interpretation):
- Bundle of essential facts which plaintiff must prove to succeed
- Arises when **right is infringed** (not when damage occurs, unless damage is element of right)

**For Contracts** (Section 19):
- **Breach of contract**: From date of breach
- **Continuing breach**: Fresh cause of action each day (but usually limitation runs from first breach)

**For Torts**:
- **Negligence**: From date of accident/injury
- **Defamation**: From date of publication

**For Property**:
- **Possession**: From date of dispossession
- **Specific performance**: From date fixed for performance OR when performance refused

## 3. KEY SCHEDULE ARTICLES

### 3.1 Contract-Related Articles

**Article 54 - Specific Performance (3 years)**:
"For specific performance of a contract" - **3 years** from date fixed for performance, or if no date fixed, when plaintiff has notice of refusal

**Article 55 - Compensation for Breach of Contract (3 years)**:
"For compensation for the breach of any contract, express or implied" - **3 years** from date of breach

**Article 58 - Suit on Contract (3 years)**:
"For relief on the ground of mistake" - **3 years** from date when mistake becomes known to plaintiff

**Article 113 - Suit on Contract (3 years - Residual)**:
"Any suit for which no period of limitation is provided elsewhere in this schedule" - **3 years** from date when right to sue accrues

**Example**:
- Contract for sale of property dated January 1, 2024
- Performance date: March 1, 2024
- Seller refuses to perform on March 1, 2024
- Limitation for specific performance: **3 years from March 1, 2024** (until March 1, 2027)

### 3.2 Property-Related Articles

**Article 65 - Possession of Immovable Property (12 years)**:
"To recover possession of immovable property" - **12 years** from date of dispossession

**Article 64 - Declaration of Title (12 years)**:
"For a declaration and consequential relief" - **12 years**

**Example**:
- Person dispossessed from land on January 1, 2020
- Limitation to file suit for recovery of possession: **12 years** (until January 1, 2032)

### 3.3 Tort-Related Articles

**Article 109 - Wrongful Act Causing Injury (3 years)**:
"For compensation for any wrongful act done to the person or movable property" - **3 years** from date of injury

**Article 95 - Damages for Defamation (1 year)**:
"For compensation for libel" - **1 year** from publication

### 3.4 Procedural Articles

**Article 116 - Appeal from Decree (90 days)**:
"Appeal from a decree or order" - **90 days** from date of decree (for most civil appeals)

**Article 117 - Appeal from Order (30/60 days)**:
"Appeal from an order under the Code of Civil Procedure" - **30 days** (some orders 60 days)

**Article 137 - Residual (3 years)**:
"Any application for which no period of limitation is provided elsewhere in this Schedule" - **3 years** from date when right to apply accrues

## 4. EXTENSION AND EXCLUSION

### 4.1 Acknowledgment (Section 18)

**Section 18 - Acknowledgment Extends Limitation**:
If before limitation expires, person liable **acknowledges liability** in writing signed by him:
- **Fresh limitation period** starts from date of acknowledgment

**Requirements**:
1. **Written** acknowledgment
2. **Signed** by person liable (or authorized agent)
3. Made **before limitation expires**
4. **Admits liability** (clearly recognizes debt/obligation)

**Example**:
- Debt due: January 1, 2020 (limitation expires January 1, 2023 - 3 years)
- Debtor writes letter on December 1, 2022: "I acknowledge owing you ₹5 lakhs. Will pay soon."
- Fresh limitation: **3 years from December 1, 2022** (until December 1, 2025)

**Acknowledgment Needs NO Fresh Consideration**:
- Mere acknowledgment extends limitation (unlike fresh contract which requires consideration)

### 4.2 Part Payment (Section 19)

**Section 19 - Part Payment Extends Limitation**:
If before limitation expires, person liable makes **part payment** of debt/legacy:
- **Fresh limitation** starts from date of payment

**Requirements**:
1. **Actual payment** (not mere promise)
2. Made **before limitation expires**
3. Payment of **part** (not full debt, which extinguishes claim)

**Example**:
- Loan of ₹10 lakhs due January 1, 2020 (limitation expires January 1, 2023)
- Debtor pays ₹1 lakh on December 1, 2022
- Fresh limitation: **3 years from December 1, 2022**

### 4.3 Fraud/Mistake (Section 17)

**Section 17 - Exclusion in Case of Fraud or Mistake**:
If suit is for relief from consequences of **fraud** or **mistake**:
- Limitation does NOT begin until plaintiff **discovers** fraud/mistake (or could with reasonable diligence have discovered)

**Example**:
- Contract induced by fraud (January 1, 2020)
- Plaintiff discovers fraud on January 1, 2023
- Limitation for rescission: **3 years from January 1, 2023** (discovery date, not contract date)

### 4.4 Disability (Sections 6-8)

**Section 6 - Legal Disability**:
If person entitled to institute suit is:
- **Minor** (under 18 years)
- **Insane**, OR
- **Idiot**

**Effect**: Limitation begins when disability ceases (person attains majority/recovers sanity)

**Maximum Period** (Section 7): Even with disability, suit must be filed within **maximum 30 years** from accrual of right

**Example**:
- Cause of action arises when plaintiff is minor (10 years old)
- Normal limitation: 3 years
- Limitation begins when plaintiff turns 18 (attains majority)
- Plaintiff has **3 years from turning 18** to file suit (until age 21)

## 5. COVID-19 IMPACT (SUPREME COURT ORDERS 2020-2021)

### 5.1 Extension by Supreme Court

**Suo Motu Writ Petition (Civil) No. 3/2020**:
- Supreme Court **extended all limitation periods** from March 15, 2020 to March 14, 2021 (initially)
- Further extended until October 2, 2021

**Effect**:
- Period from **March 15, 2020 to October 2, 2021** (531 days) **excluded** from limitation calculation
- Applies to suits, appeals, applications, arbitration, condonation, etc.

**Example**:
- Decree passed: January 1, 2020
- Appeal limitation: 90 days (until April 1, 2020)
- COVID extension: 531 days excluded
- Effective deadline: **April 1, 2020 + 531 days** = September 14, 2021

**Post-COVID**: No further blanket extensions (as of December 2024)

## 6. ARBITRATION AND LIMITATION

### 6.1 Arbitration Act Exceptions

**Section 11 Application (Appointment of Arbitrator)**:
- NO limitation in Limitation Act 1963 (Section 43(3) Arbitration Act provides own rules)

**Section 34 Application (Set Aside Award)**:
- **3 months** from receipt of award (extendable 30 days) - Arbitration Act provision, NOT Limitation Act

**Section 9 Application (Interim Measures)**:
- Article 137 Limitation Act may apply (**3 years**) - debatable

**Commencement of Arbitration**:
- Filing request for arbitration (or appointing arbitrator) does NOT stop limitation for court suit (unless agreement excludes court jurisdiction)

## 7. EXCEPTIONS AND SPECIAL CASES

### 7.1 Suits NOT Barred by Limitation

**Section 25 - Suits for Possession**:
- No limitation for suit by sovereign to recover property (government property)

**Trust Property** (Article 65 Exception):
- No limitation against trustee for trust property while trust continues

**Permanent Injunction** (No specific article):
- Courts have held injunctions follow Article 58 or 113 (3 years)

### 7.2 Statutes Excluding Limitation Act

**Consumer Protection Act 2019**: 2-year limitation (Sections 69, 79) for complaints to consumer commissions (NOT Limitation Act 1963)

**Specific Relief Act Section 16(c)**: Readiness and willingness continuous requirement (regardless of limitation)

## 8. PRACTICAL GUIDANCE

### 8.1 Calculating Limitation - Checklist

**Step 1: Identify Nature of Claim**:
- Contract → Article 54, 55, 58, 113 (3 years)
- Property (possession) → Article 65 (12 years)
- Tort → Article 109 (3 years)
- Appeal → Article 116, 117 (90/30 days)

**Step 2: Determine When Cause of Action Arose**:
- Breach of contract → Date of breach
- Dispossession → Date of dispossession
- Fraud/mistake → Date of discovery

**Step 3: Apply Exclusions**:
- Section 14 (prior bona fide litigation) → Exclude time
- Section 17 (fraud/mistake) → From discovery date
- COVID-19 extension → Exclude March 15, 2020 - October 2, 2021 (531 days)

**Step 4: Check Extensions**:
- Section 18 (acknowledgment) → Fresh limitation from acknowledgment date
- Section 19 (part payment) → Fresh limitation from payment date

**Step 5: Compute Final Date**:
- Exclude first day (Section 12)
- Extend if last day is holiday

### 8.2 Avoiding Time-Bar

**For Plaintiffs/Appellants**:
1. **Diarize deadlines** (appeals: 90 days, specific performance: 3 years from performance date)
2. **File early** (do not wait until last day - unforeseen delays may occur)
3. **Acknowledgment/part payment**: Secure before limitation expires (extends period)
4. **Condonation application**: File with appeal/application if delayed (with detailed affidavit explaining sufficient cause)

**For Defendants**:
1. **Raise limitation** as preliminary objection
2. **Calculate from correct starting point** (cause of action date)
3. **Resist frivolous condonation applications** (no sufficient cause shown)
