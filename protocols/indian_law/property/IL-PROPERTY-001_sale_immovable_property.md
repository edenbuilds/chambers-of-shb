## Purpose
Comprehensive coverage of sale of immovable property under Transfer of Property Act (TPA) 1882, including definition, essentials, contract vs. conveyance, registration requirements under Registration Act 1908, rights and liabilities, and remedies for breach including specific performance post-2018 SRA Amendment.

## 1. LEGISLATIVE FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Transfer of Property Act 1882

**Scope**: Governs transfer of property in India (except where local law applies)

**Key Provisions**:
- **Sections 5-53**: General principles of transfer
- **Sections 54-55**: Sale
- **Sections 58-104**: Mortgage
- **Sections 105-117**: Leases
- **Sections 118-129**: Exchange, Gift, Actionable Claims

**Interplay with Registration Act 1908**:
- TPA defines **what** transfers require registration
- Registration Act prescribes **how** registration occurs

### 1.2 Constitutional Basis

**Entry 6 List III (Concurrent List)**: Transfer of property other than agricultural land

**Entry 18 List II (State List)**: Land (rights, ownership, alienation)

**Effect**: State laws may vary (agricultural land, stamp duty)

## 2. DEFINITION OF SALE (SECTION 54)

### 2.1 Section 54 - "Sale"

**"Sale"** is a transfer of ownership in exchange for **price paid or promised** or **part-paid and part-promised**.

**Essential Elements**:
1. **Transfer of ownership** (not mere possession)
2. **Immovable property** (land, buildings, hereditary allowances, rights in land)
3. **Consideration** = **price** (money, not kind)
4. **Present transfer** (immediate transfer of ownership, not agreement to transfer in future)

### 2.2 Sale vs. Agreement to Sell

**Sale** (Section 54):
- **Ownership transfers immediately**
- Creates **transfer of property**
- If immovable ≥₹100 → **Registered document** required (Section 54 TPA + Section 17 Registration Act)

**Agreement to Sell** (Contract):
- **Promise to transfer ownership in future** (on fulfillment of conditions or at future date)
- Creates **contractual rights** (not property transfer)
- **NOT required to be registered** (unless party seeks specific performance under Section 17(1-A) Registration Act)

**Key Distinction**:
- Sale → "I transfer ownership to you" → Registered deed
- Agreement to Sell → "I agree to transfer ownership upon payment/at later date" → Contract (may be unregistered)

**Example**:
- Buyer and seller execute **agreement to sell** (January 1, 2024): "Seller agrees to sell property for ₹50 lakhs, sale to complete on March 1, 2024"
  - January 1: Agreement to sell (contract), NO ownership transfer
  - March 1: **Sale deed** executed and registered → Ownership transfers

## 3. REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Section 54 TPA - Registration Mandate

**Section 54**: Sale of immovable property of value ₹**100 or upwards** can be made **ONLY by registered instrument**.

**Exception**: Lease for term not exceeding 1 year OR rent not exceeding ₹100/year (oral/unregistered valid)

**Effect of Non-Registration**:
- Unregistered sale deed (value ≥₹100) → **VOID** (Section 49 Registration Act)
- Does NOT transfer ownership
- Creates NO legal title

**Example**:
- Seller executes sale deed (unregistered) for ₹50 lakhs property
- Buyer cannot claim ownership
- At best, creates **agreement to sell** (if otherwise valid contract) → Buyer may seek specific performance (to compel registration)

### 3.2 Registration Act 1908 - Section 17

**Section 17(1)(b)**: Non-testamentary instruments which purport or operate to create, declare, assign, limit or extinguish any right, title or interest of value ₹100 or more in immovable property **MUST be registered**.

**Section 17(1-A)**: Documents required to be registered under Section 17(1) can only be received as evidence of contract in suit for specific performance if **registered**.

**Implication**:
- Unregistered agreement to sell (value ≥₹100) → NOT admissible as evidence unless registered
- Buyer seeking specific performance must first get agreement registered

**Practical Effect** (*Janak Raj v. Gurdial Singh* 2001 SC):
- Courts require registration of agreement to sell (≥₹100) for specific performance suits
- Prevents use of unregistered agreements as proof of contract

### 3.3 Registration Procedure

**Parties**:
- **Executant** (seller) must appear before Sub-Registrar
- **Claimant** (buyer) should appear (or authorized representative)

**Documents**:
- Sale deed (minimum 2 copies + 1 for registration office)
- Identity proof (Aadhar, PAN)
- Property documents (title deeds, encumbrance certificate)
- Stamp duty payment receipt
- Registration fee

**Stamp Duty**:
- State-specific (varies 4-10% of property value)
- Payable before registration
- Non-stamped document inadmissible (Section 35 Stamp Act 1899)

**Attestation**:
- **2 witnesses** required (independent, not parties to transaction)

**Effect of Registration** (Section 47 Registration Act):
- Registered document operates from **date of execution** (not registration date)
- BUT legal title recognized only after registration

## 4. TRANSFER OF OWNERSHIP

### 4.1 When Title Passes

**General Rule (Section 54)**: Ownership transfers on **registration** of sale deed

**NOT**:
- ❌ On execution of sale deed (if unregistered)
- ❌ On payment of consideration (if sale deed not registered)
- ❌ On delivery of possession (if sale deed not registered)

**Exception - Doctrine of Part Performance** (Section 53-A TPA):
- Transferee who has taken **possession** under contract and performed part of obligation (paid part price) → Protected against seller
- Seller cannot claim property back (estoppel)
- BUT does NOT transfer title (only protects possession)

### 4.2 Rights and Liabilities (Section 55)

**Seller's Duties** (Section 55(1)):
1. **Disclose material defects** in property/title known to seller
2. **Produce title documents** for inspection
3. **Execute deed** and get registered
4. **Deliver possession** on payment (unless agreed otherwise)
5. **Pay government revenue** (property tax) up to sale date

**Seller's Rights** (Section 55(1)):
1. **Keep title documents** (unless agreed to deliver)
2. **Retain property** until full payment (unless credit agreed)

**Buyer's Duties** (Section 55(4)):
1. **Disclose defects in title** discovered after contract (known to buyer, not seller)
2. **Pay/tender price** at time/place agreed
3. **Bear registration/stamp duty costs** (unless agreed otherwise)

**Buyer's Rights** (Section 55(4)):
1. **Receive title documents** (if agreed)
2. **Possession** on payment
3. **Benefits** from property (rents, crops) from registration date

**Liability for Hidden Defects** (*Kanaiyalal v. Dineshchandra* 1961 SC):
- Seller liable for defects in title **known** to seller but not disclosed
- NOT liable for defects **equally unknown** to both parties (caveat emptor - buyer beware)

## 5. SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE REMEDY

### 5.1 Post-2018 SRA Amendment - Mandatory Specific Performance

**Pre-2018**: Specific performance **discretionary** (court may refuse even if contract valid)

**Post-2018** (Section 10 SRA as amended):
Specific performance **SHALL be enforced** (mandatory) UNLESS exceptions (Sections 11, 14, 16, 20)

**For Sale of Immovable Property**:
- Valid agreement to sell → Buyer entitled to specific performance (compel seller to execute/register sale deed) **as of right**
- Burden on seller to prove exceptions

**Exceptions** (Section 11 SRA):
- Contract vague, uncertain
- Performance impossible
- Contract depends on personal qualifications (not applicable to property sale)
- Damages adequate remedy (rare for property)

**Readiness and Willingness** (Section 16(c) SRA):
- Buyer must be **ready and willing** to perform (pay balance price)
- Continuous requirement (from contract → filing → trial)

**See IL-SRA-001 for detailed specific performance analysis**

### 5.2 Alternative Remedies

**If specific performance not granted**:
1. **Damages** (Section 73 ICA): Compensation for loss
2. **Rescission** (Section 27 SRA): Cancel contract, restore parties to original position
3. **Return of earnest money/advance** with interest

## 6. TIME IS OF THE ESSENCE (SECTION 55)

### 6.1 When Time is Essence

**Section 55(3)**: Time is **NOT of essence** in contract for sale of immovable property (unless parties expressly agree otherwise)

**Effect**:
- Delay in performance does NOT automatically terminate contract
- Party can still claim specific performance (even if delayed)

**Exception - Express Clause**:
"Time is of the essence" clause in contract → Strict deadline
- Failure to perform by deadline → Breach
- Other party may terminate and claim damages

**Example**:
- Agreement to sell: "Sale to complete by March 31, 2024. **Time is of essence**."
- If seller fails to complete sale by March 31 → Buyer may:
  - Terminate contract AND claim damages (loss of bargain), OR
  - Still seek specific performance (if willing to waive strict time)

### 6.2 Delay and Condonation

**Reasonable Time**:
If time not specified → Performance within **reasonable time**

**Factors for Reasonable Time**:
- Nature of property (complexity of title verification)
- Market conditions
- Financing requirements

**Notice to Complete** (*Saradamani v. Radhakanta* 1928 PC):
- Party may serve notice to complete within specified time (making time of essence)
- If other party fails → Contract terminable

## 7. DOCTRINE OF PART PERFORMANCE (SECTION 53-A)

### 7.1 Protection of Transferee

**Section 53-A**: Where transferee has, in part performance of contract:
1. Taken **possession** of property, OR
2. Performed **part of obligations** (paid part price, made improvements)

**Effect**:
- Transferor **cannot enforce against transferee** any right inconsistent with contract
- Transferee protected in **possession** (seller cannot evict)

**Requirements**:
1. **Written contract** (signed by transferor or authorized agent)
2. Transferee **performed part** (possession + payment/improvements)
3. Performance in **good faith**

**Limitation**:
- Does NOT transfer title (only protects possession)
- Transferee must still seek specific performance to obtain title

**Example**:
- Agreement to sell signed, unregistered
- Buyer paid 50% price, took possession, made renovations
- Seller attempts to sell to third party → Buyer protected under Section 53-A (seller cannot evict or sell to another)
- Buyer should file specific performance suit to compel registration

## 8. FIXTURES AND IMPROVEMENTS

### 8.1 Fixtures (Section 3 TPA)

**"Attached to Earth"**: Property attached to earth or permanently fastened to anything attached to earth

**Examples of Fixtures**:
- Buildings, trees, shrubs
- Machinery embedded in earth (factory machinery on foundation)
- Doors, windows (if permanently affixed)

**Transfer of Fixtures**:
- Sale of land includes fixtures (unless excluded)
- Buyer entitled to fixtures existing at time of sale

### 8.2 Improvements by Buyer in Possession

**Before Registration**:
- Buyer in possession under part performance (Section 53-A)
- Makes improvements (construction, repairs)

**Effect**:
- Buyer entitled to reimbursement (if seller defaults and specific performance not decreed)
- Improvements belong to landowner (seller) but buyer has lien for value

## 9. PRACTICAL GUIDANCE

### 9.1 Due Diligence Checklist

**Before Entering Agreement to Sell**:
1. **Title Verification**:
   - Chain of title documents (past 30 years minimum)
   - Encumbrance Certificate (last 13-30 years)
   - Tax receipts (property tax paid up to date)
2. **Physical Inspection**:
   - Site visit, boundary verification
   - Possession status (vacant or occupied)
3. **Statutory Approvals**:
   - Building plan approval (if constructed)
   - Occupancy Certificate (OC)
   - Commencement Certificate (CC)
4. **Encumbrances**:
   - No mortgages/liens (or to be discharged at sale)
   - No pending litigation
5. **Seller's Authority**:
   - Power of attorney (if seller represented)
   - Seller's identity verification

### 9.2 Agreement to Sell - Essential Clauses

**Must Include**:
1. **Parties**: Full names, addresses, identification
2. **Property description**: Survey number, boundaries, area
3. **Consideration**: Total price, payment schedule
4. **Timeline**: Date for sale completion
5. **Possession**: When delivered (on sale or earlier)
6. **Title warranty**: Seller warrants clear, marketable title
7. **Defects disclosure**: Known defects in property/title
8. **Specific performance clause**: Parties may seek specific performance
9. **Time is essence**: If strict deadline required
10. **Registration**: Parties agree to execute and register sale deed

### 9.3 Common Pitfalls

**Unregistered Sale Deeds** (≥₹100):
- Void, no title transfer
- Cannot be used as evidence (Section 49 Registration Act)

**Unregistered Agreements to Sell** (≥₹100):
- Valid as contract
- BUT inadmissible for specific performance (Section 17(1-A) Registration Act)
- Must be registered to use as evidence

**Inadequate Due Diligence**:
- Hidden encumbrances (mortgages, liens)
- Forged documents, impersonation
- Litigation pending

**Possession Without Registration**:
- Part performance protection (Section 53-A) but NO title
- Must file specific performance to secure title
