# Indian IP Law: Geographical Indications Protection

## Purpose
Comprehensive guidance on Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration and Protection) Act 1999, covering GI registration, protection of regional products, authorized users, infringement remedies, and GI strategy for traditional/regional products.

## Activation Triggers
Use when: Regional/traditional product requires GI protection | GI registration application | Unauthorized use of GI | Authorized user registration | GI infringement action | Agricultural/handicraft product protection | International GI recognition | GI vs trademark conflict.

## Core Principles

### Section 2(1)(e): "Geographical Indication" Defined

**"Geographical indication, in relation to goods, means an indication which identifies such goods as agricultural goods, natural goods or manufactured goods as originating, or manufactured in the territory of a country, or a region or locality in that territory, where a given quality, reputation or other characteristic of such goods is essentially attributable to its geographical origin"**

**Essential Elements:**

1. **INDICATION** - Name or sign identifying geographical origin
2. **GOODS** - Agricultural, natural, or manufactured goods
3. **ORIGINATING IN** - Territory, region, locality
4. **QUALITY/REPUTATION/CHARACTERISTIC** - Attributable to geographical origin

**Examples:**

**Registered Indian GIs:**
- **Darjeeling Tea** (West Bengal) - Unique flavor due to climate, altitude
- **Basmati Rice** (Punjab, Haryana) - Distinct aroma, grain length
- **Kanchipuram Silk Sarees** (Tamil Nadu) - Traditional weaving techniques
- **Alphonso Mango** (Maharashtra) - Unique taste, texture
- **Mysore Sandalwood Oil** (Karnataka) - Specific fragrance
- **Chanderi Fabric** (Madhya Pradesh) - Traditional craft

**What Makes a GI:**
- Link between product and geography (climate, soil, tradition)
- Distinctive quality/reputation due to origin

**NOT GI:**
- Generic names (e.g., "Table" - no geographical link)
- Brand names (e.g., "Tata" - company name, not geographical)

### Section 2(1)(f): Who Can Be Registered as "Authorized User"

**Authorized User = Producer/Trader Authorized to Use GI**

**Criteria:**
- Person producing goods in geographical area
- Dealing/trading in goods originating in area
- Quality, reputation, characteristics attributable to geography

**Examples:**
- Tea estate in Darjeeling → Authorized user for "Darjeeling Tea"
- Weaver in Kanchipuram → Authorized user for "Kanchipuram Silk"

## GI Registration Procedure

### Phase 1: Application

**Who Can Apply (Section 11):**

**(a) Association of Persons** - Producers' association, traders' association (preferred)

**(b) Producer** - Individual producer from geographical area

**(c) Organization/Authority** - Government organization, local authority

**Example:**
- Darjeeling Planters' Association applied for "Darjeeling Tea" GI
- Association of producers (not individual tea estate)

**Application Form: GI-1**

**Information Required:**

1. **Geographical Indication** - Name/sign (e.g., "Darjeeling Tea")
2. **Goods** - Description, class
3. **Geographical Area** - Territory, region, locality (map, boundaries)
4. **Quality/Reputation/Characteristics** - What makes goods distinctive
5. **Link to Geography** - How geography contributes to quality (climate, soil, tradition, skill)
6. **Inspection Body** - Who will inspect/certify authorized users
7. **Applicant Details** - Association/producer/organization

**Fee:**
- Rs. 5,000 (application)

**Supporting Documents:**
- Statement of case (detailed description)
- Map showing geographical area
- Evidence of quality, reputation (historical use, awards, recognitions)
- Details of inspection structure

### Phase 2: Examination

**Formalities Check:**
- Application complete?
- Correct class?
- Fee paid?

**Substantive Examination:**

**Examiner Assesses:**

1. **Is it a GI?** - Meets Section 2(1)(e) definition?
2. **Link to Geography?** - Quality/reputation attributable to geography?
3. **Geographical Area Defined?** - Clear boundaries?
4. **Not Generic?** - Not common name for goods

**Examination Report:**
- Objections raised (if any)
- Timeline: 6-12 months

**Response:**
- Applicant responds to objections
- Amend statement, provide evidence

### Phase 3: Publication and Opposition

**Acceptance:**
- GI application accepted
- Published in GI Journal

**Opposition Period: 3 MONTHS**

**Who Can Oppose:** Any person (Section 13)

**Grounds:**
- Not a GI (Section 9 - absolute grounds)
- Conflicts with existing GI/trademark (Section 10 - relative grounds)
- Area not correctly defined
- Link to geography not established

**Opposition Procedure:**
- Notice of opposition
- Counter-statement
- Evidence
- Hearing
- Decision

**Timeline:** 1-2 years if opposed

### Phase 4: Registration

**If No Opposition OR Opposition Rejected:**
- GI registered
- Certificate issued
- Published in GI Journal

**Term:**
- **10 YEARS** (renewable indefinitely every 10 years)

**Renewal:**
- File renewal application before expiry
- Fee: Rs. 5,000

## Authorized User Registration

### Separate Application Required

**GI Registration ≠ Right to Use**

**Two-Step Process:**

**Step 1:** GI registered by association/organization

**Step 2:** Individual producers apply to become "Authorized Users"

**Authorized User Application: GI-2**

**Who Can Apply:**
- Producer in geographical area
- Trader dealing in goods from area
- Must meet quality standards

**Procedure:**
1. Apply to Registrar (GI-2 form)
2. Provide evidence:
   - Located in geographical area
   - Producing/dealing in goods
   - Compliance with quality standards
3. Inspection (by inspection body designated in GI registration)
4. If satisfied → Authorized User Certificate issued

**Fee:** Rs. 500

**Validity:** 5 years (renewable)

**Registered Authorized Users:**
- Can use GI on products
- Entitled to legal protection
- Must comply with quality standards

**Monitoring:**
- Inspection body monitors authorized users
- Ensures quality maintained
- Can revoke authorization if standards not met

## Rights and Protection

### Section 21: Rights of Registered Proprietor and Authorized Users

**Proprietor (Association/Organization That Registered GI):**
- Authorizes users
- Monitors quality
- Enforces rights

**Authorized Users:**
- Use GI on goods
- Prevent unauthorized use by others
- Sue for infringement

### Section 22: Infringement

**Infringement Occurs When:**

**(a) Use of GI by Unauthorized Person** - Person not registered as authorized user uses GI

Example:
- Tea producer in Assam labels tea as "Darjeeling Tea" → Infringement (not from Darjeeling)

**(b) Use of GI for Goods Not Originating from Geographical Area**

Example:
- Silk saree from Varanasi labeled "Kanchipuram Silk" → Infringement

**(c) False Representation of Origin**

Example:
- Product labeled "Made in Darjeeling" when actually made elsewhere → Infringement

**(d) Imitation/Translation of GI**

Example:
- Using "Darjiling" OR "Darjeeling-style" for non-Darjeeling tea → Infringement

**Section 22(3) - No Innocent Infringer Defense:**
- Unlike design/patent, knowledge not required
- Strict liability for unauthorized use

### Remedies (Section 28)

**Civil Remedies:**

1. **Injunction** - Restrain unauthorized use
2. **Damages/Account of Profits**
3. **Delivery Up** - Infringing goods/labels destroyed

**Criminal Remedies (Section 39):**
- **Imprisonment:** Up to 3 years
- **Fine:** Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 2,00,000

**Cognizable Offense:**
- Police can arrest (with warrant)

**Enhanced Penalty for Repeat Offense (Section 40):**
- Imprisonment: 1-3 years (mandatory minimum 1 year)
- Fine: Rs. 1,00,000 to Rs. 2,00,000

## GI vs Trademark

### Section 10: Conflicts with Trademarks

**GI Cannot Be Registered if:**

**(a) Identical/Similar to Registered Trademark (Prior Rights)**
- Trademark registered before GI application → Trademark prevails

**Example:**
- "Champagne" trademark registered in India (hypothetically) before GI application
- GI application for "Champagne" from Champagne, France → May be refused

**(b) Well-Known Trademark**
- Conflict with well-known mark → GI refused

**Section 24: Trademark Cannot Be Registered if Conflicts with GI**

**After GI Registered:**
- Trademark application for name identical/similar to GI → Refused

**Example:**
- "Darjeeling Tea" registered as GI
- Trademark application for "Darjeeling Tea" for tea products → Refused

**Exception - Bona Fide Use of Own Name:**
- If person/business using own name (which is same as GI) in good faith
- Can continue use (not infringement)

**Example:**
- "Darjeeling Tea Shop" owned by person named Darjeeling (before GI registration)
- Can continue use (bona fide use of own name)

## International GI Protection

### TRIPS Agreement (Article 22-24)

**All WTO Members Must:**
- Protect geographical indications
- Prevent misleading use
- Prevent unfair competition

**Enhanced Protection for Wines and Spirits (Article 23):**
- Absolute protection (even if no confusion)

**Example:**
- "Champagne" (France) protected globally for wine/spirits
- Cannot use "Champagne" for sparkling wine from other regions (even with qualifiers like "California Champagne")

### Bilateral Agreements

**India Has Bilateral GI Agreements With:**
- European Union
- UK
- ASEAN countries

**Protection:**
- Indian GIs protected abroad
- Foreign GIs protected in India

**Examples:**
- Scotch Whisky (UK) protected in India
- Darjeeling Tea (India) protected in EU

## GI Strategy

### 1. Identification - What Products Qualify?

**Assess:**
- Product's link to geography (climate, soil, tradition)
- Distinct quality/reputation due to origin
- Historical evidence of association
- Producer community (can form association)

**Examples:**

**Strong GI Candidates:**
- Agricultural products with terroir (climate/soil influence): Tea, coffee, rice, spices
- Traditional crafts: Textiles, pottery, handicrafts
- Foodstuffs: Cheese, wine, processed foods with traditional methods

### 2. Application - Association vs Individual

**Preferred: Association Application**
- Represents all producers
- Establishes quality standards
- Monitors compliance
- Stronger enforcement

**Individual Application:**
- If no association exists
- Faster (sometimes)
- Limited control over quality standards

### 3. Quality Standards and Inspection

**Critical for GI Success:**
- Define clear quality standards
- Establish inspection body
- Regular monitoring of authorized users
- Revoke authorization if standards not met

**Example:**
- Darjeeling Tea GI: Quality standards for plucking, processing, tea characteristics
- Inspection by designated body
- Ensures "Darjeeling Tea" maintains reputation

### 4. Enforcement and Monitoring

**Market Surveillance:**
- Monitor for unauthorized use
- Online marketplaces, retail stores

**Enforcement Actions:**
- Send cease and desist letters
- File infringement suits
- Criminal complaints for blatant misuse

### 5. International Registration

**Madrid Protocol (for GIs?):**
- NOT applicable (Madrid only for trademarks)

**Bilateral Agreements:**
- Seek protection in export markets
- Apply for GI registration in target countries

**Example:**
- Basmati Rice: Register GI in EU, US, Middle East (major export markets)
- Prevent misuse of "Basmati" label

### Common Mistakes

**1. Confusing GI with Trademark** → GI = Geographical origin; Trademark = Brand

**2. Individual Producer Applying (Instead of Association)** → Weaker control over quality

**3. Poorly Defined Geographical Area** → Disputes, enforcement difficulties

**4. Not Establishing Quality Standards** → GI loses value, reputation

**5. Not Monitoring Authorized Users** → Quality degradation, misuse

**6. Not Enforcing Rights** → Unauthorized use flourishes, GI diluted

**7. Not Seeking International Protection** → Misuse abroad, lost export revenue

**8. Assuming GI Registration = Automatic Right to Use** → Must apply as authorized user separately

**9. Not Renewing GI/Authorized User Registration** → Rights lapse

**10. Generic Use** → Allowing GI to become generic term (e.g., "Basmati" used generically for aromatic rice)
