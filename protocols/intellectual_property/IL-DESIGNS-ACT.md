# Indian IP Law: Industrial Designs Protection

## Purpose
Comprehensive guidance on Designs Act 2000 covering design registrability, application procedure, term, infringement, piracy remedies, and design protection strategy for aesthetic/functional product features.

## Activation Triggers
Use when: Product design protection needed | Design registration application | Design infringement/piracy | Copyright vs design overlap | Functionality vs aesthetics distinction | Design search and clearance | International design filing (Hague Agreement) | Design portfolio strategy | Prior art assessment.

## Core Principles

### Section 2(d): "Design" Defined

**"Design means only the features of shape, configuration, pattern, ornament or composition of lines or colors applied to any article whether in two dimensional or three dimensional or in both forms, by any industrial process or means, whether manual, mechanical or chemical, separate or combined, which in the finished article appeal to and are judged solely by the eye"**

**Essential Elements:**

1. **FEATURES** - Shape, configuration, pattern, ornament, composition of lines/colors
2. **APPLIED TO ARTICLE** - Must be applied to specific article (not abstract design)
3. **INDUSTRIAL PROCESS** - Produced by industrial means (capable of mass production)
4. **JUDGED BY EYE** - Visual/aesthetic appeal
5. **NOVELTY/ORIGINALITY** - New or original (Section 4)

**What is Protected:**
- Appearance, aesthetic features
- NOT functionality (functional features = patent subject)

**Examples:**

**Registrable Designs:**
- Shape of mobile phone ✓
- Pattern on textile ✓
- Ornamental design on furniture ✓
- Configuration of shoe ✓

**NOT Registrable:**
- Mechanical function of hinge ✗ (functional - patent)
- Chemical formula ✗ (not visual)
- Construction method ✗ (process - patent)

### Section 2(e): "Article" Defined

**"Article means any article of manufacture and includes any part of an article capable of being made and sold separately"**

**Examples:**
- Furniture, clothing, toys, utensils, vehicles, packaging

**Parts of Articles:**
- Car headlight design (separate article) ✓
- Bottle cap design ✓

### Section 4: Novelty and Originality Requirement

**Design is Registrable if:**

**(a) NEW** - Not previously published or used in any country

**Absolute Novelty Standard:**
- Prior publication/use anywhere in world destroys novelty
- NO grace period (unlike patents in some countries)

**(b) ORIGINAL** - Not previously known/used in India

**Distinction:**

| Aspect | Novelty | Originality |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Worldwide | India |
| **Test** | Not published anywhere | Not known/used in India |

**Example:**
- Design published in US magazine → Not novel (worldwide publication)
- Design used privately in India (not published) → May be original

**Section 5: What is NOT Registrable:**

**(a) Not Novel/Original** - Previously published/disclosed

**(b) Comprises/Contains Scandalous/Obscene Matter**

**(c) Section 23 Emblems and Names (Improper Use) Act** - National emblems, flags

**(d) Mere Mechanical Device** - Functional features only (no aesthetic appeal)

**(e) Trade Mark/Property Mark** - Cannot register trademark as design

**Example:**
- Design consisting solely of company logo → Not registrable (trademark, not design)

## Design Registration Procedure

### Phase 1: Design Search

**Prior Art Search:**
- Indian Design Database (official)
- International design databases (WIPO, EUIPO)
- Product catalogs, magazines
- Internet search (images)

**Objective:**
- Assess novelty/originality
- Identify similar designs
- Evaluate registration prospects

### Phase 2: Application Filing

**Form 1: Application for Registration of Design**

**Information Required:**

1. **Applicant Details** - Name, address, nationality
2. **Article** - Description (e.g., "Table lamp")
3. **Class of Article** - Locarno Classification (32 classes)
4. **Design Representation** - Drawings/photographs showing design from all views (front, back, side, top, perspective)
5. **Statement of Novelty** - What is new/original
6. **Priority Claim** - If claiming foreign priority (6 months under Paris Convention)

**Locarno Classification:**
- International classification for industrial designs
- 32 classes (by type of article)

Examples:
- Class 06: Furnishing (furniture, mirrors, etc.)
- Class 09: Packages and containers
- Class 12: Means of transport

**Multiple Articles/Classes:**
- Single application can cover multiple designs for articles in SAME class
- Different classes → Separate applications

**Fee:**
- Application: Rs. 1,000 (individual/startup) | Rs. 4,000 (SME) | Rs. 8,000 (large entity)

**Filing Receipt:**
- Application number issued
- Priority date: Filing date

### Phase 3: Examination

**Formalities Check:**
- Application complete? (Form, fees, representations)
- Correct class?
- Prohibited matter?

**Substantive Examination (Novelty/Originality):**
- Prior art search by examiner
- Assess novelty

**Examination Report:**
- Objections raised (if any)
- Timeline: 3-6 months from filing

**Response:**
- Deadline: 6 MONTHS (non-extendable)
- Amend representations, statement of novelty
- Argue novelty/originality

**Refusal:**
- If not satisfied → Application refused
- No hearing in design examination (unlike patents/trademarks)

### Phase 4: Registration

**If No Objections OR Objections Resolved:**
- Design registered
- Certificate of Registration issued
- Published in Design Journal

**Rights Effective:**
- From date of registration (filing date)

**Term:**
- **10 YEARS** from registration
- **Extension:** Additional 5 YEARS (total 15 years max)
- Extension fee: Rs. 2,000 (individual/startup) | Rs. 8,000 (SME) | Rs. 16,000 (large entity)

**No Opposition Procedure:**
- Unlike patents/trademarks, no pre-grant or post-grant opposition
- Design directly registered after examination
- Challenges only via cancellation petition (Section 19)

## Rights and Infringement

### Section 11: Rights of Registered Proprietor

**Exclusive Rights:**

**(1) Apply Design to Article** - Only proprietor can apply design to article for which registered

**(2) Import/Sell/Publish Articles Bearing Design** - Prevent others from importing, selling, publishing articles with design

**Example:**
- Design for mobile phone casing registered
- Third party manufactures identical phone casing → Infringement

**Section 11(2) - Copyright in Registered Design:**

**Important Dual Protection Restriction:**

```
"Once design registered, NO copyright in features protected by design registration"
```

**Explanation:**
- Design and copyright mutually exclusive (for same features)
- If design registered → Cannot claim copyright for those features
- Prevents double protection

**Example:**
- Designer creates ornamental pattern for textile
- Registers design → Copyright in pattern extinguished (for that use)
- BUT if pattern also used in artwork (not industrial process) → Copyright may subsist

### Section 22: Piracy of Registered Design (Infringement)

**Piracy = Infringement**

**Occurs When:**

**(a) Fraudulent/Obvious Imitation of Registered Design Applied to Article in Same Class**
- Defendant's design is fraudulent OR obvious imitation
- Applied to article in same class

**Test for Imitation:**
- Compare designs (overall impression)
- Would informed user/buyer confuse?
- Exact copy not required (substantial similarity sufficient)

**Example:**
- Registered design: Ornamental vase with floral pattern
- Defendant's vase: Same shape + similar floral pattern (minor color change)
- Obvious imitation → Piracy

**(b) Importing Such Article for Sale**
- Importing infringing article → Piracy (even if not manufactured in India)

**Defense - Innocent Infringer:**
- Defendant did not know design registered
- No damages (but injunction granted)

**Section 22(2) - Prior Continuous Use Defense:**
- If defendant used design before plaintiff's registration
- Can continue use (prior user rights)

### Section 22A: Piracy as Criminal Offense

**Criminal Liability:**
- Piracy (knowing infringement) = Criminal offense
- **Imprisonment:** Up to 3 years
- **Fine:** Up to Rs. 50,000

**Conditions:**
- Intentional/knowing piracy
- First offense: Summary trial

**Example:**
- Manufacturer knowingly copies registered design
- Sells large quantities
- Criminal prosecution (in addition to civil suit)

### Remedies (Section 22(1))

**Civil Remedies:**

1. **Injunction** - Restrain further piracy
2. **Damages** - Compensatory damages OR
3. **Account of Profits** - Defendant's profits from piracy
4. **Delivery Up** - Infringing articles/tools destroyed

**Damages Calculation:**
- Proprietor's lost sales
- Reasonable royalty defendant should have paid

## Cancellation of Design (Section 19)

**Any Person Aggrieved Can Apply to Cancel Design:**

**Grounds:**

**(a) Previously Published** - Design not novel (prior publication before registration)

**(b) Not New/Original** - Design not new or original at registration date

**(c) Not Registrable** - Falls under Section 5 (scandalous, mere mechanical device, etc.)

**(d) Not a Design** - Does not meet Section 2(d) definition

**(e) Proprietor Not Entitled** - Applicant was not entitled (design belongs to applicant)

**Procedure:**
- Application to Controller
- Notice to proprietor
- Evidence by both sides
- Hearing
- Decision: Cancel OR Maintain

**Effect of Cancellation:**
- Design removed from register
- Treated as if never registered
- Prior actions for infringement may fail

## Design vs Copyright Overlap

### Section 15: Copyright in Design

**Complex Interaction:**

**Before Registration:**
- Design eligible for copyright protection (as artistic work)
- Copyright Act applies

**After Registration:**
- Design features protected by design registration → NO copyright (Section 11(2))
- Other uses (not industrial process) → Copyright may subsist

**Example:**
- Designer creates jewelry design
- Design registered → Design Act protection (industrial jewelry)
- Same design used in painting → Copyright protection (artistic work, not industrial)

**Section 15(2) - Limitation:**

If article to which design applied reproduced more than 50 times by industrial process → NO copyright in design (only design protection)

**Rationale:**
- Prevent overlap of design and copyright
- Industrial designs = Design Act
- Artistic works (not industrially reproduced >50 times) = Copyright

**Strategy:**
- For mass-produced products → Register design
- For limited art pieces → Rely on copyright

## International Design Protection

### Hague Agreement (Joined by India in 2019)

**Single Application to WIPO:**
- Designate multiple countries (EU, US, Japan, South Korea, etc.)
- Centralized filing, renewal, management

**Benefits:**
- Cost-effective (vs filing in each country separately)
- Simplified procedure
- One language, one currency

**Procedure:**
1. File international application with WIPO
2. WIPO examines formalities
3. Publishes in International Designs Bulletin
4. Each designated country examines (6-12 months)
5. Grant OR refuse in each country

**Term:**
- 5 years (renewable every 5 years, up to 15 years total)

### Paris Convention (6-Month Priority)

**File in India → 6 Months to File Abroad:**
- Claim India filing date as priority date
- Useful for securing worldwide protection

## Design Strategy

### 1. Selection - Protect Key Visual Features

**What to Protect:**
- Unique shape, pattern, ornamentation
- Product packaging design
- User interface (if visual features)

**What NOT to Protect:**
- Functional mechanisms (patent)
- Brand names/logos (trademark)

### 2. Timing - File BEFORE Public Disclosure

**NO Grace Period:**
- Any publication/sale before filing destroys novelty
- File design application BEFORE product launch

**Example:**
- Product displayed at trade show → Public disclosure
- File design application BEFORE trade show

### 3. Multiple Applications for Variations

**File Separate Applications:**
- Different design variations
- Different color combinations
- Different classes

**Example:**
- Furniture design in Class 06
- Textile pattern in Class 05 (separately)

### 4. Monitor and Enforce

**Monitor:**
- Market for copycat products
- Trade fairs, online marketplaces

**Enforce:**
- Send cease and desist
- File infringement suit
- Criminal complaint (if blatant piracy)

### Common Mistakes

**1. Public Disclosure Before Filing** → Destroys novelty

**2. Claiming Functional Features** → Not registrable (functional = patent)

**3. Not Filing in All Relevant Classes** → Limited protection

**4. Not Extending After 10 Years** → Design lapses

**5. Confusing Design with Trademark** → Trademark for brand, design for appearance

**6. Not Monitoring Market** → Copycat products flourish unchecked

**7. Delay in Filing Infringement Suit** → Damages increase, injunction delayed

**8. Expecting Automatic Copyright Protection After Registration** → Section 11(2) extinguishes copyright for industrial use

**9. Not Representing Design from All Views** → Incomplete protection

**10. Filing in Wrong Class** → May be refused OR limited protection
