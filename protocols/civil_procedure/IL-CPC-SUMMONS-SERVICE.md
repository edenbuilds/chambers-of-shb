# Protocol: Summons and Service (India)

**Protocol ID**: IL-CPC-SUMMONS-SERVICE  
**Domain**: Civil Procedure  
**Subdomain**: Pleadings and Initiation

**Last Updated**: 2025-12-06

## Purpose

This protocol provides comprehensive guidance on issuance of summons, modes of service, proof of service, and consequences of non-service under the Code of Civil Procedure 1908.

## Activation Triggers

- User asks "How is summons served in civil suits?"
- Defendant not served properly
- Need to effect service on defendant
- Service by publication required
- Defendant evading service
- Proof of service challenged
- Foreign defendant service needed

## Core Principles

### 1. Summons = Court's Command to Appear

```
Summons (Order V):
- Written notice issued by court
- Commands defendant to appear on specified date
- Accompanied by copy of plaint
- Defendant's first formal notice of suit

Rule: "No decree without service" (except ex parte after proper attempts)
→ Valid service prerequisite for binding decree
→ Irregular service → Decree liable to be set aside
```

### 2. Purpose of Service

```
1. Notice to Defendant:
   - Inform defendant of suit against him
   - Opportunity to defend

2. Jurisdiction:
   - Court's jurisdiction invoked by valid service
   - No valid service → No jurisdiction over defendant's person

3. Natural Justice:
   - Audi alteram partem (hear other side)
   - Defendant's right to be heard
```

## SUMMONS (Order V)

### Contents of Summons (Order V Rule 1)

```
Every summons shall:

1. Be signed by judge or presiding officer

2. Bear seal of court

3. Contain:
   (a) Name of court
   (b) Parties' names and descriptions
   (c) Suit number
   (d) Date of appearance
   (e) Time of appearance
   (f) Command to appear (in H.M. President's name if High Court/Supreme Court)

4. Be accompanied by:
   - Copy of plaint
   - Notice to defendant (Order V Rule 5)

5. Notice to defendant stating:
   - Time to file written statement (30 days)
   - Consequence of non-appearance (ex parte decree)
   - That defendant must appear by advocate or in person
```

**Form of Summons** (Appendix B, Form 1):

```
IN THE COURT OF [NAME OF COURT]

SUMMONS FOR APPEARANCE

Suit No. ____ of 20__

To,
[Name and address of defendant]

WHEREAS [Name of plaintiff], the plaintiff above named, has instituted a suit 
against you in this Court for [brief relief], the particulars whereof are shown 
in the annexed copy of plaint:

YOU ARE HEREBY SUMMONED to appear in this Court in person or by a pleader duly 
instructed and able to answer all material questions relating to the suit, on 
the ____ day of ____, 20__ at __ o'clock in the forenoon and to answer the 
claim, and take notice that, in default of your appearance on the said day, the 
suit will be heard and determined in your absence.

Given under my hand and the seal of the Court, this ____ day of ____, 20__.

                                                                 [Signature]
                                                                 Judge/Officer

Seal of Court
```

### When Summons Issued (Order V Rule 1)

```
Timeline:

1. Plaint Filed → Registered as suit → Suit number assigned

2. Court examines plaint:
   - If plaint in order → Issue summons
   - If defective → Order VII Rule 11 (reject) OR give time to correct

3. Summons prepared:
   - Signed by judge
   - Sealed
   - Copy of plaint annexed
   - Notice to defendant (Order V Rule 5) attached

4. Summons issued:
   - Delivered to process server OR registered post
   - Service attempted

Standard Practice:
- Summons issued within 7-15 days of plaint filing (varies by court)
- First date of appearance: Usually 30-60 days from issue (to allow service time)
```

## MODES OF SERVICE (Order V Rules 9-20)

### 1. Personal Service (Order V Rule 9, 10) - PRIMARY MODE

```
Rule 9: "Whenever it is practicable, service shall be made on the defendant in 
person by delivering or tendering to him the summons"

Procedure:

1. Process Server (Court employee):
   - Locates defendant
   - Identifies defendant personally
   - Delivers summons to defendant
   - Obtains signature on duplicate/acknowledgment

2. If Defendant Refuses to Accept:
   Rule 10: If defendant refuses to receive summons or refuses to sign 
   acknowledgment:
   → Affix original summons to outer door of house where defendant ordinarily resides
   → Service deemed effected

3. Proof of Service:
   - Affidavit by process server
   - OR defendant's signature on duplicate summons
   - OR endorsement on summons stating:
     * Date and time of service
     * Manner of service (personal delivery / refusal + affixture)
     * Signature and designation of server
```

**Valid Personal Service Requirements**:
```
1. Delivered to DEFENDANT personally (not family member/servant at this stage)
2. Server identifies defendant correctly
3. Copy of plaint accompanied summons
4. Server's affidavit/endorsement as proof
5. Service within jurisdiction (if outside, commission needed)
```

### 2. Service on Agent/Representative (Order V Rule 13-18)

```
If defendant cannot be personally served → Service on authorized agent

Rule 13: Service on Agent of Defendant

Who qualifies as agent?
- Agent expressly authorized to accept service
- Power of attorney holder (with service acceptance power)
- Manager of defendant's business
- Secretary of company (Order XXIX Rule 2)

Procedure:
- Deliver summons to agent
- Agent's acknowledgment sufficient
- Service deemed as service on defendant
```

### 3. Substituted Service (Order V Rule 20) - If Personal Service Impracticable

```
Rule 20: Where court is satisfied that service CANNOT be made in ordinary way:
→ Court may order service by:
   (a) Affixture on conspicuous part of defendant's house/last known residence
   (b) Publication in newspaper
   (c) Email/SMS to known email/mobile (if court permits)
   (d) Any other mode court deems fit

Conditions for Substituted Service:

1. Personal service attempted multiple times and failed
   - Usually 3-4 attempts required
   - Different times of day
   - Process server's report showing attempts

2. Defendant's whereabouts:
   - Address known but defendant evading service, OR
   - Address known but defendant not available despite attempts, OR
   - Defendant's address unknown (after due inquiry)

3. Court satisfaction:
   - Application by plaintiff with affidavit
   - Process server's report of attempts
   - Court orders substituted service mode

4. Proof:
   - Affidavit of affixture + photograph (if affixture)
   - Newspaper publication copy (if publication)
   - Email delivery report (if email)
```

**Procedure for Obtaining Substituted Service Order**:

```
Step 1: File Application under Order V Rule 20
- Application stating:
  * Personal service attempts made (dates, times)
  * Reason personal service failed
  * Defendant's last known address
  * Proposed mode of substituted service

Step 2: Annex Process Server's Report
- Report showing 3-4 unsuccessful attempts
- Dates and times of attempts
- Reason for failure (defendant not found / evading / address wrong)

Step 3: Court Hearing
- Court may:
  * Order one more attempt at personal service
  * Order substituted service (if satisfied)
  * Specify mode (affixture / publication / email / other)

Step 4: Effect Substituted Service
- As per court order
- Affidavit of compliance filed

Step 5: Service Deemed Complete
- On date specified in court order (usually 7-30 days from affixture/publication)
```

**Service by Publication** (Newspaper):

```
When Ordered:
- Defendant's whereabouts unknown despite diligent inquiry
- Defendant absconding/evading
- Large number of defendants (class suits)

Procedure:
1. Court orders publication in:
   - One English daily (national/state level)
   - One vernacular daily (local language)

2. Advertisement format:
   - Court name, suit number
   - Parties' names
   - Brief nature of claim
   - Command to appear on date
   - Consequence of non-appearance

3. Publication date + notice period:
   - Usually published once or twice (as court directs)
   - Notice period: 30 days from publication (or as court orders)

4. Proof:
   - Original newspaper copy (with publication visible)
   - Affidavit of publication by newspaper

Effect:
- Service deemed effective after notice period
- Defendant deemed to have knowledge of suit
```

### 4. Service by Registered Post (Order V Rule 9A) - ALTERNATIVE MODE

```
Rule 9A: Court may, in addition to personal service, order service by:
- Registered post with acknowledgment due
- Speed post
- Courier

Procedure:
1. Court directs postal service (in addition to or instead of personal service)
2. Copy of summons + plaint sent by registered post to defendant's address
3. Postal acknowledgment obtained
4. Acknowledgment filed in court

Proof of Service:
- Postal acknowledgment card signed by recipient
- OR endorsement by postal authority (if refused/not delivered)

Effect:
- If postal acknowledgment returned signed → Service complete on date of receipt
- If returned unserved (refused/address wrong) → Personal service or substituted service needed
```

### 5. Service on Government (Order XXVII Rule 3, 4)

```
Rule 3: Service on Union of India

Mode:
- Secretary of concerned Central Ministry/Department
- OR authorized officer

Address:
- Ministry's office address (New Delhi)
- Chief Secretary (if state concern)

Procedure:
1. Summons + plaint copy sent by registered post
2. OR delivered through advocate to Secretary's office
3. Acknowledgment obtained

Time to Appear:
- Minimum 60 days (extendable) - Rule 4

Rule 4: Service on State Government

Mode:
- Chief Secretary of State
- OR Secretary of concerned State Department

Time: 60 days minimum
```

### 6. Service on Company (Order XXIX Rule 2)

```
Mode of Service:
- Secretary of company
- OR Director of company
- OR Manager of principal/registered office

Address:
- Registered office (Section 12 Companies Act 2013)
- OR principal place of business

Procedure:
1. Deliver summons to secretary/director/manager
2. Personal service at registered office
3. Affidavit of server

Alternative:
- Registered post to registered office address
```

### 7. Service on Partnership Firm (Order XXX Rule 2)

```
Mode:
- Service on partner personally, OR
- Service at principal place of business of firm

If all partners cannot be served personally:
- Substituted service on firm (affixture at place of business + publication)
```

### 8. Service Outside Jurisdiction (Order V Rule 21-25)

```
Rule 21: Service in Another State (Within India)

Procedure:
1. Letter of Request (Commission):
   - Requesting court sends letter to court in defendant's state
   - Requesting service on defendant

2. Receiving Court:
   - Effects service as if suit in its own court
   - Returns proof of service

Rule 22: Service in Foreign Country

Procedure:
1. Through Central Government (Ministry of External Affairs):
   - Under Hague Service Convention (if applicable)
   - OR through diplomatic channels

2. Direct Service (if country permits):
   - Registered post to defendant's address abroad
   - Courier service

3. Proof:
   - Acknowledgment from foreign authority/defendant
   - Affidavit if served directly

Time:
- Extended time for appearance (usually 90-120 days)
```

## PROOF OF SERVICE (Order V Rule 16-19)

### Modes of Proof

```
1. Acknowledgment by Defendant (Rule 17):
   - Defendant signs duplicate summons
   - OR sends written acknowledgment
   → Best proof

2. Affidavit of Process Server (Rule 16):
   - Server's affidavit stating:
     * Date, time, place of service
     * How served (personal delivery / affixture / publication)
     * Identification of defendant (if personal)
   → Admissible as evidence (but rebuttable)

3. Registered Post Acknowledgment (Rule 19A):
   - Postal acknowledgment card signed by defendant
   → Proof of service

4. Return of Service (Rule 16):
   - Server's endorsement on summons
   - Stating manner and date of service
   → Court record
```

### Burden of Proof

```
Plaintiff's Duty:
- Prove service on defendant before obtaining ex parte decree
- File affidavit/acknowledgment showing service

Court's Duty:
- Satisfy itself that service effected before proceeding ex parte
- May order fresh service if proof inadequate

Defendant's Challenge:
- Can challenge service by showing:
  * No service received
  * Service not in accordance with CPC
  * Summons not accompanied by plaint copy
```

## CONSEQUENCES OF SERVICE/NON-SERVICE

### If Defendant Served and Appears (Order VIII)

```
Defendant's Options:

1. File Written Statement (within 30 days, extendable up to 90 days)
   - Admit/deny allegations
   - Raise defenses
   - Counterclaim/set-off

2. Appear on Hearing Date:
   - Through advocate or in person
   - Participate in proceedings

3. Challenge Jurisdiction:
   - File application under Order VII Rule 10
   - OR raise in written statement
```

### If Defendant Served but Does Not Appear (Order IX Rule 6)

```
Rule 6: Ex Parte Decree

If on hearing date defendant does not appear AND has been duly served:

Procedure:
1. Plaintiff proves service (affidavit + acknowledgment/postal receipt)
2. Court satisfied service effected
3. Plaintiff proves claim (evidence):
   - Examines witness(es)
   - Produces documents
4. Court passes ex parte decree if claim proved

Effect:
- Decree binding on defendant
- Defendant can apply to set aside (Rule 13) within 30 days if:
  * Summons not duly served, OR
  * Prevented by sufficient cause from appearing
```

### If Defendant Not Served (Order V Rule 12)

```
Rule 12: Proof of Service Required Before Ex Parte Decree

If defendant not served:
→ Court CANNOT pass ex parte decree
→ Must order fresh service OR substituted service

Plaintiff's options:
1. Attempt fresh service (personal/postal)
2. Apply for substituted service (Order V Rule 20)
3. If all modes exhausted and defendant not traceable → Suit may be dismissed for non-prosecution
```

## Setting Aside Ex Parte Decree (Order IX Rule 13)

```
Rule 13: Application to Set Aside Ex Parte Decree

Grounds:

1. Summons Not Duly Served:
   - Service not in accordance with CPC
   - No service at all
   - Summons not accompanied by plaint copy

2. Sufficient Cause Prevented Appearance:
   - Illness, accident, unavoidable circumstances
   - Court error in date/time

Time Limit: 30 days from knowledge of ex parte decree

Procedure:
1. File application with affidavit stating:
   - Grounds (non-service OR sufficient cause)
   - Defense on merits (not frivolous)

2. Court may:
   (a) Set aside ex parte decree + restore suit
   (b) Impose costs on defendant (for delay/non-appearance)
   (c) Dismiss application if no good cause shown

Effect of Setting Aside:
- Suit restored to board
- Defendant files written statement
- Trial proceeds as if ex parte not passed
```

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Defendant Evading Service

```
Facts:
- Process server visited defendant's residence 3 times
- Neighbors confirm defendant lives there
- Defendant not available each time (appears to be evading)

Solution:
Step 1: File application under Order V Rule 20 for substituted service
Step 2: Annex process server's report (3 unsuccessful attempts)
Step 3: Court orders substituted service by:
   - Affixture on outer door of residence
   - Publication in newspaper (if court deems fit)
Step 4: Affidavit of affixture filed (with photograph)
Step 5: Service deemed complete 7-30 days from affixture (as per court order)
```

### Scenario 2: Defendant's Address Unknown

```
Facts:
- Plaintiff knows defendant's name but not current address
- Last known address: Defendant vacated 2 years ago

Solution:
Step 1: Conduct due inquiry:
   - Neighbors at last known address
   - Mutual acquaintances
   - Public records (Aadhaar, PAN, voter ID - if accessible)

Step 2: File application for substituted service under Order V Rule 20:
   - State inquiry efforts made
   - Request service by publication in newspaper

Step 3: Court orders publication in:
   - English daily (national)
   - Vernacular daily (local)

Step 4: Advertisement published with suit details + appearance date

Step 5: Service deemed complete 30 days from publication
```

### Scenario 3: Service on Company

```
Facts:
- Defendant: ABC Private Limited
- Registered office: Mumbai
- No branch in Delhi (where suit filed)

Solution:
Mode of Service: Order XXIX Rule 2

Option 1: Personal Service at Registered Office
- Process server goes to Mumbai registered office
- Serves secretary/director/manager
- Obtains acknowledgment

Option 2: Registered Post
- Court sends summons by registered post to:
  Address: The Secretary, ABC Private Limited, [Registered Office Address], Mumbai
- Postal acknowledgment obtained

Option 3: Letter of Request (Commission)
- Delhi court sends letter of request to Mumbai court
- Mumbai court effects service
- Returns proof to Delhi court

Time: Company gets standard 30 days to file written statement
```

### Scenario 4: Service on Government

```
Facts:
- Suit against Union of India
- Cause of action: Contractual dispute with BSNL (Central PSU)

Solution:
Mode: Order XXVII Rule 3

Service On:
- Secretary, Department of Telecommunications, Government of India
- Address: Sanchar Bhawan, New Delhi

Procedure:
1. Summons + plaint copy sent by registered post
2. OR delivered through advocate to Secretary's office
3. Acknowledgment obtained

Time to Appear:
- Minimum 60 days from service (Rule 4)
- Can be extended on application by government

Note: Section 80 CPC notice (2 months prior) MANDATORY before suing government
```

### Scenario 5: Foreign Defendant

```
Facts:
- Defendant: UK resident
- Contract executed in India
- Defendant now in London

Solution:
Mode: Order V Rule 22

Option 1: Hague Service Convention (India and UK both signatories)
1. Court forwards summons to Central Authority (Ministry of External Affairs)
2. MEA sends to UK Central Authority
3. UK authority effects service
4. Proof of service returned through diplomatic channels

Option 2: Direct Service (if UK law permits)
1. Summons sent by registered post/courier to defendant's UK address
2. Acknowledgment obtained

Time: Extended time for appearance (90-120 days usual)

Jurisdiction Note:
- Ensure court has territorial jurisdiction (cause of action arose in India / defendant carries business in India / jurisdiction clause)
```

### Scenario 6: Service by Email/SMS

```
Facts:
- Personal service failed despite 4 attempts
- Defendant's email ID and mobile number known (from contract documents)

Solution:
Application under Order V Rule 20:
- Request substituted service by email + SMS
- Some courts permitting this mode (modern practice)

Procedure:
1. Court orders service by email to [defendant's email] + SMS to [defendant's mobile]
2. Summons + plaint copy sent via email (with read receipt)
3. SMS notification sent
4. Email delivery report + screenshot filed as proof

Service deemed complete: 7-15 days from email/SMS (as per court order)

Note: Not universally accepted; some courts prefer traditional modes (affixture + publication)
```

## Common Defects in Service

```
Defect 1: Summons Not Accompanied by Plaint Copy
Effect: Service invalid (Order V Rule 1 requirement)
Remedy: Fresh service with plaint copy

Defect 2: Service on Family Member Instead of Defendant
Effect: Not valid personal service (unless family member is authorized agent)
Remedy: Fresh personal service OR service on agent OR substituted service

Defect 3: No Proof of Service Filed
Effect: Court cannot proceed ex parte
Remedy: File affidavit/acknowledgment proving service

Defect 4: Substituted Service Without Court Order
Effect: Irregular service; decree liable to be set aside
Remedy: Obtain court order for substituted service before effecting

Defect 5: Insufficient Attempts Before Substituted Service
Effect: Court may refuse substituted service order
Remedy: Make 3-4 attempts at different times before applying for substituted service

Defect 6: Service Outside Jurisdiction Without Commission
Effect: Service invalid
Remedy: Obtain letter of request (commission) from court for service outside jurisdiction
```

## Case Law

### 1. Karam Chand Thapar v. CIT (SC 1969)
```
Facts: Service by affixture without prior attempts at personal service
Held:
- Substituted service is LAST RESORT
- Personal service must be attempted first (multiple times)
- Court must be satisfied personal service impracticable
- Substituted service without genuine attempts → Invalid
```

### 2. Umeshchandra Banerji v. Kunjilal Biswas (SC 1959)
```
Facts: Summons served on defendant's son (adult) at residence
Held:
- Service on family member NOT valid personal service (unless authorized agent)
- Order V Rule 9: Service shall be on defendant IN PERSON
- If defendant refuses, then affix on outer door
- Service on family member without defendant's refusal → Invalid
```

### 3. Pana Lal v. State of Rajasthan (SC 1975)
```
Facts: Substituted service by affixture on outer door without attempts at personal service
Held:
- Order V Rule 20: Court must be satisfied personal service impracticable
- "Impracticable" = Cannot be done after reasonable efforts
- Affixture without attempts → Irregular service
- Decree based on such service → Liable to be set aside
```

### 4. Hussainara Khatoon v. State of Bihar (SC 1979)
```
Facts: Service on defendant through registered post; acknowledgment not returned
Held:
- Registered post service (Order V Rule 19A) valid mode
- But proof of service required (postal acknowledgment)
- If acknowledgment not returned → Service not proved
- Cannot pass ex parte decree without proof
```

### 5. Roshan Lal Sood v. Rajiv Suri (SC 2016)
```
Facts: Service by courier; proof of service challenged
Held:
- Courier service permissible if court so orders (Order V Rule 20)
- Proof of service: Delivery report + signature of recipient
- If proper proof → Service valid
- Modern modes (email, courier) acceptable with court permission
```

### 6. Samir Narain Bhojwani v. Aurora Properties (SC 2018)
```
Facts: Ex parte decree set aside; defendant claimed no service
Held:
- Order IX Rule 13: Defendant can challenge service within 30 days
- Burden on plaintiff to prove service (affidavit + acknowledgment)
- If defendant shows no service received → Court may set aside ex parte decree
- Natural justice requires actual notice to defendant
```

## Summary Checklist

### Service Checklist:

```
Before Attempting Service:
☐ Summons signed by judge and sealed
☐ Copy of plaint annexed to summons
☐ Notice to defendant (Order V Rule 5) attached
☐ Defendant's correct address verified

Personal Service (Primary Mode):
☐ Process server locates defendant
☐ Identifies defendant personally
☐ Delivers summons + plaint copy
☐ Obtains acknowledgment OR notes refusal
☐ Files affidavit of service

If Personal Service Fails:
☐ Make 3-4 attempts at different times
☐ Server reports inability to serve
☐ File application for substituted service (Order V Rule 20)
☐ Court orders substituted mode (affixture / publication / email)
☐ Effect substituted service as per court order
☐ File proof of compliance

Proof of Service:
☐ Affidavit by process server filed
☐ OR postal acknowledgment filed
☐ OR defendant's written acknowledgment filed
☐ Court satisfied service effected

If Defendant Does Not Appear:
☐ Prove service to court (affidavit + acknowledgment)
☐ Proceed ex parte
☐ Plaintiff proves claim
☐ Ex parte decree obtained
```

## Common Mistakes

```
Mistake 1: Attempting Substituted Service Without Court Order
❌ Server affixes summons on door without court permission
✓ Apply to court for substituted service order FIRST, then affix

Mistake 2: Service on Family Member Treated as Personal Service
❌ Summons delivered to defendant's wife; treated as served on defendant
✓ Service on family member NOT valid personal service (unless authorized agent)

Mistake 3: No Proof of Service Filed Before Ex Parte
❌ Court passes ex parte decree without affidavit of service
✓ File affidavit of service before court proceeds ex parte

Mistake 4: Insufficient Attempts Before Substituted Service Application
❌ One unsuccessful attempt → Apply for substituted service
✓ Make 3-4 attempts at different times before applying

Mistake 5: Service Outside Jurisdiction Without Commission
❌ Delhi court's process server goes to Mumbai to serve defendant
✓ Obtain letter of request (commission) to Mumbai court for service

Mistake 6: Summons Not Accompanied by Plaint Copy
❌ Server delivers only summons (no plaint copy)
✓ Summons MUST be accompanied by plaint copy (Order V Rule 1)

Mistake 7: No Follow-Up on Returned Postal Service
❌ Registered post summons returned unserved; no further action
✓ If postal service fails → Attempt personal service OR substituted service
```
