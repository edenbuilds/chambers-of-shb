# Protocol: Plaint and Written Statement (India)

**Protocol ID**: IL-CPC-PLAINT-WRITTEN-STATEMENT  
**Domain**: Civil Procedure  
**Subdomain**: Pleadings and Initiation  
**Version**: 1.0.0  
**Last Updated**: 2025-12-06

## Purpose

This protocol provides comprehensive guidance on drafting plaints and written statements under the Code of Civil Procedure 1908, covering mandatory requirements, common defects, and strategic considerations.

## Activation Triggers

- User asks "How to draft a plaint?"
- Need to file a civil suit
- Plaint rejected (Order VII Rule 11)
- Drafting written statement
- Raising defenses and counterclaims
- Plaint/written statement amendment needed

## Core Principles

### 1. Plaint = Foundation of Suit

```
Plaint (Order VII):
- Written statement of claim
- Plaintiff's case in chief
- ALL material facts (not evidence, not law)
- Determines reliefs plaintiff entitled to seek

Rule: "Court decides suit based on pleadings"
→ If fact not pleaded → Cannot lead evidence on it
→ If relief not claimed → Court cannot grant it
```

### 2. Written Statement = Defendant's Defense

```
Written Statement (Order VIII):
- Defendant's response to plaint
- Admit/deny each allegation
- Raise defenses
- Counterclaim/set-off (if applicable)

Rule: "What is not denied is deemed admitted" (Order VIII Rule 3)
→ Defendant must specifically traverse each allegation
→ General denial insufficient
```

## PLAINT (Order VII)

### Mandatory Contents (Order VII Rule 1)

```
Every plaint shall contain:

1. Name of Court (Rule 1(a))
2. Name, Description, Residence of Plaintiff (Rule 1(b))
3. Name, Description, Residence of Defendant (Rule 1(c))
   - If defendant's residence unknown: State so
4. Facts Constituting Cause of Action (Rule 1(d))
   - WHEN cause of action arose
   - WHERE cause of action arose (jurisdiction)
5. Facts Showing Court Has Jurisdiction (Rule 1(e))
   - Pecuniary jurisdiction
   - Territorial jurisdiction (Section 20 CPC)
6. Relief Claimed (Rule 1(f))
   - Specific relief sought
   - Alternative relief (if applicable)
   - Interest, if claimed
7. Valuation of Suit (Rule 1(g))
   - For court fees (Court Fees Act 1870)
   - For pecuniary jurisdiction
8. Verification (Rule 1(i))
   - By plaintiff
   - Date and signature

Optional but Recommended:
- Statement of material facts (Rule 1(h))
- Documents relied upon (Rule 14)
```

### Detailed Requirements

#### A. Parties (Rule 1(b), (c))

```
Plaintiff Description:
- Full name: [First name] [Middle name] [Last name]
- Age: [If minor, next friend required]
- Occupation: [Relevant if affects standing]
- Residence: Complete address with pin code

Defendant Description:
- Full name (as accurately known)
- Age (if known)
- Occupation
- Residence: Complete address
  * If unknown: State "Defendant's address unknown" (then personal service waived - substituted service allowed)

Multiple Plaintiffs/Defendants:
- Number each: Plaintiff No. 1, 2, 3...
- Describe each separately
```

**Example**:
```
THE HON'BLE COURT OF CIVIL JUDGE (SENIOR DIVISION), DELHI

SUIT NO. ____ OF 2025

Plaintiff:
Ramesh Kumar, aged 45 years, S/o Late Suresh Kumar, Businessman, residing 
at House No. 123, Sector 45, Noida, Uttar Pradesh - 201301

Versus

Defendant:
Suresh Enterprises Private Limited, a company incorporated under the Companies 
Act 2013, having its registered office at 456 MG Road, Bangalore, Karnataka - 560001
```

#### B. Cause of Action (Rule 1(d))

```
Must state:
1. Material facts (not evidence)
2. Chronological narrative
3. WHEN each fact occurred (dates)
4. WHERE each fact occurred (places - for jurisdiction)

Material Facts vs Evidence vs Law:

Material Facts (MUST plead):
- Plaintiff and defendant entered contract on DD/MM/YYYY
- Consideration: Rs. X
- Defendant breached by failing to deliver goods
- Plaintiff suffered loss Rs. Y

Evidence (DO NOT plead):
- "I have a copy of the contract" → NO (produce at trial)
- "Witness ABC will testify" → NO (evidence stage)

Law (Generally NOT pleaded, court knows law):
- "Under Section 73 Indian Contract Act, damages recoverable" → NO
- Exception: Foreign law must be pleaded and proved
```

**Example**:
```
CAUSE OF ACTION:

1. On 15/01/2024, at Mumbai, the Plaintiff and Defendant entered into a 
   written agreement for sale of 1000 widgets at Rs. 500 per widget, total 
   consideration Rs. 5,00,000/-.

2. The Defendant agreed to deliver the widgets to the Plaintiff's warehouse 
   in Delhi by 28/02/2024.

3. The Plaintiff paid advance of Rs. 1,00,000/- vide cheque no. 123456 dated 
   20/01/2024, which was encashed by the Defendant on 25/01/2024.

4. The Defendant failed to deliver any widgets by 28/02/2024 or thereafter.

5. The Plaintiff issued legal notice dated 05/03/2024 calling upon the Defendant 
   to perform the contract, which was received by the Defendant on 10/03/2024.

6. The Defendant replied vide letter dated 15/03/2024 refusing to perform the 
   contract without lawful excuse.

7. The Plaintiff has suffered loss and damage amounting to Rs. 2,00,000/- being 
   the difference between contract price and market price, and Rs. 1,00,000/- 
   advance paid.

8. Cause of action arose at Mumbai (where contract entered), Delhi (where goods 
   to be delivered), and continues.
```

#### C. Jurisdiction (Rule 1(e))

```
MUST specifically state facts conferring jurisdiction:

Pecuniary:
"The suit value is Rs. ____, which is within the pecuniary jurisdiction of 
this Hon'ble Court as per [State notification/Court's jurisdiction limit]."

Territorial (Section 20):
"The Defendant resides/carries on business at [place] within the territorial 
jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court."
OR
"Cause of action arose wholly/in part at [place] within the territorial 
jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court, as evidenced by the following facts: [...]"

If Section 16 (immovable property):
"The suit immovable property is situated at [place] within the territorial 
jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court."
```

#### D. Relief Claimed (Rule 1(f))

```
Specific Relief:
State exactly what plaintiff wants court to grant

Types:
1. Money Decree:
   "a) Decree for Rs. ____ with interest @ ___% p.a. from DD/MM/YYYY till realization"

2. Specific Performance:
   "a) Decree for specific performance of the contract dated DD/MM/YYYY directing 
   the Defendant to execute sale deed in favor of Plaintiff for the suit property"

3. Injunction:
   "a) Perpetual injunction restraining the Defendant from [specific act]"

4. Declaration:
   "a) Declaration that the Plaintiff is the absolute owner of the suit property"

5. Possession:
   "a) Decree for possession of the suit property"

6. Partition:
   "a) Decree for partition of the suit property and separate possession of 
   Plaintiff's 1/3rd share"

Alternative Relief:
"b) In the alternative, decree for damages amounting to Rs. ____"

General Relief:
"c) Costs of the suit"
"d) Any other relief the Hon'ble Court deems fit"

Interest:
If claiming interest, MUST specify:
- Rate (percentage)
- From date (typically breach date or suit institution date)
- Till realization (pendente lite + future)
```

**Example**:
```
PRAYER:

In light of the above facts, the Plaintiff humbly prays that this Hon'ble Court 
be pleased to:

a) Decree in favor of the Plaintiff and against the Defendant for recovery of 
   Rs. 3,00,000/- (Rupees Three Lakhs only) being the advance paid (Rs. 1,00,000/-) 
   and damages suffered (Rs. 2,00,000/-);

b) Award interest @ 18% per annum on the decretal amount from the date of suit 
   till realization;

c) Award costs of the suit to the Plaintiff; and

d) Pass such other orders as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the 
   circumstances of the case.

AND FOR THIS ACT OF KINDNESS, THE PLAINTIFF SHALL DUTY BOUND FOREVER PRAY.
```

#### E. Valuation (Rule 1(g) + Court Fees Act 1870)

```
Purpose:
1. Court fees calculation
2. Pecuniary jurisdiction determination

Valuation Rules (Court Fees Act Sections 7-11):

Money Suit:
Amount claimed (principal + interest due, NOT future interest)

Immovable Property:
Market value at suit institution

Specific Performance:
Consideration amount in contract

Injunction:
Value of property/right protected

Partition:
Plaintiff's share value (NOT whole property value)

Declaration:
Market value of property (if property involved)

Multiple Reliefs:
Value of relief giving jurisdiction (NOT aggregate)

Example:
Suit for Rs. 5 lakhs (money) + specific performance of land worth Rs. 20 lakhs
→ Valuation: Rs. 20 lakhs (higher relief)
→ Court fees on Rs. 20 lakhs
```

#### F. Verification (Rule 1(i) + Rule 15)

```
Verification Clause:

"VERIFICATION

I, [Name of Plaintiff], the Plaintiff above named, do hereby verify that the 
contents of paragraphs 1 to ___ of the above plaint are true to my personal 
knowledge, and the contents of paragraphs ___ to ___ are based on legal advice 
and I believe them to be true."

Place: [City]
Date: [DD/MM/YYYY]

                                                         [Signature of Plaintiff]

Note:
- Verification by plaintiff (not advocate)
- Personal knowledge vs legal advice distinction
- Date and place mandatory
- False verification → Prosecution for perjury
```

#### G. Documents (Order VII Rule 14)

```
Rule 14: Documents relied upon must be annexed or produced with plaint

Categories:
1. Documents in plaintiff's possession → ANNEX as exhibits
2. Documents in defendant's possession → MENTION in plaint, seek discovery later
3. Documents in third party's possession → MENTION, seek commission to produce

Annexure:
"Annex P-1: Copy of Sale Agreement dated 15/01/2024"
"Annex P-2: Copy of Cheque No. 123456 dated 20/01/2024"
"Annex P-3: Copy of Legal Notice dated 05/03/2024"

Certified Copies:
- Public documents: Certified copies (if originals with government)
- Private documents: Original or certified copy
```

### Rejection of Plaint (Order VII Rule 11)

```
Court SHALL reject plaint if:

(a) Suit appears from plaint to be BARRED BY LAW
    Example: Limitation expired, jurisdiction clause violated

(b) RELIEF CLAIMED IS UNDERVALUED and plaintiff fails to correct within time
    Example: Court fees short-paid due to undervaluation

(c) Plaint not VERIFIED as required
    Example: Verification missing or defective

(d) Facts do not disclose CAUSE OF ACTION
    Example: Plaint shows no legal right violated

(e) Suit appears from plaint to be BARRED by res judicata
    Example: Same matter already decided between parties

(f) Suit appears from plaint to be BARRED by limitation (if clear from plaint)
    Example: Plaint shows suit filed after 3 years limitation

(g) Suit appears from plaint to be BARRED by agreement (arbitration clause)
    Example: Mandatory arbitration clause exists

Rejection Effect:
- Plaint returned to plaintiff
- Can file fresh suit (limitation saved if filed within prescribed time)
- Appeal: Against rejection under Order VII Rule 11 → Appeal under Order 43 Rule 1(a)
```

### Return of Plaint (Order VII Rule 10)

```
Court MAY return plaint for presentation to proper court if:
- Suit filed in court lacking jurisdiction
- Proper court identifiable

Difference from Rejection:
- Rejection (Rule 11): Plaint defective, cannot be cured by re-filing in same court
- Return (Rule 10): Jurisdiction lacking, can file in proper court

Effect:
- Limitation saved if re-filed in proper court within time prescribed (Section 14 Limitation Act)
```

## WRITTEN STATEMENT (Order VIII)

### Mandatory Contents (Order VIII Rule 1)

```
Defendant shall, within 30 days from service of summons (extendable up to 90 days 
from service), file written statement containing:

1. Admission or Denial of Each Allegation (Rule 3)
   - Specific traversal required
   - General denial insufficient

2. Affirmative Defenses (Rule 2)
   - New facts in defendant's favor
   - Example: Payment made, limitation, res judicata

3. Counterclaim (Rule 6A) - Optional
   - Defendant's own claim against plaintiff

4. Set-Off (Rule 6) - Optional
   - Defendant's claim for debt/money against plaintiff's claim

5. Verification (Rule 5)
   - By defendant
   - Similar to plaint verification
```

### Detailed Requirements

#### A. Specific Traversal (Order VIII Rule 3)

```
Rule 3: "Every allegation of fact in the plaint, if not denied specifically or 
by necessary implication, or stated to be not admitted, shall be taken to be 
admitted."

Effect: "WHAT IS NOT DENIED IS DEEMED ADMITTED"

Modes of Denial:

1. Specific Denial:
   Plaint: "Plaintiff paid Rs. 1 lakh on 15/01/2024"
   Written Statement: "Contents of para ___ are denied. Plaintiff did not pay 
   any amount on 15/01/2024 or at all."

2. Denial with Alternative Fact:
   Plaint: "Defendant breached contract by not delivering goods"
   Written Statement: "Contents of para ___ are denied. The contract was 
   frustrated due to force majeure event (pandemic) beyond defendant's control."

3. Denial Due to Lack of Knowledge:
   Plaint: "Defendant has 10 branches across India"
   Written Statement: "Defendant has no knowledge of the contents of para ___ 
   and therefore denies the same."

4. Not Admitted (Requiring Plaintiff to Prove):
   Plaint: "Market value of land is Rs. 50 lakhs"
   Written Statement: "Contents of para ___ are not admitted and plaintiff is 
   put to strict proof thereof."

General Denial (INSUFFICIENT):
❌ "All contents of plaint are denied" → INVALID
✓ "Contents of para 1, 2, 3, 5, 7 are denied. Para 4 and 6 are admitted."
```

#### B. Affirmative Defenses (Order VIII Rule 2)

```
Defendant must plead new facts showing:
- Plaintiff's claim defeated/reduced
- Defendant has valid excuse

Types:

1. Limitation (Section 3 Limitation Act 1963):
   "The suit is barred by limitation under Article ___ of the Limitation Act 
   1963, as the cause of action arose on DD/MM/YYYY and the suit was filed on 
   DD/MM/YYYY, after expiry of ___ years prescribed period."

2. Res Judicata (Section 11 CPC):
   "The suit is barred by res judicata, as the same matter was decided in Suit 
   No. ____ by [Court] vide judgment dated DD/MM/YYYY."

3. Payment/Satisfaction:
   "The defendant has already paid Rs. ___ vide cheque no. ____ dated DD/MM/YYYY, 
   which extinguishes plaintiff's claim."

4. Waiver/Acquiescence:
   "The plaintiff waived his right by continuing to perform the contract after 
   becoming aware of the alleged breach."

5. Estoppel:
   "The plaintiff is estopped from claiming [relief] as he represented to the 
   defendant on DD/MM/YYYY that [representation], and the defendant acted on 
   such representation to his detriment."

6. Accord and Satisfaction:
   "The parties mutually agreed on DD/MM/YYYY to settle all disputes by payment 
   of Rs. ____, which was paid and accepted in full and final settlement."

7. Force Majeure/Frustration:
   "The contract became impossible to perform due to [event] on DD/MM/YYYY, 
   frustrating the contract under Section 56 Indian Contract Act."

8. Jurisdiction Objection:
   "This Hon'ble Court lacks territorial jurisdiction as the defendant does not 
   reside/carry business within the jurisdiction, nor did any part of cause of 
   action arise here."
```

#### C. Counterclaim (Order VIII Rule 6A)

```
Counterclaim = Defendant's independent claim against plaintiff arising out of 
same/connected transaction

Conditions:
1. Must arise out of SAME transaction OR connected transaction
2. Must be within court's jurisdiction (pecuniary + territorial)

Procedure:
- Include in written statement as separate section "COUNTERCLAIM"
- Treat as separate suit (plaintiff becomes defendant to counterclaim)
- Plaintiff files reply to counterclaim (within 30 days)

Effect:
- Both claim and counterclaim tried together
- Can result in mutual set-off
- If counterclaim exceeds claim → Defendant gets decree for difference

Example:
Plaintiff's Claim: Rs. 5 lakhs for goods sold
Defendant's Counterclaim: Rs. 7 lakhs for defective goods causing loss

Result: If both proved → Decree for defendant for Rs. 2 lakhs (7 - 5)
```

#### D. Set-Off (Order VIII Rule 6)

```
Set-Off = Defendant's claim for ASCERTAINED SUM against plaintiff's claim

Conditions:
1. Ascertained sum (not unliquidated damages)
2. Legally recoverable from plaintiff
3. Defendant entitled at suit institution

Types:

Legal Set-Off (Rule 6):
- Ascertained sum
- Recoverable as debt
- Example: Plaintiff claims Rs. 5 lakhs; Defendant has decree against plaintiff for Rs. 2 lakhs
  → Set-off Rs. 2 lakhs → Decree for Rs. 3 lakhs to plaintiff

Equitable Set-Off (English law origin, recognized in India):
- Claims arising from same transaction
- Inequitable to allow plaintiff to recover without set-off
- Example: Sale contract - Plaintiff claims price; Defendant claims damages for defect in same goods

Procedure:
- Plead in written statement
- Give notice of set-off (details of claim)
- Court adjusts decretal amount
```

#### E. Verification (Order VIII Rule 5)

```
Verification Clause (similar to plaint):

"VERIFICATION

I, [Name of Defendant], the Defendant above named, do hereby verify that the 
contents of paragraphs 1 to ___ of the above written statement are true to my 
personal knowledge, and the contents of paragraphs ___ to ___ are based on 
legal advice and I believe them to be true."

Place: [City]
Date: [DD/MM/YYYY]

                                                       [Signature of Defendant]
```

### Time Limit for Written Statement (Order VIII Rule 1)

```
Original Time: 30 days from service of summons

Extension: Court MAY extend up to 90 days from service (with recording reasons)

Post-90 Days: Court SHALL NOT allow written statement (MANDATORY)
(Amendment 2002 - Section 26, CPC Amendment Act 2002)

Recent SC Judgment - Salem Advocate Bar Association (2005):
- Strict adherence to 90-day outer limit
- Extension beyond 90 days ONLY in EXCEPTIONAL circumstances
- Delay defeats entire purpose of CPC amendments for speedy disposal

Consequences of Non-Filing:
- Defendant loses right to file written statement
- Plaintiff's allegations deemed admitted (Order VIII Rule 10)
- Ex parte decree against defendant (Order IX Rule 6)
```

### Defendant's Failure to Appear (Order IX)

```
Rule 6: If defendant does not appear on hearing day AND has not filed written 
statement:
→ Plaintiff may prove claim
→ Court passes EX PARTE decree

Rule 7: If defendant appears but does not file written statement:
→ Allegations deemed admitted (Order VIII Rule 10)
→ Plaintiff needs minimal proof
→ Decree likely

Rule 8: If defendant files written statement but does not appear on hearing:
→ Court may proceed with trial
→ Plaintiff proves case
→ Decree passed

Setting Aside Ex Parte Decree (Rule 13):
Defendant can apply to set aside if:
- Summons not duly served, OR
- Prevented by sufficient cause from appearing

Time: Within 30 days from knowledge of decree
Procedure: Application + affidavit showing cause
Court may set aside and restore suit (if satisfied)
```

## Amendments (Order VI Rule 17)

### Plaint/Written Statement Amendment

```
Rule 17: Court may allow amendment at any stage to determine real questions in controversy

Conditions:

Before Trial Commences:
- Liberal approach
- Amendment allowed unless:
  * Changes cause of action completely
  * Introduces new parties beyond limitation
  * Defeats justice

After Trial Commences:
- Restrictive approach
- Amendment allowed ONLY if:
  * Necessary for determining real controversy
  * No prejudice to other party (OR compensate by costs/adjournment)
  * Not changing cause of action fundamentally

After Decree:
- Very narrow
- Correction of clerical/arithmetical errors only
- NOT substantive amendment

Limitation Considerations (Section 14 Limitation Act):
If amendment introduces new claim beyond limitation:
→ Amendment DENIED (unless relates back to original institution date)
```

**Case Law - Pirgonda Hongonda Patil v. Kalgonda Shidgonda Patil (SC 1957)**:
```
"The object of the provision is that the court should try the merits of the case 
that come before it and, therefore, amendments should be allowed to facilitate a 
decision on merits."

BUT NOT if:
- Opposite party prejudiced beyond compensation by costs
- Fundamentally alters case
- Introduces time-barred claim
```

## Common Defects and Remedies

### Defects in Plaint

```
Defect 1: Cause of Action Not Disclosed
Consequence: Rejection under Order VII Rule 11(d)
Remedy: Amend plaint to add material facts showing legal right violated

Defect 2: Jurisdiction Not Shown
Consequence: Rejection/Return under Order VII Rule 10-11
Remedy: Add specific averments showing pecuniary + territorial jurisdiction

Defect 3: Relief Undervalued
Consequence: Rejection under Order VII Rule 11(b) if court fees short
Remedy: Correct valuation + pay additional court fees within time given by court

Defect 4: Verification Defective
Consequence: Rejection under Order VII Rule 11(c)
Remedy: File corrected verification (if court allows)

Defect 5: Suit Barred by Limitation (Clear from Plaint)
Consequence: Rejection under Order VII Rule 11(f)
Remedy: Plead Section 5 Limitation Act (condonation) + show sufficient cause for delay

Defect 6: Documents Not Annexed
Consequence: Plaintiff cannot rely on documents not annexed/mentioned (Order XIII Rule 1)
Remedy: Seek leave to produce additional documents (with explanation for non-production)
```

### Defects in Written Statement

```
Defect 1: General Denial (Not Specific)
Consequence: Allegations deemed admitted (Order VIII Rule 3)
Remedy: Amend written statement to specifically traverse each allegation

Defect 2: Affirmative Defense Not Pleaded
Consequence: Cannot lead evidence on defense not pleaded (Order XIV Rule 2)
Remedy: Seek amendment to add defense (if trial not commenced)

Defect 3: Filed Beyond 90 Days
Consequence: Court SHALL NOT allow (Order VIII Rule 1 proviso)
Remedy: Show exceptional circumstances OR accept consequences (deemed admission)

Defect 4: Counterclaim Beyond Jurisdiction
Consequence: Counterclaim rejected/tried separately
Remedy: Withdraw counterclaim from written statement, file independent suit

Defect 5: Verification Missing
Consequence: Written statement liable to be struck off
Remedy: File verification immediately
```

## Strategic Considerations

### Drafting Plaint - Best Practices

```
1. Chronological Narrative:
   - Facts in date order
   - Clear timeline
   - Cause of action easily identifiable

2. Precision in Parties:
   - Correct legal names (especially companies)
   - Accurate addresses (for service)
   - Capacity clearly stated

3. Jurisdiction Emphasis:
   - Detailed averments on territorial jurisdiction
   - Multiple grounds (Section 20(a) + (c))
   - Anticipate jurisdiction challenge

4. Relief Clarity:
   - Specific relief sought
   - Alternative relief (if primary may fail)
   - Interest clearly claimed (rate + period)

5. Documents:
   - Annex all key documents
   - Mention documents in defendant's possession (for discovery)
   - Mark exhibits consistently (P-1, P-2...)

6. Valuation:
   - Accurate (not undervalued)
   - Court fees correctly calculated
   - Jurisdiction confirmed

7. Anticipate Defenses:
   - Address potential defenses in plaint itself
   - Example: If limitation issue, plead Section 5 condonation grounds
```

### Drafting Written Statement - Best Practices

```
1. Specific Traversal:
   - Admit what is true
   - Deny specifically what is false
   - Put to proof what is uncertain

2. Affirmative Defenses Early:
   - Jurisdiction objection (first defense)
   - Limitation (if applicable)
   - Res judicata, waiver, estoppel

3. Counterclaim Strategy:
   - If strong counterclaim → Include (deters plaintiff)
   - If exceeds plaintiff's claim → Negotiation leverage
   - If weak → Avoid (plaintiff gets discovery of defendant's case)

4. Documents:
   - Annex documents proving defense
   - Example: Payment receipts, settlement agreements, notices

5. Verification Careful:
   - True to knowledge vs legal advice distinction
   - No false verification (perjury risk)

6. Timeline Adherence:
   - File within 30 days (get extension if needed)
   - NEVER exceed 90 days (fatal)
```

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Suit for Recovery of Money

```
Plaint Structure:

Parties: Lender (Plaintiff) vs Borrower (Defendant)

Cause of Action:
1. Loan agreement dated DD/MM/YYYY at [place]
2. Loan amount Rs. ____ advanced on DD/MM/YYYY
3. Repayment due DD/MM/YYYY
4. Default on DD/MM/YYYY
5. Legal notice dated DD/MM/YYYY served
6. No response/refusal to pay

Jurisdiction:
- Defendant resides at [place] (Section 20(a))
- Cause of action arose at [place where loan advanced] + [place of repayment] (Section 20(c))

Relief:
- Decree for Rs. ____ (principal + interest @ __% from DD/MM/YYYY till realization)
- Costs

Valuation: Rs. ____ (principal + interest DUE, not future interest)

Annexures:
- Loan agreement (P-1)
- Advance payment proof (P-2)
- Legal notice (P-3)
- Postal acknowledgment (P-4)
```

### Scenario 2: Suit for Specific Performance

```
Plaint Structure:

Parties: Buyer (Plaintiff) vs Seller (Defendant)

Cause of Action:
1. Sale agreement dated DD/MM/YYYY for property at [address]
2. Consideration Rs. ____, advance Rs. ____ paid
3. Defendant agreed to execute sale deed by DD/MM/YYYY
4. Plaintiff ready and willing to pay balance
5. Defendant refusing to execute without lawful excuse
6. Legal notice served, no response

Jurisdiction (Section 16 - MANDATORY):
- Suit property situated at [place] within jurisdiction of this court

Relief:
- Primary: Specific performance of contract dated DD/MM/YYYY
- Alternative: Refund of advance Rs. ____ + damages Rs. ____
- Possession after sale deed execution
- Costs

Valuation: Rs. ____ (consideration amount in sale agreement)

Annexures:
- Sale agreement (P-1)
- Advance payment receipt (P-2)
- Property documents (P-3)
- Legal notice (P-4)
```

### Scenario 3: Written Statement with Counterclaim

```
Plaint: Seller sues Buyer for Rs. 10 lakhs (price of goods sold)

Written Statement Structure:

Admissions:
Para 1, 2 admitted (parties and contract)

Denials:
Para 3 denied - Goods were not delivered on DD/MM/YYYY
Para 5 denied - Quality of goods delivered was defective

Affirmative Defense:
- Goods delivered were not as per specifications
- Plaintiff breached contract by delivering defective goods
- Plaintiff not entitled to price

Counterclaim:
- Defective goods caused loss to defendant
- Defendant had to purchase replacement goods from market at Rs. 12 lakhs
- Defendant suffered loss Rs. 2 lakhs (difference)
- Defendant entitled to damages Rs. 2 lakhs

Counterclaim Relief:
- Dismiss plaintiff's suit
- Decree for defendant on counterclaim for Rs. 2 lakhs
- Costs
```

## Case Law

### 1. Urmila Devi v. Yashpal Singh (SC 2022)
```
Facts: Written statement filed beyond 90 days
Held:
- 90-day limit MANDATORY (Order VIII Rule 1 proviso post-2002 amendment)
- Extension beyond 90 days ONLY in EXCEPTIONAL circumstances
- Mere negligence/busy schedule NOT sufficient cause
```

### 2. Virendra Kashinath v. Vinayak N. Joshi (SC 2019)
```
Facts: Plaint amendment sought to introduce new cause of action after limitation
Held:
- Amendment cannot introduce time-barred claim
- Unless amendment "relates back" to original institution date
- New cause of action beyond limitation → Amendment denied
```

### 3. Rajesh Kumar Aggarwal v. K.K. Modi (SC 2006)
```
Facts: Plaint rejected under Order VII Rule 11(d) for not disclosing cause of action
Held:
- Cause of action = Bundle of facts entitling plaintiff to relief
- Court sees plaint ALONE (not documents/evidence)
- If plaint shows no legal right violated → Rejection proper
```

### 4. Devi Prasad v. Delhi Administration (SC 1973)
```
Facts: Verification defective (advocate verified, not party)
Held:
- Verification by party MANDATORY (not advocate)
- Defective verification → Plaint liable to rejection (Order VII Rule 11(c))
- Can be cured if court allows
```

### 5. Sangram Singh v. Election Tribunal (SC 1955)
```
Facts: General denial in written statement
Held:
- Order VIII Rule 3: What not denied deemed admitted
- General denial INSUFFICIENT
- Specific traversal of each allegation REQUIRED
```

```
☐ Court name
☐ Parties (full name, age, occupation, residence)
☐ Cause of action (material facts, dates, places)
☐ Jurisdiction averments (pecuniary + territorial)
☐ Relief claimed (specific + alternative)
☐ Valuation for court fees
☐ Documents annexed (P-1, P-2...)
☐ Verification by plaintiff
☐ Court fees paid (affixed/paid)
☐ Vakalatnama (if advocate engaged)

```

### Written Statement Checklist:

```
☐ Specific admission/denial of each allegation
☐ Affirmative defenses pleaded
☐ Jurisdiction objection (if applicable)
☐ Counterclaim/set-off (if applicable)
☐ Documents proving defense annexed
☐ Verification by defendant
☐ Filed within 30 days (or extended time up to 90 days)
☐ Reply to counterclaim filed by plaintiff (if counterclaim raised)
```

## Common Mistakes

```
Mistake 1: General Denial in Written Statement
❌ "All allegations are denied"
✓ "Para 1, 2, 3 denied specifically. Para 4 admitted. Para 5 not admitted."

Mistake 2: Pleading Evidence Instead of Facts
❌ "I have 10 witnesses who will testify"
✓ "Defendant paid Rs. 5 lakhs on 15/01/2024"

Mistake 3: Claiming Relief Not Pleaded
❌ Plaint claims Rs. 5 lakhs; at trial seeks specific performance
✓ Claim specific performance in plaint (or amend before evidence)

Mistake 4: Undervaluation to Save Court Fees
❌ Land worth Rs. 50 lakhs valued at Rs. 10 lakhs
✓ Accurate valuation (court may reject plaint under Order VII Rule 11(b))

Mistake 5: Missing 90-Day Deadline for Written Statement
❌ File written statement on 95th day
✓ File within 30 days OR get extension within 90 days

Mistake 6: Verification by Advocate
❌ "I, [Advocate name], verify..."
✓ "I, [Party name], verify..."

Mistake 7: Not Raising Jurisdiction Objection in Written Statement
❌ Raise jurisdiction after filing written statement
✓ Raise in written statement itself (earliest opportunity)
```
