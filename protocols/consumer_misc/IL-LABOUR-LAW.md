# Indian Consumer & Misc Law: Labour Law and Industrial Relations

## Purpose
Comprehensive guidance on Indian labour law covering Industrial Disputes Act 1947, termination/retrenchment, layoff, closure, strikes/lockouts, labour courts/tribunals, Payment of Wages Act, Provident Fund (PF), Employees' State Insurance (ESI), Gratuity, maternity benefits, contract labour, and remedies for wrongful termination.

## Activation Triggers
Use when: Wrongful termination/dismissal | Retrenchment without compensation | Unpaid wages/salary | Illegal strike/lockout | Industrial dispute (workers vs management) | Labour court reference | Contract labour exploitation | PF/ESI non-compliance | Gratuity denial | Maternity leave denial | Standing orders violation | Domestic enquiry flaws | Unfair labour practice.

## Core Principles

### Constitutional Foundation

**Article 14:** Equality before law (no arbitrary termination)

**Article 16:** Equality of opportunity in employment

**Article 19(1)(c):** Right to form associations/unions

**Article 21:** Right to livelihood (cannot be deprived arbitrarily)

**Article 23-24:** Prohibition of forced labour, child labour

**Article 39(d), 42, 43:** DPSP - living wage, humane working conditions, worker participation

### Industrial Jurisprudence Principles

**1. Social Justice:**
- Labour laws are beneficial legislation
- Interpretation in favor of workers (liberal construction)

**2. Master-Servant Relationship:**
- Employer has control over employee
- Employee economically dependent
- Protection needed against arbitrary employer actions

**3. Industrial Peace:**
- Minimize disputes, maximize production
- Collective bargaining, conciliation, adjudication

## Industrial Disputes Act 1947

### Section 2(k): "Industrial Dispute" Defined

**"Dispute between employers and workmen OR between workmen and workmen, connected with employment/non-employment/terms of employment/conditions of labour"**

**Essential Elements:**
1. Dispute (not individual grievance - must be raised by union OR substantial body of workers)
2. Between employer and workmen (OR workmen inter se)
3. Connected with employment matters

**Individual Dispute vs Industrial Dispute:**

| Individual Dispute | Industrial Dispute |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| Single worker's complaint | Espoused by union OR substantial number of workers |
| Civil suit jurisdiction | Labour Court/Tribunal jurisdiction |
| Example: One worker's termination | Example: Union takes up termination, OR mass retrenchment |

**Bangalore Water Supply v. Rajappa (SC 1978):**
- Broad definition of "industry" (any systematic activity by cooperation between employer and employees)
- Includes cooperatives, societies, hospitals, educational institutions (if commercial element)

### Section 2(oo): "Workman" Defined

**Includes:**
- Skilled, unskilled, manual, operational, clerical workers
- Earning up to Rs. 10,000/month (as of 2017 - subject to periodic revision)

**Excludes:**
- Managerial/supervisory employees drawing > Rs. 10,000/month
- Employees in administrative capacity
- Armed forces, police

**Test:** Nature of work (skilled/unskilled manual/clerical) + wage threshold

## Termination of Employment

### Types of Termination

**1. Dismissal (Misconduct):**
- For proven misconduct (theft, insubordination, absenteeism, fraud)
- After domestic enquiry (principles of natural justice)
- No compensation

**2. Retrenchment (Section 2(oo)):**
- Termination for reasons other than misconduct (redundancy, efficiency, cost-cutting)
- Compensation mandatory (Section 25F)

**3. Layoff (Section 2(kkk)):**
- Temporary suspension of employment (not permanent termination)
- Due to shortage of coal, power, raw materials, breakdown, accumulation of stocks

**4. Closure (Section 25FFA-25FFC):**
- Permanent shutdown of establishment

**5. Voluntary Retirement/Resignation:**
- By employee's own will
- No compensation (unless VRS scheme)

### Section 25F: Conditions for Valid Retrenchment

**Three Conditions (ALL mandatory):**

**1. One Month Notice OR Notice Pay**
- Notice in writing
- OR payment equivalent to 1 month wages

**2. Retrenchment Compensation:**
- Formula: 15 DAYS average pay for each completed year of service
- OR as per standing orders/collective agreement (if higher)

**Example:**
- Service: 10 years
- Average pay: Rs. 30,000/month (Rs. 1,000/day)
- Compensation: 15 days × 10 years = 150 days' wages = Rs. 1,50,000

**3. Notice to Appropriate Government:**
- For establishments with >100 workers (Section 25N)
- Prior approval required (before retrenchment)

**Non-Compliance:**
- Retrenchment VOID
- Remedy: Reinstatement with back wages

### Section 25G-25H: Layoff Compensation

**If layoff > 45 days in year:**
- Compensation @ 50% of basic wages + DA
- For period of layoff

**Conditions:**
- Worker not on leave/strike
- Available for work

### Section 25O: Last Come, First Go (LIFO)

**Retrenchment Principle:**
- Last hired, first retrenched (seniority-based)

**Exception:** If agreement with union specifies different criteria

## Misconduct and Domestic Enquiry

### Standing Orders (Industrial Employment Standing Orders Act 1946)

**For establishments with >100 workers:**
- Must certify standing orders with Labour Commissioner
- Define:
  - Misconduct categories
  - Disciplinary procedure
  - Termination grounds

**Model Standing Orders (if not certified):**
- Default rules apply

### Misconduct Categories

**Major Misconduct (Dismissal):**
- Theft, fraud, embezzlement
- Wilful insubordination
- Habitual absence without leave
- Assault, violence
- Intoxication at work

**Minor Misconduct (Warning/Suspension):**
- Late attendance
- Minor negligence
- Discourteous behavior

### Domestic Enquiry Procedure (Principles of Natural Justice)

**Requirements (ALL mandatory):**

**1. Charge Sheet:**
- Specific allegations with dates, facts
- Not vague (must enable defense)

**2. Suspension Pending Enquiry (Optional):**
- If employee's presence prejudicial
- Subsistence allowance (50% wages) during suspension

**3. Enquiry Notice:**
- Date, time, venue
- Reasonable time to prepare defense (minimum 7 days)

**4. Right to Defense:**
- Inspect documents
- Cross-examine witnesses
- Lead defense evidence
- Engage representative (coworker/union representative - not advocate)

**5. Enquiry Officer (Unbiased):**
- Not complainant
- Independent assessment

**6. Enquiry Report:**
- Findings on each charge
- Evidence considered

**7. Second Show-Cause Notice:**
- On proposed punishment
- Opportunity to submit mitigation

**8. Final Order:**
- Reasoned order (dismissal/suspension/warning)

**Procedural Flaws = Bad Domestic Enquiry:**
- No opportunity to defend
- Denial of cross-examination
- Biased enquiry officer
- No charge sheet/vague charges

**Remedy:**
- Labour Court/Tribunal can reinstate if enquiry flawed (even if misconduct substantive)

### Quantum of Proof (Domestic Enquiry)

**Preponderance of Probabilities (Not Beyond Reasonable Doubt):**
- Standard lower than criminal trial
- Substantial evidence sufficient

## Labour Courts and Tribunals (Section 7-7A)

### Three-Tier Adjudication

**1. Conciliation Officer (Section 4):**
- Appointed by Government
- Attempts settlement before adjudication
- If settlement fails → Matter referred to Court/Tribunal

**2. Labour Court (Section 7):**
- One presiding officer (judicial background)
- Jurisdiction: Schedule II matters
  - Propriety/legality of employer's orders (termination, transfer)
  - Application/interpretation of standing orders
  - Illegal strikes/lockouts
  - Individual disputes

**3. Industrial Tribunal (Section 7A):**
- One presiding officer (judicial background)
- Jurisdiction: Schedule III matters (larger disputes)
  - Wages, hours of work
  - Bonus, profit-sharing
  - Retrenchment, closure, layoff
  - Collective bargaining agreements

**National Tribunal (Section 7B):**
- For disputes of national importance
- Appointed by Central Government

### Section 10: Reference to Labour Court/Tribunal

**By Appropriate Government (Central/State):**
- After conciliation fails
- Dispute referred for adjudication
- Compulsory (if government refers)

**Application by Workman (Section 10A):**
- Individual workman can apply directly (since 2010 amendment)
- If dispute individual and not espoused by union

### Procedure Before Labour Court (Similar to Civil Court)

**Pleadings:**
- Statement of claim (workman)
- Written statement (employer)

**Evidence:**
- Documentary (charge sheet, enquiry report, standing orders, wage slips)
- Oral (witnesses)

**Arguments:**
- Parties/counsel

**Award (Section 17):**
- Within 3 months (extended in practice)
- Binding on parties
- Published in official gazette

**Execution:**
- Award executed as decree (CPC provisions apply)

### Section 11A: Appeal

**Appeal to Labour Appellate Tribunal (if constituted) OR High Court (writ under Article 226/227):**
- Limited grounds (error of law apparent on record, perversity)
- Not re-appreciation of evidence

## Strikes and Lockouts

### Section 2(q): "Strike" Defined

**"Cessation of work by body of persons employed, acting in combination, OR concerted refusal to work"**

### Section 2(l): "Lockout" Defined

**"Closing of place of employment, OR suspension of work, OR refusal to continue employment by employer"**

### Section 22-23: Illegal Strikes and Lockouts

**Strike/Lockout ILLEGAL if:**

**1. Without Notice (Section 22):**
- 14 days notice required (in public utility services)
- 6 weeks after conciliation proceedings end (in other establishments)

**2. During Conciliation/Adjudication (Section 23):**
- Strike/lockout prohibited during pendency of proceedings + 7 days thereafter

**3. Contrary to Award/Settlement:**
- Strike against binding award/settlement

**Public Utility Services (Schedule I):**
- Railways, postal, telegraph
- Ports, airways
- Banks, electricity, water supply
- Hospitals

**Penalty (Section 26):**
- Illegal strike: Imprisonment up to 1 month OR fine up to Rs. 50 (per worker)
- Illegal lockout: Imprisonment up to 1 month OR fine up to Rs. 1,000

**Remedies:**
- Employer can dismiss workers participating in illegal strike (misconduct)
- Workers can challenge illegal lockout (wages for lockout period)

### Section 24: Go-Slow, Work-to-Rule

**Prohibited:**
- Deliberately slowing production
- "Work-to-rule" (strict adherence to rules to disrupt production)

## Unfair Labour Practices (Schedule IV-V)

### Unfair Labour Practices by Employer

1. **Interference with Union Formation:**
   - Threatening/intimidating workers forming unions
   - Blacklisting union leaders

2. **Discrimination:**
   - Transfer/termination of union office-bearers without cause
   - Denying promotion to union members

3. **Coercion:**
   - Forced resignation
   - Retrenchment without compliance with Section 25F

4. **Victimization:**
   - Punitive transfers
   - False charges against union activists

### Unfair Labour Practices by Workers/Unions

1. **Violence/Intimidation:**
   - Gherao (confining management)
   - Violence against non-strikers

2. **Coercion of Workers:**
   - Forcing workers to join union/strike

3. **Damage to Property**

**Remedy:**
- Labour Court can restrain unfair labour practice
- Compensation to affected party

## Wages and Benefits

### Payment of Wages Act 1936

**Section 3: Employer Must Pay Wages:**

**Timelines (Section 5):**
- Establishments with <1,000 workers: By 7th day of next month
- Establishments with >1,000 workers: By 10th day of next month

**Deductions Permissible (Section 7):**
- Fines (if standing orders allow, for misconduct)
- Absence from duty
- Damage/loss caused by employee
- House accommodation
- Loans/advances
- PF, ESI, tax

**Unlawful Deductions:**
- All deductions not in Section 7
- Example: Arbitrary fines without standing order provision

**Penalty (Section 20):**
- Fine up to Rs. 3,750

**Complaint (Section 15):**
- To Labour Commissioner/Inspector
- Within 12 months

### Minimum Wages Act 1948

**Section 3: Fixation of Minimum Wages:**
- Central/State Government notifies minimum wages (industry, occupation-wise)
- Revised periodically

**Current Minimum Wages (vary by state, industry):**
- Unskilled: Rs. 9,000-20,000/month
- Semi-skilled: Rs. 10,000-22,000/month
- Skilled: Rs. 12,000-25,000/month

**Penalty (Section 22):**
- Paying below minimum wage: Imprisonment up to 6 months OR fine up to Rs. 500

### Payment of Bonus Act 1965

**Section 10: Minimum Bonus:**
- 8.33% of wages OR Rs. 100 (whichever higher)

**Section 11: Maximum Bonus:**
- 20% of wages

**Eligibility:**
- Employees earning up to Rs. 21,000/month
- Worked for minimum 30 days in accounting year

**Disqualification:**
- Dismissal for fraud, theft, sabotage

**Calculation Formula:**
```
Bonus = (Available Surplus × Employee's Wages) / Total Wages Bill
Subject to minimum 8.33%, maximum 20%
```

### Payment of Gratuity Act 1972

**Section 4: Gratuity Entitlement:**

**Eligibility:**
- 5 YEARS continuous service (minimum)
- OR death/disablement (no minimum service)

**Formula:**
```
Gratuity = (Last drawn wages × 15 days × Years of service) / 26

Maximum: Rs. 20 LAKHS (as of 2023 amendment)
```

**Example:**
- Service: 10 years
- Last drawn wages: Rs. 50,000/month
- Gratuity: (Rs. 50,000 × 15 days × 10 years) / 26 days = Rs. 2,88,462

**Payment Timeline:**
- Within 30 days of eligibility

**Forfeiture (Section 4(6)):**
- Dismissal for moral turpitude (act involving moral depravity - fraud, theft, violence)

**Penalty (Section 9):**
- Non-payment: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR fine OR both

**Complaint:**
- To Controlling Authority (within 90 days)
- Recovery as arrears of land revenue

### Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) Act 1952

**Section 6: Contribution:**

**Employer Contribution: 12% of basic wages + DA**
**Employee Contribution: 12% of basic wages + DA**

**Components:**
- EPF: 8.33% (employer) + 12% (employee)
- EPS (Pension): 3.67% (employer)

**Applicability:**
- Establishments with >20 employees
- Applicable to employees earning up to Rs. 15,000/month (threshold)

**Withdrawal:**
- On retirement, resignation (after 2 months unemployment), death

**Penalty (Section 14):**
- Non-deduction/non-deposit: Damage @ 12% per annum + prosecution

**Interest (EPF):**
- Currently 8.15% per annum (declared annually by EPFO)

### Employees' State Insurance (ESI) Act 1948

**Section 38: Contribution:**

**Employer Contribution: 3.25% of wages**
**Employee Contribution: 0.75% of wages**

**Applicability:**
- Establishments with >10 employees
- Employees earning up to Rs. 21,000/month

**Benefits:**
- Medical care (cashless treatment at ESI hospitals)
- Sickness benefit (cash benefit during sickness leave)
- Maternity benefit
- Disablement benefit
- Dependents' benefit (death)

**Penalty (Section 85):**
- Non-compliance: Imprisonment up to 3 years + fine

### Maternity Benefit Act 1961

**Section 5: Maternity Leave:**

**Entitlement: 26 WEEKS (since 2017 amendment)**
- 8 weeks must be postnatal
- Up to 8 weeks can be pre-natal

**For Third Child Onwards: 12 WEEKS**

**Applicability:**
- Establishments with >10 employees
- Employee must have worked for 80 days in 12 months preceding expected delivery

**Wages During Leave:**
- Full wages (average of last 3 months)

**Section 11: Dismissal During Maternity Leave Prohibited**

**Penalty (Section 21):**
- Violation: Imprisonment up to 3 months to 1 year + fine Rs. 2,000-5,000

## Contract Labour Regulation Act 1970

### Section 2(1)(b): "Contract Labour" Defined

**"Workman employed by or through contractor, in connection with work of establishment"**

**Triangular Relationship:**
- Principal Employer (establishment)
- Contractor (supplies labour)
- Contract Labour (workers)

### Section 10: Prohibition of Contract Labour

**If work:**
- Perennial (not seasonal/temporary)
- Directly related to principal employer's core business

**Appropriate Government can prohibit contract labour in certain processes**

**If Prohibited → Workers become Employees of Principal Employer**

### Section 21: Amenities

**Contractor Must Provide:**
- Canteen (if >100 workers)
- Rest rooms
- Drinking water
- First-aid
- Washing facilities

**Principal Employer Liable (if contractor fails)**

### Regularization of Contract Workers

**Steel Authority v. National Union (SC 2001):**
- Contract labour in perennial work + employed for substantial period → Deemed employees of principal employer
- Entitled to regularization

**Violation:**
- Non-registration of contractor
- Non-provision of amenities
- Penalty: Imprisonment up to 3 months OR fine up to Rs. 1,000

## Wrongful Termination Remedies

### Remedies Available

**1. Reinstatement (Primary Remedy):**
- Employee reinstated to same position
- **Back Wages:** Wages for period of unemployment (from termination to reinstatement)
- Continuity of service (for PF, gratuity, seniority)

**Limitation on Back Wages:**
- Courts may limit to 3 years (if delay in adjudication)
- 50% back wages (if employee contributed to misconduct, but enquiry flawed)

**2. Compensation in Lieu of Reinstatement:**
- If reinstatement not feasible (loss of mutual trust, closed establishment)
- Lump sum compensation (12-36 months' wages, depending on service period)

**3. Retrenchment Compensation (if Valid Retrenchment):**
- 15 days' average pay per year of service

### Factors for Reinstatement vs Compensation

**Reinstatement Ordered:**
- Enquiry flawed (procedural violation)
- No misconduct OR misconduct not proved
- Retrenchment without compliance with Section 25F

**Compensation Ordered (Instead of Reinstatement):**
- Relationship of trust broken (even if enquiry flawed)
- Establishment closed/shifted
- Long delay in adjudication (10+ years)

**Dharangadhra Chemical v. State of Saurashtra (SC 1957):**
- Primary relief = reinstatement with back wages
- Compensation only if reinstatement impracticable

## Common Mistakes

**1. Terminating Without Domestic Enquiry (For Misconduct)** → Reinstatement with full back wages

**2. Flawed Domestic Enquiry (No Opportunity to Defend)** → Reinstatement even if misconduct proved

**3. Retrenchment Without Section 25F Compliance** → Retrenchment void, reinstatement

**4. Not Maintaining Standing Orders** → Labor Commissioner penalties, unfair termination claims succeed

**5. Deducting Wages Arbitrarily (Not in Section 7 Payment of Wages Act)** → Complaint to Labour Commissioner, repayment + penalty

**6. Not Depositing PF/ESI Contributions** → Damage, prosecution

**7. Denying Gratuity Claiming Service < 5 Years (Without Proof)** → Recovery proceedings, penalty

**8. Treating Contract Labour as Employees (No Contractor Registered)** → Deemed employees, regularization

**9. Illegal Strike/Lockout Without Notice** → Dismissal of workers OR wages during illegal lockout

**10. Maternity Leave Denial/Dismissal During Maternity** → Reinstatement + full wages + prosecution

## Key Judgments

**Workman of Firestone Tyre v. Management (SC 1973):**
- Misconduct must be proved by employer (burden of proof)
- Labour Court can interfere if punishment disproportionate

**Delhi Cloth Mills v. Ludh Budh (SC 1972):**
- Natural justice in domestic enquiry mandatory
- Even for summary dismissal, basic fairness required

**Bharti Cellular v. Suresh (SC 2009):**
- Private sector termination (not workman) → Labour Court no jurisdiction
- Civil suit for wrongful termination
