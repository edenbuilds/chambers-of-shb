# Indian Consumer & Misc Law: Information Technology Act & Cyber Law

## Purpose
Comprehensive guidance on IT Act 2000 covering digital signatures, electronic records, cyber crimes (hacking, identity theft, data breach), intermediary liability, data protection, cybersecurity obligations, and remedies for cyber offenses.

## Activation Triggers
Use when: Cyber crime (hacking, phishing, identity theft) | Data breach | Unauthorized access to computer system | Website defacement | Online fraud | Intermediary liability (ISP, social media) | Digital signature validity | E-commerce dispute | Data protection compliance | Cybersecurity incident | Privacy violation | Revenge porn/morphed images.

## Core Principles

### Section 4: Legal Recognition of Electronic Records

**Electronic records = Paper records** (legal equivalence)

**"Information shall not be denied legal effect solely on ground it is in electronic form"**

**Application:**
- Contracts (e-commerce, online agreements)
- Government filings (e-filing of taxes, forms)
- Banking (online transactions)

**Exception - Documents Requiring Physical Presence:**
- Negotiable instruments (cheques - but moving to electronic)
- Powers of attorney (some states)
- Wills, trusts
- Sale/transfer of immovable property (registration required)

### Section 5: Digital Signatures

**Digital Signature = Electronic authentication**

**Valid if:**
- Affixed using asymmetric crypto system + hash function
- Issued by Certifying Authority (licensed by CCA - Controller of Certifying Authorities)

**Legal Effect:** Deemed authenticated (Section 3A Evidence Act)

**Use Cases:**
- E-filing (income tax, company filings)
- E-tendering (government procurement)
- Electronic contracts

**Certifying Authority:** Licensed by CCA (government body under IT Act)

## Cyber Crimes (Section 43-74)

### Section 43: Unauthorized Access/Damage (Civil Penalty)

**Acts Constituting Offense (Damages up to Rs. 1 crore):**

**(a) Unauthorized Access** - Accessing computer/network without permission

**(b) Downloading/Copying Data** - Without authorization

**(c) Introducing Virus** - Computer contaminant

**(d) Damaging Computer System** - Destroying/deleting/altering data

**(e) Denial of Service** - Disrupting computer access

**(f) Facilitating Unauthorized Access** - Providing means (password, security code)

**Remedy:** Compensation up to Rs. 1 crore (adjudication by Adjudicating Officer)

### Section 66: Hacking (Criminal Offense)

**"Whoever with intent to cause wrongful loss/gain, destroys/deletes/alters information, or diminishes value/utility, or affects injuriously"**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs

**Example:** Hacker accesses company server, deletes database → Section 66

### Section 66A: Offensive Messages (STRUCK DOWN by Supreme Court 2015)

**Was:** Sending offensive/menacing messages via communication service

**Supreme Court (Shreya Singhal v. Union of India):** Unconstitutional (violates free speech - Article 19(1)(a))

**Now:** Section 66A NOT enforceable

### Section 66B: Dishonestly Receiving Stolen Computer Resource

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh

**Example:** Knowingly receiving stolen credit card data

### Section 66C: Identity Theft

**"Using electronic signature/password/unique identification of another person fraudulently/dishonestly"**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh

**Example:** Using someone's Aadhaar/PAN for fraudulent transactions

### Section 66D: Cheating by Personation Using Computer Resource

**"Cheating by personation by using computer resource"**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh

**Example:** Creating fake profile impersonating person, defrauding others

### Section 66E: Privacy Violation (Publishing Private Images)

**"Capturing/publishing/transmitting image of private area without consent"**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 2 lakhs (First offense) | Up to 5 years + Fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs (Subsequent offense)

**Application:** Revenge porn, morphed images

**Private Area:** Genitals, pubic area, buttocks, breasts (female)

### Section 66F: Cyber Terrorism

**"Threatens unity, integrity, security, sovereignty of India by denying access to computer resource"**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to life

**Example:** Hacking critical infrastructure (power grid, banks, defense systems)

### Section 67: Publishing Obscene Material

**Publishing/transmitting obscene material in electronic form**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs (First offense) | Up to 5 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs (Subsequent offense)

### Section 67A: Sexually Explicit Material

**Publishing sexually explicit acts/conduct**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 5 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs (First offense) | Up to 7 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs (Subsequent offense)

### Section 67B: Child Pornography

**Publishing/transmitting material depicting children in sexually explicit act**

**Punishment:** Imprisonment up to 5 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs (First offense) | Up to 7 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs (Subsequent offense)

**Also:** POCSO Act 2012 (stricter punishment - up to life imprisonment)

## Intermediary Liability (Section 79 + IT Rules 2021)

### Section 79: Safe Harbor for Intermediaries

**Intermediary:** Person providing telecom/internet service (ISP, hosting, search engine, social media)

**Safe Harbor (NO liability) if:**
1. Function = Intermediary (not originating/modifying information)
2. No actual knowledge of illegal content OR
3. On obtaining knowledge, expeditiously removes/disables access
4. Observes due diligence (IT Rules 2021)

**Loses Safe Harbor if:**
- Actual knowledge of illegal content + fails to remove
- Fails to comply with due diligence (IT Rules)

### IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules 2021

**Due Diligence Obligations:**

**All Intermediaries:**
1. **Terms of Use** - Prohibit hosting illegal content
2. **Privacy Policy** - Data collection, usage
3. **Grievance Officer** - Appoint within India, acknowledge complaint (24 hours), resolve (15 days)
4. **Takedown** - Remove illegal content within 24 hours of court order/government notification

**Significant Social Media Intermediaries (Users > 50 lakhs):**
1. **Chief Compliance Officer** - Ensure compliance
2. **Nodal Contact Person** - 24/7 contact for law enforcement
3. **Resident Grievance Officer** - Based in India
4. **Monthly Report** - Proactive monitoring, content removal
5. **Traceability** - Enable identification of first originator (encrypted messaging - controversial)

**Illegal Content (Must Not Host):**
- Violates law (defamation, obscenity, child porn, IP infringement)
- Threatens unity/integrity of India
- Incites violence
- Invades privacy

**Content Moderation:**
- User complaints → Intermediary reviews → Remove/retain (with reasons)
- Automated tools + human moderation

**Criticism:** Concerns over censorship, privacy (traceability of encrypted messages)

## Data Protection (No Comprehensive Law Yet - Bills Pending)

**Current Framework:**

**IT Act Section 43A + IT (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules 2011:**

**"Body corporate possessing sensitive personal data must implement reasonable security practices"**

**Sensitive Personal Data:**
- Passwords
- Financial information (bank, credit card)
- Health records
- Biometric information
- Sexual orientation

**Reasonable Security Practices:**
- IS/ISO/IEC 27001 certification OR
- Comprehensive documented information security program

**Data Breach:**
- If breach due to negligence → Compensation (up to Rs. 5 crores)

**Consent Required:**
- Collecting sensitive personal data requires consent
- Purpose limitation (collect only for stated purpose)

**Future:** Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (passed but not yet notified - will replace current framework)

## Cybersecurity (Section 70B + CERT-In)

**CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team):**
- National nodal agency for cybersecurity incidents
- Collects, analyzes, disseminates info on cyber incidents

**Cybersecurity Directions (Section 70B(6)):**

**Mandatory Reporting (CERT-In Directions 2022):**
- Service providers, intermediaries, data centers must report cyber incidents to CERT-In within 6 HOURS
- Incidents: Data breach, unauthorized access, malware, DDoS

**Log Maintenance:**
- ICT systems must maintain logs for 180 days (for investigation)

**VPN/Cloud Service Providers:**
- Maintain customer data (name, address, email, IP, purpose) for 5 years (controversial - privacy concerns)

## Adjudication and Appeals

### Adjudicating Officer (Section 46)

**Appointed by Central/State Government**

**Powers:**
- Adjudicate claims under Section 43 (unauthorized access, damage)
- Compensation up to Rs. 1 crore

**Procedure:**
- Complaint filed
- Notice to respondent
- Hearing (oral/written submissions)
- Order (within 6 months)

**Appeal:** To Cyber Appellate Tribunal (within 45 days)

### Cyber Appellate Tribunal (Section 48)

**Appeals from:**
- Adjudicating Officer orders
- Controller of Certifying Authorities orders

**Further Appeal:** To High Court (within 60 days)

## Investigation (Section 78-81)

**Police Investigation:**
- Cyber crimes cognizable (police can arrest without warrant)
- Specialized cyber crime cells in states

**Section 78: Power to Investigate**
- Police Officer (rank Inspector and above) can investigate

**Section 80: Power of Police to Enter/Search**
- With warrant from Magistrate
- Seize computers, devices, evidence

**Jurisdiction (Section 75):**
- Offense committed anywhere (if victim/accused in India)
- Extraterritorial application

## Common Cyber Crimes - Remedies

**Phishing/Online Fraud:**
- Sections: 66C (identity theft), 66D (cheating by personation), 420 IPC (cheating)
- Report to cyber crime portal (https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- File FIR with cyber cell

**Hacking:**
- Section 66 (hacking), Section 43 (unauthorized access)
- Civil compensation + Criminal prosecution

**Data Breach:**
- Section 43A (compensation if negligence)
- Report to CERT-In (within 6 hours)

**Defamation Online:**
- Section 499/500 IPC (defamation - criminal)
- Civil defamation suit
- Section 79 notice to intermediary (takedown)

**Revenge Porn/Morphed Images:**
- Section 66E (privacy violation), Section 67/67A (obscene material)
- Section 354C IPC (voyeurism), Section 509 IPC (insult to modesty)
- File FIR, seek takedown from platform

## Common Mistakes

**1. Not Reporting Cyber Crime Timely** → Evidence lost (digital evidence volatile)

**2. Tampering with Evidence** → Destroying device/data (obstruction of justice)

**3. Not Preserving Digital Evidence** → Screenshots, emails, URLs (with timestamp)

**4. Assuming Anonymity Online** → IP addresses traceable, digital footprint

**5. Not Complying with CERT-In Reporting** → Penalty, regulatory action

**6. Intermediaries Not Appointing Grievance Officer** → Lose safe harbor, liability

**7. Not Implementing Data Security (Section 43A)** → Compensation liability for breach

**8. Not Obtaining Consent for Sensitive Data** → Violation of IT Rules

**9. Delay in Takedown (Intermediaries)** → Liability for illegal content

**10. Not Updating Cybersecurity Measures** → Vulnerable to attacks, compliance gaps
