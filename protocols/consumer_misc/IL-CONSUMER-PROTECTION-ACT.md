# Indian Consumer & Misc Law: Consumer Protection

## Purpose
Comprehensive guidance on Consumer Protection Act 2019, covering consumer rights, unfair trade practices, defective goods/deficient services, product liability, e-commerce regulations, consumer dispute forums (District/State/National Commission), remedies, and complaint procedures.

## Activation Triggers
Use when: Defective product purchased | Deficient service received | Unfair trade practice (misleading advertisement, false representation) | Product liability claim (manufacturing defect, design defect) | E-commerce dispute | Consumer complaint filing | Forum jurisdiction determination | Product recall | Class action by consumers | Mediation/settlement in consumer disputes.

## Core Principles

### Section 2(7): "Consumer" Defined

**"Consumer means any person who:**
**(i) buys any goods for consideration (paid/promised/part-paid/deferred/any mode);**
**(ii) hires/avails any service for consideration;**

**Excludes person obtaining goods for resale OR commercial purpose"**

**Essential Elements:**
1. **GOODS/SERVICES** - Purchased/hired
2. **CONSIDERATION** - Paid (not free/gratuitous)
3. **NOT FOR RESALE** - For personal use (not commercial)

**Examples:**

**Consumer ✓:**
- Individual buying mobile phone for personal use
- Hiring cab service for personal travel
- Patient availing medical services

**NOT Consumer ✗:**
- Wholesaler buying goods for resale
- Company purchasing office equipment (commercial purpose)
- Free goods/services (no consideration)

**Important - Commercial Purpose Exclusion:**
- Laxmi Engineering v. PSG College (SC 1995): Goods for commercial purpose ≠ Consumer
- But if earning livelihood (sole source of income) → May be consumer (liberal interpretation)

### Section 2(42): "Service" Defined

**Broad Definition:** Any service made available to potential users (banking, insurance, transport, medical, education, etc.)

**Excludes:**
- Free services
- Service under contract of personal service (employment)

## Consumer Rights (Section 2(9))

**Six Fundamental Rights:**

1. **Right to Safety** - Protection against hazardous goods/services
2. **Right to Information** - Informed choice (quality, quantity, price, ingredients)
3. **Right to Choose** - Access to variety at competitive prices
4. **Right to be Heard** - Voice in consumer policy making
5. **Right to Redress** - Remedies for unfair trade practices, defective goods
6. **Right to Consumer Education** - Awareness of rights

---

## Unfair Trade Practices (Section 2(47))

**Prohibited Practices:**

**1. False/Misleading Representation:**
- Standard/quality/grade
- Ingredients/contents
- Sponsorship/approval
- Place of origin

**Example:** Labeling product "Made in Switzerland" when manufactured in India

**2. Misleading Advertisement:**
- Exaggerated claims
- False testimonials
- Bait advertising (advertise low price, refuse to sell)

**Example:** Ad claims "Cures cancer" (unsubstantiated medical claim)

**3. Bargain Sale (False Discount):**
- "50% off" when original price inflated

**4. Defective Goods Sold as New:**
- Refurbished sold as brand new

**5. Hoarding/Destruction to Raise Prices**

**Remedies:**
- Cease practice
- Refund
- Damages
- Penalty (up to Rs. 10 lakhs + Rs. 1 lakh per day for continuing offense)

## Defective Goods / Deficient Services (Section 2(10), (11))

**Defect (Section 2(10)):**
- Fault, imperfection, shortcoming in quality, quantity, purity, standard
- Goods not conforming to claims/representations

**Deficiency (Section 2(11)):**
- Shortfall in quality, nature, manner of performance of service
- Service not as per standard/contract

**Examples:**

**Defective Goods:**
- Mobile phone not working (manufacturing defect)
- Food item contaminated
- Medicine expired

**Deficient Services:**
- Flight delayed without justification
- Hospital negligence
- Bank charging unauthorized fees

**Remedies:**
- Replacement
- Refund
- Repair
- Compensation for loss/injury

## Product Liability (Section 82-87 - NEW in 2019 Act)

**Strict Liability Regime:**

**Section 84: Product Liability Action**

**When manufacturer/seller liable (NO FAULT required - strict liability):**
- Product has manufacturing defect OR
- Product design defective OR
- Deviation from manufacturing specifications OR
- Product not conforming to express warranty OR
- Failure to provide adequate instructions/warnings

**Defendants:**
- Product manufacturer
- Product service provider
- Product seller (in certain cases)

**Defenses (Section 85):**

1. **Compliance with Law** - Product conforms to legal standards
2. **No Defect** - Defect did not exist when sold
3. **Not Engaged in Business** - Occasional sale (not commercial)

**Damages (Section 87):**
- Compensatory damages (actual loss)
- Punitive damages (if malice/gross negligence)

**Example:**
- Pressure cooker explodes due to design defect → Manufacturer strictly liable (even if no negligence)

## E-Commerce Regulations (Section 2(16) + Rules 2020)

**E-Commerce Entity (Section 2(16)):**
- Online platform (marketplace OR inventory-based)

**Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules 2020:**

**Obligations of E-Commerce Entities:**

1. **Display Information:**
   - Legal name, address, contact
   - Terms of use, return/refund policy
   - Country of origin (products)
   - Grievance officer details

2. **No Misleading Ads/Unfair Trade Practices**

3. **Protect Consumer Data** - Privacy policy, consent for data use

4. **Cancellation/Return Rights:**
   - Clear return/refund/exchange policy

5. **Grievance Redressal:**
   - Appoint Grievance Officer
   - Acknowledge complaint within 48 hours
   - Redress within 1 month

**Flash Sales:**
- Ensure adequate inventory (no back-to-back cancellations)

**Marketplace vs Inventory Model:**

| Aspect | Marketplace | Inventory |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Role** | Platform (connects sellers-buyers) | Seller (owns inventory) |
| **Liability** | Generally NOT liable for seller's products (unless unfair practices) | LIABLE as seller |
| **Example** | Amazon, Flipkart (marketplace) | Amazon (own brands), Myntra |

## Consumer Dispute Forums (Three-Tier System)

### District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (Section 34)

**Jurisdiction:**
- **Pecuniary:** Goods/services value up to Rs. 1 CRORE

**Composition:**
- President (District Judge OR qualified to be District Judge)
- 2-4 members

**Procedure:**
- File complaint (Form CPA-1)
- Fee: Nil (for complaints up to Rs. 5 lakhs) OR Rs. 200-5,000 (above Rs. 5 lakhs)
- Timeline: Dispose within 3 MONTHS (can extend to 5 months)

### State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (Section 45)

**Jurisdiction:**
- **Pecuniary:** Goods/services value > Rs. 1 crore, up to Rs. 10 CRORES
- **Appellate:** Appeals from District Commission

**Composition:**
- President (High Court Judge OR qualified to be HC Judge)
- 4-8 members

**Procedure:**
- Complaint OR Appeal (from District Commission order)
- Appeal: Within 30 days (extendable to 45 days)

### National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (Section 58)

**Jurisdiction:**
- **Pecuniary:** Goods/services value > Rs. 10 CRORES
- **Appellate:** Appeals from State Commission

**Composition:**
- President (Supreme Court Judge OR qualified to be SC Judge)
- 4+ members

**Procedure:**
- Complaint OR Appeal (from State Commission order)
- Appeal: Within 30 days (extendable to 45 days)

**Further Appeal:**
- To Supreme Court (within 30 days from National Commission order)

## Complaint Procedure

### Filing Complaint (Section 35)

**Who Can File:**
- Consumer OR
- Registered voluntary consumer association OR
- Central/State Government OR
- One or more consumers (class action) OR
- Legal heir/representative (if consumer deceased/incapacitated)

**Against Whom:**
- Manufacturer, seller, service provider

**Form:** CPA-1 (online OR offline)

**Mode:**
- E-filing (preferred - https://edaakhil.nic.in)
- Physical filing at Commission office

**Contents:**
1. Complainant details
2. Opposite party details (manufacturer, seller, trader)
3. Facts (when, where goods purchased/service availed)
4. Defect/deficiency details
5. Relief sought (refund, replacement, compensation)
6. Valuation (for jurisdiction)
7. Affidavit verifying complaint

**Documents:**
- Purchase invoice/bill
- Warranty card
- Correspondence with seller/manufacturer
- Medical reports (if injury)
- Expert opinion (if technical product)

**Limitation:**
- **2 YEARS** from cause of action (date of purchase/service/defect discovered)
- **Exception:** Condonable if sufficient cause (up to additional 2 years in State/National Commission)

### Admission and Notice (Section 37)

**Commission:**
- Admits complaint OR rejects (if not maintainable/frivolous)
- Sends notice to opposite party (20 days to file response)

**Opposite Party Response:**
- File written version (defense)
- 30 days from notice

### Evidence and Hearing

**Not Bound by CPC/Evidence Act:**
- Informal procedure (but principles of natural justice)
- Oral/documentary evidence
- Expert evidence (if required)

**Mediation (Section 37A - NEW):**
- Parties can opt for mediation
- Appointed mediator (from panel)
- Settlement within 60 days (extendable to 90 days)
- If settled → Order as per settlement
- If not settled → Proceed to hearing

### Order (Section 38)

**Remedies Commission Can Grant:**

1. **Remove Defect** - Repair goods

2. **Replace Goods** - With new goods of similar description (free from defect)

3. **Refund Price Paid** - Plus interest + costs

4. **Compensation** - For loss/injury suffered (physical, mental, financial)

5. **Punitive Damages** - If malicious/gross negligence

6. **Cease Unfair Trade Practice** - Discontinue practice

7. **Withdrawal of Hazardous Goods** - Not to offer for sale

8. **Corrective Advertisement** - At opposite party's cost

**Timeline:**
- Order within 3-5 months (District Commission)

**Non-Compliance:**
- Penalty: Imprisonment up to 1 year OR fine up to Rs. 10,000 OR both
- Execution as decree (CPC provisions apply)

## Class Action / Representative Complaints (Section 35(1)(a))

**One or More Consumers on Behalf of Class:**

**Conditions:**
1. Numerous consumers having same interest
2. Common question of law/fact
3. Representative adequate

**Example:**
- 1000 customers bought defective batch of product → 10 customers file on behalf of all 1000

**Benefits:**
- Efficiency (one proceeding instead of 1000)
- Access to justice (small individual claims)

**Procedure:**
- File complaint stating class action
- Commission gives wide publicity (notice to other class members)
- Class members can opt in/out

**Order:**
- Binds all class members (who did not opt out)

## Key Judgments and Principles

**Indian Medical Association v. V.P. Shantha (SC 1995):**
- Medical services are "service" under Consumer Protection Act
- Doctors/hospitals liable for medical negligence as service providers

**Lucknow Development Authority v. M.K. Gupta (SC 1994):**
- Housing construction service falls under Consumer Protection Act
- Delay in delivery/defects in construction = Deficiency in service

**Narmada Bachao Andolan v. Union of India (SC 2011):**
- Public interest litigation possible in consumer matters
- NGOs can file on behalf of affected consumers

## Common Scenarios

**Scenario 1: E-Commerce Purchase - Product Not Delivered**

**Facts:**
- Consumer orders phone from e-commerce site
- Payment made (Rs. 20,000)
- Product not delivered for 3 months
- E-commerce company not responding

**Remedy:**
1. File complaint with District Commission (value < Rs. 1 crore)
2. Against: E-commerce company (if inventory model) OR Seller (if marketplace) + E-commerce (if unfair practice)
3. Relief: Refund Rs. 20,000 + interest + compensation for mental agony

**Likely Outcome:**
- District Commission orders refund + interest (12% p.a.) + Rs. 10,000 compensation

**Scenario 2: Medical Negligence**

**Facts:**
- Patient undergoes surgery
- Doctor's negligence → Complications
- Patient suffers permanent disability

**Remedy:**
1. File complaint (valuation based on compensation sought)
2. Against: Doctor + Hospital
3. Relief: Compensation for medical expenses + loss of income + pain/suffering

**Expert Evidence:**
- Medical expert opinion (establish negligence, causation)

**Likely Outcome:**
- Compensation Rs. 10-50 lakhs (depending on injury severity, earning capacity)

**Scenario 3: Misleading Advertisement - False Claims**

**Facts:**
- Advertis

ement claims product cures disease
- No scientific basis
- Consumer purchases, no benefit

**Remedy:**
1. File complaint for unfair trade practice
2. Against: Manufacturer + Advertiser
3. Relief: Refund + compensation + penalty

**Regulatory Action:**
- CCPA (Central Consumer Protection Authority) can initiate suo motu proceedings
- Penalty on manufacturer

## Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA) - NEW (Section 10)

**Regulatory Body (Established 2020):**

**Powers:**
1. **Investigate** unfair trade practices, misleading ads
2. **Recall Products** (unsafe/hazardous)
3. **Refund** to consumers (if product withdrawn)
4. **Impose Penalty** (up to Rs. 10 lakhs + Rs. 1 lakh per day for continuing violation)
5. **File Class Action** on behalf of consumers

**Complaint to CCPA:**
- Any consumer OR consumer association
- CCPA investigates
- Penalty proceedings (separate from consumer dispute redressal)

## Common Mistakes

**1. Filing in Wrong Forum** → Dismissed for lack of pecuniary jurisdiction

**2. Filing Beyond Limitation** → Barred by time (2 years + condonation)

**3. Not Preserving Evidence** → Weak case (no invoice, no proof)

**4. Commercial Purchase Claimed as Consumer** → Not maintainable

**5. Not Attempting Settlement/Mediation** → Prolonged litigation

**6. Insufficient Compensation Claim** → Under-compensation

**7. Not Following Up on E-Commerce Grievance Officer** → Direct forum approach (should exhaust internal grievance)

**8. Not Joining Necessary Parties** → Manufacturer, seller, service provider (all may be liable)

**9. Not Providing Expert Evidence (Technical/Medical Cases)** → Difficult to prove defect/negligence

**10. Ignoring Execution** → Order passed but not enforced (follow up on compliance)
