# Indian Consumer & Misc Law: Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act

## Purpose
Comprehensive guidance on RERA 2016 covering real estate project registration, promoter obligations, allottee rights, real estate agents regulation, complaint procedures, RERA Authority powers, penalties, and remedies for delayed possession, defective construction, and unfair practices.

## Activation Triggers
Use when: Buying apartment/villa in ongoing project | Delayed possession by builder | Defective construction/deficiencies | Builder changed project plan without consent | Unregistered real estate project | Real estate agent misconduct | RERA complaint filing | Refund claim from builder | Carpet area vs super built-up area dispute | Common area encroachment.

## Core Principles

### Section 2(zn): "Real Estate Project" Defined

**"Development of building(s) for sale of apartments/plots in phases, having >500 sq.m. land area OR >8 apartments"**

**Includes:**
- Residential apartments/villas
- Commercial projects (offices, shops)
- Plotted development

**Excludes (Section 3):**
- Renovations/repairs (not adding apartments/altering FAR)
- Government projects
- Industrial/institutional projects

### Section 3: Mandatory Registration

**ALL real estate projects (ongoing + new) MUST register with RERA Authority**

**Timeline:**
- New projects: BEFORE advertising/selling
- Ongoing projects (as on July 2017): Within 3 months

**Exemptions:**
- Completion certificate already received (before RERA came into force)
- < 8 apartments AND < 500 sq.m. land

**Penalty for Non-Registration:**
- Up to 10% of project cost OR
- Imprisonment up to 3 years OR both

## Promoter Obligations

### Section 4: Registration Application

**Mandatory Information:**
1. **Project Details:**
   - Layout plans (approved by competent authority)
   - Carpet area of each apartment
   - Completion schedule
   - Land title status (ownership OR development agreement)

2. **Financial Details:**
   - Cost of project
   - Source of funds
   - Estimated completion cost

3. **Encumbrances:**
   - Existing loans, mortgages, charges on land
   - Litigation status

**Documents:**
- Land title deed OR development agreement
- Sanctioned plan/building permission
- Legal clearances (environmental, fire, etc.)

**RERA Authority:**
- Verifies documents (30 days)
- Grants registration certificate (valid till completion + 3 months)
- Assigns RERA registration number (must be displayed on all ads)

### Section 11: Promoter's Duties

**Critical Obligations:**

**1. Maintain 70% Funds in Separate Bank Account (Section 4(2)(l)(D)):**
- 70% of amounts received from allottees for THAT PROJECT
- Dedicated account (not commingled with other projects)
- Withdrawals ONLY for:
  - Land cost
  - Construction cost
  - Approved proportionate cost
- Certified by engineer/architect/chartered accountant
- Banks must report withdrawals to RERA quarterly

**Violation:** Up to 5% of estimated cost of project OR imprisonment up to 3 years

**2. Not Accept >10% as Advance Before Registration (Section 13):**
- Cannot collect more than 10% before signing agreement for sale
- Full payment schedule only after registered agreement for sale

**3. Execute Registered Agreement for Sale (Section 13):**
- Within 90 days of allotment/booking
- As per model agreement (Schedule II)
- Registration mandatory (Registration Act 1908)

**4. Timely Possession (Section 18):**
- Complete project as per completion schedule (registered with RERA)
- Handover possession on date specified in agreement
- Delay → Refund + interest (MCLR + 2%) OR compensation

**5. No Modification Without Allottee Consent (Section 14):**
- Cannot alter sanctioned plans (structural design, layout)
- Cannot change specifications/amenities
- Requires consent of 2/3 allottees + RERA approval

**6. Obtain Completion/Occupancy Certificate (Section 11(3)(d)):**
- Within timeline stated in registration
- File certificate with RERA within 30 days

**7. Rectify Defects (Section 11(4)):**
- Structural defects within 5 YEARS from possession (at no cost)
- Other defects/deficiencies as per agreement

**8. Convey Undivided Share of Land (Section 17):**
- Transfer undivided proportionate share of land to allottees
- Execute conveyance deed (flat buyers' association) within 3 months of majority formation

**9. Maintain Common Areas (Until Handover to Association) (Section 11(3)(g)):**
- Common areas, facilities, services
- Until residents' association formed + handover

### Section 5: Project Information Disclosure

**On Website + RERA Website:**
- Quarterly progress report
- Status of approvals
- Number of units booked
- Available units
- Promoter details

**Penalty for Non-Disclosure:** Rs. 5% of estimated cost of project

## Allottee Rights

### Section 18: Refund/Compensation for Delay

**Three Options (Allottee's choice):**

**Option 1: Withdraw from Project + Refund**
- Promoter refunds:
  - Entire amount paid (principal)
  - Interest @ MCLR + 2% (from date of payment till refund)
  - Compensation (if delay due to promoter's fault)
- Timeline: Within 45 days (with interest accrued till refund date)

**Option 2: Wait for Possession + Compensation**
- Continue with project
- Promoter pays interest @ MCLR + 2% for delay period
- Calculate from due date to actual possession

**Option 3: Approach RERA Authority**
- File complaint
- Authority orders refund OR compensation

**MCLR (Marginal Cost of Funds Based Lending Rate):**
- State Bank of India MCLR (1-year)
- Currently ~8.5% (varies quarterly)
- MCLR + 2% = ~10.5% effective interest

**Penalty on Promoter:**
- Interest @ MCLR + 2% (to allottee) PLUS
- Penalty up to 10% of project cost (to RERA)

### Section 19: Compensation for Defects/Deficiencies

**Structural Defects (5 Years Liability):**
- Cracks in walls/floors
- Seepage/water leakage
- Defective foundation
- Deviation from sanctioned plans

**Remedy:**
- Allottee gives notice (30 days to rectify)
- If not rectified → Complaint to RERA
- RERA can order:
  - Rectification at promoter's cost
  - Compensation for diminution in value
  - Refund (if defects severe)

**Other Deficiencies:**
- As per agreement (warranty period)

### Section 12: Allottee's Obligations

1. **Pay Installments** - As per payment schedule
2. **Take Possession** - Within 2 months of offer of possession notice
3. **Pay Proportionate Share** - Of registration charges, taxes
4. **Comply with Association Bye-Laws** - After formation

## Carpet Area vs Super Built-Up Area

### Section 2(k): "Carpet Area" Defined

**"Net usable floor area of apartment, excluding:**
- External walls
- Service shafts
- Balconies (exclusive open/covered areas)
- Verandah areas
- Exclusive terrace/open roof areas"

**Includes:**
- Internal partition walls
- Kitchen
- Bathrooms
- Utility area

### Key Principle: ALL MEASUREMENTS/CHARGES BASED ON CARPET AREA

**Section 11(2): Sale on Carpet Area Basis**
- Agreement for sale must state carpet area
- Charges based on carpet area (not super built-up)

**Super Built-Up Area (Not Recognized Under RERA):**
- Carpet area + common areas (pro-rata)
- Lobbies, lifts, staircases
- RERA prohibits charging based on super built-up

**Variation in Carpet Area (Section 13(3)):**

**If final carpet area differs from booked area:**

**Excess area (>3%):**
- Allottee pays proportionate price OR
- Refuses to accept excess area

**Deficiency (3-10%):**
- Promoter compensates allottee (price for deficit area)

**Deficiency (>10%):**
- Allottee can withdraw + full refund

## Real Estate Agents Regulation (Section 9-10)

### Section 9: Agent Registration

**Mandatory for All Real Estate Agents**

**Who is Agent (Section 2(r)):**
- Person facilitating sale/purchase of property
- For commission/fee

**Registration:**
- Apply to RERA Authority
- Fee: Rs. 10,000-50,000
- Valid: 5 years (renewable)

**Documents:**
- Identity proof
- Professional qualifications (if any)
- Consent to act as agent

**Penalty for Unregistered Agent:** Rs. 10,000 per day (up to 5% of property value)

### Section 10: Agent's Obligations

1. **Display RERA Registration Number** - On all communications
2. **Facilitate Only RERA-Registered Projects**
3. **Provide Correct Information** - No false claims/misrepresentation
4. **Maintain Records** - Transactions, commissions
5. **Not Charge More Than Agreed Commission**

**Violation:** Revocation of registration + penalty

## RERA Authority (Section 20-21)

### Section 20: Constitution

**State/Union Territory RERA Authority:**
- Chairperson (Judge/District Judge rank)
- 2+ members (expertise in real estate, finance, law, administration)
- Term: 5 years OR 65 years (whichever earlier)

**Powers (Section 37-38):**
1. Investigate complaints
2. Call for information/documents
3. Inspect projects
4. Enforce compliance
5. Adjudicate disputes
6. Impose penalties

### Section 31: Complaint Procedure

**Who Can File:**
- Allottee
- Association of allottees
- Promoter (against allottee for non-payment)
- Real estate agent (disputes)

**Against Whom:**
- Promoter (for violation of obligations)
- Allottee (for non-payment)
- Real estate agent (for misconduct)

**Forum:**
- **RERA Authority:** Regulatory violations (non-registration, 70% account breach)
- **Adjudicating Officer:** Individual disputes (delayed possession, refund, compensation)

**Timeline:**
- File within limitation (varies by nature of complaint)
- Dispose within 60 days (extendable to 90 days)

**Procedure:**
1. File complaint (online/offline)
2. Notice to respondent (15 days to reply)
3. Hearing (oral/documentary evidence)
4. Order (refund/compensation/specific performance/penalty)

**Remedies:**
- Refund (with interest)
- Compensation
- Specific performance (complete project, handover possession)
- Rectification of defects
- Penalty on promoter

### Section 40: Appeal

**Appeal to Real Estate Appellate Tribunal:**
- From RERA Authority orders
- Within 60 days
- Tribunal: Chairperson (judge) + 2 members
- Dispose within 60 days

**Further Appeal:**
- To High Court (Section 44)
- Within 60 days from Tribunal order

## Common Violations and Remedies

### Scenario 1: Delayed Possession

**Facts:**
- Possession promised: Dec 2020
- Actual possession: Dec 2023 (3 years delay)
- No valid reason (not force majeure)
- Amount paid: Rs. 50 lakhs

**Remedy (Section 18):**

**Option A - Refund:**
- Rs. 50 lakhs (principal)
- Interest @ MCLR + 2% (10.5%) for 3 years = Rs. 15.75 lakhs
- Total refund: Rs. 65.75 lakhs
- Timeline: Within 45 days

**Option B - Compensation + Possession:**
- Rs. 50 lakhs × 10.5% × 3 years = Rs. 15.75 lakhs (compensation for delay)
- Allottee gets possession + Rs. 15.75 lakhs

**RERA Penalty on Promoter:**
- Additional 10% of project cost (to RERA, separate from allottee compensation)

### Scenario 2: Unregistered Project

**Facts:**
- Builder selling apartments
- Project not registered with RERA
- Advertises without RERA registration number

**Consequences:**
- Promoter penalty: 10% of project cost OR imprisonment up to 3 years
- Agreement for sale VOID (voidable at allottee's option)
- Allottee can seek refund + interest

**Remedy:**
1. File complaint with RERA Authority
2. RERA directs registration OR cease operations
3. Penalty proceedings

### Scenario 3: Defective Construction

**Facts:**
- Possession taken in 2021
- Major seepage/cracks in 2023 (within 5 years)
- Promoter refuses to rectify

**Remedy (Section 11(4) + Section 19):**
1. Send notice to promoter (30 days to rectify)
2. If not rectified → File complaint with RERA
3. RERA orders:
   - Rectification at promoter's cost
   - Compensation for diminution in value
   - Allow allottee to engage contractor (cost from promoter)

**Timeline:** Structural defects - 5 YEARS liability (from date of possession)

### Scenario 4: Carpet Area Deficiency >10%

**Facts:**
- Booked: 1,000 sq.ft. carpet area @ Rs. 5,000/sq.ft. = Rs. 50 lakhs
- Actual: 850 sq.ft. carpet area (15% deficiency)

**Remedy (Section 13(3)):**

**Allottee can:**
- **Option 1:** Withdraw from agreement
  - Full refund: Rs. 50 lakhs + interest
- **Option 2:** Accept unit with compensation
  - Promoter refunds: 150 sq.ft. × Rs. 5,000 = Rs. 7.5 lakhs
  - Allottee pays balance only for 850 sq.ft.

### Scenario 5: 70% Account Violation

**Facts:**
- Project cost: Rs. 100 crores
- Collections from allottees: Rs. 60 crores
- Amount in 70% account: Rs. 30 crores (should be Rs. 42 crores)
- Rs. 12 crores diverted to other project

**Consequences:**
- Promoter violation: Section 4(2)(l)(D)
- Penalty: 5% of estimated project cost = Rs. 5 crores
- Imprisonment: Up to 3 years
- RERA can:
  - Freeze bank accounts
  - Appoint receiver (to complete project)
  - Debar promoter from new projects

**Remedy for Allottees:**
- File complaint
- RERA orders refund (if project delayed due to fund diversion)

## Force Majeure and Extension of Completion Date

### Section 6: Extension of Registration

**Promoter can apply for extension if:**
1. **Force majeure** - War, flood, earthquake, pandemic (COVID-19 extensions granted)
2. **Court order/Stay**
3. **Government delay** - Approvals/clearances not received on time

**Procedure:**
- Apply to RERA before expiry of registration
- RERA examines reasons
- Grants extension (if justified) + revises completion date

**COVID-19 Extensions:**
- Most state RERAs granted 6-9 months extension (2020-2021)
- Force majeure clause (pandemic = valid reason)

**Not Force Majeure:**
- Lack of funds (promoter's failure to raise funds)
- Internal delays (contractor disputes, design changes)

## Ongoing Projects (Transitional Provisions - Section 85)

**Projects Ongoing as on 1st May 2017:**
- Must register with RERA within 3 months
- Existing agreements continue (unless allottee opts for RERA benefits)

**Allottee Choice:**
- Continue under old agreement (pre-RERA) OR
- Invoke RERA provisions (refund, compensation, carpet area basis)

**Majority Approach:**
- Courts allow allottees to invoke RERA benefits even for pre-RERA bookings (if project incomplete as on May 2017)

## Key Judgments

**Fortune Infrastructure v. Trevor D'Lima (SC 2018):**
- RERA provisions beneficial legislation
- Retrospective application to ongoing projects valid
- Section 18 (refund with interest) applies to all projects (ongoing + new)

**Imperia Structures v. Anil Patni (SC 2020):**
- RERA overrides Arbitration Act
- Disputes under RERA → RERA Authority jurisdiction (not arbitration)
- Arbitration clause in builder-buyer agreement cannot oust RERA

**Kolkata West International City v. Devasis Rudra (SC 2019):**
- Flat buyers can file insolvency petition against builder (IBC)
- RERA and IBC remedies parallel (not mutually exclusive)

**Pioneer Urban Land v. Union of India (SC 2019):**
- Section 4(2)(l)(D) (70% account) constitutional
- Not arbitrary, ensures project completion

## Common Mistakes

**1. Not Verifying RERA Registration Before Booking** → Money at risk (unregistered project)

**2. Relying on Super Built-Up Area (Instead of Carpet Area)** → Paying for common areas (inflated cost)

**3. Not Executing Registered Agreement Within 90 Days** → Delay weakens legal position

**4. Accepting Possession Without Completion Certificate** → Defects/illegal construction risk

**5. Not Inspecting for Defects at Possession** → Waiver of defect claims

**6. Not Sending Defect Notice (30 Days to Promoter)** → Mandatory precondition to RERA complaint

**7. Delay in Filing RERA Complaint** → Limitation issues (file promptly)

**8. Not Checking 70% Account Compliance** → Project may be under-funded (completion risk)

**9. Accepting Promoter's Arbitrary Charges** → Demand breakdown, verify against agreement

**10. Not Joining Residents' Association** → Collective bargaining strength lost

## Checklist for Allottee (Before Booking)

**1. RERA Verification:**
- Project registered? (Check state RERA website)
- RERA registration number displayed in advertisement?
- Quarterly reports filed? (project progress)

**2. Promoter Due Diligence:**
- Promoter's track record (past projects delivered on time?)
- Land title clear? (Check registration certificate - encumbrances disclosed)
- Approvals obtained? (building plan sanctioned, environmental clearance)

**3. Agreement Review:**
- Carpet area specified (not super built-up)
- Completion date mentioned
- Payment schedule linked to construction milestones (not time-based)
- Specifications/amenities listed
- Penalty clause for delayed possession

**4. Financial Health:**
- 70% account maintained? (Ask for bank certification)
- No diversion of funds to other projects?

**5. Completion Certificate:**
- At possession, insist on completion/occupancy certificate
- Do not accept provisional possession
