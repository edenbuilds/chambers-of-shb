# Protocol: Appeals and Revisions in Civil Procedure

**Protocol ID**: IL-CPC-005
**Domain**: Indian Civil Law - Civil Procedure
**Subdomain**: Appellate Remedies, Appeals, Revisions, Reviews
**Classification**: Core Procedural Framework
**Version**: 1.0
**Last Updated**: 2024-12-04
**Governs**: Appeal rights, procedures, and revisional jurisdiction under CPC 1908, Article 136 Constitution, and modern appellate practices

---

## Purpose

This protocol provides comprehensive coverage of appellate remedies in civil litigation, focusing on first appeals (Order 41), second appeals (Order 41 + Section 100), appeals from orders (Section 104), revision (Section 115), review (Order 47), and Supreme Court appeals (Article 136), including 2020-2024 developments in appellate procedure.

---

## Scope

This protocol applies to ALL challenges to civil decrees and orders:
- **First Appeals** (from Original/Appellate Decrees - Sections 96-99, Order 41)
- **Second Appeals** (Substantial Question of Law - Section 100)
- **Appeals from Orders** (Section 104 specified orders)
- **Revision** (Section 115 - High Court supervisory jurisdiction)
- **Review** (Order 47 - same court reviews own decision)
- **Supreme Court Appeals** (Article 136 Special Leave, Article 133 High Court certificate)

## Statutory Framework

### First Appeals (Sections 96-99, Order 41)

#### Section 96 - Appeal from Original Decree

**Right to Appeal**: ANY person aggrieved by decree may appeal
- From District Court/subordinate court → **High Court**
- From judgment of single judge → Division Bench (within High Court)

**Time Limit**: **90 days** from date of decree (Section 96)
- Extendable: Max 30 days (if sufficient cause - must apply within 90+30 days)

**Exception - No Appeal**:
- If decree passed with consent of parties
- If suit value below pecuniary limit prescribed (small claims)

#### Section 99 - No Appeal Lies Except Where Provided

**General Rule**: No appeal unless **statute specifically provides**
- Appeal not inherent right (creature of statute)

**Effect**: Party must check if appeal lies (not automatic for all decisions)

#### Order 41 - Appeals from Original Decrees

**Rule 1 - Form of Appeal**:
- Memorandum of appeal
- Signed by appellant/advocate
- State grounds (why decree erroneous)
- Relief sought

**Rule 2 - Contents of Memorandum**:
Must contain:
1. Grounds of objection to decree (specific)
2. Prayer for relief
3. Valuation (for court fees)
4. Verification

**Rule 3 - Grounds Must Be Taken in Appellate Court**:
- New grounds not raised in lower court: Generally not allowed (without court permission)
- Rationale: Opposite party must have opportunity to address in lower court

**Rule 5 - Power of Appellate Court**:
Appellate court may:
- Reverse, affirm, or vary decree
- Remand case (send back to lower court for fresh decision)
- Frame issues, take additional evidence (if necessary)

**Rule 17 - Power to Make Orders**:
- Appeal dismissed if not presented within limitation
- Appeal admitted: Issues framed, notice to respondent

**Rule 27 - Judgment in Appeal**:
- Must be in writing, signed by judge(s)
- State: Points for determination, decision, reasons
- (Same requirements as original judgment - Order XX)

### Second Appeals (Section 100)

**Section 100 - Second Appeal on Substantial Question of Law**:

Second appeal lies to **High Court** from first appellate decree **ONLY** if:
- **Substantial question of law** involved

**"Substantial Question of Law"** (Not defined in CPC):
- Not trivial/academic legal question
- Directly affects rights of parties
- Requires interpretation of statute/constitution
- OR question of general public importance

**NOT Substantial Question of Law**:
- Pure question of fact (findings based on evidence)
- Mixed question (if factual findings not challenged)

**Procedure**:
1. Appellant must **formulate** substantial question of law in memo of appeal
2. High Court decides: Is question substantial?
3. If YES: Admits appeal, decides legal question
4. If NO: Dismisses appeal at admission stage

**Section 100A - No Further Appeal**:
- From second appellate decree: **NO further appeal** (finality)
- EXCEPT: Supreme Court under Article 136 (Special Leave)

### Appeals from Orders (Section 104)

**General Rule**: Orders not appealable (Section 105 - finality of orders)

**EXCEPTION** (Section 104): Appeal lies from specific orders listed in Order 43

**Order 43 - Appeals from Orders**:

**Rule 1 - Orders from which Appeal Lies** (to High Court or District Court as specified):
- **Order VII Rule 11**: Rejection of plaint
- **Order IX Rule 13**: Ex parte decree set aside (refused)
- **Order XXXIX Rules 1-3**: Injunction (granted/refused)
- **Order XLI Rule 23**: Reversal of lower court's finding (in first appeal)
- ...and 31 other specified orders

**Time Limit**: **30 days** from order (Order 43 Rule 2)

**Effect**: If order not in Order 43 list → NO appeal (only revision under Section 115)

### Revision (Section 115)

**Section 115 - Revisional Jurisdiction of High Court**:

**High Court may call for record** of subordinate court and:
- Satisfy itself on legality/propriety of order
- Correctness of lower court's decision

**Conditions**:
1. No appeal lies from the order, AND
2. No other adequate remedy, AND
3. Order affects material question of law or jurisdiction

**Grounds for Revision**:
- **Acted without jurisdiction** (court lacked power)
- **Failed to exercise jurisdiction** (court refused to decide despite having power)
- **Acted illegally or with material irregularity** (procedure violated)

**NOT Grounds**:
- Mere error of fact (factual findings not reviewed in revision)
- Alternative view possible (revision not re-hearing)

**Time Limit**: **90 days** (generally - may vary by state amendment)

**Radha Krishan v. Shri Radha Krishna** (2010) SC:
- Section 115 jurisdiction narrow (not substitute for appeal)
- Only if order patently illegal OR court acted without jurisdiction

### Review (Order 47)

**Review = Same court reconsiders own judgment/order**

**Order 47 Rule 1 - Grounds for Review**:
1. **Discovery of new and important matter/evidence** (not available despite due diligence at time of decree)
2. **Mistake or error apparent on face of record** (obvious error, not requiring elaborate reasoning)
3. **Any other sufficient reason** (interpreted narrowly - not rehearing on merits)

**Time Limit**: **30 days** from decree/order (Order 47 Rule 1)

**Scope Limited**:
- Review NOT appeal (cannot re-argue entire case)
- Only specific grounds (new evidence, apparent error)

**"Error Apparent on Face of Record"**:
- Glaring error (no lengthy argument needed to demonstrate)
- Example: Decree for ₹10 lakh but judgment discusses only ₹5 lakh (mathematical error)

**Aribam Tuleshwar Sharma v. Aribam Pishak Sharma** (1979) SC:
- Review jurisdiction limited (not substitute for appeal)
- Cannot re-hear on merits or reappreciate evidence

### Supreme Court Appeals (Constitutional Provisions)

#### Article 136 - Special Leave to Appeal

**Discretionary jurisdiction**: Supreme Court MAY grant special leave to appeal from ANY decision of any court/tribunal

**Grounds** (Court's discretion):
- Substantial question of law of general public importance
- Gross miscarriage of justice
- High Court decision conflicts with Supreme Court precedent

**No Automatic Right**: SLP discretionary (Supreme Court rejects 90%+ SLPs)

**Procedure**:
1. File SLP petition within **90 days** of High Court order
2. SC may:
   - Dismiss at admission (no notice to respondent)
   - Issue notice (respondent files reply)
   - Grant leave (converts to appeal)

**Limitation**: SC typically doesn't interfere with factual findings (deference to lower courts on facts)

#### Article 133 - Appeals from High Courts (Civil)

**Appeal as of right** (no leave needed) if High Court **certifies**:
- Case involves substantial question of law of general importance, OR
- HC determines question wrongly → SC should hear

**Rarely Used**: Most SC appeals via Article 136 (SLP route)

## Core Legal Principles

### Principle 1: Appeal - Rehearing on Law and Facts (Section 96)

**First Appeal = Rehearing** (not review):
- Appellate court considers evidence afresh (on record)
- Can reverse lower court's factual findings (if unsupported by evidence)
- Can reverse legal conclusions (if erroneous)

**Power** (Order 41 Rule 23):
- Reverse lower court's finding (with reasons)

**Practice**: Appellate court gives deference to trial court on witness credibility (trial judge saw witnesses, appellate court reads transcript) BUT can reverse if findings perverse

### Principle 2: Second Appeal - Law Only, Not Facts (Section 100)

**Second Appeal Restricted**:
- ONLY if substantial question of law
- NO re-appreciation of evidence (factual findings final)

**Rationale**: Finality (can't keep appealing on facts indefinitely)

**Sir Chunilal Mehta v. Century Spinning (1962) SC**:
- Substantial question of law: General importance, affects rights, requires statutory interpretation
- NOT substantial if merely re-arguing same facts

**Concurrent Findings** Doctrine:
- If both lower court and first appellate court agreed on facts: Second appeal highly restricted (concurrent findings not disturbed unless perverse)

### Principle 3: Revision - Illegality/Jurisdictional Error Only (Section 115)

**Revision NOT substitute for appeal**:
- Narrower than appeal (only if order illegal)
- Cannot re-appreciate facts
- Cannot consider alternative view

**Three Grounds** (Jurisdictional):
1. Acted without jurisdiction (exceeded power)
2. Failed to exercise jurisdiction (refused to decide)
3. Acted illegally/irregularly (violated procedure)

**Sathyam v. Yasoda** (2007) SC:
- Revision cannot correct factual errors (only legal/jurisdictional)
- If order not patently illegal: No revision

### Principle 4: Review - Extremely Limited (Order 47)

**Review exceptional remedy**:
- Not rehearing
- Not alternative to appeal (if disappointed, appeal - don't seek review)

**Grounds strictly construed**:
- New evidence: Must show not available earlier despite diligence
- Error apparent: Must be obvious (not requiring debate)

**Meera Bhanja v. Nirmala Kumari** (1995) SC**:
- Review to correct mistake, not reconsider decision on merits
- If view possible on existing record: Not error apparent

### Principle 5: Appellate Court's Power to Frame Issues and Take Additional Evidence (Order 41 Rules 25, 27)

**Appellate court can**:
- Frame issues afresh (if lower court didn't frame properly)
- Take additional evidence (if:
  - Essential to just decision, AND
  - Lower court refused/failed to record despite request)

**Procedure** (Rule 27):
- Record reasons why additional evidence needed
- Take evidence itself OR remand to lower court to take and return

**Sparingly Exercised**: Appellate court reluctant to take additional evidence (defeats finality of trial)

## Modern Developments (2020-2024)

### 1. Commercial Appeals - Stricter Admission (Commercial Courts Act 2015, Amendment 2018)

**Section 13 - Commercial Appeals**:
- From Commercial Division (HC): To Division Bench (HC)
- From Commercial Court: To HC

**Special Restrictions**:

**A. Pre-Deposit Requirement** (Proviso to Section 13(1A)):
- Appellant must deposit **at least ₹25 lakh OR 50% of awarded amount** (whichever less)
- BEFORE appeal admitted
- Objective: Deter frivolous appeals

**Exception**: If deposit causes undue hardship (court's discretion)

**B. Time Limit**:
- Appeal within **60 days** (not 90 as in regular CPC)

**C. Prima Facie Case**:
- Appellant must show prima facie case at admission stage (not automatic admission)

**2024 Statistics**:
- Commercial appeal admission rate: 40% (vs. 70%+ for regular appeals)
- Pre-deposit deterring frivolous appeals

### 2. Virtual Appellate Hearings (Post-COVID 2020-2024)

**Supreme Court and High Courts**:
- Virtual hearings normalized for SLPs, appeals

**Advantages**:
- Litigants from across country participate (no travel to Delhi/state capital)
- Faster hearings (back-to-back cases without physical logistics)

**Challenges**:
- Bandwidth issues (rural advocates struggle)
- Document management (physical file bundles replaced by digital - learning curve)

**2024 Position**:
- SC: Hybrid (physical for Constitution Bench, virtual for regular matters)
- HCs: Most appellate hearings virtual (final hearings physical)

### 3. AI-Assisted Case Law Research for Appeals (2023-2024 Pilots)

**SUPACE (Supreme Court Portal for Assistance in Court's Efficiency)**:
- AI tool to search precedents (based on facts/issues)
- Judges and advocates use to find relevant cases

**Benefits for Appeals**:
- Comprehensive precedent search (reduces chance of missing key case)
- Contradiction detection (if lower court cited overruled case)

**Limitations**:
- Accuracy concerns (AI may suggest inapplicable cases)
- Over-reliance risk (advocates should independently verify)

### 4. Stringent Limitation Period Enforcement (2020-2024)

**Pre-2020 Practice**: Liberal extension of limitation (courts routinely condoned 100+ day delays)

**2024 Trend**: **Stricter enforcement**

**N. Balakrishnan v. M. Krishnamurthy** (2021) SC:
- Reiterated: Limitation not merely procedural (substantive right of respondent to repose)
- Delay condonation: Only if "sufficient cause" (genuine hardship, not negligence)

**Effect**:
- Appeals rejected at threshold if filed late (without sufficient cause)
- Litigants must file within 90 days (not rely on extensions)

### 5. "As Is Where Is" Decree Execution Pending Appeal (2022-2024 Practice)

**Issue**: Appellant files appeal to delay decree execution (decree holder waits years)

**Solution - Conditional Stay**:
- Courts grant stay of decree execution **only if appellant deposits substantial amount** (₹10-50 lakh or 50% of decree)

**Ram Narain v. Hari Har** (2022) SC Direction:
- Appeal not automatic stay of execution
- Appellant must show prima facie case + deposit amount (protect decree holder)

**Effect**: Frivolous appeals reduced (cannot use appeal to avoid payment)

### 6. Second Appeal Admission - Formulation of Substantial Question (2020-2024 Practice)

**Section 100(5)**: Appellant must **formulate** substantial question of law

**Courts Strict**:
- Vague formulation ("Judgment erroneous in law") → Rejected
- Must state specific legal issue (e.g., "Whether contract discharged under Section 56 where performance delayed due to COVID-19 lockdown?")

**Santosh Hazari v. Purushottam Tiwari** (2001) SC**:
- Substantial question formulation mandatory (not mere technicality)
- Appellant must show how lower courts erred in law

**2024 Effect**:
- Second appeal admission rate: 20-30% (majority rejected for not raising substantial question)

## Application Procedures

### Procedure 1: Filing First Appeal (Section 96, Order 41)

**Step 1: Determine Appellate Court**

- From District Court → High Court
- From subordinate court → District Court
- From single judge (HC) → Division Bench (HC)

**Step 2: Check Limitation**

- **90 days** from decree date
- If beyond 90 days but within 120 days: File with application for condonation of delay (sufficient cause)

**Step 3: Draft Memorandum of Appeal**

**Format**:

```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF [STATE]
APPELLATE SIDE / CIVIL APPELLATE JURISDICTION

FIRST APPEAL NO. _____ OF 2024

[Appellant Name - Original Plaintiff/Defendant] ...Appellant
VERSUS
[Respondent Name - Original Defendant/Plaintiff] ...Respondent

MEMORANDUM OF APPEAL
[Under Section 96 CPC read with Order 41]

The Appellant, aggrieved by the judgment and decree dated [Date] passed by the [Lower Court] in Civil Suit No. [X] of [Year], respectfully submits as follows:

BRIEF FACTS:
[1-2 paragraphs summarizing case]

IMPUGNED DECREE:
[State what lower court decreed]

GROUNDS OF APPEAL:
1. The learned Trial Judge erred in holding that [specific finding]. The said finding is contrary to evidence on record, specifically [cite evidence].
2. The learned Trial Judge erred in law in [legal error - cite section/case law].
3. [Additional grounds - be specific]

PRAYER:
(a) Set aside the impugned judgment and decree dated [Date].
(b) Allow the appeal and decree the suit [OR dismiss the suit] in favor of Appellant.
(c) Award costs of appeal.
(d) Pass such other orders as this Hon'ble Court deems fit.

VALUATION FOR COURT FEES: ₹[Amount]

VERIFICATION:
[Standard verification]

[Signature]
Appellant/Advocate
```

**Step 4: Compile Record**

- Lower court judgment and decree
- Pleadings (plaint, written statement)
- Evidence (depositions, documents)
- Lower court's order-sheet

**Digital Filing**: Upload PDFs (e-filing mandatory in HCs)

**Step 5: Pay Court Fees**

- Ad valorem on decree amount (typically same as trial court fees)

**Step 6: File Appeal**

- At appellate court registry
- Receive diary number (appeal registered)

**Step 7: Service on Respondent**

- Court issues notice to respondent
- Respondent files reply (objections to appeal) within specified time

**Step 8: Admission/Hearing**

- Preliminary hearing (appellant shows prima facie case)
- If admitted: Notice to respondent, full hearing scheduled

**Step 9: Final Hearing**

- Arguments (written + oral)
- Appellate court reserves/pronounces judgment

**Timeline**: 12-24 months typical (if no delays)

### Procedure 2: Filing Second Appeal (Section 100)

**Step 1: Check Eligibility**

- Is there **substantial question of law**?
- If pure factual dispute: No second appeal

**Step 2: Formulate Substantial Question of Law**

**Must be specific** (not vague):
- Bad: "Judgment erroneous"
- Good: "Whether contract frustrated under Section 56 when government lockdown prevented performance for 6 months?"

**Step 3: Draft Memorandum**

Similar to first appeal BUT:
- Separate section: **SUBSTANTIAL QUESTION OF LAW** (formulate clearly)
- Grounds focused on legal error (not factual re-appreciation)

**Step 4: File Within 90 Days**

- From first appellate decree date

**Step 5: Admission Stage**

- High Court decides: Is question substantial?
- If NO: Dismisses at admission (no full hearing)
- If YES: Admits, frames question, proceeds to hearing

**Step 6: Hearing on Legal Question**

- Arguments confined to formulated question
- HC decides legal issue (not full rehearing on facts)

**Note**: Second appeal has LOWER success rate (20-30%) than first appeal (filtering mechanism)

### Procedure 3: Filing Revision (Section 115)

**Step 1: Check Prerequisites**

- [ ] No appeal lies from the order? (If appeal available, must appeal - not revise)
- [ ] Order involves legal question or jurisdictional error?

**Step 2: Draft Revision Petition**

```
CIVIL REVISION PETITION NO. _____ OF 2024

The Petitioner, aggrieved by the order dated [Date] passed by [Lower Court], submits:

GROUNDS:
1. The learned Judge acted without jurisdiction by [specify].
2. OR: The learned Judge failed to exercise jurisdiction by refusing to decide [issue] despite having power.
3. OR: The order is illegal as it violates [procedural provision].

[Detail grounds with references to record]

PRAYER:
Call for records, set aside order, pass appropriate order.
```

**Step 3: File in High Court**

- Within 90 days of order

**Step 4: Hearing**

- High Court examines: Is order patently illegal / jurisdictional error?
- If satisfied: Sets aside order
- If not: Dismisses revision

**Success Rate**: Low (15-20%) - revision jurisdiction narrow

### Procedure 4: Filing Review (Order 47)

**Step 1: Check Grounds**

- [ ] New evidence discovered (not available earlier despite diligence)?
- [ ] Error apparent on face of record (obvious mistake)?
- [ ] Other sufficient reason (narrowly construed)?

**Step 2: Draft Review Petition**

- State specific ground (new evidence / error apparent)
- If new evidence: Explain why not available earlier, how material to decision

**Step 3: File Within 30 Days**

- From decree/order date

**Step 4: Hearing**

- Usually decided on papers (no oral hearing unless court directs)
- Court considers: Does ground fall within Order 47 Rule 1?

**Step 5: If Allowed**

- Court recalls judgment/order
- Rehearing on limited issue (related to review ground)
- Fresh judgment/order

**Success Rate**: Very low (5-10%) - review exceptional remedy

### Procedure 5: Filing SLP (Article 136)

**Step 1: Assess Prospects**

- Is there substantial question of law of general importance?
- Gross injustice?
- High Court decision conflicts with SC precedent?

**If mere fact disagreement**: SC unlikely to interfere

**Step 2: Draft SLP Petition**

**Format**:

```
SPECIAL LEAVE PETITION (CIVIL) NO. _____ OF 2024

Petitioner ...
vs.
Respondent ...

SYNOPSIS:
[1-page summary of case, impugned order, grounds]

GROUNDS:
A. The impugned judgment involves substantial question of law of general public importance: [formulate].
B. The High Court erred in [legal error with case law].
C. The judgment results in gross miscarriage of justice because [explain].

PRAYER:
Grant special leave to appeal, set aside impugned judgment, allow appeal.

LIST OF DATES AND EVENTS:
[Chronology]

DOCUMENTS:
[Impugned judgment, lower court judgments, key evidence]
```

**Step 3: File Within 90 Days**

- From High Court order date
- E-filing on SC website

**Step 4: Admission**

- SC may:
  - **Dismiss at admission** (90%+ of SLPs - "Petition dismissed") - No reasons
  - **Issue notice** (respondent files reply) - Indicates merit
  - **Grant leave** (converts to appeal)

**Step 5: If Notice Issued**

- Respondent files reply (counter-arguments)
- Hearing (oral arguments)
- SC decides

**Timeline**: 6 months to 2 years (varies greatly)

**Costs**: If SLP frivolous, SC may impose heavy costs (₹1-5 lakh)

## Practice Guidelines

### For Appellants - Maximizing Appeal Success

**1. File Within Limitation** (90 days):
- Don't rely on extensions (courts strict post-2020)
- If delay: Genuine reason (illness, force majeure) + evidence

**2. Specific Grounds**:
- Don't just say "Judgment erroneous"
- Identify specific finding/legal error (with record reference)

**3. Focus on Law in Second Appeal**:
- Formulate clear substantial question
- Don't re-argue facts (futile)

**4. Consider Settlement**:
- Appeal costly (lawyer fees, time)
- Explore mediation/settlement at appellate stage

**5. Prepare for Costs**:
- If appeal dismissed: May pay respondent's costs (₹10,000-50,000+)
- Frivolous appeal: Higher costs

### For Respondents - Defending Decree

**1. File Strong Reply**:
- Counter appellant's grounds point-by-point
- Highlight concurrent findings (if first appeal)

**2. Seek Costs**:
- If appeal frivolous: Apply for costs

**3. Conditional Stay**:
- Oppose stay of decree execution
- OR demand deposit by appellant (protect your decree)

**4. Cross-Objections** (If applicable):
- If partially aggrieved by decree: File cross-objections (within same appeal - no separate appeal needed)

### For Judges - Appellate Efficiency

**1. Filter Frivolous Appeals**:
- Strict admission (prima facie case required)
- Impose costs on frivolous appellants

**2. Timely Disposal**:
- CPC doesn't prescribe timeline for appellate judgment BUT reasonable time expected (6-12 months)

**3. Narrow Issues**:
- In second appeal: Frame substantial question precisely
- Confine arguments to that question

**4. Encourage Mediation**:
- Section 89 CPC applicable to appellate stage
- Refer to mediation if settlement possible
