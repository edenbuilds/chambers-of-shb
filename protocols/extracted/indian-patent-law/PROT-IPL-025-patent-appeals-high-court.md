# Protocol: Patent Appeals to High Court

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-025
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Litigation & Appeals
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for patent appeals to High Courts in India following IPAB abolition (2021), including appellate jurisdiction, procedures, timelines, grounds of appeal, and practice considerations across different High Courts.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Controller decision rejection
- Opposition decision adverse
- Revocation petition outcome
- Compulsory licensing decision
- Pre-grant/post-grant opposition dismissal
- Any appealable Patent Office order

---

## Prerequisites

- Controller/IPAB order to appeal
- Understanding of IPAB abolition implications
- Knowledge of relevant High Court rules
- Appeal deadlines awareness
- Legal representation

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Post-IPAB Landscape

#### IPAB Abolition (2021):

**Tribunals Reforms Act, 2021**:
- Intellectual Property Appellate Board dissolved
- Jurisdiction transferred to High Courts
- Writ and appellate jurisdiction both available
- Pending IPAB matters transferred

#### Current Appellate Structure:

```
Patent Office Decision
        ↓
    High Court (Appellate Jurisdiction)
        ↓
    Division Bench (if applicable)
        ↓
    Supreme Court (SLP/Appeal)
```

### Step 2: High Court Jurisdiction

#### Jurisdictional High Courts:

| Patent Office Location | Appellate High Court |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Delhi | Delhi High Court |
| Mumbai | Bombay High Court |
| Chennai | Madras High Court |
| Kolkata | Calcutta High Court |

#### Section 117A - Appeals:

"Any person aggrieved by any decision, order or direction of the Controller... may prefer an appeal to the High Court"

#### Appealable Orders:
- Refusal of patent application
- Grant of patent (by opponent)
- Opposition decisions (pre-grant, post-grant)
- Revocation decisions
- Compulsory licensing decisions
- Working of patent decisions
- Amendment refusals
- Restoration refusals

### Step 3: Appeal Timeline

#### Limitation Period:

| Matter | Limitation |
|--------|------------|
| Appeal against Controller order | 3 months from order |
| Condonation of delay | High Court discretion |
| Writ petition | No specific limit (but reasonable time) |

#### Key Dates to Track:
```
Order Date → 3 months → Appeal Deadline
    ↓
Certified copy date (may extend limitation)
    ↓
Filing date with High Court
```

### Step 4: Grounds of Appeal

#### Common Grounds:

| Ground | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Error of law** | Misapplication of Patents Act |
| **Perverse finding** | Against evidence on record |
| **Procedural violation** | Natural justice breach |
| **Arbitrary decision** | Without adequate reasoning |
| **Ignoring material evidence** | Relevant evidence not considered |
| **Incorrect claim construction** | Misinterpretation of claims |

#### Technical Grounds:
- Wrong novelty assessment
- Incorrect obviousness analysis
- Misapplication of Section 3 exclusions
- Erroneous prior art interpretation
- Wrong claim interpretation

### Step 5: Appeal Procedure

#### Delhi High Court (Patent Appeals):

**Step-by-Step**:

1. **Draft Appeal Memo**:
   - Grounds of appeal
   - Relief sought
   - Facts and chronology

2. **Supporting Documents**:
   - Impugned order (certified copy)
   - Complete Patent Office record
   - Evidence relied upon
   - Relevant prior art

3. **Filing**:
   - File in appropriate division
   - Pay court fees
   - Serve respondents

4. **Admission Hearing**:
   - Prima facie case assessment
   - Notice to respondents

5. **Full Hearing**:
   - Arguments on merits
   - Evidence examination
   - Final decision

#### Court Fees (Delhi HC - indicative):
| Value | Fee |
|-------|-----|
| Up to ₹1 lakh | ₹520 |
| ₹1-5 lakh | ₹1,040 |
| ₹5 lakh - ₹50 lakh | Ad valorem |
| Above ₹50 lakh | ₹15,600 + ad valorem |

### Step 6: High Court-Specific Practices

#### Delhi High Court:
- Most active in patent matters
- Specialized IP Division
- Technical assessors available
- Strong patent jurisprudence

#### Bombay High Court:
- IP commercial courts
- Growing patent caseload
- Mumbai jurisdiction for West India filings

#### Madras High Court:
- Chennai Patent Office appeals
- IP benches designated
- Strong pharmaceutical patent jurisprudence

#### Calcutta High Court:
- Kolkata Patent Office appeals
- Less frequent patent appeals
- General civil procedures apply

### Step 7: Writ Jurisdiction Alternative

#### Writ Petition Under Article 226:

**When to Use**:
- Challenge Patent Office procedure
- Violation of natural justice
- Unreasonable delay in examination
- Statutory interpretation questions
- Constitutional questions

**Types of Writs**:
| Writ | Use |
|------|-----|
| Certiorari | Quash invalid order |
| Mandamus | Direct performance of duty |
| Prohibition | Prevent ultra vires action |

**Caution**: Writ not alternative to appeal where appeal remedy available

### Step 8: Stay Applications

#### Interim Stay of Controller Order:

**Requirements**:
- Prima facie case
- Balance of convenience
- Irreparable injury

**Types of Stay**:
| Type | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Stay of grant | Patent not published |
| Stay of revocation | Patent continues |
| Stay of opposition decision | Status quo maintained |

## Appeal Drafting Template

```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF [DELHI/BOMBAY/MADRAS/CALCUTTA]

FAO(OS) No. ___/2026

IN THE MATTER OF:
[Appellant Name]                           ...APPELLANT
        Versus
[Respondent 1/Controller of Patents]       ...RESPONDENT(S)

APPEAL UNDER SECTION 117A OF THE PATENTS ACT, 1970

1. PARTICULARS OF THE APPEAL
   - Patent/Application No.: [...]
   - Impugned Order dated: [...]
   - Date of receipt of certified copy: [...]

2. BRIEF FACTS
   [Chronological narrative]

3. GROUNDS OF APPEAL

   A. The learned Controller erred in law by...
   B. The finding that [...] is perverse because...
   C. The Controller failed to consider...
   D. The decision violates principles of natural justice...

4. RELIEF SOUGHT
   a) Set aside the impugned order dated [...]
   b) Direct the Controller to [grant/reinstate/reconsider]
   c) Costs of this appeal
   d) Any other relief

5. INTERIM RELIEF SOUGHT
   [If stay required]

DECLARATION
[Verification and signature]

LIST OF DATES AND EVENTS
[Chronological table]
```

## Common Appeal Mistakes

1. **Missing limitation**: File within 3 months
2. **Incomplete record**: Include all Patent Office documents
3. **Vague grounds**: Be specific about errors
4. **Wrong High Court**: File where Patent Office located
5. **No certified copy**: Required for limitation calculation
6. **Ignoring writ option**: Consider for procedural issues
