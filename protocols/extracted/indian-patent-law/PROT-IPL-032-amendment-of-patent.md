# Protocol: Amendment of Patent and Application

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-032
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Prosecution
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for amending patent applications and granted patents under Sections 57-59, including amendment timing, scope limitations, procedures, and strategic considerations for narrowing claims.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Controller requests claim amendment
- Claim scope refinement needed
- Opposition response requiring amendment
- Post-grant correction required
- Clerical/typographical error correction

---

## Prerequisites

- Pending application or granted patent
- Understanding of amendment limitations
- Knowledge of added matter prohibition
- Specification as filed available

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Amendment Framework Overview

#### Key Sections:

| Section | Subject | Stage |
|---------|---------|-------|
| **57** | Amendment of application | Pre-grant |
| **58** | Amendment of granted patent | Post-grant |
| **59** | Supplementary provisions | Scope limitations |

#### Section 59 - Fundamental Limitation:

"No amendment... shall be allowed except by way of disclaimer, correction or explanation, and no amendment shall be allowed the effect of which would be that the specification as amended would claim or describe matter not in substance disclosed in the specification before the amendment"

### Step 2: Types of Amendments

#### Pre-Grant Amendments (Section 57):

| Type | Purpose | Allowed? |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Disclaimer** | Exclude subject matter | Yes |
| **Correction** | Fix errors | Yes |
| **Explanation** | Clarify meaning | Yes |
| **New matter** | Add new disclosure | NO |
| **Broadening claims** | Expand scope | Generally NO |

#### Post-Grant Amendments (Section 58):

| Amendment | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Specification | Application to Controller |
| Claims | Must not broaden |
| Drawings | Correct errors only |
| Title | Correct errors |

### Step 3: Amendment Limitations

#### "Added Matter" Prohibition:

**Test**: Would the amendment result in the specification claiming or describing matter NOT in substance disclosed before?

**Examples**:

| Scenario | Allowed? | Reason |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Delete claim element | Usually yes | Narrowing, no new matter |
| Add new claim element | No | New matter |
| Add new example | No | New disclosure |
| Correct typo | Yes | No substantive change |
| Clarify ambiguity | Yes | Using original disclosure |
| Substitute term | Depends | Must be equivalent |

#### Claim Amendment Scope:

```
Original Claim: A device comprising A, B, and C
                        ↓
Allowed: A device comprising A, B, C, and D (if D in spec)
Allowed: A device comprising A and B (narrower - disclaimer)
NOT Allowed: A device comprising A, B, and E (if E not in spec)
NOT Allowed: A device comprising A only (if broadens)
```

### Step 4: Pre-Grant Amendment Procedure

#### Voluntary Amendment:

**Timing**: Before grant

**Procedure**:
1. File request for amendment (no specific form)
2. Provide clean and marked-up specification
3. Explain basis in original disclosure
4. Pay fee (if applicable)
5. Controller examines
6. Allowed if compliant with Section 59

#### Amendment in Response to FER:

**Timing**: With FER response (within 6 months)

**Best Practices**:
- Address each objection
- Provide claim chart (original vs. amended)
- Point to support in specification
- Explain why amendment overcomes objection

#### Amendment Request Format:

```
REQUEST FOR AMENDMENT
Application No.: [...]

1. AMENDMENT DETAILS

Original Claim 1:
"A device comprising A, B, and C"

Amended Claim 1:
"A device comprising A, B, C, and D, wherein D is [...]"

2. BASIS IN SPECIFICATION

Support for element D is found at:
- Page [...], paragraph [...]: "[quoted text]"
- Figure [...]: [description]

3. COMPLIANCE WITH SECTION 59

The amendment:
- Is by way of correction/explanation
- Does not add new matter
- Is supported by original disclosure

4. REASON FOR AMENDMENT

[Response to FER objection / voluntary improvement]
```

### Step 5: Post-Grant Amendment Procedure

#### Section 58 - Amendment by Patentee:

**Application to Controller**:
- File application for amendment
- Publish in Patent Office Journal
- Open for opposition (3 months)
- Controller hearing if opposed
- Controller decision

#### Publication and Opposition:

```
Amendment Application Filed
        ↓
Published in Patent Journal
        ↓
3-month opposition period
        ↓
Opposition Filed? → Hearing → Decision
        ↓
No Opposition → Amendment allowed
```

#### Amendment During Infringement/Revocation:

**Section 58(4)**:
If amendment sought during infringement suit or revocation proceeding:
- Court/Controller may allow amendment
- Subject to Section 59 limitations
- May affect cost orders
- Discretionary allowance

### Step 6: Correction of Errors

#### Clerical Errors:

**Rule 127 - Correction of Clerical Errors**:
- Typographical errors
- Obvious mistakes
- Formal errors

**Procedure**:
- Application to Controller
- Evidence of error
- Proposed correction
- No opposition for obvious errors

#### Types of Correctable Errors:

| Error Type | Example | Correctable? |
|------------|---------|--------------|
| Spelling mistake | "Conposition" → "Composition" | Yes |
| Wrong patent number | In cross-reference | Yes |
| Missing word | Obviously omitted | Usually yes |
| Calculation error | If obvious | Yes |
| Substantive change | Changes meaning | No |

### Step 7: Strategic Amendment Considerations

#### When to Amend:

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| FER objection to claims | Amend to overcome |
| Prior art discovered | Narrow claims proactively |
| Opposition filed | Consider responsive amendment |
| Infringement suit | Amend to target infringer (cautiously) |
| Validity challenge | Narrow to valid scope |

#### Amendment Strategy:

**Prosecution Stage**:
- Maintain broadest defensible claims
- Have fallback dependent claims
- Amend only as necessary

**Post-Grant**:
- Amend sparingly (triggers opposition)
- Ensure validity strengthened
- Consider public interest considerations

---

## Common Amendment Mistakes

1. **Adding new matter**: Fatal - amendment rejected
2. **Broadening claims post-grant**: Not allowed
3. **Missing deadline**: Lost opportunity
4. **No support citation**: Burden on applicant
5. **Ignoring opposition period**: May face challenge
6. **Failing to explain basis**: Examiner rejection

---

## Amendment During PCT National Phase

### Special Considerations:

**Rule 20(4) - Amendments at Entry**:
- Amendments allowed at national phase entry
- Cannot exceed international scope
- Must comply with Section 59

**Timing**:
- With national phase entry (31 months)
- Before examination commences
