# Protocol: Government Use of Patents (Sections 100-102)

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-026
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Compulsory Mechanisms
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for government use of patented inventions under Sections 100-102 of the Patents Act, including use for government purposes, acquisition of patents, and secrecy directions, with focus on public interest applications.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Government need for patented technology
- National emergency or defense need
- Public health crisis response
- Government procurement decision
- National security considerations
- Secrecy order on patent application

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of government use provisions
- Knowledge of Section 100-102 framework
- Awareness of procedural requirements
- Understanding of compensation mechanisms

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Government Use Framework

#### Three Key Provisions:

| Section | Subject | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **100** | Government use of patents | Use without consent |
| **101** | Acquisition of patents | Compulsory purchase |
| **102** | Secrecy directions | National security |

#### Section 100 - Use for Government Purposes:

"Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, at any time after an application for a patent has been filed... any department of the Central Government... may use the invention for the purposes of Government..."

### Step 2: Section 100 - Government Use

#### Scope of Government Use:

**Who Can Use**:
- Central Government departments
- State Governments (with Central authorization)
- Authorized third parties (contractors)

**When**:
- After patent application filed (even before grant)
- At any time during patent term
- Without negotiation with patentee

**For What**:
- "Purposes of Government"
- Includes defense, public utilities
- Public health emergencies
- Any governmental function

#### What "Purposes of Government" Includes:

| Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Defense | Military equipment production |
| Public health | Epidemic drug production |
| Infrastructure | Public utility equipment |
| Agriculture | Food security technology |
| Education | Research and teaching |

### Step 3: Section 100 Procedure

#### Government Use Authorization:

```
Step 1: Identify patented invention needed
        ↓
Step 2: Government department determines necessity
        ↓
Step 3: Authorization by appropriate authority
        ↓
Step 4: Use commences (may be immediate)
        ↓
Step 5: Intimation to patentee
        ↓
Step 6: Remuneration determination
```

#### Authorization Requirements:
- Written authorization preferred
- Can be retrospective for ongoing use
- No prior negotiation required
- No prior approval from patentee

### Step 4: Remuneration for Government Use

#### Section 100(3) - Compensation:

"[T]he terms [shall be] agreed upon between [Government] and the patentee... or in default of agreement [determined by] the High Court"

#### Remuneration Factors:
1. Scope of use
2. Duration of use
3. Commercial value
4. Investment by patentee
5. Comparable licenses
6. Economic circumstances

#### Determination Process:
```
Negotiation between Government and Patentee
        ↓
    Agreement → Compensation paid
        ↓
    No Agreement → High Court determination
```

### Step 5: Section 101 - Acquisition of Patents

#### Compulsory Acquisition:

**Section 101(1)**:
"Where... the Central Government is satisfied that a patent... should be acquired... for a public purpose, [it] may [acquire] the patent"

#### Grounds for Acquisition:
- National defense
- Public interest
- Food security
- Public health
- Any public purpose

#### Acquisition Procedure:

1. **Central Government Notification**:
   - Published in Official Gazette
   - Specifies patent to be acquired
   - States public purpose

2. **Effect of Acquisition**:
   - Patent vests in Central Government
   - All rights transferred
   - Existing licenses affected

3. **Compensation**:
   - Fair compensation
   - Determined by agreement or arbitration
   - Market value consideration

### Step 6: Section 102 - Secrecy Directions

#### National Security Secrecy:

**When Applied**:
- Defense-related inventions
- Atomic energy matters
- National security concerns

**Procedure**:

```
Patent application filed
        ↓
Controller identifies sensitive subject
        ↓
Reference to Central Government
        ↓
Government directs secrecy (Section 35)
        ↓
Application prohibited from publication
        ↓
Foreign filing restricted (Section 39)
        ↓
Periodic review (Section 36)
```

#### Section 102 Compensation:
- Compensation for loss due to secrecy
- Period of secrecy considered
- Agreement or High Court determination

### Step 7: COVID-19 Context and Recent Applications

#### Government Use During Pandemic:

**Potential Applications**:
- Drug production for epidemic
- Vaccine manufacturing
- Medical device production
- Diagnostic technology

**Section 100 vs. Compulsory Licensing**:

| Aspect | Section 100 | Section 84 CL |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Who uses | Government | Third party |
| Prior negotiation | No | Yes (generally) |
| Controller application | No | Yes |
| Speed | Immediate | Months |
| Compensation | Later determined | Pre-determined |

#### Recommendation for Emergencies:
Section 100 provides faster mechanism for government response than Section 84 compulsory licensing.

## Legal Analysis: Government Use Scope

### "Purposes of Government" Interpretation:

**Broad Interpretation** (Preferred):
- All governmental functions
- Public utilities
- Public welfare activities
- Defense and security
- Public health programs

**Narrow Interpretation** (Rejected):
- Only sovereign functions
- Only defense purposes

### Third-Party Authorization:

Section 100(1): "...any person authorized in writing by a Government department"

**Includes**:
- Government contractors
- PSUs (Public Sector Undertakings)
- Authorized manufacturers
- Research institutions

## Template: Government Use Authorization

```
GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
MINISTRY OF [HEALTH/DEFENSE/COMMERCE]

AUTHORIZATION FOR USE OF PATENTED INVENTION
(Under Section 100 of the Patents Act, 1970)

Authorization No.: [Number]
Date: [Date]

WHEREAS:
1. Patent No. IN [Number] relates to [invention description];
2. The patented invention is required for government purposes, namely [specify purpose];
3. The Central Government considers it necessary to authorize use of said invention;

NOW THEREFORE:
Under Section 100 of the Patents Act, 1970, [Department/Entity] is hereby authorized to use the invention covered by Patent No. [Number] for the purposes of [specify].

TERMS:
1. Scope: [Description of authorized use]
2. Duration: [Period]
3. Notification: Patentee shall be notified within [30] days
4. Compensation: To be determined per Section 100(3)

Authorized by:
[Name]
[Designation]
[Department]
```

## Comparison: Government Use Mechanisms

| Feature | Section 100 | Section 84 CL | Section 92 |
|---------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| User | Government | Any person | Any person |
| Purpose | Govt purposes | Non-working | Emergency |
| Prior negotiation | No | Yes | No |
| Controller role | None | Full | Notification |
| Speed | Immediate | 6+ months | Fast |
| Compensation | Later | Pre-set | Later |

## Common Government Use Mistakes

1. **No documentation**: Proper authorization essential
2. **Excessive scope**: Use limited to stated purpose
3. **Ignoring compensation**: Patentee entitled to remuneration
4. **No patentee notice**: Required post-use
5. **Confusing with CL**: Different mechanism
6. **State Government authority**: Needs Central authorization
