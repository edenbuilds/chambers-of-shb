# Protocol: Patent Working Requirements (Form 27)

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-020
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for patent working statement requirements under Section 146 and Rule 131, including Form 27 filing procedures, working definitions, consequences of non-working, and recent developments including proposed amendments.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Annual Form 27 filing requirement
- Receiving notice for non-filing
- Compulsory licensing defense preparation
- Working status assessment
- Commercial exploitation documentation

---

## Prerequisites

- Granted Indian patent
- Production/import records
- Licensing agreement details (if applicable)
- Sales and revenue data
- Understanding of "working" definition

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Working Requirements

#### Legal Framework:
- **Section 83**: General principles on working
- **Section 146**: Patentee to furnish information
- **Rule 131**: Statement regarding working of patent

#### Policy Rationale:
- Patents granted as social contract
- Quid pro quo: monopoly for disclosure AND working
- Public interest in availability of patented products
- Data for compulsory licensing decisions

### Step 2: Form 27 Requirements

#### Who Must File:
- Every patentee (including joint patentees)
- Every licensee (including compulsory licensees)
- Through authorized agent if applicable

#### What to Disclose:

| Field | Information Required |
|-------|---------------------|
| Patent details | Number, date of grant, title |
| Working status | Worked/not worked in India |
| Manner of working | Manufacture/import/license |
| Quantum | Units produced/imported |
| Revenue | Value of production/import |
| Geographic extent | Where worked in India |
| Meeting public demand | Adequate quantity at reasonable price |
| Licensing details | Licensees and terms |
| Reasons for non-working | If applicable |

### Step 3: Filing Procedure

#### Timeline:
- **Filing period**: Within 6 months from end of each calendar year
- **Due date**: June 30 for previous calendar year
- **First filing**: After calendar year of grant

#### How to File:
1. Login to Indian Patent Office e-filing portal
2. Navigate to Form 27 submission
3. Enter patent details
4. Provide working information
5. Upload supporting documents (if any)
6. Pay filing fee
7. Submit and obtain acknowledgment

#### Fees:
| Entity | E-filing Fee |
|--------|--------------|
| Natural Person/Startup | ₹800 |
| Small Entity | ₹2,000 |
| Large Entity | ₹4,000 |

### Step 4: What Constitutes "Working"

#### Definition (Per Section 83):
Working includes:
- Manufacture in India
- Import and sale in India
- Licensing for manufacture in India

#### Adequate Working Factors:
1. **Reasonable requirements**: Is public demand met?
2. **Reasonable price**: Affordable to public?
3. **Availability**: Product accessible in India?
4. **Maximum extent**: Territory covered?

### Step 5: Consequences of Non-Filing

#### Administrative Consequences:
- Reminder notices from Patent Office
- Potential inquiry under Section 146
- Reputational impact

#### Legal Consequences:
- Evidence in compulsory licensing application
- Factor in public interest determination
- Cannot claim patent "worked" without proof

#### Penalty Provisions:
- Section 122: Contravention of Section 146
- Fine up to ₹10 lakh

### Step 6: Common Working Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Manufacturing in India
```
Working Status: Worked
Manner: Manufactured in India
Details: [Factory location, units produced, sales value]
```

#### Scenario 2: Import Only
```
Working Status: Worked (through import)
Manner: Imported and sold
Details: [Import quantity, value, distribution]
Note: Import alone may not satisfy "working" for Section 84
```

#### Scenario 3: Licensed to Third Party
```
Working Status: Worked through licensee
Manner: Licensed for manufacture
Details: [Licensee name, license terms, production data]
```

#### Scenario 4: Not Worked
```
Working Status: Not worked
Reasons: [Development stage/regulatory approval pending/
          market conditions/commercial decision]
Steps taken: [Plans to work/licensing efforts]
```

### Step 7: Strategic Considerations

#### For Compulsory Licensing Defense:
- Maintain detailed working records
- Document efforts to meet public demand
- Keep reasonable pricing evidence
- Track licensing negotiations

#### For Portfolio Management:
- Assess working status annually
- Consider licensing to ensure working
- Plan manufacturing partnerships
- Document R&D activities if not yet commercialized

## Form 27 Template

```
FORM 27
STATEMENT REGARDING WORKING OF PATENTED INVENTION

Patent No.: [Number]
Year of Grant: [Year]
Calendar Year: [Reporting Year]

Part A: Patent Details
- Title: [...]
- Patentee: [...]
- Date of Grant: [...]

Part B: Working Status
□ Worked in India
□ Not Worked in India

Part C: Manner of Working (if worked)
□ Manufactured in India
  - Location(s): [...]
  - Quantity: [...]
  - Value: ₹[...]

□ Imported to India
  - Quantity: [...]
  - Value: ₹[...]

□ Licensed for Manufacture
  - Licensee(s): [...]
  - License terms: [...]

Part D: Public Requirement Satisfaction
- Demand met: [Yes/No/Partially]
- Price reasonability: [...]
- Territory covered: [...]

Part E: Reasons for Non-Working (if applicable)
[Detailed explanation]

Part F: Future Plans
[Steps being taken to work the patent]

Declaration:
I/We declare that the above information is true and correct.

Signature: [...]
Date: [...]
```

---

## Common Form 27 Mistakes

1. **Missing deadline**: File by June 30 annually
2. **Incomplete data**: Fill all applicable fields
3. **No documentary support**: Maintain backup records
4. **Ignoring licensee obligation**: Each licensee must file
5. **Inconsistent reporting**: Align with financial records
