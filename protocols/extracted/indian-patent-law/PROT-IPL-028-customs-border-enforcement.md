# Protocol: Customs Border Enforcement

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-028
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Enforcement

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for customs border measures for patent enforcement in India, including IPR (Imported Goods) Rules 2007, recording procedures, detention and seizure mechanisms, and coordination with customs authorities.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Infringing goods being imported
- Counterfeiting detected at border
- Parallel import concerns
- Customs recordation planning
- Border seizure request

## Prerequisites

- Granted Indian patent
- Evidence of infringement
- Knowledge of customs procedures
- Understanding of IPR Rules 2007
- Coordination with customs broker

## Core Process

### Step 1: Legal Framework

#### Intellectual Property Rights (Imported Goods) Enforcement Rules, 2007:

**Authority**: Ministry of Finance, Department of Revenue

**Scope**:
- Applies to imported goods
- Covers patents, trademarks, copyrights, designs
- Customs detention and seizure
- Destruction of infringing goods

**Key Provisions**:
- Rule 3: Right holder registration
- Rule 4: Suspension of clearance
- Rule 5: Inspection and examination
- Rule 6: Release or destruction

### Step 2: IPR Recordation with Customs

#### Rule 3 - Notice to Customs:

**Who Can File**:
- Patent owner (patentee)
- Exclusive licensee
- Authorized agent

**What to File (Form IPR-1)**:

| Information | Details Required |
|-------------|------------------|
| Patent details | Number, date, title, claims |
| Ownership | Patentee name, address |
| Authorization | If filed by agent |
| Product description | What's covered by patent |
| Infringing goods | Expected imports |
| Evidence | Samples, images, technical description |
| Ports | Where infringement expected |
| Security | Bank guarantee |

#### Form IPR-1 Components:

1. **Right holder particulars**
2. **IPR details** (patent number, date of grant)
3. **Product description**
4. **Description of suspected infringing goods**
5. **Expected ports of entry**
6. **Indemnity undertaking**
7. **Security/bank guarantee**

### Step 3: Customs Recordation Procedure

#### Filing Process:

```
Prepare Form IPR-1 with supporting documents
        ↓
File with Commissioner of Customs (HQ)
        ↓
Provide security/bank guarantee
        ↓
Customs verifies and accepts recording
        ↓
Information circulated to all ports
        ↓
Recording valid for 5 years (renewable)
```

#### Required Documents:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Patent certificate | Proof of rights |
| Claim chart | Technical identification |
| Sample products | Genuine vs. infringing |
| Power of attorney | If agent filing |
| Indemnity bond | Protect customs |
| Bank guarantee | Security (typically ₹1-10 lakh) |

#### Validity:
- 5 years from recording
- Renewable
- Can be revoked on patent expiry

### Step 4: Customs Detention (Ex-Officio/On Request)

#### Rule 4 - Suspension of Clearance:

**Ex-Officio Action** (Customs initiated):
- Customs identifies suspected goods
- Notifies right holder
- Right holder must respond within 10 working days

**On Request** (Right holder initiated):
- Right holder files complaint
- Provides prima facie evidence
- Customs detains goods

#### Detention Notice:
```
CUSTOMS DETENTION NOTICE
(Under IPR Rules, 2007)

To: [Importer Name]
    [Right Holder Name]

Bill of Entry No.: [...]
Goods Description: [...]
Quantity: [...]
Origin: [...]

Notice is hereby given that the above goods have been detained
on suspicion of infringing Patent No. IN [...] owned by [...].

Right Holder: Respond within 10 working days
Importer: May provide counter-evidence

Commissioner of Customs
Port: [...]
Date: [...]
```

### Step 5: Examination and Determination

#### Rule 5 - Inspection:

**Right Holder Rights**:
- Inspect detained goods
- Take samples (with approval)
- Provide technical analysis
- Submit expert opinion

**Importer Rights**:
- Respond to infringement claim
- Provide evidence of authorization
- Challenge patent validity
- Request release

#### Technical Examination:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Customs shares product details |
| 2 | Right holder examines samples |
| 3 | Technical comparison report |
| 4 | Claims mapping |
| 5 | Infringement determination |

### Step 6: Disposition of Goods

#### Rule 6 - Release or Destruction:

**Outcome Options**:

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Infringement confirmed | Destruction or re-export |
| No infringement | Release to importer |
| Right holder fails to act | Release after 10 days |
| Litigation pending | Hold pending court order |

#### Destruction Procedure:
1. Infringement confirmed
2. Importer given opportunity to be heard
3. Destruction order issued
4. Goods destroyed under supervision
5. Certificate of destruction issued

### Step 7: Coordination with Litigation

#### Customs Action + Court Action:

**Strategy**:
```
Customs detention (border)
        +
Civil suit for infringement (court)
        +
Interim injunction application
        =
Comprehensive enforcement
```

#### Court Involvement:
- Customs detention is administrative
- Court order for permanent remedy
- Anton Piller orders for evidence
- Injunction to prevent future imports

## Border Enforcement Flowchart

```
Infringing Goods Imported
        ↓
Customs Detection (Ex-Officio) OR Right Holder Complaint
        ↓
Detention Notice Issued
        ↓
Right Holder Notified (10 days to respond)
        ↓
┌─────────────────┬─────────────────┐
│   Right Holder  │   Right Holder  │
│   Confirms      │   No Response   │
│   Infringement  │                 │
├─────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│   Examination   │   Goods Released│
│   & Analysis    │   to Importer   │
└─────────────────┴─────────────────┘
        ↓
Infringement Determination
        ↓
┌─────────────────┬─────────────────┐
│   Infringement  │   No           │
│   Confirmed     │   Infringement │
├─────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│   Destruction   │   Release      │
│   or Re-export  │   to Importer  │
└─────────────────┴─────────────────┘
```

## Patent-Specific Challenges

### Technical Complexity:

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Claim interpretation | Detailed claim chart |
| Technical expertise | Expert declarations |
| Process patents | Difficult at border |
| Component infringement | Full product analysis |

### Process Patents:
- Section 104A: Reversal of burden of proof
- But difficult to assess at customs
- May require court involvement

### Parallel Imports:
- India follows international exhaustion
- Genuine goods may be imported
- Price differential motivation
- Limited customs remedy

## Template: IPR Recordation Application

```
FORM IPR-1
APPLICATION FOR RECORDATION OF IPR WITH CUSTOMS

To: The Commissioner of Customs
    [Address]

1. APPLICANT DETAILS
   Name: [...]
   Address: [...]
   Status: Owner/Exclusive Licensee/Agent

2. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY DETAILS
   Type: Patent
   Registration No.: IN [...]
   Date of Grant: [...]
   Valid Until: [...]
   Title: [...]

3. GOODS COVERED
   [Technical description of patented product]

4. SUSPECTED INFRINGING GOODS
   Description: [...]
   Origin Countries: [China, others]
   Manufacturers: [If known]
   Importers: [If known]

5. DISTINGUISHING FEATURES
   Genuine: [Features]
   Infringing: [How to identify]

6. PORTS OF EXPECTED ENTRY
   [Mumbai, Chennai, Delhi ICD, etc.]

7. SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS
   □ Patent certificate (certified copy)
   □ Claim chart
   □ Sample products (genuine)
   □ Images/technical drawings
   □ Power of attorney
   □ Indemnity bond
   □ Bank guarantee

8. DECLARATION
   I declare that the information provided is accurate and
   undertake to indemnify Customs against any liability.

Signature: [...]
Date: [...]
```

## Common Border Enforcement Mistakes

1. **No recordation**: Must file Form IPR-1 first
2. **Inadequate claim chart**: Essential for identification
3. **Missing deadline**: 10 days to respond
4. **No bank guarantee**: Required security
5. **Process patent reliance**: Difficult at border
6. **Ignoring importer response**: Must counter arguments
