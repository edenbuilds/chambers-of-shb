# Protocol: Evidence Collection and Preservation

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-029
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Litigation Support
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for evidence collection and preservation in patent matters, including Anton Piller orders, John Doe orders, electronic evidence, expert evidence, and documentary preservation for infringement and validity proceedings.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Patent infringement suspected
- Pre-litigation evidence gathering
- Court-ordered discovery
- Opposition/revocation evidence needs
- Expert witness preparation

---

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of evidence requirements
- Knowledge of Indian procedural law
- Awareness of electronic evidence rules
- Expert witness identification

## Core Process

### Step 1: Types of Evidence in Patent Cases

#### Evidence Categories:

| Category | Examples | Use |
|----------|----------|-----|
| **Documentary** | Patents, publications, contracts | Prior art, ownership |
| **Technical** | Claim charts, product analysis | Infringement |
| **Expert** | Technical opinions, declarations | Interpretation |
| **Electronic** | Source code, CAD files, emails | Technical proof |
| **Physical** | Product samples, prototypes | Comparison |
| **Testimonial** | Inventor statements, witness | Prosecution history |

### Step 2: Pre-Litigation Evidence Collection

#### Self-Help Evidence:

**Market Purchase/Trap Purchase**:
```
1. Identify suspected infringing product
2. Purchase from open market
3. Document purchase (receipt, invoice)
4. Preserve chain of custody
5. Technical analysis
6. Comparison with patent claims
```

**Documentation Requirements**:
- Date and place of purchase
- Seller identification
- Price paid
- Witness to purchase (recommended)
- Sealed sample preservation

#### Test Purchase Protocol:
- Use neutral third party if possible
- Video/photo documentation
- Notarized purchase memo
- Laboratory analysis

### Step 3: Anton Piller Orders (Search & Seizure)

#### Definition:
Ex parte civil search order allowing inspection/seizure of evidence before defendant aware of proceedings.

#### Requirements (Indian Courts):

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Strong prima facie case | Clear evidence of infringement |
| Real risk of destruction | Evidence of intent to destroy |
| Serious actual/potential damage | Significant harm |
| No adequate alternative | Other remedies insufficient |
| Proportionality | Scope justified |

#### Procedure:

```
1. Ex parte application (without notice to defendant)
2. Detailed affidavit with evidence
3. Court approval (in chambers)
4. Appointment of Local Commissioner
5. Execution at defendant's premises
6. Inventory and seizure
7. Return to court with report
```

#### Anton Piller Application Contents:

1. **Detailed facts**: Infringement evidence
2. **Prima facie case**: Claim charts, analysis
3. **Risk of destruction**: Why ex parte needed
4. **Premises to be searched**: Specific addresses
5. **Items to be seized**: Detailed list
6. **Undertakings**: Damages if wrongful
7. **Proposed Local Commissioner**: Name suggestion

### Step 4: John Doe Orders

#### Definition:
Order against unknown defendants ("Ashok Kumar" in India).

#### When to Use:
- Online infringement (unknown infringers)
- Multiple unknown manufacturers
- Counterfeit networks
- Pre-emptive protection (trade fairs)

#### Application:
```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF [DELHI]

Suit No. ___/2026

[Plaintiff]
        vs.
John Doe/Ashok Kumar & Others

APPLICATION FOR JOHN DOE ORDER

1. Plaintiff is owner of Patent No. IN [...]
2. Infringement by unknown parties detected
3. [Evidence of infringement]
4. Identity of infringers unknown
5. Irreparable harm if not restrained

PRAYER:
(a) Injunction against John Doe and any person
    manufacturing/selling infringing products
(b) Direction to internet platforms to take down
(c) Appointment of Local Commissioner
```

### Step 5: Electronic Evidence

#### Information Technology Act, 2000:

**Section 65B - Admissibility**:
- Computer output admissible if Section 65B certificate provided
- Certificate from responsible officer
- Details of computer system
- Proper working confirmation

#### Section 65B Certificate Template:

```
CERTIFICATE UNDER SECTION 65B
INDIAN EVIDENCE ACT, 1872

I, [Name], [Designation] of [Organization], hereby certify:

1. The electronic record described below was produced by
   a computer [description of system];

2. During the relevant period, the computer was in regular
   use and was operating properly;

3. The information contained in the electronic record was
   fed into the computer in the ordinary course of activities;

4. The output is a reproduction of the original electronic record.

Electronic Record Description:
- Nature: [Emails/source code/database records]
- Date range: [...]
- Format: [...]
- Storage medium: [...]

Dated: [...]
Signature: [...]
Designation: [...]
```

#### Types of Electronic Evidence in Patents:

| Type | Relevance |
|------|-----------|
| Source code | Software patent infringement |
| CAD/design files | Product patent infringement |
| Manufacturing data | Process patent infringement |
| Emails | Intent, knowledge, licensing |
| Websites | Sales evidence, public disclosure |
| Social media | Marketing, public use |

### Step 6: Expert Evidence

#### Technical Experts:

**Role**:
- Claim construction
- Infringement analysis
- Validity opinion
- Damages calculation

**Expert Selection Criteria**:
- Technical expertise in field
- Patent law understanding
- Litigation experience
- Credibility/reputation
- No conflict of interest

#### Expert Report Contents:

```
EXPERT REPORT

1. EXPERT QUALIFICATIONS
   - Education
   - Experience
   - Publications
   - Prior testimony

2. SCOPE OF ENGAGEMENT
   - Questions addressed
   - Materials reviewed
   - Methodology used

3. TECHNICAL BACKGROUND
   - Technology explanation
   - State of the art
   - Prior art landscape

4. OPINIONS
   - Claim construction
   - Infringement analysis (element-by-element)
   - Validity opinion (if applicable)
   - Basis for each opinion

5. CONCLUSION
   - Summary of findings
   - Degree of certainty

Signature: [...]
Date: [...]
```

### Step 7: Documentary Evidence

#### Types for Patent Cases:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Patent documents | Claims, specification |
| Prosecution history | Claim interpretation |
| Prior art | Validity challenge |
| Contracts/licenses | Ownership, exhaustion |
| Technical literature | State of art |
| Product documentation | Accused product |
| Financial records | Damages |

#### Authentication:

**Indian Evidence Act Requirements**:
- Primary evidence (original) preferred
- Secondary evidence with explanation
- Foreign documents: Certified copies
- Public documents: Certified copies

### Step 8: Evidence Preservation

#### Preservation Best Practices:

```
Physical Evidence:
├── Seal samples immediately
├── Document chain of custody
├── Store in secure location
├── Photograph before analysis
└── Retain for litigation duration

Electronic Evidence:
├── Create forensic images (hash verified)
├── Document metadata
├── Preserve in read-only format
├── Maintain audit trail
└── Section 65B certificate at creation
```

#### Preservation Letter:

When litigation anticipated:
```
PRESERVATION NOTICE

To: [Potential Defendant]
Date: [...]

RE: Preservation of Evidence

We represent [Client], owner of Patent No. IN [...].

We believe [Defendant] may be infringing said patent.

This letter serves as notice to preserve all evidence
relating to [products/processes] including but not limited to:

1. [Specific documents]
2. [Electronic records]
3. [Physical samples]
4. [Communications]
5. [Financial records]

Failure to preserve may result in adverse inferences.

[Attorney signature]
```

## Common Evidence Mistakes

1. **No Section 65B certificate**: Electronic evidence inadmissible
2. **Broken chain of custody**: Reliability questioned
3. **Delayed preservation**: Evidence lost/destroyed
4. **Unqualified expert**: Opinion discredited
5. **Missing authentication**: Documents challenged
6. **Vague claim charts**: Infringement unclear
