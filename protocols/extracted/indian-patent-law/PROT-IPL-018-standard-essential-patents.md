## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding SEPs in India

#### Definition:
A **Standard Essential Patent (SEP)** is a patent claiming technology essential to implementing a technical standard (e.g., 3G, 4G LTE, 5G, WiFi, HEVC).

#### Legal Framework:
- No specific SEP legislation in India
- Patents Act, 1970 applies generally
- FRAND commitments enforced through contract/equity
- Competition Commission of India (CCI) jurisdiction

#### Standard Setting Organizations (SSOs) in India:
- Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)
- Telecom Engineering Centre (TEC)
- TSDSI (Telecommunications Standards Development Society, India)

### Step 2: FRAND Obligations

#### FRAND = Fair, Reasonable, and Non-Discriminatory

#### What FRAND Requires:

| Element | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Fair** | Not exploitative, considering value of patent |
| **Reasonable** | Commercially reasonable royalty rate |
| **Non-Discriminatory** | Similar terms to similarly situated licensees |

#### FRAND Commitment:
- Made to SSO when patent declared essential
- Creates obligation to license to willing licensees
- Not a waiver of patent rights
- Courts enforce through equitable remedies

### Step 3: Willing Licensee Framework

#### Key Principle:
SEP holder must offer FRAND license to **willing licensees**

#### Characteristics of Willing Licensee:
1. Engages in good faith negotiations
2. Doesn't unreasonably delay
3. Provides necessary information
4. Accepts reasonable FRAND terms
5. Doesn't engage in reverse hold-up

#### Characteristics of Unwilling Licensee:
1. Delays negotiations unreasonably
2. Refuses reasonable offers
3. Uses litigation as delay tactic
4. Doesn't engage in good faith

### Step 4: FRAND Royalty Determination

#### Methodologies Recognized in India:

**1. Comparable Licenses Approach** (Preferred in India)
- Look to existing FRAND licenses
- Similarly situated licensees
- Same technology/standard
- *Endorsed in Ericsson v. Lava (2024)*

**2. Top-Down Approach**
- Start with total aggregate royalty for standard
- Apportion among all SEPs
- Calculate per-patent royalty

**3. Incremental Value Approach**
- Value added by patented technology
- Over next-best alternative

#### Royalty Base Debate:

| Position | Argument |
|----------|----------|
| **End-Product (SSPPU)** | Entire mobile device price |
| **Component (Chipset)** | Value of specific component |

**India Position (Ericsson v. Lava)**: For mobile devices where telecom connectivity is core functionality, **end-product level** calculation is appropriate.

### Step 5: Key Indian SEP Cases

#### Ericsson v. Lava International (Delhi HC, March 2024):

**Award**: ₹244 Crore (~$29 million)

**Key Holdings**:
- FRAND royalty rate: 1.05% of net selling price
- Comparable licensing approach endorsed
- End-product royalty base for mobile devices
- Willingness to license analyzed

**Methodology**:
- Examined Ericsson's global licensing program
- Considered rates paid by other licensees
- Adjusted for India market conditions

#### Ericsson v. Micromax (2014-2016):
- First major SEP litigation in India
- Interim injunction granted
- Established SEP enforcement precedent

### Step 6: SEP Holder Strategies

#### Pre-Litigation:
1. Declare patents to SSO as essential
2. Maintain FRAND commitment
3. Send licensing offer with specific terms
4. Document negotiation history
5. Be prepared to prove essentiality

#### Litigation:
1. Establish patent validity
2. Prove infringement (claim charts)
3. Demonstrate FRAND compliance
4. Show implementer is unwilling
5. Seek injunction and damages

### Step 7: Implementer Strategies

#### Pre-Litigation:
1. Engage in good faith negotiations
2. Request claim charts showing essentiality
3. Make counter-offers
4. Seek portfolio-wide license
5. Document willingness to license

#### Defense Strategies:
1. Challenge essentiality
2. Challenge validity
3. Argue FRAND breach
4. Challenge royalty calculation
5. CCI competition law complaint

### Step 8: Competition Law Considerations

#### CCI Jurisdiction:
- Section 4 (Abuse of Dominance) may apply
- SEP holder may have dominant position
- Exploitative pricing possible violation
- Refusal to license potential concern

#### CCI Cases:
- Multiple complaints filed against SEP holders
- CCI has investigated FRAND abuse allegations
- No final adverse orders to date

## Common SEP Mistakes

1. **Ignoring licensing demands**: Becomes unwilling licensee
2. **Not documenting negotiations**: Lose evidence of good faith
3. **Refusing reasonable offers**: May lose injunction defense
4. **Over-aggressive demands**: FRAND violation risk
5. **Delaying tactics**: Courts penalize delay
