# Protocol: Patent Renewal and Maintenance

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-013
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Portfolio Management
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for maintaining patent validity through timely renewal fee payments, including fee schedules, entity categories, grace periods, restoration procedures, and portfolio optimization strategies.

---

## Activation Triggers

- New patent granted (first renewal planning)
- Annual renewal due date approaching
- Renewal fee calculation needed
- Grace period situation
- Lapsed patent restoration consideration

---

## Prerequisites

- Granted Indian patent (or pending application post-grant)
- Knowledge of patent filing date
- Entity status determination
- Budget allocation for renewals

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understand Renewal Obligations

#### When Renewals Start:
- **Not required**: Years 1-2 (from filing date)
- **First renewal**: Due before 3rd anniversary of filing
- **After grant**: All past-due renewals must be paid within 3 months of grant

#### Key Principle:
Patent term = 20 years from filing date
Renewals must be continuous to maintain validity

### Step 2: Determine Entity Status

#### Entity Categories (Rule 7):

| Category | Definition | Fee Level |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| Natural Person | Individual inventor | Lowest |
| Startup | DPIIT recognized, <10 years, <₹100 Cr turnover | Same as Natural Person |
| Small Entity | Per MSME Act 2006 | Medium |
| Large Entity | All others | Highest |

**Form 28**: Declaration of entity status (must be filed)

### Step 3: Calculate Renewal Fees

#### Fee Schedule (E-filing, Effective 2024):

| Patent Year | Natural Person/Startup | Small Entity | Large Entity |
|-------------|------------------------|--------------|--------------|
| 3rd | ₹800 | ₹2,000 | ₹4,000 |
| 4th | ₹900 | ₹2,300 | ₹4,500 |
| 5th | ₹1,000 | ₹2,500 | ₹5,000 |
| 6th | ₹1,600 | ₹4,000 | ₹8,000 |
| 7th | ₹1,600 | ₹4,000 | ₹8,000 |
| 8th | ₹1,600 | ₹4,000 | ₹8,000 |

**Physical filing**: Add 10% to above fees

### Step 4: Payment Timing

#### Due Date:
- Before expiry of current patent year
- Patent year = anniversary of filing date

#### Example:
- Filing date: March 15, 2020
- 5th year renewal due: Before March 15, 2024
- Can pay any time during 4th year

### Step 5: Grace Period (Rule 80(1A))

#### If Deadline Missed:
- **6-month grace period** available
- Additional penalty fees apply

#### Grace Period Fees:

| Month Late | Natural Person/Startup | Small Entity | Large Entity |
|------------|------------------------|--------------|--------------|
| 1st month | +₹10,000 | +₹25,000 | +₹50,000 |
| 2nd month | +₹20,000 | +₹50,000 | +₹100,000 |
| 3rd month | +₹30,000 | +₹75,000 | +₹150,000 |
| 4th month | +₹40,000 | +₹100,000 | +₹200,000 |
| 5th month | +₹50,000 | +₹125,000 | +₹250,000 |
| 6th month | +₹60,000 | +₹150,000 | +₹300,000 |

### Step 6: Advance Payment Discount

#### Benefit:
**10% discount** when paying 4+ years in advance via e-filing

#### Strategy:
- Calculate total fees for remaining patent life
- Pay multiple years at once if budget allows
- Reduces administrative burden

### Step 7: Patent Lapse and Restoration

#### If Not Renewed Within Grace Period:
- Patent **lapses** (ceases to be in force)
- Invention enters public domain

#### Restoration (Section 60):

**Requirements**:
1. Apply within **18 months** of lapse
2. File Form 15 with restoration fee
3. Pay all unpaid renewal fees + penalties
4. Demonstrate unintentional failure

**Restoration Fees**:
| Entity | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| Natural Person/Startup | ₹2,400 |
| Small Entity | ₹6,000 |
| Large Entity | ₹12,000 |

## Renewal Monitoring System

### Best Practices:

1. **Docketing System**: Track all renewal dates
2. **90-Day Warning**: Initial reminder
3. **30-Day Warning**: Urgent reminder
4. **Grace Period Tracking**: If deadline missed
5. **Post-Payment Confirmation**: Verify on InPASS

### Monitoring Calendar Template:

```
| Patent No. | Filing Date | Entity | Year Due | Due Date | Amount | Status |
|------------|-------------|--------|----------|----------|--------|--------|
| IN/XXX | 2020-03-15 | Startup | 6th | 2025-03-14 | ₹1,600 | Pending |
| IN/YYY | 2019-08-01 | Large | 7th | 2025-07-31 | ₹8,000 | Paid |
```

## Post-Grant Renewal Catch-Up

#### Scenario:
Patent grants 4 years after filing. Years 3-4 renewals now due.

#### Procedure:
1. Calculate all unpaid renewals (Years 3-4)
2. Pay within 3 months of grant
3. No penalty during this catch-up period

---

## Working Statement Integration (Form 27)

#### 2024 Rule Change:
Working statements now due **every 3 financial years** instead of annually.

#### Connection to Renewals:
- Non-compliance can trigger compulsory licensing risk
- Track alongside renewal calendar

## Common Renewal Mistakes

1. **Wrong anniversary calculation**: Use filing date, not grant date
2. **Entity status change**: Update and adjust fees
3. **Grace period miscalculation**: Exact date critical
4. **Non-electronic filing**: Loses 10% discount
5. **No confirmation**: Always verify on InPASS

## Portfolio Optimization

#### Decision Framework for Renewals:

| Factor | Continue Renewal | Consider Lapse |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Commercial use | Active product | No use planned |
| Licensing | Licensed/licensable | No interest |
| Blocking value | Blocks competitors | Not strategic |
| Litigation risk | Potential suit | No concerns |
| Cost vs. value | Justified | Unjustified |
