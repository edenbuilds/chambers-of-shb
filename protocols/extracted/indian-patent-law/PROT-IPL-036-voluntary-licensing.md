# Protocol: Voluntary Licensing Agreements

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-036
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Commercialization
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for voluntary patent licensing in India, including exclusive vs. non-exclusive licenses, key agreement terms, royalty structures, recordation requirements, and enforcement considerations.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Licensing patent for revenue
- Seeking license to use third-party patent
- Cross-licensing negotiation
- University technology transfer
- Franchise/distribution requiring license

---

## Prerequisites

- Granted or pending patent
- Commercial opportunity identified
- Licensing strategy developed
- Understanding of agreement types

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Types of Licenses

#### License Categories:

| Type | Rights Granted | Exclusions |
|------|---------------|------------|
| **Exclusive** | Only licensee can practice | Licensor excluded |
| **Sole** | Only licensee + licensor | Third parties excluded |
| **Non-exclusive** | Licensee + others | No exclusivity |

#### Scope Limitations:

| Limitation | Example |
|------------|---------|
| **Field of use** | Pharmaceutical vs. cosmetic |
| **Territory** | India only, specific states |
| **Time** | Fixed term, patent life |
| **Quantity** | Units, revenue cap |
| **Channel** | Retail vs. OEM |

### Step 2: Key License Agreement Terms

#### Essential Clauses:

```
PATENT LICENSE AGREEMENT

1. PARTIES
   Licensor: [Patent owner details]
   Licensee: [Licensee details]

2. RECITALS
   - Licensor owns Patent No. IN [...]
   - Licensee desires to practice invention
   - Parties wish to enter license agreement

3. DEFINITIONS
   - "Licensed Patents": [List patents]
   - "Licensed Products": [Definition]
   - "Territory": [Geographic scope]
   - "Field of Use": [Technical/market scope]

4. GRANT OF LICENSE
   Licensor hereby grants to Licensee a [exclusive/non-exclusive/
   sole] license under the Licensed Patents to make, use, sell,
   offer to sell, and import Licensed Products in the Territory
   within the Field of Use.

5. RESERVATION OF RIGHTS
   - Licensor reserves right to [...]
   - Rights not expressly granted are reserved

6. SUBLICENSE RIGHTS
   [Permitted/Not permitted with conditions]

7. ROYALTIES
   - Running royalty: [X]% of Net Sales
   - Minimum royalty: ₹[Amount] per [period]
   - Payment schedule: [Quarterly/Annual]
   - Audit rights: [...]

8. TERM AND TERMINATION
   - Term: [Duration]
   - Termination for breach: [Notice period]
   - Effect of termination: [Wind-down rights]

9. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES
   - Licensor warranties: [Ownership, no encumbrances]
   - Licensee warranties: [Capability, no conflicts]
   - Disclaimer: [As-is, no validity guarantee]

10. INDEMNIFICATION
    - By Licensor: [Infringement claims by third parties]
    - By Licensee: [Use of licensed technology]

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
    - Governing law: [Indian law]
    - Arbitration: [Seat, rules]
    - Jurisdiction: [Court]
```

### Step 3: Royalty Structures

#### Common Royalty Models:

| Model | Description | When Used |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Running royalty** | % of sales | Ongoing products |
| **Lump sum** | One-time payment | Simple transactions |
| **Paid-up** | Upfront with unlimited use | M&A, high confidence |
| **Minimum + running** | Floor + percentage | Ensure base return |
| **Milestone-based** | Payments at development stages | R&D licenses |

#### Royalty Rate Factors:

| Factor | Higher Rate | Lower Rate |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Market size | Large market | Small market |
| Patent strength | Strong, validated | Uncertain validity |
| Exclusivity | Exclusive | Non-exclusive |
| Competition | Few alternatives | Many alternatives |
| Development stage | Commercial ready | Early stage |
| Licensee contribution | Low | High |

#### Indian Market Benchmarks:

| Industry | Typical Range |
|----------|---------------|
| Pharmaceuticals | 2-10% |
| Software | 5-15% |
| Electronics | 1-5% |
| Medical devices | 3-8% |
| Chemicals | 2-6% |
| FMCG | 2-5% |

### Step 4: Recordation with Patent Office

#### Section 69 - Registration of Licenses:

"Where any person becomes entitled by [license]... to a patent or to a share in a patent... he shall apply in writing to the Controller for registration"

#### Form 16 Requirements:

| Information | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Patent number | Licensed patent(s) |
| License type | Exclusive/non-exclusive |
| Licensee details | Name, address |
| Effective date | License commencement |
| Key terms | Summary |

#### Effect of Recordation:

| Aspect | Recorded | Not Recorded |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| Admissibility | Evidence of license | May be challenged |
| Third party notice | Constructive notice | No notice |
| Enforcement | Licensee can sue (if exclusive) | Limited |

### Step 5: Exclusive License Enforcement Rights

#### Section 109 - Exclusive Licensee Rights:

Exclusive licensee has same right to institute infringement proceedings as if they were patentee.

#### Requirements for Suit by Licensee:
1. Exclusive license granted
2. License in writing
3. Patentee joined as co-plaintiff or defendant
4. License recorded (advisable)

#### Remedies Available:
- Injunction
- Damages (to extent of licensee's injury)
- Account of profits (apportioned)
- Delivery up/destruction

### Step 6: Special License Types

#### Cross-License:

**Structure**:
```
Party A has Patent 1 → Grants license to B
Party B has Patent 2 → Grants license to A
                     → Balancing payment (if unequal value)
```

**Common Uses**:
- Freedom to operate
- Settlement of disputes
- Technology collaboration

#### Patent Pool:

**Definition**: Multiple patentees license to central pool, which licenses to all comers.

**Indian Examples**:
- MPEG-LA (codec patents)
- Via Licensing
- SEP pools for telecommunications

#### License of Right (Voluntary):

**Section 87 - Endorsement "Licenses of Right"**:
- Patentee may request endorsement
- Anyone entitled to license
- Controller settles terms if disputed
- Renewal fees halved

### Step 7: Termination and Post-Termination

#### Termination Events:

| Event | Right |
|-------|-------|
| Expiry of term | Automatic |
| Material breach | Terminate on notice |
| Insolvency | May terminate |
| Change of control | Negotiated |
| Patent expiry/invalidation | License ends |

#### Post-Termination Obligations:

- Cease manufacturing
- Sell off inventory (often permitted period)
- Return confidential information
- Final royalty payment
- Audit rights continue (limited period)

## Common Licensing Mistakes

1. **No recordation**: Enforcement issues
2. **Vague scope**: Disputes over coverage
3. **No audit rights**: Cannot verify royalties
4. **Missing termination clause**: Trapped in bad deal
5. **Ignoring competition law**: Anti-competitive terms
6. **No improvement clause**: Technology frozen
7. **Inadequate warranty**: Unexpected liability
