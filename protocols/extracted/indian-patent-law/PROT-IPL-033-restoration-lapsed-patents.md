# Protocol: Restoration of Lapsed Patents (Section 60)

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-033
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Maintenance
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for restoring lapsed patents under Section 60, including eligibility requirements, application procedure, grounds for restoration, and third-party rights considerations.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Patent lapsed due to non-payment of renewal fee
- Renewal deadline missed inadvertently
- Client seeking to revive expired patent
- Third party rights assessment needed

---

## Prerequisites

- Patent previously granted in India
- Lapse due to non-payment of renewal
- Within restoration time limit
- Grounds for restoration established

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Patent Lapse

#### Causes of Lapse:

| Cause | Section/Rule |
|-------|--------------|
| Non-payment of renewal fee | Section 53(1)(a) |
| Failure to pay within grace | Rule 80 |
| Intentional non-renewal | Business decision |

#### Lapse Timeline:

```
Renewal Due Date (year anniversary of patent)
        ↓
Grace Period (6 months with surcharge)
        ↓
Lapse (if still unpaid)
        ↓
Restoration Window (18 months from lapse)
        ↓
Patent Dead (cannot be restored)
```

### Step 2: Section 60 Framework

#### Section 60 - Restoration of Lapsed Patents:

"Where a patent has ceased to have effect by reason of failure to pay any renewal fee... the patentee may make an application for the restoration of the patent"

#### Eligibility Criteria:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Cause** | Lapse due to non-payment of renewal |
| **Time** | Within 18 months from date of cessation |
| **Grounds** | Failure unintentional |
| **Who** | Patentee or legal representative |

### Step 3: Grounds for Restoration

#### "Failure Was Unintentional":

The patentee must show the failure to pay was **unintentional**. This requires demonstrating:

**Acceptable Grounds**:
| Ground | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Administrative error | Clerical mistake, wrong date recorded |
| Communication failure | Notice not received, mail failure |
| Internal oversight | Staff error, handover issues |
| System failure | Software glitch, reminder failure |
| Personal circumstances | Illness, death, emergency |
| Agent error | IP agent failed to renew |

**NOT Acceptable**:
| Ground | Why Not |
|--------|---------|
| Intentional decision not to pay | Not unintentional |
| Cost-cutting measure | Business decision |
| Doubting patent value | Strategic choice |
| Changed business direction | Intentional |

### Step 4: Application Procedure

#### Form 15 - Restoration Application:

**Contents**:
```
FORM 15
APPLICATION FOR RESTORATION OF PATENT

1. PATENT DETAILS
   Patent No.: IN [...]
   Date of Grant: [...]
   Date of Cessation: [...]
   Title: [...]

2. PATENTEE DETAILS
   Name: [...]
   Address: [...]

3. RENEWAL FEE DETAILS
   Year of non-payment: [...]
   Amount due: ₹[...]
   Due date: [...]

4. GROUNDS FOR RESTORATION
   [Detailed statement explaining unintentional failure]

5. SUPPORTING EVIDENCE
   [List of exhibits]

6. DECLARATION
   I declare that the failure to pay was unintentional
   and the above information is true.

Signature: [...]
Date: [...]
```

#### Required Documents:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Form 15 | Application form |
| Statement of circumstances | Explain failure |
| Supporting evidence | Prove unintentional |
| Unpaid renewal fee | All arrears |
| Restoration fee | Form 15 fee |
| Surcharges | If applicable |

### Step 5: Timeline and Fees

#### Restoration Timeline:

| Event | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| Renewal due | Anniversary of patent |
| Grace period ends | 6 months after due date |
| Patent lapses | End of grace period |
| Restoration window | 18 months from lapse |
| Application deadline | Within 18 months |

#### Fees Payable:

| Fee | Amount (Large Entity E-filing) |
|-----|-------------------------------|
| Restoration application (Form 15) | ₹4,000 |
| Unpaid renewal fee(s) | Per Schedule I |
| Surcharge (if any) | Per rules |

*Startup/Small Entity rates: 20-50% of above*

### Step 6: Controller's Discretion

#### Assessment Process:

```
Application Filed (Form 15)
        ↓
Controller Reviews
├── Statement of circumstances
├── Supporting evidence
└── Credibility of explanation
        ↓
Prima Facie Assessment
├── Satisfied → Publish in Journal
└── Not satisfied → Hearing/Rejection
        ↓
Publication (if accepted)
        ↓
Opposition Period
        ↓
Final Decision
```

#### Factors Considered:

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Nature of failure | Genuine oversight vs. neglect |
| Evidence quality | Documentary support |
| Patentee conduct | Diligence shown |
| Time elapsed | Delay explained |
| Pattern | First time vs. repeated |

### Step 7: Third-Party Rights (Section 62)

#### Section 62 - Rights of Third Parties:

Critical protection for those who acted during lapse period.

**Third-Party Protection**:
- If during lapse, a person began manufacture/use
- In good faith
- Before restoration application publication
- They may continue such acts

**Conditions**:
| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Good faith | Believed patent was dead |
| Started during lapse | Not before lapse, not after application |
| Commercial activity | Manufacture, use, sale |
| Reasonable scale | Not mere preparations |

#### Practical Effect:
```
Patent Lapses
        ↓
Third Party X starts manufacturing (good faith)
        ↓
Restoration Application Filed
        ↓
Restoration Granted
        ↓
X can CONTINUE manufacturing (Section 62 right)
```

### Step 8: Post-Restoration

#### Effect of Restoration:

- Patent treated as if never lapsed
- Full term continues
- All renewal fees must be paid
- Rights fully restored

#### Future Renewals:

After restoration:
- Pay next renewal on time
- Set up reminder system
- Consider using renewal service
- Monitor expiry dates

## Restoration Checklist

### Pre-Filing:
- [ ] Confirm patent lapsed (not revoked)
- [ ] Within 18-month window
- [ ] Grounds established (unintentional)
- [ ] Evidence collected
- [ ] Fees calculated

### Filing:
- [ ] Form 15 completed
- [ ] Statement of circumstances detailed
- [ ] Evidence attached
- [ ] Unpaid renewal fees paid
- [ ] Restoration fee paid
- [ ] Filed within deadline

### Post-Filing:
- [ ] Monitor publication
- [ ] Check for oppositions
- [ ] Respond if opposed
- [ ] Receive restoration order
- [ ] Update renewal calendar

## Evidence Examples

### Administrative Error:
- Internal email showing wrong date recorded
- Calendar system failure documentation
- Staff memo acknowledging error

### Agent Error:
- Correspondence showing instruction given
- Agent acknowledgment of failure
- Docketing system records

### Communication Failure:
- Postal return receipts
- Email bounce-backs
- Change of address notifications

### Personal Circumstances:
- Medical certificates
- Death certificates (for representatives)
- Emergency documentation

---

## Common Restoration Mistakes

1. **Missing 18-month deadline**: Absolute bar
2. **Weak grounds**: Must be truly unintentional
3. **Insufficient evidence**: Document everything
4. **Ignoring third-party rights**: Section 62 applies
5. **Not paying all fees**: Include all arrears
6. **Vague statement**: Be specific and detailed
