# Protocol: Section 8 Foreign Filing Compliance

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-014
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Compliance
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for compliance with Section 8 of the Patents Act, 1970, covering disclosure obligations for corresponding foreign applications, Form 3 requirements, and the significant relaxations under the 2024 Amendment Rules.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Filing patent application in India
- Filing corresponding application abroad
- Receiving First Examination Report (FER)
- Reviewing prosecution history before grant
- Evaluating opposition/revocation risk

---

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of patent family
- Tracking system for foreign filings
- Knowledge of prosecution status abroad
- Awareness of deadlines

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understand Section 8 Requirements

#### Section 8(1) - Information Disclosure:
Applicant must disclose:
- Applications filed in any country outside India
- For same or substantially same invention

**Form 3 Filing Timeline (Pre-2024)**:
- At filing, or within 6 months
- Updated whenever status changes

#### Section 8(2) - Controller's Direction:
Controller may require:
- Information about prosecution in other countries
- Search reports from other offices
- Office actions and responses

### Step 2: 2024 Amendment Changes (Critical Update)

#### Major Relaxation - New Rule:

**Before 2024**:
- Continuous Form 3 updates required
- Every change in foreign applications
- Heavy compliance burden

**After March 15, 2024**:
- **Only TWO Form 3 submissions required**:

| Submission | Timing | Content |
|------------|--------|---------|
| First Form 3 | At filing OR within 6 months | All foreign applications filed |
| Updated Form 3 | Within 3 months of FER | Updated status of all applications |

#### What This Means:
- No more continuous filings
- Simplified compliance
- One update after FER captures prosecution status

### Step 3: Form 3 Content Requirements

#### Information to Include:

1. **Country/Office**: Where application filed
2. **Application Number**: Official number assigned
3. **Filing Date**: Date of filing
4. **Priority Claimed**: If priority claimed from another application
5. **Current Status**: Pending/Granted/Refused/Withdrawn/Abandoned
6. **Grant Details**: If granted - patent number, grant date

#### Template Declaration:

```
STATEMENT AND UNDERTAKING (FORM 3)
Under Section 8(1) of the Patents Act, 1970

Application No.: [Indian Application Number]
Title: [Title of Invention]

I/We hereby declare that:

1. The following patent applications have been filed for the same or substantially the same invention:

| S.No | Country | App. No. | Filing Date | Priority | Status |
|------|---------|----------|-------------|----------|--------|
| 1 | US | 17/XXX,XXX | 2024-01-15 | Yes (IN) | Pending |
| 2 | EP | 24XXXXXX.X | 2024-02-01 | Yes (IN) | Under Exam |
| 3 | CN | 2024XXXXXXX | 2024-02-15 | Yes (IN) | Granted |

2. I/We undertake to keep the Controller informed of any changes in the details/status of the above applications and of any other applications filed subsequently.

Date: [Date]
Signature: [Applicant/Agent]
```

### Step 4: Prosecution History Compliance (Section 8(2))

#### When Controller May Direct:

During examination, Controller may request:
- Search reports from USPTO, EPO, JPO, etc.
- Office actions (First, Final)
- Applicant's responses/amendments
- Claims as amended/granted abroad

#### Response Deadline:
As directed by Controller (typically within examination response period)

#### Best Practice:
Proactively file search reports from major offices with FER response

### Step 5: Consequences of Non-Compliance

#### Severe Penalties:

| Consequence | Ground |
|-------------|--------|
| Pre-grant opposition | Section 25(1)(h) |
| Post-grant opposition | Section 25(2)(h) |
| Revocation | Section 64(1)(m) |

#### Case Law Trend:
Non-compliance increasingly scrutinized. Courts have revoked patents for material non-disclosures.

#### What Constitutes Non-Compliance:
- Complete failure to file Form 3
- Failure to update after FER
- False/misleading information
- Withholding material prosecution results
- Not disclosing rejections abroad

### Step 6: Materiality Standard

#### Key Question:
Would the undisclosed information have affected examination?

#### Material Information:
- Prior art cited in foreign office action
- Rejections for novelty/inventive step
- Claim amendments narrowing scope
- Examiner interviews affecting prosecution

#### Potentially Not Material:
- Minor formality objections
- Status unchanged since last filing
- Applications in countries not examining

## Compliance Checklist

### At Filing:
- [ ] First Form 3 prepared
- [ ] All foreign applications listed
- [ ] Status accurately reported
- [ ] Filed within 6 months of Indian filing

### Upon FER:
- [ ] Updated Form 3 within 3 months
- [ ] All new applications added
- [ ] Status updated for all applications
- [ ] Search reports from major offices included
- [ ] Prior art cited abroad addressed

### Before Grant:
- [ ] All foreign prosecution history available
- [ ] No undisclosed adverse actions
- [ ] Claim scope consistent with foreign grants

## Common Compliance Mistakes

1. **Forgetting Form 3 at filing**: 6-month deadline critical
2. **Missing FER update deadline**: 3 months strict
3. **Not tracking all family members**: Include all related filings
4. **Ignoring prosecution abroad**: Search reports matter
5. **Assuming status unchanged**: Verify before each filing
