## Purpose

Comprehensive guide to alternative dispute resolution mechanisms for patent disputes in India, including mediation, arbitration, conciliation, and hybrid processes.

## Core Process

### Step 1: ADR Options for Patent Disputes

#### Available Mechanisms:

| Mechanism | Nature | Binding? |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Negotiation** | Direct party discussion | No |
| **Mediation** | Third-party facilitated | No |
| **Conciliation** | Third-party proposed solution | No |
| **Arbitration** | Third-party adjudication | Yes |
| **Med-Arb** | Mediation then arbitration | Yes (if arb) |

### Step 2: Arbitrability of Patent Disputes

#### What's Arbitrable:

```
ARBITRABILITY ANALYSIS

ARBITRABLE (Contractual/Private Rights):
├── License disputes
├── Royalty disputes
├── Technology transfer terms
├── Assignment disputes
├── Contractual infringement claims
└── Damages quantification

NOT ARBITRABLE (In Rem/Public Rights):
├── Patent validity
├── Patent revocation
├── Grant of patents
├── Opposition proceedings
└── Criminal IP matters

HYBRID SITUATIONS:
├── Infringement + validity defense
│   └── Split: Validity to court, damages to arbitration
├── License with validity clause
│   └── Validity challenge not arbitrable
└── FRAND disputes
    └── Royalty rates arbitrable, essentiality uncertain
```

#### Key Principle:

Rights *in personam* (between parties) = Arbitrable
Rights *in rem* (against world) = Not arbitrable

### Step 3: Mediation for Patent Disputes

#### When Mediation Works:

| Scenario | Suitability |
|----------|------------|
| Ongoing business relationship | High |
| License negotiation | High |
| Settlement exploration | High |
| Validity challenge | Low |
| Multi-party disputes | Moderate |

#### Mediation Process:

```
PATENT MEDIATION PROCESS

1. Agreement to Mediate:
   ├── Contractual clause OR
   └── Post-dispute agreement

2. Mediator Selection:
   ├── Technical background preferred
   ├── Institutional or ad hoc
   └── Neutral party

3. Mediation Sessions:
   ├── Opening statements
   ├── Joint sessions
   ├── Caucuses (private meetings)
   └── Proposal development

4. Outcome:
   ├── Settlement agreement (binding)
   ├── Partial resolution
   └── No agreement (proceed to litigation/arb)
```

#### Institutional Mediation:

| Institution | Features |
|-------------|----------|
| Delhi High Court Mediation Centre | Court-annexed |
| WIPO Arbitration and Mediation Center | Specialized IP |
| Indian Council of Arbitration | General commercial |
| International Centre for ADR | Various rules |

### Step 4: Arbitration Framework

#### Applicable Laws:

- Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996
- WIPO Arbitration Rules (if chosen)
- Institutional rules (ICC, SIAC, LCIA, etc.)

#### Arbitration Clause for Patents:

```
SAMPLE PATENT ARBITRATION CLAUSE

"Any dispute, controversy or claim arising out of or
relating to this [License/Agreement], including but
not limited to disputes regarding the interpretation,
performance, breach, or termination hereof, but
EXCLUDING questions of patent validity, patentability,
or enforceability, shall be resolved by arbitration
administered by [Institution] in accordance with its
[Rules] then in effect.

The place of arbitration shall be [City, India].
The language of arbitration shall be English.
The number of arbitrators shall be [one/three].

The arbitrator(s) shall have technical expertise in
[technology field] or shall have access to technical
experts as needed.

The arbitral award shall be final and binding on
the parties."
```

### Step 5: Technical Expertise in ADR

#### Addressing Technical Issues:

| Option | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Technical arbitrator** | Arbitrator with technical background |
| **Technical expert** | Appointed by tribunal |
| **Party experts** | Each party's expert |
| **Neutral evaluation** | Early technical assessment |

#### WIPO Expedited Arbitration:

- Designed for IP disputes
- Technically competent neutrals
- Expedited procedures available
- Global enforcement (NY Convention)

### Step 6: Settlement Agreement Drafting

#### Key Provisions:

```
PATENT DISPUTE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT

1. RECITALS
   - Background of dispute
   - Patent(s) involved
   - Prior positions

2. SETTLEMENT TERMS
   - License grant (if applicable)
   - Royalty terms
   - Territory/scope
   - Duration

3. RELEASES
   - Past infringement claims
   - Scope of release
   - Carve-outs

4. COVENANTS
   - Non-suit covenant
   - Confidentiality
   - Non-disparagement

5. REPRESENTATIONS
   - Authority to settle
   - No prior licenses conflicting
   - Ownership confirmation

6. ENFORCEMENT
   - Consent decree filing
   - Continued jurisdiction
   - Breach remedies

7. MISCELLANEOUS
   - Governing law
   - Amendment requirements
   - Integration clause
```

### Step 7: Court-Annexed Mediation

#### Commercial Courts Mediation:

Under Commercial Courts Act, 2015:
- Pre-institution mediation encouraged
- Court can refer disputes to mediation
- Mediation settlement = decree

#### Delhi High Court Process:

```
COURT-ANNEXED MEDIATION

1. Referral:
   ├── By court order OR
   └── Party request

2. Mediator Assignment:
   ├── Court-approved mediator
   └── Technical expertise considered

3. Timeline:
   ├── Initial: 2-3 months
   └── Extension possible

4. Outcome:
   ├── Settlement → Consent decree
   └── No settlement → Trial continues

5. Confidentiality:
   └── Mediation discussions protected
```

### Step 8: Enforcement Considerations

#### Settlement Agreement Enforcement:

| Type | Enforcement Mechanism |
|------|----------------------|
| Private agreement | Contract enforcement |
| Consent decree | Execution proceedings |
| Arbitral award | A&C Act, NY Convention |
| Mediation settlement | Section 73-74 A&C Act |

#### International Enforcement:

- Arbitral awards: New York Convention
- Mediation settlements: Singapore Convention (India signatory)

## ADR Selection Framework

```
ADR SELECTION DECISION TREE

Dispute Type?
├── Pure validity challenge → Litigation only
├── Contractual (license, royalty) → ADR suitable
│   │
│   └── Relationship preservation important?
│       ├── Yes → Mediation first
│       └── No → Arbitration
│
├── Infringement damages only → ADR possible
│   └── If validity not contested
│
└── Mixed (validity + damages)
    └── Bifurcate: Validity to court, damages to ADR
```

## Common ADR Mistakes

1. **Arbitrating validity**: Not arbitrable in India
2. **Vague clauses**: Pathological arbitration agreements
3. **Wrong institution**: Lacks IP expertise
4. **No technical input**: Complex technology needs experts
5. **Ignoring enforcement**: Consider where award enforced
6. **Confidentiality gaps**: Settlement leaks

## Template: Mediation Agreement

```
PATENT DISPUTE MEDIATION AGREEMENT

PARTIES:
[Party A] and [Party B]

DISPUTE:
[Description of patent dispute]

AGREEMENT TO MEDIATE:

1. The parties agree to attempt to resolve the
   above dispute through mediation before
   initiating or continuing litigation/arbitration.

2. MEDIATOR: [Name] or as appointed by [Institution]

3. RULES: [Institution rules / Ad hoc procedures]

4. VENUE: [Location]

5. TIMELINE: Complete within [X] days

6. COSTS: Shared equally / [Other arrangement]

7. CONFIDENTIALITY: All discussions confidential
   and inadmissible in subsequent proceedings.

8. AUTHORITY: Each party representative has
   full settlement authority.

9. NON-BINDING: Unless written settlement reached.

Signed:
Party A: _____________  Date: _______
Party B: _____________  Date: _______
```
