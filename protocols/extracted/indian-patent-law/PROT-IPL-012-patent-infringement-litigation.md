# Protocol: Patent Infringement Litigation

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-012
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Litigation
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for patent infringement litigation in India, covering jurisdiction, remedies, claim construction, infringement analysis, defenses, and recent trends in damages awards.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Patent infringement detected
- Received cease and desist letter
- Preparing infringement suit
- Defending against infringement claim
- Seeking damages for past infringement

---

## Prerequisites

- Valid, in-force Indian patent
- Clear ownership/assignment chain
- Identified infringer and infringing product/process
- Claim construction analysis
- Evidence of infringement

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Pre-Litigation Assessment

#### Patent Validity Check:
- Maintenance fees current?
- Within 20-year term?
- No pending revocation/opposition?

#### Infringement Analysis:
- Claim construction completed?
- Literal infringement or doctrine of equivalents?
- Evidence available?

#### Commercial Assessment:
- Commercial importance of infringing activity
- Defendant's financial position
- Settlement possibility

### Step 2: Jurisdiction Selection

#### Post-IPAB Abolition (2021):
Primary infringement jurisdiction: **High Courts**

#### Territorial Jurisdiction:
- Where plaintiff resides/carries on business
- Where cause of action arises (manufacture/sale)
- **Delhi High Court**: Most active, IP Division established

#### Why Delhi High Court Preferred:
- Specialized IP Division
- Experienced judges
- Recent landmark judgments
- Faster disposal

### Step 3: File Suit for Infringement

#### Required Elements:
1. **Plaint**: Statement of claim
2. **Particulars of Patent**: Complete specification, grant certificate
3. **Infringement Analysis**: Claim chart comparing patent claims vs. infringing product
4. **Evidence**: Purchase samples, catalogues, websites, advertisements

#### Forms of Relief (Section 108):

| Relief | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Injunction | Permanent restraint on infringement |
| Damages | Compensatory damages for loss |
| Accounts of Profit | Disgorgement of infringer's profits |
| Delivery-up | Surrender of infringing goods |
| Costs | Litigation costs |

### Step 4: Interim Relief (Injunctions)

#### Test for Interim Injunction:
1. **Prima facie case**: Valid patent likely infringed
2. **Balance of convenience**: Favors plaintiff
3. **Irreparable injury**: Not compensable by damages

#### Key Considerations:
- Registration alone doesn't entitle to injunction
- Courts lean against monopolies
- Credible validity challenge may deny injunction

#### Pharma Specific (Vulnerability Assessment):
Recent trends: Courts assess patent "vulnerability" to validity challenges before granting interim relief.

### Step 5: Claim Construction

#### Indian Approach:
- Purposive construction (not purely literal)
- Specification as whole considered
- Description informs claim scope
- Prosecution history may be considered

#### Infringement Types:

**Literal Infringement**:
- Every claim element present in accused product
- Element-by-element comparison

**Doctrine of Equivalents**:
- Equivalent elements performing substantially same function
- In substantially same way
- Achieving substantially same result

### Step 6: Trial Proceedings

#### Typical Timeline:
- **2-4 years** for final judgment
- Expedited in IP Division courts

#### Procedural Steps:
1. Plaint filing
2. Written statement by defendant
3. Framing of issues
4. Discovery and evidence
5. Arguments
6. Judgment

### Step 7: Damages Assessment

#### 2024 Landmark Awards:

| Case | Damages | Year |
|------|---------|------|
| Ericsson v. Lava | ₹244 Crore (~$29M) | 2024 |
| CCA v. Mobi Antenna | ₹216 Crore (~$26M) | 2024 |

#### Calculation Methods:

**Lost Profits**:
- Profits plaintiff would have made
- But for infringement

**Reasonable Royalty**:
- Hypothetical negotiated royalty
- Based on comparable licenses

**SEP/FRAND Royalties**:
- For Standard Essential Patents
- Comparable licensing approach preferred

#### Pre-Grant Damages (Section 11A(7)):
- Available from publication date
- Cannot institute suit until grant

### Step 8: Defenses to Infringement

#### Section 107 Defenses:

| Defense | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Non-infringement | Accused product doesn't fall within claims |
| Invalidity | Patent invalid on Section 64 grounds |
| Prior use | Defendant had prior user rights |
| License | Defendant is licensee |
| Government use | Use under Section 47 |
| Experimental use | Research/educational purpose |
| Parallel import | Exhaustion defense |

#### Invalidity as Defense:
- Raises same grounds as Section 64
- Creates in personam effect only
- Requires Section 71(1) for register correction

## Common Litigation Mistakes

1. **Weak claim construction**: Undermines entire case
2. **Missing elements**: No infringement without all elements
3. **Ignoring validity**: Counterclaim risk
4. **Delay in evidence**: Spoliation concerns
5. **Wrong jurisdiction**: Procedural challenges
