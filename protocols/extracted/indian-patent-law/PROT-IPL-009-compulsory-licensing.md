# Protocol: Compulsory Licensing

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-009
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Licensing & Access
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for compulsory licensing under Sections 84, 92, and 92A of the Patents Act, 1970, including application requirements, grounds, and government use provisions under Sections 100-102.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Need to work patented invention without voluntary license
- Patent not worked in India adequately
- Public health emergency requiring access to patented medicines
- Government procurement of patented technology
- Export of pharmaceuticals to countries without manufacturing capacity

---

## Prerequisites

- Three years elapsed from date of patent grant (for Section 84)
- Reasonable efforts to obtain voluntary license (documented)
- Ability to work invention to public advantage
- Understanding of applicable legal provisions

---

## Core Process

### Section 84: Standard Compulsory License

#### Step 1: Verify Eligibility

**Timing Requirement**:
- At least **3 years** from date of patent grant

**Applicant Must Demonstrate**:
- Efforts to obtain voluntary license on reasonable terms (Section 84(6)(iv))
- Ability to work the invention
- Capability to satisfy public interest

#### Step 2: Identify Grounds (Section 84(1))

Three statutory grounds:

| Ground | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| (a) | Reasonable requirements of public not satisfied |
| (b) | Patented invention not available at reasonably affordable price |
| (c) | Patented invention not worked in territory of India |

**Note**: Must establish **at least one** ground.

#### Step 3: Define "Reasonable Requirements" (Section 84(7))

Requirements not satisfied if:
- Demand not adequately met by patent working
- Working prevented by import refusal
- Domestic industry/export prejudiced by import conditions
- Establishment/development of commercial activities prejudiced
- Patent not worked on commercial scale without satisfactory reason

#### Step 4: Prepare Application

**Required Elements**:
1. Nature of applicant's interest
2. Facts establishing case for license
3. Evidence of efforts to obtain voluntary license
4. Particulars of license terms sought
5. Evidence of capability to work invention

**Filing**: Before Controller of Patents

#### Step 5: Controller's Examination

**Factors Considered (Section 84(6))**:
1. Nature of invention
2. Time elapsed since patent grant
3. Applicant's efforts for voluntary license
4. Applicant's ability to work for public advantage
5. Whether conditions include best mode disclosure
6. Whether applicant made efforts to obtain license on reasonable terms

#### Step 6: Terms of Compulsory License

If granted, license typically includes:
- Royalty to patentee (determined by Controller)
- Non-exclusive (usually)
- Predominantly domestic market supply
- Duration limits
- Working requirements

### Section 92: National Emergency/Extreme Urgency

#### When Applicable:
- National emergency declared
- Extreme urgency circumstances
- Public non-commercial use

#### Key Differences from Section 84:
- **No 3-year waiting period**
- **No prior negotiation required** (for urgency/emergency)
- Government notification triggers process
- Expedited procedure

#### COVID-19 Context:
Supreme Court in April 2021 noted Central Government could consider Sections 92, 100, 102 for pandemic response.

---

### Section 92A: Export to Countries Without Manufacturing

#### Purpose:
Enable export of pharmaceutical products to countries with insufficient manufacturing capacity.

#### Requirements:
- Importing country has granted CL or permitted import
- Notification received from importing country
- Product only for that country's market

---

### Sections 100-102: Government Use

#### Section 100 - Use for Government Purposes:

**No license needed** - Government may:
- Use any patented invention for government purposes
- Authorize any person to use for government purposes
- During national emergency or extreme urgency, no prior notice required

**Patentee Entitlement**:
- Adequate compensation/royalty
- Terms settled by agreement or High Court

#### Section 102 - Government Acquisition:

**Full Acquisition Power**:
- Central Government may acquire entire patent
- For public purpose
- Rights vest in Government
- Compensation determined by agreement or High Court

## Landmark Case: Natco v. Bayer (2012)

**First (and Only) Successful CL in India**:
- Drug: Sorafenib Tosylate (Nexavar) for cancer
- Bayer price: ₹2,80,000/month
- Natco proposed: ₹8,800/month
- Royalty: 6% to Bayer
- Affirmed through IPAB, Bombay HC, and Supreme Court

**Key Learnings**:
1. All three Section 84(1) grounds can be established simultaneously
2. Import ≠ Working in India
3. Price affordability assessed against Indian purchasing power
4. Royalty rate: balances patentee compensation and public access

---

## Failed CL Applications

### BDR v. Bristol-Myers Squibb (2013):
- Drug: Sprycel
- **Rejected**: No genuine effort to obtain voluntary license
- No demonstrated capability to work

### Lee Pharma v. AstraZeneca (2015):
- Drug: Saxagliptin
- **Rejected**: Substitutes available in market
- Reasonable requirements of public satisfied

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] 3 years from patent grant elapsed (Section 84)
- [ ] Genuine efforts to negotiate voluntary license documented
- [ ] At least one Section 84(1) ground established
- [ ] Capability to work invention demonstrated
- [ ] Proposed license terms defined
- [ ] Public interest arguments articulated
- [ ] Evidence of inadequate working/pricing compiled

---

## Common CL Application Mistakes

1. **Premature filing**: Before 3-year period
2. **No negotiation efforts**: Section 84(6)(iv) mandatory
3. **No working capability**: Must show ability to manufacture
4. **Ignoring substitutes**: Controller considers alternatives
5. **Unrealistic royalty proposal**: Balance required

## Compulsory License Application Template

```
APPLICATION FOR COMPULSORY LICENSE
UNDER SECTION 84 OF PATENTS ACT, 1970

To: The Controller of Patents
Patent Office, [City]

Patent No.: [Number]
Title: [Title of Invention]
Patentee: [Name]
Date of Grant: [Date]

APPLICANT:
Name: [Name]
Address: [Address]
Nature of Interest: [Manufacturer/Importer/etc.]

GROUNDS UNDER SECTION 84(1):

1. Reasonable requirements of public not satisfied because:
   [Detailed facts and evidence]

2. Invention not available at reasonably affordable price because:
   [Detailed facts and evidence - pricing data]

3. Invention not worked in India because:
   [Evidence of import-only or non-working]

EFFORTS TO OBTAIN VOLUNTARY LICENSE:
[Chronological documentation of negotiations]

CAPABILITY TO WORK:
[Manufacturing facilities, regulatory approvals, etc.]

PROPOSED LICENSE TERMS:
- Royalty: [X]% of net sales
- Duration: [Period]
- Territory: India
- Scope: [Products covered]

EVIDENCE ANNEXED:
[List of documents]

PRAYER:
Grant of compulsory license on above terms

Date: [Date]
Signature: [Applicant]
```
