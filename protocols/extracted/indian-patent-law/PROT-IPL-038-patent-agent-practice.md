## Core Process

### Step 1: Patent Agent Definition

#### Section 125 - Who is a Patent Agent:

"[A] patent agent means a person for the time being registered under this Act as a patent agent"

#### Role of Patent Agent:

| Function | Authority |
|----------|-----------|
| Draft specifications | Primary role |
| File applications | On behalf of applicant |
| Prosecute applications | Respond to office actions |
| Represent at hearings | Before Controller |
| Advise on patent matters | Professional consultation |
| Oppose patents | File oppositions |

### Step 2: Qualification Requirements

#### Section 126 - Qualifications:

**Educational Qualification**:
- Degree in Science, Engineering, or Technology from recognized university, OR
- Diploma in Technology/Engineering from recognized institution

**Additional Requirements**:
- Indian citizen or resident
- Passed Patent Agent Examination
- No disqualification under Section 127

#### Patent Agent Examination:

**Conducted by**: Controller General of Patents

**Subjects**:
| Paper | Topics |
|-------|--------|
| **Paper I** | Patents Act, Rules, Procedures |
| **Paper II** | Drafting of specifications and claims |

**Eligibility**:
- Qualifying degree/diploma
- Application in Form 22
- Prescribed fee

### Step 3: Registration Process

#### Form 22 - Application for Registration:

**Contents**:
```
FORM 22
APPLICATION FOR REGISTRATION AS PATENT AGENT

1. PERSONAL DETAILS
   Name: [...]
   Date of Birth: [...]
   Address: [...]
   Nationality: [...]

2. EDUCATIONAL QUALIFICATION
   Degree/Diploma: [...]
   University/Institution: [...]
   Year of passing: [...]

3. EXAMINATION DETAILS
   Roll number: [...]
   Year of passing: [...]
   Marks obtained: [...]

4. DECLARATION
   I declare that I am not subject to any disqualification
   under Section 127 of the Patents Act, 1970.

5. UNDERTAKING
   I undertake to abide by the provisions of the Patents Act
   and Rules and maintain professional ethics.

Signature: [...]
Date: [...]
```

**Process**:
```
Pass Patent Agent Examination
        ↓
File Form 22 with fee
        ↓
Controller verifies qualifications
        ↓
Registration granted
        ↓
Name entered in Register of Patent Agents
```

### Step 4: Disqualifications (Section 127)

#### Cannot Register/Remain Registered:

| Disqualification | Duration |
|------------------|----------|
| Undischarged insolvent | Until discharge |
| Convicted of offense involving moral turpitude | Period specified |
| Minor | Until attaining majority |
| Unsound mind | Until recovery |
| Disciplinary removal | As specified |

### Step 5: Rights and Obligations

#### Rights of Registered Patent Agent:

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Practice before Patent Office | Full representation |
| Sign documents | On behalf of clients |
| Appear at hearings | Represent applicants |
| Use designation | "Patent Agent" |

#### Professional Obligations:

| Obligation | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| Maintain confidentiality | Client information protected |
| Avoid conflicts | No conflicting interests |
| Professional competence | Stay updated on law |
| Proper authorization | Valid Power of Attorney |
| Accurate representation | True statements to Patent Office |
| Fee transparency | Clear fee arrangements |

### Step 6: Power of Attorney

#### Requirements:

**General Power of Attorney**:
- One-time filing
- Covers all future applications
- Registered at Patent Office

**Specific Power of Attorney**:
- For specific application(s)
- Filed with application

**Form 26 - General Power of Attorney**:
```
FORM 26
POWER OF ATTORNEY

I/We, [Name], of [Address], hereby appoint
[Patent Agent Name], a registered Patent Agent,
to act on my/our behalf before the Indian Patent Office
in all matters relating to patents.

This Power of Attorney shall be valid for
[all applications / specific applications listed].

Dated: [...]
Signature: [...]
```

### Step 7: Disciplinary Proceedings

#### Section 131 - Removal from Register:

**Grounds**:
- Obtained registration by fraud/misrepresentation
- Convicted of offense involving moral turpitude
- Guilty of professional misconduct
- Ceased to be qualified

**Procedure**:
```
Complaint/suo motu action
        ↓
Notice to Patent Agent
        ↓
Opportunity to be heard
        ↓
Inquiry by Controller
        ↓
Finding of misconduct
        ↓
Removal from Register
```

#### Professional Misconduct Examples:

| Misconduct | Nature |
|------------|--------|
| Conflict of interest | Representing opposing parties |
| False statements | To Patent Office |
| Fee overcharging | Unconscionable fees |
| Negligence | Causing client loss |
| Unauthorized practice | Holding out without registration |
| Breach of confidentiality | Disclosing client information |

### Step 8: Foreign Patent Agent Practice

#### Practice in India:

**General Rule**: Only registered Indian patent agents can practice before Indian Patent Office

**Foreign Attorneys**:
- Cannot appear directly
- Must work through registered Indian agent
- Can advise on foreign filings

#### Indian Agents for Foreign Clients:

- Can represent foreign applicants
- Power of Attorney required
- Address for service in India needed

## Patent Agent Examination Preparation

### Syllabus Overview:

**Paper I - Law and Procedure**:
- Patents Act, 1970 (all sections)
- Patents Rules, 2003 (all rules)
- Patent examination guidelines
- Recent amendments
- Case law

**Paper II - Drafting**:
- Complete specification drafting
- Claim drafting (various types)
- Response to office actions
- Amendment practice

### Preparation Tips:

1. **Study Materials**:
   - Bare Act and Rules
   - P. Narayanan's Patent Law
   - IPO Manual of Patent Practice
   - Recent amendments

2. **Practice**:
   - Draft specifications from technical disclosures
   - Write claim sets
   - Practice FER responses

3. **Mock Tests**:
   - Time management critical
   - Paper II requires structured approach

## Common Patent Agent Mistakes

1. **Missing deadlines**: Track all prosecution dates
2. **Conflict of interest**: Check all clients
3. **No POA**: Must be filed before action
4. **Unauthorized practice**: Registration required
5. **Poor communication**: Keep clients informed
6. **Insufficient claim scope**: Draft strategically
