# Protocol: Research Exemption and Experimental Use

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-045
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Defenses
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide to research exemption and experimental use defense in Indian patent law, covering scope, limitations, academic research, commercial R&D, and distinction from Bolar exemption.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Academic research using patented technology
- Commercial R&D on patented inventions
- Research collaboration involving patents
- Teaching activities using patented materials
- Basic science investigations

---

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of research exemption scope
- Knowledge of commercial vs. academic distinction
- Awareness of Bolar exemption differences
- Research purpose documentation

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Legal Framework

#### Indian Position:

**No Explicit Statutory Exemption**

Unlike Bolar (Section 107A), India has no explicit statutory research exemption.

**Source of Exemption**:
- Common law principles
- Judicial interpretation
- TRIPS Article 30 flexibility
- General principles of patent law

### Step 2: Scope of Research Exemption

#### Generally Accepted Exemptions:

| Activity | Likely Exempt? | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Pure academic research | Yes | Non-commercial inquiry |
| Research ON the invention | Yes | Understanding how it works |
| Research WITH the invention | Uncertain | Using as tool may infringe |
| Teaching demonstrations | Yes | Educational purpose |
| Validation/verification | Yes | Scientific verification |
| Improvement research | Uncertain | Depends on commercialization |

#### Distinction: Research ON vs. WITH:

```
Research ON the Invention:
├── Understanding mechanism
├── Verifying claims
├── Exploring limitations
└── Generally EXEMPT

Research WITH the Invention:
├── Using as research tool
├── Incorporating in methodology
├── Commercial research
└── Generally NOT EXEMPT
```

### Step 3: Commercial vs. Academic Research

#### Academic Research:

**Characteristics**:
- University/research institution
- Non-commercial purpose
- Publication oriented
- Knowledge generation
- Government funded

**Treatment**: Generally exempt

#### Commercial Research:

**Characteristics**:
- Private company
- Profit motive
- Product development
- Competitive advantage
- Private funding

**Treatment**: Less clearly exempt

#### Gray Areas:

| Scenario | Analysis |
|----------|----------|
| University with industry sponsor | Purpose matters, not funding |
| Startup doing basic research | Commercial intent may reduce exemption |
| Academic leading to spin-off | May lose exemption retroactively |
| Government lab for defense | Likely exempt (government purpose) |

### Step 4: TRIPS Article 30 Context

#### Limited Exception Provision:

"Members may provide limited exceptions to the exclusive rights conferred by a patent, provided that such exceptions do not unreasonably conflict with a normal exploitation of the patent and do not unreasonably prejudice the legitimate interests of the patent owner"

#### Three-Step Test:
1. **Limited exception**: Not broad
2. **No unreasonable conflict**: With normal exploitation
3. **No unreasonable prejudice**: To patent owner interests

### Step 5: Research Exemption vs. Bolar

#### Key Distinctions:

| Aspect | Research Exemption | Bolar (107A(a)) |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Statutory basis | Common law | Section 107A |
| Purpose | Knowledge generation | Regulatory approval |
| Commercial intent | Generally none | Yes (generic launch) |
| Scope | Narrower | Specific |
| Certainty | Less certain | More certain |

### Step 6: Practical Application

#### When Research Exemption Applies:

✅ **Likely Protected**:
- Understanding patent claims
- Teaching patent concepts
- Verifying reported results
- Basic science using patented tools (limited)
- Government/defense research

❌ **Likely NOT Protected**:
- Using patented tool in commercial R&D
- Developing competing products
- Large-scale experimentation
- Contract research for commercial client

### Step 7: Documentation for Research Defense

#### If Claiming Research Exemption:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Research proposal | Show academic purpose |
| Funding source | Non-commercial nature |
| Publication plan | Knowledge dissemination |
| Institutional affiliation | Academic context |
| Scale of activity | Reasonable for research |

#### Research Protocol:

```
RESEARCH EXEMPTION DOCUMENTATION

1. Research Objective:
   [Describe scientific inquiry]

2. Institution:
   [Academic/research institution]

3. Funding:
   [Government/academic grant]

4. Commercial Intent:
   [None / Disclosure]

5. Publication Plan:
   [Academic journals/conferences]

6. Scale:
   [Reasonable for research purpose]

7. Duration:
   [Time-limited project]
```

### Step 8: International Comparison

#### Research Exemption Approaches:

| Country | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **US** | Very narrow (Madey v. Duke) |
| **UK** | Broader statutory exemption |
| **Germany** | Robust research exemption |
| **India** | Uncertain, likely moderate |
| **Japan** | Research exemption recognized |

#### Lessons for India:

US narrow approach warns against:
- Assuming academic status protects
- Large-scale commercial-like activity
- Research primarily benefiting industry

### Step 9: Risk Management

#### For Researchers:

**Low Risk**:
- Pure academic inquiry
- Government funded
- No commercial intent
- Publication focus
- Limited scale

**Higher Risk**:
- Industry collaboration
- Patent-as-tool use
- Commercial application potential
- Large scale

#### For Patent Holders:

**Enforcement Considerations**:
- Academic research: Rarely enforced
- Commercial R&D: May enforce
- Reputation concerns: Suing academics
- Actual damage: Often minimal for research

## Common Research Exemption Mistakes

1. **Assuming academic = exempt**: Not automatic
2. **Large-scale experiments**: Beyond research needs
3. **Industry collaboration blindness**: May change character
4. **No documentation**: Hard to prove purpose
5. **Tool use assumption**: May not be exempt
6. **Commercialization transition**: Retroactive issues
