# Protocol: Design and Patent Interface

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-040
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Related IP
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide to understanding the interface between design protection and patent protection in India, including when to use each, overlap considerations, and combined protection strategies.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Product has both functional and aesthetic elements
- Choosing between design and patent
- Combined protection strategy needed
- Competitor using similar design and function
- GUI/software interface protection

---

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of Designs Act, 2000
- Knowledge of Patents Act, 1970
- Clarity on what needs protection
- Product analysis completed

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding the Distinction

#### Fundamental Difference:

| Aspect | Design | Patent |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Protects** | Appearance/aesthetic | Function/technical |
| **Subject** | Shape, pattern, ornament | Invention, process |
| **Test** | Visual similarity | Claim coverage |
| **Infringement** | Look-alike products | Products using claimed invention |

#### Legal Basis:

**Designs Act, 2000, Section 2(d)**:
"Design means... features of shape, configuration, pattern, ornament or composition of lines or colours applied to any article..."

**Patents Act, 1970, Section 2(1)(j)**:
"Invention means a new product or process involving an inventive step and capable of industrial application"

### Step 2: Design Registration Basics

#### Registrable Designs:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **New** | Not published before |
| **Original** | Not mere trade variant |
| **Applied to article** | Industrial application |
| **Visual features** | Shape, configuration, pattern |
| **Not functional** | Not dictated solely by function |

#### What Designs CANNOT Protect:

- Methods/principles of construction
- Mere mechanical devices
- Functional features (solely)
- Contrary to public morality
- National emblems

### Step 3: Overlap Scenarios

#### When Both May Apply:

```
Product Feature
        │
        ├── Purely functional → PATENT only
        │   (How it works)
        │
        ├── Purely aesthetic → DESIGN only
        │   (How it looks)
        │
        └── Mixed functional + aesthetic → BOTH possible
            (Looks AND works)
```

#### Examples of Mixed Products:

| Product | Functional (Patent) | Aesthetic (Design) |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Chair | Ergonomic mechanism | Unique shape |
| Phone | Technical circuitry | External form |
| Bottle | Dispensing mechanism | Ornamental shape |
| Faucet | Water flow system | Decorative design |

### Step 4: Strategy Selection

#### Decision Framework:

**Choose Design When**:
- Primary innovation is visual
- Lower cost protection needed
- Faster registration desired
- Functional aspects are standard
- Term of 15 years sufficient

**Choose Patent When**:
- Technical function is innovative
- 20-year protection needed
- Competitors might design around appearance
- Invention has multiple embodiments
- Claim scope important

**Choose Both When**:
- Product has valuable aesthetic AND function
- Budget allows dual protection
- Maximum exclusivity desired
- High-value product launch
- Competitor activity expected

### Step 5: GUI and Software Interface

#### Design Protection for GUIs:

**Status in India**: GUIs ARE registrable as designs

**Requirements**:
- Applied to article (screen/device)
- Visual features
- New and original

**Filing Strategy**:
```
GUI Design Filing
├── Article: "Screen display" or specific device
├── Multiple views: Different states of GUI
├── Class: 14-04 (Screen displays)
└── Description: Functional association
```

#### Patent Protection for GUIs:

GUIs may also have patent protection if:
- Technical effect achieved
- User interaction creates technical result
- Not merely aesthetic presentation

**CRI Approach**:
- System claims with GUI elements
- Method claims for user interaction
- Technical effect emphasized

### Step 6: Prior Art Considerations

#### Design Prior Art:

| Source | Relevance |
|--------|-----------|
| Published designs | Novelty destroying |
| Products in market | If publicly available |
| Catalogs, brochures | Prior publication |
| Trade fairs | If displayed before filing |

#### Patent Prior Art:

Same sources PLUS:
- Scientific publications
- Patent documents (global)
- Technical disclosures
- Prior use (anywhere in world)

#### Conflict Situations:

**Design as Patent Prior Art**:
- Published design may destroy patent novelty
- If functional features disclosed
- Consider filing timing

**Patent as Design Prior Art**:
- Patent drawings may anticipate design
- Publication date matters
- Claim scope irrelevant for design

### Step 7: Coordinated Filing Strategy

#### Timeline Optimization:

```
Day 0: Invention/creation
        ↓
Day 1-30: File patent application (complete or provisional)
        ↓ (simultaneous or shortly after)
Day 1-30: File design application
        ↓
Design typically registered: 6-12 months
        ↓
Patent examination: 2-4 years
        ↓
Both in force for overlapping period
```

#### Portfolio Approach:

| Strategy | Implementation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Serial filing** | Design first (faster), patent second |
| **Parallel filing** | Both simultaneously |
| **Selective** | Only most valuable form |
| **Cascading** | Improvement designs + improvement patents |

### Step 8: Enforcement Differences

#### Design Infringement:

**Test**: Would informed user see designs as substantially similar?

**Evidence**:
- Visual comparison
- Overall impression
- Expert evidence on design trends

#### Patent Infringement:

**Test**: Does accused product have all claim elements?

**Evidence**:
- Claim chart
- Technical analysis
- Expert evidence on function

#### Combined Enforcement:

Can sue for both if:
- Different aspects infringed
- Distinct causes of action
- May get cumulative damages (for different aspects)

## Comparison Table

| Criterion | Design | Patent |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Application** | Form 1 (Designs) | Form 1 (Patents) |
| **Examination** | Formal | Substantive |
| **Time to grant** | 6-12 months | 3-5 years |
| **Term** | 15 years | 20 years |
| **Renewal** | 5-year renewal once | Annual |
| **Cost** | Lower | Higher |
| **Registration cert** | Yes | Grant certificate |
| **Post-grant opposition** | Cancellation | Post-grant opposition |

## Common Interface Mistakes

1. **Filing only one**: Missing complementary protection
2. **Self-conflicting**: Design publication destroying patent novelty
3. **Timing mismatch**: Not coordinating filing dates
4. **Wrong categorization**: Claiming aesthetics as function
5. **Ignoring GUI designs**: Valuable protection missed
6. **Not claiming both damages**: In infringement suits
