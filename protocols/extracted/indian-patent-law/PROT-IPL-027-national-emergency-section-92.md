# Protocol: National Emergency & Public Health (Section 92)

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-027
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Compulsory Mechanisms
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for compulsory licensing during national emergencies, extreme urgency, and public non-commercial use under Section 92 of the Patents Act, with focus on public health emergencies and pandemic response.

---

## Activation Triggers

- National emergency declaration
- Public health crisis (pandemic, epidemic)
- Extreme urgency circumstances
- Public non-commercial use need
- Essential medicine access crisis

---

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of Section 92 framework
- Distinction from Section 84 CL
- Knowledge of notification requirements
- TRIPS Article 31 awareness

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Section 92 Framework

#### Section 92 - Special Compulsory Licensing:

"If the Central Government is satisfied... that it is necessary that compulsory licenses should be granted... in circumstances of national emergency or in circumstances of extreme urgency or in case of public non-commercial use"

#### Key Characteristics:

| Feature | Section 92 | Section 84 (Normal CL) |
|---------|------------|------------------------|
| Trigger | Emergency/Urgency | Non-working after 3 years |
| Prior negotiation | Not required | Required (6 months) |
| Waiting period | None | 3 years from grant |
| Government role | Central notification | Application to Controller |
| Speed | Immediate | 6-12 months |

### Step 2: Grounds for Section 92

#### Three Distinct Grounds:

**1. National Emergency**
- War or armed conflict
- Natural disasters
- Pandemic/epidemic
- Economic crisis

**2. Extreme Urgency**
- Public health emergency
- Critical shortage of essential goods
- Imminent threat requiring immediate action

**3. Public Non-Commercial Use**
- Government use without profit motive
- Public health programs
- Research and education
- Public infrastructure

### Step 3: Notification Process

#### Central Government Notification:

```
Health/Industrial Crisis Identified
        ↓
Central Government Assessment
        ↓
Notification in Official Gazette
        ↓
Specific patents identified (optional)
        ↓
Compulsory license applications invited
        ↓
Controller grants license (expedited)
```

#### Notification Content:

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Circumstances | Emergency/urgency described |
| Scope | Product categories or specific patents |
| Duration | Period of notification validity |
| Terms reference | General terms or specific conditions |

### Step 4: Application for Section 92 License

#### Simplified Application:

**What's NOT Required** (Unlike Section 84):
- 3-year waiting period
- 6-month prior negotiation
- Proof of patentee's failure
- Reasonable terms evidence

**What IS Required**:
- Application to Controller
- Statement of capability
- Proposed terms
- Reference to notification

#### Application Format:

```
APPLICATION FOR COMPULSORY LICENSE
UNDER SECTION 92 OF THE PATENTS ACT, 1970

To: Controller of Patents

1. APPLICANT DETAILS
   - Name: [...]
   - Address: [...]
   - Manufacturing capability: [...]

2. PATENT DETAILS
   - Patent No.: [...]
   - Title: [...]
   - Patentee: [...]

3. BASIS FOR APPLICATION
   - Notification reference: [Gazette reference]
   - Ground: [Emergency/Urgency/Public non-commercial]

4. PROPOSED USE
   - Product: [...]
   - Quantity: [...]
   - Market: [India/Export under 92A]

5. PROPOSED TERMS
   - Royalty: [...]
   - Duration: [...]
   - Reporting: [...]

6. DECLARATION
   [Capability and compliance undertaking]
```

### Step 5: Controller's Expedited Procedure

#### Section 92(3) - Fast-Track Grant:

"[T]he Controller shall... grant to the applicant a license under the patent on such terms and conditions as he thinks fit"

#### Timeline:
- No hearing required unless Controller deems necessary
- Expedited processing
- Grant can be immediate

#### Terms Determination:

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Royalty | Adequate remuneration (lower than normal) |
| Scope | Limited to emergency purpose |
| Duration | Limited to emergency period |
| Territory | Predominantly India |
| Quantity | As needed for purpose |

### Step 6: Section 92A - Export for Public Health

#### Special Export License:

"Compulsory license shall be available for manufacture and export of patented pharmaceutical products to any country having insufficient or no manufacturing capacity... to address public health problems"

#### Doha Declaration Implementation:

**Eligible Importing Countries**:
- Least developed countries (automatic)
- Countries with public health emergency
- Countries with insufficient manufacturing capacity

**Requirements for Export License**:
- Importing country notification to WTO
- Compulsory license or government use in importing country
- Quantity limited to importing country's need
- Special packaging/labeling required

#### Section 92A Procedure:

```
Importing country declares need to WTO
        ↓
Importing country issues CL/govt authorization
        ↓
Indian manufacturer applies under Section 92A
        ↓
Controller grants export-specific CL
        ↓
Manufacture and export (special labeling)
        ↓
Adequate remuneration to patentee
```

### Step 7: COVID-19 and Pandemic Applications

#### Pandemic Response Toolkit:

| Mechanism | Speed | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Section 92 CL | Fast | Drug/vaccine manufacture |
| Section 100 | Immediate | Government direct use |
| Section 92A | Medium | Export to other countries |
| Voluntary license | Variable | Cooperative arrangement |

#### COVID-19 Precedent Considerations:

**TRIPS Waiver Discussions**:
- 2020-2022 WTO negotiations
- Ministerial Decision June 2022
- Limited waiver for vaccines

**India's Position**:
- Strong flexibilities exist in law
- Section 92 available for pandemic
- Voluntary licensing preferred (Covaxin)

## Section 92 vs. Other Mechanisms

### Comparative Analysis:

| Aspect | Section 84 | Section 92 | Section 100 |
|--------|------------|------------|-------------|
| User | Any person | Any person | Government |
| Trigger | Non-working | Emergency | Govt purpose |
| Waiting | 3 years | None | None |
| Negotiation | Required | Not required | Not required |
| Speed | 6-12 months | Days-weeks | Immediate |
| Controller | Full procedure | Expedited | Not involved |
| Royalty | Reasonable | Adequate | Later |

### When to Use Section 92:

✅ **Use Section 92 when**:
- National emergency declared
- Public health crisis
- Extreme time pressure
- Government notification issued

❌ **Use Section 84 when**:
- Non-working after 3 years
- Price concerns
- Availability issues
- No emergency

## Template: Section 92 Government Notification

```
GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE

NOTIFICATION

New Delhi, [Date]

S.O. [Number].— In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 92 of the Patents Act, 1970 (39 of 1970), the Central Government, being satisfied that circumstances of [national emergency/extreme urgency/public non-commercial use] exist in relation to [describe circumstances, e.g., "COVID-19 pandemic"], hereby notifies that compulsory licenses may be granted for the following categories of patented inventions:

1. [Category 1: e.g., vaccines for COVID-19]
2. [Category 2: e.g., antiviral drugs]
3. [Category 3: e.g., medical devices]

Applications for compulsory licenses under this notification may be made to the Controller of Patents in Form [27A] within [period] from the date of this notification.

The terms and conditions of licenses granted under this notification shall be determined by the Controller having regard to the circumstances and in accordance with Section 92(3) of the Act.

This notification shall remain in force until [date/until further orders].

[Name]
Joint Secretary to the Government of India
```

## Common Section 92 Mistakes

1. **No notification**: Government notification is trigger
2. **Excessive royalty**: Emergency rates lower
3. **Beyond scope**: Limited to emergency purpose
4. **Ignoring 92A requirements**: Export has special rules
5. **Assuming automatic**: Still need Controller application
6. **Confusing with Section 100**: Different mechanisms
