# Protocol: Patent Revocation

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-008
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Invalidity Proceedings
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for patent revocation proceedings under Section 64 of the Patents Act, 1970, before High Courts, including grounds, procedures, and strategic considerations post-IPAB abolition.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Challenge validity of competitor's granted patent
- Defense in infringement action (counterclaim)
- Post-grant opposition period expired
- Need for in rem invalidation effect
- Central Government initiated revocation

---

## Prerequisites

- Granted Indian patent to challenge
- Identified grounds under Section 64(1)
- Standing as "person interested" or Central Government
- Evidence supporting revocation grounds

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understand Jurisdictional Changes (Post-IPAB 2021)

#### Post-Tribunal Reforms Act, 2021:
- IPAB abolished effective April 4, 2021
- Revocation jurisdiction transferred to **High Courts**
- Primary High Courts: Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai
- IP Divisions established in Delhi and Madras High Courts

#### Which High Court?
- Patent office where application filed (for original jurisdiction)
- Where cause of action arises (for revocation)

### Step 2: Identify Revocation Grounds (Section 64(1))

17 statutory grounds:

| Ground | Section | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| (a) | 64(1)(a) | Wrongfully obtained |
| (b) | 64(1)(b) | Granted to non-entitled person |
| (c) | 64(1)(c) | Prior claim in valid earlier patent |
| (d) | 64(1)(d) | Invention not new |
| (e) | 64(1)(e) | Obvious/lack inventive step |
| (f) | 64(1)(f) | Not useful |
| (g) | 64(1)(g) | Insufficient description |
| (h) | 64(1)(h) | Not entitled to priority date |
| (i) | 64(1)(i) | Claims not fairly based |
| (j) | 64(1)(j) | Non-patentable under Section 3 |
| (k) | 64(1)(k) | Withheld information under Section 8 |
| (l) | 64(1)(l) | Non-compliance with Section 8 direction |
| (m) | 64(1)(m) | Patent obtained on false suggestion |
| (n) | 64(1)(n) | Undisclosed invention used by government |
| (o) | 64(1)(o) | Application should have been refused |
| (p) | 64(1)(p) | Against public order/morality |
| (q) | 64(1)(q) | Non-compliance at examination |

### Step 3: Filing Revocation Petition

#### Who Can File:
1. **Any person interested** (wide standing)
2. **Central Government** (public interest)
3. **Counterclaim in infringement suit** (defendant)

#### Required Documents:
1. Petition with grounds clearly stated
2. Supporting evidence/documents
3. Claim analysis showing invalidity
4. Prior art documents with translations
5. Expert affidavits (if technical complexity)

### Step 4: Procedure Before High Court

1. **Filing**: Petition filed with prescribed fees
2. **Notice**: Served on patentee
3. **Written Statement**: Patentee's response
4. **Evidence**: Documentary and expert evidence
5. **Arguments**: Oral hearing before judge
6. **Judgment**: Court's reasoned order

### Step 5: Counterclaim in Infringement Suit

#### Section 64(1) proviso:
When revocation sought by counterclaim:
- Filed in same court as infringement suit
- High Court with original jurisdiction

#### Strategic Advantage:
- Defensive strategy in infringement
- Validity tested in same proceeding
- If successful, patent revoked (not just invalidity finding)

### Step 6: Effect of Revocation

#### In Rem Effect:
- Patent removed from Register of Patents
- "As if never existed"
- Binding on all parties (not just litigants)
- Cannot be enforced against anyone

#### Contrast with Invalidity Defense:
- Section 107 defense only creates in personam effect
- Requires additional steps under Section 71(1) for register correction
- Only binds parties to that suit

---

## Revocation vs. Section 107 Invalidity Defense

| Aspect | Revocation (Sec 64) | Invalidity Defense (Sec 107) |
|--------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| Effect | In rem (everyone) | In personam (parties only) |
| Outcome | Patent removed from register | Patent remains on register |
| Post-decision | No further action | Rectification under Sec 71(1) needed |
| Standing | Person interested | Defendant in infringement |
| Timing | Any time (even after patent expiry) | During infringement suit |

### Key Case: Macleods v. Lilly (Delhi HC)
Court clarified scope differences between revocation and invalidity defense.

---

## Timing Considerations

#### No Limitation Period:
- Revocation petition can be filed even after patent expiry
- Delhi HC confirmed in recent ruling
- Useful for clearing validity questions
- Important for follow-on products/generics

#### Strategic Timing:
- Before launching competing product
- Upon receiving infringement notice
- As counterclaim immediately upon suit filing

---

## Section 66: Government Power to Revoke

#### Grounds:
Patent invention used against public interest, specifically:
- Contrary to public order or morality
- Prejudicial to security of India
- Unreasonably restricts trade
- Not working to public benefit

#### Procedure:
1. Central Government forms opinion
2. Notification to patentee with grounds
3. Opportunity to be heard
4. After hearing, revocation ordered if satisfied

## Common Revocation Mistakes

1. **Wrong forum**: File before appropriate High Court
2. **Insufficient specificity**: Generic grounds fail
3. **Missing evidence**: Claims without proof dismissed
4. **Section 8 pleading**: Must show materiality
5. **Standing issues**: Establish "person interested"

## Revocation Petition Template Structure

```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF [DELHI/MUMBAI/KOLKATA/CHENNAI]
(INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY DIVISION)

Petition No. ___ of 2026

[Petitioner Name]
vs.
[Patentee Name]

PETITION FOR REVOCATION UNDER SECTION 64
OF THE PATENTS ACT, 1970

IN THE MATTER OF:
Patent No.: [IN/Patent Number]
Title: [Title of Invention]
Date of Grant: [Date]
Patentee: [Name]

PETITIONER'S INTEREST:
[Establish standing as person interested]

GROUNDS FOR REVOCATION:

I. UNDER SECTION 64(1)(d) - LACK OF NOVELTY:
[Detailed ground with prior art reference]

II. UNDER SECTION 64(1)(e) - OBVIOUSNESS:
[Detailed ground with TSM analysis]

III. UNDER SECTION 64(1)(k) - SECTION 8 NON-COMPLIANCE:
[Specific allegations with evidence]

EVIDENCE:
[List of exhibits]

PRAYER:
The Petitioner prays that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:
1. Revoke Patent No. [Number] in its entirety
2. Order rectification of Register of Patents
3. Award costs to Petitioner
4. Grant such other relief as deemed fit

[Verification]
[Signature of Petitioner/Advocate]
```
