# Protocol: Post-Grant Opposition

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-007
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Opposition Proceedings
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for filing and prosecuting post-grant oppositions under Section 25(2) of the Patents Act, 1970, including Opposition Board procedures and timeline management.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Competitor patent granted threatens business
- Prior art discovered invalidating granted patent
- Alternative to revocation petition before High Court
- Public interest grounds for challenging patent

---

## Prerequisites

- Granted Indian patent (published in Patent Journal)
- "Person interested" status (locus standi)
- Identified grounds for opposition under Section 25(2)
- Evidence supporting opposition grounds
- Within 12-month window from grant publication

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Verify Eligibility

#### Standing Requirement ("Person Interested"):
Must establish:
- Engaged in same field of technology, OR
- Research in relevant field, OR
- Commercially affected by patent, OR
- Public interest stake

#### Timeline Requirement:
- **Within 12 months** from date of grant publication in Patent Journal
- Publication occurs after certificate issuance

### Step 2: Identify Opposition Grounds (Section 25(2))

Same grounds as pre-grant opposition:

| Ground | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| (a) | Wrongfully obtained from opponent |
| (b) | Published before priority date (anticipation) |
| (c) | Publicly known/used in India before priority |
| (d) | Obvious in view of prior art |
| (e) | Not an invention under the Act |
| (f) | Non-patentable under Section 3 |
| (g) | Insufficient description |
| (h) | Non-disclosure under Section 8 |
| (i) | Convention application beyond 12 months |
| (j) | Not entitled to claim priority |
| (k) | Claims not fairly based on disclosure |

### Step 3: File Notice of Opposition

#### Required Documents:

1. **Form 7** - Notice of Opposition
2. **Written Statement of Opposition**
   - Detailed grounds with specificity
   - Reference to each claim opposed
   - Evidence list
3. **Evidence in Support**
   - Prior art documents
   - Expert declarations
   - Proof of standing
4. **Fee Payment**
   - ₹40,000 (large entities)
   - ₹10,000 (small entities/individuals)

#### Filing Location:
Same patent office where patent granted (territorial jurisdiction)

### Step 4: Notice to Patentee

Controller issues notice to patentee:
- Copy of opposition notice
- Request for reply
- Timeline for response

### Step 5: Constitution of Opposition Board

#### Board Composition:
- Three members
- Technical and legal expertise
- Independent assessment

#### Board's Role:
1. Examine all documents submitted
2. Review evidence from both parties
3. Submit recommendations to Controller
4. Timeline: **2 months** (per 2024 Rules, reduced from 3)

### Step 6: Evidence Submission

#### Opponent's Evidence:
- Filed with notice of opposition
- Additional evidence with leave of Controller

#### Patentee's Reply Evidence:
- Filed within prescribed timeline
- Counter-evidence addressing opposition

### Step 7: Hearing

1. **Notice of Hearing**: To both parties
2. **Hearing Fee**: ₹7,500 (2024 Rules)
3. **Pre-Hearing Submissions**: Written arguments
4. **Oral Hearing**: Both parties present case
5. **Controller's Decision**: Based on Board recommendation

### Step 8: Controller's Decision

Possible outcomes:
1. **Opposition Upheld**: Patent revoked (fully or partially)
2. **Opposition Rejected**: Patent maintained
3. **Patent Amended**: Claims modified to overcome objection

---

## Timeline Summary

| Event | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| Notice of Opposition | 12 months from grant publication |
| Patentee's Reply | As directed by Controller |
| Opposition Board Report | 2 months (2024 Rules) |
| Hearing | Scheduled by Controller |
| Controller's Decision | After hearing |

---

## Comparison: Pre-Grant vs. Post-Grant Opposition

| Aspect | Pre-Grant (Sec 25(1)) | Post-Grant (Sec 25(2)) |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Timing | After publication, before grant | Within 12 months of grant |
| Standing | Any person | Person interested |
| Prima Facie Filter | Yes (2024 Rules) | No |
| Opposition Board | No | Yes (3 members) |
| Fee | ₹20,000 | ₹10,000-40,000 |
| Effect if Successful | Application refused | Patent revoked |
| Appeal | Limited | To High Court |

## Strategic Considerations

### When to Use Post-Grant Opposition:
- Missed pre-grant window
- Additional prior art discovered after grant
- Prefer administrative route over litigation
- Cost-effective challenge mechanism

### Evidence Strategy:
- Front-load best evidence
- Expert declarations strengthen technical arguments
- Section 8 grounds increasingly scrutinized
- Document commercial interest for standing

### Timing:
- File early in 12-month window
- Allows time for complete evidence gathering
- Strategic timing with business considerations

---

## Appeals from Post-Grant Decision

#### If Opposition Upheld (Patent Revoked):
- Patentee may appeal to **High Court**
- Under Section 117A

#### If Opposition Rejected:
- Opponent may appeal to **High Court**
- Or pursue revocation under Section 64

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Standing as "person interested" established
- [ ] Within 12-month window verified
- [ ] Form 7 properly completed
- [ ] Grounds clearly identified with specificity
- [ ] Evidence organized and properly referenced
- [ ] Prior art documents complete with translations
- [ ] Fee paid correctly
- [ ] All claims targeted are addressed

---

## Common Post-Grant Opposition Mistakes

1. **Missing 12-month deadline**: No extension available
2. **Failing to prove standing**: Opposition dismissed
3. **Insufficient evidence**: Burden on opponent
4. **Not addressing all claims**: Partial success only
5. **Missing hearing fee**: Cannot attend hearing

## Notice of Opposition Template

```
FORM 7
NOTICE OF OPPOSITION TO GRANT OF PATENT
(Under Section 25(2))

Patent No.: [IN/Patent Number]
Title: [Title of Invention]
Patentee: [Name]
Date of Grant: [Date]

Opponent:
Name: [Full Name]
Address: [Complete Address]
Status: [Individual/Small Entity/Large Entity]

STANDING AS PERSON INTERESTED:
[Establish connection to field/commercial interest/research activity]

GROUNDS OF OPPOSITION:

Ground 1: [Section 25(2)(b) - Anticipation]
[Detailed statement with prior art references]

Ground 2: [Section 25(2)(d) - Obviousness]
[Detailed statement with prior art combination]

Ground 3: [Section 25(2)(h) - Section 8 Non-Compliance]
[Specific instances of non-disclosure]

CLAIMS OPPOSED: [List specific claim numbers]

EVIDENCE FILED: [List of documents annexed]

RELIEF SOUGHT: Revocation of Patent No. [Number]

Date: [Date]
Signature: [Opponent/Agent]

Fee: ₹[Amount] paid via [method]
```
