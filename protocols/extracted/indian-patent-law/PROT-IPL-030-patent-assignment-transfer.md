# Protocol: Patent Assignment and Transfer

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-030
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Transactions
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for patent assignment and transfer in India, covering Section 68-70 requirements, registration procedures, validity requirements, and documentation for complete and partial assignments.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Selling patent rights
- Corporate restructuring/M&A
- Inventor assignment to company
- Partial assignment (field of use)
- Succession/inheritance transfer

---

## Prerequisites

- Granted or pending patent
- Assignment agreement drafted
- Stamp duty assessment
- Understanding of registration requirements

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Assignment Framework

#### Legal Basis:

**Section 68 - Assignment by Patentee/Applicant**:
"A patent, or any right in a patent, or any interest in a patent, may be assigned... by an instrument in writing"

**Section 69 - Registration of Assignments**:
"Where any person becomes entitled by assignment, transmission or operation of law to a patent or to a share in a patent... he shall apply in writing to the Controller for registration"

#### Types of Transfers:

| Type | Description | Registration |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Complete Assignment** | All rights transferred | Required |
| **Partial Assignment** | Limited rights (field/territory) | Required |
| **Exclusive License** | Exclusive rights to use | Recordal |
| **Transmission** | By operation of law (death, merger) | Required |
| **Mortgage/Pledge** | Security interest | Recordal recommended |

### Step 2: Requirements for Valid Assignment

#### Statutory Requirements:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Writing** | Must be in writing (Section 68) |
| **Parties** | Clearly identified assignor/assignee |
| **Subject** | Patent number or application number |
| **Rights** | Scope of rights transferred |
| **Consideration** | Consideration stated (even if nominal) |
| **Execution** | Signatures of all parties |

#### Additional Best Practices:
- Notarization (recommended)
- Stamp duty payment (mandatory)
- Witness signatures
- Date of execution
- Governing law clause

### Step 3: Assignment Agreement Key Clauses

#### Essential Clauses:

```
PATENT ASSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

PARTIES:
Assignor: [Name, Address]
Assignee: [Name, Address]

RECITALS:
A. Assignor is the owner of Patent No. IN [...]
B. Assignor wishes to assign, and Assignee wishes to acquire...

OPERATIVE CLAUSES:

1. ASSIGNMENT
   Assignor hereby assigns to Assignee all right, title, and
   interest in and to Patent No. IN [...], including:
   - The patent itself
   - Right to sue for past infringement
   - All renewals and extensions
   - All foreign filing rights (if applicable)

2. CONSIDERATION
   In consideration of [₹Amount / terms], receipt acknowledged.

3. WARRANTIES
   Assignor warrants:
   - Sole owner with right to assign
   - No prior assignments or encumbrances
   - Patent is valid and subsisting
   - No pending litigation (or disclosed)

4. COOPERATION
   Assignor agrees to execute all documents and do all acts
   necessary to perfect the assignment.

5. RECORDATION
   Assignee shall register this assignment with the Indian
   Patent Office within [timeframe].

6. GOVERNING LAW
   This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of India.

SIGNATURES:
Assignor: _________________ Date: _______
Assignee: _________________ Date: _______
Witness: _________________ Date: _______
```

### Step 4: Stamp Duty

#### Requirement:
Stamp duty applicable under Indian Stamp Act, 1899 (varies by state)

#### Stamp Duty Calculation:

| State | Rate (Approximate) |
|-------|-------------------|
| Maharashtra | 3% of consideration or market value |
| Delhi | 3% |
| Karnataka | 5% |
| Tamil Nadu | 7% |
| Other states | Varies (2-8%) |

#### Payment Process:
1. Draft assignment agreement
2. Determine consideration/value
3. Calculate stamp duty
4. Pay duty before or at execution
5. Affix stamps or e-stamp certificate

#### Consequences of Non-Payment:
- Document inadmissible as evidence
- Impounding by court
- Penalty (up to 10x stamp duty)

### Step 5: Registration with Patent Office

#### Form 16 - Application for Registration:

**Timeline**: Within 6 months from assignment date (or before any rights exercised)

**Required Documents**:

| Document | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Form 16 | Application form |
| Assignment deed | Original or certified copy |
| Stamp duty proof | Receipt/e-stamp certificate |
| POA | If filed through agent |
| Fee | As per Schedule I |

#### Form 16 Contents:
```
FORM 16
APPLICATION FOR REGISTRATION OF ASSIGNMENT

1. Patent/Application No.: [...]
2. Title of Invention: [...]
3. Name of Assignor: [...]
4. Name of Assignee: [...]
5. Date of Assignment: [...]
6. Nature of Assignment: Complete/Partial
7. If Partial, describe: [...]
8. Consideration: ₹[...]

DECLARATION:
The particulars given above are true to the best of my knowledge.

Signature of Assignee: [...]
Date: [...]
```

#### Registration Fees (E-filing):

| Entity | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| Natural Person/Startup | ₹800 |
| Small Entity | ₹2,000 |
| Large Entity | ₹4,000 |

### Step 6: Effect of Registration

#### Section 69(3) - Until Registered:
"[T]he document shall not be admitted as evidence of the title... in any court"

#### Practical Effects:

| Aspect | Registered | Not Registered |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| Ownership proof | Valid | Not admissible |
| Right to sue | Assignee can sue | Cannot sue |
| License validity | Can grant | Questionable |
| Transfer chain | Clear | Broken |
| Third parties | Binding | May not bind |

### Step 7: Special Assignment Scenarios

#### Pending Application Assignment:

**Section 68(1)**: Applicants may also assign
- Same process as granted patents
- Form 16 filed with application number
- Important for inventor-to-company transfers

#### Partial Assignment:

**Types**:
- Territory limited (India/specific states)
- Field of use limited (pharma/agriculture)
- Time limited (specified period)

**Drafting Considerations**:
- Clear scope definition
- No overlap with retained rights
- Enforcement provisions

#### Transmission by Operation of Law:

**Death of Patentee**:
- Assignment to legal heirs
- Succession certificate/probate required
- Form 16 with succession documents

**Corporate Events**:
- Merger: Automatic by operation of law
- Demerger: Specific assignment
- Bankruptcy: Liquidator assigns

## Common Assignment Mistakes

1. **No stamp duty**: Document inadmissible
2. **Delayed registration**: Ownership disputes
3. **Incomplete assignment deed**: Missing essential terms
4. **Wrong party signing**: Authority issues
5. **No warranty clause**: Liability gaps
6. **Ignoring pending applications**: Must assign separately
