# Protocol: Indian Patent Application Filing

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-001
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Filing Procedures
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive protocol for filing patent applications with the Indian Patent Office (IPO), covering ordinary applications, convention applications, PCT national phase entries, and divisional applications under the Patents Act, 1970 and Patents Rules, 2003 (as amended 2024).

---

## Activation Triggers

- User needs to file a patent application in India
- Questions about patent filing requirements, forms, or deadlines
- PCT national phase entry into India
- Divisional application filing strategy
- Startup/small entity fee benefits inquiry

---

## Prerequisites

- Invention disclosure or draft specification
- Inventor and applicant details
- Priority document (if claiming convention priority)
- Power of Attorney (Form 26) if using patent agent
- Entity status determination (natural person/startup/small entity/large entity)

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Determine Application Type

1. **Ordinary Application**: No foreign priority claim
2. **Convention Application**: Claiming priority from Paris Convention country (within 12 months)
3. **PCT National Phase**: Entry from international application (within 31 months + 6 months extension)
4. **Divisional Application**: Splitting from parent application under Section 16

### Step 2: Prepare Required Documents

#### For All Applications:
- **Form 1**: Application for grant of patent
- **Form 2**: Complete/Provisional Specification
- **Form 3**: Statement and undertaking regarding foreign applications (Section 8)
- **Form 5**: Declaration of inventorship
- **Drawings** (if applicable)

#### Additional for Convention/PCT:
- Priority document (certified copy)
- Verified English translation (if not in English)
- Form 26: Power of Attorney

### Step 3: Determine Appropriate Patent Office

| Applicant Location | Patent Office |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Delhi, HP, J&K, Punjab, Rajasthan, UP, Haryana, Uttarakhand | Delhi |
| Maharashtra, Gujarat, MP, Goa, Chhattisgarh | Mumbai |
| West Bengal, Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, NE States | Kolkata |
| Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, AP, Telangana, Puducherry | Chennai |

### Step 4: Calculate and Pay Fees

#### Filing Fees (Electronic Filing):
| Entity Type | Complete Spec | Provisional Spec |
|-------------|---------------|------------------|
| Natural Person/Startup | ₹1,600 | ₹1,600 |
| Small Entity | ₹4,000 | ₹4,000 |
| Large Entity | ₹8,000 | ₹8,000 |

**Note**: 10% additional for physical filing

### Step 5: File Application

1. Access IPO e-filing portal: https://ipindiaonline.gov.in
2. Create/login to account
3. Upload all documents
4. Pay fees online
5. Download filing receipt with application number

### Step 6: Post-Filing Compliance

1. **Form 3 Updates**: File within 6 months of Indian filing, then updated Form 3 within 3 months of First Examination Report (FER)
2. **Request for Examination (Form 18)**: Within 31 months (for applications filed after March 15, 2024)
3. **Publication**: Automatic at 18 months, or early publication via Form 9

| Milestone | Deadline |
|-----------|----------|

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Correct application type selected
- [ ] All required forms completed accurately
- [ ] Specification meets disclosure requirements (Section 10)
- [ ] Claims are clear, succinct, and fairly based on specification
- [ ] Priority document filed (if applicable)
- [ ] Form 3 compliance checked
- [ ] Correct entity status declared (Form 28)
- [ ] Fees calculated and paid correctly
- [ ] E-filing receipt obtained

---

## Common Errors to Avoid

1. **Missing Form 3**: Can lead to opposition/revocation grounds
2. **Incorrect priority claim**: Verify dates and application numbers
3. **Late RFE filing**: Application deemed withdrawn
4. **Wrong entity status**: Can invalidate fee benefits
5. **Incomplete specification**: Insufficient disclosure objections
