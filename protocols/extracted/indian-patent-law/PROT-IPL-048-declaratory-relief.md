# Protocol: Declaratory Relief and Non-Infringement Actions

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-048
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Litigation
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide to seeking declaratory relief in patent matters, including declarations of non-infringement, invalidity, and related remedies under Indian law.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Receiving cease and desist letter
- Launching new product with patent concerns
- Patent threat creating business uncertainty
- Need for pre-emptive clarity
- Counterclaim strategy in litigation

---

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of declaratory relief principles
- Knowledge of jurisdiction requirements
- Awareness of standing requirements
- Familiarity with High Court procedures

## Core Process

### Step 1: Types of Declaratory Relief

#### Available Declarations:

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Non-infringement** | Product/process doesn't infringe |
| **Invalidity** | Patent should not have been granted |
| **Non-patentability** | Subject matter not patentable |
| **Groundless threats** | Threats without basis (Section 106) |
| **Revocation** | Patent should be revoked |

### Step 2: Legal Framework

#### Statutory Provisions:

**Section 105 - Power of Court to Grant Relief**:
Courts may grant declarations of non-infringement.

**Section 106 - Groundless Threats**:
Remedies for groundless threats of infringement proceedings.

#### Jurisdiction:

| Forum | Declaratory Relief |
|-------|-------------------|
| **High Court** | General declaratory jurisdiction |
| **District Court** | Limited (< Rs. 1 crore) |
| **Commercial Court** | Yes, if commercial dispute |

### Step 3: Standing Requirements

#### Who Can Seek Declaration:

```
STANDING FOR DECLARATORY RELIEF

Required Elements:
├── Legal interest in declaration
├── Actual controversy (not hypothetical)
├── Reasonable apprehension of litigation
└── Justiciable dispute

Typically:
├── Cease and desist recipient
├── Product launcher with patent concern
├── Party threatened with litigation
└── Licensee disputing validity
```

#### Justiciable Controversy:

| Scenario | Standing? |
|----------|----------|
| Received C&D letter | Yes |
| Competitor patent granted | Uncertain |
| Planning product launch | If specific threat |
| General market uncertainty | No |
| Active licensing negotiation | Yes |

### Step 4: Groundless Threats (Section 106)

#### Elements:

**What Constitutes Groundless Threat**:
- Written or oral threat
- Of infringement proceedings
- Relating to patent
- Without justification

#### Remedies Available:

| Remedy | Nature |
|--------|--------|
| Declaration | Threats are unjustifiable |
| Injunction | Against continuation of threats |
| Damages | For loss caused by threats |

#### What's NOT a Threat:

- Notification of patent existence
- Mere assertion of patent ownership
- General industry communications

### Step 5: Declaration of Non-Infringement

#### Requirements:

```
NON-INFRINGEMENT DECLARATION

Plaintiff Must Show:
1. Identified patent
2. Specific product/process
3. Claim-by-claim analysis
4. No infringement

Court Examines:
├── Claim construction
├── Accused product analysis
├── Element-by-element comparison
└── Conclusion on infringement
```

#### Burden of Proof:

| Element | Burden |
|---------|--------|
| Claim interpretation | Court determines |
| Product characteristics | Plaintiff proves |
| Non-infringement | Plaintiff proves |

### Step 6: Declaration of Invalidity

#### Grounds (Similar to Section 64):

| Ground | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Section 3 exclusions | Not patentable subject matter |
| Prior art | Anticipated or obvious |
| Insufficiency | Not fully disclosed |
| False suggestion | Material misrepresentation |
| Wrong inventorship | True inventor not named |

#### Procedure:

Can be sought:
- As standalone action
- As counterclaim in infringement suit
- Combined with non-infringement

### Step 7: Procedural Aspects

#### Filing Declaratory Suit:

**Required Contents**:

```
DECLARATORY SUIT PLAINT

1. PARTIES
   - Plaintiff: [Party seeking declaration]
   - Defendant: [Patentee]

2. JURISDICTION
   - [Basis for High Court jurisdiction]

3. FACTS
   - Patent details
   - Plaintiff's product/process
   - Threat/controversy details

4. CAUSE OF ACTION
   - [Why declaration needed]

5. ANALYSIS
   - Claim construction
   - Non-infringement reasoning
   - OR invalidity grounds

6. PRAYER
   (a) Declaration that plaintiff's product
       does not infringe Patent No. [X]
   (b) Declaration that Patent No. [X]
       is invalid on grounds of [...]
   (c) Costs
```

#### Evidence:

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Technical analysis | Non-infringement |
| Prior art | Invalidity |
| Threat correspondence | Standing/controversy |
| Expert evidence | Claim interpretation |

### Step 8: Tactical Considerations

#### When to Seek Declaration:

**Advantages**:
- Forum selection (sue in preferred court)
- First mover advantage
- Control of litigation timing
- Resolve uncertainty

**Disadvantages**:
- Costs of initiating
- May trigger infringement counterclaim
- Disclosure of your product
- Relationship impact

#### Forum Shopping:

```
DECLARATORY SUIT JURISDICTION

Options:
├── Where plaintiff carries on business
├── Where defendant resides/works
├── Where cause of action arose
└── Commercial Court (if applicable)

Strategy:
- Choose favorable forum
- Consider patent-experienced courts
- Delhi/Mumbai HC preferred for complexity
```

## Groundless Threats Analysis

### Section 106 Procedure:

```
Threat Received
      │
      ├── Is it a threat of proceedings?
      │   ├── No → No Section 106 action
      │   └── Yes → Continue
      │
      ├── Are threats justified?
      │   ├── Patent valid + infringement → Justified
      │   └── Either lacking → Groundless
      │
      └── Remedies available
          ├── Declaration
          ├── Injunction against threats
          └── Damages
```

### Safe Harbor (Section 106 Proviso):

Threats are NOT actionable if:
- Made to primary infringer (manufacturer)
- Relate to making or importing
- Defendant is actual infringer

## Common Declaratory Relief Mistakes

1. **No actual controversy**: Speculative concerns
2. **Wrong forum**: Jurisdiction challenge
3. **Incomplete analysis**: Missing claim elements
4. **No standing**: No threat or apprehension
5. **Premature filing**: Before real controversy
6. **Missing counterclaim risk**: Invites infringement suit

## Template: Declaration of Non-Infringement Prayer

```
PRAYER

The Plaintiff most respectfully prays that this
Hon'ble Court be pleased to:

(a) DECLARE that the Plaintiff's product known as
    [Product Name] and described in Annexure [X]
    does not infringe any claim of Indian Patent
    No. [Patent Number];

(b) DECLARE that any threats of patent infringement
    proceedings made by the Defendant are groundless
    and unjustifiable;

(c) PERMANENTLY INJUNCT the Defendant from making
    further threats of infringement proceedings;

(d) AWARD damages for loss caused by the Defendant's
    groundless threats;

(e) AWARD costs of this petition;

(f) GRANT such other and further relief as this
    Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper.
```
