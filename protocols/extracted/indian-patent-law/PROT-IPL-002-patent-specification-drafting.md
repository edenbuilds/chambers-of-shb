# Protocol: Patent Specification Drafting

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-002
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Specification Drafting
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guidelines for drafting patent specifications (provisional and complete) under Indian patent law, ensuring compliance with Section 10 disclosure requirements and optimizing claim scope for protection.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Drafting a new patent specification
- Converting provisional to complete specification
- Amending claims during prosecution
- Claim drafting strategy questions
- Disclosure sufficiency concerns

---

## Prerequisites

- Complete invention disclosure from inventor
- Prior art search results
- Understanding of commercial embodiments
- Knowledge of competitive landscape

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understand Section 10 Requirements

The specification must:
1. **Fully and particularly describe** the invention and its operation
2. **Disclose the best method** of performing the invention known to applicant
3. **End with claims** defining scope of protection
4. Claims must be **clear, succinct, and fairly based** on the specification

### Step 2: Structure the Specification

#### Title
- Concise, descriptive of invention
- Match with independent claim preamble
- Avoid marketing language

#### Field of Invention
- Technical field to which invention pertains
- Sets context for novelty/inventive step analysis

#### Background/Prior Art
- Describe existing solutions objectively
- Identify technical problems/shortcomings
- DO NOT admit prior art that isn't documented

#### Summary of Invention
- Brief description of solution
- Key technical advantages
- Reference to all independent claims

#### Detailed Description
- Multiple embodiments (preferred and alternatives)
- Enable person skilled in art (POSITA) to practice
- Describe all figures/drawings
- Include examples where applicable

#### Claims
- Independent claims: Broadest scope
- Dependent claims: Specific features, fallback positions

### Step 3: Claim Drafting Best Practices

#### Independent Claim Structure
```
A [preamble - what the invention is],
comprising/characterized by:
[body - essential elements and their relationships]
```

#### Avoid Two-Part Claims When Possible
- Indian law doesn't mandate two-part format
- IPO examiners may request amendment to two-part
- Two-part format can limit claim interpretation

#### Claim Types to Include
1. **Product/Apparatus claims**: Physical structure
2. **Method/Process claims**: Steps to achieve result
3. **System claims**: Multiple components working together
4. **Use claims**: Specific applications (where applicable)

### Step 4: Special Considerations by Technology

#### Computer-Related Inventions (CRIs)
- Focus on **technical effect** (per 2025 CRI Guidelines)
- Avoid claiming "computer program per se"
- Include hardware elements in system claims
- Describe algorithm implementation, not just algorithm

**Example (Allowable):**
"A system for preventing unauthorized access comprising:
- a processor configured to execute...
- a memory storing instructions that...
- resulting in reduced latency and improved security"

**Example (Not Allowable):**
"A method of pricing comprising calculating... [pure business method]"

#### Biotechnology/Pharmaceuticals
- Avoid Section 3(d) pitfalls (mere new forms)
- Demonstrate **enhanced therapeutic efficacy**
- Include experimental data supporting efficacy
- Markush claims for chemical variations

#### Mechanical Inventions
- Detailed structural descriptions
- Multiple drawing views
- Dimensional relationships where critical

### Step 5: Disclosure Best Practices

1. **Enablement**: Can POSITA make and use without undue experimentation?
2. **Written Description**: Full support for all claims
3. **Best Mode**: Disclose inventor's preferred embodiment
4. **Examples**: Include working examples, especially for chemistry/biotech

### Step 6: Drawings

- **Rule 15**: Drawings must be in permanent ink, black lines
- Scale: Suitable for reproduction at 2/3 reduction
- Reference numerals consistent with specification
- Flow charts for method claims
- Block diagrams for system claims

---

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Title matches independent claim preamble
- [ ] Background doesn't admit unnecessary prior art
- [ ] All elements in claims described in specification
- [ ] Claims are clear and succinct
- [ ] Claims fairly based on specification
- [ ] Best mode disclosed
- [ ] Multiple embodiments included
- [ ] Drawings properly numbered and referenced
- [ ] No prohibited subject matter (Section 3)
- [ ] CRI claims show technical effect
- [ ] Pharma claims avoid Section 3(d)

---

## Common Drafting Pitfalls

1. **Over-narrow claims**: Lost commercial coverage
2. **Over-broad claims**: Invalid over prior art
3. **Insufficient disclosure**: Grounds for revocation
4. **Best mode not disclosed**: Revocation ground (Section 64)
5. **Claims not fairly based**: Rejection during prosecution
6. **Pure algorithm/business method claims**: Section 3(k) rejection

## Claim Hierarchy Example

```
Claim 1 (Independent - Broadest):
A method for [technical result] comprising:
  step a);
  step b);
  step c).

Claim 2 (Dependent):
The method of claim 1, wherein step a) includes [specific feature].

Claim 3 (Dependent):
The method of claim 1 or 2, further comprising step d).

Claim 4 (Independent - Apparatus):
A system for implementing the method of claim 1, comprising:
  processor;
  memory;
  [specific components].
```
