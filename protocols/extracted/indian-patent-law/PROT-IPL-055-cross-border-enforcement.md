# Protocol: Cross-Border Patent Enforcement

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-055
**Version**: 1.0.0

**Category**: Enforcement
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide to cross-border patent enforcement issues involving India, including enforcing Indian patents against foreign actors, coordinating multi-jurisdiction enforcement, and addressing transnational infringement.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Infringer located outside India
- Products imported into India
- Manufacturing abroad, sales in India
- Export-based infringement
- Multi-national infringement network
- Global enforcement coordination

## Core Process

### Step 1: Jurisdictional Framework

#### Indian Patent Territorial Scope:

```
INDIAN PATENT TERRITORIAL LIMITS

Indian Patent Protects:
├── Making in India
├── Using in India
├── Selling in India
├── Offering for sale in India
├── Importing into India
└── Stocking for above purposes

Does NOT Protect (directly):
├── Activities wholly outside India
├── Making in foreign country
├── Sales in foreign country
└── Transit through India (unclear)
```

#### Key Principle:

Indian patents are territorial - enforceable only for acts in India.

### Step 2: Import-Based Infringement

#### Common Scenario:

| Activity | Where | Infringement? |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| Manufacturing | Abroad | No (by India) |
| Importing | Into India | Yes |
| Selling | In India | Yes |
| Transit | Through India | Uncertain |

#### Enforcement Against Importers:

```
IMPORT INFRINGEMENT ENFORCEMENT

Defendants:
├── Importer of record
├── Distributor
├── Retailer
├── End user (unusual)
└── NOT foreign manufacturer (directly)

Evidence Needed:
├── Import records
├── Customs data
├── Product samples
├── Chain of custody
└── Technical analysis

Remedies:
├── Injunction against import
├── Damages for past imports
├── Account of profits
├── Customs border measures
└── Seizure and destruction
```

### Step 3: Customs Border Enforcement

#### Section 28 Border Measures:

```
CUSTOMS RECORDATION SYSTEM

1. Register Patent with Customs:
   ├── Application to Commissioner of Customs
   ├── Patent details
   ├── Product descriptions
   └── Intelligence on shipments

2. Notice of Infringing Goods:
   ├── Provide information to Customs
   ├── Expected arrival details
   └── Technical identification

3. Customs Action:
   ├── Examination of shipment
   ├── Detention if suspected
   ├── Notification to patentee
   └── Opportunity to confirm

4. Follow-Up:
   ├── Formal complaint if confirmed
   ├── Court proceedings
   └── Seizure and disposition
```

#### Practical Challenges:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Detection | Intelligence, monitoring |
| Time pressure | Pre-registration, rapid response |
| Technical assessment | Expert assistance |
| Multiple ports | Pan-India coordination |

### Step 4: Multi-Jurisdiction Coordination

#### Coordinated Enforcement Strategy:

```
GLOBAL ENFORCEMENT COORDINATION

1. Portfolio Mapping:
   ├── India patent
   ├── US patent
   ├── EU patents
   ├── Other key markets
   └── PCT strategy

2. Identify Infringer Network:
   ├── Manufacturer (where?)
   ├── Distributors (which countries?)
   ├── Importers (each market)
   └── Retailers

3. Enforcement Sequencing:
   ├── Preliminary injunction (fastest forum)
   ├── Manufacturing country action
   ├── Major market actions
   └── Smaller market follow-up

4. Coordination:
   ├── Consistent claim interpretation
   ├── Evidence sharing
   ├── Settlement leverage
   └── Resource allocation
```

### Step 5: Acting Against Foreign Defendants

#### Jurisdiction Over Foreign Parties:

| Basis | Requirements |
|-------|--------------|
| Business in India | Office, sales, agents |
| Website sales | Targeting Indian market |
| Import activity | Direct or through agents |
| Contractual submission | Consent to jurisdiction |

#### Service on Foreign Defendants:

```
SERVICE ON FOREIGN DEFENDANT

Options:
1. Service through Indian agent/distributor

2. Direct service in foreign country:
   ├── Letter rogatory
   ├── Hague Convention (if applicable)
   └── Diplomatic channels

3. Substituted service:
   ├── If defendant evading
   ├── Court permission required
   └── By advertisement/email

4. Contractual service:
   └── Per agreed mechanism
```

### Step 6: Evidence Gathering Across Borders

#### Cross-Border Discovery:

| Method | Application |
|--------|------------|

CROSS-BORDER EVIDENCE

### Step 8: Practical Enforcement Strategies

#### Against Import-Based Infringement:

```
STRATEGY: IMPORT ENFORCEMENT

Phase 1 - Intelligence:
├── Monitor market for infringing products
├── Trace supply chain
├── Identify importer(s)
└── Gather evidence

Phase 2 - Customs:
├── Register with Customs
├── Provide product intelligence
├── Coordinate with authorities
└── Respond to detentions

Phase 3 - Litigation:
├── Sue importer in India
├── Seek injunction
├── Pursue damages
└── Destruction of goods

Phase 4 - Upstream:
├── Demand letter to foreign manufacturer
├── Foreign litigation (if patent exists)
├── Settlement negotiation
└── Licensing discussion
```

#### Against Manufacturing Abroad + India Sales:

| Approach | Target | Action |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **India action** | Importer/distributor | Injunction, damages |
| **Source action** | Manufacturer | Foreign patent suit |
| **Customs** | Border | Block imports |
| **Settlement** | All parties | License agreement |

## Cross-Border Enforcement Flowchart

```
Infringing Activity Detected
        │
        ├── Where is manufacture?
        │   ├── In India → Standard India suit
        │   └── Abroad → Continue analysis
        │
        ├── Where are sales?
        │   ├── India → India suit vs. importer
        │   ├── Both → Multi-jurisdiction
        │   └── Only abroad → Foreign action needed
        │
        └── Strategy Selection:
            ├── Import blocking → Customs + injunction
            ├── Manufacturer pressure → Foreign suit
            ├── Distributor targeting → India action
            └── Global settlement → Coordinated approach
```

## Common Cross-Border Mistakes

1. **Suing wrong party**: Foreign manufacturer not amenable
2. **No border enforcement**: Missing customs opportunity
3. **Evidence gaps**: Cannot prove import chain
4. **Inconsistent positions**: Different arguments in different countries
5. **Poor coordination**: Duplicated efforts
6. **Ignoring settlement**: Over-litigation
