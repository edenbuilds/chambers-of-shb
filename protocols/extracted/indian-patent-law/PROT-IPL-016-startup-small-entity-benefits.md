# Protocol: Startup and Small Entity IP Benefits

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-016
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Fee Benefits & Expedited Procedures
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide to startup and small entity benefits under Indian patent law, including SIPP scheme, expedited examination eligibility, fee reductions, and procedures for claiming and maintaining entity status.

---

## Activation Triggers

- New startup planning patent filing
- Evaluating entity status for fee benefits
- Requesting expedited examination
- DPIIT startup recognition process
- Entity status change assessment

- Understanding of MSME Act definitions
- DPIIT startup recognition (if applicable)
- Financial records for status verification

## Core Process

### Step 1: Determine Entity Category

#### Entity Categories Under Patent Rules:

| Category | Definition | Key Criteria |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| **Natural Person** | Individual | Human being |
| **Startup** | DPIIT recognized | <10 years old, <₹100 Cr turnover |
| **Small Entity** | Per MSME Act | Investment/turnover limits |
| **Large Entity** | All others | Default category |

### Step 2: Startup Recognition (DPIIT)

#### Eligibility Criteria:

1. **Age**: Not more than 10 years from incorporation/registration
2. **Turnover**: Less than ₹100 Crore in any financial year
3. **Innovation**: Working towards innovation, development, or deployment of new products/processes/services driven by technology or IP

**Exclusion**: Entity formed by splitting up or reconstruction of existing business

#### Recognition Process:

1. Register on Startup India portal (startupindia.gov.in)
2. Submit self-certification
3. Provide details of innovation/technology
4. Receive DPIIT recognition certificate

#### Foreign Applicants as Startups:

Per Rule 2(fa) interpretation:
- Foreign entities can qualify as startups
- Must meet same age and turnover criteria
- Self-certification acceptable

### Step 3: Small Entity Definition

#### Per MSME Act, 2006 (Updated 2020):

| Category | Investment in Plant/Machinery | Turnover |
|----------|------------------------------|----------|
| Micro | Up to ₹1 Crore | Up to ₹5 Crore |
| Small | ₹1-10 Crore | ₹5-50 Crore |
| Medium | ₹10-50 Crore | ₹50-250 Crore |

**For Patent Rules**: Micro + Small + Medium = Small Entity

**Note**: Medium enterprises can also claim small entity status for patent purposes

### Step 4: Fee Benefits

#### Comparative Fee Schedule (E-filing):

| Fee Type | Natural Person/Startup | Small Entity | Large Entity |
|----------|------------------------|--------------|--------------|
| Filing (Complete) | ₹1,600 | ₹4,000 | ₹8,000 |
| RFE | ₹4,000 | ₹10,000 | ₹20,000 |
| Expedited Exam | ₹8,000 | ₹20,000 | ₹40,000 |
| Grant Fee | ₹4,000 | ₹10,000 | ₹20,000 |
| Renewal (3rd year) | ₹800 | ₹2,000 | ₹4,000 |
| Pre-grant opposition | ₹20,000 | ₹20,000 | ₹20,000 |
| Post-grant opposition | ₹10,000 | ₹10,000 | ₹40,000 |

**Effective Discount**:
- Startup/Natural Person: **80% savings** vs. Large Entity
- Small Entity: **50% savings** vs. Large Entity

### Step 5: SIPP Scheme Benefits

#### Scheme for Facilitating Startups Intellectual Property Protection (SIPP):

**Professional Fee Subsidy**:
- Government pays patent agent/attorney fees
- Startup only pays official fees
- Available for patents, trademarks, designs

**Facilitation Centers**:
- Free IP advisory services
- Assistance with filing process
- Examination and prosecution support

**How to Access**:
1. Get DPIIT startup recognition
2. Register on Startup India IP portal
3. Select empaneled facilitator
4. File through SIPP scheme

### Step 6: Expedited Examination

#### Eligibility for Expedited Examination (Rule 24C):

| Eligible Applicants | Additional Requirement |
|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Startups | DPIIT recognition |
| Small Entities | MSME certificate |
| Female Applicants | Natural person |
| Government/Public Institutions | Documentary proof |
| PPH Agreement | Japan only (currently) |
| Green Technology | Self-certification |

#### Expedited Examination Process:

1. **File Form 18A** with required fee
2. **Provide eligibility evidence** (recognition certificate, Form 28)
3. **Controller acceptance** within 30 days
4. **FER issuance** within 105 days of acceptance
5. **Target grant** within 12-18 months

#### Expedited Examination Fees:
| Entity | Fee (E-filing) |
|--------|----------------|
| Natural Person/Startup | ₹8,000 |
| Small Entity | ₹20,000 |
| Large Entity | ₹40,000 |

### Step 7: Claiming Entity Status

#### Form 28 - Declaration of Status:

Must file Form 28 declaring:
- Entity category
- Basis for claim (DPIIT recognition number, MSME registration, etc.)
- Undertaking to inform if status changes

#### When to File:
- At the time of filing application, OR
- Before any fee payment claiming reduced rate

#### Supporting Documents:
- **Startup**: DPIIT recognition certificate
- **Small Entity**: MSME registration certificate
- **Female Applicant**: Self-declaration

### Step 8: Maintaining Entity Status

#### If Status Changes During Prosecution:

**Rule**: Once expedited examination requested as startup/small entity, status change doesn't affect that application

**Per Rule 24C(5)**:
"A request for expedited examination...shall not be questioned merely on the ground that the startup or small entity...ceases to be a startup or small entity"

#### For Future Applications:
- Update Form 28 for new applications
- Pay appropriate fees based on current status

## Common Entity Status Mistakes

1. **No Form 28 filed**: Cannot claim reduced fees
2. **Expired DPIIT recognition**: Must renew or requalify
3. **Incorrect MSME classification**: Verify current criteria
4. **Foreign startup assumption**: Must meet Indian criteria
5. **Status change not tracked**: Different rates for future filings
