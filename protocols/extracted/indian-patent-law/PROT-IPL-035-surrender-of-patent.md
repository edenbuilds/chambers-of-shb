# Protocol: Surrender of Patent (Section 63)

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-035
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Termination
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for voluntary surrender of patents under Section 63, including procedure, third-party interests, strategic considerations, and effects of surrender.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Patent no longer commercially valuable
- Maintenance costs exceed benefits
- Settlement of litigation
- License agreement requirements
- Strategic portfolio pruning

---

## Prerequisites

- Granted Indian patent
- Understanding of surrender effects
- Assessment of third-party rights
- Strategic decision made

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Surrender

#### Section 63 - Surrender of Patents:

"A patentee may at any time by giving notice in the prescribed manner to the Controller offer to surrender his patent"

#### Key Characteristics:

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Who** | Patentee only |
| **When** | Any time after grant |
| **Effect** | Patent ceases to have effect |
| **Reversible?** | No |
| **Cost** | Official fee |

### Step 2: Reasons for Surrender

#### Strategic Reasons:

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cost savings** | Avoid renewal fees |
| **Settlement** | Part of litigation settlement |
| **Invalidity risk** | Avoid adverse precedent |
| **Obsolete technology** | No commercial value |
| **Portfolio pruning** | Focus on valuable assets |
| **License agreement** | Contractual obligation |

#### Surrender vs. Non-Renewal:

| Aspect | Surrender | Non-Renewal |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Timing | Immediate | Next renewal date |
| Third parties | Must advertise | No notice |
| Opposition | Possible | Not applicable |
| Restoration | Not possible | Possible (Section 60) |
| Publication | Published | Not published |

### Step 3: Procedure

#### Form 29 - Notice of Surrender:

**Contents**:
```
FORM 29
NOTICE OF SURRENDER OF PATENT

1. PATENT DETAILS
   Patent No.: IN [...]
   Date of Grant: [...]
   Title: [...]

2. PATENTEE DETAILS
   Name: [...]
   Address: [...]

3. STATEMENT
   The patentee hereby offers to surrender Patent No. [...]
   under Section 63 of the Patents Act, 1970.

4. REASONS (optional)
   [...]

5. DECLARATION
   The patentee declares that all persons having interest
   in the patent have been notified/there are no persons
   having interest other than the patentee.

Signature: [...]
Date: [...]
```

#### Procedure Flow:

```
Patentee decides to surrender
        ↓
File Form 29 with fee
        ↓
Controller publishes notice
        ↓
3-month opposition period
        ↓
Opposition filed? → Hearing → Decision
        ↓
No opposition → Patent surrendered
        ↓
Published in Official Gazette
```

### Step 4: Third-Party Rights

#### Section 63(2) - Interested Persons:

"If any person... has any interest in the patent, [they] may within the prescribed period... give notice to the Controller of opposition"

#### Who May Oppose:

| Category | Interest |
|----------|----------|
| **Licensee** | License rights affected |
| **Sub-licensee** | Derived rights |
| **Mortgagee** | Security interest |
| **Registered user** | Usage rights |
| **Potential infringement defendant** | Declaratory interest |

#### Opposition Grounds:

- Surrender prejudices their rights
- License agreement prohibits surrender
- Surrender made in bad faith
- Other legitimate interests affected

### Step 5: Controller's Decision

#### Assessment:

After opposition period:
- If no opposition: Accept surrender
- If opposition: Hearing held

#### Hearing Considerations:

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Nature of opponent's interest | Primary |
| Prejudice to opponent | Important |
| Reasons for surrender | Considered |
| Overall equity | Balanced |

#### Possible Outcomes:

| Outcome | When |
|---------|------|
| **Accept surrender** | No valid opposition |
| **Reject surrender** | Opposition upheld |
| **Conditional acceptance** | Subject to terms |

### Step 6: Effects of Surrender

#### Immediate Effects:

```
Surrender accepted
        ↓
Patent ceases to have effect
        ↓
Rights under patent extinguished
        ↓
Publication in Gazette
        ↓
Register updated
```

#### Consequences:

| Aspect | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Patentee rights** | Extinguished |
| **Licensee rights** | Generally terminate |
| **Infringement claims** | Past claims may survive |
| **Third party freedom** | Free to practice |
| **Renewal fees** | No longer due |

### Step 7: Strategic Considerations

#### Before Surrendering:

**Consider**:
- [ ] Remaining patent term
- [ ] Maintenance costs vs. potential value
- [ ] Licensee agreements (termination clauses)
- [ ] Pending litigation
- [ ] Cross-license implications
- [ ] Competitor impact
- [ ] Tax implications

#### Alternatives to Surrender:

| Alternative | When Appropriate |
|-------------|------------------|
| **Non-renewal** | Cost saving, no urgency |
| **Sale** | If market value exists |
| **Donation** | University/research use |
| **Dedication** | Public interest |
| **Abandonment during prosecution** | Pre-grant |

### Step 8: Special Situations

#### During Infringement Litigation:

- Surrender may be strategic to avoid invalidity finding
- Opposing party may oppose surrender
- Court may consider surrender in costs

#### During Compulsory Licensing:

- Patentee may seek to surrender to avoid CL
- Controller may consider CL application
- Third party interest in maintaining patent

#### Partial Surrender (via Amendment):

Not technically surrender, but:
- Disclaim certain claims
- Narrow scope
- Section 57/58 amendment route

## Common Surrender Mistakes

1. **Not checking licensees**: May have interest in opposition
2. **Ignoring tax implications**: May be asset disposal
3. **Surrendering during litigation**: May harm position
4. **No strategic analysis**: Hasty decision
5. **Forgetting cross-licenses**: Implications for other rights
6. **Joint patentee not joining**: All must agree
