## Purpose

Comprehensive guide on traditional knowledge protection under Indian patent law, including Section 3(p) exclusion, Traditional Knowledge Digital Library (TKDL), biopiracy prevention, and access and benefit sharing requirements.

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understand Section 3(p) Exclusion

#### Section 3(p) - Traditional Knowledge:
"An invention which, in effect, is traditional knowledge or which is an aggregation or duplication of known properties of traditionally known component or components"

#### What's Excluded:
- Direct use of traditional knowledge
- Aggregation of known traditional properties
- Duplication of traditional formulations

#### What May Be Patentable:
- Novel derivatives with enhanced efficacy (subject to 3(d))
- New combinations with non-obvious synergy
- New applications genuinely not known traditionally

### Step 2: Understanding TKDL

#### Traditional Knowledge Digital Library (TKDL):

**Established**: 2001 (CSIR + Ministry of AYUSH)

**Purpose**: Prevent biopiracy by documenting traditional knowledge as searchable prior art

**Coverage** (as of 2024):
| System | Formulations |
|--------|--------------|
| Ayurveda | 127,533 |
| Unani | 240,850 |
| Siddha | 70,158 |
| Sowa Rigpa | 5,445 |
| Yoga | 4,778 |
| **Total** | **~448,764** |

#### Languages:
- Original: Sanskrit, Hindi, Arabic, Persian, Urdu, Tamil, Bhoti
- Transcribed into: English, French, German, Spanish, Japanese

### Step 3: TKDL Access Framework

#### Access Agreements:
16 international patent offices have access:
- Indian Patent Office (IPO)
- European Patent Office (EPO)
- United States Patent Office (USPTO)
- Japan Patent Office (JPO)
- German Patent Office
- Canadian Patent Office
- UK Patent Office
- Australian Patent Office
- And 8 others

#### How Patent Offices Use TKDL:
1. Search TKDL during examination
2. Cite as prior art if relevant
3. Reject/oppose applications based on TKDL evidence

#### Impact:
**283+ patent applications** refused, amended, or withdrawn based on TKDL evidence (as of 2024)

### Step 4: Conducting TKDL-Aware Prior Art Search

#### If You're an Applicant:

**Before Filing**:
1. Search publicly available traditional medicine literature
2. Review Ayurvedic/Unani/Siddha pharmacopoeias
3. Check academic literature on traditional uses
4. Consider if claimed invention merely documents traditional practice

**Strategy**:
- Identify what is genuinely novel beyond traditional knowledge
- Document inventive contribution clearly
- Prepare to distinguish from traditional practices

#### If You're Opposing/Challenging:

**Sources Beyond TKDL** (for non-patent office access):
- Ayurvedic Pharmacopoeia of India
- Unani Pharmacopoeia of India
- Siddha Pharmacopoeia of India
- Indian Materia Medica
- Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita (classical texts)
- Published academic research on traditional medicine
- People's Biodiversity Registers (PBRs)

### Step 5: Biological Diversity Act Compliance

#### Access and Benefit Sharing (ABS):

**Biological Diversity Act, 2002** requires:

| Applicant Type | Requirement |
|----------------|-------------|
| Foreign entity | Prior approval from National Biodiversity Authority (NBA) |
| Indian company with foreign participation | Prior approval from NBA |
| Indian citizen/entity | Prior intimation to State Biodiversity Board |

#### For Patent Applications:
- Section 6: Prior approval before applying for IP rights on biological resources
- Failure to comply: Patent opposition/revocation ground

#### National Biodiversity Authority (NBA):
- Reviews applications for access to biological resources
- Ensures benefit sharing with local communities
- Can impose conditions on IP rights

### Step 6: Famous Biopiracy Cases

#### Turmeric Patent (1995 - USPTO):
- US Patent 5,401,504 on wound healing use
- CSIR challenged based on traditional knowledge
- **Revoked** by USPTO in 1997

#### Neem Patent (1995 - EPO):
- EP Patent 0436257 on neem-based fungicide
- Challenged by Indian NGOs
- **Revoked** by EPO in 2005

#### Basmati Patent (1997):
- RiceTec's US patent on "Basmati" rice
- India challenged as geographic indication/TK
- Claims narrowed significantly

## Patentability Assessment for TK-Related Inventions

### Decision Tree:

```
Is the invention:
│
├─ Direct traditional practice → NOT PATENTABLE (3(p))
│
├─ Aggregation of known TK properties → NOT PATENTABLE (3(p))
│
├─ Duplication of TK formulation → NOT PATENTABLE (3(p))
│
├─ New form of TK substance → Subject to 3(d) analysis
│   ├─ Enhanced efficacy shown → May be patentable
│   └─ No enhanced efficacy → NOT PATENTABLE
│
├─ Novel combination with synergy → Analyze novelty + inventive step
│   └─ Non-obvious synergistic effect → May be patentable
│
└─ Genuinely new application → Analyze as normal invention
    └─ Novel + Non-obvious + No TK prior art → May be patentable
```

## Opposition/Invalidity Based on Traditional Knowledge

### Section 25(1)(k) & Section 64(1)(q):

**Ground**: Subject matter which "is traditional knowledge or...aggregation or duplication of known properties of traditionally known component"

### Evidence Sources:
1. Classical texts (with translations)
2. Pharmacopoeias
3. TKDL citations (via patent office)
4. Academic literature
5. Expert declarations from Ayurveda/traditional medicine practitioners

## Common Mistakes with TK Inventions

1. **Assuming TK = unpatentable**: Novel improvements may be patentable
2. **Ignoring NBA approval**: Invalidity ground
3. **Not documenting inventive step**: Beyond traditional knowledge
4. **Overlooking regional TK**: Not just classical texts
5. **Missing PBR sources**: Local community knowledge
