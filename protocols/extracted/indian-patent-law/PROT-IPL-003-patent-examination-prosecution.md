# Protocol: Patent Examination and Prosecution

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-003
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Prosecution
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Guide the patent prosecution process from Request for Examination through grant, including responding to examination reports, amendments, hearings, and expedited examination procedures.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Filing Request for Examination
- Responding to First Examination Report (FER)
- Amending claims during prosecution
- Preparing for hearing before Controller
- Seeking expedited examination

---

## Prerequisites

- Filed patent application with application number
- Understanding of prior art landscape
- Claim scope strategy defined
- Timeline awareness for deadlines

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: File Request for Examination (RFE)

#### Timeline (Post-March 15, 2024):
- **Deadline**: 31 months from priority/filing date (whichever earlier)
- **Extension**: Up to 6 months with fee
- **Consequence of missing**: Application deemed withdrawn

#### Form and Fees:
| Entity Type | Form 18 Fee (E-filing) |
|-------------|------------------------|
| Natural Person/Startup | ₹4,000 |
| Small Entity | ₹10,000 |
| Large Entity | ₹20,000 |

### Step 2: Expedited Examination Options

#### Eligible Applicants (Rule 24C):
1. **Startups** (DPIIT recognized)
2. **Small Entities** (MSME Act definition)
3. **Female applicants** (natural persons)
4. **Government/public institutions**
5. **Applicants under PPH** (currently with Japan only)
6. **Green/eco-friendly inventions**

#### Expedited Examination Process:
1. File Form 18A with eligibility documents
2. Pay expedited examination fee
3. FER issued within **105 days** of acceptance
4. Target grant: **12-18 months** vs. 3-5 years normal

### Step 3: Receive and Analyze FER

#### FER Contains:
- Prior art citations
- Objections under Sections 3 (non-patentable subject matter)
- Novelty objections (Section 2(1)(l))
- Inventive step objections (Section 2(1)(ja))
- Sufficiency of disclosure objections (Section 10)
- Unity of invention objections (Section 10(5))
- Formal objections

#### Key Analysis Points:
1. Identify most damaging prior art
2. Assess claim differentiation options
3. Evaluate amendment strategy
4. Consider divisional applications if unity issue

### Step 4: Prepare Response to FER

#### Response Deadline:
- **6 months** from FER date
- **Extension**: 3 months (request can be filed during extension period per 2024 Rules)

#### Response Components:

**1. Arguments (Mandatory):**
- Address each objection point-by-point
- Distinguish prior art
- Support patentability arguments with evidence

**2. Claim Amendments (If Needed):**
- Narrow claims to overcome prior art
- Add features from dependent claims
- Ensure amendments don't add new matter

**3. Specification Amendments (If Needed):**
- Clarify description
- Fix inconsistencies
- Cannot add new matter

**4. Declaration/Evidence (If Applicable):**
- Technical expert declarations
- Comparative data
- Commercial success evidence

### Step 5: Form 3 Compliance

Per 2024 Rules, updated Form 3 required within **3 months of FER** containing:
- Status of all corresponding foreign applications
- Prior art cited in foreign prosecution
- Examination results from other offices

### Step 6: Subsequent Examination Reports

If Controller not satisfied:
1. Second examination report issued
2. Additional arguments/amendments filed
3. Process continues until:
   - Grant
   - Final rejection
   - Abandonment

### Step 7: Hearing Before Controller

#### When Hearing Occurs:
- Controller intends to refuse application
- Complex technical/legal issues
- Applicant requests hearing

#### Hearing Preparation:
1. Prepare written submissions in advance
2. Organize technical evidence
3. Anticipate Controller questions
4. Consider technical expert attendance
5. Prepare claim amendments as backup

#### Hearing Fee (2024 Rules):
₹7,500 - must be paid with notice of intention to attend

### Step 8: Grant of Patent

Upon acceptance:
1. Application published for opposition window
2. If no opposition, patent granted
3. Certificate issued with claims, drawings
4. Patent valid for 20 years from filing date

## Amendment Best Practices

### During Prosecution:
- Amendments must not broaden scope
- Must have support in originally filed specification
- No new matter
- Track all changes clearly

### Amendment Strategy:
1. **Fallback positions**: Use dependent claim features
2. **Characterization**: Add distinguishing features
3. **Functional limitations**: Narrow scope without losing coverage
4. **Method vs. product**: Consider format changes

---

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] RFE filed within deadline
- [ ] All FER objections addressed
- [ ] Claim amendments don't add new matter
- [ ] Prior art distinguished with specificity
- [ ] Form 3 updated with foreign prosecution info
- [ ] Response filed within deadline
- [ ] Hearing preparation complete (if applicable)

---

## Common Prosecution Pitfalls

1. **Missing deadlines**: No substantive extensions available
2. **Insufficient arguments**: Generic responses rejected
3. **Incomplete Form 3**: Opposition/revocation ground
4. **New matter in amendments**: Rejected
5. **Overly aggressive narrowing**: Commercial coverage lost
