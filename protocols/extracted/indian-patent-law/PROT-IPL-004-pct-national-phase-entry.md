# Protocol: PCT National Phase Entry in India

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-004
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: International Filing
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-16

---

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for entering the national phase in India from an international (PCT) application, including requirements, deadlines, documentation, and strategic considerations.

---

## Activation Triggers

- PCT application approaching national phase deadline
- Strategy for India national phase entry
- Document requirements for PCT-NP filing
- Deadline calculations and extensions
- Amendment options at national phase entry

---

## Prerequisites

- International application filed under PCT
- International publication (or filing receipt)
- Priority document (if claiming priority)
- Assignment documents (if applicant different from inventor)
- Understanding of Indian patentability requirements

---

## Core Process

### Step 1: Calculate Entry Deadline

#### Standard Deadline:
- **31 months** from priority date (or international filing date if no priority)

#### Extension Available:
- Up to **6 months additional** with extension fee
- Total possible: **37 months** from priority date

#### Fee for Extension:
| Entity Type | Extension Fee (per month) |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Natural Person/Startup | ₹10,000 |
| Small Entity | ₹25,000 |
| Large Entity | ₹50,000 |

### Step 2: Prepare Required Documents

#### Mandatory Documents:

1. **Form 1** - Application for grant of patent
   - Mark as "National Phase Application"
   - Include international application number
   - Include international filing date
   - Include international publication number

2. **Complete Specification** (Form 2)
   - Copy of PCT specification in English
   - Or verified English translation if filed in other language

3. **Form 3** - Statement under Section 8
   - Details of all foreign applications
   - Must be filed within 6 months of national phase entry

4. **Form 5** - Declaration of inventorship
   - If not already on record from PCT

5. **Drawings** (if any)
   - As published with PCT application

#### Additional Documents (If Applicable):

6. **Power of Attorney (Form 26)**
   - If filing through Indian patent agent
   - Scanned copy acceptable initially
   - Original within 15 days if requested

7. **Assignment Deed**
   - If applicant is not the inventor
   - Notarized copy required
   - Must be filed within 6 months

8. **Priority Document**
   - Not required if PCT published in English
   - Required with verified translation if PCT in other language

9. **Form 28** - Entity Status Declaration
   - If claiming startup/small entity benefits

### Step 3: Language Requirements

| PCT Language | India Requirement |
|--------------|-------------------|
| English | File as-is |
| Other | Verified English translation required |

**Translation Deadline**:
- At filing, or within 3 months of IPO invitation
- Extension up to 6 months with fee available

### Step 4: Calculate and Pay Fees

#### National Phase Entry Fees (E-filing):
| Fee Type | Natural Person/Startup | Small Entity | Large Entity |
|----------|----------------------|--------------|--------------|
| Filing Fee | ₹1,600 | ₹4,000 | ₹8,000 |
| Per additional claim (>10) | ₹160 | ₹400 | ₹800 |
| Per page specification (>30) | ₹160 | ₹400 | ₹800 |
| Per additional sheet drawing (>10) | ₹160 | ₹400 | ₹800 |

### Step 5: File National Phase Application

1. Access IPO e-filing portal
2. Select "National Phase Application" option
3. Enter international application details
4. Upload all required documents
5. Pay applicable fees
6. Obtain Indian application number

### Step 6: Amendments at National Phase Entry

#### What CAN be Done:
- Delete claims (cannot amend)
- Reflect amendments already published on WIPO
- Correct obvious errors
- Submit translations

#### What CANNOT be Done:
- Amend claim text (no broadening or narrowing)
- Add new claims
- Change applicant/inventor details not on WIPO record

**Note**: Amendments possible AFTER entry during prosecution

### Step 7: Post-Entry Requirements

1. **Request for Examination (Form 18)**
   - Deadline: 31 months from priority date
   - For applications entered before March 15, 2024: 48 months

2. **Form 3 Updates**
   - Within 6 months of entry
   - Updated Form 3 within 3 months of FER

3. **Expedited Examination**
   - Available via Form 18A if eligible

---

## Strategic Considerations

### Timing Entry:
- **Early entry**: Faster prosecution, earlier grant
- **Late entry**: More time to assess commercial value
- **Extension**: Buy additional time if needed

### Claim Strategy:
- Review PCT search report and written opinion
- Consider amendments needed for India-specific issues
- Plan divisional applications if unity objection likely

### Entity Status:
- Claim startup/small entity status at entry
- Significant fee savings throughout patent life

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Entry deadline calculated correctly
- [ ] International application details accurate
- [ ] Complete specification in English (or translation)
- [ ] Form 1 marked as national phase
- [ ] All inventors listed
- [ ] Applicant details match assignment chain
- [ ] Priority claim details verified
- [ ] Entity status declared if applicable
- [ ] Fees calculated and paid
- [ ] Form 3 planned for filing

---

## Common Entry Mistakes

1. **Wrong deadline calculation**: Double-check priority date
2. **Missing verified translation**: Delays prosecution
3. **Incomplete assignment chain**: Ownership challenges
4. **Wrong entity status**: Fee benefits invalidated
5. **Attempting claim amendments**: Not permitted at entry
