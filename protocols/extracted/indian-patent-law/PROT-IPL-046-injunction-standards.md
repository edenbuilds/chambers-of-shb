## Core Process

### Step 1: Types of Injunctions

#### Injunction Categories:

| Type | Stage | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Ex parte interim** | Before notice | Urgent relief |
| **Interim (ad interim)** | After notice, before trial | Preserve status quo |
| **Permanent** | After trial | Final relief |
| **Quia timet** | Before infringement | Prevent threatened harm |

### Step 2: Three-Pronged Test

#### Traditional Indian Standard:

| Prong | Test |
|-------|------|
| **Prima facie case** | Plaintiff likely to succeed |
| **Irreparable harm** | Damages inadequate remedy |
| **Balance of convenience** | Favors injunction |

### Step 3: Prima Facie Case Analysis

#### Establishing Prima Facie Case:

**Patentee Must Show**:
1. Valid patent (not obviously invalid)
2. Ownership/title
3. Infringement likely
4. Jurisdiction proper

**Claim Chart Requirement**:
```
CLAIM ELEMENT MAPPING

Patent Claim 1:
├── Element A: [Description]
│   └── Accused Product: [Corresponding feature]
├── Element B: [Description]
│   └── Accused Product: [Corresponding feature]
└── Element C: [Description]
    └── Accused Product: [Corresponding feature]

Analysis: All elements present → Prima facie infringement
```

#### Defendant's Challenges:

| Challenge | Effect on Prima Facie |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Invalidity evidence | May negate |
| Non-infringement | May negate |
| License/authorization | Complete defense |
| Bolar/research | Exemption applies |

### Step 4: Irreparable Harm

#### What Constitutes Irreparable Harm:

| Factor | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| Loss of market share | Hard to quantify |
| Reputation damage | Non-monetary |
| Price erosion | Market disruption |
| Customer confusion | Ongoing harm |
| Design-around difficulty | After market established |

#### What's NOT Irreparable:

| Factor | Why Not |
|--------|---------|
| Quantifiable losses | Damages adequate |
| Established infringer | Can pay damages |
| Historical infringement | Already occurred |

### Step 5: Balance of Convenience

#### Factors Considered:

| Factor | Plaintiff | Defendant |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Investment | R&D, patent prosecution | Manufacturing setup |
| Employees | - | Job losses if shut down |
| Public interest | Innovation incentive | Access to goods |
| Status quo | Enforcement of rights | Continuation of business |
| Financial capacity | To wait for trial | To pay damages |

#### Special Considerations:

**Pharmaceutical Cases**:
- Public interest in drug access
- Life-saving drugs: Higher bar for injunction
- Price differential: Social concerns

**Technology Cases**:
- Fast-moving markets
- Design-around possibility
- Standard essentiality (SEPs: FRAND concerns)

### Step 6: Indian Jurisprudence

#### Key Cases:

| Case | Holding |
|------|---------|
| **F. Hoffmann-La Roche v. Cipla (2012)** | Balanced public interest; no injunction for cancer drug |
| **Bayer v. Cipla (Nexavar)** | Public interest weighed against injunction |
| **Merck v. Glenmark** | Prima facie case sufficient for interim relief |
| **Ericsson v. Micromax (SEPs)** | FRAND undertaking relevant |

#### Delhi High Court Approach:

Generally follows:
1. Strong prima facie case required
2. Balance of convenience carefully weighed
3. Public interest considered
4. Undertakings may substitute for injunction

### Step 7: SEP-Specific Considerations

#### Standard Essential Patents:

**Different Standard Applies**:
- FRAND commitment affects injunction
- "Willing licensee" analysis
- Proportionality emphasized

**Huawei-ZTE Framework** (Influencing India):
1. SEP holder must offer FRAND terms
2. Implementer must respond in good faith
3. Injunction only against unwilling licensee

### Step 8: Procedural Requirements

#### Injunction Application:

**Contents**:
```
APPLICATION FOR INTERIM INJUNCTION

1. FACTS
   [Brief statement of dispute]

2. PRIMA FACIE CASE
   - Patent validity: [Grant details, presumption]
   - Ownership: [Plaintiff's title]
   - Infringement: [Claim chart attached]

3. IRREPARABLE HARM
   [Specific harm that cannot be compensated]

4. BALANCE OF CONVENIENCE
   [Why favors injunction]

5. URGENCY
   [Why ex parte/immediate relief needed]

6. UNDERTAKING
   Plaintiff undertakes to abide by any order
   as to damages if injunction wrongly granted.

PRAYER:
(a) Interim injunction restraining Defendant from...
(b) [Specific relief]
```

#### Defendant's Response:

```
REPLY TO INJUNCTION APPLICATION

1. NO PRIMA FACIE CASE
   - Patent invalidity: [Prior art, Section 3]
   - No infringement: [Claim differences]
   - Authorization: [License, exhaustion]

2. NO IRREPARABLE HARM
   [Plaintiff can be compensated in damages]

3. BALANCE OF CONVENIENCE
   - Defendant's investment: [Amount]
   - Employees affected: [Number]
   - Public interest: [Drug access, etc.]

4. DELAY/ACQUIESCENCE
   [Plaintiff delayed seeking relief]

5. UNDERTAKING
   Defendant offers to maintain accounts
```

## Injunction Decision Framework

```
Injunction Application
        │
        ├── Prima Facie Case?
        │   ├── No → Deny injunction
        │   └── Yes → Continue
        │
        ├── Irreparable Harm?
        │   ├── No (damages adequate) → Deny
        │   └── Yes → Continue
        │
        └── Balance of Convenience?
            ├── Favors Defendant → Deny (or conditional)
            ├── Favors Plaintiff → Grant
            └── Uncertain → Consider undertakings/conditions
```

## Conditions and Undertakings

### Common Conditions:

| Condition | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Plaintiff undertaking** | Compensate if wrongly granted |
| **Account maintenance** | Track sales during pendency |
| **Security deposit** | Ensure defendant's protection |
| **Time limit** | Expedited hearing requirement |

### Alternative to Injunction:

| Alternative | When Used |
|-------------|-----------|
| Royalty deposit | FRAND/licensing cases |
| Account of profits | Maintain records |
| Expedited trial | Balance concerns |
| Anton Piller | Evidence preservation |

---

## Common Injunction Mistakes

1. **Weak claim chart**: Element-by-element required
2. **No irreparable harm**: Must be specific
3. **Ignoring defendant's equities**: Balance matters
4. **Delay in filing**: Acquiescence argument
5. **SEP without FRAND offer**: May deny injunction
6. **Public interest ignored**: Especially pharma
