## Core Process

### Step 1: Understand Valuation Context

#### Purpose-Specific Valuation:

| Context | Primary Methodology | Key Considerations |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Licensing** | Income approach | Royalty rates, market size |
| **M&A** | Market + Income | Comparable transactions |
| **Financing** | Cost or Market | Liquidation value, marketability |
| **Litigation** | Income approach | Lost profits, reasonable royalty |
| **Tax/Transfer Pricing** | Arm's length price | OECD guidelines, comparables |
| **Financial Reporting** | Fair value (Ind AS) | Impairment testing |

### Step 2: Three Primary Valuation Approaches

#### A. Income Approach

**Principle**: Value based on future economic benefits

**Methods**:
1. **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**
   - Project patent-related cash flows
   - Discount to present value
   - Consider remaining patent life

2. **Relief from Royalty**
   - Estimate royalty savings from ownership
   - Apply market royalty rate
   - Discount to present value

3. **Excess Earnings**
   - Calculate total business earnings
   - Subtract returns on other assets
   - Attribute residual to patent

**Formula (Relief from Royalty)**:
```
Patent Value = Σ [(Revenue × Royalty Rate × (1-Tax)) / (1+r)^t]

Where:
- Revenue = Projected revenue from patented product
- Royalty Rate = Market comparable rate
- Tax = Applicable tax rate
- r = Discount rate
- t = Time period (remaining patent life)
```

#### B. Market Approach

**Principle**: Value based on comparable transactions

**Methods**:
1. **Comparable Transactions**
   - Identify similar patent sales/licenses
   - Adjust for differences
   - Apply multiples

2. **Industry Standards**
   - Standard royalty rates by industry
   - "25% rule" (of gross profit) - now disfavored
   - Industry-specific benchmarks

**Data Sources for India**:
- RoyaltyStat database
- ktMINE
- Public licensing disclosures
- Court damage awards (Ericsson v. Lava)

#### C. Cost Approach

**Principle**: Value based on cost to recreate

**Methods**:
1. **Reproduction Cost**
   - Actual cost to develop patent
   - R&D expenses, prosecution costs

2. **Replacement Cost**
   - Cost to develop equivalent today
   - Adjusted for obsolescence

**Limitations**:
- Does not reflect market value
- Ignores economic benefits
- Best as floor value

### Step 3: Key Value Factors

#### Legal Factors:

| Factor | Impact on Value |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Claim scope** | Broader = Higher value |
| **Remaining term** | Longer = Higher value |
| **Geographic coverage** | More countries = Higher |
| **Validity strength** | Stronger = Higher value |
| **Enforcement history** | Successful = Higher |
| **Prosecution history** | Clean = Higher value |

#### Technical Factors:

| Factor | Impact on Value |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Technology lifecycle** | Mature vs. emerging |
| **Design-around ease** | Harder = Higher value |
| **Standard essentiality** | SEP = Higher value |
| **Technical contribution** | Greater = Higher |
| **Improvement patents** | May reduce value |

#### Commercial Factors:

| Factor | Impact on Value |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Market size** | Larger = Higher value |
| **Market growth** | Higher = Higher value |
| **Competition** | Less = Higher value |
| **Implementation cost** | Lower = Higher value |
| **Revenue potential** | Higher = Higher value |

### Step 4: Indian-Specific Considerations

#### Section 3 Risk Factors:
- **Section 3(d)**: Pharmaceutical new form patents - validity risk
- **Section 3(k)**: Software/CRI patents - enforcement risk
- **Section 3(p)**: Traditional knowledge - biopiracy risk

#### Compulsory Licensing Risk:
- Section 84 availability after 3 years
- Natco v. Bayer precedent (royalty: 6% of net sales)
- Public interest considerations

#### Transfer Pricing (India):
- OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines
- Section 92 of Income Tax Act
- Arm's length price requirement
- Safe harbor provisions

#### Foreign Exchange:
- FEMA compliance for cross-border payments
- RBI approval requirements
- Withholding tax (TDS) considerations

### Step 5: Valuation for Litigation Damages

#### Damage Types Under Patents Act:

| Type | Calculation |
|------|-------------|
| **Lost Profits** | Patentee's lost sales × margin |
| **Reasonable Royalty** | Hypothetical negotiation rate |
| **Account of Profits** | Infringer's profits from infringement |

#### Indian Damage Awards (Recent):
- Ericsson v. Lava (2024): ₹244 Crore
- CCA v. Dinesh Gupta (2023): ₹216 Crore
- Bayer v. Cadila (2021): ₹25 Lakh

#### Georgia-Pacific Factors (adapted for India):
1. Established royalty rates
2. Rates paid by licensee for similar patents
3. Nature and scope of license
4. Licensor's policy on licensing
5. Commercial relationship
6. Effect on derivative sales
7. Duration of patent
8. Profitability of products
9. Utility/advantages over alternatives
10. Nature of patented invention
11. Extent of use by infringer
12. Customary profit/selling price
13. Portion of profit attributable to patent
14. Expert testimony

### Step 6: Valuation Report Requirements

#### Standard Components:

1. **Executive Summary**
   - Valuation conclusion
   - Key assumptions
   - Methodology used

2. **Patent Description**
   - Technical description
   - Legal status
   - Remaining term

3. **Market Analysis**
   - Industry overview
   - Competitive landscape
   - Market projections

4. **Methodology Selection**
   - Rationale for approach
   - Data sources
   - Assumptions

5. **Calculations**
   - Detailed workings
   - Sensitivity analysis
   - Range of values

6. **Conclusion**
   - Point estimate or range
   - Confidence level
   - Qualifications

## Common Valuation Mistakes

1. **Single methodology**: Use multiple for triangulation
2. **Ignoring Section 3 risk**: Validity affects value
3. **Overestimating market share**: Be realistic
4. **Wrong discount rate**: Consider patent-specific risk
5. **Ignoring compulsory licensing**: Affects pharma value
6. **Static analysis**: Consider market dynamics
