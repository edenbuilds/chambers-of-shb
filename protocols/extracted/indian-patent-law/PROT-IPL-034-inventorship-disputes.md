# Protocol: Inventorship Disputes

**Protocol ID**: PROT-IPL-034
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Domain**: Indian Patent Law
**Category**: Disputes

## Purpose

Comprehensive guide for handling inventorship disputes in India, including determining true inventor, correcting inventorship, joint inventorship issues, and employer-employee inventorship claims.

---

## Activation Triggers

- Inventor claims omission from patent
- Request to add/remove inventor
- Employment invention dispute
- Joint invention ownership conflict
- Derivation allegations

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of inventorship concepts
- Knowledge of Sections 6, 28 framework
- Employment agreement review
- Evidence of contribution assessment

## Core Process

### Step 1: Understanding Inventorship

#### Legal Framework:

**Section 6 - Persons Entitled to Apply**:
"[A]n application for a patent... may be made by any of the following persons... the true and first inventor of the invention"

**Section 28 - Mention of Inventor**:
"If the Controller is satisfied... that the person in respect of whom the request was made is the inventor, the Controller shall cause him to be mentioned as inventor"

#### Key Concepts:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Inventor** | Person who conceived the invention |
| **True inventor** | Actual conceiver (vs. false claimant) |
| **First inventor** | First to conceive (not necessarily first to file) |
| **Joint inventor** | Co-contributor to conception |

### Step 2: Determining Inventorship

#### Inventorship vs. Ownership:

| Aspect | Inventorship | Ownership |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Nature | Creative contribution | Property right |
| Who | Always natural person | Person or entity |
| Transfer | Cannot be transferred | Can be assigned |
| Criteria | Conception | Derivation/assignment |

#### Contribution Test:

To be an inventor, must contribute to **conception**:

**Conception** = Formation in the mind of the definite and permanent idea of the complete invention

**Must Contribute To**:
- Novel features claimed
- Solution to technical problem
- Inventive elements

**Not Sufficient**:
- Following instructions
- Providing routine work
- Testing/verification only
- Providing materials/funding
- Administrative support

### Step 3: Joint Inventorship

#### Requirements for Joint Inventors:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Each contributed | To conception of claims |
| Same invention | Working toward same goal |
| Collaboration | Joint effort (physical presence not required) |
| Identifiable contribution | Each made concrete contribution |

#### Joint Inventorship NOT Required:

- Work on same project
- Same laboratory/team
- Supervised the work
- Contributed to different inventions
- Contributed only to unclaimed aspects

#### Apportionment:

Joint inventors may:
- Have different shares (by agreement)
- Own undivided interest (default)
- Each work the patent independently

### Step 4: Correction of Inventorship

#### Adding Inventor:

**Section 28 Request**:
- Before grant: With Controller
- After grant: Application to Controller

**Procedure**:
```
Request for mention of inventor (Form 8)
        ↓
Evidence of contribution
        ↓
Controller examination
        ↓
Grant of request → Patent amended
```

#### Removing Inventor:

**When Applicable**:
- Person incorrectly named
- No actual contribution to conception
- Fraud or mistake

**Procedure**:
- Application to Controller
- Evidence of non-contribution
- Hearing (if disputed)
- Order for removal

#### Form 8 Contents:

```
FORM 8
REQUEST FOR MENTION OF INVENTOR

Application/Patent No.: [...]
Title: [...]

1. INVENTOR DETAILS
   Name: [...]
   Address: [...]
   Nationality: [...]

2. CURRENT INVENTORS
   [List of named inventors]

3. BASIS FOR REQUEST
   □ Omitted inventor seeking addition
   □ Incorrectly named inventor to be removed
   □ Correction of inventor details

4. STATEMENT OF CONTRIBUTION
   [Detailed description of inventive contribution]

5. SUPPORTING EVIDENCE
   [List of exhibits]

6. DECLARATION
   The person named above made the following contribution
   to the conception of the claimed invention: [...]

Signature: [...]
Date: [...]
```

### Step 5: Employment Inventorship

#### General Rule (Section 6(1)):

Employee inventors generally own their inventions unless:
- Employment agreement assigns to employer
- Invention made in course of employment
- Specific invention assignment

#### Employment Agreement Analysis:

| Clause Type | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| **Pre-assignment** | Future inventions automatically assigned |
| **Notification** | Must notify employer of inventions |
| **First right of refusal** | Employer can choose to acquire |
| **Shop rights** | Employer gets non-exclusive license |
| **Silent** | Default rules apply |

#### Employer vs. Employee Claims:

**Factors Favoring Employer**:
- Invention within scope of employment
- Used employer's resources
- Made during working hours
- Assignment agreement exists

**Factors Favoring Employee**:
- Outside scope of employment
- Personal time/resources
- No employment agreement on IP
- Unrelated to job duties

### Step 6: Derivation Issues

#### Derivation vs. Independent Invention:

**Derivation**: Copying another's conception

**Proof Required**:
1. Prior conception by true inventor
2. Communication to deriver
3. Derivation from communication

#### Remedies:
- Request correction of inventorship
- Seek revocation (misrepresentation ground)
- Civil suit for damages

### Step 7: Dispute Resolution Mechanisms

#### Before Controller:

- Form 8 request
- Opposition based on Section 25(1)(a) - not true inventor
- Request for hearing

#### Civil Court:

- Ownership/title disputes
- Contract interpretation
- Damages for misappropriation

#### Arbitration:

- If arbitration clause in employment/collaboration agreement
- Faster resolution
- Confidential proceedings

## Evidence for Inventorship

### Documentary Evidence:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Lab notebooks | Date of conception |
| Emails/correspondence | Collaboration evidence |
| Meeting minutes | Discussion of invention |
| Draft specifications | Contribution to claims |
| Drawings/designs | Technical contribution |

### Witness Evidence:

- Co-workers who observed conception
- Supervisors familiar with work
- Collaborators who can confirm contributions

### Expert Evidence:

- Technical expert on nature of contribution
- Whether contribution rose to level of conception

## Common Inventorship Mistakes

1. **Confusing ownership and inventorship**: Different concepts
2. **Including supervisors automatically**: Must have conception contribution
3. **Omitting joint inventors**: Each contributor must be named
4. **Including technicians**: Routine work not conception
5. **No documentation**: Hard to prove later
6. **Ignoring employment agreements**: May assign rights
