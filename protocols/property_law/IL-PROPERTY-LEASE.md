# Indian Property Law: Lease of Immovable Property

## Purpose
This protocol provides comprehensive guidance on lease of immovable property under the Transfer of Property Act 1882 (Sections 105-117) and state-specific Rent Control Acts. It covers essential elements of lease, rights and duties of lessor and lessee, types of leases (fixed term, periodic, perpetual), determination/termination of lease, forfeiture, ejectment procedures, subletting, assignment, fixtures, and rent control legislation implications.

## Activation Triggers
Use this protocol when:
- Client wants to lease property (as lessor or lessee)
- Drafting lease agreement/rent agreement
- Dispute over lease terms, rent payment, maintenance
- Termination/eviction issues
- Subletting/assignment disputes
- Registration requirements for lease deed
- Forfeiture for breach of covenant
- Fixtures and improvements ownership issues
- Rent control legislation applicability
- Distinction between lease and license
- Leave and license vs lease determination
- Ejectment suit by landlord
- Redemption/continuation of lease

## Core Principles

### 1. Section 105: Lease Defined

**"Lease of immovable property is a transfer of a right to enjoy such property, made for a certain time, express or implied, or in perpetuity, in consideration of a price paid or promised, or of money, a share of crops, service, or any other thing of value, to be rendered periodically or on specified occasions to the transferor by the transferee, who accepts the transfer on such terms."**

Essential Elements of Lease:
1. **TRANSFER** - Of right to enjoy (not ownership)
2. **IMMOVABLE PROPERTY** - Land, buildings, rights in land
3. **CERTAIN TIME** - Fixed period OR perpetual OR periodic
4. **CONSIDERATION** - Rent/premium/share of crops/service
5. **PERIODIC OR SPECIFIED** - Payment at intervals OR specified occasions
6. **TWO PARTIES** - Lessor (landlord/owner) and Lessee (tenant)
7. **ACCEPTANCE** - Lessee accepts transfer

**Key Distinctions:**

| Aspect | Lease | License | Sale |
|--------|-------|---------|------|
| **Transfer** | Right to enjoy | Personal permission | Ownership |
| **Interest Created** | Proprietary (in rem) | Personal (in personam) | Absolute ownership |
| **Transferability** | Assignable (unless restricted) | Not assignable | Permanent |
| **Duration** | Fixed/Perpetual | Revocable | Permanent |
| **Possession** | Transferred | Not transferred (mere use) | Transferred |
| **Registration** | Required (>12 months) | Not required | Required (Rs. 100+) |

### 2. Section 106: Duration of Lease

**A. Fixed-Term Lease:**
- Specific period stated (1 year, 5 years, 10 years, 99 years)
- Terminates automatically on expiry (unless renewed)
- No notice required for termination

**B. Periodic Lease:**
- Month-to-month, year-to-year
- Continues automatically until notice given
- Notice period: Equal to period of tenancy (1 month for monthly, 6 months for yearly)

**C. Perpetual Lease:**
- In perpetuity (no fixed end date)
- Rare in modern practice
- Terminable only by forfeiture or agreement

**Section 106 Notice Requirements (Periodic Tenancy):**

For Month-to-Month Tenancy:
- Notice: **15 days** minimum
- Expiring with end of month of tenancy

For Year-to-Year Tenancy:
- Notice: **6 months** minimum
- Expiring with end of year of tenancy

Example:
- Monthly rent payable on 1st of each month
- Lessor wants to terminate
- Notice: 15 days before end of month
- If notice given on 10th January, earliest termination: 28th/29th February

### 3. Registration Requirements (Section 107)

**Section 107 TPA + Section 17 Registration Act:**

**Registration MANDATORY if:**
1. Lease period > 12 months, OR
2. Lease reserves rent AND period > 12 months

**Registration NOT Required if:**
1. Lease period ≤ 12 months, AND
2. No future rent reserved (lump sum payment)

**Examples:**

| Lease Terms | Registration Required? |
|-------------|------------------------|
| 1 year lease, monthly rent Rs. 10,000 | NO (≤12 months) |
| 2 year lease, monthly rent Rs. 10,000 | YES (>12 months) |
| 11 month lease, monthly rent Rs. 10,000 | NO (≤12 months) |
| 5 year lease, one-time payment Rs. 5 lakhs | YES (>12 months) |
| 6 month lease, renewable | NO (initial period ≤12 months) |

**11-Month Lease Practice:**
- Common to avoid registration
- Lease for 11 months + option to renew
- Each renewal is fresh 11-month lease
- Legal validity upheld by courts

**Effect of Non-Registration (If Required):**
- Lease deed inadmissible as evidence (Section 49 Registration Act)
- But oral lease/possession may create periodic tenancy
- Lessee's possession not illegal (becomes tenant at will/sufferance)

### 4. Lease vs License (Easement Act 1882 Section 52)

**Critical Distinction:**

**License (Section 52 Easement Act):**
"Where one person grants to another, or to a definite number of other persons, a right to do, or continue to do, in or upon the immovable property of the grantor, something which would, in the absence of such right, be unlawful, and such right does not amount to an easement or an interest in the property, the right is called a license."

**Test (Authoritative):**

Associated Hotels of India Ltd. v. R.N. Kapoor (SC 1959):
"The test is: Does the document create an interest in the property? If YES → LEASE; If NO → LICENSE"

**Factors Indicating LEASE:**
1. Exclusive possession to transferee
2. Fixed term/duration specified
3. Rent reserved and payable periodically
4. Transferable interest
5. Restriction on lessor's entry
6. Renewal clause
7. Lessee can exclude others (including lessor)

**Factors Indicating LICENSE:**
1. Personal permission (no exclusive possession)
2. Revocable at will
3. No interest in property created
4. Licensor retains control
5. Not transferable/assignable
6. Licensor can enter anytime
7. No fixed term (or term with easy revocation)

**"Leave and License" Agreements:**
- Common in Maharashtra (Bombay Rent Act)
- Intended as license to avoid rent control
- Court examines substance over form
- If creates exclusive possession + fixed term + rent → LEASE (despite "license" label)

**Practical Tip:**
Many agreements labeled "Leave and License" are actually LEASES - courts look beyond nomenclature to actual rights transferred.

## Framework

### Phase 1: Creating Lease

**Step 1: Pre-Lease Verification (Lessee's Due Diligence)**

**A. Lessor's Title Verification:**
- Examine lessor's sale deed, previous chain
- Verify lessor is OWNER or authorized to lease
- Check for co-owners (all must consent if ownership joint)
- Verify no mortgage/charge prohibiting lease

**B. Property Status Check:**
- Physical inspection (boundaries, condition, encroachment)
- Municipal tax status (arrears, if any)
- Encumbrance certificate (no litigation/charges)
- Building approval (if construction)
- Occupancy certificate

**C. Rent Control Applicability:**
- Check if property falls under Rent Control Act (state-specific)
- If covered: Restrictions on rent increase, eviction grounds limited
- If exempt: Freedom to contract

**Step 2: Negotiate Lease Terms**

**Key Commercial Terms:**

1. **RENT**
   - Monthly/quarterly/annual amount
   - Payment mode (cash, cheque, online transfer)
   - Due date (1st of month, advance/arrears)
   - Late payment penalty/interest

2. **SECURITY DEPOSIT**
   - Amount (usually 2-10 months' rent)
   - Refundable on termination
   - Interest on deposit (if agreed)
   - Adjustment against last months' rent (if permitted)

3. **DURATION**
   - Fixed term (11 months, 1 year, 3 years, 5 years, etc.)
   - Start date and end date
   - Lock-in period (cannot terminate before specified period)

4. **RENEWAL**
   - Option to renew (how many times)
   - Rent escalation on renewal (% increase)
   - Notice period for renewal (30-60 days before expiry)

5. **MAINTENANCE**
   - Who bears maintenance cost (lessor/lessee)
   - Major repairs vs minor repairs (responsibility split)
   - Property tax, water/electricity charges (who pays)

6. **USE**
   - Permitted use (residential, commercial, industrial)
   - Restrictions (no alterations, no subletting, no illegal activities)

7. **TERMINATION**
   - Notice period for termination (1-3 months)
   - Early termination penalty (if lock-in breached)
   - Grounds for termination (breach, non-payment)

**Step 3: Draft Lease Deed**

**Mandatory Contents:**

**1. PARTIES**
   - Lessor (Landlord): Full name, address, age
   - Lessee (Tenant): Full name, address, age

**2. RECITALS**
   - Lessor's ownership/title
   - Property description

**3. PROPERTY DESCRIPTION (Schedule)**
   - Address, survey number, boundaries, area
   - Specific portion (if part of larger property)

**4. TERM/DURATION**
   - Start date: ___
   - End date: ___
   - Lock-in period: ___

**5. RENT**
   - Monthly rent: Rs. ___
   - Payable on: ___ day of each month
   - Mode: ___
   - Escalation: ___% per year (if applicable)

**6. SECURITY DEPOSIT**
   - Amount: Rs. ___
   - Refundable: Yes/No
   - Interest: ___% per annum (if applicable)
   - Conditions for forfeiture: ___

**7. PERMITTED USE**
   - Residential/Commercial/Industrial
   - Restrictions: No subletting, no alterations without permission

**8. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS**
   - Minor repairs: Lessee
   - Major repairs: Lessor
   - Property tax: Lessor
   - Water/electricity: Lessee (actual consumption)

**9. LESSEE'S COVENANTS**
   - Pay rent punctually
   - Use property for permitted purpose only
   - Maintain property in good condition
   - Not make alterations without consent
   - Not sublet without consent
   - Allow lessor to inspect (with reasonable notice)
   - Return property in good condition on termination

**10. LESSOR'S COVENANTS**
   - Quiet enjoyment (no disturbance)
   - Deliver possession
   - Maintain structural soundness
   - Pay property tax

**11. TERMINATION**
   - Notice period: ___ months
   - Grounds for termination: Non-payment, breach, illegal use
   - Forfeiture clause (if breach)

**12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION**
   - Arbitration clause / Jurisdiction

**13. ATTESTATION**
   - Two witnesses

**Sample Clauses:**

```markdown
## Rent Clause
The Lessee shall pay to the Lessor monthly rent of Rs. 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only) on or before the 5th day of each English calendar month, commencing from 1st January 2025. The rent shall be paid by electronic transfer to the Lessor's bank account (details provided separately).

## Escalation Clause
The rent shall be increased by 5% annually on each anniversary of the commencement date. For the second year (starting 1st January 2026), the rent shall be Rs. 52,500/-, and so on.

## Security Deposit Clause
The Lessee has paid to the Lessor a security deposit of Rs. 5,00,000/- (Rupees Five Lakhs only) to secure the performance of the Lessee's obligations. The deposit shall be refunded within 30 days of termination, subject to deductions for any damages, arrears, or unpaid charges. No interest shall accrue on the security deposit.

## Maintenance Clause
The Lessee shall be responsible for all minor repairs and day-to-day maintenance, including plumbing, electrical fittings, painting, and upkeep. The Lessor shall be responsible for major structural repairs, including roof, walls, foundation, and external drainage. Property tax, building insurance, and society charges (if any) shall be borne by the Lessor. Electricity, water, and gas charges shall be borne by the Lessee based on actual consumption.

## Subletting Clause
The Lessee shall not sublet, assign, or part with possession of the leased property or any part thereof without the prior written consent of the Lessor. Any subletting without consent shall be deemed a material breach, entitling the Lessor to terminate the lease forthwith.

## Termination Clause
Either party may terminate this lease by giving 2 (two) months' prior written notice to the other party. However, during the initial 11 months (lock-in period), neither party shall terminate except in case of material breach by the other party.

## Forfeiture Clause
If the Lessee fails to pay rent for 2 (two) consecutive months, or commits any material breach of the covenants herein, the Lessor shall have the right to forfeit the lease and re-enter the property after giving 15 days' written notice to remedy the breach. If the breach is not remedied within 15 days, the lease shall stand terminated, and the Lessee shall vacate forthwith.
```

**Step 4: Stamp Duty and Registration**

**Stamp Duty on Lease:**
- Varies by state
- Based on: Rent + Security Deposit + Lease Period
- Formula (common): (Average Annual Rent × Lease Period) × Stamp Duty Rate

**Example Calculation (Maharashtra):**
- Monthly rent: Rs. 50,000
- Annual rent: Rs. 6,00,000
- Lease period: 5 years
- Stamp duty rate: 0.25% for lease
- Stamp duty: (Rs. 6,00,000 × 5) × 0.25% = Rs. 7,500

**Registration Procedure (If Required):**

1. **Draft Lease Deed** - On stamp paper OR e-stamping
2. **Pay Stamp Duty** - Calculate and pay as per state rules
3. **Execute** - Both parties sign before two witnesses
4. **Appear at Sub-Registrar** - Within 4 months of execution
5. **Document Verification** - Sub-Registrar verifies
6. **Pay Registration Fee** - Usually 1% of stamp duty value (varies by state)
7. **Registration Complete** - Deed entered in register, serial number assigned
8. **Collect Certified Copies** - Registered lease deed (1-7 days)

**11-Month Leases:**
- Stamp duty payable (even if not registered)
- Stamp duty on notarized leave and license: Lower rates (state-specific)
- Maharashtra: 0.25% of total rent OR Rs. 1,000 (whichever lower) + notary charges

**Step 5: Delivery of Possession**

Methods:
1. **Physical Handover** - Keys delivered, lessee enters property
2. **Symbolic Delivery** - Handing over keys at lessor's office
3. **Constructive Delivery** - Attornment by prior occupier (if any)

**Possession Receipt:**
Lessee should give written acknowledgment of:
- Possession received on [date]
- Condition of property (good/fair)
- Inventory of fixtures (if furnished)
- Meter readings (electricity, water)

### Phase 2: During Lease Term

**Lessee's Ongoing Obligations:**

1. **Pay Rent Timely**
   - On due date specified
   - Late payment → Interest + penalty (as per agreement)
   - Persistent default → Forfeiture

2. **Use Property Properly**
   - For permitted purpose only
   - No illegal activities (drug trafficking, manufacturing without license)
   - No nuisance to neighbors

3. **Maintain Property**
   - Keep in good condition (ordinary wear and tear excepted)
   - Minor repairs (plumbing, electrical)
   - Not commit waste (damage/destruction)

4. **Not Alter Property**
   - No structural changes without lessor's consent
   - Fixtures installed by lessee removable (unless agreed otherwise)

5. **Allow Inspection**
   - Lessor can inspect with reasonable notice (usually 24-48 hours)
   - Not unreasonably refuse entry

6. **Not Sublet**
   - Without lessor's consent (if covenant prohibits)
   - Subletting without consent → Forfeiture

**Lessor's Ongoing Obligations:**

1. **Quiet Enjoyment** (Section 108(a))
   - Not disturb lessee's possession
   - Not interfere with lessee's use

2. **Maintain Structural Soundness**
   - Major repairs (roof, walls, foundation)
   - Keep property habitable/fit for purpose

3. **Pay Property Tax** (Unless Otherwise Agreed)
   - Lessor responsible for statutory dues
   - Lessee not liable for lessor's tax defaults

4. **Disclose Defects** (Section 108(b))
   - Inform lessee of material defects known to lessor

**Common Issues During Lease:**

**Issue 1: Rent Increase Dispute**
- If rent control applicable: Statutory limit on increase
- If no rent control: As per agreement OR market practice
- Standard: 5-10% annual escalation

**Issue 2: Maintenance Responsibility**
- Minor repairs: Lessee (Section 108(o))
- Major repairs: Lessor (Section 108(c))
- Ambiguous cases: Refer agreement OR general principle (structural = lessor)

**Issue 3: Subleasing Request**
- If agreement prohibits: Lessor can refuse arbitrarily
- If agreement silent: Lessee can sublet (Section 108(j))
- Lessor's consent: Should not be unreasonably withheld (if permitted)

### Phase 3: Termination of Lease

**Section 111: Determination of Lease**

**A. By Efflux of Time (Fixed-Term Lease)**
- Lease expires automatically on end date
- No notice required
- Lessee must vacate and return possession

**B. By Notice (Periodic Lease)**
- Section 106 notice requirements apply
- Month-to-month: 15 days notice
- Year-to-year: 6 months notice
- Notice must expire with end of period

**C. By Agreement**
- Mutual consent to terminate
- Surrender and acceptance
- Execute termination deed (advisable)

**D. By Forfeiture (Breach by Lessee)**
- Section 111(g): Lessor's right to forfeit
- Grounds: Non-payment, breach of covenant, unauthorized subletting
- Procedure: Notice to remedy breach → If not remedied, re-enter

**E. By Disclaimer (Lessee Renounces)**
- Lessee communicates intent to abandon lease
- Lessor accepts disclaimer
- Lease terminates

**F. By Merger**
- Lessee acquires lessor's interest (purchases property)
- Lease merges into ownership
- Terminates by operation of law

**G. By Destruction of Property**
- Property completely destroyed (fire, earthquake, flood)
- Lease frustrated (Section 108(e) exception)
- Terminates automatically

**H. By Operation of Law**
- Court decree
- Acquisition by government
- Insolvency of lessee

**Forfeiture Procedure (Section 111(g)):**

**Step 1: Breach Occurs**
- Lessee defaults (non-payment, unauthorized subletting, illegal use, waste)

**Step 2: Notice to Remedy** (Mandatory)
- Lessor gives written notice
- Specifying breach
- Demanding remedy within reasonable time (15-30 days)
- Warning of forfeiture if not remedied

**Step 3: Wait for Remedy Period**
- If lessee remedies breach → Lease continues
- If lessee does not remedy → Proceed to forfeiture

**Step 4: Re-Entry**
- Lessor re-enters property OR
- Files suit for possession (safer method)

**Step 5: Lessee's Relief (Section 114)**
- Lessee can apply to court for relief against forfeiture
- If breach remedied + compensation paid + court satisfied → Lease restored
- Discretionary remedy

**Notice Format (Sample):**

```
To: [Lessee Name and Address]

Date: [Date]

Subject: NOTICE UNDER SECTION 111(g) TPA - BREACH OF LEASE COVENANT

This is to inform you that you are in breach of the lease agreement dated [Date] for the property at [Address], on the following grounds:

1. Non-payment of rent for the months of [Months], totaling Rs. [Amount].
2. [Other breaches, if any]

You are hereby called upon to remedy the said breach by paying the arrears of rent and [remedy other breaches] within 15 days from the date of this notice.

Please note that if the breach is not remedied within the stipulated period, we shall be constrained to forfeit the lease and take possession of the property without further notice to you.

[Lessor Signature]
```

**Step 6: Ejectment Suit (If Lessee Refuses to Vacate)**

If lessee does not vacate after forfeiture:
- Lessor cannot forcibly evict (breach of peace)
- Must file ejectment suit

**Grounds for Ejectment (Typical):**
1. **Non-Payment of Rent** - Arrears beyond reasonable period
2. **Expiry of Lease** - Fixed term ended, notice given
3. **Breach of Covenant** - Subletting, illegal use, waste
4. **Bona Fide Need** - Lessor requires property for self-use (rent control jurisdictions)
5. **Unauthorized Construction** - Structural alterations without consent

### Phase 4: Ejectment Proceedings

**Route 1: Regular Civil Suit (If No Rent Control)**

**Court:** Civil Court having jurisdiction (where property situated)

**Procedure:**
1. File suit for ejectment + arrears + mesne profits
2. Plaint contents: Lease details, breach, forfeiture, demand for possession
3. Service of summons on lessee
4. Lessee files written statement (defense)
5. Framing of issues
6. Evidence (documentary + oral)
7. Arguments
8. Judgment
9. Decree for possession

**Timeline:** 1-3 years (depending on court backlog)

**Decree:**
- Ejectment of lessee
- Recovery of arrears
- Mesne profits (damages for wrongful possession)
- Costs

**Route 2: Rent Control Tribunal (If Rent Control Applicable)**

If property covered by Rent Control Act:
- Ejectment only on statutory grounds
- File application before Rent Controller/Tribunal
- Limited grounds: Non-payment, bona fide need, subletting, etc.
- Expedited procedure (theoretically)

**State-Specific Rent Control Acts:**
- Maharashtra: Maharashtra Rent Control Act 1999
- Delhi: Delhi Rent Control Act 1958
- West Bengal: West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act 1997
- Tamil Nadu: Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1960
- Karnataka: Karnataka Rent Control Act 1961

**Route 3: Summary Procedure (Section 14 Specific Relief Act - Suit for Injunction)**

If lessee creating nuisance/illegal use:
- File suit for injunction to restrain illegal activity
- Temporary injunction (Order XXXIX CPC)
- Quick interim relief

**Execution of Ejectment Decree:**

After obtaining decree:
1. **Execution Application** - Apply to court for execution
2. **Court Bailiff** - Court appoints bailiff to enforce possession
3. **Delivery of Possession** - Bailiff physically hands over possession to lessor
4. **Panchnama** - Report prepared by bailiff (possession delivered)

If lessee resists:
- Police assistance can be sought
- Contempt of court if willful disobedience

## Rights and Duties of Lessor and Lessee

### A. Rights of Lessee (Section 108)

**Right 1: Right to Possession (Section 108(a))**
- Lessee entitled to possession for lease period
- Lessor cannot disturb possession (quiet enjoyment)

**Right 2: Right to Fixtures (Section 108(k))**
- Fixtures attached by lessee remain lessee's property
- Removable at end of lease (unless agreed otherwise)
- Must not damage property during removal

**Fixtures vs Improvements:**
- **Fixtures:** Removable attachments (fans, ACs, shelves, curtains)
- **Improvements:** Permanent additions (cannot be removed without damage - built-in wardrobes, tiles)

**Right 3: Right to Sublet/Assign (Section 108(j))**
- Unless restricted by lease agreement
- If agreement silent: Lessee can transfer interest
- If prohibited: Subletting without consent = Forfeiture

**Right 4: Right to Emblements (Crops)**
- If agricultural lease
- Crops sown by lessee belong to lessee (even if harvested after lease ends)

**Right 5: Right to Compensation for Improvements (Section 108(l))**
- If lessee makes improvements with lessor's consent
- Entitled to compensation on termination
- Improvement increases property value

**Right 6: Right to Relief Against Forfeiture (Section 114)**
- If lease forfeited for breach
- Lessee can apply to court for relief
- If breach remedied + compensation paid → Court may restore lease

**Right 7: Right to Set Off (Section 108(m))**
- Lessee can set off lessor's breaches against rent
- If lessor fails major repairs → Lessee repairs and deducts from rent

**Right 8: Right to Defend Title (Section 108(h))**
- If third party claims property
- Lessee entitled to defend possession
- Lessor must indemnify if title defeated

### B. Duties of Lessee (Section 108)

**Duty 1: Pay Rent (Section 108(n))**
- Primary obligation
- On time and mode specified
- Failure → Forfeiture

**Duty 2: Disclose Defects (Section 108(b))**
- If lessee discovers material defects
- Inform lessor within reasonable time

**Duty 3: Maintain Property (Section 108(o))**
- Keep property in good condition
- Not commit waste (permissive or voluntary)
- Return property in good condition (wear and tear excepted)

**Duty 4: Give Notice of Transfer (Section 108(h))**
- If lessor sells property to third party
- Lessee must attorn to new owner (recognize as lessor)

**Duty 5: Allow Inspection**
- Lessor entitled to inspect (reasonable notice)
- Lessee must permit entry

**Duty 6: Deliver Possession on Termination (Section 108(q))**
- Vacate property
- Remove fixtures (without damage)
- Hand over keys

### C. Rights of Lessor (Section 109)

**Right 1: Right to Rent (Section 109(a))**
- Receive rent on due dates
- Sue for arrears
- Forfeit lease for non-payment

**Right 2: Right to Forfeiture (Section 111(g))**
- If lessee breaches covenant
- Follow notice procedure
- Re-enter and terminate lease

**Right 3: Right to Inspection**
- Inspect property to check condition
- Reasonable notice required (24-48 hours)

**Right 4: Right to Ejectment**
- On lease termination
- If lessee refuses to vacate → Sue for ejectment

**Right 5: Right to Mesne Profits (Section 109(e))**
- If lessee holds over after termination without consent
- Damages for wrongful possession (market rent OR double rent)

**Right 6: Right to Transfer Interest (Section 109(c))**
- Lessor can sell property
- Lease continues (buyer steps into lessor's shoes)
- Lessee's rights unaffected (Section 108(h))

### D. Duties of Lessor (Section 108)

**Duty 1: Deliver Possession (Section 108(a))**
- At commencement of lease
- Vacant possession (unless otherwise agreed)

**Duty 2: Quiet Enjoyment**
- Not interfere with lessee's possession
- Not disturb lessee's use

**Duty 3: Major Repairs (Section 108(c))**
- Structural repairs (roof, walls, foundation)
- Keep property fit for purpose

**Duty 4: Disclose Defects (Section 108(b))**
- Material defects known to lessor
- Inform lessee before lease

**Duty 5: Pay Property Tax** (Unless Otherwise Agreed)
- Statutory liability on owner
- Cannot recover from lessee (unless agreement permits)

## Special Situations

### 1. Holding Over (Section 116)

**Definition:** Lessee continues in possession after lease expiry without lessor's consent

**Legal Position:**
- Lessee becomes **Tenant at Sufferance**
- Lessor can:
  - Accept holding over → New lease created (same terms, month-to-month)
  - Refuse → Sue for ejectment + mesne profits

**Lessor Accepts Holding Over:**
- If lessor accepts rent after lease expiry
- Implies consent to continuation
- New lease created by implication
- Duration: Month-to-month OR year-to-year (as per previous rent payment cycle)

**Lessor Refuses Holding Over:**
- Demand possession
- If lessee refuses → Sue for ejectment + mesne profits
- Mesne profits: Market rent OR double the rent (whichever higher)

**Section 116 Effect:**
```
"If a lessee or sub-lessee of property remains in possession after determination 
of the lease granted to the lessee, and the lessor or his legal representative 
accepts rent from the lessee or sub-lessee, the lessor shall be deemed to have 
consented to the lessee's continuing in possession."
```

**Acceptance of Rent = Consent:**
- Even single acceptance creates new tenancy
- Lessor cannot claim arrears as mesne profits

**Avoiding Holding Over:**
- Lessor should:
  - Send termination notice well in advance
  - Demand vacation before expiry
  - Not accept rent after expiry (if wants to eject)
  - File ejectment suit immediately if lessee refuses

### 2. Subletting and Assignment

**Subletting (Section 108(j)):**
- Lessee leases property (or part) to third party (sub-lessee)
- Sub-lease period cannot exceed head-lease period
- Lessee remains liable to lessor for rent and covenants
- Sub-lessee liable to lessee (not lessor directly)

**Assignment:**
- Lessee transfers entire interest to assignee
- Assignee steps into lessee's shoes
- Assignee becomes tenant of lessor
- Original lessee may remain liable (depends on agreement)

**Difference:**

| Aspect | Subletting | Assignment |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Transfer** | Part of interest | Entire interest |
| **Period** | Cannot exceed head-lease | Entire remaining period |
| **Relationship** | Lessee-Sub-lessee | Lessor-Assignee (Lessee drops out) |
| **Liability** | Lessee remains liable | Assignee liable (Lessee may be released) |

**Covenant Against Subletting:**
- Absolute prohibition: Cannot sublet at all
- Qualified prohibition: Cannot sublet without consent (consent not to be unreasonably withheld)

**Subletting Without Consent (If Prohibited):**
- Breach of covenant
- Lessor can forfeit lease
- Notice procedure (Section 111(g))

**Consent to Subletting:**
- Lessor should give written consent
- Specify conditions (identity of sub-lessee, rent, use)
- Consent to one subletting ≠ General permission (unless stated)

### 3. Fixtures and Improvements

**Section 108(k): Lessee's Fixtures Removable**

**Fixtures Defined:**
- Chattels attached to property for use/ornament
- Removable without substantial damage

**Examples:**
- Air conditioners (split/window)
- Fans, lights, chandeliers
- Curtain rods, blinds
- Kitchen cabinets (if removable)
- Shelves, racks

**Improvements Defined:**
- Permanent additions increasing property value
- Not removable without damage

**Examples:**
- False ceiling
- Built-in wardrobes
- Tiling, flooring (replacing existing)
- Plumbing/electrical upgrades (if embedded)

**Lessee's Rights:**
- Remove fixtures at lease end
- Not damage property during removal
- If damage caused → Compensate lessor

**Improvements Compensation (Section 108(l)):**
- Lessee makes improvements with lessor's consent
- On termination, lessee entitled to:
  - Take away improvements (if removable), OR
  - Compensation equal to increase in property value

**Lessor's Rights:**
- Refuse consent to improvements
- If consent given → May purchase improvements at fair value
- If no consent → Lessee not entitled to compensation

### 4. Transfer of Lessor's Interest

**Section 109(c) + Section 108(h): Sale of Leased Property**

**Effect of Sale:**
- Buyer (new owner) steps into lessor's shoes
- Lease continues with all terms
- Lessee's rights unaffected

**Lessee's Duties:**
- Recognize new owner as lessor (attornment)
- Pay rent to new owner
- If disputes old owner's sale → Lessor must defend title

**Protections for Lessee:**
- Lease deed registered → Notice to world (buyer cannot claim ignorance)
- Lease deed unregistered → If buyer has notice (actual/constructive), still bound
- If bona fide buyer without notice → May not be bound (but rare)

**Practical Tip:**
- Always register lease deed (if >12 months)
- Creates notice to subsequent buyers

### 5. Rent Control Legislation

**Purpose:**
- Protect tenants from unreasonable rent increases
- Prevent arbitrary eviction
- Housing affordability

**Key Features (Typical):**

**A. Rent Regulation:**
- Standard rent fixed (based on property value/construction cost)
- Cannot increase beyond ceiling
- Formula: 8-10% of construction cost OR historical benchmark

**B. Eviction Protection:**
- Landlord cannot evict except on statutory grounds:
  1. **Non-Payment** - Arrears beyond reasonable period (6+ months)
  2. **Bona Fide Need** - Landlord needs for self/family
  3. **Unauthorized Subletting** - Without consent
  4. **Illegal Use** - Using for unlawful purpose
  5. **Waste** - Damaging property
  6. **Denial of Title** - Tenant denies landlord's title
  7. **Nuisance** - Creating nuisance to neighbors

**C. Inheritance Rights:**
- Tenancy passes to family members (spouse, children)
- Protects from eviction on tenant's death

**D. Exemptions (Modern Acts):**
Many states exempt:
- New constructions (post-1995/2000)
- High-rent properties (>Rs. 25,000/50,000 pm)
- Commercial properties (in some states)
- Leave and license agreements (11 months)

**Applicability Examples:**

**Maharashtra Rent Control Act 1999:**
- Applies to premises let before 1999 (old tenancies)
- Exempts: New tenancies, high rent (>Rs. 5,000 pm in 1999 - adjusted upward)

**Delhi Rent Control Act 1958:**
- Applies to most premises
- Exempts: Government premises, hotels, dharamshalas

**Practical Impact:**
- Old tenancies (pre-rent control exemption) enjoy strong protection
- New tenancies (post-exemption) have minimal protection
- Landlords prefer 11-month leave and license to avoid rent control

## Common Scenarios and Solutions

### Scenario 1: Lessee Refuses to Vacate After Lease Expiry

**Facts:**
- 11-month lease expired
- Lessee refuses to vacate
- No renewal agreed
- Lessor wants possession

**Lessor's Options:**

**Option 1: Accept Rent and Create New Tenancy**
- If lessor accepts rent for months after expiry
- New periodic tenancy created (Section 116)
- Month-to-month tenancy
- Terminate by 15 days notice (Section 106)

**Option 2: Refuse Rent and Sue for Ejectment**
- Do not accept rent post-expiry
- Send legal notice demanding vacation
- If lessee refuses → File ejectment suit
- Claim mesne profits (double rent)

**Timeline:**
- Notice: 15-30 days
- Suit filing: Immediate
- Decree: 6-12 months
- Execution: 3-6 months
- Total: 9-18 months

**Lessee's Defense:**
- Claim renewal was agreed (difficult without writing)
- Offer to vacate if adequate time given
- Pay mesne profits to avoid adverse decree

---

### Scenario 2: Lessee Sublets Without Consent

**Facts:**
- Lease agreement prohibits subletting without consent
- Lessor discovers lessee has sublet to third party
- Rent being collected by lessee from sub-lessee

**Lessor's Remedies:**

**Remedy 1: Forfeit Lease (Section 111(g))**
- Send notice specifying breach (unauthorized subletting)
- Demand termination of sub-lease within 15 days
- If not complied → Forfeit lease and sue for possession

**Remedy 2: Seek Injunction**
- File suit to restrain lessee from subletting
- Temporary injunction (Order XXXIX CPC)

**Remedy 3: Damages**
- Claim damages for breach of covenant
- Loss = Difference between market rent and agreed rent (if sub-lease at higher rent)

**Lessee's Defense:**
- Argue subletting permitted (if agreement silent)
- Section 108(j): Lessee can transfer unless restricted
- If agreement ambiguous, interpret in favor of lessee

---

### Scenario 3: Lessor Fails to Do Major Repairs

**Facts:**
- Roof leaking, causing water seepage
- Lessee informed lessor multiple times
- Lessor refuses to repair
- Lessee's belongings damaged

**Lessee's Remedies:**

**Remedy 1: Self-Help and Set-Off (Section 108(m))**
- Lessee does repairs himself
- Deduct repair cost from rent
- Retain receipts and proof
- Inform lessor in writing

**Remedy 2: Sue for Specific Performance**
- File suit compelling lessor to do repairs
- Section 10 Specific Relief Act
- Obtain decree directing repair

**Remedy 3: Damages**
- Claim compensation for:
  - Damage to lessee's belongings
  - Mental agony (if severe)
  - Loss of use/enjoyment

**Remedy 4: Terminate Lease (If Substantial)**
- If lessor's breach frustrates lease purpose
- Treat as repudiation
- Vacate and claim damages

**Lessor's Defense:**
- Repairs are minor (lessee's responsibility)
- Lessee caused damage (not wear and tear)
- Lessee did not give reasonable notice

---

### Scenario 4: Rent Control Applicability Dispute

**Facts:**
- Property leased in 2000 (in Delhi)
- Monthly rent Rs. 40,000
- Landlord wants to increase rent to Rs. 60,000
- Tenant claims rent control protection

**Analysis:**

**Step 1: Check Rent Control Act Applicability**
- Delhi Rent Control Act 1958 applies to most premises
- Exemptions: Government, hotels, dharamshalas, certain new constructions

**Step 2: Determine Standard Rent**
- If property falls under DRC Act, standard rent fixed by Rent Controller
- Cannot exceed statutory ceiling

**Step 3: Rent Increase Procedure**
- Landlord can apply to Rent Controller for rent increase
- Based on property value, construction cost, market rate
- Tenant can contest

**Outcome:**
- If covered: Rent increase subject to Rent Controller approval
- If exempt: Rent as per agreement/market rate

**Practical Tip:**
- New leases often exempt from rent control (check state-specific exemptions)
- Landlords prefer 11-month leave and license to avoid rent control

---

### Scenario 5: Lessee Claims Ownership by Adverse Possession

**Facts:**
- Lessee in possession for 15 years
- Paid rent initially, then stopped
- Lessor did not pursue eviction
- Now lessee claims ownership by adverse possession

**Legal Position:**

**Adverse Possession Requirements:**
1. **Hostile Possession** - Against owner's title
2. **Actual Possession** - Physical occupation
3. **Open and Notorious** - Visible to owner
4. **Continuous** - Uninterrupted for 12 years (Article 65 Limitation Act)
5. **Exclusive** - To exclusion of owner

**Lessee's Challenge:**
- **Permissive Possession** - Lessee's possession is NOT hostile (it's permissive)
- Adverse possession does NOT apply to lessees (unless explicitly deny title)

**When Lessee Can Claim Adverse Possession:**
- If lessee explicitly denies lessor's title
- AND possesses adversely for 12 years thereafter
- Courts require clear, unequivocal denial

**Lessor's Defense:**
- Lessee cannot claim adverse possession (possession is permissive)
- Even if rent not paid, possession remains under lease
- Adverse possession clock does not start

**Outcome:**
- Lessor likely to succeed
- Lessee should be evicted (not become owner)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Not Registering Lease (>12 Months)
**Consequence:** Lease inadmissible as evidence; difficult to prove terms
**Solution:** ALWAYS register lease exceeding 12 months

### 2. Accepting Rent After Lease Expiry (If Want to Eject)
**Consequence:** Creates new tenancy (Section 116); cannot eject easily
**Solution:** Refuse rent; send termination notice; file ejectment suit

### 3. Subletting Without Lessor's Consent (If Prohibited)
**Consequence:** Forfeiture of lease; eviction
**Solution:** Obtain written consent before subletting

### 4. Ignoring Rent Control Applicability
**Consequence:** Eviction grounds limited; rent increase restricted
**Solution:** Verify rent control applicability before leasing

### 5. Not Conducting Property Inspection Before Leasing
**Consequence:** Discover defects later; disputes over maintenance
**Solution:** Thorough inspection; document condition; take photographs

### 6. Vague Maintenance Clauses
**Consequence:** Disputes over repair responsibility
**Solution:** Clearly specify lessor vs lessee responsibilities (major vs minor repairs)

### 7. No Written Lease Agreement
**Consequence:** Difficult to prove terms; disputes likely
**Solution:** Always execute written lease agreement (even if not registrable)

### 8. Forfeiture Without Notice
**Consequence:** Court may grant relief against forfeiture (Section 114)
**Solution:** Always send Section 111(g) notice before forfeiting

### 9. Accepting "Leave and License" Label Blindly
**Consequence:** Agreement may actually be lease (if exclusive possession + rent)
**Solution:** Examine substance, not label; ensure true license characteristics

### 10. Not Taking Security Deposit Receipt
**Consequence:** Dispute over refund amount at termination
**Solution:** Obtain written receipt specifying deposit amount, refund conditions
