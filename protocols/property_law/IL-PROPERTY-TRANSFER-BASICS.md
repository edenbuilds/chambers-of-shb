## Purpose

## Core Principles

### 1. Transfer of Property Defined (Section 5)

```
Section 5: "Transfer of property means an act by which a living person conveys 
property, in present or in future, to one or more other living persons, or to 
himself and one or more other living persons"

Essential Elements:

1. LIVING PERSON (Transferor):
   - Natural person OR juristic person (company, trust)
   - NOT testamentary disposition (will - governed by Succession Act)

2. CONVEYANCE:
   - Act of transferring rights
   - Voluntarily (not by operation of law)

3. PROPERTY:
   - Immovable (land, buildings, hereditary allowances, rights in land)
   - Movable (goods, actionable claims, money)

4. LIVING PERSON (Transferee):
   - Natural person OR juristic person
   - OR to himself + others (self-transfer + joint)

5. PRESENT OR FUTURE:
   - Immediate transfer (possession now)
   - Future transfer (possession later, but interest vests now)

NOT Transfer of Property:
✗ Will/testamentary disposition (Succession Act)
✗ Transfer by operation of law (inheritance, insolvency, escheat)
✗ Transfer of mere license to use (not property interest)
```

### 2. What Can Be Transferred (Section 6)

```
Section 6: Property of any kind may be transferred

Transferable Property:
✓ Immovable property (land, buildings)
✓ Movable property (goods)
✓ Actionable claims (debts, benefits of contracts)
✓ Contingent interests (Section 21)
✓ Leasehold interests
✓ Life estates
✓ Future interests (vested, contingent)

Section 6 Exceptions (Cannot Be Transferred):

(a) SPES SUCCESSIONIS (Expectancy of Succession):
    - Chance of inheritance (before death)
    - Example: "A's son B cannot transfer his expectancy to inherit A's property 
      while A is alive"
    - After inheritance vests (A dies) → B can transfer inherited property

(b) MERE RIGHT TO SUE:
    - Right to sue for damages (unliquidated)
    - Exception: Actionable claim (liquidated debt) CAN be transferred

(c) MERE RIGHT OF RE-ENTRY:
    - Landlord's right to re-enter leased property (cannot be transferred separately)
    - BUT lease with right of re-entry CAN be transferred

(d) EASEMENT SEPARATELY FROM DOMINANT HERITAGE:
    - Easement (right of way) attached to land
    - Cannot transfer easement without transferring land
    - Easement transfers WITH land automatically

(e) RESTRICTED INTEREST:
    - Property expressly forbidden from transfer by law
    - Example: Agricultural land (some states restrict transfer to non-agriculturists)

(f) RIGHT TO FUTURE MAINTENANCE:
    - Widow's right to maintenance (personal, not transferable)

(g) GOVERNMENT PENSIONS:
    - Non-transferable (except as permitted by government rules)
```

### 3. Who Can Transfer (Section 7)

```
Section 7: Every person competent to contract and entitled to transferable 
property, or authorized to dispose of transferable property not his own, is 
competent to transfer such property either wholly or in part

Competent Transferor:

1. COMPETENT TO CONTRACT (Section 11 Indian Contract Act):
   - Age of majority (18 years)
   - Sound mind
   - Not disqualified by law

2. ENTITLED TO PROPERTY:
   - Owner (absolute owner can transfer whole)
   - Limited owner (life estate holder can transfer his interest only)
   - Co-owner (can transfer his undivided share)

3. AUTHORIZED TO TRANSFER:
   - Agent (with power of attorney)
   - Guardian (with court permission for minor's property)
   - Manager (of HUF property, with consent of coparceners for permanent transfers)
   - Trustee (in accordance with trust deed)
   - Court-appointed receiver/administrator

NOT Competent:
✗ Minor (void transfer - Mohori Bibee principle)
✗ Person of unsound mind (void)
✗ Person not entitled (stranger to property)
✗ Unauthorized person (agent without power)
```

## TYPES OF TRANSFERS (Sections 54-123)

### Overview of Transfer Types

```
TRANSFER OF PROPERTY ACT 1882 COVERS:

1. SALE (Section 54):
   - Transfer for price (money consideration)
   - Absolute transfer of ownership

2. MORTGAGE (Section 58):
   - Transfer of interest in property as security for loan
   - Ownership reverts on repayment

3. LEASE (Section 105):
   - Transfer of right to enjoy property for specified time
   - Rent consideration
   - Ownership remains with lessor

4. EXCHANGE (Section 118):
   - Transfer of property for other property
   - Barter (not money)

5. GIFT (Section 122):
   - Transfer without consideration
   - Gratuitous transfer

6. ACTIONABLE CLAIM (Section 130):
   - Transfer of debt, benefit of contract
   - Assignment

NOT Covered (but related):
- Will: Succession Act (testamentary)
- Partition: Partition Act (division among co-owners)
- Easement creation: Easements Act
```

### 1. Sale (Section 54)

```
Section 54: "Sale is a transfer of ownership in exchange for price paid or 
promised or part-paid and part-promised"

Essential Elements:

1. TRANSFER OF OWNERSHIP:
   - Seller transfers all rights in property
   - Buyer becomes absolute owner

2. PRICE (Consideration):
   - Money (not goods - that's exchange)
   - Paid, promised, or part-paid & part-promised

3. TWO PARTIES:
   - Seller (transferor)
   - Buyer (transferee)

Contract of Sale vs Sale:
- Contract of Sale (Section 54): Agreement to sell (future transfer)
- Sale (Section 54): Immediate transfer of ownership

Registration Requirement (Section 54):

IMMOVABLE PROPERTY:

Value Rs. 100 or more:
- MUST be registered (Section 54 + Section 17(1)(b) Registration Act 1908)
- If not registered → VOID (no title passes)

Value less than Rs. 100:
- May be by registered instrument OR delivery of possession (rare)

MOVABLE PROPERTY:
- No registration required (delivery sufficient)

Writing Requirement:
- Immovable property sale: MUST be in writing + registered
- Movable property sale: Oral contract sufficient (but writing advisable)
```

### 2. Mortgage (Section 58)

```
Section 58: "Mortgage is the transfer of an interest in specific immovable 
property for the purpose of securing the payment of money advanced or to be 
advanced by way of loan, an existing or future debt, or the performance of an 
engagement which may give rise to a pecuniary liability"

Key Elements:

1. SECURITY (Not Sale):
   - Property interest transferred as security
   - Ownership reverts to mortgagor on repayment

2. LOAN/DEBT:
   - Money advanced or to be advanced
   - OR existing debt (mortgage to secure prior debt)

3. IMMOVABLE PROPERTY:
   - Mortgage only of immovable property (land, buildings)
   - Movable property: "Pledge" (not mortgage)

4. REDEMPTION RIGHT:
   - Mortgagor can redeem (get property back) by repaying loan

Types of Mortgages (Section 58):

(a) SIMPLE MORTGAGE:
    - No possession delivered
    - Mortgagee gets right to sell property if default

(b) MORTGAGE BY CONDITIONAL SALE:
    - Ostensible sale with condition to re-convey on repayment
    - Sale defeasible on payment

(c) USUFRUCTUARY MORTGAGE:
    - Mortgagee gets possession + right to receive rent/profits
    - Rent applied toward interest + principal (no separate payment)

(d) ENGLISH MORTGAGE:
    - Mortgagor binds to repay by certain date
    - On default, property absolutely mortgagee's
    - On payment, property re-conveyed

(e) MORTGAGE BY DEPOSIT OF TITLE DEEDS (Equitable Mortgage):
    - Delivery of title deeds to mortgagee
    - In certain towns (notified - Calcutta, Bombay, Madras, etc.)
    - Creates equitable mortgage (enforceable)

(f) ANOMALOUS MORTGAGE:
    - Combination of types
    - Hybrid features
```

### 3. Lease (Section 105)

```
Section 105: "Lease of immovable property is a transfer of a right to enjoy 
such property, made for a certain time, express or implied, or in perpetuity, 
in consideration of a price paid or promised, or of money, a share of crops, 
service or any other thing of value, to be rendered periodically or on 
specified occasions to the transferor by the transferee"

Essential Elements:

1. TRANSFER OF RIGHT TO ENJOY (Not Ownership):
   - Lessee gets possession + right to use property
   - Lessor retains ownership

2. CERTAIN TIME:
   - Fixed period (1 year, 5 years, 99 years)
   - OR perpetuity (rare - certain states allow)

3. CONSIDERATION (Rent):
   - Money (monthly, yearly)
   - OR share of crops (agricultural lease)
   - OR service
   - OR other thing of value

4. LESSOR and LESSEE:
   - Lessor: Owner transferring enjoyment
   - Lessee: Person receiving enjoyment

Registration Requirement (Section 107):

Lease for > 12 months (more than one year):
- MUST be registered (Section 107 + Registration Act)
- If not registered → Valid only as license (not lease)

Lease for ≤ 12 months:
- No registration required (oral or written)

Transfer of Leasehold:
- Lessee can transfer lease (unless prohibited in lease deed - Section 108)
- Sublease permitted (unless prohibited)
```

### 4. Exchange (Section 118)

```
Section 118: "When two persons mutually transfer the ownership of one thing for 
the ownership of another, neither thing nor both things being money only, the 
transaction is called an 'exchange'"

Essential Elements:

1. MUTUAL TRANSFER:
   - A transfers property X to B
   - B transfers property Y to A

2. OWNERSHIP TRANSFER:
   - Complete transfer (not lease/mortgage)

3. NOT MONEY:
   - At least one property must be non-money
   - If both money → Not exchange (two sales)

Example:
A owns land in Mumbai. B owns land in Delhi. A and B exchange properties.
→ Exchange (not sale)

Registration:
- If immovable property: MUST be registered (Section 118 applies Section 54)
```

### 5. Gift (Section 122)

```
Section 122: "Gift is the transfer of certain existing movable or immovable 
property made voluntarily and without consideration, by one person, called the 
donor, to another, called the donee, and accepted by or on behalf of the donee"

Essential Elements:

1. TRANSFER:
   - Voluntary (not forced)
   - Existing property (not future property - exception: Section 124 onerous gift)

2. WITHOUT CONSIDERATION:
   - No price paid
   - Gratuitous (love, affection, natural love)

3. ACCEPTANCE:
   - Donee must accept (express or implied)
   - During donor's lifetime (for immovable property gift)

4. DONOR and DONEE:
   - Living persons (not testamentary - that's will)

Registration Requirement (Section 123):

IMMOVABLE PROPERTY GIFT:
- MUST be registered (Section 123 + Registration Act)
- If not registered → VOID (no transfer)

MOVABLE PROPERTY GIFT:
- Delivery of possession sufficient (no registration)
- Writing advisable (evidence)

Revocability:
- Gift generally IRREVOCABLE (once completed)
- Exception: Onerous gift (Section 127 - can be repudiated if burden too heavy)
```

## ESSENTIAL REQUIREMENTS FOR VALID TRANSFER

### 1. Competency (Section 7)

```
Transferor Must Be:

1. Competent to Contract:
   - Major (18+ years)
   - Sound mind
   - Not disqualified

2. Entitled to Property:
   - Owner OR limited interest holder OR authorized person

Invalid Transfers:

Minor's Transfer:
- VOID (Mohori Bibee principle)
- Minor cannot transfer property
- Even with guardian consent (unless court permission for minor's benefit)

Unsound Mind:
- VOID (lacks capacity)

Unauthorized Person:
- VOID (nemo dat quod non habet - cannot give what you don't have)
```

### 2. Consideration (Where Required)

```
Sale (Section 54):
- Consideration REQUIRED (price)
- If no price → Gift (not sale)

Mortgage (Section 58):
- Loan/debt is consideration

Lease (Section 105):
- Rent/share of crops is consideration

Exchange (Section 118):
- Mutual transfer of properties (consideration in form of counter-property)

Gift (Section 122):
- NO consideration required (gratuitous)
```

### 3. Registration (Where Mandatory)

```
Section 17 Registration Act 1908:

MUST Be Registered:

1. Sale of immovable property (value Rs. 100+)
2. Mortgage of immovable property (except deposit of title deeds in notified towns)
3. Lease for > 12 months
4. Gift of immovable property
5. Exchange of immovable property
6. Transfer of actionable claim

Effect of Non-Registration (Section 49 Registration Act):
- Document VOID (does not pass title)
- Cannot be used as evidence of transfer
- Cannot be admitted in court

Exception - Deposit of Title Deeds (Equitable Mortgage):
- In notified towns (Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai, etc.)
- Delivery of title deeds creates equitable mortgage
- No registration required (Section 58(f) proviso)
```

### 4. Writing (Where Required)

```
Immovable Property Transfers:
- MUST be in writing (instrument executed)
- Signed by transferor
- Registered (if value Rs. 100+)

Oral Transfer:
- Immovable property: VOID (if value Rs. 100+)
- Movable property: Valid (delivery sufficient)

Exceptions:
- Movable property gifts: Delivery sufficient (oral valid)
- Leases ≤ 12 months: Oral valid
```

### 5. Delivery/Possession (Where Required)

```
Movable Property:
- Delivery of possession essential (for sale, gift)
- Symbolical delivery permitted (keys to warehouse)

Immovable Property:
- Delivery of possession NOT essential for sale (ownership passes by registration)
- BUT possession delivery essential for:
  * Lease (lessee must get possession)
  * Usufructuary mortgage (mortgagee gets possession)
  * Gift of movables (physical delivery)
```

## VOID VS VOIDABLE TRANSFERS

### Void Transfers (Section 7, 10, 11, 23-26)

```
VOID = No legal effect from beginning

Void Transfers:

1. By Minor (Mohori Bibee):
   - Minor's transfer VOID ab initio
   - Cannot be ratified on attaining majority

2. By Unauthorized Person:
   - Stranger transferring property he doesn't own
   - Nemo dat quod non habet

3. Not Registered (when mandatory):
   - Section 49 Registration Act - document void

4. Opposed to Public Policy (Section 23):
   - Transfer for illegal purpose
   - Transfer defeating provisions of law

5. Uncertain (Section 14):
   - Transfer of uncertain interest
   - Example: "To A, if he marries" (too uncertain)

6. Perpetual Restriction on Alienation (Section 10):
   - "To A, but A cannot transfer to anyone"
   - Absolute restriction void (property must be alienable)
   - Limited restriction valid (e.g., "Cannot transfer for 5 years")

Effect:
- No title passes
- Property remains with transferor
- Transferee cannot claim ownership
```

### Voidable Transfers (Section 19A, 39, 53, 64 Contract Act)

```
VOIDABLE = Valid until rescinded by aggrieved party

Voidable Transfers:

1. Coercion (Section 15 Contract Act):
   - Transfer obtained by threat/force
   - Transferor can rescind

2. Undue Influence (Section 16 Contract Act):
   - Dominant position used to obtain unfair advantage
   - Transferor can rescind

3. Fraud (Section 17 Contract Act):
   - False representation inducing transfer
   - Transferor can rescind

4. Misrepresentation (Section 18 Contract Act):
   - Innocent false statement
   - Transferor can rescind

5. Transfer by Person with Limited Interest (Section 43-55):
   - Person with limited interest (life estate) transferring absolute interest
   - Voidable at option of true owner (after limited interest expires)

Effect:
- Valid until rescinded
- Aggrieved party can:
  * Rescind (cancel transfer, restore status quo)
  * Affirm (continue with transfer)
- If rescinded → Property reverts to transferor
```

## TRANSFER BY UNAUTHORIZED PERSONS (Section 43-55)

### Section 43: Transfer by Unauthorized Person

```
Section 43: "Where, with the consent, express or implied, of the persons 
interested in immovable property, a person is the ostensible owner of such 
property and transfers the same for consideration, the transfer shall not be 
voidable on the ground that the transferor was not authorized to make it, 
provided the transferee, after taking reasonable care to ascertain that the 
transferor had power to make the transfer, has acted in good faith"

Doctrine of Ostensible Owner (Feeding the Estoppel):

Elements:

1. Consent of True Owner:
   - True owner allows unauthorized person to appear as owner
   - Express or implied consent

2. Ostensible Ownership:
   - Unauthorized person appears as owner
   - Documents, possession create appearance

3. Transfer for Consideration:
   - Bona fide purchaser pays value

4. Good Faith + Reasonable Care:
   - Purchaser believes transferor is owner
   - Makes reasonable inquiry

Effect:
- Transfer valid (binds true owner)
- True owner estopped from challenging

Example:
A (true owner) allows B (A's manager) to appear as owner (B's name on documents with A's consent). B sells property to C (bona fide purchaser). 
→ Sale valid (A estopped from claiming property)
```

### Section 41: Transfer by Tenant

```
Section 41: Transfer by person ostensibly owning but having limited interest

If lessee transfers as if owner (conceals lease), transferee gets lessee's 
interest only (not ownership).

Exception: If lessor estopped (allowed lessee to appear as owner) → Transfer 
binds lessor (Section 43)
```

## COMMON SCENARIOS

### Scenario 1: Minor's Transfer (Void)

```
Facts:
- A (17 years old, minor) owns land (inherited from father)
- A sells land to B for Rs. 50 lakhs
- Sale deed registered

Issue: Is sale valid?

Analysis:
- Section 7: Only persons competent to contract can transfer
- Section 11 Contract Act: Minor not competent
- Mohori Bibee: Minor's agreement VOID ab initio

Result:
- Sale VOID (no title passes to B)
- A can reclaim land (even after attaining majority)
- B cannot enforce sale deed
- B's remedy: Sue guardian/parents for fraud (if they facilitated sale)
```

### Scenario 2: Unregistered Sale of Immovable Property (Void)

```
Facts:
- A agrees to sell land to B for Rs. 10 lakhs
- B pays Rs. 10 lakhs
- A executes sale deed (not registered)
- A delivers possession to B

Issue: Is B the owner?

Analysis:
- Section 54: Sale of immovable property (value Rs. 100+) MUST be registered
- Section 49 Registration Act: Unregistered document VOID
- Possession delivery irrelevant (ownership passes by registered document, not possession)

Result:
- Sale VOID (B is NOT owner)
- A remains owner (title not passed)
- B's remedies:
  * Specific performance (compel A to register sale deed)
  * Damages for breach of contract
- B in possession as licensee/trespasser (not owner)
```

### Scenario 3: Ostensible Owner Transfer (Valid - Section 43)

```
Facts:
- A (true owner) allows B (A's manager) to manage property
- Property documents in B's name (with A's consent for convenience)
- B sells property to C for Rs. 1 crore
- C believes B is owner (checks documents, all in B's name)
- C pays value, acts in good faith

Issue: Can A reclaim property from C?

Analysis:
- Section 43: Transfer by ostensible owner
- Elements satisfied:
  * A's consent (allowed B to appear as owner)
  * B ostensible owner (documents in B's name)
  * C bona fide purchaser (reasonable care + good faith)
  * Consideration paid

Result:
- Sale VALID (C gets title)
- A estopped from claiming (Section 43)
- A's remedy: Sue B for fraud/breach of trust
- C protected (bona fide purchaser)
```

### Scenario 4: Gift Without Registration (Void)

```
Facts:
- A gifts land to B (daughter) out of love
- Gift deed executed (signed)
- NOT registered
- B takes possession

Issue: Is gift valid?

Analysis:
- Section 122-123: Gift of immovable property MUST be registered
- Section 49 Registration Act: Unregistered document VOID

Result:
- Gift VOID (B does NOT become owner)
- A remains owner (title not passed)
- B's possession does not confer ownership (mere licensee)
- Solution: Execute fresh gift deed + register
```

## CASE LAW

### 1. Mohori Bibee v. Dharmodas Ghose (PC 1903)
```
Facts: Minor's agreement to mortgage property
Held:
- Minor's agreement VOID ab initio (not voidable)
- Cannot be ratified on attaining majority
- Minor cannot transfer property validly
- Section 11 Contract Act: Minor not competent to contract
```

### 2. Ram Coomar Coondoo v. Chunder Canto Mookerjee (PC 1876)
```
Facts: Doctrine of feeding the estoppel (Section 43)
Held:
- True owner who allows unauthorized person to appear as owner → Estopped
- Bona fide purchaser from ostensible owner gets valid title
- Estoppel feeds the grant (subsequent acquisition by transferor enures to transferee)
```

### 3. Suraj Lamp & Industries v. State of Haryana (SC 2012)
```
Facts: Unregistered sale deed
Held:
- Section 54 + Section 17(1)(b) Registration Act: Sale of immovable property (Rs. 100+) MUST be registered
- Section 49: Unregistered document VOID (no title passes)
- Possession without registration does not confer ownership
```

### 4. Khwaja Muhammad Khan v. Husaini Begum (PC 1910)
```
Facts: Transfer by person with limited interest (life estate)
Held:
- Life estate holder transferring absolute interest → Transfer valid to extent of life estate only
- After life estate holder's death, property reverts to remainderman
- Purchaser gets only limited interest (life estate), not absolute ownership
```

## SUMMARY CHECKLIST

### Valid Transfer Checklist:

```
☐ Competent transferor (major, sound mind, entitled to property)
☐ Competent transferee (major, sound mind)
☐ Property transferable (not in Section 6 exceptions)
☐ Consideration (if required - sale, lease, mortgage, exchange)
☐ Writing (for immovable property)
☐ Registration (if mandatory):
   ☐ Sale of immovable (Rs. 100+)
   ☐ Mortgage (except equitable)
   ☐ Lease (> 12 months)
   ☐ Gift of immovable
   ☐ Exchange of immovable
☐ Delivery of possession (if required - movable gifts, lease)
☐ Acceptance (for gifts)
☐ No vitiation (no coercion, fraud, undue influence)
☐ Not opposed to public policy
```

## COMMON MISTAKES

```
Mistake 1: Minor Transferring Property
❌ Minor executes sale deed (even with guardian consent, unless court permission)
✓ Guardian obtains court permission (for minor's benefit) OR wait till majority

Mistake 2: Not Registering Immovable Property Transfer
❌ Execute sale deed, deliver possession, but don't register
✓ MUST register (Section 54 + Registration Act) for title to pass

Mistake 3: Oral Gift of Immovable Property
❌ Orally gift land to son, deliver possession
✓ Execute gift deed + register (Section 123 - mandatory)

Mistake 4: Absolute Restriction on Alienation
❌ "To A, but A cannot transfer to anyone ever" (void - Section 10)
✓ Limited restriction valid: "A cannot transfer for 10 years"

Mistake 5: Transfer of Future Property
❌ "I transfer the property I will inherit from my father" (spes successionis - void)
✓ Wait till inheritance vests, then transfer

Mistake 6: Assuming Possession = Ownership
❌ Take possession without registered deed, assume ownership
✓ Registration essential for immovable property (possession ≠ ownership)
```
