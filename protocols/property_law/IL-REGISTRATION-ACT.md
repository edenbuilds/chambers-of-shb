# Indian Property Law: Registration of Documents (Registration Act 1908)

## Purpose
This protocol provides comprehensive guidance on the Registration Act 1908, covering mandatory registration requirements (Section 17), optional registration (Section 18), effects of registration/non-registration (Sections 47-49), priority between registered and unregistered documents (Section 27), time limits for registration (Section 23-25), procedure for registration (Section 32-35), and practical considerations for property document registration.

## Activation Triggers
Use this protocol when:
- Determining if document requires registration
- Sale deed, gift deed, mortgage deed, lease deed execution
- Understanding consequences of non-registration
- Priority disputes between registered/unregistered documents
- Late registration application (beyond 4 months)
- Registration procedure and requirements
- Refusal of registration by Sub-Registrar
- Presentment of document for registration
- Certified copies application
- Cancellation/rectification of registered document
- Title verification based on registered documents
- Enforcement of unregistered document

## Core Principles

### 1. Purpose of Registration

**Why Registration Required:**

1. **Publicity** - Notice to world about transactions
2. **Authentication** - Government verification of execution
3. **Preservation** - Permanent record maintenance
4. **Priority** - Determines priority between competing claims
5. **Evidence** - Admissibility in court
6. **Prevention of Fraud** - Reduces fraudulent transactions

**Section 47 - Registration Creates Constructive Notice:**
```
"Every registered document shall operate as notice to all persons of the 
contents thereof from the time of its registration."
```

**Effect:**
- Registered document gives notice to world
- Subsequent buyers/mortgagees deemed to have knowledge
- Cannot claim ignorance

### 2. Section 17: Documents of Which Registration is COMPULSORY

**Section 17(1) - Mandatory Registration:**

**Compulsorily Registrable Documents:**

**(a) Instruments of GIFT of Immovable Property**
- Gift of immovable property (any value)
- Must be registered
- Non-registration: Gift VOID

**(b) Other NON-TESTAMENTARY Instruments Transferring Immovable Property of Value Rs. 100+**
- **Sale deed** - Rs. 100 or more
- **Mortgage deed** - All mortgages (except deposit of title deeds)
- **Exchange deed** - Rs. 100 or more
- **Lease deed** - Period > 12 months OR reserving rent > 12 months

**(c) Non-Testamentary Instruments Transferring ANY INTEREST in Immovable Property Rs. 100+**
- Transfer of easement (perpetual OR > 1 year)
- Transfer of mining lease, water rights

**(d) Acknowledgment of Debt/Liability > Rs. 100 if Property Charged**
- Document acknowledging debt secured by immovable property

**(e) Powers of Attorney Authorizing Alienation of Immovable Property**
- Power of Attorney to sell, mortgage, gift property
- General/special power

**(f) Leases of Immovable Property - Period > 12 Months**
- Lease exceeding 12 months from execution date
- Lease reserving rent for period > 12 months

**(g) Certain Special Documents**
- Adoption deed (if immovable property involved)
- Award by arbitration (if immovable property)

**Section 17(2) - Additional Compulsory Documents:**

**(a) Documents Rectifying/Affecting Registered Document**
- Supplementary deed
- Variation deed
- Rectification deed

**Summary Table:**

| Document Type | Registration Required? | Value Threshold |
|---------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **Sale deed** | YES | Rs. 100+ |
| **Gift deed** | YES | Any value |
| **Mortgage deed** | YES | All mortgages |
| **Equitable mortgage (deposit title)** | NO | NA |
| **Lease** | YES | >12 months |
| **Lease** | NO | ≤12 months |
| **Easement** | YES | >1 year OR perpetual |
| **Power of Attorney (sale/mortgage)** | YES | Any value |
| **Will** | NO | NA (probate required) |
| **Agreement to sell** | NO | Optional |
| **Partition deed** | NO | Optional (Section 18) |

### 3. Section 18: Documents of Which Registration is OPTIONAL

**Section 18 - Optionally Registrable:**

**(a) Instruments of No Value (Documents Not Required by Section 17)**
- Agreement to sell
- Memorandum of understanding
- Affidavit

**(b) Will (Testamentary Documents)**
- Optional to register
- Usually not registered (probate obtained after death)

**(c) Decree or Final Order of Court**
- Can be registered for creating notice

**(d) Instrument of Partition**
- Partition deed (co-owners dividing property)
- Registration optional but advisable

**Why Register Optional Documents:**
- Creates public notice
- Admissible as evidence
- Priority protection
- Preservation

**Example:**
- Agreement to sell: Optional
- But advisable if substantial advance paid
- Registration creates notice to prevent seller's double sale

### 4. Section 23-25: Time Limit for Registration

**Section 23 - General Rule:**

**Within 4 MONTHS** from date of execution

**Execution Date:**
- Date when document signed/executed
- NOT date when transaction completed

**Example:**
- Sale deed executed on 1st January 2025
- Must be registered by 1st May 2025 (4 months)

**Section 25 - Extension:**

**Additional 4 Months (With Penalty)**

- Application with late fee/penalty
- Discretion of Registrar
- Maximum total: 8 MONTHS from execution

**After 8 Months:**
- Registration REFUSED
- Document becomes unregistrable
- Effect: Void for purposes requiring registration

**Calculation:**
- Exclude day of execution
- Include day of presentment

**Section 24 - Special Cases (Extended Time):**

**(a) Document Executed Outside India:**
- 4 months from arrival in India

**(b) Document Detained for Stamp Duty Adjudication:**
- Time during adjudication excluded

**(c) Document Executed by Person Abroad:**
- 4 months from receipt in India by attorney

### 5. Section 47-49: Effect of Registration and Non-Registration

**Section 47 - Effect of REGISTRATION:**

```
"Every registered document shall operate as notice to all persons of the 
contents thereof from the time of its registration."
```

**Benefits of Registration:**

1. **Constructive Notice to World**
   - All persons deemed to have knowledge
   - Subsequent buyers cannot claim ignorance

2. **Admissible as Evidence (Section 47-A)**
   - Certified copy admissible in court
   - Presumption of genuineness

3. **Priority Protection (Section 27)**
   - Registered document prevails over unregistered

4. **Legal Effect (Transfer of Title)**
   - Ownership transferred (if sale/gift)
   - Interest created (if mortgage/lease)

**Section 49 - Effect of NON-REGISTRATION:**

```
"No document required by Section 17 to be registered shall:
(a) Affect any immovable property comprised therein, OR
(b) Confer any power to adopt, OR  
(c) Be received as evidence of any transaction affecting such property or 
    conferring such power,

UNLESS it has been REGISTERED."
```

**Consequences of Non-Registration (If Registration Mandatory):**

**Consequence 1: VOID as to Property Transfer**
- No effect on immovable property
- Ownership NOT transferred
- Interest NOT created

**Example:**
- Unregistered sale deed (Rs. 1 crore property)
- Buyer paid full price, took possession
- But ownership NOT transferred (seller still owner)
- Buyer has NO legal title

**Consequence 2: INADMISSIBLE as Evidence**
- Cannot be produced in court as proof
- Cannot rely on unregistered document

**Example:**
- Dispute over property ownership
- Plaintiff produces unregistered sale deed
- Court: Inadmissible (Section 49)
- Plaintiff loses

**Consequence 3: NO Power Conferred**
- Power of attorney (unregistered) confers NO power
- Sale by unregistered PoA holder = VOID

**Exception - Collateral Purpose:**
- Section 49 proviso: Unregistered document can be used for COLLATERAL purpose
- Not to prove transfer, but to prove other facts

**Example (Collateral Purpose):**
- Unregistered sale deed cannot prove transfer
- BUT can prove:
  - Defendant admitted debt (if recital mentions debt)
  - Parties' relationship
  - Part payment of consideration

### 6. Section 27: Priority Between Registered and Unregistered Documents

**Section 27:**

```
"Every document registered under this Act shall take effect as against any 
unregistered document relating to the same property and executed on or after 
the date of registration."
```

**Rule: REGISTERED PREVAILS over UNREGISTERED**

**Even if:**
- Unregistered document earlier in time
- Unregistered party paid consideration
- Unregistered party took possession

**Priority Order:**

1. **Registered document** (first priority)
2. **Unregistered document** (no priority)

**Example 1:**
- 1st January: A sells property to B (sale deed NOT registered)
- 1st March: A sells same property to C (sale deed REGISTERED)
- Priority: C > B (C gets property)
- B's only remedy: Sue A for fraud/damages

**Example 2:**
- 1st January: A mortgages property to Bank B (mortgage deed NOT registered)
- 1st June: A mortgages same property to Bank C (mortgage deed REGISTERED)
- Priority: C > B (C has first charge)

**Exception - Actual Notice:**

If subsequent registered buyer/mortgagee has ACTUAL NOTICE of prior unregistered transaction:
- Courts may not grant protection
- Bona fide requirement

**Bona Fide Purchaser/Mortgagee:**
- Paid consideration
- No actual notice of prior transaction
- Section 27 protection applicable

**Practical Implication:**
- ALWAYS register documents requiring registration
- Registration gives priority protection

## Framework

### Phase 1: Pre-Registration

**Step 1: Determine Registration Requirement**

**Check if Document Falls Under Section 17 (Compulsory):**

- Sale deed (Rs. 100+)? → YES
- Gift deed? → YES
- Mortgage deed? → YES
- Lease (>12 months)? → YES
- Power of Attorney (sell/mortgage)? → YES
- Agreement to sell? → NO (optional)

**Step 2: Calculate Stamp Duty**

**Stamp Duty Varies by State:**
- Based on: Property value OR Circle rate/guidance value (whichever higher)
- Rate: 3-8% (state-specific)

**Mode of Payment:**
- E-stamping (preferred - online)
- Non-judicial stamp paper (being phased out)
- Franking

**Under-Stamping Penalty:**
- Section 33 Stamp Act + Registration Act
- Penalty: 2% per month (maximum 200% of deficit)
- Document inadmissible until deficit paid

**Step 3: Prepare Documents**

**Documents Required for Registration:**

**For Seller/Transferor:**
1. Original document (sale deed, gift deed, etc.)
2. Two photocopies
3. Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, passport)
4. Address proof
5. Previous title documents (chain)
6. Encumbrance certificate (15 years)
7. Property tax receipts
8. NOC (if applicable - society, bank, municipality)

**For Buyer/Transferee:**
1. Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN)
2. Address proof
3. PAN card (mandatory)

**For Witnesses:**
1. Identity proof (2 witnesses required)

**Step 4: Book Appointment (Online)**

- Visit state's registration department website
- Select Sub-Registrar office (jurisdiction: where property situated)
- Book slot (date and time)
- Pay online appointment fee (if applicable)

**Jurisdiction (Section 28):**
- Property location determines registering office
- If property in multiple sub-districts → Any of those offices

### Phase 2: Registration Procedure

**Section 32-35: Procedure**

**Step 1: Presentment (Section 32)**

**Who Must Appear:**
- ALL executants (parties signing document)
- Two witnesses (attesting witnesses)
- If any party absent: Special procedure (Section 32(c))

**When to Appear:**
- Within 4 months of execution
- During office hours (usually 10 AM - 5 PM)

**Where to Appear:**
- Sub-Registrar office having jurisdiction

**Section 32 - Personal Appearance:**
- Executants must appear personally
- Identification verified
- Acknowledge execution (admit signature)

**Exceptions to Personal Appearance:**
- Person abroad/sick: Attorney can appear (with notarized PoA)
- Purdahnashin woman: Special provisions (Section 40)

**Step 2: Examination by Sub-Registrar (Section 34-35)**

**Sub-Registrar Examines:**

1. **Identity of Parties** - Aadhaar, PAN, passport, physical appearance
2. **Execution** - Parties admit execution/signature
3. **Free Consent** - No coercion, fraud (Section 35)
4. **Stamp Duty** - Adequate duty paid
5. **Property Description** - Complete and accurate
6. **Witnesses** - Two witnesses present and identified

**Section 35 - Examination Questions:**
- "Did you execute this document?"
- "Is this your signature?"
- "Did you execute this voluntarily without force?"

**If Satisfied:**
- Proceed to registration
- If not satisfied: Refuse registration

**Step 3: Signature/Thumb Impression (Section 59)**

- Parties sign document before Sub-Registrar
- If illiterate: Thumb impression
- Witnesses attest

**Step 4: Payment of Registration Fee**

**Registration Fee:**
- Usually 1% of property value (varies by state)
- Example: Property Rs. 50 lakhs, registration fee Rs. 5,000

**Additional Charges:**
- Scanning charges
- Copying charges
- Miscellaneous fees

**Step 5: Registration Entry (Section 51-52)**

**Sub-Registrar:**
- Enters document details in Register
- Serial number assigned
- Date and time of registration recorded
- Document filed in office

**Section 51 - Register Maintenance:**
- Book No. 1: Details of documents registered
- Indexed by year, serial number, parties' names

**Step 6: Endorsement and Return (Section 60)**

**Endorsement on Document:**
- Registration certificate
- Serial number, date, Sub-Registrar's signature

**Return of Document:**
- Original returned to party
- Registered and endorsed

**Timeline:**
- Usually same day (if no issues)
- May take 1-7 days for certified copies

**Step 7: Collect Certified Copies (Section 57)**

**Certified Copy:**
- Exact copy of registered document
- Certified by Sub-Registrar
- Admissible as evidence (Section 47-A)

**Application:**
- Any person can apply for certified copy
- Fee payable (per page)

**Uses:**
- Property verification
- Court proceedings
- Mortgage applications

### Phase 3: Post-Registration

**Step 1: Mutation of Records**

Update revenue records:
- Revenue Department (land records - 7/12, RTC, property card)
- Municipal Corporation (property tax)
- Electricity Board, Water Department

**Step 2: Title Verification**

For future transactions:
- Encumbrance certificate (shows all registered documents)
- Search title chain (30-40 years)

## Special Provisions

### 1. Section 32(c) - Registration Without All Executants Present

**Scenario:** One party abroad/sick/unavailable

**Procedure:**

**Option 1: Agent/Attorney Appears**
- Executant executes document abroad
- Notarized by Indian Embassy/Consulate
- Attorney appears before Sub-Registrar with notarized PoA
- Attorney acknowledges execution on behalf of executant

**Option 2: Document Registered Based on Acknowledgment Before Consular Officer**
- Executant appears before Indian Consular Officer abroad
- Consular officer records acknowledgment
- Document sent to India with certificate
- Registered without executant's appearance

### 2. Section 40 - Purdahnashin Women

**Special Provision for Women Observing Purdah:**

- Sub-Registrar (or authorized officer) goes to woman's residence
- Identifies woman (through known persons)
- Takes acknowledgment
- Registers document

**Rationale:** Respect for cultural practices + ensure free consent

### 3. Section 69 - Refusal to Register

**Grounds for Refusal:**

1. **Stamp Duty Deficient** - Insufficiently stamped
2. **Not Executed by Parties** - Parties deny execution
3. **Consent Vitiated** - Coercion, fraud evident
4. **Jurisdiction** - Property not within jurisdiction
5. **Time Limit Expired** - Beyond 8 months
6. **Incomplete Document** - Missing essential clauses

**Appeal:**
- Section 72: Appeal to District Registrar
- Further appeal to High Court

**Mandamus:**
- If refusal illegal/arbitrary
- Writ of mandamus in High Court

### 4. Section 47-A - Admissibility of Certified Copies

**Section 47-A:**

```
"Certified copies of registered documents shall be admissible in evidence 
without production of original."
```

**Practical Importance:**
- Original document may be lost/destroyed
- Certified copy from Sub-Registrar office = Sufficient
- Court accepts certified copy

**Procedure to Obtain Certified Copy:**

1. Apply to Sub-Registrar (where registered)
2. Provide document details (serial number, year, parties)
3. Pay fee (per page)
4. Collect certified copy (usually 1-3 days)

**Any Person Can Apply:**
- Not restricted to parties
- Third parties (e.g., prospective buyers) can obtain

### 5. Section 77 - Cancellation/Rectification

**Cancellation of Document:**

**Grounds:**
- Document registered by fraud/mistake
- Consent vitiated
- Property description incorrect

**Procedure:**
- File suit in civil court
- Obtain decree canceling registration
- Decree registered (cancels original registration)

**Rectification:**
- Minor errors/omissions
- Supplementary deed executed and registered

## Common Scenarios and Solutions

### Scenario 1: Sale Deed Executed But Not Registered Within 4 Months

**Facts:**
- Sale deed executed on 1st January 2025
- Stamp duty paid
- Buyer took possession
- Now 1st July 2025 (6 months elapsed)
- Registration not done

**Legal Position:**

**Time Limit:**
- 4 months: 1st January to 1st May (expired)
- Extension available: Up to 8 months (1st September 2025)

**Remedy:**
- Apply for late registration (Section 25)
- Pay late fee/penalty
- Register by 1st September 2025

**After 8 Months:**
- If not registered by 1st September 2025:
  - Registration REFUSED
  - Sale deed VOID (Section 49)
  - Buyer has NO title
  - Must execute FRESH sale deed

**Prevention:**
- Register within 4 months
- Set reminders, appoint lawyer to follow up

---

### Scenario 2: Unregistered Sale Deed vs Registered Sale Deed (Same Property)

**Facts:**
- 1st March: A sells property to B (Rs. 80 lakhs, sale deed NOT registered)
- B pays full price, takes possession
- 1st August: A sells same property to C (Rs. 70 lakhs, sale deed REGISTERED)
- C has no knowledge of B's purchase

**Legal Position:**

**Section 27 - Priority Rule:**
- Registered document (C) prevails over unregistered (B)
- Even though B earlier in time and paid more

**Outcome:**
- C gets property (registered sale deed)
- B loses property (unregistered sale deed void)

**B's Remedies:**
- Sue A for:
  - Fraud (Section 420 IPC)
  - Breach of contract (damages - Section 73 Contract Act)
  - Return of Rs. 80 lakhs + interest
- Civil suit for damages
- Criminal complaint (cheating)

**B Cannot Get Property:**
- Section 49: Unregistered document void
- Section 27: C's registration creates priority

**Lesson:**
- ALWAYS register sale deed immediately
- Registration is not formality - it's ESSENTIAL

---

### Scenario 3: Agreement to Sell (Unregistered) vs Sale Deed (Registered)

**Facts:**
- 1st January: A agrees to sell property to B (agreement to sell, NOT registered)
- B pays advance Rs. 20 lakhs (out of Rs. 1 crore)
- 1st June: A sells property to C (registered sale deed)
- C had no notice of B's agreement

**Legal Position:**

**Agreement to Sell:**
- Optional registration (Section 18)
- B did not register
- No constructive notice to C

**Sale Deed to C:**
- Registered (Section 17 compulsory)
- C becomes owner

**Priority:**
- Section 27: Registered sale deed (C) prevails
- Unregistered agreement (B) creates only personal right (not proprietary)

**B's Remedies:**
- Sue A for:
  - Specific performance (compel A to complete sale to B)
  - If specific performance not granted: Damages
  - Return of advance Rs. 20 lakhs + interest

**But B Cannot Get Property:**
- C already registered owner
- B's remedy limited to damages from A

**Prevention for B:**
- Register agreement to sell (creates notice)
- Take possession (Section 53A part performance protection)
- Complete sale quickly

**Prevention for C:**
- Title verification (encumbrance certificate shows registered documents only)
- Physical inspection (check if anyone else in possession)
- Ask seller to warrant no prior agreements

### Scenario 4: Stamp Duty Deficiency Discovered During Registration

**Facts:**
- Property value: Rs. 1 crore (as per circle rate)
- Sale deed shows consideration: Rs. 60 lakhs
- Stamp duty paid: 5% of Rs. 60 lakhs = Rs. 3 lakhs
- Sub-Registrar discovers deficiency (should be on Rs. 1 crore)

**Legal Position:**

**Stamp Duty Payable:**
- On HIGHER of:
  - Agreement value (Rs. 60 lakhs), OR
  - Circle rate/guidance value (Rs. 1 crore)
- Correct stamp duty: 5% of Rs. 1 crore = Rs. 5 lakhs
- Deficiency: Rs. 2 lakhs

**Sub-Registrar Action:**
- Refuse registration until deficiency paid
- OR register with deficiency notice

**Penalty:**
- Section 33 Stamp Act: 2% per month (max 200% of deficit)
- Immediate payment: Rs. 2 lakhs
- If delayed (say 6 months): Rs. 2 lakhs + (Rs. 2 lakhs × 2% × 6) = Rs. 2.24 lakhs

**Parties' Options:**

**Option 1: Pay Deficiency Immediately**
- Pay Rs. 2 lakhs + penalty
- Complete registration

**Option 2: Contest Circle Rate**
- Challenge circle rate (if excessive)
- Adjudication by Collector
- Risk: Delay, registration refused if circle rate upheld

**Prevention:**
- Check circle rate before execution
- Pay stamp duty on circle rate (even if agreement value lower)

### Scenario 5: Power of Attorney Sale - Registration Issues

**Facts:**
- A gives General Power of Attorney to Agent B (to sell property)
- B executes sale deed in favor of C
- B signs as "Attorney for A"
- Sale deed presented for registration

**Legal Position:**

**Section 17(1)(e) - PoA Registration:**
- Power of Attorney for sale must be REGISTERED
- If PoA not registered → Agent has NO power
- Sale by unregistered PoA holder = VOID

**Registration Procedure:**

1. **PoA Must Be Registered First**
   - A (principal) registers PoA in favor of B
   - PoA gives B power to sell

2. **Sale Deed Signed by Agent**
   - B signs sale deed as "Attorney for A"
   - Sale deed must recite PoA details (registration number, date)

3. **Registration of Sale Deed**
   - Sale deed registered
   - Sub-Registrar verifies PoA registered
   - If PoA not registered: Refuse sale deed registration

**Suraj Lamp Principle (SC 2012):**
- PoA sale (without registered PoA + sale deed in principal's name) is NOT valid sale
- Agent cannot transfer property in his own name
- Only principal (A) can transfer property

**Valid PoA Sale Requirements:**
1. PoA registered
2. Sale deed executed in PRINCIPAL'S name (A), signed by agent (B as attorney)
3. Sale deed registered

### Scenario 6: Rectification of Registered Document (Typographical Error)

**Facts:**
- Sale deed registered
- Property description contains typographical error (Survey No. 123 written as 132)
- Parties want to rectify

**Options:**

**Option 1: Supplementary Deed (Section 17(2))**
- Execute supplementary deed correcting error
- Refer to original sale deed
- Recite correction
- Register supplementary deed (compulsory - Section 17(2))

**Option 2: Court Decree**
- File suit for rectification
- Obtain decree correcting error
- Register decree

**Option 3: Endorsement (Minor Errors)**
- Apply to Sub-Registrar
- If minor clerical error (typo)
- Sub-Registrar may endorse correction

**Preferred Method:**
- Supplementary deed (quickest, simplest)

**Sample Supplementary Deed Clause:**

```markdown
WHEREAS by a deed of sale dated [Date] and registered as Document No. [Number] 
at the office of Sub-Registrar, [Place], the Vendor sold to the Purchaser the 
property described therein;

AND WHEREAS due to a clerical error, the Survey Number of the said property 
was mentioned as 132 instead of the correct Survey Number 123;

NOW THIS SUPPLEMENTARY DEED WITNESSETH that the parties hereby agree that the 
correct Survey Number of the said property is 123, and the said sale deed is 
hereby rectified accordingly.
```

## Integration with Other Protocols

### Property Transfer Integration

**All Property Transfer Documents:**
- Sale, gift, mortgage, lease, easement, partition
- Registration requirements as per Section 17
- Non-registration consequences (Section 49)

### Civil Procedure Integration

**Suits Involving Registered Documents:**
- Certified copies admissible (Section 47-A)
- Original not required
- Presumption of genuineness

**Territorial Jurisdiction:**
- Immovable property suits: Where property situated (Section 16 CPC)
- Registration also: Where property situated (Section 28 Registration Act)

### Evidence Act Integration

**Section 47-A Registration Act + Section 79 Evidence Act:**
- Certified copy of registered document = Public document
- Admissible without proof of execution

### Stamp Act Integration

**Stamp Duty + Registration:**
- Both required for immovable property transfers
- Stamp duty paid BEFORE registration
- Under-stamping: Penalty + Inadmissibility

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Not Registering Compulsory Document (Section 17)
**Consequence:** Document VOID; no transfer of title
**Solution:** ALWAYS register documents listed in Section 17

### 2. Delaying Registration Beyond 4 Months
**Consequence:** Late fee/penalty; after 8 months, registration refused
**Solution:** Register within 4 months of execution

### 3. Under-Stamping (Paying Less Stamp Duty)
**Consequence:** Registration refused OR penalty (200% of deficit)
**Solution:** Pay stamp duty on circle rate (if higher than agreement value)

### 4. Assuming Unregistered Document Has Legal Effect
**Consequence:** No transfer, inadmissible as evidence
**Solution:** Understand Section 49 - unregistered document void

### 5. Relying on Possession Alone (Without Registration)
**Consequence:** Subsequent registered buyer gets priority
**Solution:** Register deed + take possession

### 6. Not Verifying Registered PoA Before Accepting PoA Sale
**Consequence:** PoA sale void if PoA not registered
**Solution:** Verify PoA registered before accepting sale by agent

### 7. Not Obtaining Certified Copies for Records
**Consequence:** Original lost/damaged, difficult to prove transaction
**Solution:** Obtain certified copies immediately after registration

### 8. Ignoring Registration of Supplementary/Rectification Deed
**Consequence:** Rectification not effective
**Solution:** Register supplementary deed (Section 17(2) compulsory)

### 9. Not Checking Encumbrance Certificate Before Purchase
**Consequence:** Discover prior registered mortgages/charges/litigation later
**Solution:** Obtain 15-year encumbrance certificate; verify all registered documents

### 10. Assuming Agreement to Sell Protects Against Double Sale
**Consequence:** Unregistered agreement gives no priority; registered buyer wins
**Solution:** Register agreement to sell if substantial advance paid
