# AUDIT COMMITTEE - INDIAN CORPORATE LAW

## OVERVIEW

The Audit Committee is the cornerstone of corporate governance, serving as an independent oversight body for financial reporting, internal controls, risk management, and regulatory compliance. Introduced as mandatory for listed companies under Clause 49 of Listing Agreement (2000) and extended to all public companies by the Companies Act 2013, the Audit Committee protects shareholder interests through independent financial scrutiny.

**PURPOSE**:
- Ensure integrity and reliability of financial statements
- Oversee statutory and internal audit functions
- Review internal control systems and risk management
- Approve related party transactions
- Establish whistleblower mechanism for reporting concerns

**LEGAL FRAMEWORK**:
- **Companies Act 2013**: Section 177 (Audit Committee constitution and functions)
- **Companies (Meetings of Board and its Powers) Rules 2014**: Rule 6 (Audit Committee procedures)
- **SEBI LODR Regulations 2015**: Regulation 18 (for listed companies - stricter requirements)
- **Secretarial Standards SS-3**: Secretarial Standard on Audit Committee (ICSI)

## 1. MANDATORY CONSTITUTION (Section 177)

### 1.1 Companies Required to Have Audit Committee

**Section 177(1)**: Following companies MUST constitute Audit Committee:

```
MANDATORY AUDIT COMMITTEE:

1. ALL LISTED COMPANIES (public or private listed on stock exchanges)

2. ALL PUBLIC COMPANIES with:
   - Paid-up share capital ≥₹10 CRORE, OR
   - Turnover ≥₹100 CRORE, OR
   - Outstanding loans/borrowings/debentures/deposits ≥₹50 CRORE
```

**Examples**:

```
Company A: Public Ltd, paid-up capital ₹15 crore, turnover ₹80 crore, no borrowings
→ Audit Committee MANDATORY (paid-up capital ≥₹10 crore)

Company B: Public Ltd, paid-up capital ₹8 crore, turnover ₹120 crore, borrowings ₹30 crore
→ Audit Committee MANDATORY (turnover ≥₹100 crore)

Company C: Public Ltd, paid-up capital ₹5 crore, turnover ₹60 crore, borrowings ₹55 crore
→ Audit Committee MANDATORY (borrowings ≥₹50 crore)

Company D: Private Ltd, paid-up capital ₹50 crore, turnover ₹200 crore
→ Audit Committee NOT MANDATORY (Section 177 applies only to public companies, except listed)

Company E: Private Ltd (listed on BSE), paid-up capital ₹3 crore
→ Audit Committee MANDATORY (all listed companies, regardless of size)
```

### 1.2 Voluntary Audit Committee

**Companies Not Required**:
- Private companies (unless listed)
- Public companies below all thresholds (paid-up <₹10 crore AND turnover <₹100 crore AND borrowings <₹50 crore)

**Voluntary Constitution**:
- Even if not mandatory, companies may voluntarily constitute Audit Committee
- Best practice for good governance (especially if seeking PE/VC funding)
- Investor comfort (demonstrates strong financial controls)

## 2. COMPOSITION AND QUALIFICATIONS

### 2.1 Composition Requirements (Section 177(2))

**Statutory Composition**:
```
Section 177(2): Audit Committee shall consist of:
- MINIMUM 3 DIRECTORS
- MAJORITY: Non-executive directors
- For LISTED COMPANIES: At least 2/3rd (66.7%) INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS
- All members: Financially literate (ability to read and understand financial statements)
```

**Financially Literate**:
- Ability to read and understand financial statements (balance sheet, P&L, cash flow)
- No formal CA/CFA qualification required (but desirable)
- Can be demonstrated through experience (CFO, finance professional, CA, MBA Finance)

**Example - Composition for Listed Company**:
```
Board: 8 directors (5 executive, 3 independent)

Audit Committee: 3 directors
- 2 independent directors (2/3 = 66.7%) ✓
- 1 non-executive director (not independent)
- All 3 directors financially literate ✓

Composition COMPLIANT ✓

If Audit Committee: 1 independent + 2 non-executive
- 1/3 = 33.3% independent directors
- NON-COMPLIANT ✗ (listed companies need 2/3 independent)
```

**Example - Composition for Unlisted Public Company**:
```
Board: 6 directors (4 executive, 2 non-executive)

Audit Committee: 3 directors
- 1 executive director → NON-COMPLIANT ✗ (majority must be non-executive)
- 2 non-executive directors

Corrected Composition:
- 3 non-executive directors (100% non-executive) ✓
- All financially literate ✓

Composition COMPLIANT ✓
```

### 2.2 Chairperson of Audit Committee (Section 177(3))

**Statutory Requirement**:
```
Section 177(3): Chairperson of Audit Committee shall be INDEPENDENT DIRECTOR
```

**Rationale**: Independent chairperson ensures objective oversight (not influenced by management).

**Chairperson's Role**:
1. Preside over Audit Committee meetings
2. Set agenda (in consultation with CFO, Statutory Auditor, Internal Auditor)
3. Ensure all items are discussed thoroughly
4. Report to Board on Audit Committee's findings and recommendations
5. Attend AGM to answer shareholder queries on audit matters

**Best Practice**: Chairperson should have finance/accounting background (CA, CFA, MBA Finance, CFO experience).

### 2.3 Disqualifications and Restrictions

**Cannot be Audit Committee Member**:
1. **Executive Director**: No executive director (including MD, CEO, Whole-Time Director) can be member
2. **Related to Auditors**: Person related to statutory auditor (audit firm partner's relative)
3. **Financially Illiterate**: Person unable to understand financial statements

**Cooling-Off Period (SEBI LODR)**:
- For listed companies: If independent director serves as Audit Committee chairperson for 5 consecutive years, 1-year cooling-off before reappointment as chairperson
- Can continue as member (just not chairperson)

### 2.4 Quorum for Audit Committee Meetings (Rule 6)

**Quorum**: EITHER:
- **2 members**, OR
- **1/3rd of total members**, WHICHEVER IS GREATER

**Majority**: Must include at least 1 independent director (if committee has independent directors)

**Examples**:
```
Audit Committee: 3 members (2 independent, 1 non-executive)
Quorum: Greater of (2 OR 3/3 = 1) = 2 members
Majority Requirement: At least 1 independent director present

Valid Meeting:
- Present: 2 independent directors → Quorum present ✓, Majority satisfied ✓

Invalid Meeting:
- Present: 1 non-executive director (not independent) → Quorum NOT present ✗
```

## 3. POWERS OF AUDIT COMMITTEE (Section 177(5))

### 3.1 Statutory Powers

**Section 177(5)**: Audit Committee shall have powers to:

```
(a) INVESTIGATE any activity within its terms of reference
    - Can investigate financial irregularities, fraud, non-compliance
    - No prior board approval required for investigation

(b) SEEK INFORMATION from any employee
    - All employees must cooperate with Audit Committee
    - Refusal to provide information = misconduct

(c) OBTAIN OUTSIDE LEGAL OR OTHER PROFESSIONAL ADVICE
    - Can engage external consultants, lawyers, forensic auditors
    - Company bears cost of professional advice

(d) SECURE ATTENDANCE of outsiders with relevant expertise
    - Can call statutory auditor, internal auditor, CFO, cost auditor
    - Outsider must attend if summoned
```

**Example - Investigation Power**:
```
Scenario: Whistleblower complaint alleges CFO is inflating revenues by recognizing fake sales.

Audit Committee Action:
1. Invoke investigation power (Section 177(5)(a))
2. Engage forensic auditor (external expert under Section 177(5)(c))
3. Summon CFO, Finance Manager, Sales Head for questioning (Section 177(5)(b))
4. Review contracts, invoices, bank statements (Section 177(5)(b) - seek information)
5. Forensic auditor report: Confirms ₹5 crore fake revenues over 2 years
6. Audit Committee recommendation to Board:
   - Terminate CFO
   - Restate financial statements
   - Report to SFIO (Serious Fraud Investigation Office)
   - Strengthen internal controls to prevent recurrence
```

### 3.2 Access to Information

**Unrestricted Access**:
- Financial records, accounting books, ledgers
- Contracts, agreements, purchase orders
- Internal audit reports, compliance reports
- Emails, communications (with board approval for employee emails)

**Statutory Auditor Access**:
- Audit Committee can meet statutory auditor WITHOUT management present
- Candid discussion of concerns (management pressure, accounting disputes)
- Section 143(12): Statutory auditor can directly report fraud to Audit Committee

**Internal Auditor Reporting**:
- Internal auditor reports to Audit Committee (not to CFO/management)
- Ensures independence of internal audit function

## 4. FUNCTIONS OF AUDIT COMMITTEE (Section 177(4))

### 4.1 Oversight of Financial Reporting

**Section 177(4)(i)**: Recommendation for appointment, remuneration, terms of appointment of auditors.

**Section 177(4)(ii)**: Review and monitor auditor independence and performance.

**Section 177(4)(iii)**: Approval or modification of transactions with related parties (RPTs).

**Section 177(4)(iv)**: Scrutiny of inter-corporate loans and investments.

**Section 177(4)(v)**: Valuation of undertakings or assets (for M&A, capital raising).

**Section 177(4)(vi)**: Evaluation of internal financial controls and risk management systems.

**Section 177(4)(vii)**: Monitoring end-use of funds raised through public offers or rights issues.

### 4.2 Detailed Functional Responsibilities

#### 4.2.1 Financial Statement Review

**Pre-Audit Review**:
- Review draft financial statements BEFORE board approval
- Examine significant accounting policies, estimates, judgments
- Question management on accounting treatments, revenue recognition
- Verify compliance with Accounting Standards (Ind AS / AS)

**Quarterly Results (Listed Companies)**:
- Review limited review report by auditor
- Analyze variance from budget, prior period, guidance
- Scrutinize one-time items, exceptional items, discontinued operations

**Annual Accounts**:
- Review annual financial statements, Board's Report, notes to accounts
- Discuss audit observations, qualifications (if any) with statutory auditor
- Ensure all statutory disclosures made (RPTs, contingent liabilities, capital commitments)

**Example - Audit Committee Review**:
```
Audit Committee Meeting: June 20, 2024 (Q1 FY 2024-25 Results Review)

Present: 3 members (2 independent directors including chairperson, 1 non-executive director)
Invitees: CFO, Statutory Auditor, Internal Auditor

Agenda: Review Q1 FY 2024-25 financial results

Key Discussion Points:

1. REVENUE RECOGNITION:
   - CFO: Q1 revenue ₹50 crore (up 25% YoY)
   - Committee Question: Driver of 25% growth?
   - CFO: New product launch + price increase (10%)
   - Statutory Auditor: Revenue recognition policy compliant with Ind AS 115 ✓

2. INVENTORY WRITE-DOWN:
   - CFO: Inventory write-down ₹2 crore (slow-moving raw material)
   - Committee: Adequate write-down? Any further exposure?
   - CFO: Remaining inventory adequate coverage, no further write-down expected
   - Committee: Satisfied ✓

3. RELATED PARTY TRANSACTION:
   - CFO: ₹1.5 crore purchase from director's company (within omnibus approval)
   - Committee: Verify arms-length pricing
   - CFO: Market rate ₹1.4-1.6 crore, transaction at ₹1.5 crore (midpoint) ✓
   - Committee: Approved ✓

4. AUDITOR COMMENTS:
   - Statutory Auditor: Limited review completed, no qualifications
   - Auditor: Suggests strengthening revenue cut-off procedures (prevent premature recognition)
   - Committee: Directs management to implement auditor's suggestion by Q2

RECOMMENDATION TO BOARD:
Audit Committee recommends approval of Q1 FY 2024-25 financial results, subject to management implementing auditor's suggestion on revenue cut-off controls.

Approved Unanimously: 3/3 members
```

#### 4.2.2 Auditor Appointment and Independence

**Auditor Appointment (Section 177(4)(i))**:
- Audit Committee recommends auditor appointment, reappointment, removal
- Assesses auditor qualifications, experience, industry expertise
- Recommends audit fee (adequate for scope, not so low as to compromise quality)
- Board approves based on Audit Committee recommendation
- Shareholders appoint auditor at AGM

**Auditor Independence Review**:
- Verify auditor independence (no financial interest in company, no business relationship)
- Review non-audit services provided by auditor (tax, advisory) - ensure not >audit fee (SEBI cap 100% for listed companies)
- Rotation: Audit partner rotates every 5 years, audit firm rotates every 10 years (listed companies)

**Example - Auditor Independence Issue**:
```
Scenario: Statutory auditor (Audit Firm A) also provides tax advisory services. Audit fee: ₹20 lakh. Tax advisory fee: ₹30 lakh (150% of audit fee).

Audit Committee Review:
- Non-audit fees (₹30 lakh) > Audit fees (₹20 lakh) → Independence concern
- SEBI LODR Regulation 24: Non-audit fees should not exceed audit fees for listed companies
- Audit Committee Action:
  1. Discuss with auditor: Can audit fee be increased to ₹30 lakh? (No, audit scope unchanged)
  2. Option: Separate tax advisory engagement to different firm
  3. Decision: Engage different firm for tax advisory (maintain auditor independence)
  4. Going forward: Non-audit fees capped at 100% of audit fees
```

#### 4.2.3 Related Party Transaction Approval

**Section 177(4)(iv)**: ALL related party transactions require Audit Committee approval BEFORE board approval.

**Audit Committee Review Process**:
1. **Identification**: CFO submits all proposed RPTs for quarter
2. **Classification**: Ordinary course + arms-length OR material transaction
3. **Pricing Verification**: Market benchmarking, independent valuation (if material)
4. **Commercial Rationale**: Why is company entering into RPT? Benefit to company?
5. **Conflict Assessment**: Impact on minority shareholders
6. **Recommendation**: Approve / Reject / Approve with conditions

**Omnibus Approval**:
- Audit Committee can grant omnibus approval for repetitive RPTs (e.g., regular purchases within limits)
- Validity: 1 year maximum
- Quarterly review: Verify transactions within omnibus approval limits

**Example - RPT Approval**:
```
RPT: Purchase of IT services from director's company (₹40 lakh per annum)

Audit Committee Review:
1. Ordinary Course: IT services in ordinary course ✓ (all companies need IT support)
2. Arms-Length Pricing:
   - Related party charges: ₹40 lakh for 3 IT personnel full-time
   - Market rate: ₹12-15 lakh per IT personnel per annum → ₹36-45 lakh for 3 personnel
   - Related party pricing within market range ✓
3. Alternative Options: Can company hire IT personnel directly? Cost comparison?
   - Direct hiring: ₹36 lakh salary + ₹6 lakh infrastructure = ₹42 lakh
   - Related party: ₹40 lakh (marginally cheaper, no infrastructure investment)
4. Commercial Rationale: Outsourcing more flexible (scale up/down), no long-term employment commitment
5. Conflict: Director benefits (profit from IT services company), but transaction at arms-length, so acceptable risk

RECOMMENDATION: Approve subject to:
(a) Annual contract (renewable)
(b) Quarterly review (verify continued arms-length pricing)
(c) 90-day termination notice by either party
```

#### 4.2.4 Internal Audit and Control Systems

**Internal Audit Oversight**:
- Approve internal audit plan, scope, frequency
- Review internal audit reports (findings, management responses, action plans)
- Ensure significant findings addressed promptly
- Assess adequacy of internal audit function (staff, budget, training)

**Internal Financial Controls (Section 134(5)(e))**:
- Board's Report must state that adequate internal financial controls exist and are operating effectively
- Audit Committee reviews internal financial controls (entity-level, process-level, IT controls)
- Tests controls for design and operating effectiveness

**Risk Management**:
- Review risk management policy and framework
- Identify top risks (financial, operational, compliance, strategic, reputational)
- Assess mitigation strategies for each risk
- Monitor risk indicators (early warning signals)

**Example - Internal Audit Findings**:
```
Internal Audit Report: Procurement Process Audit (Q2 FY 2024-25)

Finding 1: Purchase orders >₹10 lakh approved by single signatory (policy requires 2 signatories)
Risk: Fraud, unauthorized purchases
Management Response: Acknowledged, implementing dual signatory for >₹10 lakh by September 2024

Finding 2: Vendor payments made without 3-way matching (PO, GRN, Invoice)
Risk: Payments for goods not received, duplicate payments
Management Response: 3-way matching mandatory from August 2024, ERP configured

Finding 3: 15% of vendors not KYC-verified (bank details, GST, PAN)
Risk: Payments to fraudulent vendors, tax compliance issues
Management Response: KYC drive initiated, all vendors to be verified by October 2024

Audit Committee Action:
- Follow-up review in Q3 (verify management has implemented corrective actions)
- CFO to present implementation status at next Audit Committee meeting
- If not implemented, escalate to Board (accountability for CFO)
```

#### 4.2.5 Fraud Reporting and Whistleblower Mechanism

**Section 177(9)**: Audit Committee must establish Vigil Mechanism (Whistleblower Policy) for:
- Directors and employees to report concerns (unethical behavior, fraud, misuse of office)
- Adequate safeguards against victimization of whistleblower
- Direct access to Audit Committee chairperson (bypassing management)

**Whistleblower Policy Components**:
1. **Reporting Channels**: Email, hotline, postal address (anonymous reporting allowed)
2. **Protected Disclosures**: Fraud, financial irregularities, misappropriation, RPT violations, Code of Conduct violations
3. **Confidentiality**: Whistleblower identity protected (disclosed only if legally required)
4. **Non-Retaliation**: No adverse action against whistleblower (termination, demotion, harassment)
5. **Investigation**: Audit Committee investigates or appoints investigator
6. **Outcome**: Action against perpetrator, corrective measures, feedback to whistleblower (if not anonymous)

**Section 143(12)**: Statutory auditor must report fraud to Audit Committee (or Board if no Audit Committee).

**Example - Whistleblower Complaint**:
```
Whistleblower Complaint (Anonymous Email to Audit Committee Chairperson):

Subject: Expense Fraud by Sales Manager

"The Sales Manager (Mr. X) is submitting fake travel and entertainment expenses. He is claiming hotel stays and dinners that never occurred. I have evidence (hotel confirms no booking under his name for dates claimed). Estimated fraud: ₹5 lakh over last 6 months."

Audit Committee Action:
1. Acknowledge receipt (if whistleblower provided email, send acknowledgment maintaining confidentiality)
2. Appoint investigation team: Internal Auditor + HR Head
3. Investigation findings:
   - Verified 12 instances of fake expenses (hotels, restaurants confirm no booking/bill)
   - Total fraud: ₹4.8 lakh over 6 months
   - Mr. X confessed when confronted with evidence
4. Audit Committee Recommendation to Board:
   - Terminate Mr. X (gross misconduct, breach of trust)
   - Recover ₹4.8 lakh from Mr. X (legal action if not paid)
   - Strengthen expense approval controls (require supporting documents, random audits)
   - Police complaint (if Board decides to prosecute - embezzlement under IPC Section 403)
5. Protect Whistleblower:
   - Ensure no retaliation (monitor for 6 months)
   - Communicate outcome to whistleblower (if email provided): "Thank you for bringing this to our attention. Investigation completed, appropriate action taken against perpetrator."
```

## 5. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Frequency of Meetings (Section 177(8))

**Minimum Meetings**:
```
Section 177(8): Audit Committee shall meet at least FOUR TIMES per year
- Maximum gap between two meetings: 120 DAYS (same as board meetings)
```

**Typical Meeting Schedule**:
```
Q1: July (Review Q1 results)
Q2: October (Review Q2 results, half-year audit)
Q3: January (Review Q3 results, approve internal audit plan for next year)
Q4: April (Review annual accounts, auditor observations, Board's Report)
```

**Additional Meetings**:
- As required for urgent matters (fraud investigation, auditor concerns, RPT approval)

### 5.2 Meeting Procedures

**Notice**:
- Minimum 7 days notice (same as board meetings)
- Shorter notice allowed with consent of all members

**Agenda**:
- Prepared by Company Secretary in consultation with Audit Committee chairperson
- Circulated 3-5 days before meeting (with board papers)

**Invitees (Non-Voting)**:
- CFO (mandatory invitee)
- Statutory Auditor (at least 4 times per year)
- Internal Auditor (at least 4 times per year)
- CEO/MD (as required)
- Functional heads (as required for specific agenda items)

**Minutes**:
- Minutes recorded and signed by Audit Committee chairperson
- Placed before Board at next board meeting
- Audit Committee chairperson reports key findings/recommendations to Board

**Example - Audit Committee Meeting Agenda**:
```
AUDIT COMMITTEE MEETING AGENDA

Date: July 20, 2024
Time: 2:00 PM
Venue: Registered Office

PRESENT:
- Independent Director 1 (Chairperson)
- Independent Director 2
- Non-Executive Director 3

INVITEES:
- CFO
- Statutory Auditor (Partner, Audit Firm A)
- Internal Auditor (Head of Internal Audit)
- Company Secretary (recording minutes)

AGENDA:

1. Confirmation of minutes of previous meeting (April 15, 2024)

2. Review of Q1 FY 2024-25 Financial Results
   - CFO presentation (15 min)
   - Statutory Auditor limited review report
   - Discussion: Key accounting judgments, estimates

3. Related Party Transactions Review
   - RPTs entered in Q1 (within omnibus approval)
   - Verification of arms-length pricing
   - New RPTs proposed for Q2 (approval sought)

4. Internal Audit Report (Q1)
   - Internal Auditor presentation
   - Management response to findings
   - Status of prior quarter findings (closure verification)

5. Statutory Auditor - Private Discussion (Management exits)
   - Any concerns regarding management, financials, controls?
   - Auditor independence, non-audit services

6. Fraud Reporting
   - Any fraud/irregularities reported under Section 143(12)?
   - Whistleblower complaints received (if any)

7. Any other matter with permission of chair

Meeting adjourned at 4:30 PM

Next Meeting: October 18, 2024 (Q2 Results Review)
```

### 5.3 Attendance Requirements

**Directors' Attendance**:
- Members expected to attend all meetings
- Poor attendance = ineffective oversight
- If director misses 2 consecutive meetings without leave, Board may consider replacement

**Chairperson Attendance at AGM (Section 177(8))**:
```
Section 177(8): Chairperson of Audit Committee shall attend AGM to answer shareholder queries
- Shareholders can ask questions on:
  1. Auditor appointment, remuneration, qualifications
  2. Financial statement items, accounting policies
  3. Internal controls, risk management
  4. Fraud, irregularities, whistleblower complaints (if disclosed)
```

**Example - AGM Questions to Audit Committee Chairperson**:
```
Shareholder Question 1: "The auditor has qualified the accounts regarding inventory valuation. What is the Audit Committee's view? Should we be concerned?"

Audit Committee Chairperson Response:
"The auditor's qualification pertains to ₹3 crore of slow-moving inventory. Management values it at ₹3 crore (cost), auditor believes fair value is ₹1.5 crore (50% write-down). The Audit Committee has reviewed this matter in detail. We obtained an independent valuation, which estimates fair value at ₹2-2.5 crore. We have asked management to recognize a ₹1 crore write-down in Q1 FY 2024-25. Auditor has accepted this approach. We are monitoring inventory levels closely to prevent recurrence."

Shareholder Question 2: "What steps has the Audit Committee taken to prevent fraud after the recent procurement fraud incident?"

Audit Committee Chairperson Response:
"We conducted a comprehensive review of procurement controls. Key steps implemented: (1) Dual signatory for purchases >₹10 lakh, (2) Mandatory 3-way matching (PO, GRN, Invoice), (3) Vendor KYC verification, (4) Random audit of 10% of transactions monthly, (5) Whistleblower mechanism strengthened. Internal audit is monitoring implementation. We are confident these controls significantly reduce fraud risk."
```

## 6. RELATIONSHIP WITH STATUTORY AUDITOR

### 6.1 Auditor Appointment Process

**Step 1: Audit Committee Recommendation (Section 177(4)(i))**:
- Audit Committee evaluates auditor candidates (qualifications, experience, independence)
- Assesses audit fee proposal (adequate for scope, competitive)
- Recommends auditor to Board

**Step 2: Board Approval (Section 139(1))**:
- Board approves auditor based on Audit Committee recommendation
- Board recommends auditor to shareholders

**Step 3: Shareholder Appointment (Section 139(1))**:
- Shareholders appoint auditor at AGM (ordinary resolution)
- Auditor holds office till conclusion of next AGM

**First Auditor Appointment**:
- Section 139(6): Board appoints first auditor within 30 days of incorporation (till first AGM)
- Audit Committee recommends (if constituted at incorporation)

**Reappointment and Rotation**:
- Listed companies: Audit firm rotates every 10 years, audit partner rotates every 5 years
- Unlisted companies: No rotation (can continue indefinitely if shareholders approve)

### 6.2 Auditor Independence

**Threats to Independence**:
1. **Financial Interest**: Auditor holds shares in company
2. **Business Relationship**: Auditor provides significant non-audit services (fees >audit fees)
3. **Employment Relationship**: Auditor's relative employed in company (key position)
4. **Familiarity**: Audit partner serves >5 years (becomes too familiar, loses objectivity)
5. **Intimidation**: Management pressures auditor (threatens to change auditor if unfavorable opinion)

**Audit Committee's Role**:
- Review auditor's independence declaration (submitted annually)
- Monitor non-audit services, cap fees (SEBI: non-audit fees ≤100% audit fees for listed companies)
- Ensure partner rotation every 5 years
- Provide safe channel for auditor to raise concerns (private discussion without management)

**Section 143(12) - Auditor's Fraud Reporting Duty**:
```
If auditor discovers fraud:
1. Report to Audit Committee IMMEDIATELY (within 2 days)
2. If Audit Committee does not act within 45 days → Auditor reports to Central Government (Ministry of Corporate Affairs)
3. Fraud ≥₹1 crore: Auditor MUST report to Central Government (even if Audit Committee acts)
```

**Example - Auditor Reports Fraud**:
```
Scenario: Statutory auditor discovers CFO is diverting funds via fake vendor payments (₹2 crore fraud).

Day 1: Auditor completes investigation, confirms fraud
Day 2: Auditor reports to Audit Committee chairperson (email + meeting)
Day 3: Audit Committee meeting convened (urgent)
Day 5: Audit Committee appoints forensic auditor (external expert)
Day 20: Forensic audit confirms ₹2 crore fraud
Day 25: Audit Committee recommends to Board: Terminate CFO, file police complaint, recover funds
Day 30: Board approves recommendations, CFO terminated, police complaint filed

Auditor's Duty:
- Fraud ≥₹1 crore → MUST report to Central Government (Section 143(12)(c)(ii))
- Day 35: Auditor files Form ADT-4 with Ministry of Corporate Affairs (SFIO investigation triggered)
```

### 6.3 Management Representation Letter

**Auditor's Requirement**:
- Before signing audit opinion, auditor obtains Management Representation Letter
- Management confirms: Financial statements accurate, all information disclosed, no fraud concealed

**Audit Committee's Role**:
- Review draft Management Representation Letter before CFO signs
- Ensure representations are accurate and not misleading
- If Audit Committee disagrees with management's representations → Discuss with auditor

**Example - Management Representation Letter**:
```
MANAGEMENT REPRESENTATION LETTER

To: M/s. Audit Firm A (Statutory Auditor)
Date: May 15, 2024

Subject: Representations for Audit of FY 2023-24 Financial Statements

Dear Auditor,

We, the undersigned, confirm the following representations:

1. RESPONSIBILITY: We acknowledge responsibility for preparation of financial statements in accordance with Ind AS.

2. COMPLETENESS: All financial records, supporting documents, and information have been made available to you.

3. TRANSACTIONS: All transactions are recorded in accounting records and reflected in financial statements.

4. FRAUD: We are not aware of any fraud involving management or employees with significant role in internal controls.

5. LITIGATION: All actual and potential litigation disclosed in Note 25 (contingent liabilities ₹3 crore).

6. RELATED PARTIES: All related parties and RPTs disclosed in Note 30 (₹8 crore RPTs in FY 2023-24).

7. SUBSEQUENT EVENTS: No events between March 31, 2024 and May 15, 2024 require adjustment/disclosure except as disclosed in Note 35.

8. GOING CONCERN: Company is a going concern. No material uncertainty exists regarding ability to continue operations.

Signed:
Mr. A, Managing Director
Ms. B, Chief Financial Officer
Date: May 15, 2024

[Audit Committee has reviewed and approved this letter on May 14, 2024]
```

## 7. BEST PRACTICES FOR AUDIT COMMITTEE EFFECTIVENESS

### 7.1 Composition Best Practices

**Diversity**:
- Mix of skills: Financial expert (CA/CFA), industry expert, legal/compliance expert
- Gender diversity: At least 1 female director (SEBI requirement for top 1000 listed companies)
- Age diversity: Mix of experienced and younger directors (fresh perspective)

**Financial Expert**:
- SEBI recommends at least 1 member with financial expertise (CA, CFA, CFO experience)
- Financial expert can chair Audit Committee (ideal)

**Tenure Limits**:
- Avoid indefinite tenure (reduces objectivity over time)
- Best practice: 5-year maximum tenure as chairperson, then rotate (can continue as member)

### 7.2 Meeting Effectiveness

**Pre-Meeting Preparation**:
- Board papers circulated 5-7 days in advance (allow adequate pre-read time)
- Members review papers thoroughly before meeting (come prepared with questions)

**Meeting Duration**:
- Adequate time for discussion (2-3 hours typical)
- Avoid rushing through agenda (prioritize quality discussion over speed)

**Executive Sessions**:
- Meet WITHOUT management (CFO, CEO) at least twice per year
  - Private discussion with statutory auditor (any concerns?)
  - Private discussion with internal auditor (any management interference?)
  - Private discussion among Audit Committee members (any concerns about management integrity?)

**Management Accountability**:
- Management presents action plans for audit findings
- Follow-up at next meeting: Verify implementation
- If management repeatedly fails to implement, escalate to Board (CEO accountability)

### 7.3 Communication with Board

**Audit Committee Report to Board**:
- After every Audit Committee meeting, chairperson reports key findings/recommendations to Board
- Board considers Audit Committee's recommendations (high credibility due to independence)

**Board's Report Disclosure**:
- Section 134(3)(n): Board's Report must state that Audit Committee recommendations accepted by Board
- If Board disagrees, must disclose reasons for disagreement (rare, signals governance concern)

**Example - Audit Committee Report to Board**:
```
AUDIT COMMITTEE REPORT TO BOARD

Audit Committee Meeting: July 20, 2024
Board Meeting: July 25, 2024

SUMMARY:

1. Q1 FY 2024-25 RESULTS:
   Audit Committee reviewed Q1 results. No material concerns. Revenue growth 25% driven by new product launch. Statutory auditor's limited review: No qualifications. RECOMMENDATION: Board may approve Q1 results.

2. RELATED PARTY TRANSACTIONS:
   Reviewed RPTs for Q1 (₹3.5 crore total, within omnibus approval limits). Arms-length pricing verified. RECOMMENDATION: Board may note Q1 RPTs.

3. INTERNAL AUDIT FINDINGS:
   Internal auditor reported 3 findings (procurement controls). Management committed to implement corrective actions by September 2024. RECOMMENDATION: Board may direct CFO to implement by September 2024. Audit Committee to verify in Q2.

4. AUDITOR APPOINTMENT:
   Current auditor (Audit Firm A) completing 8th year. Eligible for reappointment (max 10 years for listed companies). Auditor performance satisfactory. Audit fee ₹25 lakh (vs ₹22 lakh prior year, justified by increased scope). RECOMMENDATION: Board may recommend reappointment of Audit Firm A to shareholders at AGM.

5. WHISTLEBLOWER COMPLAINT:
   Received 1 complaint (expense fraud by Sales Manager). Investigation confirmed fraud ₹4.8 lakh. RECOMMENDATION: Board may terminate Sales Manager, recover ₹4.8 lakh, strengthen expense controls.

Submitted by:
[Name], Chairperson, Audit Committee
Date: July 21, 2024
```

## 8. STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS

### 8.1 Red Flags for Audit Committees

**Financial Reporting Red Flags**:
1. **Aggressive Revenue Recognition**: Recognizing revenue before goods shipped, services performed
2. **Cookie Jar Reserves**: Creating excessive provisions in good years, reversing in bad years (income smoothing)
3. **Off-Balance Sheet Entities**: SPVs, trusts not consolidated (hiding liabilities)
4. **Related Party Transactions**: Large RPTs without proper arms-length pricing
5. **Management Overrides**: CFO/CEO bypassing internal controls (unauthorized journal entries)

**Auditor Relationship Red Flags**:
1. **Frequent Auditor Changes**: Changing auditor every 1-2 years (management shopping for favorable opinion)
2. **High Non-Audit Fees**: Non-audit fees >audit fees (independence concern)
3. **Auditor Resignation**: Auditor resigns mid-tenure (often signals dispute with management)
4. **Qualified Opinion**: Auditor qualifies accounts (material misstatement)
5. **Management Disputes with Auditor**: Management pressures auditor to change opinion

**Governance Red Flags**:
1. **Dominant CEO**: CEO dominates Board/Audit Committee (intimidation)
2. **Lack of Independent Directors**: No truly independent directors (board controlled by management)
3. **Weak Internal Audit**: Internal audit reports to CFO (not Audit Committee), inadequate budget/staff
4. **Poor Whistleblower Response**: Whistleblower complaints ignored, whistleblowers retaliated against
5. **Rapid Growth**: Revenue/profit growing >50% per year (unsustainable, potential fraud to meet expectations)

### 8.2 Satyam Fraud Case - Audit Committee Failure

**Satyam Computer Services (2009)**:
```
Facts:
- Ramalinga Raju (Chairman) siphoned ₹7,000+ crore over 7 years
- Inflated cash, bank balances (₹5,040 crore fake cash)
- Inflated revenues via fake invoices from 13,000+ fake suppliers
- Audit Committee existed but was INEFFECTIVE (rubber-stamped management proposals)
- Statutory auditor (PwC) also failed (did not verify bank confirmations adequately)

Audit Committee Failures:
1. Did not review major transactions independently
2. Did not meet statutory auditor without management present
3. Did not verify bank balances (could have requested bank confirmations)
4. Did not question rapid revenue growth (37% CAGR over 7 years in competitive industry)
5. Members lacked financial expertise (unable to scrutinize complex accounting)

Consequences:
- Raju and CFO convicted (7 years imprisonment)
- Audit Committee directors sued by shareholders (negligence)
- Company merged with Tech Mahindra (distress sale)
- SEBI banned Raju, CFO, auditors from securities market

Lessons:
- Audit Committee must be ACTIVE, not passive
- Members must have financial literacy, courage to challenge management
- Must meet auditor privately (without management)
- Must verify material items independently (not rely solely on management representations)
- Must question unusual trends (rapid growth, high profitability in competitive industry)
```

**Post-Satyam Reforms**:
- Section 177 introduced in Companies Act 2013 (mandatory Audit Committee for certain companies)
- Section 177(4): Detailed functions of Audit Committee prescribed
- Section 149(4): Mandatory independent directors for listed companies (1/3rd or 1/2 depending on chairman)
- Section 143(12): Auditor's fraud reporting duty to Audit Committee

## 9. COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

**Constitution and Composition**:
- [ ] Audit Committee constituted (if mandatory under Section 177(1))
- [ ] Minimum 3 directors
- [ ] Majority non-executive (all members for listed companies)
- [ ] At least 2/3rd independent (for listed companies)
- [ ] All members financially literate
- [ ] Chairperson is independent director
- [ ] Composition disclosed in Board's Report

**Meetings**:
- [ ] Minimum 4 meetings per year
- [ ] Maximum gap 120 days between meetings
- [ ] Quorum: 2 members or 1/3rd (whichever higher), including 1 independent director
- [ ] Minutes recorded and signed by chairperson
- [ ] Chairperson reports to Board after each meeting
- [ ] Chairperson attends AGM

**Functions**:
- [ ] Review quarterly/annual financial statements before board approval
- [ ] Recommend auditor appointment, remuneration to Board
- [ ] Review auditor independence (non-audit fees, partner rotation)
- [ ] Approve all related party transactions before board approval
- [ ] Review internal audit reports, monitor implementation of recommendations
- [ ] Evaluate internal financial controls, risk management systems
- [ ] Establish vigil mechanism (whistleblower policy)
- [ ] Review fraud reports from auditor (Section 143(12))
- [ ] Meet statutory auditor without management (at least twice per year)
- [ ] Meet internal auditor without management (at least twice per year)

- [ ] Composition disclosed in Annual Report
- [ ] Number of meetings disclosed
- [ ] Attendance of each member disclosed
- [ ] Section 134(3)(n): Board's Report states Audit Committee recommendations accepted
- [ ] Any disagreements with Audit Committee disclosed (if applicable)

## 10. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

**Section 177(11)**:
```
If company fails to constitute Audit Committee or Audit Committee does not meet as required:

Company: Fine up to ₹5 LAKH
Every officer in default: Fine up to ₹1 LAKH

Continuing Default: ₹5,000 per day (capped at ₹5 lakh)
```

**Example**:
```
Company: ABC Public Ltd (paid-up capital ₹15 crore) - Audit Committee mandatory
Issue: Audit Committee not constituted for 1 year (April 1, 2023 to March 31, 2024)

Penalty:
- Company: ₹5 lakh (base penalty)
- Directors (3 directors): ₹1 lakh each × 3 = ₹3 lakh
- Continuing default: 365 days × ₹5,000 per day = ₹18.25 lakh (capped at ₹5 lakh)
- TOTAL PENALTY: ₹5 lakh (company) + ₹3 lakh (directors) + ₹5 lakh (continuing) = ₹13 LAKH

Additional Consequences:
- Auditor may qualify accounts (material non-compliance with Companies Act)
- SEBI enforcement action (if listed company)
- Investor loss of confidence, stock price decline
```

```
AUDIT COMMITTEE - TERMS OF REFERENCE
ABC PRIVATE LIMITED

1. CONSTITUTION:
   The Audit Committee is constituted by the Board pursuant to Section 177 of the Companies Act 2013.

2. COMPOSITION:
   - Minimum 3 directors
   - Majority: Non-executive directors
   - Chairperson: Independent director
   - All members: Financially literate

3. TENURE:
   Members appointed by Board. Chairperson appointed by Board. Term: Till resignation, retirement, or removal by Board.

4. QUORUM:
   2 members or 1/3rd of total strength (whichever higher), including at least 1 independent director.

5. FREQUENCY OF MEETINGS:
   Minimum 4 meetings per year (maximum 120-day gap). Additional meetings as required.

6. POWERS (Section 177(5)):
   - Investigate any activity within terms of reference
   - Seek information from any employee
   - Obtain outside legal or professional advice (company bears cost)
   - Secure attendance of outsiders with relevant expertise

7. FUNCTIONS (Section 177(4)):
   (a) Recommend auditor appointment, remuneration, terms to Board
   (b) Review and monitor auditor independence and performance
   (c) Approve or modify related party transactions
   (d) Scrutinize inter-corporate loans and investments
   (e) Review valuation of undertakings or assets
   (f) Evaluate internal financial controls and risk management
   (g) Monitor end-use of public issue proceeds

   ADDITIONAL FUNCTIONS:
   (h) Review quarterly and annual financial statements before board approval
   (i) Review statutory auditor's audit report, qualifications, observations
   (j) Review internal audit reports, monitor corrective actions
   (k) Review vigil mechanism (whistleblower complaints, investigations)
   (l) Review fraud reports from auditor (Section 143(12))
   (m) Review major accounting policies and estimates
   (n) Review compliance with statutory requirements

8. REPORTING:
   Audit Committee chairperson reports to Board after each meeting. Minutes of Audit Committee meetings placed before Board.

9. AGM ATTENDANCE:
   Audit Committee chairperson attends AGM to answer shareholder queries (Section 177(8)).

10. REVIEW:
    Terms of Reference reviewed annually and updated as required.

Approved by Board: [Date]
```
