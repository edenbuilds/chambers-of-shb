## Overview

**Director** = Person appointed to the board of directors to manage company affairs (Section 2(34)).

**Legal Framework**:
- Companies Act 2013: Sections 149-172 (Directors)
- Companies (Appointment and Qualification of Directors) Rules 2014
- SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations 2015 (for listed companies)

**Key Principle**: Directors owe **fiduciary duties** to company (duty of care, loyalty, act in good faith). Directors act as agents of company, not shareholders.

---

## Director Identification Number (DIN)

### Section 153: DIN Mandatory

**Definition**: DIN = Unique identification number issued by Central Government to every person intending to be appointed as director.

**Purpose**:
- Track directors across multiple companies
- Prevent disqualified persons from becoming directors
- Facilitate ROC compliance monitoring

**Characteristics**:
- Permanent (once allotted, valid for life)
- Unique (one person, one DIN)
- Portable (applies across all companies where person is director)

---

### DIN Application Procedure

**Form**: DIR-3 (Application for allotment of DIN)

**Documents Required**:
- PAN card
- Aadhaar card (for Indian nationals) OR Passport (for foreign nationals)
- Proof of identity + address
- Photograph
- Email ID, mobile number

**Processing Time**: 1-3 days (if all documents correct)

**Fee**: NIL (free)

**Validity**: Permanent (no renewal required)

**DIN Deactivation** (Rule 10):
- If director doesn't file financial statements/annual return for **3 consecutive years** across ALL companies
- MCA deactivates DIN
- Reactivation: File Form DIR-3C (KYC) + pending ROC filings

## Types of Directors

### 1. Executive Directors

**Definition**: Directors involved in day-to-day management, holding executive position (CEO, MD, CFO, CTO, COO).

**Key Features**:
✅ Full-time employees of company (employment contract)
✅ Draw salary, allowances, benefits (as per contract)
✅ Report to board, but have operational authority
✅ Subject to board oversight, performance review

**Appointment**: Board resolution + employment contract + shareholder approval (if MD/whole-time director)

**Tenure**: Typically 3-5 years (renewable)

**Examples**:
- **Managing Director (MD)**: Chief executive, overall charge of business operations (Section 2(54))
- **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**: Top management, strategic decisions
- **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)**: Financial management, accounts, compliance
- **Whole-Time Director (WTD)**: Full-time director, specific portfolio (sales, operations, HR)

---

### 2. Non-Executive Directors

**Definition**: Directors NOT involved in day-to-day management, attend board meetings, provide strategic guidance.

**Key Features**:
✅ Part-time (no employment contract, only directorship)
✅ Receive sitting fees (per board meeting attended) + commission (if approved)
✅ Provide oversight, strategic guidance
✅ No operational authority (only board-level decisions)

**Appointment**: Board resolution + shareholder approval (ordinary resolution)

**Examples**:
- Promoter/shareholder directors (founders, investors)
- Industry experts, advisors
- Government nominees (in PSUs)

---

### 3. Independent Directors (Section 149(5))

**Definition**: Directors independent of management, no material/pecuniary relationship with company/promoters.

**Criteria for Independence** (Section 149(6)):
- ✅ NOT promoter or related to promoter/director
- ✅ NOT employed by company (current or past 3 years)
- ✅ NOT relative of director/KMP
- ✅ NOT material business relationship with company (>2% revenue OR ₹50 lakh, whichever lower)
- ✅ NOT partner/executive of company's auditor (current or past 3 years)
- ✅ NOT holding >2% equity shares
- ✅ NOT nominee director (appointed by institution/lender)

**Mandatory For** (Section 149(4)):
- **Listed companies**: Min 1/3rd board must be independent directors
- **Public companies** (paid-up capital ≥₹10 crore OR turnover ≥₹100 crore OR outstanding loans/debentures ≥₹50 crore): Min 2 independent directors

**Tenure** (Section 149(10)):
- Max 2 consecutive terms of 5 years each (total 10 years)
- After 10 years, cannot be reappointed for next 3 years
- Can be reappointed after 3-year cooling-off period

**Appointment Procedure**:
1. Board identifies potential independent director candidates
2. Nomination & Remuneration Committee (NRC) recommends candidate
3. Board passes resolution proposing appointment
4. Shareholders approve via **special resolution** (75% majority) - Section 149(7)
5. Independent director files declaration of independence (Form DIR-8)

**Removal**: Only via special resolution (75% majority) + opportunity to be heard

**Role**:
- Oversight and governance (not management)
- Member of audit committee, NRC, stakeholders relationship committee (mandatory for listed companies)
- Independent judgment on strategy, performance, resources, standards of conduct

---

### 4. Nominee Directors

**Definition**: Directors nominated by specific institutions/lenders (banks, financial institutions, investors).

**Appointment**: Per nomination rights in loan agreement, SHA, or statute (e.g., government nominee in PSUs).

**Examples**:
- Bank nominee director (in companies with large borrowings)
- Investor nominee director (per SHA)
- Government nominee (in PSUs, Section 8 companies)

**Fiduciary Duty**: Despite being nominee, owes duty to **company** (not just nominating entity) - *Scottish Co-operative Wholesale Society v. Meyer* [1959] AC 324

---

### 5. Alternate Directors (Section 161(2))

**Definition**: Director appointed to act in place of original director during their absence (≥3 months).

**Appointment**: Board resolution (if AOA permits)

**Tenure**: Till original director returns (max duration = original director's absence period)

**Powers**: Same as original director (attend board meetings, vote)

**Use Case**: Original director on long overseas assignment, medical leave

---

### 6. Additional Directors (Section 161(1))

**Definition**: Directors appointed by board between two general meetings (to fill casual vacancy OR increase board strength).

**Appointment**: Board resolution (if AOA permits)

**Tenure**: Till next AGM (at AGM, shareholders elect directors)

**Use Case**: Urgent need for director (e.g., investor nominee director after funding round closes, cannot wait for next AGM)

---

### 7. Small Shareholders' Director (Section 151)

**Definition**: Director elected by small shareholders (holding ≤₹20,000 shares OR ≤ such amount prescribed).

**Mandatory For**: Listed companies OR public companies with ≥1,000 small shareholders

**Election**: By special resolution of small shareholders (not all shareholders)

**Tenure**: 3 years (can be reelected)

**Purpose**: Represent small shareholders' interests on board

## Qualifications and Disqualifications

### Minimum Age (Section 196(3))

**Minimum Age**: 18 years

**No Maximum Age** (for private companies)

**Public Companies**: Directors aged >75 years require **special resolution** for appointment (Section 196(3))

---

### Residency Requirement (Section 149(3))

**Mandatory**: At least **1 director** must be resident in India (stayed in India for ≥182 days in previous calendar year)

**Purpose**: Ensure Indian presence, regulatory compliance

---

### Number of Directorships (Section 165)

**Maximum Limit**:
- **20 companies** total (including private + public companies)
- Out of 20, max **10 public companies**

**Exclusions** (not counted):
- Alternate directorships
- Directorship in dormant companies
- Private companies (Section 8 companies, wholly owned subsidiaries)

**Penalty for Excess**: ₹5,000 per additional directorship (beyond limit)

---

### Independent Director Limit (Section 165)

**Maximum**: Independent director in max **7 listed companies**

**If Executive Director in ANY Listed Company**: Independent director in max **3 OTHER listed companies**

**Purpose**: Prevent over-commitment, ensure effective oversight

---

### Disqualifications (Section 164)

**Disqualified from Appointment**:

**1. Undischarged Insolvent** (Section 164(1)(a)):
- Person adjudicated insolvent, insolvency proceedings pending
- Disqualified till discharge from insolvency

**2. Convicted of Fraud/Moral Turpitude** (Section 164(1)(b)):
- Convicted and sentenced to ≥6 months imprisonment
- Disqualified for **5 years** from conviction date + sentence period

**3. Non-Payment of Calls** (Section 164(1)(c)):
- Failed to pay calls on shares (within 6 months of due date)
- Disqualified till calls paid

**4. Non-Filing of Financial Statements** (Section 164(2)):
- Company (where person is director) failed to file financial statements/annual return for **3 consecutive financial years**
- Disqualified in **ALL companies** (not just defaulting company)
- Disqualified for **5 years** from end of 3rd financial year

**5. Disqualified by Court/Tribunal** (Section 164(1)(d)):
- Court/NCLT order disqualifying person (for fraud, mismanagement)
- Disqualified for period specified in order

**Verification**: Company must verify DIN on MCA portal before appointing director (check if disqualified).

**Penalty**: If company appoints disqualified person:
- Company: ₹1 lakh - ₹5 lakh fine
- Officer in default: ₹50,000 - ₹5 lakh fine

## Appointment Procedure

### First Directors (Section 152(1))

**Appointment**: Named in MOA OR Articles of Association

**Tenure**: Till first AGM (at first AGM, shareholders elect directors)

**No Board/Shareholder Approval Needed**: Pre-incorporated appointment (subscribers sign MOA agreeing to first directors)

---

### Subsequent Directors

#### 1. Appointment in General Meeting (Section 152(2))

**Procedure**:
1. **Board Proposes Candidate**:
   - Board identifies candidate
   - NRC (if mandatory) recommends candidate
   - Board resolution proposing candidate for shareholder approval

2. **Notice to Shareholders** (21 days before general meeting):
   - Agenda: Appointment of [Name] as director
   - Explanatory statement (Section 102): Candidate's background, expertise, directorships

3. **Shareholders Vote**:
   - **Ordinary Resolution** (simple majority) for non-independent directors
   - **Special Resolution** (75% majority) for independent directors

4. **Director Consents** (Form DIR-2):
   - Candidate files consent to act as director
   - Declaration: Not disqualified u/s 164, complies with DIN requirements

5. **ROC Filing** (within 30 days):
   - Form DIR-12: Notice of appointment
   - Documents: Board resolution, consent, special resolution (if independent director)

---

#### 2. Appointment by Board (Section 161)

**When Allowed**:
- Casual vacancy (director death, resignation, removal)
- Additional director (increase board strength between AGMs)
- If AOA permits

**Procedure**:
1. Board resolution appointing director
2. Director files Form DIR-2 (consent)
3. ROC filing: Form DIR-12 (within 30 days)

**Tenure**: Till next AGM (at AGM, shareholders must approve appointment via ordinary resolution)

---

### Director's Consent and Disclosures

**Form DIR-2**: Consent to Act as Director
- Candidate's details (name, DIN, address)
- Declaration: Not disqualified, complies with directorships limit
- Consent to act as director

**Form DIR-8**: Declaration by Independent Director
- Declaration of independence (meets Section 149(6) criteria)
- Filed annually (at first board meeting of financial year)

**Form MBP-1**: Disclosure of Interest by Director (Section 184)
- Director discloses interests in other companies/LLPs/firms
- Filed at first board meeting + updated when interests change

## Tenure and Retirement

### Managing Director / Whole-Time Director (Section 196)

**Maximum Tenure**: 5 years per appointment (can be reappointed)

**Cooling-Off**: No cooling-off period required (can be immediately reappointed after 5 years)

**Shareholder Approval**: Ordinary resolution for appointment/reappointment

---

### Retirement by Rotation (Section 152)

**Applicability**: **Public companies** only (not private companies, unless AOA provides)

**Rule**: 2/3rd of directors (excluding independent directors, MD/WTD) retire by rotation

**Rotation Schedule**:
- At every AGM, 1/3rd of rotational directors retire
- Longest-serving directors retire first (FIFO)
- Retiring directors eligible for reelection

**Example**:
- Board: 6 directors (2 independent, 1 MD, 3 rotational directors)
- Rotational: 3 directors (2/3rd of 6 - 2 independent - 1 MD = 3)
- At AGM: 1 director retires (3 / 3 years = 1 per year)

**Exemption**: Directors appointed via special resolution exempted from retirement by rotation (Section 152(6))

## Removal of Directors

### 1. Removal by Shareholders (Section 169)

**Grounds**: Any reason (with or without cause)

**Procedure**:

**Step 1: Special Notice** (14 days before general meeting):
- Shareholder(s) intending to remove director must give **special notice** to company (14 days before meeting)
- Special notice can be given by **any shareholder** (no minimum shareholding required)

**Step 2: Company Sends Notice to Director**:
- Company forwards copy of special notice to director (whose removal proposed)
- Director has right to make representations (written submission)

**Step 3: Director's Right to Be Heard**:
- Director entitled to attend general meeting
- Director can speak, defend against removal
- Director's written representations circulated to shareholders (if received 7 days before meeting)

**Step 4: Shareholders Vote** (Ordinary Resolution):
- Simple majority (>50%) required to remove director
- Director (whose removal proposed) can vote on resolution (if shareholder)

**Step 5: ROC Filing** (within 30 days):
- Form DIR-12: Notice of cessation
- Copy of ordinary resolution

**Compensation** (Section 202):
- If director removed before tenure ends, entitled to compensation for **breach of employment contract**
- Compensation = Remaining salary + benefits as per employment contract
- Not applicable if removal for cause (fraud, gross misconduct)

**Landmark Case: Shanti Prasad Jain v. Kalinga Tubes (1965)**
**Facts**: Director removed without opportunity to be heard.
**Held**: Natural justice requires opportunity to be heard (even though statute doesn't explicitly mandate). Director's right to attend meeting, speak, make representations is implied.

---

### 2. Removal by NCLT (Sections 241, 242)

**Grounds**: Oppression and mismanagement, class action suits

**Procedure**: NCLT petition by minority shareholders → NCLT hearing → NCLT order removing director

**Example**: Director diverting company funds → Minority shareholders file oppression petition → NCLT removes director

---

### 3. Automatic Vacation of Office (Section 167)

**Director's Office Automatically Vacated If**:

**a. Disqualification** (Section 164):
- Becomes undischarged insolvent, convicted of fraud, non-payment of calls, non-filing for 3 years

**b. Absence from Board Meetings** (Section 167(1)(b)):
- Absent from **all board meetings** for 12 months (with or without leave)
- Board can grant leave of absence (prevents automatic vacation)

**c. Resignation Not Accepted** (Section 168(1)):
- Director files resignation, company doesn't accept/reject within 30 days
- Resignation takes effect after 30 days (automatic vacation)

**d. Acts as Director Despite Disqualification**:
- Penalty: ₹1 lakh - ₹5 lakh fine + imprisonment up to 1 year (Section 167(2))

**e. Non-Disclosure of Interest** (Section 184):
- Repeatedly fails to disclose interests in contracts

## Resignation of Directors

### Section 168: Resignation Procedure

**Step 1: Director Submits Resignation**:
- Written resignation letter to company
- Effective date (immediate OR future date)

**Step 2: Company Board Meeting** (within 30 days):
- Board considers resignation
- Board can **accept** OR **reject** (if resignation harms company, e.g., all directors resigning simultaneously)

**Step 3: Resignation Takes Effect**:
- **If accepted**: Effective from acceptance date
- **If rejected**: Director can approach NCLT (Section 168(4)) for relief
- **If no response within 30 days**: Resignation deemed accepted, effective after 30 days

**Step 4: ROC Filing** (within 30 days of cessation):
- Form DIR-12: Notice of cessation
- Copy of resignation letter

**Director's Liability After Resignation**:
- Liability for acts done during tenure (not retrospectively extinguished)
- Example: Director approved related party transaction (violating law) → Resigned → Still liable for that act

---

### Director's Right to Resign

**General Rule**: Director has right to resign anytime (no permission needed)

**Exception** (Section 168(2)):
- If resignation results in **all directors resigning** (board becomes vacant), resigning directors must call general meeting **before resignation takes effect**
- Purpose: Ensure continuity (shareholders elect new directors before old directors resign)

## Best Practices

### Director Appointment

✅ **Due Diligence**: Verify DIN, check disqualifications on MCA portal before appointing
✅ **Background Check**: Verify candidate's professional background, past directorships, litigation history
✅ **Conflict of Interest Check**: Ensure candidate has no material conflict with company (competitors, related parties)
✅ **Independent Directors**: For listed companies, ensure robust selection process (NRC recommendation, special resolution)
✅ **Diversity**: Consider gender diversity (at least 1 woman director for prescribed companies - Section 149(1)), skill diversity (finance, operations, legal, tech)

---

### Director Tenure Management

✅ **Retirement by Rotation Schedule**: Maintain rotation schedule (for public companies), ensure retiring directors are reelected (if desired)
✅ **Succession Planning**: Identify potential directors in advance (for retirement, resignation, expansion)
✅ **Performance Review**: Annual board evaluation (assess each director's performance, attendance, contributions)

---

### Director Removal/Resignation

✅ **Exit Interviews**: Understand reasons for resignation (improve governance)
✅ **Smooth Transition**: Ensure knowledge transfer, handover (if executive director)
✅ **Communication**: Announce departure to stakeholders (investors, employees, regulators)
✅ **Avoid Simultaneous Resignations**: Stagger resignations (prevent board vacancy)

---

## Conclusion

Director appointment, tenure, and removal are governed by Companies Act 2013 with emphasis on governance, independence, and shareholder protection.

**Key Takeaways**:

1. ✅ **DIN Mandatory**: Every director must have DIN (permanent, unique identifier)
2. ✅ **Types**: Executive (day-to-day management), non-executive (oversight), independent (1/3rd for listed companies)
3. ✅ **Disqualifications**: Insolvency, fraud conviction, non-filing for 3 years, non-payment of calls
4. ✅ **Appointment**: Shareholders elect (ordinary resolution for non-independent, special resolution for independent)
5. ✅ **Removal**: Ordinary resolution (simple majority) with special notice (14 days), director has right to be heard
6. ✅ **Resignation**: Director can resign anytime, takes effect after 30 days if not accepted/rejected

**Strategic Advice**:

- **For Companies**: Appoint independent directors early (avoid last-minute compliance), maintain director diversity (skills, gender), conduct annual board evaluations
- **For Directors**: File DIN KYC annually (avoid deactivation), disclose interests (Section 184), attend board meetings (avoid automatic vacation for absence)
- **For Shareholders**: Use Section 169 removal for underperforming/conflicted directors, ensure special notice (14 days) for removal
