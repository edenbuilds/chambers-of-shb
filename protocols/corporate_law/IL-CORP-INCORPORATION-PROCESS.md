## Overview

Company incorporation in India is governed by the **Companies Act 2013** and administered by the **Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)** through the **Registrar of Companies (ROC)**.

**Key Regulatory Framework**:
- Companies Act 2013 (as amended by Companies Amendment Act 2020)
- Companies (Incorporation) Rules 2014
- Companies (Prospectus and Allotment of Securities) Rules 2014

**Timeline**: 7-10 working days (standard procedure)
**Costs**: ₹7,000 - ₹15,000 (government fees + professional fees)

---

## Pre-Incorporation Steps

### Step 1: Decide Company Type

**Private Limited Company** (Most Common for Startups):
- **Minimum**: 2 directors, 2 shareholders
- **Maximum**: 200 shareholders
- **Restrictions**: No public invitation for shares/debentures
- **Compliance**: Moderate (no annual general meeting if all shareholders are directors)
- **Benefits**: Limited liability, perpetual succession, easier fundraising

**Public Limited Company**:
- **Minimum**: 3 directors, 7 shareholders
- **Maximum**: Unlimited shareholders
- **Compliance**: High (AGM mandatory, stricter audit, SEBI regulations if listed)
- **Benefits**: Can raise funds from public, higher credibility

**One Person Company (OPC)**:
- **Minimum**: 1 director, 1 shareholder (same person allowed)
- **Maximum**: 1 shareholder, 15 directors
- **Turnover Limit**: ≤₹2 crore, Paid-up capital ≤₹50 lakh (if exceeded, must convert to Pvt Ltd within 6 months)
- **Benefits**: Solo entrepreneur structure, limited liability

**Section 8 Company** (Non-Profit):
- **Purpose**: Charitable objectives (education, health, environment, arts, science, sports, social welfare)
- **No "Limited" Suffix**: Can use name without "Private Limited"
- **Profit Distribution**: No dividend distribution (profits used for objectives only)
- **License**: Requires Central Government license u/s 8

---

### Step 2: Identify Stakeholders

**Directors**:
- **Director Identification Number (DIN)**: All proposed directors must obtain DIN
- **Residential Status**: At least 1 director must be Indian resident (182+ days in previous calendar year)
- **Disqualifications**: Check Section 164 disqualifications (undischarged insolvent, convicted of fraud, etc.)

**Shareholders**:
- **Individuals**: PAN card, Aadhaar, address proof
- **Bodies Corporate**: CIN, board resolution authorizing investment
- **Foreign Shareholders**: Passport, FEMA compliance

**Registered Office**:
- **Proof of Ownership**: Rent agreement (min 11 months) OR Sale deed OR NOC from owner
- **Utility Bill**: Electricity bill / gas bill / water bill (within 2 months)
- **Address**: Cannot be residential flat without NOC from housing society / landlord

---

### Step 3: Check FEMA Compliance (if Foreign Investment)

**FDI Policy**:
- **Automatic Route**: Most sectors allow up to 100% FDI without government approval
- **Government Route**: Defense (>74%), Broadcasting, Satellite, Print media, Civil aviation, Multi-brand retail
- **Prohibited Sectors**: Lottery, gambling, chit funds, Nidhi companies, real estate (except development)

**Pricing Guidelines**:
- **Shares**: Fair valuation required (DCF method by chartered accountant)
- **Reporting**: Form FC-GPR within 30 days of receiving consideration

## Name Reservation (RUN Service)

### Step 1: Check Name Availability

**RUN (Reserve Unique Name) Service**: Online name application on MCA portal

**Naming Guidelines** (Rule 8 of Companies (Incorporation) Rules 2014):

**Prohibited Names**:
❌ Identical/similar to existing company name
❌ Names suggesting government patronage (Government, National, President, etc.) without approval
❌ Names suggesting illegal activity, obscenity, communal disharmony
❌ Names of living persons without written consent
❌ Trademark violations (check trademark database)

**Mandatory Suffixes**:
- Private Limited Company: "Private Limited" or "Pvt. Ltd."
- Public Limited Company: "Limited" or "Ltd."
- One Person Company: "One Person Company" or "OPC"
- Section 8 Company: No "Limited" required (if license obtained)

**Name Structure**:
- **Prefix**: Brand name (e.g., "ABC Technologies")
- **Suffix**: Legal status (Pvt. Ltd. / Limited / OPC)
- Example: "ABC Technologies Private Limited"

---

### Step 2: Submit RUN Application

**Form**: RUN (Reserve Unique Name)
**Fee**: ₹1,000 per name application
**Names to Submit**: 2 proposed names (in order of preference)

**Processing Time**: Instant approval OR 2-3 days if manual check required

**Validity**: Name reserved for 20 days (can be extended by re-applying)

**Rejection Reasons**:
- Name too similar to existing company (phonetic similarity)
- Trademark conflict
- Violates naming guidelines

## SPICe+ Form Filing

### SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus)

**Integrated Services**: SPICe+ combines 10 services in one form:
1. Company incorporation (formerly INC-32)
2. DIN allotment for directors
3. PAN application
4. TAN application
5. EPFO registration (Employees' Provident Fund)
6. ESIC registration (Employees' State Insurance)
7. Profession Tax registration (Maharashtra, West Bengal, Karnataka only)
8. Opening bank account (linked Part B)
9. GST registration (linked AGILE form)
10. Shops & Establishments registration (linked AGILE form)

---

### Step 1: Prepare SPICe+ Form (Part A)

**Section 1: Company Details**:
- Reserved name (from RUN)
- Registered office address (with proof)
- Main objects (business activities - select industrial codes)
- Authorized share capital
- Paid-up share capital

**Section 2: Subscribers (Shareholders)**:
- Name, father's name, nationality
- PAN, Aadhaar (for Indian nationals)
- Passport (for foreign nationals)
- Number of shares subscribed
- Amount paid per share
- Residential address

**Section 3: Directors**:
- Name, father's name, nationality
- DIN (if existing) OR DIN application (if new)
- PAN, Aadhaar (for Indian nationals)
- Passport (for foreign nationals)
- Residential address, director category (executive/non-executive/independent)
- Shareholding (if director is also shareholder)

**Section 4: Authorized Signatories**:
- Managing Director / CEO details
- Company Secretary details (if appointed)
- CFO details (if appointed)

---

### Step 2: Attach Mandatory Documents

**Document Checklist**:

1. **Memorandum of Association (MOA)**:
   - Digitally signed by all subscribers
   - Format: As per Table A/B/C/D/E of Schedule I of Companies Act 2013

2. **Articles of Association (AOA)**:
   - Digitally signed by all subscribers
   - Format: As per Table F (for company limited by shares) OR customized AOA

3. **Declaration by First Directors (Form INC-9)**:
   - Affidavit stating directors not disqualified u/s 164
   - Consent to act as director

4. **Registered Office Proof**:
   - Rent Agreement (notarized) OR Sale Deed
   - Utility Bill (electricity/gas/water, within 2 months)
   - NOC from owner (if rented)

5. **Identity & Address Proof** (for each subscriber and director):
   - **Indian Nationals**: PAN card + Aadhaar + passport/voter ID/driving license
   - **Foreign Nationals**: Passport + visa + address proof in home country

6. **Professional Certificate** (if applicable):
   - Practicing Chartered Accountant / Company Secretary / Cost Accountant certificate for capital verification (if paid-up capital > ₹1 lakh)

---

### Step 3: Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

**Requirement**: All subscribers and directors must have DSC (Class 2 or Class 3)

**DSC Providers**:
- eMudhra, Sify, nCode Solutions (licensed certifying authorities)
- **Cost**: ₹1,000 - ₹2,500 per DSC (valid for 1-2 years)

**Usage**: DSC used to digitally sign MOA, AOA, forms, and all MCA filings

---

### Step 4: File SPICe+ Part A

**Filing Fee** (Stamp Duty + MCA Fee):
- **Authorized Capital ≤₹1 lakh**: ₹200 stamp duty + ₹400 MCA fee = ₹600
- **₹1 lakh < Authorized Capital ≤₹5 lakh**: ₹300 stamp duty + ₹600 MCA fee = ₹900
- **₹5 lakh < Authorized Capital ≤₹10 lakh**: ₹500 stamp duty + ₹800 MCA fee = ₹1,300
- **₹10 lakh < Authorized Capital ≤₹1 crore**: ₹2,000 stamp duty + ₹2,000 MCA fee = ₹4,000
- **Authorized Capital > ₹1 crore**: ₹3,000 stamp duty + ₹4,000 MCA fee = ₹7,000

**Processing Time**: 2-7 working days (if all documents correct)

---

### Step 5: Obtain CIN (Corporate Identity Number)

**Issuance**: ROC issues Certificate of Incorporation with CIN after approval

**CIN Format**: U/L + [Industry Code] + [State Code] + [Year] + [Type] + [Serial Number]
- Example: U72900DL2024PTC123456
  - U = Public sector undertaking (L = Government company)
  - 72900 = Industry code (Computer programming)
  - DL = Delhi
  - 2024 = Year of incorporation
  - PTC = Private company limited by shares
  - 123456 = Serial number

**Certificate of Incorporation**: Digital certificate issued, contains:
- Company name
- CIN
- Date of incorporation
- Registered office address
- Authorized share capital

---

### Step 6: File SPICe+ Part B (Linked Services)

**Within 30 Days of Incorporation**:

**AGILE Form** (Application for GST, EPFO, ESIC, Professional Tax, Shops & Establishments):
- PAN & TAN auto-issued (no separate application needed)
- GST registration (if turnover expected >₹40 lakh for services, >₹20 lakh for goods)
- EPFO registration (if 20+ employees)
- ESIC registration (if 10+ employees, wage ≤₹21,000/month)

**Bank Account Opening**:
- Part B of SPICe+ sent to bank
- Bank account opened with CIN as reference

## Post-Incorporation Compliance

### Within 30 Days of Incorporation

**1. First Board Meeting (Section 173)**:
- **Agenda**:
  - Adoption of MOA & AOA
  - Appointment of first auditor (Section 139)
  - Approval of common seal (if any)
  - Authorization for bank account opening
  - Appointment of CEO, CFO, Company Secretary (if applicable)
  - Issuance of share certificates to subscribers

**2. Issue Share Certificates (Section 56)**:
- Share certificates to all subscribers within 2 months of incorporation
- Format: Certificate with company name, CIN, shareholder name, number of shares, share numbers, date

**3. Statutory Registers (Section 88)**:
- Register of Members (shareholder details)
- Register of Directors & KMP
- Register of Charges (if any loans/mortgages taken)
- Minutes Book (board meetings, general meetings)

---

### Within 60 Days of Incorporation

**1. Open Bank Account**:
- Corporate bank account in company name
- Documents required: CIN, MOA, AOA, Board Resolution, PAN, TAN

**2. GST Registration** (if applicable):
- Turnover threshold: ₹40 lakh (services), ₹20 lakh (goods)
- GSTIN auto-issued via SPICe+ AGILE OR separate application

---

### Within 180 Days of Incorporation

**1. Commencement of Business (Section 10A)**:
- **Public Companies**: File Form INC-20A (declaration of commencement of business) + bank verification of paid-up capital
- **Private Companies**: No commencement certificate required (Section 10A exemption)

---

### Annual Compliance (After First Year)

**1. Annual General Meeting (AGM)** - Section 96:
- **First AGM**: Within 9 months of end of first financial year (not within 15 months of incorporation)
- **Subsequent AGMs**: Within 6 months of end of financial year
- **Gap**: Max 15 months between two AGMs
- **Private Company Exemption**: If all shareholders are directors, AGM not required (deemed approval via written resolution)

**2. Annual Return (Form MGT-7)** - Section 92:
- Filed within 60 days of AGM
- Contains: Shareholder details, director details, meetings held, changes in capital
- **Fee**: Based on authorized capital (₹200 - ₹2,000)

**3. Financial Statements Filing (Form AOC-4)** - Section 137:
- Filed within 30 days of AGM
- Contains: Balance sheet, profit & loss, auditor's report, board report
- **Fee**: Based on authorized capital (₹200 - ₹2,000)

**4. Income Tax Return (ITR-6)**:
- Filed by September 30 (for companies with March 31 financial year end)
- Tax rate: 25% (for companies with turnover ≤₹400 crore) OR 30% (for larger companies)

**5. GST Returns** (if registered):
- GSTR-1 (monthly/quarterly): Outward supplies
- GSTR-3B (monthly): Summary return
- GSTR-9 (annual): Annual return

## Timeline & Costs

### Standard Incorporation Timeline

| Step | Timeline | Cumulative |
|------|----------|-----------|
| DIN Application (if new directors) | 1-2 days | 1-2 days |
| Name Reservation (RUN) | 1-3 days | 2-5 days |
| DSC Procurement | 2-4 days | 4-9 days |
| SPICe+ Form Preparation | 1 day | 5-10 days |
| SPICe+ Filing & ROC Processing | 2-7 days | **7-17 days** |
| CIN Issuance | Same day as approval | **7-17 days** |
| PAN & TAN Allotment | Auto-issued | **7-17 days** |
| AGILE Form (GST, EPFO, ESIC) | 7-10 days | 14-27 days |

**Total Time**: **7-10 working days** (if no rejections, all documents ready)
**With Delays**: 15-30 days (if rejections, re-submissions, document issues)

### Incorporation Costs Breakdown

**Government Fees**:
- Name Reservation (RUN): ₹1,000
- SPICe+ Filing Fee: ₹600 - ₹7,000 (based on authorized capital)
- Stamp Duty: ₹200 - ₹3,000 (state-wise variation)

**Professional Fees** (if CA/CS engaged):
- Consultation: ₹2,000 - ₹5,000
- MOA/AOA Drafting: ₹2,000 - ₹5,000
- Form Preparation: ₹1,000 - ₹3,000
- Total Professional Fees: ₹5,000 - ₹13,000

**Ancillary Costs**:
- DSC (per person): ₹1,000 - ₹2,500 × 2 directors = ₹2,000 - ₹5,000
- Registered Office Rent: ₹5,000 - ₹20,000/month (if not owned)

**Total Costs**:
- **DIY (Do-It-Yourself)**: ₹4,000 - ₹10,000 (govt fees + DSC)
- **With Professional**: ₹10,000 - ₹25,000 (govt fees + DSC + professional fees)

## Common Rejection Reasons

### Name Rejection

**Reason 1: Similarity to Existing Name**:
- **Example**: "ABC Technologies Pvt Ltd" rejected if "ABC Technology Pvt Ltd" exists
- **Remedy**: Add distinctive element (location, person's name, unique word)

**Reason 2: Trademark Conflict**:
- **Example**: "Tata Motors Pvt Ltd" rejected (Tata is registered trademark)
- **Remedy**: Obtain NOC from trademark owner OR choose different name

**Reason 3: Government Approval Required**:
- **Example**: "National Healthcare Pvt Ltd" (word "National" requires approval)
- **Remedy**: Remove "National" OR apply for government approval (3-6 months delay)

---

### SPICe+ Rejection

**Reason 1: Incomplete/Incorrect Documents**:
- Rent agreement not notarized
- Utility bill older than 2 months
- MOA/AOA not digitally signed by all subscribers
- **Remedy**: Re-submit with correct documents

**Reason 2: DIN Mismatch**:
- Director's name in SPICe+ doesn't match DIN database
- **Remedy**: Correct name OR update DIN database via DIR-6 (change of particulars)

**Reason 3: Registered Office Address Issue**:
- Residential address used without NOC
- Address already used by 50+ companies (ROC suspicious)
- **Remedy**: Obtain NOC OR change registered office address

**Reason 4: Object Clause Ambiguity**:
- Main objects not clearly defined
- Objects not aligned with selected industrial code
- **Remedy**: Rewrite objects clause clearly, select correct industrial code

## Best Practices

### Planning Phase

✅ **Research Name Availability**: Check MCA database, trademark database before RUN application
✅ **Decide Shareholding Structure**: Clear allocation (50-50 OR 60-40 OR 75-25) to avoid future disputes
✅ **Agree on Directors**: Minimum 2 directors with DIN ready
✅ **Finalize Registered Office**: Ensure rent agreement/ownership proof ready
✅ **FEMA Compliance**: If foreign investment, check FDI policy sectoral caps

### Execution Phase

✅ **Engage Professional (Optional but Recommended)**: CA/CS for first-time incorporators
✅ **Prepare Documents in Advance**: MOA, AOA, rent agreement, utility bill, ID proofs
✅ **Obtain DSC Early**: DSC takes 2-4 days, can delay filing
✅ **Review SPICe+ Before Filing**: One mistake = rejection + re-filing (7-day delay)

### Post-Incorporation Phase

✅ **Hold First Board Meeting**: Within 30 days (compliance + operational decisions)
✅ **Issue Share Certificates**: Within 60 days (shareholder confidence)
✅ **Open Bank Account**: Within 60 days (operational necessity)
✅ **File AGILE Form**: If GST/EPFO/ESIC applicable (within 30 days)
✅ **Maintain Statutory Registers**: From Day 1 (avoid penalty ₹10,000 + ₹1,000/day)

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Late Filing Penalties

**SPICe+ Late Filing**: Not applicable (filed at incorporation)

**Annual Return (MGT-7) Late Filing** - Section 92:
- **Company**: ₹100/day (max ₹5 lakh)
- **Every Officer in Default**: ₹100/day (max ₹5 lakh)

**Financial Statements (AOC-4) Late Filing** - Section 137:
- **Company**: ₹1,000/day (max ₹10 lakh)
- **Every Officer in Default**: ₹1,000/day (max ₹10 lakh)

**First Board Meeting Not Held** - Section 173:
- **Company**: ₹25,000
- **Every Officer in Default**: ₹5,000

## Case Law Principles

### Separate Legal Entity (Salomon v. Salomon & Co Ltd)

**Facts**: Aron Salomon transferred his boot-making business to a company (Salomon & Co Ltd) and became majority shareholder and director. Company went into liquidation. Creditors argued company is mere "agent" of Salomon, so he should be personally liable.

**Held**: Company is a separate legal entity distinct from its shareholders. Once validly incorporated, company has its own legal personality. Shareholders not personally liable for company debts (limited liability).

**Principle**: Corporate veil protects shareholders from personal liability (except when pierced for fraud, tax evasion, etc.)

**Application**: Even single-shareholder companies (OPC) have separate legal personality. Creditors cannot pursue shareholder's personal assets (only company assets).

---

### Corporate Personality (Lee v. Lee's Air Farming Ltd)

**Facts**: Lee was sole shareholder, director, and employee (pilot) of Lee's Air Farming Ltd. He died in plane crash while working. His widow claimed compensation under Workmen's Compensation Act. Question: Can Lee be both employer (as company) and employee (as pilot)?

**Held**: Yes. Company is separate legal entity. Lee as director (representing company) employed Lee as pilot (as individual). Two distinct legal capacities.

**Principle**: Individual can contract with their own company (as director employs self as employee).

**Application**: OPC owner can be employee of own company, entitled to salary, compensation, benefits.

---

### Incorporation Date (Tata Engineering v. State of Bihar)

**Facts**: Tax assessment disputed based on whether company incorporated before or after cut-off date.

**Held**: Company comes into existence on the date Certificate of Incorporation issued by ROC. Not the date of application, not the date MOA/AOA signed.

**Principle**: Incorporation date = Date on Certificate of Incorporation (CIN issuance date)

**Application**: For limitation periods, tax assessments, contractual obligations—incorporation date is the controlling date.

## Strategic Considerations

### Pvt Ltd vs OPC

**Choose Private Limited IF**:
✅ Multiple co-founders (2+ persons)
✅ Planning to raise external funding (investors prefer Pvt Ltd over OPC)
✅ Anticipate turnover >₹2 crore OR paid-up capital >₹50 lakh
✅ Want flexibility to add shareholders easily (no conversion required)

**Choose OPC IF**:
✅ Solo entrepreneur (single founder)
✅ Small business (turnover ≤₹2 crore, capital ≤₹50 lakh)
✅ Want limited liability protection without co-founder
✅ Not planning external funding immediately (can convert to Pvt Ltd later)

---

### Authorized Capital vs Paid-Up Capital

**Authorized Capital**: Maximum capital company is authorized to raise (as per MOA)
- Example: ₹10 lakh authorized capital

**Paid-Up Capital**: Actual capital paid by shareholders
- Example: ₹1 lakh paid-up capital (shareholders paid ₹1 lakh, company can raise ₹9 lakh more in future)

**Strategy**:
- **Higher Authorized Capital**: Gives flexibility to issue more shares later without MOA alteration
- **Downside**: Higher stamp duty (calculated on authorized capital)
- **Recommendation**: Authorized capital = 3-5x immediate paid-up capital need

---

### Foreign Investment Structure

**For FDI (Foreign Direct Investment)**:
- Foreign investor subscribes shares directly in Indian company
- FEMA compliance: Automatic route (most sectors) OR Government route (restricted sectors)
- Pricing: Fair valuation required (DCF method)
- Reporting: Form FC-GPR within 30 days

**For FVCI (Foreign Venture Capital Investor)**:
- SEBI-registered FVCI invests in Indian startup
- Tax benefits: Capital gains exemption u/s 10(23FB) Income Tax Act
- Lock-in: 1 year for FVCI investments
