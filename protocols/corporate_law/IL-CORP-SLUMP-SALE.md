# Slump Sale of Undertaking - Indian Corporate Law

## Overview

### What is a Slump Sale?

A **slump sale** is the transfer of one or more undertakings (a going concern business) for a **lump sum consideration** without assigning values to individual assets and liabilities. The key characteristic is that the undertaking is sold as a whole at an aggregate price, not as itemized assets.

**Section 2(42C) Definition** (Companies Act 2013 & Income Tax Act 1961):
> "Slump sale means the transfer of one or more undertakings, for a lump sum consideration, without values being assigned to the individual assets and liabilities in such sales."

**Key Features**:
1. **Going Concern Transfer**: The business is transferred as a running operation capable of continuing independently
2. **Lump Sum Consideration**: Single aggregate price for the entire undertaking (not individual asset valuations)
3. **Undertaking**: Identifiable business unit with separate books, operations, employees, and contracts
4. **Asset Sale Structure**: Legally an asset sale (not share sale), but treated holistically
5. **Capital Gains Tax**: Computed on net worth differential (Section 50B)

### Why Use a Slump Sale?

**When Slump Sale is Preferred**:
1. **Speed**: 2-4 months vs 9-12 months for demerger (no NCLT approval required)
2. **Third-Party Sale**: Selling to external buyer (not distributing to shareholders like demerger)
3. **Cash Consideration**: Seller wants cash proceeds (vs shares in demerger)
4. **Simpler Process**: Bilateral agreement vs court scheme + shareholder approvals
5. **Selective Assets**: Sell one undertaking while retaining others (partial divestment)
6. **Tax Planning**: LTCG (20% with indexation) can be lower than STCG (30%) for long-held businesses

**When NOT to Use Slump Sale**:
1. **Tax Sensitivity**: Triggers immediate capital gains tax (vs tax-neutral demerger)
2. **Shareholder Distribution**: Want to distribute undertaking to shareholders (use demerger instead)
3. **Share Consideration**: Buyer wants to pay in shares (slump sale typically cash-based)
4. **Contingent Liabilities**: Want clean separation (share sale may be cleaner for buyer's liability concerns)

### Slump Sale vs Other M&A Structures

| Feature | Slump Sale | Share Sale | Demerger | Itemized Asset Sale |
|---------|-----------|------------|----------|-------------------|
| **What's Sold** | Undertaking (going concern) | Company shares | Undertaking | Individual assets |
| **Consideration** | Lump sum (no individual values) | Per-share price | Shares to shareholders | Individual asset prices |
| **Timeline** | 2-4 months | 1-3 months | 9-12 months (NCLT) | 3-6 months |
| **Tax to Seller** | Capital gains on net worth | Capital gains on shares | Tax-neutral (if conditions met) | Capital gains + GST on some assets |
| **Buyer Liability** | Disclosed liabilities only | ALL liabilities (successor) | Disclosed liabilities only | Cherry-pick assets, no liabilities |
| **Approvals** | Board + Shareholders (if >50% net worth) | Board + Shareholders (if transfer control) | NCLT + Shareholders (75%) | Board |
| **Stamp Duty** | 3-7% (state-dependent) | 0.25% (share transfer) | Nil (court scheme) | 3-7% per asset |
| **Buyer** | Third party | Third party | Existing shareholders (proportionate) | Third party |

## Legal Framework

### Section 2(42C) - Definition of Slump Sale

**Statutory Language** (Companies Act 2013 & Income Tax Act 1961):
> "Slump sale means the transfer of one or more undertakings, for a lump sum consideration, without values being assigned to the individual assets and liabilities in such sales."

**Key Interpretive Principles**:

1. **"Undertaking"** (Not Defined in Act):
   - Courts have interpreted as: "Going concern capable of independent operation"
   - Must have identifiable business activity (manufacturing, trading, services)
   - Separate books of account maintained (or capable of being separately maintained)
   - Separate employees, assets, contracts attributable to the undertaking
   - **Adhunik Metaliks Ltd. case (2009)**: Transfer of iron ore business with mines, machinery, employees = undertaking (going concern), NOT mere land sale

2. **"Lump Sum Consideration"**:
   - Single aggregate price negotiated for the undertaking as a whole
   - Individual asset values NOT assigned in the sale agreement
   - Valuation report shows net worth (aggregate), not itemized asset values
   - **Example**: Sale agreement states "₹500 crore for the pharmaceutical formulations undertaking" (lump sum), NOT "₹200cr for plant, ₹150cr for inventory, ₹100cr for goodwill, ₹50cr for brand" (itemized)

3. **"Transfer"**:
   - Includes sale, exchange, relinquishment of assets
   - Must transfer ALL assets and liabilities of the undertaking (going concern principle)
   - Partial transfer of undertaking (retaining some assets) may not qualify (tax department may challenge)

### Section 50B - Capital Gains Computation

**Statutory Provision** (Income Tax Act 1961):
> "Where the capital asset is transferred in a slump sale, the net worth of the undertaking or division shall be deemed to be the cost of acquisition and cost of improvement for the purposes of sections 48 and 49."

**Formula**:
```
Capital Gains on Slump Sale = Sale Consideration - Net Worth of Undertaking

Where:
Net Worth = Total Assets (at book value) - Total Liabilities (at book value)
```

**Key Rules**:

1. **No Indexation for Net Worth**: Unlike other capital assets, you cannot inflate the net worth using Cost Inflation Index (CII). Net worth is taken at book value only.

2. **Unrecorded Goodwill NOT Added** (Manish Buildwell Pvt. Ltd. v. CIT, 2016):
   - Net worth = Balance sheet assets - Liabilities (book value)
   - Even if business has inherent goodwill (brand value, customer relationships), if not recorded on balance sheet, it's NOT added to net worth
   - This increases capital gains (lower net worth → higher gains)
   - **Controversy**: Taxpayers argue fair value should include unrecorded goodwill, but courts have rejected this

3. **Long-Term vs Short-Term Capital Gains**:
   - **Long-Term** (undertaking held >36 months): 20% tax with indexation (but indexation only applies to monetary consideration, not net worth)
   - **Short-Term** (undertaking held ≤36 months): 30% tax (slab rate for companies)
   - Holding period = From date undertaking acquired/established to transfer date

4. **Losses**: If Net Worth > Sale Consideration → Capital Loss (can be set off against capital gains, carried forward 8 years)

### Section 180 - Shareholder Approval

**Companies Act 2013, Section 180(1)(a)**:
> "The Board of Directors shall not, except with the consent of the company by special resolution, sell, lease or otherwise dispose of the whole or substantially the whole of the undertaking of the company or where the company owns more than one undertaking, of the whole or substantially the whole of any such undertaking."

**Interpretation**:
- **"Substantially the whole"** = >50% of net worth (based on judicial precedents)
- **Special Resolution Required** = 75% shareholder approval (Section 114)
- **Board Approval Sufficient** if undertaking <50% of net worth

**Example**:
- Company net worth: ₹1,000 crore
- Undertaking A net worth: ₹600 crore (60% of total)
- Sale of Undertaking A: Requires special resolution (>50% threshold)
- Undertaking B net worth: ₹400 crore (40% of total)
- Sale of Undertaking B: Board approval sufficient (<50% threshold)

## Slump Sale Transaction Process

### Step 1: Due Diligence (30-60 days)

**Buyer's Objectives**:
1. **Verify Assets**: Physical inspection of plant/machinery, inventory count, IP ownership verification
2. **Identify Liabilities**: Known liabilities (balance sheet) + Contingent liabilities (off-balance sheet)
3. **Legal Compliance**: Environmental clearances, labor law compliance, tax assessments
4. **Commercial Viability**: Customer contracts transferability, supplier agreements, employee retention

**Key Due Diligence Areas**:

**a) Hidden Liabilities (Critical for Buyer)**:
- **Tax Liabilities**: Pending assessments, disputed tax demands (₹50cr demand under appeal → contingent liability)
- **Labor Liabilities**: Unreported gratuity, provident fund non-compliance, pending labor disputes
- **Environmental Liabilities**: Pollution control violations, remediation costs for contaminated land
- **Contractual Liabilities**: Liquidated damages clauses, warranty obligations on past sales
- **Litigation**: Pending lawsuits (product liability, commercial disputes, IP infringement)

**Example - Hidden Liability Discovery**:
- Balance Sheet Liability: ₹200 crore (loans, trade payables)
- Due Diligence Reveals:
  - Tax demand under appeal: ₹30 crore (50% probability of loss → ₹15cr provision)
  - Environmental remediation: ₹10 crore (soil contamination at factory site)
  - Warranty claims: ₹5 crore (defective products sold last year)
- **Adjusted Net Worth**: Book Net Worth ₹300cr - Hidden Liabilities ₹30cr = ₹270cr
- **Impact on Price**: Buyer negotiates ₹270cr (not ₹300cr), or Seller provides indemnity for hidden liabilities

**b) Asset Verification**:
- **Fixed Assets**: Physical verification matches fixed asset register (missing/damaged assets adjusted)
- **Inventory**: Stock count, obsolete inventory identification (₹50cr book value, ₹40cr realizable value)
- **Receivables**: Aging analysis, doubtful debts provision (₹100cr receivables, ₹10cr >365 days → provision required)
- **Intellectual Property**: Patent/trademark ownership, licenses validity, no IP litigation

**c) Employee Matters**:
- **Transfer of Employment**: Employees automatically transfer under Section 25FF (Industrial Disputes Act) if going concern
- **Retention Bonus**: Key employees may demand retention bonus to stay post-transfer
- **Provident Fund**: Transfer PF account from seller to buyer (EPFO approval required)

### Step 2: Valuation (15-30 days)

**Registered Valuer Requirement** (Section 2(42C) read with Rule 2 of Companies (Registered Valuers and Valuation) Rules 2017):
- **Mandatory**: Independent registered valuer must certify net worth of undertaking
- **Net Worth Certificate**: Valuer provides certificate showing Assets - Liabilities = Net Worth
- **Valuation Methods**:
  1. **Book Value Method** (Most Common for Tax): Balance sheet net worth (unadjusted)
  2. **Fair Market Value Method** (Negotiation): Adjust for fair value of assets/liabilities
  3. **DCF Method** (Commercial Valuation): Discounted cash flows (future profitability-based)

**Example - Valuation Report**:

**Undertaking: Pharmaceutical Formulations Division**

| Component | Book Value (₹ Crore) | Fair Market Value (₹ Crore) | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------------|-------|
| **Assets** | | | |
| Land & Building | 100 | 250 | Acquired 20 years ago, appreciated 150% |
| Plant & Machinery | 150 | 120 | Depreciated equipment, lower FMV |
| Inventory | 80 | 75 | Obsolete stock ₹5cr written off |
| Receivables | 70 | 65 | Doubtful debts ₹5cr |
| Cash & Bank | 50 | 50 | At book value |
| **Total Assets** | **450** | **560** | |
| **Liabilities** | | | |
| Loans | 100 | 100 | At book value |
| Trade Payables | 50 | 50 | At book value |
| Provisions | 20 | 35 | Additional ₹15cr for tax demand |
| **Total Liabilities** | **170** | **185** | |
| **Net Worth** | **280** | **375** | |

**Valuation Outcomes**:
1. **For Tax Purposes (Section 50B)**: Net Worth = ₹280 crore (book value only, no FMV adjustments)
2. **For Negotiation**: Parties negotiate between ₹280cr (book) and ₹375cr (FMV), typical settlement ₹320-350cr
3. **Capital Gains Calculation**: If sale at ₹350cr, Capital Gains = ₹350cr - ₹280cr = ₹70cr (taxed)

### Step 3: Transaction Documents (30-45 days)

**a) Business Transfer Agreement (BTA)** - Primary Document

**Key Clauses**:

1. **Purchase Price**:
   ```
   "The Buyer agrees to acquire the Pharmaceutical Formulations Undertaking from the Seller
   for a lump sum consideration of ₹350 crores (Rupees Three Hundred Fifty Crores only)."
   ```

2. **Transferred Assets** (Schedule 1):
   - All fixed assets (land, building, plant, machinery, furniture, vehicles)
   - Current assets (inventory, receivables, cash in division bank account)
   - Intangible assets (brand "PharmaCure", formulation IP, regulatory approvals)
   - Employees (Schedule 2 lists all 450 employees with salaries, tenure)
   - Contracts (Schedule 3 lists customer/supplier contracts)

3. **Assumed Liabilities** (Schedule 4):
   - Loans allocated to undertaking: ₹100 crore (specific loan agreement references)
   - Trade payables: ₹50 crore (vendor list attached)
   - Employee liabilities: Provident fund, gratuity accrued (₹20 crore)

4. **Excluded Assets/Liabilities**:
   ```
   "The following assets and liabilities shall NOT transfer and remain with the Seller:
   - Corporate office land and building (Seller's registered office)
   - Inter-corporate deposits from group companies (unrelated to undertaking)
   - Seller's corporate tax liabilities (unrelated to undertaking operations)"
   ```

5. **Closing Conditions Precedent**:
   - CCI approval (if thresholds exceeded)
   - Lender consents (for loans being transferred/assumed)
   - Material customer consents (top 10 customers representing >50% revenue)
   - No Material Adverse Change (MAC) in undertaking (revenue drop >20%, key employee resignation)

6. **Closing Mechanics**:
   ```
   "On the Closing Date:
   a) Buyer pays ₹350 crores to Seller's designated account
   b) Seller executes Asset Transfer Deed (all assets listed in Schedule 1)
   c) Seller delivers physical possession of assets
   d) Buyer assumes liabilities (Schedule 4) from Closing Date onwards"
   ```

**b) Representations & Warranties**

**Seller's Reps (Typical)**:
1. **Title**: Good and marketable title to all assets, free from encumbrances
2. **Financials**: Financial statements are accurate, no undisclosed liabilities
3. **Compliance**: All legal/regulatory compliance (tax, labor, environmental) up-to-date
4. **Contracts**: All material contracts disclosed, no defaults, transferable
5. **Litigation**: No pending/threatened litigation except as disclosed (Schedule 5)
6. **Employees**: No labor disputes, all statutory payments (PF, ESI, gratuity) current
7. **IP**: Owns all IP used in undertaking, no infringement of third-party IP
8. **Assets**: All assets in good working condition, no material defects

**Buyer's Reps (Fewer)**:
1. **Corporate Power**: Authority to enter transaction, all approvals obtained
2. **Financing**: Funds available to pay purchase price
3. **No Conflicts**: Transaction does not violate any agreements/court orders

**Survival & Indemnity**:
```
"Seller's representations survive for 3 years from Closing Date. Buyer may claim indemnity
for breach if:
- Individual claim >₹1 crore (threshold - de minimis)
- Aggregate claims >₹10 crore (basket - Buyer bears first ₹10cr of losses)
- Subject to cap of ₹100 crore (28% of purchase price - seller's maximum liability)"
```

**Warranty & Indemnity (W&I) Insurance** (Optional):
- **Purpose**: Buyer purchases insurance policy covering seller's breach of reps/warranties
- **Benefit**: Avoid escrow (release full purchase price to seller on closing), sue insurer (not seller) for breach
- **Cost**: 1-2% of purchase price (₹3.5-7 crore for ₹350cr transaction)
- **Coverage**: Typically 10-30% of purchase price (₹35-105 crore coverage)
- **Common in**: Large private equity transactions (₹500cr+ deal size)

**c) Ancillary Documents**:
1. **Asset Transfer Deed**: Specific transfer deed for each asset class (land deed, machinery transfer, IP assignment)
2. **Assignment Agreements**: Assign contracts to buyer (customer/supplier consent required if non-assignable clauses)
3. **Employee Transfer Letters**: Inform employees of transfer under Section 25FF, new employer = Buyer (continuity of service maintained)
4. **Lender Consent Letters**: Lenders consent to loan assumption by buyer (or substitution of security)

### Step 4: Regulatory Approvals (30-60 days, parallel with Step 3)

**a) Competition Commission of India (CCI)** - If Thresholds Exceeded

**Thresholds** (Competition Act, Section 5):
- **Assets**: Combined assets in India >₹2,000 crore OR Target assets >₹750 crore
- **Turnover**: Combined turnover in India >₹6,000 crore OR Target turnover >₹2,250 crore

**Example**:
- Buyer's India assets: ₹5,000 crore
- Target undertaking assets: ₹800 crore (>₹750cr threshold)
- **CCI approval required**: Form I filing, 30-day approval (Phase I) unless AAEC concerns

**b) Lender Consents**:
- If undertaking has secured loans (e.g., term loan for plant, hypothecation of inventory), lender consent required
- **Options**:
  1. **Loan Assumption**: Buyer assumes seller's loan (lender substitutes borrower, releases seller's guarantee)
  2. **Loan Prepayment**: Seller repays loan from sale proceeds, buyer funds free from debt
- **Typical**: Buyer assumes debt (reduces cash outflow), lender evaluates buyer's creditworthiness

**c) Sector-Specific Approvals**:
- **Pharma/Healthcare**: Drug license transfer, FDA approval transfer (manufacturing license specific to entity)
- **Telecom**: Spectrum license transfer (DoT approval, 1-2 months)
- **Infrastructure**: Environmental clearance transfer, land use permission
- **Financial Services**: RBI/SEBI/IRDAI approval for regulated entities

### Step 5: Closing (1 day)

**Closing Checklist**:

**Seller Delivers**:
1. ✅ Board resolution approving transfer (certified copy)
2. ✅ Shareholder resolution (if Section 180 triggered - special resolution certified copy)
3. ✅ Asset transfer deeds (signed, notarized)
4. ✅ Physical possession of assets (keys to plant, inventory handover certificates)
5. ✅ Employee transfer letters (signed by Seller CEO)
6. ✅ Contract assignments (customer/supplier consents attached)
7. ✅ IP assignment deeds (trademark, patent assignment registered with IP office within 90 days post-closing)
8. ✅ Regulatory approvals (CCI order copy, lender consent letters)

**Buyer Delivers**:
1. ✅ Purchase price payment (₹350 crore wire transfer to seller's account)
2. ✅ Assumption of liabilities (undertaking to pay transferred liabilities from closing date)
3. ✅ Board resolution approving acquisition (certified copy)

**Post-Closing Actions** (Within 30 days):
1. **Asset Registration**: Register asset transfers (land with Sub-Registrar, vehicles with RTO, IP with IP office)
2. **Employee Formalities**: Transfer PF accounts (EPFO Form 13), ESI accounts
3. **Contract Novation**: Formally novate contracts (tripartite agreement: Seller, Buyer, Counterparty)
4. **Regulatory Filings**: Inform ROC of asset disposal (Seller), inform ROC of asset acquisition (Buyer)

### Step 6: Stamp Duty & Registration (Within 30 days of Closing)

**Stamp Duty Liability**:
- **Payable By**: Typically buyer (commercial custom), but can be negotiated
- **Rate**: State-specific, 3-7% of purchase price (varies by state and asset type)
  - **Maharashtra**: 5% on sale of immovable property, 0.25% on movable property (machinery, inventory)
  - **Karnataka**: 5.6% on immovable property
  - **Delhi**: 6% on immovable property
- **Base**: Purchase price OR Market value of assets, **whichever is higher** (stamp duty authority may challenge low valuations)

**Example**:
- Purchase Price: ₹350 crore
- Immovable Property (land, building): ₹150 crore (FMV ₹250cr per valuation report)
- **Stamp Duty Base**: ₹250 crore (higher of purchase allocation ₹150cr vs FMV ₹250cr)
- **Stamp Duty** (Maharashtra 5%): ₹250cr × 5% = ₹12.5 crore (payable by buyer)
- **Total Buyer Outflow**: ₹350cr purchase + ₹12.5cr stamp duty = ₹362.5 crore

**Registration**:
- **Immovable Property**: Must register sale deed with Sub-Registrar within 4 months (Section 17, Registration Act)
- **Penalty**: If late, additional fee 10% per year (up to 5 years max), then registration barred
- **Process**: Pay stamp duty → Get sale deed executed on stamp paper → Submit to Sub-Registrar with documents (ID proof, title deeds, NOCs) → Registration within 7-30 days

## Capital Gains Taxation on Slump Sale

### Section 50B Computation

**Statutory Formula** (Income Tax Act, Section 50B):
```
Capital Gains = Sale Consideration - Net Worth of Undertaking

Net Worth = Total Assets (book value) - Total Liabilities (book value)
```

**Example 1 - Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)**:

**Facts**:
- Seller established undertaking in Year 1 (10 years ago)
- Undertaking net worth (book value): ₹280 crore
- Sale consideration: ₹350 crore
- Holding period: 10 years (>36 months → Long-Term)

**Calculation**:
```
Capital Gains = ₹350cr - ₹280cr = ₹70 crore

Tax:
LTCG = ₹70cr × 20% = ₹14 crore (Section 112 - LTCG rate for companies)

Seller's Net Proceeds = ₹350cr - ₹14cr tax = ₹336 crore
```

**Note**: Unlike other capital assets, **NO indexation benefit** for net worth. The ₹280cr net worth (from 10 years ago) is NOT adjusted using Cost Inflation Index (CII) to current year values. This increases the capital gains and tax liability.

**Example 2 - Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)**:

**Facts**:
- Seller acquired undertaking 2 years ago (purchased from another company)
- Undertaking net worth (book value): ₹280 crore
- Sale consideration: ₹350 crore
- Holding period: 2 years (≤36 months → Short-Term)

**Calculation**:
```
Capital Gains = ₹350cr - ₹280cr = ₹70 crore

Tax:
STCG = ₹70cr × 30% = ₹21 crore (Section 111A does not apply to slump sale, taxed at normal slab rate 30% for companies)

Seller's Net Proceeds = ₹350cr - ₹21cr tax = ₹329 crore
```

**Comparison**: LTCG (₹14cr tax, 20%) vs STCG (₹21cr tax, 30%) → ₹7cr saving if held >36 months

### Unrecorded Goodwill Controversy

**Issue**: What if the undertaking has significant inherent goodwill (brand value, customer relationships, market reputation) NOT recorded on the balance sheet?

**Tax Department Position**: Net worth = Balance sheet assets - Liabilities only (book value)

**Taxpayer Position**: Net worth should include unrecorded goodwill (fair market value)

**Judicial Position** - **Manish Buildwell Pvt. Ltd. v. CIT (2016)**:

**Facts**:
- Taxpayer sold real estate undertaking for ₹61 crore
- Balance sheet net worth: ₹15 crore (land at cost ₹15cr, acquired decades ago)
- Taxpayer argued: Fair value of land ₹61cr (appreciated), unrecorded goodwill/appreciation should increase net worth to ₹61cr → Zero capital gains

**Held** (High Court):
- **Net worth = Book value only** (₹15 crore), not fair market value
- Unrecorded goodwill (inherent appreciation) is NOT added to net worth for Section 50B
- **Capital Gains** = ₹61cr - ₹15cr = ₹46 crore (taxable)

**Rationale**:
- Section 50B clearly states "net worth" without qualification, means balance sheet net worth (historical cost basis)
- If legislature intended fair value, it would have specified (as in Section 56 for FMV)
- Taxpayer cannot self-generate goodwill to reduce tax liability

**Impact**:
- **For Sellers**: Higher capital gains tax (lower net worth → higher gains)
- **Tax Planning**: Consider recording goodwill on balance sheet BEFORE slump sale (through internal valuation, but tax department may challenge if done proximity to sale)

### LTCG Exemptions (Section 54EC)

**Section 54EC** (Income Tax Act):
> "Capital gains arising from transfer of long-term capital asset (other than residential house property) shall not be charged to tax if invested in specified bonds within 6 months."

**Key Features**:
1. **Eligible Investments**:
   - NHAI (National Highways Authority of India) bonds
   - REC (Rural Electrification Corporation) bonds
2. **Maximum Investment**: ₹50 lakh per financial year (limited benefit)
3. **Lock-in**: 5 years (bonds cannot be sold/pledged/transferred)
4. **Interest**: Approximately 5-5.5% per annum (taxable)
5. **Timeline**: Must invest within 6 months of transfer date

**Example**:
- LTCG on slump sale: ₹70 crore
- Invest ₹50 lakh in NHAI bonds (within 6 months)
- **Tax Saving**: ₹50L × 20% = ₹10 lakh (exempted)
- **Taxable LTCG**: ₹69.5 crore (₹70cr - ₹50L exempted)
- **Tax Payable**: ₹69.5cr × 20% = ₹13.9 crore (vs ₹14cr without Section 54EC)

**Limitation**: ₹50L cap is trivial for large slump sales (₹70cr LTCG → only ₹50L exemption = <1% of gains). More relevant for individual sellers/smaller transactions.

## Slump Sale vs Demerger - Detailed Comparison

| Aspect | Slump Sale | Demerger (Tax-Neutral under Section 2(19AA)) |
|--------|-----------|------------------------------------------|
| **Buyer** | Third-party acquirer | Existing shareholders (proportionate distribution) |
| **Consideration** | Cash (lump sum) | Shares of resulting company |
| **Tax to Seller** | Capital gains tax (20% LTCG or 30% STCG) | **Nil** (tax-neutral if conditions met) |
| **Timeline** | **2-4 months** (bilateral agreement) | **9-12 months** (NCLT court scheme) |
| **Process** | BTA + Regulatory approvals | 10-step NCLT process + meetings + regulatory approvals |
| **Shareholder Approval** | Special resolution if >50% net worth | Special resolution (75%) always required |
| **Court Involvement** | None | NCLT approval mandatory |
| **Stamp Duty** | **3-7%** (state-specific, on purchase price) | **Nil** (court schemes exempt under state stamp acts) |
| **Flexibility** | High (negotiable terms, price, assets/liabilities) | Lower (mirror principle, no cash, all assets/liabilities transfer) |
| **Partial Sale** | Yes (can sell one undertaking, retain others) | Yes (partial demerger - demerged company continues) |
| **Strategic Rationale** | Monetize asset, exit business line, debt repayment | Unlock conglomerate discount, separate management focus, no tax |

### When to Choose Slump Sale over Demerger

**Choose Slump Sale if**:
1. **Third-Party Buyer**: Selling to external party (not distributing to shareholders)
2. **Cash Need**: Seller needs cash proceeds (debt repayment, other investments)
3. **Speed**: Need faster transaction (2-4 months vs 9-12 months)
4. **Tax Immaterial**: LTCG tax (20%) acceptable, or STCG period unavoidable
5. **Negotiation**: Want flexibility to negotiate terms, price, partial assets/liabilities

**Choose Demerger if**:
1. **Shareholder Distribution**: Want to distribute undertaking to existing shareholders (proportionate)
2. **Tax Savings**: Tax-neutral structure critical (no capital gains tax)
3. **Share Consideration**: Shareholders prefer shares in resulting company (not cash)
4. **Unlock Discount**: Remove conglomerate discount (diversified holding company trading below sum-of-parts)
5. **Long-Term**: Shareholder value creation over time (vs immediate cash exit)

**Example - Tax Comparison**:

**Scenario**: Seller wants to separate hotels undertaking (₹500cr FMV, ₹280cr net worth)

**Option 1 - Slump Sale**:
- Sell to third party for ₹500 crore cash
- Capital gains: ₹500cr - ₹280cr = ₹220 crore
- **Tax** (LTCG 20%): ₹220cr × 20% = ₹44 crore
- **Net Proceeds**: ₹500cr - ₹44cr = ₹456 crore cash

**Option 2 - Demerger** (Tax-Neutral):
- Demerge hotels to new company "HotelsCo"
- Shareholders receive HotelsCo shares (mirror shareholding)
- **Tax**: ₹0 (tax-neutral under Section 2(19AA))
- **Value to Shareholders**: HotelsCo shares worth ₹500cr (no tax leakage)

**Analysis**:
- Slump Sale: ₹456cr cash (after ₹44cr tax), but immediate liquidity
- Demerger: ₹500cr value (no tax), but illiquid shares (until listing/subsequent sale)
- **Tax Saving**: ₹44cr (9.6% of sale proceeds)

**Decision Factors**:
- If seller needs cash NOW (debt repayment, capex, dividends) → Slump Sale despite tax
- If shareholders can hold HotelsCo shares (value realization over time) → Demerger (tax-efficient)

## Practical Examples

### Example 1 - Pharmaceutical Company Divests API Unit

**Background**:
- **PharmaCorp Ltd.**: Pharmaceutical company with 2 undertakings (APIs manufacturing + Formulations)
- **Strategic Shift**: Focus on formulations (higher margin), exit APIs (capital-intensive, lower ROCE)
- **Buyer**: ChemIndia Ltd. (specialty chemicals company) wants to enter pharma APIs

**Transaction Structure**:

**Step 1 - Undertaking Identification**:
- **APIs Undertaking**:
  - Assets: Plant in Hyderabad (₹150cr), machinery (₹80cr), inventory (₹30cr), receivables (₹20cr) = ₹280cr total
  - Liabilities: Term loan (₹100cr), trade payables (₹30cr), provisions (₹10cr) = ₹140cr total
  - **Net Worth**: ₹280cr - ₹140cr = ₹140 crore (book value)
  - Employees: 350 (dedicated to APIs plant)
  - Revenue: ₹400cr per annum (last 3 years avg)
  - EBITDA: ₹60cr (15% margin)

**Step 2 - Valuation**:
- **Book Value Method**: ₹140 crore (for tax purposes)
- **DCF Method**: ₹60cr EBITDA × 8x EV/EBITDA = ₹480cr enterprise value - ₹140cr debt = ₹340cr equity value
- **Negotiated Price**: ₹300 crore (between book ₹140cr and DCF ₹340cr)

**Step 3 - Tax Calculation**:
- Holding period: 12 years (>36 months → LTCG)
- Capital gains: ₹300cr - ₹140cr = ₹160 crore
- **Tax** (20% LTCG): ₹160cr × 20% = ₹32 crore
- **PharmaCorp Net Proceeds**: ₹300cr - ₹32cr = ₹268 crore

**Step 4 - Transaction Documents**:
- **BTA**: ₹300cr lump sum for APIs undertaking
- **Reps & Warranties**: PharmaCorp reps on good title, no undisclosed liabilities, regulatory compliance
- **Indemnity Cap**: ₹90cr (30% of purchase price), Basket ₹3cr, Threshold ₹50L
- **Closing Conditions**: CCI approval (ChemIndia + PharmaCorp combined assets >₹2,000cr), lender consent for ₹100cr term loan assumption

**Step 5 - Stamp Duty**:
- Immovable property (land, building): ₹120cr (allocation)
- **Stamp Duty** (Telangana 5%): ₹120cr × 5% = ₹6 crore (payable by ChemIndia)

**Step 6 - Post-Closing**:
- Employees: 350 employees transfer to ChemIndia (Section 25FF, continuity of service)
- Contracts: 50 customer contracts assigned to ChemIndia (consents obtained from top 10 customers)
- Regulatory: Drug manufacturing license transferred (State FDA approval, 60 days post-closing)

**Outcome**:
- **PharmaCorp**: ₹268cr cash (after ₹32cr tax), exits low-margin APIs, focuses on formulations
- **ChemIndia**: Acquires operational APIs plant (immediate revenue ₹400cr), enters pharma value chain
- **Total Transaction Cost**: ₹300cr purchase + ₹6cr stamp duty + ₹5cr transaction costs (legal, DD, valuation) = ₹311 crore for ChemIndia

### Example 2 - Conglomerate Divests Non-Core Hotel Business

**Background**:
- **DiverseCo Ltd.**: Conglomerate (FMCG ₹5,000cr, Hotels ₹1,000cr, Textiles ₹800cr)
- **Strategic Rationale**: Unlock conglomerate discount (trading at ₹5,500cr market cap, 20% discount to ₹6,800cr sum-of-parts)
- **Option Analysis**: Demerger vs Slump Sale

**Transaction Structure - Slump Sale**:

**Step 1 - Undertaking Valuation**:
- **Hotels Undertaking**:
  - 10 properties (5-star hotels in metro cities)
  - Net worth (book): ₹600 crore (land ₹250cr acquired 30 years ago, buildings ₹200cr depreciated, furniture ₹50cr, net working capital ₹100cr)
  - Fair Market Value: ₹1,500 crore (land appreciated 10x, properties worth ₹1,500cr per independent valuation)
  - Revenue: ₹800cr per annum, EBITDA ₹200cr (25% margin)
  - Employees: 2,000 (dedicated hotel staff)

**Step 2 - Buyer Identification**:
- **HospitalityPE Fund**: Private equity fund focused on hospitality sector
- **Offer**: ₹1,400 crore cash (close to FMV ₹1,500cr, premium to book ₹600cr)

**Step 3 - Tax Calculation**:
- Holding period: 30+ years (>36 months → LTCG)
- Capital gains: ₹1,400cr - ₹600cr = ₹800 crore
- **Tax** (20% LTCG): ₹800cr × 20% = ₹160 crore (MASSIVE tax leakage)
- **DiverseCo Net Proceeds**: ₹1,400cr - ₹160cr = ₹1,240 crore

**Step 4 - Stamp Duty**:
- Immovable property: ₹1,400cr (entire purchase price, all hotels are land + building)
- **Stamp Duty** (weighted avg across states, assume 5%): ₹1,400cr × 5% = ₹70 crore (payable by buyer)

**Transaction Costs**:
- DiverseCo: ₹160cr tax + ₹10cr transaction costs (legal, advisory) = ₹170 crore outflow
- HospitalityPE: ₹1,400cr purchase + ₹70cr stamp duty + ₹15cr transaction costs = ₹1,485 crore total

**Alternative - Demerger** (Tax-Neutral):

**Step 1 - Demerger Structure**:
- Transfer Hotels undertaking to new company "HotelsCo Ltd."
- DiverseCo shareholders receive HotelsCo shares (mirror shareholding: Promoter 55%, Public 45% → same in HotelsCo)

**Step 2 - Tax Calculation**:
- **Tax to DiverseCo**: ₹0 (tax-neutral demerger under Section 2(19AA) - conditions met)
- **Tax to Shareholders**: ₹0 (no taxable event on receiving HotelsCo shares)

**Step 3 - Shareholder Proceeds**:
- Post-demerger, shareholders own:
  - DiverseCo shares (FMCG + Textiles): ₹4,500cr market cap
  - HotelsCo shares: ₹1,500cr market cap (assuming 10% premium to FMV post-demerger, conglomerate discount removed)
- **Total Value**: ₹4,500cr + ₹1,500cr = ₹6,000cr (vs pre-demerger ₹5,500cr, 9% value creation)

**Step 4 - HospitalityPE Acquisition** (Post-Demerger):
- After demerger, HospitalityPE can acquire HotelsCo shares from open market (if listed) or make open offer (SEBI Takeover Code)
- Shareholders pay capital gains tax on share sale (20% LTCG on appreciated value from demerger to sale date)

**Comparison**:

| Metric | Slump Sale (Direct) | Demerger + Share Sale |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Tax to DiverseCo** | ₹160 crore (20% LTCG) | ₹0 (tax-neutral) |
| **Shareholder Value** | ₹1,240cr cash (after DiverseCo pays ₹160cr tax, dividends out to shareholders) | ₹1,500cr HotelsCo shares (no tax leakage) |
| **Timeline** | 3 months (slump sale) | 10 months (demerger 9-12 months) + share sale 1-2 months = 11-14 months |
| **Buyer Cost** | ₹1,485cr (including stamp duty) | ₹1,500cr (share purchase, 0.25% STT only) + ₹3.75cr STT = ₹1,504cr |
| **Tax Efficiency** | Lower (₹160cr tax leakage) | **Higher** (₹0 tax to company, shareholders pay LTCG only on subsequent sale appreciation) |

**Decision**: DiverseCo opts for **Demerger** route despite 8-month delay, to save ₹160cr corporate tax. Shareholders receive HotelsCo shares, can sell to HospitalityPE post-listing (pay personal LTCG 10% without indexation on listed shares, much lower than 20% corporate tax).

## Landmark Cases

### 1. CIT v. Gillanders Arbuthnot & Co. (1973 SC)

**Facts**:
- Gillanders Arbuthnot (tea company) sold its entire tea business as a going concern to another company for a lump sum price
- Question: Is this sale taxable as "business income" (normal rates) or "capital gains" (lower rates)?

**Held** (Supreme Court):
- Sale of entire business undertaking (going concern) is **capital gains** transaction, not revenue (business income)
- Even though seller was in the business of tea trading, the sale of the entire business itself is a capital asset disposal
- This case laid the foundation for "slump sale" concept (though the term wasn't used then)

**Principle**:
- **Going concern sale = Capital gains treatment** (not business income)
- Differentiate between: (a) Selling inventory in ordinary course (business income) vs (b) Selling the entire business (capital gains)

### 2. Amalgamated Coal Fields Ltd. v. CIT (1970 SC)

**Facts**:
- Company sold its coal mining undertaking
- Question: How to compute capital gains when an entire undertaking (not individual assets) is sold?

**Held** (Supreme Court):
- When undertaking is sold as a whole, compute capital gains on **net worth basis**
- Net worth = Total assets (book value) - Total liabilities
- Cannot compute capital gains by valuing individual assets separately (that would be itemized asset sale, not slump sale)

**Principle**:
- **Net worth method**: Cost of acquisition for slump sale = Book value net worth (assets - liabilities)
- This principle was later codified in Section 50B (2000 amendment)

### 3. CIT v. Smifs Securities Ltd. (2012 Supreme Court)

**Facts**:
- Smifs sold its broking undertaking to another broking firm for ₹55 lakh
- Smifs computed capital gains as: ₹55L - ₹8L (net worth) = ₹47L gains
- Tax department argued: Smifs should pay capital gains on each asset separately (securities, computers, etc.) using individual asset cost

**Held** (Supreme Court):
- Once it's a **slump sale** (lump sum, no individual asset values), Section 50B applies mandatorily
- Capital gains = Sale consideration - Net worth (aggregate book value)
- Cannot bifurcate and apply asset-specific capital gains rules (like Section 50 for depreciable assets)

**Principle**:
- **Section 50B is overriding** for slump sales
- Tax department cannot cherry-pick individual asset rules to maximize tax

### 4. Manish Buildwell Pvt. Ltd. v. CIT (2016 Delhi High Court)

**Facts**:
- Manish Buildwell sold real estate undertaking for ₹61 crore
- Balance sheet net worth: ₹15 crore (land at cost, acquired decades ago)
- Taxpayer argued: Net worth should include unrecorded appreciation/goodwill (land FMV ₹61cr) → Net worth ₹61cr → Zero capital gains
- Tax department: Net worth = ₹15cr (book value) → Capital gains ₹46cr

**Held** (High Court - Favored Tax Department):
- **Net worth = Book value only** (balance sheet assets - liabilities at historical cost)
- Unrecorded goodwill, appreciation, fair market value adjustments are NOT added to net worth
- Section 50B does not permit FMV adjustments to net worth

**Principle**:
- **Unrecorded goodwill NOT included in net worth** for Section 50B
- This increases capital gains (lower net worth → higher gains → higher tax)
- Tax planning: If planning slump sale, consider recording goodwill/revaluation on balance sheet BEFORE sale (but tax department may challenge proximity)

**Impact**:
- **Seller's perspective**: Harsh rule - pay tax on appreciation even if not recorded on books
- **Buyer's perspective**: Beneficial - can claim depreciation on goodwill purchased (difference between purchase price and net worth of assets acquired)

### 5. Adhunik Metaliks Ltd. case (2009 ITAT)

**Facts**:
- Adhunik sold its iron ore mining business (mines, machinery, mining licenses, employees, contracts) for lump sum consideration
- Tax department challenged: This is not slump sale, just land sale (piecemeal asset sale)

**Held** (Tribunal - Favored Taxpayer):
- Transfer of **going concern** (operational business capable of independent operation) = Slump sale
- Here, iron ore business was transferred as a whole (not just land, but entire operational setup)
- Mines + Machinery + Employees + Contracts = Undertaking (going concern)

**Principle**:
- **"Undertaking" = Going concern** capable of standalone operation
- Must transfer business as a whole (not cherry-picking assets)
- Factors: Separate operations, identifiable revenue stream, dedicated employees, transferable contracts

## Compliance Checklist

### Pre-Transaction (30-60 days before closing)

**Seller's Checklist**:
- [ ] **Undertaking Identification**: Define scope (assets, liabilities, employees, contracts to be transferred vs retained)
- [ ] **Financial Statements**: Prepare separate financial statements for undertaking (last 3 years) if not separately maintained
- [ ] **Valuation Report**: Appoint registered valuer to certify net worth (Section 2(42C) compliance)
- [ ] **Due Diligence Preparation**: Organize documents (title deeds, contracts, licenses, employee records, tax assessments) for buyer's DD
- [ ] **Board Resolution**: Convene board meeting to approve slump sale, authorize management to negotiate and execute BTA
- [ ] **Shareholder Resolution**: If undertaking >50% of net worth (Section 180), convene EGM for special resolution (75% approval)
- [ ] **Lender Consents**: Notify lenders of proposed sale, obtain in-principle consent for loan assumption/prepayment
- [ ] **Employee Communication**: Inform employees of proposed transfer (Section 25FF notice), address concerns, retention strategy for key employees

**Buyer's Checklist**:
- [ ] **Due Diligence**: Conduct comprehensive DD (financial, legal, operational, tax, environmental, IP)
- [ ] **Valuation**: Independent valuation to justify purchase price, identify hidden liabilities for price negotiation
- [ ] **Financing Arrangement**: Secure debt/equity financing for purchase price + stamp duty + transaction costs
- [ ] **Board Resolution**: Board approval to acquire undertaking, authorize management to execute BTA
- [ ] **CCI Filing**: If thresholds exceeded (assets >₹2,000cr or turnover >₹6,000cr combined), file Form I with CCI (30 days for approval)
- [ ] **Sector Approvals**: Identify sector-specific approvals (telecom, pharma, financial services) and initiate applications

### Transaction Documents (30-45 days before closing)

- [ ] **Business Transfer Agreement (BTA)**: Negotiate and finalize (purchase price, transferred assets/liabilities, reps & warranties, indemnity, closing conditions)
- [ ] **Disclosure Schedules**: Seller prepares detailed schedules (asset list, liability list, employee list with salaries, contract list, litigation list)
- [ ] **Asset Transfer Deeds**: Draft specific transfer deeds (land sale deed, machinery transfer deed, IP assignment deed)
- [ ] **Assignment Agreements**: Draft contract assignments (customer/supplier consent required if non-assignable clauses)
- [ ] **Employee Transfer Letters**: Draft transfer letters for all employees (Section 25FF notice, new employer details, continuity of service confirmation)
- [ ] **Lender Consent Letters**: Finalize loan assumption agreements (lender substitutes borrower, releases seller's guarantee)

### Closing (T-0)

- [ ] **Conditions Precedent Check**: Verify all closing conditions met (CCI approval received, lender consents obtained, no MAC in undertaking)
- [ ] **Payment**: Buyer wires purchase price to seller's account (₹350cr)
- [ ] **Asset Transfer Deeds Execution**: Seller executes and delivers signed, notarized asset transfer deeds
- [ ] **Physical Possession**: Seller delivers keys, access codes, inventory handover certificates
- [ ] **Employee Transfer Letters**: Seller delivers signed employee transfer letters to employees + copies to buyer
- [ ] **Contract Assignments**: Tripartite assignment agreements executed (Seller, Buyer, Counterparty)
- [ ] **Regulatory Approvals**: Exchange copies of CCI order, lender consent letters, sector approvals

### Post-Closing (Within 30 days)

**Registration & Filings**:
- [ ] **Stamp Duty Payment**: Buyer pays stamp duty (3-7% of purchase price or FMV, whichever higher)
- [ ] **Land Registration**: Register sale deed with Sub-Registrar (within 4 months of execution, 10% penalty if late)
- [ ] **Vehicle Transfer**: Transfer vehicles to buyer's name with RTO (Form 29, 30)
- [ ] **IP Assignment**: File trademark/patent assignment with IP office (within 90 days of assignment)
- [ ] **ROC Filings**:
  - Seller: File Form MGT-14 (special resolution copy if Section 180 triggered) within 30 days
  - Buyer: File Form MGT-14 (board resolution for acquisition) within 30 days

**Employee Formalities**:
- [ ] **PF Transfer**: File Form 13 with EPFO to transfer employees' PF accounts from seller to buyer (within 30 days)
- [ ] **ESI Transfer**: Transfer ESI accounts (Form 11)
- [ ] **New Appointment Letters**: Issue new appointment letters to transferred employees (optional, but recommended for clarity)

**Contract Novation**:
- [ ] **Customer Contracts**: Execute novation agreements (tripartite: Seller, Buyer, Customer) for non-assigned contracts
- [ ] **Supplier Contracts**: Execute novation agreements for critical supplier contracts
- [ ] **Lease Agreements**: If undertaking operates from leased premises, execute novation with landlord

**Tax Compliance**:
- [ ] **TDS Deduction (Buyer)**: Deduct TDS @ 1% on purchase price (Section 194IA if immovable property >₹50L) and deposit with tax authorities (Form 26QB within 30 days)
- [ ] **TDS Credit (Seller)**: Obtain Form 16B (TDS certificate) from buyer, claim TDS credit in income tax return
- [ ] **Capital Gains Return**: Seller files income tax return (ITR-6 for companies) reporting capital gains on slump sale
- [ ] **Advance Tax**: If capital gains tax >₹10,000, pay advance tax in remaining installments (15 Sep, 15 Dec, 15 Mar)

**Regulatory Notifications**:
- [ ] **Drug License Transfer** (Pharma): Apply for drug manufacturing license transfer (State FDA, 60-90 days)
- [ ] **Pollution Control**: Notify State Pollution Control Board of ownership change (within 15 days)
- [ ] **Factory License**: Transfer factory license to buyer's name (State Labor Department)
