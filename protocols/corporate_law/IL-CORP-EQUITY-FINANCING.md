# EQUITY FINANCING - INDIAN CORPORATE LAW

## OVERVIEW

Equity financing involves raising capital by issuing equity shares (or instruments convertible into equity) to investors. Companies use equity financing for growth, expansion, debt reduction, acquisitions, and meeting working capital requirements. Unlike debt, equity does not require repayment and does not create fixed interest obligations, but it dilutes existing shareholders' ownership.

**TYPES OF EQUITY FINANCING**:
1. **Public Offer**: IPO (Initial Public Offering) / FPO (Follow-on Public Offering)
2. **Rights Issue**: Offer to existing shareholders (pre-emptive rights)
3. **Private Placement**: Offer to select investors (<200 persons) without public offer
4. **Preferential Allotment**: Allotment to identified persons on preferential basis
5. **Qualified Institutions Placement (QIP)**: Offer to qualified institutional buyers (QIBs) - listed companies only
6. **Employee Stock Options (ESOPs)**: Equity to employees (covered separately)

**LEGAL FRAMEWORK**:
- **Companies Act 2013**: Section 23 (Public offer), Section 42 (Private placement), Section 62 (Rights issue)
- **SEBI (ICDR) Regulations 2018**: Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements (IPO/FPO/Rights/Preferential/QIP)
- **SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations 2015**: Post-listing compliance
- **Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act 1956**: Listing of securities

## 1. PUBLIC OFFER - IPO AND FPO

### 1.1 Initial Public Offering (IPO)

**Definition**: First time offer of equity shares to public, followed by listing on stock exchange.

**Purpose of IPO**:
1. Raise capital for growth, expansion, capex, working capital
2. Provide exit to early investors (PE/VC, promoters)
3. Enhance brand visibility, credibility
4. Create liquid market for shares (enable employee ESOPs monetization)
5. Currency for M&A (shares can be used as consideration)

**Eligibility Criteria (SEBI ICDR Regulations)**:

```
OPTION 1: PROFITABILITY ROUTE
- Net tangible assets ≥₹3 crore in each of preceding 3 years (at least 50% held in monetary assets)
- Net worth ≥₹1 crore in each of preceding 3 years
- Average pre-tax profit ≥₹15 crore in preceding 3 years (at least ₹15 crore in immediately preceding year)
- Track record: ≥3 years

OPTION 2: QIB ROUTE (for companies not meeting profitability route)
- At least 75% of issue size offered to Qualified Institutional Buyers (QIBs)
- Minimum post-issue capital: ₹10 crore or ₹25 crore (depending on listing exchange)
- QIBs must subscribe to at least 75% of QIB portion (else issue fails)
```

**Example - Profitability Route**:
```
Company: Tech Startup Ltd (Founded 2018, profitable since 2021)
Financials:
- FY 2021-22: Pre-tax profit ₹18 crore, Net worth ₹2 crore, Net tangible assets ₹3.5 crore (60% monetary)
- FY 2022-23: Pre-tax profit ₹22 crore, Net worth ₹3 crore, Net tangible assets ₹4 crore (65% monetary)
- FY 2023-24: Pre-tax profit ₹28 crore, Net worth ₹5 crore, Net tangible assets ₹5.5 crore (70% monetary)

Eligibility Check:
✓ Net tangible assets ≥₹3 crore in each of 3 years (50% monetary) ✓
✓ Net worth ≥₹1 crore in each of 3 years ✓
✓ Average pre-tax profit ₹22.67 crore ≥₹15 crore ✓
✓ FY 2023-24 pre-tax profit ₹28 crore ≥₹15 crore ✓
✓ Track record 6 years (2018-2024) ≥3 years ✓

RESULT: ELIGIBLE for IPO via Profitability Route
```

### 1.2 IPO Process

**Timeline: 180-240 days from start to listing**

**PHASE 1: PRE-ISSUE PREPARATION (90-120 days)**

**Step 1: Appoint Advisors**
- **Lead Managers (Book Running Lead Managers - BRLMs)**: Investment banks (manage IPO process, pricing, marketing)
  - Minimum 1 BRLM (large IPOs have 3-5 BRLMs)
  - Fees: 2-4% of issue size
- **Legal Advisors**: Company lawyers, BRLM lawyers (draft offer documents, ensure compliance)
- **Statutory Auditors**: Audit financials, comfort letters
- **Registrar to Issue**: Manage applications, allotment, refunds
- **Bankers to Issue**: Collect application money

**Step 2: Corporate Restructuring (if required)**
- Simplify shareholding structure (eliminate circular holdings)
- Resolve related party transactions (or disclose prominently)
- Appoint independent directors (SEBI requires ≥50% independent if chairman is executive)
- Constitute Audit Committee, Nomination & Remuneration Committee

**Step 3: Due Diligence**
- BRLMs conduct financial, legal, operational, management due diligence
- Identify red flags (litigation, regulatory non-compliance, RPT issues, promoter integrity concerns)
- Quantify risks, develop mitigation strategies

**Step 4: Valuation**
- BRLMs prepare valuation range (P/E ratio, DCF, comparable company analysis)
- Discuss with promoters, arrive at price band for book building

**PHASE 2: OFFER DOCUMENT PREPARATION (60-90 days)**

**Step 5: Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP)**
- Comprehensive disclosure document (300-500 pages typical)
- Contains: Company overview, financial statements (3 years audited), risk factors (50-100 risks), management, business model, use of proceeds, legal disclosures

**Mandatory Disclosures in DRHP**:
```
1. COMPANY INFORMATION:
   - Objects of issue (use of proceeds)
   - Summary of business
   - Industry overview
   - Regulations and policies

2. FINANCIAL INFORMATION:
   - Audited financials (3 years)
   - Restated consolidated financials
   - Management discussion and analysis (MD&A)
   - Capital structure pre and post-issue

3. RISK FACTORS:
   - Business risks, regulatory risks, financial risks
   - (50-100 risks typical, disclosed in order of materiality)

4. MANAGEMENT AND CORPORATE GOVERNANCE:
   - Board of directors, KMP
   - Compensation, shareholding
   - Related party transactions

5. LEGAL DISCLOSURES:
   - Litigation (pending/threatened)
   - Regulatory actions, show cause notices
   - Statutory and other material litigations

6. OTHER MATERIAL INFORMATION:
   - Material contracts
   - Related party transactions (last 3 years)
   - Defaults, non-payment of statutory dues
```

**Step 6: SEBI Filing**
- File DRHP with SEBI for review
- SEBI reviews within 30 days, raises observations/queries
- Company responds to SEBI observations (clarifications, amendments to DRHP)
- Iterative process: 2-3 rounds typical

**PHASE 3: MARKETING AND ROADSHOW (30 days)**

**Step 7: Roadshow (after SEBI observations resolved)**
- BRLMs organize investor meetings (institutional investors - mutual funds, insurance, FPIs, PE/VC)
- Roadshow cities: Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore (India) + Singapore, Hong Kong, London, New York (international)
- Management presents company story, answers investor questions
- Gauge investor appetite, refine pricing

**Step 8: Red Herring Prospectus (RHP) Filing**
- After SEBI observations resolved, file RHP (DRHP + response to SEBI observations)
- RHP published in newspapers (English + vernacular)
- Issue can open 3 working days after RHP filing

**PHASE 4: ISSUE OPENING (3 days subscription period)**

**Step 9: Price Band Announcement**
- BRLMs announce price band (e.g., ₹100-110 per share, max 20% band)
- Based on valuation, investor feedback from roadshow

**Step 10: Issue Opens**
- Subscription period: Minimum 3 working days, maximum 10 working days (typical: 3 days)
- Investors bid electronically (ASBA - Application Supported by Blocked Amount)
- Money blocked in investor's bank account (not transferred till allotment)

**Reservation Categories**:
```
CATEGORY                      RESERVATION      LOT SIZE
Qualified Institutional       ≥50% of issue    No limit (QIBs can bid for any amount)
Buyers (QIBs)

Non-Institutional             ≥15% of issue    >₹2 lakh application size
Investors (NIIs)

Retail Individual             ≥35% of issue    ≤₹2 lakh application size
Investors (RIIs)                                (minimum 1 lot, typically ₹15,000)
```

**Step 11: Book Building**
- BRLMs collect bids, aggregate demand at each price point
- If oversubscribed (demand >supply), BRLMs recommend price at higher end of band
- If undersubscribed, issue may fail (unless 90% subscribed in anchor investor + QIB, then issue proceeds)

**PHASE 5: ALLOTMENT AND LISTING (10 days)**

**Step 12: Price Finalization**
- BRLMs recommend final issue price (within price band)
- Company and BRLMs finalize price (typically at top of band if oversubscribed)

**Step 13: Basis of Allotment**
- Registrar prepares basis of allotment (who gets how many shares)
- **QIBs**: Proportionate allotment (if QIB portion oversubscribed, each QIB gets % allocation)
- **NIIs/RIIs**: Lottery system (if oversubscribed, some applicants get full allotment, others zero)
- **Minimum Allotment**: Each successful applicant gets at least 1 lot (e.g., 10 shares)

**Step 14: Allotment and Refund**
- Shares allotted to successful applicants (demat account credited)
- Excess money refunded to unsuccessful applicants (unblocked in bank account)
- Timeline: Allotment within T+6 days from issue close, refund within T+7 days

**Step 15: Listing**
- Shares listed on stock exchanges (NSE, BSE)
- Trading commences T+6 days from issue close (same day as allotment)
- Listing price may be higher (listing gain) or lower (listing loss) than issue price

**Example - IPO Timeline**:
```
IPO: ABC Ltd (Issue Size ₹500 crore, Price Band ₹95-100)

Day -180: Appoint BRLMs (Kotak, Morgan Stanley, ICICI)
Day -150: Complete due diligence, draft DRHP
Day -120: File DRHP with SEBI
Day -90: SEBI raises observations (30-day review)
Day -80: Company responds to SEBI observations
Day -70: SEBI satisfied, no further observations
Day -60: Roadshow (Mumbai, Bangalore, Singapore, Hong Kong, London)
Day -7: File RHP (DRHP + SEBI observations response)
Day -4: Publish RHP in newspapers, announce price band ₹95-100
Day 0: Issue Opens (9 AM)
Day 2: Issue Closes (5 PM)
Day 2 (end of day): Oversubscribed 8x (Total demand ₹4,000 crore for ₹500 crore issue)
Day 3: BRLMs recommend price ₹100 (top of band)
Day 4: Basis of allotment finalized (QIB 5x oversubscribed = 20% allocation, RII 15x = lottery)
Day 5: Shares allotted, excess money refunded
Day 6: Shares listed on NSE, BSE (Opening price ₹115 = 15% listing gain)
```

### 1.3 Follow-on Public Offering (FPO)

**Definition**: Public offer by already-listed company (subsequent offering after IPO).

**Purpose**: Raise additional capital for expansion, acquisitions, debt reduction.

**Types of FPO**:
1. **Dilutive FPO**: Company issues new shares (increases share capital, dilutes existing shareholders)
2. **Non-Dilutive FPO (OFS - Offer for Sale)**: Existing shareholders (promoters, PE/VC) sell shares (no new capital to company, just transfer of ownership)

**Pricing**: No price band required (market price determines floor price). FPO price typically ≤current market price to ensure subscription.

**Process**: Similar to IPO but faster (30-45 days) as company already SEBI-registered, listed, compliant.

**Example - FPO**:
```
Company: Infrastructure Ltd (listed on NSE since 2018, current share price ₹450)
FPO Size: ₹1,000 crore (Fresh issue ₹600 crore + OFS ₹400 crore)

Fresh Issue: 1.33 crore shares at ₹450 = ₹600 crore (dilution to company)
OFS: 0.89 crore shares at ₹450 = ₹400 crore (promoter stake sale, no dilution)

Use of Proceeds (₹600 crore fresh issue):
- ₹300 crore: Fund new highway projects (BOT - Build Operate Transfer)
- ₹200 crore: Reduce debt (improve debt-equity ratio from 1.5x to 1.0x)
- ₹100 crore: Working capital

Pre-FPO Promoter Stake: 55%
OFS: Promoters sell 0.89 crore shares (5% of pre-FPO shareholding)
Post-FPO Promoter Stake: 48% (compliance with SEBI minimum public shareholding 25%)
```

## 2. RIGHTS ISSUE (Section 62)

### 2.1 Pre-emptive Rights

**Section 62(1)(a)**: When company increases share capital by issuing new shares, existing shareholders have FIRST RIGHT to subscribe to new shares in proportion to their existing shareholding.

**Rationale**: Protect existing shareholders from dilution. Allow shareholders to maintain their ownership percentage.

**Pricing**: Typically issued at discount to market price (5-20% discount) to incentivize subscription.

**Example - Rights Issue**:
```
Company: Manufacturing Ltd (listed, 1 crore shares outstanding, current market price ₹200)
Rights Issue: 1:4 rights issue at ₹160 per share (20% discount to market price)

Meaning: For every 4 shares held, shareholder can subscribe to 1 additional share at ₹160

Shareholder A: Owns 4 lakh shares (4% of company)
Rights Entitlement: 4 lakh ÷ 4 = 1 lakh shares at ₹160
Total Investment: 1 lakh shares × ₹160 = ₹1.6 crore

If Shareholder A subscribes:
- Post-rights shareholding: 4 lakh + 1 lakh = 5 lakh shares
- Post-rights company shares: 1 crore + 0.25 crore = 1.25 crore shares
- Shareholder A's ownership: 5 lakh ÷ 1.25 crore = 4% (maintained)

If Shareholder A does NOT subscribe:
- Post-rights shareholding: 4 lakh shares (unchanged)
- Post-rights company shares: 1.25 crore shares
- Shareholder A's ownership: 4 lakh ÷ 1.25 crore = 3.2% (diluted from 4% to 3.2%)
```

### 2.2 Rights Issue Process

**Step 1: Board Approval**
- Board approves rights issue (quantum, issue price, record date)

**Step 2: Shareholder Approval (Section 62(1)(c))**
- Special resolution required (75% majority)
- Explanatory statement: Disclose objects of issue, use of proceeds, basis of issue price

**Step 3: Offer Letter to Shareholders**
- Letter of offer sent to all shareholders as on record date
- Offer letter discloses: Rights entitlement, issue price, subscription period (minimum 15 days, maximum 30 days), renunciation rights
- **Renunciation**: Shareholder can renounce (sell) rights to another person (tradable on stock exchange for 2 weeks)

**Step 4: Subscription**
- Shareholders apply within subscription period (15-30 days)
- Application form (CAF - Composite Application Form) + payment

**Step 5: Allotment**
- Fully subscribed portion allotted to applicants
- Undersubscribed portion: Unsubscribed shares offered to those who applied for more than entitlement (proportionate allotment)
- If still undersubscribed, promoters/underwriters subscribe (prevent issue failure)

**Timeline**: 60-90 days from board approval to allotment.

**Example - Rights Issue Timeline**:
```
Rights Issue: Pharma Ltd (1:3 rights at ₹250, market price ₹300)

Day -30: Board approves rights issue (₹500 crore)
Day -25: Shareholder EGM (special resolution passed 85% approval)
Day -20: File offer letter with SEBI, stock exchanges
Day -10: Offer letter dispatched to shareholders (record date: Day -15)
Day 0: Rights trading begins on stock exchange (shareholder can sell rights)
Day 0: Subscription opens
Day 14: Rights trading ends
Day 15: Subscription closes
Day 17: Basis of allotment (70% subscribed by shareholders, 20% by renunciation buyers, 10% undersubscribed)
Day 18: Promoters subscribe to undersubscribed 10% (prevent issue failure)
Day 20: Shares allotted, credited to demat accounts

Result: Issue successful (100% subscribed after promoter subscription)
```

### 2.3 Renunciation of Rights

**Renunciation**: Shareholder who does not wish to subscribe can sell (renounce) rights to another person.

**Rights Entitlement (RE)**: Traded separately on stock exchange (ticker: COMPANY-RE)

**Pricing**: RE price = Market Price - Rights Issue Price (approximately)

**Example - Renunciation Value**:
```
Company: Auto Ltd
Market Price: ₹500
Rights Issue: 1:5 at ₹400
Rights Entitlement (RE) Price (theoretical): ₹500 - ₹400 = ₹100

Shareholder B: Owns 1,000 shares, entitled to 200 rights shares
Options:
1. Subscribe: Pay ₹400 × 200 = ₹80,000, get 200 shares (value ₹100,000 post-rights)
2. Renounce: Sell 200 RE @ ₹100 each = ₹20,000 cash (no shares)
3. Partial: Subscribe to 100, sell 100 RE = ₹40,000 payment + ₹10,000 cash

Shareholder C (wants to buy more shares):
- Owns 500 shares, entitled to 100 rights shares (subscribes ✓)
- Buys 100 RE from market @ ₹100 = ₹10,000
- Exercises 100 RE: Pays ₹400 × 100 = ₹40,000
- Total investment: ₹10,000 (RE) + ₹40,000 (subscription) = ₹50,000 for 100 shares (effectively ₹500/share = market price)
```

## 3. PRIVATE PLACEMENT (Section 42)

### 3.1 Private Placement Rules

**Section 42**: Company can issue securities (shares, debentures, convertibles) to select persons via private placement (without public offer).

**Limits**:
```
Maximum Offerees: <200 persons per financial year (excluding QIBs and employees)
(If offer to >200 persons → Deemed public offer, requires prospectus, SEBI approval)

Minimum Application: ₹20,000 per person (retail investors minimum ticket size)

Lock-in: If shares issued at <market price or <fair value → 1 year lock-in (cannot sell)
```

**Procedure (Section 42(3))**:
1. **Board Resolution**: Approve private placement (issue size, price, terms)
2. **Private Placement Offer Letter**: Send to identified investors (not publicly advertised)
3. **Offer Letter Contents**: Objects, price, terms, risk factors, financials (last 2 years), promoter background
4. **Record Date**: Allotment only to those who received offer letter and paid before record date
5. **Shareholder Approval (if required)**: Special resolution if preferential allotment to related parties or >special resolution threshold

**Timeline**: 30-60 days from board approval to allotment.

**Example - Private Placement**:
```
Company: Software Pvt Ltd (unlisted, 100 shareholders, 10 lakh shares outstanding)
Private Placement: ₹20 crore (10 lakh shares at ₹200 per share, 50% dilution)

Investors: 15 HNIs + 5 family offices = 20 investors (within 200-person limit ✓)

Process:
Day 0: Board approves private placement (₹20 crore at ₹200/share)
Day 2: Offer letter sent to 20 identified investors (no public advertisement)
Day 15: 18 investors accept, pay ₹19 crore (95% subscribed)
Day 16: Board decides to issue 9.5 lakh shares (accept 95%, don't wait for 100%)
Day 20: Shares allotted to 18 investors

Post-Issue Capital:
- Old shareholders: 10 lakh shares (51.3%)
- New investors: 9.5 lakh shares (48.7%)
- Total: 19.5 lakh shares
```

### 3.2 Preferential Allotment

**Definition**: Allotment of shares to select persons (identified by name) on preferential basis (not on proportionate basis like rights issue).

**When Used**: Allot to strategic investor, PE/VC, promoter, or public shareholders (without offering to all).

**SEBI Requirements (ICDR Regulation 164-174)**:
```
LISTED COMPANIES ONLY (unlisted companies can use simple private placement):

1. Special Resolution: Shareholders must approve by special resolution (75% majority)

2. Pricing:
   - Minimum Price = HIGHER of:
     (a) Average of weekly high/low prices in last 26 weeks OR
     (b) Average of weekly high/low prices in last 2 weeks
   - Cannot issue at discount (except to employees under ESOP)

3. Lock-in:
   - Promoter/Promoter Group: 3 years lock-in
   - Non-promoter allottees: 1 year lock-in (if <₹25/share or <price calculated above)
   - Public shareholders: No lock-in (if issued at market price)

4. Timeline: Allotment within 15 days of shareholder approval (else approval lapses)

5. Disclosure: Publish newspaper advertisement within 10 days of board meeting (announce preferential allotment)
```

**Example - Preferential Allotment to PE Fund**:
```
Company: Retail Ltd (listed, current price ₹150, 5 crore shares outstanding)
Preferential Allotment: 1 crore shares to PE Fund XYZ at ₹140/share (₹140 crore)

Pricing Check:
- 26-week average price: ₹135
- 2-week average price: ₹148
- Minimum Price: HIGHER of (₹135 or ₹148) = ₹148
- Issue Price: ₹140 < ₹148 → VIOLATION ✗

Corrected Issue Price: ₹148/share (₹148 crore total)

Procedure:
Day 0: Board approves preferential allotment (1 crore shares at ₹148 to PE Fund XYZ)
Day 5: Newspaper advertisement published (announce preferential allotment)
Day 10: Notice of EGM sent to shareholders (explanatory statement: PE Fund to provide growth capital, PE Fund's track record, use of proceeds)
Day 30: EGM held, special resolution passed (82% approval)
Day 35: Allotment completed (within 15 days of EGM ✓)
Day 36: Stock exchange disclosure (PE Fund now owns 16.7% = 1 crore ÷ 6 crore shares)

Lock-in: 1 year (PE Fund cannot sell till Day 365)
```

## 4. QUALIFIED INSTITUTIONS PLACEMENT (QIP)

### 4.1 QIP Overview

**Definition**: Listed company's issue of equity shares/convertibles to Qualified Institutional Buyers (QIBs) only (not retail, not HNIs).

**QIBs**: Mutual funds, FPIs, banks, insurance companies, PFs, pension funds, VCFs, AIFs.

**Advantages**:
- **Fast**: 2-3 weeks (vs 6 months for IPO/FPO)
- **No Retail Participation**: Only institutions (no ASBA, no allotment complexity)
- **Lower Cost**: No underwriting fees, no extensive marketing
- **Flexible Pricing**: Price discovered via book building, close to market price

**Eligibility (SEBI ICDR Regulation 85)**:
```
1. Listed for at least 1 year on stock exchange
2. Continuous listing for at least 1 year (no delisting/suspension)
3. No outstanding convertible securities >25% of post-issue paid-up capital
```

**Limits**:
```
QIP Size: Maximum 50% of paid-up capital + free reserves per financial year
(If issue >50%, requires shareholder approval)

Aggregate: QIP + Preferential + Other Issues ≤2x paid-up capital in any FY
```

**Example - QIP Size Calculation**:
```
Company: Bank Ltd (listed since 2015)
Paid-up Capital: ₹1,000 crore (10 crore shares × ₹100 face value)
Free Reserves: ₹3,000 crore
Current Share Price: ₹500

QIP Limit: 50% of (₹1,000 crore + ₹3,000 crore) = ₹2,000 crore
Number of Shares: ₹2,000 crore ÷ ₹480 (assume QIP price) = 4.17 crore shares (29% dilution)

Post-QIP:
- Old Shares: 10 crore (71%)
- QIP Shares: 4.17 crore (29%)
- Total: 14.17 crore shares
```

### 4.2 QIP Process

**Step 1: Board Approval**
- Board approves QIP (maximum size, floor price formula, issue terms)

**Step 2: Price Floor Calculation**
```
Floor Price = HIGHER of:
(a) Average of weekly high/low prices in last 26 weeks, OR
(b) Average of weekly high/low prices in last 2 weeks

Discount: Up to 5% discount to floor price allowed
```

**Step 3: QIP Placement Document**
- Placement document sent to QIBs (not filed with SEBI, not public)
- Contains: Company overview, financials, risks, use of proceeds

**Step 4: Book Building (1-2 days)**
- QIBs submit bids (price, quantity)
- Price typically close to market price (small discount)

**Step 5: Allotment (7 days from board approval)**
```
Minimum 10% allotment to MFs (if MFs apply)
Remaining allotment: Discretionary (issuer decides which QIBs to allot, how much)
```

**Step 6: Listing (1 day after allotment)**
- Shares listed and tradable
- Lock-in: 1 year

**Timeline**: 15-20 days from board approval to listing (fastest equity raising method).

**Example - QIP Timeline**:
```
Company: FMCG Ltd (share price ₹1,200, raising ₹1,500 crore via QIP)

Day 0: Board approves QIP (up to ₹1,500 crore, floor price ₹1,140 = 5% discount to 26-week avg ₹1,200)
Day 1: Placement document sent to 50 QIBs
Day 2: Roadshow (virtual meeting with QIBs, management presents)
Day 3: Book building opens 9 AM
Day 3: Book building closes 5 PM (Total demand ₹3,000 crore = 2x oversubscribed)
Day 4: Finalize allotment (Price: ₹1,180, slightly below floor, Shares: 1.27 crore)
Day 5: Shares allotted to 35 QIBs (MFs: 15%, FPIs: 55%, Insurance: 20%, Others: 10%)
Day 6: Shares listed on NSE, BSE (trading begins)

Result: ₹1,500 crore raised in 6 days (vs 6 months for FPO)
```

## 5. PRICING AND VALUATION

### 5.1 Valuation Methods

**1. Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**:
```
P/E Ratio = Market Price per Share ÷ Earnings per Share (EPS)

Valuation: Target Price = EPS × Peer Average P/E

Example:
Company: IT Services Ltd
EPS: ₹15
Peer Average P/E: 25x (TCS 30x, Infosys 28x, Wipro 20x, HCL Tech 22x → Avg 25x)
Target Price: ₹15 × 25 = ₹375
```

**2. Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio**:
```
P/B Ratio = Market Price per Share ÷ Book Value per Share

Used for asset-heavy companies (banks, real estate, manufacturing)

Example:
Company: Bank Ltd
Book Value per Share: ₹200
Peer Average P/B: 2.5x
Target Price: ₹200 × 2.5 = ₹500
```

**3. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**:
```
DCF = Sum of (Future Cash Flows ÷ (1 + Discount Rate)^Year)

Steps:
1. Project free cash flows for 5-10 years
2. Calculate terminal value (beyond projection period)
3. Discount all cash flows to present value (WACC as discount rate)
4. Sum = Enterprise Value → Subtract debt, add cash = Equity Value → Divide by shares = Price per share

Example:
Company: Pharma Ltd (10 crore shares)
Projected FCF: Year 1: ₹100 cr, Year 2: ₹120 cr, Year 3: ₹140 cr, Year 4: ₹160 cr, Year 5: ₹180 cr
Terminal Value (Year 5): ₹180 cr ÷ (12% WACC - 3% growth) = ₹2,000 cr
Discount Rate (WACC): 12%

Present Value:
Year 1: ₹100 ÷ 1.12 = ₹89 cr
Year 2: ₹120 ÷ 1.25 = ₹96 cr
Year 3: ₹140 ÷ 1.40 = ₹100 cr
Year 4: ₹160 ÷ 1.57 = ₹102 cr
Year 5: (₹180 + ₹2,000) ÷ 1.76 = ₹1,239 cr
Total Enterprise Value: ₹1,626 cr

Equity Value: ₹1,626 cr - ₹200 cr debt + ₹50 cr cash = ₹1,476 cr
Price per Share: ₹1,476 cr ÷ 10 cr shares = ₹147.6
```

### 5.2 Pricing for Different Issue Types

| Issue Type | Pricing Method | Typical Discount/Premium |
|---|---|---|
| IPO | Book Building (discover price via investor bids) | Price band ±20%, finalized based on demand |
| FPO | Floor price (≤market price) | 0-10% discount to market price |
| Rights Issue | Discount to market price | 10-25% discount (incentivize subscription) |
| Preferential Allotment | Floor price (26-week or 2-week avg, higher) | No discount (except ESOPs) |
| QIP | Floor price (26-week or 2-week avg, higher) | 0-5% discount to floor price |

## 6. UNDERWRITING

### 6.1 Underwriting Agreement

**Definition**: Underwriter agrees to subscribe to unsubscribed portion of issue (guarantee full subscription).

**Underwriters**: BRLMs (merchant banks), institutional investors.

**Fees**: 1-3% of underwritten amount (risk-based pricing).

**Types of Underwriting**:
```
1. FIRM UNDERWRITING: Underwriter commits to buy specific number of shares (regardless of subscription level)
   - Used when issue success uncertain
   - Higher fees (2-3%)

2. STANDBY UNDERWRITING: Underwriter commits to buy ONLY unsubscribed portion
   - If issue fully subscribed, underwriter pays nothing
   - Lower fees (1-2%)
```

**Example - Underwriting**:
```
IPO: Textile Ltd (₹300 crore issue, ₹100/share, 3 crore shares)
Underwriters: Bank A (50%), Bank B (30%), Bank C (20%)
Underwriting Fee: 2% of underwritten amount

Scenario 1: 100% Subscribed (Public: 3 crore shares)
- Underwriters pay nothing
- Underwriters earn ₹6 crore fees (2% of ₹300 crore)

Scenario 2: 70% Subscribed (Public: 2.1 crore shares, Unsubscribed: 0.9 crore shares)
- Underwriters subscribe to 0.9 crore shares @ ₹100 = ₹90 crore
  - Bank A: 0.45 crore shares (50% of 0.9 cr) = ₹45 crore
  - Bank B: 0.27 crore shares (30% of 0.9 cr) = ₹27 crore
  - Bank C: 0.18 crore shares (20% of 0.9 cr) = ₹18 crore
- Underwriters earn ₹6 crore fees - ₹90 crore investment = Net ₹84 crore outflow
- Underwriters hold shares (can sell after lock-in, typically 90 days)
```

### 6.2 Green Shoe Option (Over-Allotment)

**Definition**: Option for issuer to issue additional shares (up to 15% of issue size) if demand exceeds supply (oversubscription).

**Purpose**: Stabilize post-listing price (prevent sharp price rise due to excess demand).

**Mechanism**:
1. Issue opens with green shoe option (up to 115% of original size can be allotted)
2. If oversubscribed >115%, allot 115% (original 100% + green shoe 15%)
3. If oversubscribed <115%, allot actual subscription

**Example - Green Shoe**:
```
IPO: Logistics Ltd (₹500 crore, 5 crore shares @ ₹100, with 15% green shoe)
Maximum Allotment: 115% of 5 crore = 5.75 crore shares (₹575 crore)

Scenario 1: 150% Subscribed (₹750 crore demand)
- Allot 5.75 crore shares (115% = full green shoe exercised)
- Reject 1.75 crore shares worth of applications (proportionate rejection)

Scenario 2: 110% Subscribed (₹550 crore demand)
- Allot 5.5 crore shares (110% = partial green shoe exercised)

Scenario 3: 90% Subscribed (₹450 crore demand)
- Allot 4.5 crore shares (90% only, no green shoe)

Post-Listing Stabilization:
- If price falls below issue price: Stabilizing agent (BRLM) buys shares from market (support price)
- If price rises: Green shoe shares provide supply (prevent excessive price rise)
```

## 7. LISTING REQUIREMENTS

### 7.1 Listing on Stock Exchanges

**Mandatory Listing (Section 40)**:
```
Section 40(1): Every public company making public offer MUST list shares on recognized stock exchange
- If IPO, must list (cannot remain unlisted after public offer)
- Listing application within 7 days of closure of issue
- Stock exchange approval within 7 days
```

**Listing Fees**:
- One-time listing fee: 0.01%-0.05% of issue size (NSE, BSE charge separately)
- Annual listing fee: ₹5 lakh - ₹50 lakh (based on market cap)

**Listing Conditions (SEBI LODR)**:
1. **Minimum Public Shareholding**: 25% (promoters can hold max 75%)
2. **Corporate Governance**: Independent directors (≥50% if chairman executive), Audit Committee, N&R Committee, Stakeholders' Relationship Committee
3. **Financial Reporting**: Quarterly results (within 45 days of quarter-end), annual results (within 60 days of FYE)
4. **Disclosure**: Price-sensitive information (immediately), shareholding pattern (quarterly), corporate governance report (quarterly)

**Example - Listing Conditions**:
```
Company: Healthcare Ltd (IPO ₹800 crore, promoted by Dr. A and family)
Pre-IPO: Promoters 100%
Post-IPO: Promoters 60%, Public 40%

SEBI MPS (Minimum Public Shareholding): 25% required
- Company compliant ✓ (40% > 25%)

Corporate Governance:
- Board: 8 directors (4 executive including MD, 4 independent) → 50% independent ✓
- Audit Committee: 3 independent directors ✓
- N&R Committee: 3 directors (2 independent, 1 non-executive) ✓

Quarterly Compliance:
- Q1 Results: Due August 14, 2024 (45 days from June 30 quarter-end)
- Shareholding Pattern: Filed August 14, 2024 (within 21 days of quarter-end)
- Corporate Governance Report: Filed August 14, 2024
```

### 7.2 Continuous Disclosure Obligations

**Material Events (Regulation 30)**: Disclose to stock exchange IMMEDIATELY (within 24 hours):
- Quarterly/annual financial results
- Dividend declaration
- Change in directors, KMP, auditor
- Major contracts, acquisitions, disposals
- Litigation, regulatory action
- Fundraising (QIP, preferential, buyback)

**Shareholding Pattern (Regulation 31)**: Disclose within 21 days of quarter-end:
- Promoter holding, public holding
- Top 10 shareholders
- Pledged shares (if promoters pledged shares for loans)

**Corporate Governance Report (Regulation 27)**: Quarterly disclosure:
- Board composition, committee composition
- Board meetings held, attendance
- Related party transactions
- Compliance with governance norms

## 8. POST-ISSUE COMPLIANCE

### 8.1 Use of IPO Proceeds

**Monitoring (SEBI ICDR Regulation 16)**:
- Company must use IPO proceeds ONLY for objects stated in prospectus
- Quarterly disclosure: Amount utilized, heads of utilization, unutilized amount (parked in liquid investments)
- Audit Committee monitors utilization

**Example - IPO Proceeds Utilization**:
```
IPO: Renewable Energy Ltd (₹1,000 crore IPO, objects: ₹600 cr solar projects, ₹200 cr debt repayment, ₹100 cr working capital, ₹100 cr issue expenses)

Quarter 1 (Post-IPO):
- Solar Projects: ₹150 crore (25% of ₹600 crore utilized - land acquisition, EPC contracts signed)
- Debt Repayment: ₹200 crore (100% utilized - all high-cost debt repaid)
- Working Capital: ₹20 crore (20% utilized)
- Issue Expenses: ₹100 crore (100% - BRLM fees, legal, listing)
- Unutilized: ₹530 crore (parked in liquid MFs, overnight funds)

Disclosure: Filed within 45 days of Q1 end on company website, stock exchange
```

### 8.2 Promoter Lock-in

**Pre-IPO Shareholders (Regulation 36-39)**:
```
Promoters: 3-year lock-in on PRE-ISSUE SHAREHOLDING (20% of post-issue capital OR promoter holding, whichever less)
- Cannot sell locked-in shares for 3 years from listing date
- Remaining promoter shares: 1-year lock-in

Example:
Promoter Pre-IPO: 100% of 8 crore shares
IPO: 2 crore shares (public offer)
Post-IPO: Promoter 8 crore (80%), Public 2 crore (20%)

Lock-in:
- 20% of post-issue 10 crore = 2 crore shares → 3-year lock-in
- Remaining promoter 6 crore shares → 1-year lock-in
```

**IPO Allottees (Public)**:
- No lock-in for public shareholders (can sell from Day 1 of listing)

## LANDMARK CASES

### 1. SEBI v. Sahara India Real Estate Corporation (2013) 1 SCC 1

**Facts**:
- Sahara issued ₹24,000 crore "Optionally Fully Convertible Debentures" (OFCDs) to 3 crore investors
- Sahara claimed: Private placement (issued to <200 persons per company, used 80+ subsidiary companies)
- SEBI: Deemed public offer (3 crore investors = public, requires prospectus, SEBI approval)

**Supreme Court Held**:
- OFCDs are effectively equity (option to convert, so equity-like)
- Issuing to 3 crore investors via multiple subsidiaries = circumventing law (substance over form)
- DEEMED PUBLIC OFFER → Required SEBI approval, prospectus
- Sahara violated Section 67 of Companies Act 1956 (issue without prospectus)
- **Penalty**: Sahara ordered to refund ₹24,000 crore + 15% interest to investors (deposited with SEBI)

**Principle**:
- Cannot use multiple entities to circumvent public offer limits (200-person rule)
- Substance over form: If issue is effectively public (large number of investors), it is public offer regardless of structure

**Application**:
- Companies planning private placement: Ensure aggregate offerees <200 across all group entities (not per entity)
- Convertible instruments (CCPS, OFCDs) treated as equity for regulatory purposes

---

### 2. DLF Ltd IPO (2007) - Largest Indian IPO

**Facts**:
- DLF (real estate developer) raised ₹9,200 crore via IPO (largest Indian IPO at time)
- Issue Price: ₹525 (P/E ratio 65x, very expensive vs peers at 25-30x P/E)
- Oversubscribed: 3x

**Listing**:
- Listing Day Price: ₹530 (1% gain, minimal listing pop)
- 1 Month Later: ₹400 (24% fall)
- Financial Crisis (2008): ₹150 (71% fall from issue price)

**Lessons**:
1. **Valuation Risk**: High P/E ratio (65x) unsustainable (real estate cyclical sector)
2. **Timing Risk**: IPO at market peak (2007), crash followed (2008 financial crisis)
3. **Liquidity Risk**: DLF locked in promoter shares (75% promoter holding post-IPO), limited free float → Low liquidity → High volatility
4. **Due Diligence**: Investors should scrutinize valuation, sector outlook, promoter commitment (pledge/lock-in)

## STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS

### When to Choose Each Equity Raising Method

| Method | Best For | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IPO** | First-time listing, large capital needs (₹500+ cr), brand building | High visibility, valuation premium, liquidity | Long timeline (6 months), high cost (3-5%), regulatory scrutiny |
| **FPO** | Already-listed company, large capital needs | Faster than IPO (2 months), lower cost (2-3%) | Dilution to existing shareholders, market timing risk |
| **Rights Issue** | Maintain promoter control, reward existing shareholders | No dilution if shareholders subscribe, lower cost (1-2%) | Subscription risk (may be undersubscribed if shareholders lack funds) |
| **Private Placement** | Unlisted company, mid-size capital (₹10-100 cr), quick fundraising | Fast (1 month), simple, flexible terms | Limited investor pool (<200), lower valuation vs public offer |
| **Preferential Allotment** | Strategic investor, PE/VC fundraising | Fast (2-3 weeks), targeted investor, value-add partner | Dilution, lock-in restrictions, pricing floors (listed companies) |
| **QIP** | Listed company, institutional fundraising, quick capital (₹100-2,000 cr) | Fastest (2 weeks), low cost (0.5-1%), no retail complexity | Only QIBs (no retail participation), 1-year lock-in, 50% size limit |

**IPO Compliance**:
- [ ] Appoint BRLMs, legal advisors, auditors, registrar (6 months before filing)
- [ ] Draft DRHP (comprehensive disclosure, 300-500 pages)
- [ ] File DRHP with SEBI (respond to observations within 30 days)
- [ ] Roadshow after SEBI observations resolved (2-3 weeks, multiple cities)
- [ ] File RHP (DRHP + SEBI observations response)
- [ ] Publish RHP in newspapers (English + vernacular)
- [ ] Issue opens (minimum 3 days subscription period)
- [ ] Book building (price discovery via investor bids)
- [ ] Finalize issue price (within price band, based on demand)
- [ ] Allotment within T+6 days, refund within T+7 days
- [ ] Listing within T+6 days
- [ ] Quarterly disclosure of IPO proceeds utilization (Audit Committee monitored)

**Rights Issue Compliance**:
- [ ] Board approval (issue size, price, record date)
- [ ] Shareholder approval (special resolution - 75% majority)
- [ ] File offer letter with SEBI, stock exchanges
- [ ] Dispatch offer letter to all shareholders (as on record date)
- [ ] Rights trading period (2 weeks, shareholders can sell rights)
- [ ] Subscription period (minimum 15 days, maximum 30 days)
- [ ] Allotment (fully subscribed + undersubscribed to oversubscribers + promoters)
- [ ] Listing of rights shares (within 15 days of allotment)

**Private Placement Compliance**:
- [ ] Board approval (issue size, price, terms)
- [ ] Offer letter to <200 identified persons (no public advertisement)
- [ ] Offer letter contents: Objects, price, risks, financials (last 2 years), promoter background
- [ ] Record date (allotment only to those who paid before record date)
- [ ] Allotment within 60 days of board approval
- [ ] ROC filing (Form PAS-3 - Return of Allotment) within 30 days

**QIP Compliance**:
- [ ] Board approval (maximum size, floor price formula)
- [ ] Placement document to QIBs (not filed with SEBI, not public)
- [ ] Book building (1-2 days, QIBs submit bids)
- [ ] Allotment within 7 days of board approval (minimum 10% to MFs if MFs apply)
- [ ] Listing within 1 day of allotment (shares tradable)
- [ ] Stock exchange disclosure (issue size, price, allottees)
- [ ] 1-year lock-in for QIP shares
