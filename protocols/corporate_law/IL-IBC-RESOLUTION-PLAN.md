# IBC - Resolution Plan Contents & Evaluation

## Overview

### What is a Resolution Plan?

A **Resolution Plan** (Section 30) is a comprehensive proposal submitted by a **Resolution Applicant** to acquire and revive a corporate debtor undergoing CIRP. It details:
1. **Financial Terms**: Payment to creditors (financial, operational, workmen, secured, unsecured)
2. **Business Restructuring**: Turnaround strategy, management changes, capital infusion
3. **Implementation Timeline**: Payment schedule, operational milestones, monitoring mechanism

**Objective**: Maximize value for **all** stakeholders (creditors + employees + company itself as going concern) compared to liquidation alternative.

**Key Principle** (Essar Steel case):
> "The resolution plan must balance the interests of all stakeholders, not just financial creditors. However, the **commercial wisdom of the CoC** (66% financial creditors) is paramount."

### Resolution Plan vs Liquidation

| Metric | Resolution Plan | Liquidation (Section 33) |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| **Objective** | Revive company as going concern | Sell assets, close company |
| **Recovery** | 40-60% average (financial creditors) | 20-30% average (forced sale) |
| **Timeline** | 90-180 days (implementation) | 12-24 months (asset sale + distribution) |
| **Jobs** | Preserved (typically 70-90% employees retained) | Lost (company ceases operations) |
| **Business Continuity** | Company continues operations | Company dissolved |
| **Creditor Priority** | Negotiable (CoC decides distribution) | Statutory waterfall (Section 53 - secured first, operational second, etc.) |

**When Resolution Fails** → **Mandatory Liquidation** (Section 33(2)):
- No resolution plan approved by Day 330 (180+90+60)
- CoC rejects all plans (votes <66%)
- NCLT rejects approved plan (legal violations, Section 30(2) non-compliance)

## Legal Framework

### Section 30(2) - Mandatory Resolution Plan Provisions

**Section 30(2) Checklist** (9 Mandatory Requirements):

**(a) Payment of Insolvency Resolution Process Costs**:
- **CIRP Costs**: RP fees (₹5-50L), legal fees, valuation fees, advertisement costs, IRP/RP expenses
- **Priority**: CIRP costs have **super-priority** (paid before all creditors, Section 53(1)(a))
- **Timing**: Typically paid upfront (on plan approval) from resolution applicant's first payment

**(b) Payment of Workmen Dues (24 Months)**:
- **Workmen Definition**: Employees earning ≤₹24,000/month (blue-collar, factory workers, laborers)
- **Period**: Dues for 24 months **preceding** liquidation commencement date (not CIRP commencement)
- **Components**: Unpaid wages, provident fund, gratuity, leave encashment
- **Priority**: Second priority after CIRP costs (Section 53(1)(b)(i))
- **Payment**: **100% payment mandatory** (cannot be haircut), typically paid upfront

**(c) Payment of Secured Creditors**:
- **Secured Creditors**: Banks/FIs with mortgage, hypothecation, pledge on assets
- **Minimum Payment**: Amount **NOT less than** what secured creditors would receive in liquidation (Section 53(1)(b)(ii) waterfall)
- **Calculation**: If liquidation value of secured assets = ₹500cr and secured creditors' claims = ₹800cr → Secured creditors get ₹500cr in liquidation (62.5% recovery)
  - **Resolution Plan Minimum**: Must offer ≥62.5% recovery (₹500cr minimum)
- **Flexibility**: CoC can approve lower recovery if **same class** of creditors (all secured creditors) agree (no intra-class discrimination)

**(d) Management and Control**:
- **New Management**: Details of proposed board, CEO, CFO, key management personnel
- **Ownership**: Shareholding structure post-resolution (resolution applicant becomes majority/100% owner typically)
- **Governance**: Board composition (independent directors, creditor nominees if any)

**(e) Implementation Timeline**:
- **Payment Schedule**: Upfront payment (₹__cr within 90 days of NCLT approval) + Deferred payments (Year 1: ₹__cr, Year 2: ₹__cr, etc.)
- **Operational Milestones**: Plant restart, capacity utilization targets, revenue projections
- **Monitoring**: RP monitors implementation for 90-180 days post-approval, files compliance reports with NCLT

**(f) Future Business Plan**:
- **Turnaround Strategy**: Cost reduction (operational efficiency, headcount optimization), Revenue growth (new products, market expansion), Capex plan (₹__cr for plant modernization)
- **Financial Projections**: 3-5 year P&L, balance sheet, cash flow (with assumptions)
- **Funding Sources**: Equity (resolution applicant's funds ₹__cr) + Debt (bank loans ₹__cr, financing commitment letters attached) + Asset sales (non-core assets ₹__cr)

**(g) Terms of Debt Repayment**:
- **Existing Debt Treatment**: Old debt discharged upon plan approval (Section 31(1) proviso - **debt extinguishment**)
- **New Debt** (if any): Resolution applicant may raise fresh debt post-acquisition (not covered under plan, future business operations)

**(h) Management of Corporate Debtor's Affairs**:
- **Transition**: From RP control → Resolution applicant control (within 30 days of NCLT approval)
- **Employee Retention**: Retention bonuses for key employees (prevent talent flight), VRS (Voluntary Retirement Scheme) for surplus employees with compensation

**(i) Implementation and Supervision**:
- **RP's Role**: Monitor plan implementation (90-180 days), ensure payments made per schedule, file compliance report with NCLT
- **Penalty**: If resolution applicant defaults on payments → CoC can approach NCLT for liquidation (Section 33(3))

### Section 30(4) - CoC Approval (66% Voting Share)

**Voting Mechanism**:
```
Resolution Plan APPROVED if:
Sum of Voting Shares of Financial Creditors who voted FOR ≥ 66%

Example:
Financial Creditor A (50% voting share): FOR
Financial Creditor B (30% voting share): FOR
Financial Creditor C (20% voting share): AGAINST

Total FOR: 50% + 30% = 80% ✅ (≥66%, Plan APPROVED)
```

**Binding Effect**:
- **All Creditors Bound**: Once 66% approve, dissenting 34% + operational creditors + shareholders **MUST accept** plan terms (no opt-out)
- **Debt Discharge**: Old debts extinguished (Section 31(1)), creditors cannot sue for shortfall after plan implementation

### Section 31 - NCLT's Limited Review

**NCLT Can Reject Plan ONLY If** (Section 31(1)):
1. **Does not comply with Section 30(2)**: Missing mandatory provisions (e.g., workmen dues not paid 100%, secured creditors getting <liquidation value)
2. **Contravenes any provision of law**: Environmental laws violated, FEMA violations (foreign bidder without RBI approval), labor laws breached (mass retrenchment without proper compensation)
3. **Contravenes provisions of IBC**: Section 29A violation (bidder ineligible - willful defaulter/NPA), timeline breached (>330 days)

**NCLT Cannot**:
- ❌ **Reject on commercial merits**: "Operational creditors should get more" (if CoC 66% approved distribution)
- ❌ **Modify plan sua sponte**: Change payment amounts, add conditions (only CoC can modify by re-voting)
- ❌ **Substitute its judgment**: For CoC's commercial wisdom (Essar Steel case)

**1. Applicant Identity**:
- **Individual/Company/Consortium**: Name, address, PAN, CIN (if company)
- **Group Structure**: Parent company, subsidiaries, promoters (if consortium - equity partners + debt partners)
- **Track Record**: Previous acquisitions, turnaround experience, sector expertise

## Resolution Plan Evaluation by CoC

### Evaluation Criteria (Regulation 39)

**1. Feasibility (Can the plan be implemented?)**:
- **Funding Certainty**: Are financing commitments firm? (Bank sanction letter issued vs "indicative term sheet")
- **Operational Viability**: Is turnaround plan realistic? (60% → 75% utilization achievable vs 40% → 90% in 6 months = unrealistic)
- **Management Capability**: Does resolution applicant have sector expertise? (Steel industry veteran vs first-time distressed asset investor)
- **Timeline**: Is implementation schedule achievable? (90 days restart vs 30 days = too aggressive)

**Example - Feasibility Red Flags**:
- Resolution Applicant: PE Fund (no steel industry experience)
- Business Plan: Increase EBITDA margin from 5% to 25% in Year 1 (industry average 15% - unrealistic)
- Funding: "We will raise ₹5,000cr debt subject to plan approval" (no sanction letter - conditional funding)
- **CoC Assessment**: Low feasibility, high execution risk → May reject despite high amount offered

**2. Viability (Will the company survive long-term?)**:
- **Sustainable Business Model**: Post-restructuring, can company compete? (Cost ₹3,800/tonne vs industry ₹4,000/tonne = competitive)
- **Market Position**: Market share, customer relationships, brand value retained?
- **Debt Serviceability**: Can company service new debt? (DSCR ≥1.5x by Year 3 = viable, <1.0x = unsustainable)
- **Growth Potential**: Industry tailwinds (infrastructure spending, demand growth) vs headwinds (oversupply, imports)

**Example - Viability Analysis**:
```
VIABILITY ASSESSMENT - ABC Manufacturing Ltd. Resolution Plan

Strengths:
✓ Strategically located plant (near iron ore mines, reduces logistics cost ₹200/tonne)
✓ Established customer base (20-year relationships with auto OEMs, construction companies)
✓ Modern equipment (post ₹1,300cr capex, plant life 15-20 years)
✓ Skilled workforce (2,500 employees with avg 12 years experience, low attrition)

Weaknesses:
⚠ High debt (₹4,000cr post-plan, Debt-to-Equity 2x - manageable but leveraged)
⚠ Commodity business (steel prices volatile, ±20% annual swings impact profitability)
⚠ Environmental risks (pollution clearance expires 2027, renewal uncertain - ₹500cr investment may be needed for stricter norms)

Opportunities:
✓ Govt infrastructure push (₹100 lakh crore National Infrastructure Pipeline - steel demand +8% CAGR)
✓ Import substitution (China steel dumping tariffs, domestic producers benefit)
✓ Value-added products (cold-rolled, galvanized steel - 20% higher realization, currently not produced)

Threats:
⚠ Overcapacity (India steel capacity 160MT vs demand 110MT = 30% surplus, price pressure)
⚠ Energy costs (coal, power = 40% of COGS, volatile commodity prices)
⚠ Competition (Tata Steel, JSW, AM/NS India - scale advantage, integrated operations)

Overall Viability: MEDIUM-HIGH
- Sustainable at current industry structure (15% EBITDA margins achievable)
- Vulnerable to severe downturn (if steel prices drop >30%, DSCR <1.0x → debt service stress)
- Upside if capacity expansion executed (2.5MT capacity, economies of scale improve cost structure)
```

**3. Fair & Equitable Treatment (Essar Steel Principle)**:
- **Liquidation Waterfall Benchmark**: Creditors must get **≥liquidation value** (Section 53 distribution)
- **Intra-Class Equity**: Within same class (e.g., all secured creditors), cannot discriminate without consent
- **Inter-Class Equity**: Differential treatment between classes (financial vs operational) allowed if justified

**Liquidation Waterfall (Section 53) - Benchmark for Comparison**:
```
LIQUIDATION WATERFALL (Section 53) - ABC Manufacturing Ltd.

Liquidation Asset Value: ₹800 crore (forced sale, 40% discount to book value ₹2,000cr)

Priority Distribution:
1. CIRP Costs (Section 53(1)(a)): ₹50 crore (100% paid)
   Remaining: ₹750cr

2. Workmen Dues - 24 months (Section 53(1)(b)(i)): ₹30 crore (100% paid, max ₹24,000 × 24 months per worker)
   Remaining: ₹720cr

3. Secured Creditors (Section 53(1)(b)(ii)): ₹720 crore
   - Secured Claims: ₹800 crore (Bank A ₹500cr, Bank B ₹300cr secured by land mortgage)
   - Available: ₹720cr (from secured asset sale - land sold for ₹720cr)
   - **Recovery**: 90% (₹720cr ÷ ₹800cr)
   - Bank A: ₹450cr (90% of ₹500cr claim)
   - Bank B: ₹270cr (90% of ₹300cr claim)
   Remaining: ₹0

4. Unsecured Creditors (Section 53(1)(d)): ₹0 available
   - Unsecured Financial Creditor (Bank C): ₹200cr claim → ₹0 recovery (0%)
   - Operational Creditors: ₹200cr claims → ₹0 recovery (0%)

5. Shareholders (Section 53(1)(g)): ₹0 available → ₹0 recovery (0%)

TOTAL LIQUIDATION RECOVERY:
- Financial Creditors: ₹720cr out of ₹1,000cr claims = 72% average
  (Secured 90%, Unsecured 0%)
- Operational Creditors: ₹30cr out of ₹200cr claims = 15% average
  (Workmen 100%, Others 0%)
```

**Resolution Plan vs Liquidation Comparison**:
```
FAIR & EQUITABLE ANALYSIS

Creditor Class          Resolution Plan    Liquidation (Sec 53)    Premium to Liquidation
Financial Creditors     ₹950cr (95%)       ₹720cr (72%)           +32% (✓ Better than liquidation)
  - Secured (Bank A/B)  ₹800cr (100%)      ₹720cr (90%)           +11%
  - Unsecured (Bank C)  ₹150cr (75%)       ₹0cr (0%)              +75% (infinitely better)

Operational Creditors   ₹145cr (73%)       ₹30cr (15%)            +58% (✓ Much better)
  - Employees           ₹30cr (100%)       ₹30cr (100%)           Same (mandatory 100%)
  - Suppliers           ₹100cr (67%)       ₹0cr (0%)              +67%
  - Government          ₹15cr (75%)        ₹0cr (0%)              +75%

Shareholders            ₹0cr (0%)          ₹0cr (0%)              Same (equity extinguished)

CONCLUSION: Plan is FAIR & EQUITABLE
✓ ALL creditor classes getting significantly MORE than liquidation alternative
✓ Financial creditors: 95% vs 72% liquidation (+32% premium)
✓ Operational creditors: 73% vs 15% liquidation (+58% premium)
✓ Even dissenting creditors (Bank C unsecured) getting 75% vs 0% liquidation (infinitely better)
```

**4. Commercial Wisdom (CoC's Prerogative)**:
- **Strategic Fit**: Does resolution applicant's acquisition serve industry consolidation, synergies?
- **Job Preservation**: Employment is social consideration (not legally binding, but CoC may prefer plans retaining more jobs)
- **Upfront vs Deferred**: CoC may prefer higher upfront cash (certainty, time value of money) vs higher total amount but deferred
- **Business Plan Credibility**: Aggressive growth plan (doubling revenue in 2 years) vs conservative (steady 10% growth)

**Example - CoC's Commercial Judgment**:
```
COC DELIBERATION - 3 Resolution Plans for ABC Manufacturing Ltd.

Plan A (XYZ Steel - Strategic Buyer):
- Total: ₹1,095 crore
- Upfront: ₹900cr (82%)
- Recovery: Financial 95%, Operational 73%
- Jobs: 2,500 retained (83%)
- Business Plan: Conservative (60% → 75% utilization over 3 years), credible
- Bidder: Steel industry incumbent, proven turnaround track record (3 previous successful acquisitions)

Plan B (PE Fund - Financial Buyer):
- Total: ₹1,200 crore (higher than Plan A)
- Upfront: ₹600cr (50%)
- Deferred: ₹600cr over 5 years (₹120cr/year)
- Recovery: Financial 105%, Operational 85%
- Jobs: 2,000 retained (67%, 500 additional retrenchment)
- Business Plan: Aggressive (60% → 90% utilization in 18 months), skeptical achievability
- Bidder: Distressed asset fund, limited steel sector experience

Plan C (Promoter Group - Existing Promoters via Family Trust):
- Total: ₹900 crore (lowest)
- Upfront: ₹400cr (44%)
- Deferred: ₹500cr over 4 years
- Recovery: Financial 80%, Operational 60%
- **Section 29A Issue**: Promoters classified as willful defaulters (RBI notification 2022) → INELIGIBLE

COC VOTING (66% Required):
Plan A (XYZ Steel):
- Bank A (50% vote): FOR (prefers upfront cash, credible bidder)
- Bank B (30% vote): FOR (same rationale)
- Bank C (20% vote): AGAINST (wants Plan B higher amount, willing to wait for deferred)
- RESULT: 80% FOR → Plan A APPROVED

COC's Rationale (Commercial Wisdom):
1. Certainty: 82% upfront vs 50% upfront (Plan B) - prefer liquidity NOW
2. Execution Risk: XYZ Steel has proven track record vs PE fund's aggressive plan (doubtful)
3. Amount Differential: ₹105cr less than Plan B (10% difference), but much lower risk
4. Jobs: 500 more jobs saved (2,500 vs 2,000) - social impact considered
5. Plan C: Automatically disqualified (Section 29A ineligibility)

NCLT Cannot Override: Even if NCLT thinks Plan B (₹1,200cr) is "better" commercially, once 80% CoC approved Plan A, NCLT must accept (Essar Steel - commercial wisdom of CoC is paramount)
```

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Real Estate CIRP - Homebuyers as Financial Creditors

**Background**:
- **Corporate Debtor**: Jaypee Infratech Ltd. (real estate developer)
- **Project**: Wish Town (5,000 apartments, ₹25,000cr project value)
- **Crisis**: Jaypee defaulted, 20,000 homebuyers (paid ₹15,000cr advances) + Banks (lent ₹9,000cr) + Operational creditors (₹500cr)
- **Section 5(8)(f) Amendment (2018)**: Homebuyers classified as **financial creditors** (amount paid is "consideration for time value of money" - builder holds buyer's money for 3-5 years until possession)

**Claims Admitted**:
- **Financial Creditors**:
  - Homebuyers: ₹15,000cr (60% voting share)
  - Banks: ₹9,000cr (36% voting share)
  - NBFC: ₹1,000cr (4% voting share)
  - **Total**: ₹25,000cr (100% voting)
- **Operational Creditors**: ₹500cr (contractors, suppliers - 0% voting)

**Resolution Plans**:
- **Plan A (NBCC - Government Company)**: ₹5,000cr (complete all 5,000 apartments within 3 years, deliver to homebuyers)
- **Plan B (Suraksha Realty - Private Developer)**: ₹7,000cr (complete 4,000 apartments + pay ₹3,000cr to banks)

**CoC Challenge**: Homebuyers (60% voting) want apartments (not cash), Banks (40% voting) want cash

**Resolution**:
- CoC voted: Homebuyers FOR Plan A (apartment delivery), Banks AGAINST Plan A (prefer Plan B higher cash)
- **Result**: 60% FOR Plan A → Plan A APPROVED (homebuyers' majority prevailed)
- **NCLT Issue**: Banks challenged - "Plan A gives only ₹5,000cr vs Plan B ₹7,000cr, CoC decision is commercially irrational"
- **Supreme Court (Jaypee Infratech 2020)**: Upheld Plan A
  - **"Homebuyers' right to get flats is integral to resolution plan evaluation"**
  - CoC's commercial wisdom includes social impact (20,000 families getting homes)
  - Plan A's ₹5,000cr + apartment delivery = higher value to homebuyers than ₹7,000cr cash
  - NCLT cannot substitute its judgment for CoC's (60% democratic decision)

**Outcome**:
- NBCC completed 4,500 apartments (by 2023), homebuyers got possession
- Banks recovered 40% (₹3,600cr out of ₹9,000cr) - lower than Plan B but socially impactful resolution

## Landmark Cases

### 1. Essar Steel India Ltd. CoC case (2019 Supreme Court - Constitution Bench)

**Facts** (Recap for Resolution Plan Context):
- 2 plans: ArcelorMittal (₹42,000cr) vs Numetal (₹40,000cr)
- ArcelorMittal plan: Financial creditors ₹41,000cr (100%), Operational creditors ₹1,000cr (2.4%)
- Numetal plan: Financial creditors ₹37,000cr (90%), Operational creditors ₹3,000cr (7.3%)
- **Operational Creditors' Argument**: "ArcelorMittal plan is NOT fair & equitable - we get only 2.4% while financial creditors get 100%"

**Held** (Supreme Court - Principles for Resolution Plan Evaluation):

**1. Fair & Equitable Treatment Requirement**:
- Resolution plan MUST treat creditors fairly, not arbitrarily
- **Test**: Are operational creditors getting ≥liquidation value?
  - Liquidation value for operational creditors: 0-1% (after secured creditors paid first per Section 53)
  - ArcelorMittal plan: 2.4% (>liquidation value ✓)
  - **Conclusion**: Operational creditors are better off than liquidation, hence fair

**2. CoC's Commercial Wisdom is Supreme**:
- Once 66% financial creditors approve, **NCLT cannot override** on commercial merits
- **"Court cannot don the hat of commercial wisdom of creditors"** (famous quote)
- NCLT's role limited to: (i) Section 30(2) compliance check, (ii) Legal violations check
- NCLT cannot say "Numetal plan is fairer" and reject ArcelorMittal plan approved by CoC

**3. IBC's Objective is Value Maximization**:
- IBC aims to maximize value for **all** stakeholders, not just one class
- Here, ArcelorMittal plan (₹42,000cr) maximizes **total** value vs Numetal (₹40,000cr)
- Operational creditors getting lower % is acceptable if:
  - (a) Absolute amount >liquidation value (2.4% > 0-1% ✓)
  - (b) Total value maximized (₹42,000cr > ₹40,000cr ✓)

**Impact on Resolution Plan Drafting**:
- **Must Provide Liquidation Comparison**: Every plan must show liquidation waterfall (Section 53) and demonstrate ALL creditors getting ≥liquidation value
- **Operational Creditors' Limited Rights**: Cannot block plan if financial creditors (66%) approve, as long as getting ≥liquidation value
- **CoC's Discretion**: CoC can choose lower-amount plan if better terms (upfront payment, credible bidder, job preservation)

## Compliance Checklist

### Resolution Applicant Checklist (Plan Preparation)

**Pre-Submission (Due Diligence Phase)**:
- [ ] Conduct comprehensive due diligence (financial, legal, operational, environmental)
- [ ] Review Information Memorandum (100-300 pages from RP)
- [ ] Inspect physical assets (plant visit, machinery condition assessment)
- [ ] Meet corporate debtor's management (understand business operations, key customer relationships)
- [ ] Review all claims (financial creditors, operational creditors, contingent liabilities)
- [ ] Assess liquidation value (compare your offer to Section 53 waterfall distribution)

**Section 29A Compliance Verification**:
- [ ] Verify not a willful defaulter (check RBI master list, obtain CIBIL report showing no such classification)
- [ ] Verify no NPA accounts >1 year (CIBIL report for self + all connected persons - promoters, directors, 100% subsidiaries)
- [ ] Verify no criminal convictions (court records search for offences punishable ≥2 years)
- [ ] Verify no director disqualifications (MCA portal search for DIN disqualifications)
- [ ] Verify not related to corporate debtor (no promoter/director/KMP relationship within 2 years)
- [ ] If promoter of corporate debtor: Verify if MSME eligible (Section 240A exception to Section 29A(j))

**Plan Drafting (Section 30(2) Compliance)**:
- [ ] **Part A - Payment Terms**:
  - [ ] Calculate payment to each creditor class (financial, operational, workmen, government)
  - [ ] Ensure workmen dues 100% paid upfront (Section 30(2)(b) mandatory)
  - [ ] Ensure secured creditors getting ≥liquidation value (compare to Section 53(1)(b)(ii) recovery)
  - [ ] Prepare payment schedule (upfront within 90 days + deferred over 2-5 years)
- [ ] **Part B - Funding Plan**:
  - [ ] Secure financing commitments (bank sanction letters, not just indicative term sheets)
  - [ ] Demonstrate financial capability (audited financials showing net worth, cash reserves)
  - [ ] Identify sources: Equity (own funds ₹__cr) + Debt (bank loans ₹__cr) + Asset sales (₹__cr)
- [ ] **Part C - Business Plan**:
  - [ ] Turnaround strategy (cost reduction, revenue growth, capex plan)
  - [ ] Financial projections (3-5 years P&L, balance sheet, cash flow with realistic assumptions)
  - [ ] Risk analysis (downside scenarios, mitigation strategies)
  - [ ] Management team (proposed CEO, CFO, key hires with CVs)
- [ ] **Part D - Implementation**:
  - [ ] 90-day detailed timeline (payment tranches, operational restart milestones)
  - [ ] Monitoring mechanism (KPIs, quarterly reporting to lenders)
  - [ ] Employee retention plan (retention bonuses for key employees, VRS for surplus)

**Plan Submission (Form & Documents)**:
- [ ] Resolution Plan document (50-150 pages, comprehensive)
- [ ] Section 29A affidavits (self-declaration + supporting documents)
- [ ] Financial statements (bidder's last 3 years audited accounts)
- [ ] Funding commitment letters (bank sanction letters, equity commitment from promoters/sponsors)
- [ ] Business plan (detailed, 50-100 pages with market analysis, competitor benchmarking)
- [ ] Valuation report (independent valuer's report on corporate debtor's assets)
- [ ] Management team CVs (proposed CEO, CFO, CXOs)
- [ ] Board resolution (if bidder is company, authorizing plan submission and investment)
- [ ] Power of Attorney (if signatory is representative, not promoter/director)

### CoC Checklist (Plan Evaluation)

**Preliminary Review (Week 1-2 after submission)**:
- [ ] Verify Section 29A compliance (RP to cross-check bidder's CIBIL, willful defaulter status)
- [ ] Check completeness (does plan have all Section 30(2) mandatory provisions?)
- [ ] Verify funding commitments (are bank sanction letters genuine? Call banks to confirm)
- [ ] Compare to liquidation value (is each creditor class getting ≥Section 53 distribution?)

**Detailed Evaluation (Week 3-4)**:
- [ ] **Feasibility Analysis**:
  - [ ] Funding certainty (firm commitments vs conditional - "subject to plan approval")
  - [ ] Operational viability (is turnaround plan realistic? Benchmark against industry)
  - [ ] Management capability (does bidder have sector expertise, proven track record?)
  - [ ] Timeline achievability (90-day restart feasible vs overly aggressive 30-day plan)
- [ ] **Viability Analysis**:
  - [ ] Sustainable business model (post-restructuring, can company compete long-term?)
  - [ ] Market position (customer base retained, brand value preserved?)
  - [ ] Debt serviceability (DSCR ≥1.5x by Year 3, or unsustainable?)
  - [ ] Growth potential (industry tailwinds - infrastructure spending, or headwinds - oversupply?)
- [ ] **Commercial Comparison** (if multiple plans):
  - [ ] Total amount (Plan A ₹1,095cr vs Plan B ₹1,200cr)
  - [ ] Upfront vs deferred (Plan A 82% upfront vs Plan B 50% - prefer higher upfront)
  - [ ] Recovery by class (financial 95% vs 105%, operational 73% vs 85%)
  - [ ] Risk-adjusted value (Plan A lower amount but higher certainty vs Plan B higher amount but execution risk)

**CoC Voting (Week 5-6)**:
- [ ] Conduct CoC meetings (2-3 meetings for plan presentations, Q&A with bidders, deliberations)
- [ ] Each financial creditor casts vote (FOR/AGAINST, recorded in voting form)
- [ ] Count votes (sum of voting shares FOR, check if ≥66%)
- [ ] If ≥66%: Plan approved, RP files with NCLT
- [ ] If <66%: Options - (i) Vote on alternative plan, (ii) Request modified plans, (iii) Extend CIRP for revision

### NCLT Checklist (Plan Confirmation)

**NCLT Review (Limited Scope per Section 31)**:
- [ ] **Section 30(2) Compliance**:
  - [ ] (a) CIRP costs paid? ✓
  - [ ] (b) Workmen dues 100% paid? ✓
  - [ ] (c) Secured creditors getting ≥liquidation value? ✓
  - [ ] (d) Management details provided? ✓
  - [ ] (e) Implementation timeline specified? ✓
  - [ ] (f) Business plan included? ✓
  - [ ] (g) Debt repayment terms specified? ✓
  - [ ] (h) Management transition plan? ✓
  - [ ] (i) Supervision mechanism? ✓
- [ ] **Legal Compliance**:
  - [ ] No environmental law violations (pollution clearances valid)
  - [ ] No labor law violations (retrenchment with proper compensation as per ID Act)
  - [ ] No FEMA violations (if foreign bidder, RBI approval obtained)
- [ ] **IBC Compliance**:
  - [ ] Section 29A compliance verified (bidder eligible)
  - [ ] 66% CoC approval obtained (voting record attached)
  - [ ] Timeline within 330 days (CIRP commenced Day 0, approval sought Day 210 ✓)

**If Satisfied → NCLT Approves**:
- [ ] Issue approval order (plan binding on all stakeholders per Section 31(1))
- [ ] Specify effective date (typically order date)
- [ ] Direct RP to monitor implementation (90-180 days, file compliance report)

**If Not Satisfied → NCLT Rejects**:
- [ ] Specify grounds (Section 30(2) non-compliance, legal violations, Section 29A breach)
- [ ] Allow CoC to revise plan and resubmit (if curable defects)
- [ ] If no viable plan and Day 330 approaching → Order liquidation (Section 33)
