# NCLT Scheme of Arrangement - Procedural Guide

## Overview

### What is an NCLT Scheme of Arrangement?

An **NCLT Scheme of Arrangement** is a court-supervised restructuring process under Sections 230-240 of the Companies Act 2013, where the **National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT)** approves a scheme for:
1. **Merger/Amalgamation** (two or more companies combine into one)
2. **Demerger** (transfer of undertaking from one company to another)
3. **Compromise with Creditors** (restructuring debt, forgiving obligations)
4. **Compromise with Shareholders** (capital reduction, share buyback, share exchange)
5. **Corporate Restructuring** (group reorganization, holding-subsidiary transfers)

**Key Features**:
- **Court Supervision**: NCLT oversees entire process, ensures fairness to all stakeholders
- **Statutory Protection**: Approved scheme is binding on ALL shareholders/creditors (including dissenters who voted against, if 75% majority approves)
- **Regulatory Approvals**: Integrates approvals from ROC, IT, SEBI, CCI, sectoral regulators into single court order
- **Tax Benefits**: Enables tax-neutral mergers/demergers (Sections 2(1B), 2(19AA) Income Tax Act) if court-approved
- **Timeline**: 270-365 days (9-12 months) from filing to final implementation

### Why Use NCLT Scheme (vs Other M&A Structures)?

**Advantages of NCLT Scheme**:
1. **Tax-Neutral**: Mergers/demergers can be structured to avoid capital gains tax (vs slump sale with 20% LTCG)
2. **Minority Squeeze-Out**: 75% majority can bind dissenting 25% minority (vs share sale requiring 100% consent)
3. **Regulatory Consolidation**: Single NCLT order incorporates all regulatory approvals (vs multiple separate approvals)
4. **Creditor Protection**: Court ensures creditors' interests protected (debt assumption, payment terms)
5. **Legal Certainty**: Court-approved scheme has higher enforceability (vs contractual agreements with dispute risk)

**Disadvantages**:
1. **Time-Consuming**: 9-12 months process (vs slump sale 2-4 months, share sale 1-3 months)
2. **Expensive**: Legal fees (₹50-100 lakh), court fees (₹5-10 lakh), valuation reports, advertisement costs
3. **Public Disclosure**: Scheme must be publicly advertised, disclosed to all stakeholders (vs private share sale)
4. **Rigid Process**: Court-driven timeline, procedural compliance mandatory (missed deadlines delay further)
5. **NCLT Discretion**: Court can reject or modify scheme even if 75% approved (minority protection)

### Types of Schemes

| Type | Purpose | Parties Involved | Typical Timeline |
|------|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Merger/Amalgamation** | Combine two companies into one | Transferor(s) + Transferee | 9-12 months |
| **Demerger** | Separate undertaking into new company | Demerged + Resulting company | 9-12 months |
| **Compromise with Creditors** | Debt restructuring, partial payment | Company + Creditors (banks, bondholders) | 6-9 months |
| **Capital Reduction** | Return capital to shareholders, write off losses | Company + Shareholders | 6-9 months |
| **Share Exchange/Buyback** | Reorganize capital structure | Company + Shareholders | 6-9 months |

## Legal Framework

### Section 230 - Power of NCLT to Order Compromise or Arrangement

**Statutory Language** (Companies Act 2013):
> "(1) Where a compromise or arrangement is proposed—
> (a) between a company and its creditors or any class of them; or
> (b) between a company and its members or any class of them,
> the Tribunal may, on the application of the company or of any creditor or member of the company, or in the case of a company which is being wound up, of the liquidator, order a meeting of the creditors or class of creditors, or of the members or class of members, as the case may be, to be called, held and conducted in such manner as the Tribunal directs."

**Key Principles**:

1. **"Compromise or Arrangement"** (Broad Definition):
   - **Compromise**: Settlement of disputed claims, debt reduction, payment terms modification
   - **Arrangement**: Reorganization of capital, share structure, business operations
   - Courts have interpreted broadly to include: mergers, demergers, capital reduction, share buybacks, group reorganizations
   - **Miheer H. Mafatlal case (1997)**: "Arrangement" includes any reorganization affecting members' rights (even if no dispute, e.g., merger)

2. **"Creditors"** (Inclusive Definition):
   - **Actual Creditors**: Current lenders, bondholders, trade payables
   - **Contingent Creditors**: Potential creditors (e.g., guarantor if primary borrower defaults, warranty claimants)
   - **Prospective Creditors**: Future creditors (e.g., deferred payment contracts)
   - **Hindustan Lever case (1995)**: Employees with future gratuity entitlements are "contingent creditors" (must be included in meetings)

3. **"Class" of Creditors/Members**:
   - Must classify based on **common interests** (secured vs unsecured creditors, equity vs preference shareholders)
   - Each class votes separately (75% in value within each class required)
   - **Example**:
     - Class 1 (Secured Creditors - Banks): 3 banks with ₹500cr loans
     - Class 2 (Unsecured Creditors - Suppliers): 50 suppliers with ₹100cr payables
     - Class 3 (Equity Shareholders): 10,000 shareholders with 1cr shares
     - Each class must approve separately (75% in value + 50% in number within class)

4. **NCLT's Discretion** (Section 230(11)):
   - Even if 75% approve, NCLT can **reject** scheme if:
     - Unfair to minority (oppressive terms, undervalued shares)
     - Non-disclosure of material facts (hidden liabilities, related party transactions)
     - Against public interest (monopolistic merger, layoffs of thousands)
     - Procedural non-compliance (inadequate notice, suppressed information)
   - **Azadi Bachao Andolan case (2003)**: NCLT refused Vodafone-Hutchison merger citing tax avoidance (though later allowed by Supreme Court on appeal)

### Section 232 - Merger and Amalgamation

**Statutory Provision**:
> "(1) Two or more companies may amalgamate, or a company may merge with another company in accordance with the provisions of this section and with the approval of the Tribunal."

**Key Requirements**:
1. **Scheme of Amalgamation**: Draft scheme specifying transfer of ALL assets/liabilities, share exchange ratio, effective date
2. **Approval**: Board + Shareholders (75%) + Creditors (if affected, 75%) + NCLT final order
3. **Transferor Dissolution**: Transferor company dissolved without winding up (automatic upon scheme effectiveness)
4. **Transferee Succession**: Transferee becomes successor to transferor (inherits assets, liabilities, contracts, employees)

### Section 233 - Fast Track Merger (Exemption from NCLT Process)

**Eligibility** (Section 233):
1. **Holding-Subsidiary Merger**: Parent holds ≥90% of subsidiary's shares
2. **Sister Subsidiary Merger**: Two 100% subsidiaries of same parent
3. **Small Company Merger**: Both companies are "small companies" (paid-up capital ≤₹2cr, turnover ≤₹20cr)

**Process** (ROC Approval, No NCLT):
1. Board approval of both companies
2. Shareholder approval (75% special resolution) - can be dispensed with if 100% owned
3. Filing Form FTM-1 with ROC (scheme + resolutions + affidavits)
4. Central Government (ROC) approval within 30 days (no objection)
5. Implementation + Filing Form INC-28 with ROC
6. **Timeline**: 60-90 days (vs 270-365 days for NCLT scheme)

**Conditions**:
- No material liabilities (debt <10% of assets)
- No objection from secured creditors
- No pending SEBI/CCI/SFIO investigation

## NCLT Scheme Process - Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Due Diligence & Scheme Drafting (60-90 days)

**Objectives**:
1. **Financial Due Diligence**: Audit both companies' financials, identify hidden liabilities
2. **Legal Due Diligence**: Verify assets ownership, contracts transferability, pending litigation
3. **Valuation**: Independent valuer certifies share exchange ratio (fair and reasonable)
4. **Scheme Drafting**: Legal counsel drafts comprehensive scheme document (40-80 pages)

**Scheme Document Structure**:

**Part A - Background & Definitions** (~5 pages):
- Definitions (Appointed Date, Effective Date, Transferor, Transferee, Undertaking)
- Background (rationale for scheme, business synergies, shareholder benefits)

**Part B - Transfer & Vesting** (~10 pages):
- **Clause 4**: Transfer of ALL assets and liabilities of Transferor to Transferee
- **Clause 5**: Vesting of assets/liabilities from Appointed Date (retrospective accounting)
- **Clause 6**: Transferee assumes all contracts, licenses, permits (novation)
- **Clause 7**: Employees transfer to Transferee (Section 25FF, continuity of service)

**Part C - Share Capital & Issue** (~10 pages):
- **Clause 8**: Transferee's authorized capital increase (if required)
- **Clause 9**: Share Exchange Ratio (e.g., 1 Transferor share = 0.5 Transferee shares)
- **Clause 10**: Issuance of Transferee shares to Transferor shareholders (proportionate)
- **Clause 11**: Listing of new shares (SEBI approval for listing)

**Part D - Accounting Treatment** (~5 pages):
- **Clause 12**: Pooling of interests method (if merger of equals) or Purchase method (if acquisition)
- **Clause 13**: Treatment of reserves, goodwill, depreciation

**Part E - Tax Treatment** (~5 pages):
- **Clause 14**: Tax-neutral amalgamation (Section 2(1B) conditions satisfied)
- **Clause 15**: Carry-forward of losses (Section 72A, if ≥75% shareholder continuity)

**Part F - Conditions Precedent** (~5 pages):
- **Clause 16**: NCLT sanction, ROC approval, SEBI approval (if listed), CCI approval (if thresholds exceeded), IT approval (no objection), Stock Exchange approval

**Part G - Dissolution & Implementation** (~3 pages):
- **Clause 17**: Transferor dissolved upon scheme effectiveness (no winding up)
- **Clause 18**: Effective Date (last of all conditions precedent satisfied)
- **Clause 19**: Filing with ROC (Form INC-28 within 30 days of effective date)

**Example - Share Exchange Ratio Clause**:
```
"Upon the scheme becoming effective:
a) Each equity share of ₹10 face value in Transferor Company held by public shareholders
   shall be exchanged for 0.5 (half) equity shares of ₹10 face value in Transferee Company.
b) The Share Exchange Ratio of 1:0.5 has been determined based on the independent valuation
   report dated [Date] by [Valuer Name], IBBI Registered Valuer (Registration No. [XXX]).
c) Transferee shall issue [X] equity shares to Transferor shareholders, credited as fully paid-up,
   without payment, in lieu of their shares in Transferor Company."
```

### Step 2: Board & Shareholder Approvals (15-30 days)

**Board Meetings** (Both Transferor & Transferee):
1. **Agenda**: Approve scheme of amalgamation, authorize filing with NCLT
2. **Quorum**: 1/3rd of total strength or 2 directors (Section 174)
3. **Resolution**: Board resolution approving scheme (attached as Annexure to NCLT application)
4. **Notice**: 7 days' notice to all directors (Section 173)

**Shareholder Meetings** (Not Mandatory at This Stage):
- **Option 1**: Obtain shareholder approval BEFORE NCLT filing (demonstrates support, strengthens application)
- **Option 2**: Obtain shareholder approval AFTER first NCLT hearing (when NCLT directs meetings)
- **Common Practice**: Many companies wait for NCLT directions (avoid double meetings if scheme is modified post-filing)

**Creditors' Meetings**:
- If scheme affects creditors' rights (e.g., debt restructuring, payment terms modification), creditors must approve
- If scheme DOES NOT affect creditors (simple merger, creditors' rights unchanged), no creditor meeting required
- **Hindustan Lever case**: Employees (contingent creditors) must be included if retrenchment/gratuity impact

### Step 3: NCLT Application Filing (Form NCLT-1) - Day 0

**Form NCLT-1 Contents**:

**Section A - Applicant Details**:
- Company name, CIN, registered office, incorporation date
- Authorized capital, paid-up capital, number of shareholders, creditors

**Section B - Scheme Particulars**:
- Type of scheme (amalgamation/demerger/compromise/capital reduction)
- Appointed Date (retrospective date from which accounting impact begins, e.g., 1st April of previous financial year)
- Estimated Effective Date (date by which scheme expected to be implemented)

**Section C - Parties to Scheme**:
- Transferor Company (merging company)
- Transferee Company (surviving company)
- If demerger, Resulting Company

**Section D - Approvals Obtained/Required**:
- Board resolutions (both companies)
- Shareholder resolutions (if obtained pre-filing)
- Regulatory approvals (pending/obtained): ROC, IT, SEBI, CCI, RBI (if financial services)

**Section E - Classes of Creditors/Members**:
- List of classes (secured creditors, unsecured creditors, equity shareholders, preference shareholders)
- Number of members/creditors in each class
- Total value of claims/shares in each class

**Attachments** (20-30 Documents):
1. **Scheme Document** (40-80 pages, main exhibit)
2. **Board Resolutions** (certified copies from both companies)
3. **Shareholder Resolutions** (if obtained, certified copies)
4. **Valuation Reports** (share exchange ratio fairness opinion)
5. **Financial Statements** (last 3 years audited accounts for both companies)
6. **List of Creditors** (names, addresses, amounts owed, classification)
7. **List of Shareholders** (top 50 shareholders + total count, shareholding pattern)
8. **Affidavit of Promoter Directors** (verifying truth of facts stated in scheme, no suppression)
9. **PAN, TAN, GST Certificates** (both companies)
10. **Certificate of Incorporation** (both companies)
11. **MOA & AOA** (both companies)
12. **ROC Master Data** (from MCA portal)
13. **Independent Directors' Certificate** (confirming scheme is fair and reasonable)
14. **Fairness Opinion** (from investment banker/merchant banker, if listed company)

**Filing Fee**:
- **Formula**: ₹5,000 per ₹1 crore of paid-up capital (subject to minimum ₹1 lakh, maximum ₹10 lakh)
- **Example**: Transferor paid-up capital ₹200cr + Transferee ₹500cr = ₹700cr total
  - Fee: ₹700cr ÷ ₹1cr × ₹5,000 = ₹35 lakh (capped at ₹10 lakh)
  - **Actual Fee**: ₹10 lakh (maximum cap applies)

**Filing Process**:
1. **e-Filing**: Upload Form NCLT-1 with attachments on NCLT e-filing portal (https://nclt.gov.in)
2. **Payment**: Pay filing fee online (NEFT/RTGS)
3. **Physical Filing**: Submit hard copies (3 sets) at NCLT registry within 7 days of e-filing
4. **Acknowledgment**: NCLT registry issues acknowledgment within 15 days (with case number, e.g., CP(CAA)/123/MB/2024)

### Step 4: First Hearing (30-60 days after filing)

**NCLT Preliminary Scrutiny** (Before First Hearing):
- NCLT registry checks documents for completeness (all attachments present, fees paid, affidavits proper)
- If deficiencies found: Issues deficiency memo, applicant has 15 days to rectify
- If complete: Lists for first hearing (typically 30-60 days after filing)

**First Hearing Agenda**:

**1. Scheme Presentation** (15-30 minutes):
- Applicant's counsel presents scheme overview (parties, rationale, share exchange ratio, benefits to stakeholders)
- Valuation report highlights (fairness to minority shareholders)
- Regulatory approvals status (CCI, SEBI, IT - pending/obtained)

**2. NCLT's Scrutiny**:
- **Jurisdiction Check**: Are companies registered in NCLT's jurisdiction? (e.g., Mumbai bench for companies with registered office in Mumbai/Thane)
- **Scheme Validity**: Is scheme bonafide (genuine commercial rationale) or colorable device (tax avoidance, defrauding creditors)?
- **Stakeholder Impact**: How does scheme affect creditors, employees, minority shareholders?
- **Public Interest**: Any anti-competitive concerns (market monopoly, layoffs)?

**3. Directions for Meetings** (NCLT Order):

**NCLT First Hearing Order** (Typical Contents):
```
"The Tribunal is satisfied that a meetings of the equity shareholders of the Transferor Company
and Transferee Company ought to be held for considering and, if thought fit, approving the Scheme.

IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

1. MEETINGS:
   a) Equity Shareholders' Meeting of Transferor Company on [Date] at [Time] at [Venue]
   b) Equity Shareholders' Meeting of Transferee Company on [Date] at [Time] at [Venue]
   (No creditors' meeting required as scheme does not affect creditors' rights)

2. NOTICE:
   - Individual notice to be sent to all shareholders 21 days before meeting date
   - Notice to be advertised in newspapers (English + vernacular) 21 days before meeting
   - Notice to be sent to ROC, RD, Official Liquidator, IT Department, SEBI (if listed),
     CCI (if thresholds exceeded) 14 days before meeting

3. QUORUM:
   - 10% of shareholders present in person or by proxy (Section 103)
   - If quorum not met, meeting adjourned by 30 minutes, then those present = quorum

4. VOTING:
   - Poll voting mandatory (no show of hands)
   - Electronic voting (e-voting) to be provided for listed companies (48 hours before meeting)
   - Proxy voting allowed (Form MGT-11, deposited 48 hours before meeting)

5. CHAIRMAN:
   - One of the promoter directors shall chair the meeting
   - Chairman to adjourn meeting if unruly (violence, shouting), reconvene after 7 days

6. SCRUTINIZER:
   - Practicing Company Secretary [Name] appointed to scrutinize voting
   - Scrutinizer to submit report (Form CA-12) within 48 hours of meeting

7. ADVERTISEMENT:
   - Scheme to be advertised in [Newspaper 1 - English] and [Newspaper 2 - Regional language]
     21 days before meeting (minimum 10cm × 15cm size)
   - Advertisement cost borne by applicant companies

8. AFFIDAVITS:
   - Applicant to file affidavits of dispatch of notices, advertisement proofs 7 days before
     second hearing
   - Chairman's report (Form CA-12 with scrutinizer's report) to be filed within 7 days of meeting

9. SECOND HEARING:
   - Listed for [Date] (typically 60-90 days after first hearing)
   - Applicant to file updated status on regulatory approvals by second hearing date
"
```

### Step 5: Dispatch of Notices & Advertisement (21 days before meeting)

**Individual Notices to Shareholders**:

**Notice Format** (Section 234):
```
NOTICE OF MEETING OF EQUITY SHAREHOLDERS
pursuant to the Order dated [Date] of the Hon'ble National Company Law Tribunal, [Bench]

NOTICE is hereby given that a Meeting of the Equity Shareholders of [Company Name] will be held
on [Date] at [Time] at [Venue] to consider and, if thought fit, to approve with or without
modifications, the Scheme of Amalgamation between [Transferor] and [Transferee].

Attached herewith:
1. Explanatory Statement (50-100 pages) containing:
   - Full text of the Scheme
   - Rationale for scheme (business synergies, financial benefits)
   - Share Exchange Ratio with valuation report summary
   - Impact on shareholders (number of shares post-merger, % dilution)
   - Financial information (last 3 years financials, pro forma combined financials)
   - Board's recommendation (why board believes scheme is beneficial)
   - Dissenting shareholder rights (Section 230(11) - can object at NCLT hearing)
   - Proxy form (MGT-11) for shareholders unable to attend
   - e-voting instructions (for listed companies)

2. NCLT Order dated [Date] (First Hearing Order)

Members are requested to:
- Attend the meeting in person or by proxy (proxy form attached)
- Vote electronically (e-voting facility open from [Date] to [Date])
- Review scheme documents available on company website and BSE/NSE websites (if listed)

For any queries, contact:
[Company Secretary Name]
[Email] [Phone]

By Order of the Board
[Company Secretary]
[Date]
```

**Newspaper Advertisement** (Rule 6, NCLT Rules 2016):

**Requirements**:
- **Languages**: English (widely circulated) + Regional language (state where registered office located)
- **Newspapers**: Major national/regional newspapers (e.g., Economic Times + Maharashtra Times for Mumbai companies)
- **Size**: Minimum 10cm × 15cm (prominently visible)
- **Timing**: 21 days before meeting date
- **Content**:
  - Company names, scheme type (amalgamation)
  - Meeting date, time, venue
  - Share exchange ratio
  - How to obtain full scheme (company website, ROC office, stock exchange website)
  - Objection rights (stakeholders can file objections with NCLT till 7 days before second hearing)
  - Contact details for queries

**Notices to Regulatory Authorities** (14 days before meeting):
- **ROC** (Registrar of Companies): Copy of notice + scheme + NCLT first hearing order
- **RD** (Regional Director, MCA): Same as ROC
- **Official Liquidator**: Same as ROC (even though company not in liquidation, statutory requirement)
- **Income Tax Department**: Copy of scheme (for no-objection to tax-neutral treatment)
- **SEBI**: If listed, copy of scheme + valuation reports + fairness opinion
- **CCI**: If thresholds exceeded, copy of scheme + market analysis
- **Stock Exchanges**: If listed, copy of scheme + NCLT order (for trading suspension/approvals)

### Step 6: Shareholder/Creditor Meetings (Meeting Date)

**Meeting Conduct**:

**Quorum**:
- **Listed Company**: 5 shareholders present in person or by proxy (Section 103)
- **Unlisted Company**: 2 shareholders present (Section 103)
- **Larger Threshold** (NCLT typically orders): 10% of total shareholders
- **If Quorum Not Met**: Adjourn by 30 minutes, then those present constitute quorum

**Chairman's Role**:
- Open meeting, confirm quorum present
- Read NCLT order (first hearing order with directions)
- Present scheme highlights (5-10 minute summary)
- Open floor for questions (30-60 minutes typically)
- Clarify doubts on share exchange ratio, valuation, post-merger benefits
- Conduct poll voting (no show of hands allowed for NCLT schemes)

**Voting Process**:

**Poll Voting** (Mandatory - Section 109):
- Every shareholder votes by ballot paper (in-person) or e-voting (for listed)
- Each share = one vote (equity), unless DVR shares (differential voting rights)
- **Resolution**: "RESOLVED that the Scheme of Amalgamation be and is hereby approved."

**E-Voting** (Listed Companies - SEBI LODR Regulation 44):
- E-voting facility open 48 hours before meeting till 1 hour before meeting end
- Shareholders can vote remotely via NSDL/CDSL e-voting platform
- Chairman announces e-voting results at meeting

**Scrutinizer's Role** (Appointed by NCLT):
- Practicing Company Secretary appointed as scrutinizer
- Collects ballot papers, verifies signatures, counts votes
- Reconciles e-votes (from NSDL/CDSL) with physical votes
- Prepares scrutinizer's report within 48 hours of meeting

**Voting Threshold** (Section 230(6)):
```
The scheme shall be deemed approved if:
a) Majority in number (>50% of shareholders who voted, not total shareholders), AND
b) 75% in value (shares held by approving shareholders / total shares that voted ≥ 75%)

Both conditions must be satisfied.
```

**Example**:
- Total Shareholders: 10,000 (holding 1 crore shares)
- Shareholders who voted: 1,000 (holding 80 lakh shares, i.e., 80% turnout in value)
- Voted FOR: 600 shareholders (60% in number ✅) holding 65 lakh shares (81.25% in value ✅)
- Voted AGAINST: 400 shareholders (40% in number) holding 15 lakh shares (18.75% in value)
- **Result**: Scheme APPROVED (both thresholds met: >50% in number + ≥75% in value)

**Chairman's Report (Form CA-12)**:

**Contents**:
- Meeting details (date, time, venue, quorum)
- Attendance (number of shareholders present, shares represented)
- Voting results (for vs against, in number and value)
- Scrutinizer's report (attached)
- Chairman's declaration (scheme approved by requisite majority)
- **Filing**: With NCLT within 7 days of meeting (attached to second hearing documents)

### Step 7: Regulatory Approvals (Parallel with Steps 3-6, 90-120 days)

**Key Regulatory Approvals Required**:

**1. Competition Commission of India (CCI) - Section 6, Competition Act 2002**:

**Thresholds**:
- Combined assets in India >₹2,000 crore OR Combined turnover >₹6,000 crore
- Target assets >₹750 crore OR Target turnover >₹2,250 crore

**Process**:
- **Filing**: Form I (notice of combination) with CCI
- **Fee**: ₹20 lakh (turnover >₹10,000cr) OR ₹5 lakh (₹1,000cr-₹10,000cr)
- **Phase I** (30 days): CCI prima facie review, approves if no AAEC (Appreciable Adverse Effect on Competition) concerns
- **Phase II** (180 days): Detailed investigation if AAEC concerns, can approve with conditions (divestiture, price caps) or prohibit
- **Outcome**: CCI approval order (no objection letter), attach to NCLT second hearing documents

**2. Income Tax Department - No Objection Certificate (NOC)**:

**Purpose**: Ensure scheme qualifies for tax-neutral treatment (Sections 2(1B), 2(19AA))

**Process**:
- File scheme with jurisdictional Assessing Officer (AO)
- AO reviews conditions:
  - Amalgamation (Section 2(1B)): ALL assets/liabilities transferred, ≥75% shareholder continuity, transferor dissolved
  - Demerger (Section 2(19AA)): Undertaking transfer, ≥75% shares issued, going concern
- **Timeline**: 60-90 days (no statutory timeline, often delayed)
- **Outcome**: IT Department issues NOC (or raises objections if conditions not met)

**3. SEBI & Stock Exchanges - Listing Approvals** (If Listed Companies):

**SEBI Approval**:
- **Regulation 37, SEBI LODR**: Scheme affecting shareholders' rights requires SEBI approval
- **Filing**: Draft scheme + valuation reports + fairness opinion (from merchant banker)
- **SEBI Review**: Valuation methodology, fairness to minority, disclosure adequacy
- **Timeline**: 30-45 days
- **Outcome**: SEBI no-objection letter

**Stock Exchange Approval**:
- **BSE/NSE Filing**: Submit scheme + NCLT order + valuation reports
- **Trading Suspension**: Exchanges may suspend trading in transferor shares (to prevent speculative trading)
- **In-Principle Listing Approval**: For new shares to be issued by transferee
- **Timeline**: 15-30 days after SEBI NOC
- **Outcome**: Stock exchange approval letter (for listing new shares)

**4. ROC & Regional Director - No Objection**:

**ROC Review**:
- Ensure no pending prosecutions, ROC dues, annual filing defaults
- If defaults exist, companies must regularize (pay penalties, file pending returns) before scheme approval
- **Timeline**: 30-45 days
- **Outcome**: ROC no-objection (often given in second hearing itself if no objections)

**5. Sectoral Regulators** (If Applicable):
- **RBI**: For banks, NBFCs, financial services companies (60-90 days)
- **IRDAI**: For insurance companies (60-90 days)
- **TRAI**: For telecom companies (spectrum license transfer approval, 30-60 days)
- **Petroleum Ministry**: For oil & gas companies (mining lease transfer, 60-90 days)

### Step 8: Second Hearing (Final Hearing) - 60-90 days after First Hearing

**Pre-Hearing Filings** (7 days before second hearing):

**Applicant Must File**:
1. **Affidavit of Dispatch**: Verifying individual notices sent to all shareholders 21 days before meeting (postal receipts/courier tracking attached)
2. **Affidavit of Advertisement**: Newspaper advertisement proofs (cuttings from newspapers, 21 days before meeting)
3. **Chairman's Report** (Form CA-12): Voting results, scrutinizer's report (scheme approved by 75%)
4. **Regulatory Approvals**: CCI order, IT NOC, SEBI NOC, Stock Exchange approval (originals + copies)
5. **Updated Affidavit**: Any material changes since first hearing (financial performance, shareholding changes, litigation)

**Objections by Dissenting Stakeholders** (Section 230(11)):

**Who Can Object**:
- Dissenting shareholders (voted against, or didn't vote)
- Creditors (if scheme affects their rights)
- ROC, RD, Official Liquidator, IT Department (if any legal/regulatory concerns)
- Public Interest Groups (if scheme affects public interest, e.g., large-scale layoffs, environmental damage)

**Grounds for Objection**:
1. **Unfair Valuation**: Share exchange ratio undervalues shares (minority getting raw deal)
2. **Non-Disclosure**: Material facts suppressed (hidden liabilities, related party transactions not disclosed)
3. **Procedural Defects**: Inadequate notice (received <21 days), defective explanatory statement
4. **Prejudice to Creditors**: Debt assumption by weaker company (creditors' security at risk)
5. **Tax Avoidance**: Scheme designed solely to avoid tax (no commercial substance)
6. **Public Interest**: Anti-competitive (market monopoly), mass layoffs, environmental harm

**Objection Filing**:
- File written objection with NCLT (7 days before second hearing)
- Serve copy on applicant companies
- Appear at second hearing (in person or through lawyer) to argue objection

**Second Hearing Proceedings**:

**1. Applicant's Presentation** (30-45 minutes):
- Confirm shareholder/creditor meetings held, 75% majority approved scheme
- Present regulatory approvals (CCI, IT, SEBI, ROC - all NOCs obtained)
- Highlight benefits to stakeholders (synergies, shareholder value, employee continuity)
- Address any concerns raised in first hearing

**2. Objectors' Arguments** (15-30 minutes per objector):
- Dissenting shareholder argues unfair valuation (comparables show higher SER warranted)
- Creditor argues debt assumption increases credit risk
- Public interest group argues monopoly concerns

**3. Applicant's Rebuttal** (15-30 minutes):
- Counter objections with valuation justifications (independent valuer certified SER fair)
- Provide additional disclosures (if non-disclosure alleged)
- Demonstrate commercial rationale (not tax avoidance)

**4. Regulatory Authorities' Submissions**:
- ROC: No objection (pending filings regularized)
- IT Department: No objection (conditions for tax-neutral treatment satisfied)
- CCI: No AAEC concerns (market share analysis shows <25% post-merger)

**5. NCLT's Decision**:

**NCLT May**:
a) **Approve Scheme** (most common if 75% approved + no material objections)
b) **Modify Scheme** (e.g., adjust SER, add creditor protections, specify employee retention conditions)
c) **Reject Scheme** (if unfair to minority, non-disclosure, public interest concerns)
d) **Adjourn Hearing** (seek further clarifications, additional regulatory approvals)

**NCLT Sanction Order** (If Approved):
```
"Having heard the learned counsels for the applicant companies, perused the records, and
considering the approval of the scheme by the requisite majority of shareholders and the
no-objections from regulatory authorities, the Tribunal is satisfied that the scheme is
fair and reasonable to all stakeholders.

IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

1. The Scheme of Amalgamation between [Transferor] and [Transferee] is SANCTIONED and
   shall become effective from the Effective Date (defined as the date on which certified
   copy of this order is filed with ROC).

2. Upon effectiveness:
   - ALL assets and liabilities of Transferor vest in Transferee without further act or deed
   - Transferor shareholders receive [X] shares in Transferee for every [Y] shares held in Transferor
   - Transferor is dissolved without winding up (Section 232(6))
   - Transferee succeeds to all contracts, licenses, permits of Transferor

3. Applicant companies to file certified copy of this order with ROC (Form INC-28) within 30 days.

4. Transferee to issue shares to Transferor shareholders within 60 days of Effective Date.

5. Transferee to file confirmation of implementation (with ROC) within 90 days of Effective Date.

Certified copy as per rules.

[NCLT Member - Judicial]
[NCLT Member - Technical]
Date: [Date]
"
```

### Step 9: Post-Sanction Implementation (30-90 days)

**Filing with ROC (Form INC-28) - Within 30 Days of NCLT Order**:

**Form INC-28 Contents**:
- Certified copy of NCLT sanction order (obtained from NCLT registry, ₹500 per certified copy)
- Scheme document (final version as approved by NCLT)
- Particulars of transferor (CIN, name, registered office - to be struck off)
- Particulars of transferee (CIN, name, registered office - surviving company)
- Effective Date declaration (typically date of Form INC-28 filing)

**Effective Date**:
- **Option 1**: Date of filing Form INC-28 with ROC (most common)
- **Option 2**: Date specified in NCLT order (e.g., 30 days after NCLT order)
- **Option 3**: Last of all conditions precedent satisfied (if scheme specifies pending conditions, e.g., awaiting one regulatory approval)

**Share Issuance - Within 60 Days of Effective Date**:

**Process**:
1. **Board Meeting**: Transferee board allots shares to transferor shareholders per SER
2. **Share Certificates/Demat Credits**:
   - Physical Shareholders: Issue physical share certificates (printed, signed by directors + company secretary)
   - Demat Shareholders: Credit shares to demat accounts via NSDL/CDSL (corporate action - merger, upload shareholder data)
3. **Listing**: If listed, apply to stock exchanges for listing new shares (in-principle approval already obtained, now final listing)
4. **Trading**: New shares tradeable on stock exchange from listing date (typically T+7 from demat credit)

**Asset & Liability Transfer**:

**Legal Effect**: By operation of NCLT order, ALL assets/liabilities vest in transferee from Effective Date (Section 232(6))
- No individual asset transfer deeds required (unlike slump sale)
- No stamp duty (court order exempt under stamp acts)
- **Automatic Vesting**: Land, buildings, machinery, inventory, cash, receivables, loans, payables - ALL transfer automatically

**Practical Steps** (Administrative, Not Legal Requirements):
1. **Land/Property**: Inform Sub-Registrar (mutation of property records from transferor to transferee name)
2. **Bank Accounts**: Transfer cash balances, close transferor accounts, novate loan agreements
3. **Contracts**: Notify customers/suppliers of automatic novation (no consent required - by NCLT order)
4. **Licenses**: Inform regulatory authorities (drug licenses, factory licenses, pollution clearances - transfer to transferee name)
5. **IP**: File assignment applications with IP office (trademarks, patents - though vested by NCLT order, administrative transfer for records)

**Employee Transfer** (Section 25FF, Industrial Disputes Act):

**Automatic Transfer**: ALL employees transfer to transferee on terms not less favorable than transferor
- **Continuity of Service**: Tenure with transferor counts towards transferee service (for gratuity, PF, leave)
- **No Retrenchment**: Cannot terminate employees merely due to transfer (violation = unfair labor practice)
- **PF Transfer**: File Form 13 with EPFO (transfer PF accounts from transferor to transferee)

**Dissolution of Transferor**:
- Upon filing Form INC-28, ROC strikes off transferor from register (Section 232(6))
- **No Winding Up**: Transferor dissolved without liquidation (no winding up petition, no liquidator appointment)
- **PAN Cancellation**: Inform IT Department to cancel transferor's PAN (file Form 49A)

**Confirmation of Implementation (Form INC-28 Supplementary) - Within 90 Days**:
- Transferee files confirmation: Shares issued ✓, Assets transferred ✓, Liabilities assumed ✓, Transferor dissolved ✓
- Attach: Share allotment statement, bank statements (cash transferred), board resolution (confirming implementation)

### Step 10: Post-Implementation Compliance (Ongoing)

**Financial Statements**:
- **First Year Post-Merger**: Transferee prepares consolidated financials (including transferor's operations from Appointed Date)
- **Auditor's Report**: Mention scheme implementation, accounting treatment (pooling/purchase method)

**Tax Filings**:
- **Transferor**: File final income tax return (till Effective Date), indicate "merged" status
- **Transferee**: File return including transferor's income (from Appointed Date to Effective Date)
- **Carry-Forward Losses**: Transferee can utilize transferor's losses (if Section 72A conditions met - ≥75% shareholder continuity)

**Annual Report Disclosure**:
- **Transferee's Annual Report**: Disclose scheme implementation, impact on financials (sales, assets, EPS)
- **Comparative Financials**: Restate prior year comparatives (as if merged company existed in prior year, for pooling method)

## Practical Examples

### Example 1 - Holding-Subsidiary Merger (Listed Companies)

**Background**:
- **TechCorp Ltd.** (Listed on BSE/NSE): IT services company, market cap ₹10,000cr
- **TechSub Pvt. Ltd.** (Unlisted subsidiary): Software products company, 100% owned by TechCorp
- **Rationale**: Simplify group structure, consolidate operations, reduce compliance costs (one audit, one board vs two)

**Transaction Structure**:

**Scheme**: TechSub (Transferor) merges into TechCorp (Transferee)

**Share Exchange Ratio**: NOT APPLICABLE (TechCorp owns 100% of TechSub, no minority shareholders)
- TechCorp cancels its own shareholding in TechSub (no shares issued to itself)
- TechSub dissolved, TechCorp continues as surviving entity

**NCLT Process**:

**Step 1 - Filing** (Day 0):
- File Form NCLT-1 with Mumbai bench (both companies registered in Mumbai)
- Attached: Scheme, board resolutions, financials, valuation report (net worth method showing TechSub worth ₹500cr)
- Filing fee: ₹10 lakh (maximum cap)

**Step 2 - First Hearing** (Day 45):
- NCLT directs meeting of TechCorp shareholders only (no TechSub meeting as 100% owned)
- **Rationale**: Section 230(7) exempts meetings if ≥90% owned (can dispense with subsidiary's meeting)
- NCLT orders newspaper advertisement (Economic Times + Maharashtra Times)

**Step 3 - Meeting** (Day 90):
- TechCorp shareholders meeting: 500 shareholders attend (10,000 total), 80% turnout in value
- **Voting Result**: 62% in number (310/500 voted FOR), 78% in value (approved ✓)
- **Chairman's Report**: Scheme approved by requisite majority

**Step 4 - Regulatory Approvals** (Parallel, Days 0-120):
- **CCI**: Not required (no change in control, wholly-owned subsidiary merger exempt)
- **SEBI**: Reviewed scheme, no concerns (merger within group, no related party benefit)
- **IT Department**: Issued NOC (conditions for tax-neutral amalgamation satisfied)
- **Stock Exchanges**: BSE/NSE approved (in-principle listing not required as no new shares issued)

**Step 5 - Second Hearing** (Day 120):
- TechCorp presents regulatory approvals (IT NOC, SEBI NOC, ROC no objection)
- **Objection**: 1 minority shareholder objected (claimed merger will dilute TechCorp's focus, TechSub losses will drag profitability)
  - **NCLT's View**: Commercial decision, board has discretion, no unfairness to minority (TechSub profitable ₹50cr EBITDA, not loss-making)
- **NCLT Sanction Order** issued (Scheme approved)

**Step 6 - Implementation** (Days 120-150):
- File Form INC-28 with ROC (Day 125) → Effective Date = 125
- TechSub dissolved (struck off from ROC register)
- Assets transfer: TechSub's software IP, employees (200), contracts (all vest in TechCorp)
- **No Shares Issued**: TechCorp cancels its investment in TechSub (₹100cr investment, now eliminated against ₹500cr net assets acquired → ₹400cr reserves increase)

**Outcome**:
- TechCorp: Simplified structure, reduced compliance cost (₹50L per year - one audit, one board vs two)
- Employees: Continue with TechCorp (no disruption, continuity of service maintained)
- Shareholders: Benefit from consolidated operations (combined revenue ₹5,000cr + ₹500cr = ₹5,500cr)

**Tax Treatment**:
- **Tax-Neutral Amalgamation**: No capital gains tax (Section 2(1B) conditions met - 100% ownership, all assets transferred)
- TechCorp inherits TechSub's MAT credit (₹20cr), tax losses (₹30cr) - can utilize against future profits

## Landmark Cases

### 1. Miheer H. Mafatlal v. Mafatlal Industries Ltd. (1997 Supreme Court)

**Facts**:
- Scheme of amalgamation between two group companies (Mafatlal Industries + Mafatlal Fine Spinning)
- Scheme approved by 75% majority at shareholder meeting
- Minority shareholder (Miheer Mafatlal, 1% shareholding) objected: Share exchange ratio unfair (undervalued his shares)
- NCLT approved scheme despite objection (held: 75% approved, minority cannot block)

**Issue**: Can NCLT approve scheme despite minority objection, even if 75% majority achieved?

**Held** (Supreme Court - Remanded to NCLT):
- **NCLT must independently evaluate fairness**, not merely rubberstamp 75% majority
- "The court should be satisfied that the arrangement is such as a man of business would reasonably approve, and is not unfair or unjust to the minority."
- Just because 75% approved does not mean minority's objections are irrelevant
- **NCLT must scrutinize**: Valuation methodology, fairness to minority, disclosure adequacy

**Principle**:
- **Minority Protection**: NCLT is guardian of minority interests, must ensure scheme is not oppressive
- **Valuation Scrutiny**: NCLT should appoint independent valuer if doubts on SER fairness
- **Disclosure**: Full and frank disclosure of all material facts mandatory (non-disclosure = ground for rejection)

**Impact**: NCLT now routinely scrutinizes valuation reports, appoints independent valuers (at company's cost) if minority raises credible concerns on SER fairness

### 2. Hindustan Lever Employees' Union v. Hindustan Lever Ltd. (1995 Supreme Court)

**Facts**:
- Hindustan Lever (HLL) proposed merger with subsidiary Brooke Bond (tea company)
- Scheme involved plant closure at Brooke Bond Kolkata factory (500 employees to be retrenched)
- **No meeting of employees convened** (HLL argued: employees are not "creditors or members")
- Employees' union objected: We are "contingent creditors" (future gratuity, retrenchment compensation), should have been given meeting

**Issue**: Are employees "creditors" under Section 230, entitled to vote on scheme affecting them?

**Held** (Supreme Court - Favored Employees):
- **Employees are "contingent creditors"** (future gratuity, leave encashment, retrenchment compensation = contingent liability)
- If scheme affects employees' rights (retrenchment, transfer, compensation), **employees' meeting required**
- Cannot exclude employees merely because liability is contingent (not crystallized)

**Principle**:
- **Broad Definition of "Creditors"**: Includes contingent, prospective, future creditors (not just present debts)
- **Employees' Rights**: Employees affected by scheme must be classified as separate class, given meeting to vote
- **Procedural Fairness**: Cannot deprive stakeholders of voting rights by artificially excluding them

**Impact**: Now, schemes involving employee impact (retrenchment, transfer, VRS) require employees' meeting (classified as "contingent creditors")

### 3. Azadi Bachao Andolan v. Union of India (2003 Supreme Court)

**Facts**:
- Vodafone acquired Hutchison's India telecom business via Cayman Islands holding company (to avoid capital gains tax in India)
- Structure: Vodafone (Netherlands) → Cayman Holding → Hutchison India (no direct India-to-India transfer)
- Public interest group "Azadi Bachao Andolan" objected: Tax avoidance, against public interest
- NCLT initially refused to approve scheme (held: colorable device to avoid tax)

**Issue**: Can NCLT reject scheme on grounds of tax avoidance/public interest, even if no shareholder/creditor objection?

**Held** (Supreme Court - Allowed Scheme on Appeal):
- NCLT has **discretion to reject scheme if against public interest**, even if 75% approved
- However, in this case: Structure was legal (Mauritius-India tax treaty allowed), no illegality
- "Business restructuring for tax efficiency is permissible, as long as not illegal"

**Principle**:
- **Public Interest**: NCLT can consider public interest (competition, tax avoidance, employment) beyond just shareholder/creditor interests
- **Tax Avoidance vs Evasion**: Tax avoidance (legal structuring to minimize tax) is permissible, tax evasion (illegal non-payment) is not
- **NCLT's Discretion**: Wide discretion to reject scheme if bonafide doubt on intent (but must not act arbitrarily)

**Impact**: NCLT now scrutinizes schemes for commercial substance (not just legal form), can question if sole purpose is tax avoidance with no business rationale

## Compliance Checklist

### Pre-Filing Checklist (Before NCLT Application)

**Due Diligence**:
- [ ] Financial due diligence completed (last 3 years financials audited, hidden liabilities identified)
- [ ] Legal due diligence completed (title deeds verified, litigation disclosed, contracts reviewed)
- [ ] Valuation report prepared by registered valuer (SER certified as fair and reasonable)
- [ ] Tax structuring confirmed (scheme qualifies for tax-neutral treatment under Section 2(1B) or 2(19AA))

**Internal Approvals**:
- [ ] Board resolutions approved by both transferor and transferee companies
- [ ] Audit committee approval (if required - Section 177, for listed companies)
- [ ] Independent directors' certificate obtained (confirming scheme fairness)

**Draft Scheme**:
- [ ] Scheme document drafted (40-80 pages, comprehensive coverage of transfer, vesting, share issue, accounting, tax)
- [ ] Appointed Date selected (retrospective accounting date, typically 1st April of previous FY)
- [ ] Share Exchange Ratio finalized (based on valuation report)
- [ ] Conditions precedent listed (NCLT sanction, CCI, IT, SEBI, ROC approvals)

### Filing Checklist (Form NCLT-1 Preparation)

**Forms & Documents**:
- [ ] Form NCLT-1 prepared (company details, scheme particulars, classes of creditors/members)
- [ ] Scheme document (signed by directors of both companies)
- [ ] Board resolutions (certified true copies, signed by company secretary with seal)
- [ ] Shareholder resolutions (if obtained pre-filing, certified copies)
- [ ] Valuation reports (2-3 valuers if listed company per SEBI LODR)
- [ ] Financial statements (last 3 years audited accounts for both companies)
- [ ] List of creditors (names, addresses, amounts, classification)
- [ ] List of shareholders (top 50 + total count, shareholding pattern for listed)
- [ ] Affidavits of directors (verifying facts, no suppression of material information)
- [ ] PAN, TAN, GST certificates, Certificate of Incorporation, MOA/AOA
- [ ] ROC master data (current status of companies from MCA portal)
- [ ] Independent directors' certificate (for listed companies)
- [ ] Fairness opinion (from merchant banker, for listed companies)

**Filing Fee**:
- [ ] Filing fee calculated (₹5,000 per ₹1cr paid-up capital, max ₹10L)
- [ ] Fee payment proof (NEFT/RTGS transaction receipt)

**e-Filing**:
- [ ] Form NCLT-1 uploaded on NCLT e-filing portal (https://nclt.gov.in)
- [ ] All attachments uploaded (PDF format, <5MB per file)
- [ ] E-filing acknowledgment received (save acknowledgment number for future reference)

**Physical Filing** (Within 7 Days of E-Filing):
- [ ] Hard copies prepared (3 sets: 1 for NCLT, 1 for ROC/RD, 1 for Official Liquidator)
- [ ] Index prepared (list of all documents with page numbers)
- [ ] Documents bound (spiral binding, indexed, paginated)
- [ ] Submitted at NCLT registry (obtain receipt with case number)

### First Hearing Checklist

**Preparation**:
- [ ] Lead counsel briefed (scheme summary, key points, anticipated questions)
- [ ] Supporting counsel/CA/valuer on standby (to answer technical questions)
- [ ] Presentation prepared (5-10 slides: scheme overview, rationale, SER, benefits)
- [ ] NCLT directions draft prepared (proposed meeting dates, notice requirements - submit to NCLT for consideration)

**First Hearing Attendance**:
- [ ] Directors of both companies present (or authorized representative with board resolution)
- [ ] Company secretaries present (to take notes, assist counsel)
- [ ] Lead counsel argues (presents scheme, requests directions for meetings)

**Post-First Hearing**:
- [ ] NCLT order received (directions for meetings, notice requirements, advertisement)
- [ ] Meeting dates finalized (coordinate with auditorium/venue, COVID protocols if applicable)
- [ ] Notice drafting started (explanatory statement, proxy form, e-voting instructions)

### Meeting Preparation Checklist (21 Days Before Meeting)

**Notice Dispatch**:
- [ ] Explanatory statement finalized (50-100 pages: scheme text, rationale, financials, SER, board recommendation)
- [ ] Individual notices dispatched to all shareholders (by courier/registered post, obtain tracking proof)
- [ ] Affidavit of dispatch prepared (list of shareholders with dispatch dates, tracking numbers)

**Advertisement**:
- [ ] Newspapers identified (English national + regional vernacular)
- [ ] Advertisement content finalized (meeting details, scheme summary, objection rights)
- [ ] Advertisement published (21 days before meeting, 10cm×15cm minimum size)
- [ ] Advertisement proofs obtained (newspaper cuttings, affidavit of publication)

**Regulatory Notices**:
- [ ] Notices sent to ROC, RD, Official Liquidator (14 days before meeting)
- [ ] Notices sent to IT Department (scheme copy, request for NOC)
- [ ] Notices sent to SEBI (if listed, scheme + valuation + fairness opinion)
- [ ] Notices sent to CCI (if thresholds exceeded, Form I filed, seeking approval)
- [ ] Notices sent to Stock Exchanges (if listed, scheme + NCLT order)

**E-Voting Setup** (For Listed Companies):
- [ ] E-voting window opened (48 hours before meeting till 1 hour before meeting end)
- [ ] Shareholders notified of e-voting facility (email + SMS with login details)
- [ ] Technical support arranged (helpline for shareholders facing e-voting issues)

### Meeting Day Checklist

**Venue Setup**:
- [ ] Auditorium/hall booked (capacity sufficient for anticipated attendance)
- [ ] Registration desk setup (attendance register, ballot papers, proxy verification)
- [ ] Presentation AV equipment ready (projector, screen, mics)
- [ ] COVID protocols (if applicable - masks, sanitizers, social distancing)

**Meeting Conduct**:
- [ ] Chairman present (promoter director, as designated in NCLT order)
- [ ] Scrutinizer present (practicing company secretary, appointed by NCLT)
- [ ] Company secretary present (to record minutes, assist chairman)
- [ ] Quorum confirmed (10% shareholders or as per NCLT order)
- [ ] NCLT order read (first hearing order with directions)
- [ ] Scheme presented (10-minute summary by chairman or CEO)
- [ ] Questions answered (30-60 minutes, clarify doubts on SER, valuation, benefits)
- [ ] Poll voting conducted (ballot papers distributed, e-voting results announced)
- [ ] Meeting concluded (chairman thanks shareholders, declares voting results after scrutinizer's report)

**Post-Meeting** (Within 48 Hours):
- [ ] Scrutinizer prepares report (vote count, majority achieved, recommendation)
- [ ] Chairman signs Form CA-12 (chairman's report with scrutinizer's report attached)
- [ ] Form CA-12 filed with NCLT (within 7 days of meeting, attach to second hearing documents)

### Second Hearing Checklist (7 Days Before Hearing)

**Filings**:
- [ ] Affidavit of dispatch filed (postal receipts for individual notices)
- [ ] Affidavit of advertisement filed (newspaper cuttings, 21 days before meeting)
- [ ] Chairman's report filed (Form CA-12 with voting results, scrutinizer's report)
- [ ] Regulatory approvals filed (CCI order, IT NOC, SEBI NOC, Stock Exchange approval)
- [ ] Updated affidavit filed (any material changes since first hearing - financials, litigation, shareholding)

**Preparation**:
- [ ] Review objections filed by dissenters (prepare responses, additional disclosures if needed)
- [ ] Update regulatory status (confirm all approvals received, attach originals/certified copies)
- [ ] Prepare final arguments (address objections, demonstrate scheme fairness, request sanction)

**Second Hearing Attendance**:
- [ ] Directors present (both companies)
- [ ] Lead counsel argues (presents regulatory approvals, addresses objections, requests sanction)
- [ ] Supporting experts present (valuer, CA - if NCLT seeks clarifications on technical points)

**Post-Second Hearing**:
- [ ] NCLT sanction order obtained (if approved, collect certified copy from NCLT registry)
- [ ] If adjourned: Comply with NCLT's directions (additional disclosures, modified SER, etc.), relist for next hearing

### Implementation Checklist (Within 30-90 Days of Sanction)

**ROC Filing (Within 30 Days)**:
- [ ] Form INC-28 prepared (certified copy of NCLT order, scheme document, effective date declaration)
- [ ] Form INC-28 filed with ROC (both transferor and transferee jurisdictions)
- [ ] Effective Date confirmed (date of filing INC-28 OR date specified in NCLT order)
- [ ] Transferor dissolved (struck off from ROC register automatically upon Form INC-28 filing)

**Share Issuance (Within 60 Days)**:
- [ ] Board meeting convened (transferee board allots shares per SER)
- [ ] Share allotment letters sent to transferor shareholders (within 15 days of allotment)
- [ ] Demat credits processed (for demat shareholders via NSDL/CDSL, corporate action - merger)
- [ ] Physical share certificates issued (for physical shareholders, printed and dispatched)
- [ ] Listing application submitted (to BSE/NSE for listing new shares, if listed company)

**Asset & Liability Transfer**:
- [ ] Bank accounts transferred (cash balances moved, transferor accounts closed)
- [ ] Loan novation (lenders notified, loan agreements novated to transferee)
- [ ] Contracts novated (customers/suppliers notified of automatic novation by NCLT order)
- [ ] Property mutation (inform Sub-Registrar for land records transfer)
- [ ] Licenses transferred (drug licenses, factory licenses, pollution clearances - apply for name change to transferee)
- [ ] IP assignment (file with IP office - trademarks, patents, though vested by NCLT order)

**Employee Transfer**:
- [ ] Employees notified (transfer letters issued, confirming continuity of service)
- [ ] PF accounts transferred (Form 13 filed with EPFO within 30 days)
- [ ] ESI accounts transferred (notify ESI authorities)
- [ ] Gratuity liability assumed (transferee becomes liable for gratuity from employee's joining date with transferor)

**Tax Compliance**:
- [ ] IT Department notified (transferor's final return filed till effective date, marked "merged")
- [ ] PAN cancellation (transferor's PAN surrender application filed, Form 49A)
- [ ] TDS credits transferred (transferor's TDS credit transferred to transferee's account)

**Confirmation of Implementation (Within 90 Days)**:
- [ ] Form INC-28 supplementary filed (confirmation: shares issued, assets transferred, liabilities assumed, transferor dissolved)
- [ ] Attach: Share allotment statement, bank statements (cash transferred), board resolution (confirming implementation)
