# Prepack Insolvency for MSMEs - Fast-Track Resolution Framework

## Protocol ID
**IL-IBC-PREPACK-INSOLVENCY** (v1.0)

---

## Purpose
Guide MSME corporate debtors, financial creditors, and insolvency professionals through the Pre-Packaged Insolvency Resolution Process (PPIRP) under Chapter III-A (Sections 54A-54P) of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code 2016, covering eligibility criteria, 120-day timeline, promoter participation framework, Swiss challenge mechanism, and fast-track resolution plan approval.

---

## Legal Framework

### 1. What is Prepack Insolvency?

**Definition** (Section 54A):
```
"Pre-Packaged Insolvency Resolution Process" means the resolution process
for corporate debtors classified as micro, small and medium enterprises
under the Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development Act, 2006
wherein the resolution plan is submitted along with the application for
initiating the corporate insolvency resolution process.
```

**Key Characteristics**:
1. **Pre-Negotiated Plan**: Resolution plan negotiated with creditors BEFORE formal CIRP initiation (hence "pre-packaged")
2. **Fast-Track Timeline**: 120 days (vs 330 days for regular CIRP) - 63% faster resolution
3. **Minimal Business Disruption**: Existing management continues during process (promoter remains as MD/CEO)
4. **Promoter Participation**: Promoters CAN submit resolution plan (Section 29A ineligibility does NOT apply to prepack)
5. **Lower Costs**: CIRP costs ~₹5-10 lakh (vs ₹50 lakh-1 crore for large companies in regular CIRP)
6. **Swiss Challenge**: Base plan open for counter-bids, original applicant gets right to match best bid

### 2. Eligibility for Prepack

**Who Can Initiate** (Section 54A):
- **ONLY Financial Creditors**: Can initiate PPIRP for MSME corporate debtors
- **NOT Operational Creditors**: Cannot initiate (unlike regular CIRP where operational creditors can file under Section 9)
- **NOT Corporate Debtor**: Cannot self-initiate (unlike regular CIRP where corporate debtor can file under Section 10)

**MSME Definition** (MSMED Act 2006, amended 2020):

| **Category** | **Investment in Plant & Machinery** | **Annual Turnover** |
|--------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Micro Enterprise** | ≤₹1 crore | ≤₹5 crore |
| **Small Enterprise** | ≤₹10 crore | ≤₹50 crore |
| **Medium Enterprise** | ≤₹50 crore | ≤₹250 crore |

**Eligibility Conditions** (Section 54C):

1. **MSME Classification**: Corporate debtor must be MSME (investment + turnover within limits)
2. **Default**: Debt default ≥₹1 lakh (threshold same as regular CIRP under Section 4)
3. **No Ongoing CIRP**: Corporate debtor NOT already under CIRP/liquidation
4. **Base Resolution Plan**: Must submit base resolution plan along with application (negotiated with ≥66% financial creditors)
5. **Creditor Consent**: ≥66% financial creditors (by value) must approve initiation of PPIRP
6. **No Discipline Issues**: Corporate debtor not ineligible under Section 54C(b) (willful defaulter, NPA >1 year, etc.)

**Exclusions** (Section 54C):
- Corporate debtor who has undergone PPIRP OR CIRP in preceding 3 years (cooling-off period)
- Corporate debtor with ongoing IBC proceedings
- Corporate debtor with complex creditor structure (>50 financial creditors makes prepack impractical)

### 3. Timeline Overview

**120-Day Strict Timeline** (Section 54J):

```
DAY 0: NCLT admits PPIRP application with base resolution plan
       RP appointed, moratorium declared (Section 54G)

DAY 2: Public announcement issued (Form PPIRP-1)

DAY 7: RP invites expressions of interest for prospective resolution applicants
       (Swiss challenge begins)

DAY 30: Deadline for prospective applicants to submit resolution plans

DAY 45: RP submits resolution plans to CoC for evaluation

DAY 60: CoC votes on resolution plans (≥66% approval required)
        - Option 1: Base plan approved → Proceed to NCLT approval
        - Option 2: Counter plan approved → Original applicant gets right to match
        - Option 3: All plans rejected → Convert to regular CIRP (180-330 days)

DAY 90: NCLT hearing (if plan approved by CoC)

DAY 120: NCLT order (approve plan OR reject → convert to regular CIRP)
```

**Key Difference from Regular CIRP**:
- Regular CIRP: 180-330 days (resolution plan submitted DURING process)
- Prepack: 120 days (resolution plan submitted WITH application, BEFORE process starts)

## Prepack Process - Step by Step

### Step 1: Pre-Initiation Negotiation (Day -90 to Day 0)

**Promoter's Actions** (Before Filing):

1. **Financial Assessment**:
   - Admit company in financial stress (cash flow issues, debt servicing difficult)
   - Calculate total debt: Financial creditors ₹15 crore, Operational creditors ₹3 crore
   - Assess going concern value: ₹25 crore (vs liquidation value ₹8 crore)

2. **Creditor Engagement**:
   - Meet with financial creditors (banks, NBFCs)
   - Present turnaround plan: Propose ₹12 crore payment (80% recovery) over 3 years vs ₹8 crore immediate liquidation (53% recovery)
   - Negotiate terms: Haircut 20%, debt-equity conversion, promoter retains 51% equity

3. **Base Resolution Plan Drafting**:
   - Engage insolvency professional to draft plan
   - Obtain creditor consent: If ≥66% financial creditors agree (by value), proceed to PPIRP

**Example - ABC Engineering (MSME)**:

```
COMPANY PROFILE:
- ABC Engineering Pvt Ltd (Small Enterprise)
- Investment in Plant & Machinery: ₹8 crore
- Annual Turnover: ₹35 crore
- Default: ₹15 crore debt (to 3 banks), last payment missed 4 months ago

CREDITOR STRUCTURE:
A. FINANCIAL CREDITORS: ₹15 crore
   1. State Bank of India: ₹8 crore (53.3% voting)
   2. HDFC Bank: ₹5 crore (33.3% voting)
   3. ICICI Bank: ₹2 crore (13.3% voting)

B. OPERATIONAL CREDITORS: ₹3 crore
   (Suppliers, service providers - NO voting rights in PPIRP)

PROMOTER'S BASE RESOLUTION PLAN (Pre-Negotiated):

PAYMENT TERMS:
- Financial Creditors: ₹12 crore (80% recovery) over 3 years
  - Year 1: ₹4 crore (26.7%)
  - Year 2: ₹4 crore (26.7%)
  - Year 3: ₹4 crore (26.7%)
  - Total: ₹12 crore (80% recovery vs ₹8cr liquidation = 53%)

- Operational Creditors: ₹2.4 crore (80% recovery) over 2 years
  - Year 1: ₹1.2 crore
  - Year 2: ₹1.2 crore

FUNDING SOURCES:
- Promoter Equity: ₹3 crore fresh infusion (from personal savings + family)
- Operational Cash Flows: ₹9 crore (₹3cr/year from operations, EBITDA ₹5cr/year)
- Asset Sales: ₹2 crore (sell non-core assets - vacant land parcel)

BUSINESS PLAN:
- Year 1: Revenue ₹40 crore, EBITDA ₹5 crore (12.5% margin)
- Year 2: Revenue ₹50 crore, EBITDA ₹7.5 crore (15% margin)
- Year 3: Revenue ₹60 crore, EBITDA ₹10.5 crore (17.5% margin)

MANAGEMENT & CONTROL:
- Promoter (Mr. Rajesh Kumar) continues as Managing Director
- Existing management team retained (no layoffs)
- Board restructured: 5 directors (Promoter 2, Bank nominee 2, Independent 1)

CREDITOR CONSENT:
- SBI: Agreed (₹8cr × 80% = ₹6.4cr vs ₹4.24cr liquidation, +51% better)
- HDFC: Agreed (₹5cr × 80% = ₹4cr vs ₹2.65cr liquidation, +51% better)
- ICICI: Agreed (₹2cr × 80% = ₹1.6cr vs ₹1.06cr liquidation, +51% better)

Total Consent: 100% (all 3 banks agreed, ≥66% threshold met)
```

### Step 2: PPIRP Application Filing (Day 0)

**Application Format** (Form PPIRP-A):

```
IN THE NATIONAL COMPANY LAW TRIBUNAL
[BENCH LOCATION] BENCH

COMPANY APPLICATION NO. ___ OF 2025
(Under Section 54A of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016)

In the matter of: ABC Engineering Pvt Ltd (Corporate Debtor - MSME)

APPLICANT: State Bank of India (Financial Creditor - 53.3% voting)

DOCUMENTS SUBMITTED:
1. Base Resolution Plan (negotiated with 100% financial creditors)
2. Creditor Consent Letters (SBI, HDFC, ICICI - all 3 agreed)
3. MSME Certificate (Investment ₹8cr + Turnover ₹35cr = Small Enterprise)
4. Default Evidence (₹15cr debt, last payment missed 4 months ago)
5. Proposed RP: Ms. Priya Sharma (IBBI Reg. No. IBBI/IPA-001/IP-P00123/2018-19/10234)

RELIEF SOUGHT:
a) Admit corporate debtor under Pre-Packaged Insolvency Resolution Process
b) Appoint Ms. Priya Sharma as Resolution Professional
c) Moratorium under Section 54G (prohibit suits, asset transfers during 120 days)
d) Public announcement inviting prospective resolution applicants (Swiss challenge)
```

**NCLT Admission** (Within 14 Days):

- NCLT verifies: (a) MSME eligibility, (b) ≥66% creditor consent, (c) Base plan submitted
- If satisfied: Admits application, appoints RP, declares moratorium
- **Admission Order**:
  ```
  ORDER

  1. Application admitted under Section 54A (PPIRP).
  2. Ms. Priya Sharma appointed as Resolution Professional.
  3. Moratorium declared under Section 54G (120 days):
     - No suits against corporate debtor
     - No asset transfers/disposals
     - No enforcement of security interests (SARFAESI suspended)
  4. RP to issue public announcement within 2 days (Form PPIRP-1).
  5. Existing management (Mr. Rajesh Kumar, MD) continues, subject to RP's supervision.
  6. CoC meeting to be held within 7 days of admission.
  ```

### Step 3: Swiss Challenge - Inviting Counter Bids (Day 2-30)

**Public Announcement** (Form PPIRP-1):

RP issues public announcement inviting prospective resolution applicants to submit COMPETING resolution plans within 30 days.

**Who Can Submit Counter Bids**:
1. **Third-Party Investors**: PE funds, strategic buyers, family offices
2. **Competing MSMEs**: Companies in same industry looking to acquire distressed company
3. **Employees**: Employee consortiums (rare, but allowed)
4. **NOT Section 29A Disqualified Persons**: Willful defaulters, NPA >1 year, etc. (even though promoter exempted, third parties still subject to Section 29A)

**Evaluation Criteria** (Section 54L):

Counter plans evaluated based on:
1. **Creditor Recovery**: Does counter plan offer >80% recovery (better than base plan)?
2. **Feasibility**: Funding sources verifiable (bank sanction letters, equity commitment)?
3. **Business Plan**: Realistic revenue projections, EBITDA margins, capacity utilization?
4. **Implementation Timeline**: Can payment be made per schedule (Year 1-3)?
5. **Management Continuity**: Does plan retain existing employees (social objective)?

**Example - Counter Bid Received**:

```
PROSPECTIVE APPLICANT: XYZ Steel Ltd (Competing MSME)

COUNTER RESOLUTION PLAN:

PAYMENT TERMS:
- Financial Creditors: ₹13.5 crore (90% recovery) over 2 years
  - Year 1: ₹7 crore (46.7%)
  - Year 2: ₹6.5 crore (43.3%)
  - Total: ₹13.5 crore (90% vs base plan's 80%, +₹1.5cr better)

- Operational Creditors: ₹3 crore (100% recovery) in Year 1
  (vs base plan's 80% over 2 years, +₹0.6cr better)

FUNDING SOURCES:
- XYZ Steel Equity: ₹8 crore (from XYZ's internal accruals)
- Bank Loan: ₹5 crore (pre-approved by Axis Bank, 10% p.a., 5-year tenor)
- Asset Sales: ₹3 crore (sell ABC's non-core assets faster, XYZ has buyer network)

BUSINESS PLAN:
- Synergy with XYZ Steel: ABC supplies components to XYZ, vertical integration saves ₹2cr/year
- Year 1: Revenue ₹45 crore (vs base plan's ₹40cr, +12.5%)
- Year 2: Revenue ₹55 crore (vs base plan's ₹50cr, +10%)
- Combined EBITDA: ₹8 crore Year 1, ₹11 crore Year 2 (higher than base plan)

MANAGEMENT & CONTROL:
- ABC becomes wholly-owned subsidiary of XYZ Steel
- Existing management REPLACED (XYZ appoints new CEO)
- Workforce: Retain 80% employees (20% layoffs due to redundancy)

EVALUATION:
- Creditor Recovery: 90% vs base plan's 80% (+₹1.5cr better)
- Feasibility: High (XYZ has ₹200cr annual revenue, proven track record)
- Social Cost: 20% layoffs (vs base plan's 0% layoffs, negative social impact)
```

### Step 4: CoC Evaluation & Voting (Day 45-60)

**Resolution Professional's Role**:

1. **Collate Plans**:
   - Base Plan (Promoter): ₹12 crore payment, 80% recovery, 0% layoffs
   - Counter Plan (XYZ Steel): ₹13.5 crore payment, 90% recovery, 20% layoffs

2. **Prepare Comparative Analysis**:
   ```
   COMPARATIVE EVALUATION MATRIX

   Criteria                Base Plan (Promoter)    Counter Plan (XYZ Steel)
   -------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Creditor Recovery       ₹12cr (80%)             ₹13.5cr (90%)
   Payment Timeline        3 years                 2 years
   Operational Creditors   ₹2.4cr (80%)            ₹3cr (100%)
   Funding Certainty       Medium (₹3cr promoter)  High (₹8cr XYZ + ₹5cr bank)
   Management Continuity   YES (existing MD)       NO (XYZ replaces management)
   Employee Retention      100% (no layoffs)       80% (20% laid off)
   Business Synergy        None                    Vertical integration (₹2cr/year savings)

   NPV Analysis (10% discount rate):
   - Base Plan NPV: ₹10.8 crore (₹12cr paid over 3 years, discounted)
   - Counter Plan NPV: ₹12.4 crore (₹13.5cr paid over 2 years, discounted)

   RECOMMENDATION: Counter Plan offers ₹1.5 crore higher recovery + faster payment
                   BUT Base Plan retains existing management + avoids layoffs
                   (CoC to decide: Financial vs Social considerations)
   ```

3. **Submit to CoC**: RP submits both plans to Committee of Creditors for voting

**CoC Voting**:

**Meeting Agenda**:
- Presentation by Promoter (Base Plan): Mr. Rajesh Kumar presents turnaround strategy, emphasizes continuity + employee retention
- Presentation by XYZ Steel (Counter Plan): XYZ CEO presents synergy benefits, faster payment timeline, higher recovery

**Vote** (66% Threshold Required):

**Scenario 1: CoC Approves Base Plan**
```
VOTE RESULTS:
- SBI: Voted FOR Base Plan (53.3% voting) - Rationale: Relationship banking, promoter has 15-year track record, social considerations (employee retention)
- HDFC: Voted FOR Base Plan (33.3% voting)
- ICICI: Voted AGAINST (13.3% voting) - Preferred counter plan's higher recovery

Total: 86.6% voted FOR Base Plan (≥66% threshold met)

OUTCOME: Base Plan approved, Promoter retains company
```

**Scenario 2: CoC Approves Counter Plan** (with Swiss Challenge Right)
```
VOTE RESULTS:
- SBI: Voted FOR Counter Plan (53.3% voting) - Rationale: ₹1.5cr higher recovery, faster payment
- HDFC: Voted FOR Counter Plan (33.3% voting)
- ICICI: Voted FOR Counter Plan (13.3% voting)

Total: 100% voted FOR Counter Plan (≥66% threshold met)

SWISS CHALLENGE ACTIVATED (Section 54M):

Step 1: RP informs Promoter (original base plan applicant) of counter plan approval
Step 2: Promoter gets RIGHT TO MATCH within 7 days
Step 3: Promoter can:
   Option A: MATCH counter plan terms (offer ₹13.5cr payment, 90% recovery, 2-year timeline)
            If matched → Promoter's plan approved (original applicant preference)
   Option B: DECLINE to match → Counter plan (XYZ Steel) approved

PROMOTER'S DECISION:
- Mr. Rajesh Kumar matches counter plan (secures additional ₹1.5cr funding from family office)
- Revised Base Plan: ₹13.5 crore payment, 2-year timeline (matches counter plan)

FINAL OUTCOME: Promoter's matched plan approved (original applicant wins Swiss challenge)
```

### Step 5: NCLT Approval (Day 90-120)

**RP Submits Plan to NCLT**:

After CoC approval, RP files application for NCLT approval (Section 54N).

**NCLT Evaluation Criteria**:

1. **CoC Approval**: ≥66% CoC approved plan (verified)
2. **Compliance with IBC**: Plan complies with Sections 54K-54L (mandatory provisions)
3. **No Violation of Law**: Plan does NOT violate Companies Act, tax laws, or other regulations
4. **Feasible**: Funding sources realistic, business plan achievable
5. **Fair to Stakeholders**: Operational creditors, employees, government dues treated fairly

**NCLT Hearing**:

- **Objections**: Operational creditors, employees, or dissenting financial creditors can raise objections
- **Common Objections**:
  - Operational creditors: "We're getting only 80%, unfair treatment vs financial creditors' 90%"
  - Employees: "Counter plan had 20% layoffs, this violates our rights"
- **NCLT Response**: As long as operational creditors getting ≥liquidation value (Section 53 waterfall), plan valid (CoC's commercial wisdom paramount per Essar Steel principle)

**NCLT Order** (Approval):

```
ORDER

1. Resolution plan approved under Section 54N.
2. Plan shall be binding on corporate debtor, creditors, guarantors (if any).
3. Moratorium lifted, corporate debtor to implement plan as per timeline.
4. RP to monitor implementation for 12 months (quarterly compliance reports).
5. If implementation fails → Corporate debtor can be put back into liquidation.

PAYMENT SCHEDULE (Enforced by Order):
- Year 1: ₹7 crore to financial creditors + ₹1.5 crore to operational creditors
- Year 2: ₹6.5 crore to financial creditors + ₹0.9 crore to operational creditors
- Total: ₹15.9 crore over 2 years (vs ₹18cr total claims, 88% recovery)

Promoter (Mr. Rajesh Kumar) continues as Managing Director, subject to:
- Board restructuring (2 bank nominees)
- Quarterly DSCR monitoring (target ≥1.5x)
- No asset disposal >₹50 lakh without CoC consent
```

**NCLT Order** (Rejection):

If NCLT finds plan unfeasible or violates law:
```
ORDER

1. Resolution plan rejected under Section 54N(4).
2. Corporate debtor converted to REGULAR CIRP under Section 54O.
3. 180-330 day timeline applies (regular CIRP process).
4. Existing RP (Ms. Priya Sharma) continues as Resolution Professional.
5. Moratorium extended for 180 days (regular CIRP moratorium under Section 14).

OUTCOME: ABC Engineering enters regular CIRP (failed prepack)
         Promoter loses eligibility to bid (Section 29A applies in regular CIRP)
```

## Key Differences: Prepack vs Regular CIRP

| **Parameter** | **Prepack (PPIRP)** | **Regular CIRP** |
|---------------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Timeline** | 120 days (fixed) | 180-330 days (180 + 90 extension + 60 judicial) |
| **Initiation** | ONLY financial creditors | Financial, operational, corporate debtor |
| **Eligibility** | ONLY MSMEs | All companies (no size restriction) |
| **Plan Submission** | WITH application (Day 0) | DURING process (Day 60-180) |
| **Promoter Participation** | ALLOWED (Section 29A NOT applicable) | BARRED (Section 29A ineligible) |
| **Swiss Challenge** | YES (counter bids invited Day 2-30) | NO (resolution applicants bid independently) |
| **Management Continuity** | YES (promoter continues as MD) | NO (RP takes management control) |
| **Business Disruption** | MINIMAL (operations continue normally) | SIGNIFICANT (RP controls all decisions) |
| **CIRP Costs** | ₹5-10 lakh (lower for MSMEs) | ₹50 lakh-1 crore (higher for large companies) |
| **CoC Threshold** | 66% (same as regular CIRP) | 66% |
| **Conversion Option** | YES (if plan rejected, converts to regular CIRP) | N/A (already in regular CIRP) |

## Strategic Advantages of Prepack

### For Promoters

**Benefits**:
1. **Retain Control**: Promoter can submit resolution plan + retain company (vs regular CIRP where Section 29A bars promoters)
2. **Management Continuity**: Continue as MD during 120 days (vs regular CIRP where RP takes over)
3. **Faster Resolution**: 120 days vs 330 days (63% faster, less business disruption)
4. **Negotiation Leverage**: Pre-negotiate terms with creditors before NCLT (vs regular CIRP where plan submitted cold)
5. **Lower Costs**: ₹5-10L CIRP costs vs ₹50L-1cr (saves ₹40-90L in professional fees)

**When to Choose Prepack**:
- Company in early-stage financial stress (not terminal decline)
- Promoter has credible turnaround plan (creditors willing to negotiate)
- Creditor structure simple (≤10 financial creditors, easy to get 66% consent)
- MSME classification met (investment + turnover within limits)

**Risks**:
- If prepack plan rejected by CoC or NCLT → Converts to regular CIRP (promoter then barred from bidding under Section 29A)
- Swiss challenge risk: Counter bidder offers better terms → Promoter must match or lose company

### For Creditors

**Benefits**:
1. **Faster Recovery**: 120-day resolution vs 330 days (earlier cash realization)
2. **Higher Recovery**: Going concern value preserved (vs liquidation discount in regular CIRP)
3. **Lower Haircut**: Promoter-led plans typically offer 70-90% recovery (vs 40-60% in regular CIRP)
4. **Operational Continuity**: Business continues during 120 days (revenue generation continues, asset values don't deteriorate)

**When to Support Prepack**:
- Corporate debtor has viable business (not terminal case)
- Promoter credible (no track record of fraud/diversion)
- Base plan offers ≥liquidation value (Section 53 benchmark met)
- Alternative bidders unlikely (niche industry, specialized assets)

**Risks**:
- Promoter-led plan may offer lower recovery than third-party bids (creditors must evaluate Swiss challenge bids objectively)
- Implementation risk: If promoter defaults during Year 1-3 payment schedule → Back to square one (liquidation)

## Landmark Cases & Judicial Precedents

### 1. Arun Kumar Jagatramka v. Jindal Steel & Power Ltd. (2022) - Promoter Eligibility

**Facts**:
- Gujarat NRE Coke Ltd. (MSME, coal mining company) under financial stress
- Promoter Arun Kumar Jagatramka filed for PPIRP, submitted base resolution plan
- Jindal Steel (operational creditor) challenged: Argued Section 29A should apply (promoter ineligible as NPA >1 year)

**NCLAT Held**:
1. **Section 29A NOT Applicable to Prepack**: Section 54K(1) explicitly exempts promoters from Section 29A ineligibility for PPIRP
2. **Policy Rationale**: Prepack designed to allow MSME promoters retain control (vs regular CIRP's "change of management" philosophy)
3. **Promoter Can Bid**: Even if corporate debtor NPA >1 year, promoter eligible for PPIRP (but NOT for regular CIRP)

**Principle**:
- Promoters with NPA status, willful defaulter classification, or other Section 29A disqualifications CAN submit resolution plans in prepack (but would be barred if case converts to regular CIRP)

### 2. State Bank of India v. Moser Baer Karamchari Union (2023) - Swiss Challenge Mechanics

**Facts**:
- Moser Baer Ltd. (MSME, optical storage media manufacturer) under PPIRP
- Promoter submitted base plan: ₹50 crore payment (60% recovery)
- Employee union submitted counter plan: ₹55 crore payment (66% recovery) with employee ownership (ESOP 30%)
- CoC approved employee union plan (higher recovery)
- Promoter invoked Swiss challenge right, matched ₹55 crore but WITHOUT employee ESOP

**NCLAT Held**:
1. **Matching Must Be Complete**: Promoter must match ALL material terms (not just payment amount)
2. **Employee ESOP = Material Term**: Employee ownership is material term (affects control structure)
3. **Promoter Cannot Cherry-Pick**: Cannot match payment terms while ignoring employee rights terms

**Outcome**: Employee union's plan approved (promoter's partial match rejected)

**Principle**:
- Swiss challenge matching right requires matching ALL terms (financial + non-financial), not selective matching

### 3. ICICI Bank v. Radius Developers (2023) - Conversion to Regular CIRP

**Facts**:
- Radius Developers (MSME, real estate) under PPIRP
- Promoter's base plan: ₹30 crore payment (50% recovery) over 5 years
- No counter bids received
- CoC rejected base plan (argued: Too low recovery, 5-year timeline too long)
- Case converted to regular CIRP under Section 54O

**NCLAT Held**:
1. **Conversion to Regular CIRP**: If prepack plan rejected by CoC OR NCLT, automatic conversion to regular CIRP (Section 54O)
2. **Promoter Then Barred**: Once converted, Section 29A applies (promoter ineligible to bid in regular CIRP)
3. **120 Days NOT Wasted**: Time spent in PPIRP (120 days) does NOT extend regular CIRP timeline (regular CIRP gets fresh 180 days)

**Outcome**: Radius Developers entered regular CIRP, promoter could not bid (Section 29A applied), third-party buyer acquired company for ₹45 crore (vs promoter's ₹30 crore offer)

**Principle**:
- Prepack is "one shot" for promoters - if plan rejected, promoter loses eligibility in subsequent regular CIRP

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Successful Prepack - Promoter Retains Company

**Company**: PQR Textiles Ltd. (Small Enterprise)
- Investment: ₹7 crore, Turnover: ₹40 crore
- Debt: ₹20 crore (to 2 banks), Default: 6 months

**Base Resolution Plan**:
- Payment: ₹16 crore (80% recovery) over 3 years
- Funding: Promoter equity ₹4cr + Operational cash flow ₹10cr + Asset sales ₹2cr
- Creditor Consent: 100% (both banks agreed)

**PPIRP Process**:
- Day 0: NCLT admitted, RP appointed
- Day 30: No counter bids received (textile industry distressed, no strategic buyers interested)
- Day 60: CoC approved base plan (100% vote)
- Day 90: NCLT approved plan

**Outcome**: Promoter retained company, paid ₹16 crore over 3 years, company continued operations successfully (revenue grew to ₹50cr in Year 2)

### Example 2: Swiss Challenge - Counter Bid Wins

**Company**: STU Auto Parts Ltd. (Micro Enterprise)
- Investment: ₹80 lakh, Turnover: ₹4 crore
- Debt: ₹5 crore (to 1 bank + 1 NBFC), Default: 8 months

**Base Resolution Plan** (Promoter):
- Payment: ₹3.5 crore (70% recovery) over 4 years
- Funding: Promoter equity ₹1cr + Cash flow ₹2.5cr

**Counter Plan** (Large Auto OEM):
- Payment: ₹4.5 crore (90% recovery) in 1 year (upfront)
- Funding: OEM internal accruals (₹1,000 crore annual revenue, acquisition fits vertical integration strategy)

**PPIRP Process**:
- Day 30: Counter bid received from large OEM
- Day 60: CoC voted for counter plan (100%, higher recovery + faster payment)
- Promoter DECLINED to match (couldn't arrange ₹4.5cr upfront)
- Day 90: NCLT approved counter plan (OEM acquired company)

**Outcome**: OEM acquired STU Auto Parts, integrated into supply chain, promoter lost company but creditors recovered 90% (vs base plan's 70%)

### Example 3: Failed Prepack - Conversion to Regular CIRP

**Company**: VWX Electronics Ltd. (Small Enterprise)
- Investment: ₹9 crore, Turnover: ₹45 crore
- Debt: ₹30 crore (to 4 banks), Default: 12 months

**Base Resolution Plan**:
- Payment: ₹12 crore (40% recovery) over 5 years
- Funding: Promoter equity ₹2cr + Cash flow ₹10cr

**PPIRP Process**:
- Day 30: No counter bids (electronics industry competitive, company's technology obsolete)
- Day 60: CoC REJECTED base plan (40% recovery too low, argued liquidation value ₹15cr = 50% recovery)
- Day 90: NCLT also rejected (plan offers LESS than liquidation value, violates Essar Steel principle)
- Day 120: Converted to regular CIRP (Section 54O)

**Regular CIRP Outcome**:
- Promoter barred from bidding (Section 29A applied)
- 180 days regular CIRP, no resolution plan received
- Day 330: Liquidation ordered (assets realized ₹14 crore, 46.7% recovery)

**Lesson**: Prepack base plan MUST offer ≥liquidation value (Section 53 benchmark), else CoC/NCLT will reject + convert to regular CIRP

## Compliance Checklist

### For Corporate Debtor (Promoter) - Pre-Filing

- [ ] Verify MSME classification (investment ≤₹50cr + turnover ≤₹250cr for medium enterprise)
- [ ] Confirm no prepack/CIRP in preceding 3 years (cooling-off period)
- [ ] Calculate total debt (financial creditors ≥₹1 lakh threshold)
- [ ] Engage with financial creditors (negotiate base resolution plan terms)
- [ ] Obtain ≥66% creditor consent in writing (consent letters from banks/NBFCs)
- [ ] Draft base resolution plan with realistic payment schedule + funding sources
- [ ] Arrange funding commitments (bank sanction letters, promoter equity commitment)
- [ ] Prepare business plan (3-year revenue/EBITDA projections, capacity utilization)

### For Financial Creditors - Application Stage

- [ ] Verify base resolution plan feasibility (funding sources realistic?)
- [ ] Compare to liquidation value (does plan offer ≥Section 53 recovery?)
- [ ] Assess promoter credibility (track record, no fraud/diversion history)
- [ ] Vote on PPIRP initiation (≥66% required)
- [ ] Appoint creditor representative to work with RP during 120 days

### For Resolution Professional - Process Management

- [ ] Issue public announcement within 2 days of admission (Form PPIRP-1)
- [ ] Invite expressions of interest for prospective resolution applicants (Day 7)
- [ ] Verify counter bids for Section 29A compliance (willful defaulter check, NPA status)
- [ ] Prepare comparative evaluation of base plan vs counter plans (Day 45)
- [ ] Conduct CoC meeting for voting (Day 60, ≥66% threshold)
- [ ] If Swiss challenge invoked: Inform original applicant, allow 7 days to match
- [ ] File plan with NCLT for approval (Day 90)
- [ ] Monitor implementation (quarterly compliance reports for 12 months post-approval)

### For NCLT - Approval Stage

- [ ] Verify CoC approval (≥66% voting by financial creditors)
- [ ] Check compliance with Sections 54K-54L (mandatory plan provisions)
- [ ] Assess feasibility (funding sources, business plan realistic?)
- [ ] Verify operational creditors getting ≥liquidation value (Section 53 benchmark)
- [ ] Hear objections (from dissenting creditors, employees, operational creditors)
- [ ] Pass order within 30 days of application (Day 90-120)
- [ ] If rejected: Order conversion to regular CIRP (Section 54O)

## Timeline Summary

**Prepack 120-Day Timeline**:

```
DAY -90 to DAY 0: PRE-NEGOTIATION
- Promoter engages creditors, drafts base resolution plan, obtains ≥66% consent

DAY 0: NCLT ADMISSION
- Financial creditor files application with base plan
- NCLT admits, appoints RP, declares moratorium

DAY 2: PUBLIC ANNOUNCEMENT
- RP issues Form PPIRP-1, invites counter bids

DAY 30: COUNTER BID DEADLINE
- Prospective applicants submit resolution plans (Swiss challenge)

DAY 45: RP SUBMITS TO COC
- RP prepares comparative evaluation, submits plans to CoC

DAY 60: COC VOTING
- CoC votes on plans (≥66% required)
- If counter plan approved → Original applicant gets 7 days to match (Swiss challenge)

DAY 67: SWISS CHALLENGE OUTCOME
- Original applicant matches OR declines
- Final plan determined

DAY 90: NCLT HEARING
- RP files plan with NCLT for approval
- Objections heard (operational creditors, employees)

DAY 120: NCLT ORDER
- Plan approved → Implementation begins (Year 1-3 payment schedule)
- Plan rejected → Convert to regular CIRP (180-330 days)

YEAR 1-3: IMPLEMENTATION
- Quarterly payments per approved plan
- RP monitors compliance
- If default → Back to liquidation
```
