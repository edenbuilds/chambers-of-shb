## Purpose
This protocol provides comprehensive guidance on statutory and non-statutory leave entitlements for employees in India, covering earned leave, maternity benefits, casual and sick leave, leave encashment, and tax treatment under various labour laws.

---

## Core Framework

### 1. FACTORIES ACT 1948 - EARNED LEAVE (Section 79)

#### 1.1 Applicability
```
APPLIES TO:
✓ All factories (≥10 workers with power, ≥20 without power)
✓ Adult workers (≥18 years)
✓ Workers who worked ≥240 days in a calendar year

DOES NOT APPLY TO:
✗ Shops and commercial establishments (covered by state-specific S&E Acts)
✗ IT/ITES employees (often under Shop & Establishment Acts)
✗ Workers who worked <240 days (no leave earned that year)
```

#### 1.2 Leave Accrual Formula
```
EARNED LEAVE = 1 day for every 20 days worked

ANNUAL ACCRUAL:
- If worked 240 days: 240 ÷ 20 = 12 days earned leave
- If worked 300 days: 300 ÷ 20 = 15 days earned leave
- If worked full year (365 days): 365 ÷ 20 = 18.25 days ≈ 18 days

CALCULATION OF "DAYS WORKED":
Includes:
✓ Actual working days
✓ Rest days/weekly offs
✓ Holidays with wages
✓ Leave with wages (previous earned leave taken)
✓ Layoff period (if wages paid)

Excludes:
✗ Leave without pay
✗ Absence without permission
✗ Strike period (unless declared legal)
```

#### 1.3 Accumulation & Carry Forward
```
MAXIMUM ACCUMULATION:
- General Rule: 30 days (can accumulate up to 30 days total)
- State Variations:
  - Maharashtra: 45 days maximum
  - Karnataka: 45 days maximum
  - Tamil Nadu: 30 days maximum

CARRY FORWARD:
- Unused earned leave carries forward to next year
- Once maximum reached, further accrual lapses
- Example:
  - Year 1: Earned 18 days, took 8 days → Balance 10 days
  - Year 2: Earned 18 days, balance = 28 days
  - Year 3: Earned 18 days, but cap is 30 → Only 2 days added, 16 days lapse
```

#### 1.4 Encashment on Exit
```
RESIGNATION/RETIREMENT:
✓ All accumulated earned leave MUST be encashed
✓ Payment = Leave balance × (Basic Salary ÷ 30 days)
✓ No maximum limit (unlike gratuity ₹20L cap)

EXAMPLE:
Employee resigns with 25 days earned leave balance
Basic Salary: ₹40,000/month
Leave Encashment = 25 × (₹40,000 ÷ 30) = 25 × ₹1,333 = ₹33,325

TERMINATION FOR CAUSE:
✓ Still entitled to leave encashment (Delhi High Court in Bharat Forge)
✓ Different from gratuity (which can be forfeited for moral turpitude)
```

#### 1.5 Encashment During Service
```
DURING EMPLOYMENT:
- Factories Act: Silent (neither permits nor prohibits)
- Company Policy: Many allow encashment of 50% accumulated leave
- Tax Treatment: Taxable as salary (no exemption during service)

EXAMPLE POLICY:
"Employees may encash up to 50% of accumulated earned leave (minimum 10 days balance)
once per calendar year. Encashment paid at Basic + DA rate."

Employee with 20 days balance:
- Can encash: 10 days (50%)
- Remaining balance: 10 days
- Payment: 10 × (Basic + DA) ÷ 30
```

### 2. MATERNITY BENEFIT ACT 1961 (Amended 2017)

#### 2.1 Applicability
```
APPLIES TO:
✓ Establishments with ≥10 employees (any day in preceding 12 months)
✓ Women employed (including work-from-home, contractual)
✓ All sectors: Factories, shops, mines, plantations, establishments

EXCLUDES:
✗ Employees covered by ESI Act (ESIC provides maternity benefit directly)
✗ Domestic workers (not yet covered)
```

#### 2.2 Leave Duration - 26 Weeks
```
FIRST TWO CHILDREN:
- Total: 26 weeks (182 days) paid leave
- Breakdown:
  - Pre-delivery: Up to 8 weeks (can take from 8th month of pregnancy)
  - Post-delivery: Remaining weeks (minimum 18 weeks)

THIRD CHILD ONWARDS:
- Total: 12 weeks (84 days) paid leave
- Pre-delivery: Up to 6 weeks
- Post-delivery: Remaining weeks (minimum 6 weeks)

ADOPTION & COMMISSIONING MOTHER (surrogacy):
- 12 weeks from date of adoption/surrogacy
- Applies to child <3 months old
```

#### 2.3 Eligibility
```
WORKING DAYS REQUIREMENT:
Must have worked ≥80 days in 12 months immediately preceding expected delivery date

CALCULATION:
Employee joined: 1st June 2024
Expected delivery: 1st March 2025
Qualifying period: 1st March 2024 to 28th Feb 2025 (12 months)
Days worked: 220 days (including weekly offs)
Result: ELIGIBLE (220 > 80 days)

PAYMENT ELIGIBILITY:
✓ No minimum service required for LEAVE (can avail even on Day 1)
✓ But PAID leave requires 80 days work in preceding 12 months
```

#### 2.4 Payment Calculation
```
WAGE RATE: Average daily wages of immediately preceding 3 months

EXAMPLE:
Last 3 months salary:
- December 2024: ₹45,000 (22 working days)
- January 2025: ₹45,000 (21 working days)
- February 2025: ₹45,000 (20 working days)

Total wages: ₹1,35,000
Total working days: 63 days
Average daily wage: ₹1,35,000 ÷ 63 = ₹2,143/day

Maternity benefit (26 weeks = 182 days):
182 × ₹2,143 = ₹3,90,026

PAYMENT TIMELINE:
- Advance payment: 50% can be paid 6 weeks before expected delivery
- Balance: Paid after delivery upon submission of birth certificate
```

#### 2.5 Additional Benefits
```
MEDICAL BONUS:
₹3,500 one-time payment (if employer doesn't provide free medical care)

NURSING BREAKS:
- 2 breaks per day (each 15-20 minutes)
- Until child reaches 15 months
- Paid breaks (counted as working time)

WORK-FROM-HOME:
- Option available (if nature of work permits)
- After availing 26 weeks leave
- Until child reaches 1 year

CRÈCHE FACILITY:
- Mandatory for establishments with ≥50 employees
- Mother allowed 4 visits/day (including nursing breaks)
```

#### 2.6 Dismissal Protection
```
PROHIBITED ACTS BY EMPLOYER:
✗ Cannot dismiss during maternity leave or notice period
✗ Cannot change employment conditions to disadvantage
✗ Cannot issue termination notice during pregnancy/maternity leave

PROTECTION PERIOD:
From date of notice of pregnancy → 1 month after maternity leave ends

EXCEPTION: Dismissal allowed for:
- Gross misconduct (theft, fraud, violence)
- Closure of establishment
Must follow due process (domestic inquiry)
```

### 3. CASUAL LEAVE & SICK LEAVE (State-Specific)

#### 3.1 Legal Framework
```
NO CENTRAL LEGISLATION for casual/sick leave

STATE LAWS (Shops & Establishment Acts):
- Maharashtra Shops & Establishments Act 1948
- Karnataka Shops & Establishments Act 1961
- Delhi Shops & Establishments Act 1954
- Tamil Nadu Shops & Establishments Act 1947

Each state prescribes different leave entitlements
```

#### 3.2 Typical Provisions - Casual Leave
```
MAHARASHTRA S&E ACT:
- 7 days casual leave per year (worked ≥240 days)
- Non-accumulating (cannot carry forward)
- Non-encashable (no payment on exit)
- Can be taken for urgent personal reasons

KARNATAKA S&E ACT:
- 12 days casual leave per year
- Pro-rata for service <12 months
- Non-accumulating, non-encashable

DELHI S&E ACT:
- 10 days casual leave per year (worked ≥180 days)
- Non-accumulating
```

#### 3.3 Typical Provisions - Sick Leave
```
MAHARASHTRA:
- 7 days sick leave per year (worked ≥180 days)
- Medical certificate required for >2 consecutive days
- Accumulate up to 14 days (1 year carry forward)
- Encashable on exit (at half the wage rate in some states)

KARNATAKA:
- 12 days sick leave per year
- Accumulation: Up to 1 month
- Medical certificate mandatory for >3 days

TAMIL NADU:
- 1 day sick leave per 20 days worked (18 days/year)
- Medical certificate for >2 days
- Can accumulate up to 12 months
```

#### 3.4 Privilege Leave (Company Policy)
```
COMMON PRIVATE SECTOR PRACTICE:
Instead of separate earned/casual/sick, companies provide "Privilege Leave"

TYPICAL POLICY:
- 21-24 days privilege leave per year (1.75-2 days/month)
- Can be used for any purpose (casual, sick, vacation)
- Accumulation: 30-45 days maximum
- Encashable on exit

EXAMPLE - TCS Leave Policy:
- 21 days privilege leave/year
- 6 days casual leave/year (non-accumulating)
- 12 days sick leave/year (max 90 days accumulation)
- 3 days bereavement leave
- Total: 42 days/year (among most generous)
```

### 4. LEAVE ENCASHMENT - TAX TREATMENT

#### 4.1 Section 10(10AA) - Income Tax Act 1961
```
EXEMPTION ON RETIREMENT/RESIGNATION:

For NON-GOVERNMENT EMPLOYEES:
Exempt amount = LEAST of:
A) Actual leave encashment received
B) 10 months × average salary
C) ₹25,00,000 (₹25 lakh maximum)
D) Cash equivalent of unutilized earned leave (at time of retirement)

DEFINITIONS:
"Average Salary" = Basic + DA + Commission (fixed %) averaged over 10 months before retirement
(Excludes: HRA, Special Allowance, Bonus, Perquisites)

"Unutilized Earned Leave" = Earned leave balance as per service rules
```

#### 4.2 Calculation Examples

**EXAMPLE 1 - Below ₹25L Cap:**
```
Employee retiring after 30 years service
Earned leave balance: 180 days (6 months)
Last drawn Basic: ₹80,000/month
Average salary (last 10 months): ₹78,000/month

Leave encashment received = 180 days × (₹80,000 ÷ 30) = ₹4,80,000

EXEMPTION CALCULATION:
A) Actual received: ₹4,80,000
B) 10 months × ₹78,000 = ₹7,80,000
C) ₹25,00,000 (maximum)
D) Unutilized leave: 180 days

LEAST = ₹4,80,000

Tax Treatment:
- Exempt: ₹4,80,000 (no tax)
- Taxable: NIL
```

**EXAMPLE 2 - Above ₹25L Cap:**
```
Senior executive retiring after 35 years
Earned leave balance: 300 days (10 months)
Last drawn Basic: ₹3,00,000/month
Average salary (last 10 months): ₹2,90,000/month

Leave encashment received = 300 days × (₹3,00,000 ÷ 30) = ₹30,00,000

EXEMPTION CALCULATION:
A) Actual received: ₹30,00,000
B) 10 months × ₹2,90,000 = ₹29,00,000
C) ₹25,00,000 (maximum cap)
D) Unutilized leave: 300 days

LEAST = ₹25,00,000

Tax Treatment:
- Exempt: ₹25,00,000
- Taxable: ₹5,00,000 (at applicable slab rate, likely 30% = ₹1,50,000 tax)
```

**EXAMPLE 3 - Limited by 10 Months:**
```
Employee resigning after 15 years
Earned leave balance: 45 days (company allows high accumulation)
Last drawn Basic: ₹1,50,000/month
Average salary (last 10 months): ₹1,45,000/month

Leave encashment received = 45 days × (₹1,50,000 ÷ 30) = ₹2,25,000

EXEMPTION CALCULATION:
A) Actual received: ₹2,25,000
B) 10 months × ₹1,45,000 = ₹14,50,000
C) ₹25,00,000
D) Unutilized leave: 45 days

LEAST = ₹2,25,000

Tax Treatment:
- Exempt: ₹2,25,000 (no tax)
- Taxable: NIL
```

#### 4.3 Encashment During Service
```
TAXATION:
If leave encashed during employment (while still working):
- NO exemption under Section 10(10AA)
- Fully taxable as "Salary" income
- TDS deducted at applicable slab rate

EXAMPLE:
Employee encashes 10 days leave (mid-career)
Basic: ₹60,000/month
Encashment = 10 × (₹60,000 ÷ 30) = ₹20,000

Tax Treatment:
- Added to salary income for that month
- TDS deducted as per slab (say 30% = ₹6,000)
- No exemption available
```

### 5. PATERNITY LEAVE (Non-Statutory)

#### 5.1 Legal Status
```
NO STATUTORY REQUIREMENT in India for paternity leave

COMPANY POLICY DRIVEN:
- Progressive companies offer 5-15 days paternity leave
- Paid leave (at full wages)
- Can be availed within 3-6 months of child's birth

INTERNATIONAL COMPARISON:
- Sweden: 90 days (90% wages)
- Norway: 10 weeks (100% wages)
- Japan: 52 weeks (58-67% wages)
- India: 0 days (no statutory provision)
```

#### 5.2 Corporate Best Practices
```
LEADING COMPANIES - PATERNITY LEAVE POLICIES:

INFOSYS:
- 5 days paternity leave (paid)
- Can be combined with earned leave for extended time

TCS:
- 15 days paternity leave (among best in India)
- Paid at full wages
- Can avail within 6 months of birth

GOOGLE INDIA:
- 6 weeks (42 days) paternity leave
- Fully paid
- Can be taken flexibly over first year

MICROSOFT INDIA:
- 4 weeks (28 days) paternity leave
- 100% pay
- Applies to birth, adoption, surrogacy
```

#### 5.3 Tax Treatment
```
PATERNITY LEAVE SALARY:
- Paid paternity leave = Regular salary (taxable as usual)
- No special exemption under Income Tax Act
- Not treated as "leave encashment"

UNPAID PATERNITY LEAVE:
If company doesn't offer paid paternity leave:
- Employee can take earned leave
- OR leave without pay (LWP)
- LWP period: Pro-rata salary deduction
```

### 6. SPECIAL LEAVES

#### 6.1 Bereavement Leave
```
TYPICAL POLICY:
- 3-5 days for immediate family death (spouse, parent, child, sibling)
- 1-2 days for extended family (grandparent, in-laws)
- Paid leave
- May require death certificate

NOT STATUTORY (company policy)
```

#### 6.2 Marriage Leave
```
COMMON PROVISION:
- 5-7 days for employee's own marriage
- 2-3 days for sibling's marriage
- Paid leave (usually once in employment tenure)
- Advance notice required (30-60 days)

STATE VARIATIONS:
Some state S&E Acts mandate 5 days marriage leave
```

#### 6.3 Sabbatical Leave
```
SENIOR EMPLOYEES:
- 3-12 months unpaid leave (after 5-10 years service)
- For personal development, travel, study
- Position guaranteed on return
- No salary during sabbatical

TECH COMPANIES:
- Google: Sabbatical after 4 years
- Adobe: 4-6 weeks sabbatical every 5 years (paid)
```

## Landmark Cases

### 1. **Air India v. Nergesh Meerza (1981) - Supreme Court**
**Facts**: Air India terminated air hostesses upon marriage/pregnancy.

**Held**:
- Termination for pregnancy is unconstitutional (Article 14, 15)
- Retirement age discrimination between male/female employees struck down
- Strengthened maternity protection

**Impact**: Basis for strong dismissal protection during pregnancy/maternity leave.

---

### 2. **Bharat Forge v. Uttam Manohar Nakate (2005) - Bombay High Court**
**Facts**: Employee terminated for misconduct. Company refused leave encashment.

**Held**:
- Leave encashment is a statutory right under Factories Act
- Cannot be forfeited even for termination for cause
- Different from gratuity (which can be forfeited for moral turpitude)

**Impact**: Establishes that leave encashment is absolute right, not discretionary.

---

### 3. **Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Female Workers (2000) - Supreme Court**
**Facts**: Casual female workers denied maternity benefit.

**Held**:
- Maternity benefit extends to casual/daily wage workers (not just permanent)
- Any woman employed ≥80 days in preceding year entitled
- Equal protection under Constitution Article 14

**Impact**: Expanded maternity coverage beyond permanent employees.

---

### 4. **Vishaka v. State of Rajasthan (1997) - Supreme Court**
**Facts**: Not directly about leave, but sexual harassment at workplace.

**Held**:
- Employers must provide conducive work environment
- Led to maternity provisions like nursing breaks, crèche facilities

**Impact**: Influenced 2017 amendment adding work-from-home, crèche requirements.

## Compliance Checklist

### For Employers

**Earned Leave (Factories Act):**
- [ ] Maintain leave register (Form 12 under Factories Act)
- [ ] Display leave notice at workplace (Form 12A)
- [ ] Accrual: 1 day per 20 days worked
- [ ] Maximum accumulation: 30-45 days (check state rules)
- [ ] Encashment on exit: All accumulated leave at Basic salary rate
- [ ] Annual leave statement to employees (balance, accrued, availed)

**Maternity Benefit:**
- [ ] Display abstract of Act at workplace
- [ ] Accept notice of pregnancy with expected delivery date
- [ ] Verify 80 days working requirement
- [ ] Calculate average daily wages (preceding 3 months)
- [ ] Pay 50% advance 6 weeks before expected delivery
- [ ] Pay balance after delivery (upon birth certificate submission)
- [ ] Provide nursing breaks (2 per day × 15 minutes)
- [ ] Arrange crèche (if ≥50 employees)
- [ ] Offer work-from-home option (if feasible)
- [ ] Maintain maternity benefit register (Form A)
- [ ] Submit annual return (Form F)

**Casual/Sick Leave:**
- [ ] Check applicable state S&E Act provisions
- [ ] Credit casual leave on 1st January (or pro-rata on joining)
- [ ] Require medical certificate for sick leave >2 days
- [ ] Track accumulation limits (sick leave only)
- [ ] Encash sick leave on exit (if state law allows)

**Leave Encashment Taxation:**
- [ ] Calculate exemption under Section 10(10AA) on retirement
- [ ] Issue Form 16 showing exempt vs taxable components
- [ ] Deduct TDS on taxable portion (if exceeds threshold)
- [ ] Report in Form 24Q (TDS return)

**Record Keeping:**
- [ ] Individual leave ledgers (earned, casual, sick, privilege)
- [ ] Leave application and approval trail
- [ ] Medical certificates (for sick leave)
- [ ] Maternity benefit register
- [ ] Leave encashment calculations (on exit)

### For Employees

**Earned Leave:**
- [ ] Track leave accrual (1 day per 20 worked)
- [ ] Monitor accumulation limit (30-45 days max)
- [ ] Apply for leave in advance (company policy, typically 7-15 days notice)
- [ ] Check leave balance before resignation (for encashment calculation)
- [ ] Verify leave encashment in F&F settlement
- [ ] Ensure exemption applied correctly in Form 16 (on retirement)

**Maternity Leave:**
- [ ] Notify employer in writing (7 weeks before expected delivery)
- [ ] Attach medical certificate (expected delivery date)

- [ ] Request advance payment (6 weeks before delivery)
- [ ] Submit birth certificate after delivery (for balance payment)
- [ ] Claim nursing breaks (2 per day)
- [ ] Request work-from-home option (if needed)

**Casual/Sick Leave:**
- [ ] Apply in advance (casual leave, typically 1 day notice)
- [ ] Obtain medical certificate (sick leave >2 days)

- [ ] Leave encashment on retirement: Exempt up to ₹25 lakh (Section 10(10AA))
- [ ] Leave encashment during service: Fully taxable (no exemption)
- [ ] Plan retirement timing to optimize leave accumulation
- [ ] Verify exemption calculation in Form 16

## Common Pitfalls

### For Employers
1. **Denying earned leave encashment to terminated employees**: Even misconduct terminations are entitled to leave encashment (Bharat Forge case).

2. **Terminating pregnant employees**: Illegal even during probation. Heavy penalties + reinstatement order.

3. **Applying Maternity Benefit Act to ESIC-covered employees**: ESIC provides maternity benefit directly. No double benefit, but employer can top-up.

4. **Not maintaining leave registers**: Violation of Factories Act/S&E Acts. Penalties + prosecution risk.

5. **Incorrect leave encashment tax calculation**: Ensure Section 10(10AA) applied correctly. Include only Basic + DA in "average salary".

### For Employees
1. **Not tracking leave balance**: May lose leave beyond accumulation cap (30-45 days).

2. **Encashing leave during service for tax-free income**: No exemption during service. Fully taxable as salary.

3. **Assuming paternity leave is statutory right**: Not mandatory. Check company policy. Plan ahead if not offered.

4. **Not submitting documents for maternity benefit**: Birth certificate mandatory for balance payment. Medical certificate for advance.

5. **Expecting leave encashment above ₹25 lakh to be tax-free**: Hard cap under Section 10(10AA). Excess is taxable.

## Calculation Examples

### Example 1: Earned Leave Accrual & Encashment
```
SCENARIO:
Employee in factory, worked 280 days in 2024
Basic Salary: ₹35,000/month
Leave balance (1st Jan 2024): 18 days
Leave taken (2024): 10 days
Resigns on 31st Dec 2024

CALCULATION:

1. LEAVE ACCRUED (2024):
   280 days worked ÷ 20 = 14 days earned

2. LEAVE BALANCE (31st Dec 2024):
   Opening: 18 days
   + Accrued: 14 days
   - Taken: 10 days
   = Closing: 22 days

3. LEAVE ENCASHMENT (on resignation):
   22 days × (₹35,000 ÷ 30) = 22 × ₹1,167 = ₹25,674

4. TAX TREATMENT (resignation mid-career):
   Not eligible for Section 10(10AA) exemption (only on retirement)
   Fully taxable as salary
   TDS: ₹25,674 × 30% (assuming 30% slab) = ₹7,702
   Net received: ₹17,972
```

### Example 2: Maternity Benefit Calculation
```
SCENARIO:
Employee joined: 1st March 2024
Expected delivery: 15th January 2025
Last 3 months salary:
  - October 2024: ₹48,000 (22 days)
  - November 2024: ₹48,000 (21 days)
  - December 2024: ₹48,000 (22 days)

ELIGIBILITY CHECK:
Qualifying period: 15th Jan 2024 to 14th Jan 2025
Service: 10.5 months (from 1st March 2024)
Days worked: ~270 days (including weekly offs)
Result: ELIGIBLE (270 > 80 days)

PAYMENT CALCULATION:

1. AVERAGE DAILY WAGE:
   Total wages (3 months): ₹1,44,000
   Total working days: 22 + 21 + 22 = 65 days
   Average: ₹1,44,000 ÷ 65 = ₹2,215/day

2. MATERNITY BENEFIT (26 weeks = 182 days):
   182 × ₹2,215 = ₹4,03,130

3. MEDICAL BONUS:
   ₹3,500 (if no medical facility provided by employer)

TOTAL: ₹4,06,630

PAYMENT SCHEDULE:
- Advance (6 weeks before delivery, i.e., 4th Dec 2024):
  50% × ₹4,03,130 = ₹2,01,565

- Balance (after delivery, with birth certificate):
  ₹2,01,565 + ₹3,500 (medical bonus) = ₹2,05,065
```

### Example 3: Leave Encashment Tax Calculation (Retirement)
```
SCENARIO:
Employee retiring after 32 years (age 60)
Earned leave balance: 240 days (8 months)
Last drawn Basic: ₹90,000/month
Last drawn DA: ₹10,000/month
Last drawn HRA: ₹45,000/month (excluded from average salary)

Average salary calculation (last 10 months):
Basic (avg): ₹88,000
DA (avg): ₹9,500
Total average salary (Basic + DA only): ₹97,500/month

LEAVE ENCASHMENT RECEIVED:
240 days × (₹90,000 + ₹10,000) ÷ 30 = 240 × ₹3,333 = ₹7,99,920

EXEMPTION CALCULATION (Section 10(10AA)):
A) Actual received: ₹7,99,920
B) 10 months × ₹97,500 = ₹9,75,000
C) ₹25,00,000 (maximum)
D) Unutilized leave: 240 days (as per service rules)

LEAST = ₹7,99,920

TAX TREATMENT:
- Exempt under Section 10(10AA): ₹7,99,920
- Taxable: NIL
- Tax saved: ₹2,39,976 (assuming 30% slab)
```

### Example 4: Casual + Sick Leave (Maharashtra S&E Act)
```
SCENARIO:
Employee in retail shop (Mumbai)
Joined: 1st April 2024
Worked: 260 days in 2024
Basic: ₹25,000/month

LEAVE ENTITLEMENT (2024):

1. CASUAL LEAVE:
   Maharashtra S&E Act: 7 days (worked ≥240 days)
   Credit: 1st Jan 2024 (or pro-rata on joining)
   Employee joined April: Pro-rata = 7 × 9/12 = 5.25 ≈ 5 days

2. SICK LEAVE:
   Maharashtra: 7 days (worked ≥180 days)
   Credit: 1st Jan or pro-rata
   Employee joined April: 7 × 9/12 = 5.25 ≈ 5 days

LEAVE TAKEN:
- Casual: 4 days (balance 1 day lapses on 31st Dec - non-accumulating)
- Sick: 2 days (balance 3 days carries forward to 2025)

ON RESIGNATION (31st Dec 2024):
- Casual leave: 1 day balance → LAPSES (non-encashable)
- Sick leave: 3 days balance → Check state rules
  - If encashable: 3 × (₹25,000 ÷ 30) × 0.5 = 3 × ₹833 × 0.5 = ₹1,250 (at half rate)
  - If not encashable: LAPSES
```

## Conclusion

Leave entitlements form a critical component of employee welfare and statutory compliance in India. Key takeaways:

1. **Earned Leave (Factories Act)**: 1 day per 20 worked, accumulate 30-45 days, mandatory encashment on exit.

2. **Maternity Benefit**: 26 weeks paid leave (first 2 children), 80 days working requirement, ₹3,500 medical bonus, nursing breaks, crèche facility.

3. **Casual/Sick Leave**: State-specific (S&E Acts), typically 7-12 days each, casual non-accumulating, sick may accumulate.

4. **Tax Treatment**: Leave encashment on retirement exempt up to ₹25 lakh (Section 10(10AA)), during service fully taxable.

5. **Compliance**: Maintain leave registers, display notices, calculate entitlements correctly, ensure timely payment.

Employers must navigate central laws (Factories Act, Maternity Benefit Act) and state-specific S&E Acts. Employees should track leave balances, understand tax implications, and exercise statutory rights (especially maternity protection).
