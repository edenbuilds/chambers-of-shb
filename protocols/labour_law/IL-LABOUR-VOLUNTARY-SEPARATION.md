# Voluntary Separation - VRS, Resignation & Exit Procedures

## Protocol ID
**IL-LABOUR-VOLUNTARY-SEPARATION** (v1.0)

---

## Purpose
Guide employers and HR professionals through the legal framework, procedures, and best practices for managing voluntary employee separations under Indian labour law, covering Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS), employee resignation, notice period requirements, full & final settlement calculations, exit formalities, tax implications under Section 10(10C) Income Tax Act 1961, and difference between voluntary separation vs retrenchment.

---

## Legal Framework

### 1. Types of Voluntary Separation

| **Type** | **Who Initiates** | **Legal Basis** | **Notice Period** | **Severance Pay** | **Tax Treatment** |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS)** | Employer offers, employee opts in | Section 10(10C) IT Act 1961 | Typically no notice (immediate exit upon acceptance) | VRS compensation (formula-based, up to ₹5L tax-exempt) | Up to ₹5 lakh exempt under Section 10(10C) |
| **Voluntary Resignation** | Employee initiates | Employment Agreement | 30-90 days (per employment agreement) | None (unless company policy offers) | Salary taxable, leave encashment partially exempt |
| **Mutual Separation** | Both parties agree | Contract Act 1872 (mutual consent) | As mutually agreed (can be waived) | Negotiable (lump sum as per negotiation) | Depends on nature (if VRS-like, Section 10(10C); else fully taxable) |

### 2. VRS vs Retrenchment (Key Distinction)

**Retrenchment** (Section 25F Industrial Disputes Act):
- **Employer-Initiated**: Employer terminates employment due to business reasons (downsizing, closure, redundancy)
- **Involuntary**: Employee has NO choice (forced termination)
- **Notice Required**: 1 month written notice to employee + government (Labour Commissioner)
- **Compensation**: 15 days average wages × years of service (statutory minimum)
- **Restrictions**: Last-in-first-out rule (junior employees retrenched before seniors)

**VRS** (Voluntary Retirement):
- **Employer Offers, Employee Chooses**: Employer announces scheme, employee opts in voluntarily
- **Voluntary**: Employee's free choice (no compulsion, can decline without penalty)
- **No Notice Required**: Immediate exit upon acceptance (no Section 25F notice)
- **Compensation**: Higher than retrenchment (typically ₹5-50 lakh depending on scheme)
- **No LIFO**: Employer can design eligibility criteria (e.g., ≥10 years service, age ≥40)

**Landmark Case**: SBI v. Employees Union (SC 2001)
- **Held**: VRS is NOT retrenchment under Section 25F if truly voluntary (no coercion, pressure, forced resignation). Employer not required to give Section 25F notice/compensation if employee opts for VRS voluntarily.

## Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS)

### 1. Legal Framework - Section 10(10C) Income Tax Act 1961

**Tax Exemption** (For Qualifying VRS):
```
Section 10(10C): Any payment received by an employee at the time of his voluntary
retirement or termination of his service in accordance with any scheme of voluntary
retirement shall be exempt from tax to the extent of ₹5,00,000.

Conditions:
(a) Scheme framed in accordance with prescribed guidelines
(b) Employee has completed 10 years of service OR attained age of 40 years
(c) Scheme applies to whole class of employees (not selective/discriminatory)
(d) Vacancy not filled up subsequently (genuine workforce reduction)
```

**Qualifying Scheme Criteria** (Rule 2BA Income Tax Rules):
1. **Applicable to All**: Scheme open to all employees in a category (e.g., "all employees with ≥10 years service" OR "all employees aged ≥40 years")
2. **No Selective Targeting**: Cannot be offered to specific individuals (employer cannot say "only Mr. X eligible")
3. **Genuine Retirement**: Employee actually retires (doesn't join same employer/group company within 2 years post-VRS)
4. **No Replacement**: Employer doesn't fill vacancy created by VRS (within 1 year) - proves genuine downsizing

### 2. VRS Package Calculation

**Typical Formula**:
```
VRS Compensation = LESSER OF:
(a) Last drawn salary × Remaining months of service (till retirement age 60)
OR
(b) Last drawn salary × [15 to 30 months] (employer's policy)

Where "Last drawn salary" = Basic + DA (excluding HRA, allowances, bonuses)
```

**Example - Public Sector VRS (BSNL 2019)**:
```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
- Name: Rajesh Kumar
- Age: 50 years (10 years till retirement at age 60)
- Service: 25 years with BSNL
- Last Drawn Salary: ₹80,000/month (Basic ₹60,000 + DA ₹20,000)

VRS SCHEME TERMS (BSNL 2019):
- Formula: 60 months of last drawn Basic + DA OR remaining service months, whichever LOWER
- Tax exemption: ₹5 lakh (Section 10(10C))
- Additional: Full pension benefits (no reduction for early exit)

CALCULATION:
Remaining service till age 60: 10 years = 120 months
VRS formula: 60 months (as per scheme)
Lesser of 120 months vs 60 months = 60 months

VRS Compensation: ₹80,000 × 60 = ₹48,00,000

TAX TREATMENT:
- Exempt amount: ₹5,00,000 (Section 10(10C))
- Taxable amount: ₹43,00,000 (taxed as salary income in year of receipt)
- Tax liability: ₹43 lakh taxed at 30% slab (if income >₹10L) = ₹12.9 lakh tax

NET TAKE-HOME: ₹48 lakh - ₹12.9 lakh tax = ₹35.1 lakh

ADDITIONAL BENEFITS:
- Full pension: ₹40,000/month (50% of last drawn Basic) starting immediately
- Medical benefits: Continued for life (CGHS/company medical)
- Leave encashment: 300 days accumulated leave × ₹80,000/30 = ₹8 lakh (separate
  payment, tax-exempt up to ₹25 lakh per Section 10(10AA))

TOTAL PACKAGE: ₹48L VRS + ₹8L leave + ₹40,000/month pension
```

**Example - Private Sector VRS (IT Company Restructuring)**:
```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
- Name: Priya Sharma
- Age: 42 years (18 years till retirement at 60)
- Service: 15 years with XYZ Tech Pvt Ltd
- Last Drawn Salary: ₹1,20,000/month (Basic ₹50,000 + HRA ₹25,000 + Special Allowance ₹45,000)

VRS SCHEME TERMS:
- Formula: Basic + DA × 18 months (employer's policy: 1.5 months per year of service, max 18 months)
- Eligibility: Employees with ≥10 years service
- Tax exemption: ₹5 lakh (Section 10(10C))
- No pension (private sector, only gratuity + PF)

CALCULATION:
VRS Compensation: ₹50,000 (Basic only, no HRA/allowances) × 18 months = ₹9,00,000

TAX TREATMENT:
- Exempt: ₹5,00,000
- Taxable: ₹4,00,000 (taxed at applicable slab rate)

ADDITIONAL PAYMENTS:
- Gratuity: (15 × ₹50,000 × 15 years) ÷ 26 = ₹4,32,692 (tax-exempt up to ₹20L)
- EPF: ₹18 lakh (employee's contribution ₹9L + employer's ₹9L, accumulated over 15 years)
  - Employee's contribution: Tax-free
  - Employer's contribution: Tax-free if service ≥5 years (Section 10(11))
- Leave encashment: 45 days leave × ₹50,000/30 = ₹75,000 (tax-exempt up to ₹25L)

TOTAL PACKAGE: ₹9L VRS + ₹4.32L gratuity + ₹18L EPF + ₹75K leave = ₹32.07 lakh
```

### 3. VRS Scheme Design - Best Practices

**Window Period** (Application Deadline):
```
VRS SCHEME ANNOUNCEMENT: 1st January 2025

SCHEME DETAILS:
- Eligibility: All employees with ≥10 years service OR age ≥40 years
- Window Period: 1st January 2025 to 28th February 2025 (60 days)
- VRS Compensation: Basic + DA × 24 months (max)
- Effective Date: 31st March 2025 (all VRS acceptances exit on same date)

EMPLOYEE ACCEPTANCE PROCESS:
Step 1: Employee submits VRS acceptance form (by 28th February)
Step 2: HR verifies eligibility (service ≥10 years OR age ≥40)
Step 3: VRS compensation calculated and communicated to employee (by 15th March)
Step 4: Employee confirms final acceptance (by 20th March)
Step 5: Exit formalities completed (hand over duties, assets returned)
Step 6: Last working day: 31st March 2025
Step 7: VRS payment made: 15th April 2025 (within 15 days of exit)
```

**Irrevocability Clause**:
```
"Once you have accepted this VRS offer and submitted the acceptance form, your
acceptance is IRREVOCABLE. You cannot withdraw your acceptance after the window
period closes (28th February 2025).

Upon acceptance, you agree to:
(a) Resign from employment effective 31st March 2025
(b) Not seek re-employment with the Company or its group companies for 5 years
(c) Not solicit Company's clients, customers, or employees for 12 months
(d) Maintain confidentiality of Company's proprietary information perpetually
(e) Return all Company assets (laptop, phone, documents, access cards) by 31st March

Failure to comply with the above conditions will result in forfeiture of VRS
compensation and legal action for recovery."
```

**Re-employment Prohibition**:
- Typically 2-5 years prohibition (to prove VRS was genuine retirement, not disguised transfer to subsidiary)
- Section 10(10C) exemption may be challenged by Income Tax Department if employee rejoins same employer/group within 2 years

### 2. Notice Period - Buyback & Negotiation

**Notice Buyback** (Employee Pays to Shorten Notice):

**Scenario**: 90-day notice period, employee wants to join new employer in 30 days

**Calculation**:
```
NOTICE SHORTFALL: 90 days - 30 days served = 60 days unserved

BUYBACK AMOUNT:
Option A (Basic Salary Only): ₹50,000/month ÷ 26 days = ₹1,923/day × 60 days = ₹1,15,385
Option B (Gross Salary): ₹1,20,000/month ÷ 26 days = ₹4,615/day × 60 days = ₹2,76,923

Employment Agreement Clause: "Notice pay calculated on Basic salary only"

BUYBACK PAYMENT: ₹1,15,385 (deducted from final settlement)
```

**Negotiation Tactics**:
1. **Offer to Train Replacement**: "I will stay an extra 2 weeks (total 6 weeks) to train my replacement, can you waive the remaining 6 weeks?"
2. **Highlight Pending Projects**: "I will complete Project X before leaving (deliverable worth ₹5L to company), can you release me early?"
3. **New Employer Urgency**: "My new employer has a critical project starting 1st May, can I serve 45 days instead of 90 days?"

**Employer's Considerations** (Whether to Grant Early Release):
- **Replacement Availability**: If successor already identified/trained, early release feasible
- **Project Criticality**: If employee is working on critical project (delivery in 2 months), employer may insist on full notice
- **Goodwill**: Early release maintains goodwill (employee may refer candidates, positive word-of-mouth)

## Full & Final Settlement (F&F)

### 1. Components of F&F Settlement

**Payments DUE TO Employee**:

```
FULL & FINAL SETTLEMENT STATEMENT

Employee: Mr. Rohan Verma (ID: XYZ12345)
Designation: Senior Software Engineer
Last Working Day: 14th June 2025
F&F Payment Date: 30th June 2025

A. SALARY FOR JUNE 2025 (Pro-rated):
   Days worked: 1st June to 14th June = 14 days
   Monthly Gross Salary: ₹1,20,000
   Pro-rated Salary: (₹1,20,000 ÷ 30) × 14 days = ₹56,000

B. LEAVE ENCASHMENT:
   Earned Leave Accumulated: 45 days (2 years service × 21 days/year + 3 days carried forward)
   Encashment Rate: Basic salary (₹50,000/month)
   Leave Encashment: 45 days × (₹50,000 ÷ 26) = ₹86,538
   Tax Exemption: Up to ₹25 lakh exempt (Section 10(10AA))
   Taxable: Nil (₹86,538 < ₹25L)

C. PERFORMANCE BONUS (Pending):
   FY 2024-25 Bonus: ₹1,80,000 (target: 12% of annual fixed salary)
   Pro-rated for 2.5 months (April-June): ₹1,80,000 × (2.5/12) = ₹37,500
   (NOTE: Some companies forfeit bonus if employee resigns before payout date,
   check employment agreement)

D. REIMBURSEMENTS:
   Pending expense claims (March-May travel): ₹12,500
   Internet reimbursement (Apr-Jun): ₹3,000
   Total: ₹15,500

E. GRATUITY:
   Service: 2 years (less than 5 years)
   Gratuity: NOT APPLICABLE (Section 4 Payment of Gratuity Act - eligible only
   if ≥5 years service)

F. PROVIDENT FUND (EPF):
   Employee's EPF balance: ₹2,40,000 (accumulated over 2 years)
   Employer's EPF balance: ₹2,40,000
   Total EPF: ₹4,80,000
   Withdrawal: Employee can withdraw 100% after resignation (submit Form 19, Form 10C)
   Tax Treatment:
   - If withdrawn <5 years of service: Employer's contribution taxable (₹2,40,000 taxed as salary)
   - If withdrawn ≥5 years of service: Fully tax-exempt

GROSS AMOUNT DUE: ₹56,000 + ₹86,538 + ₹37,500 + ₹15,500 = ₹1,95,538

DEDUCTIONS:
1. Income Tax (TDS): ₹8,500 (on June salary + bonus, as per employee's tax slab)
2. Professional Tax: ₹200
3. Notice Pay Shortfall: Nil (full 90 days served)
4. Company Assets Not Returned: Nil (laptop, phone returned on 14th June)
5. Outstanding Loan/Advances: Nil

TOTAL DEDUCTIONS: ₹8,700

NET F&F SETTLEMENT: ₹1,95,538 - ₹8,700 = ₹1,86,838

PAYMENT MODE: NEFT to employee's salary account (ending xxxx1234)
PAYMENT DATE: 30th June 2025

[Signature]
HR Manager

EMPLOYEE ACKNOWLEDGMENT:
I acknowledge receipt of the full & final settlement amount of ₹1,86,838 and
confirm that I have no further claims against XYZ Technologies Pvt Ltd.

Employee Signature: _______________
Date: _______________
```

### 2. Common F&F Disputes & Resolutions

**Dispute 1: Bonus Forfeiture Clause**:

**Employment Agreement Clause**:
```
"Performance bonus is payable in April following the performance year, subject to:
(a) Employee being on the rolls on the payout date (30th April)
(b) No disciplinary proceedings pending
(c) Employee not under notice period on payout date
```

**Scenario**: Employee resigns in February, serves 90-day notice (last working day 31st May)
**Bonus payout date**: 30th April (employee still on rolls, but under notice period)

**Employer's Position**: Bonus forfeited (employee under notice period, Clause (c) applies)
**Employee's Position**: Bonus earned (worked full FY 2024-25, performance rating 4/5, clause (c) is unconscionable)

**Resolution**:
- **Pro-rated Bonus**: Fair approach - pay bonus pro-rated for months worked (April-February = 11 months, so 11/12th of full bonus)
- **Legal Risk**: Courts may strike down forfeiture clauses as "unreasonable restraint" (Section 27 Indian Contract Act) if employee worked full year and earned bonus

**Dispute 2: EPF Withdrawal Delay**:

**Problem**: Employee submits Form 19 (EPF withdrawal) on last working day, but EPFO takes 30-60 days to process

**Timeline**:
- Last working day: 14th June 2025
- Form 19 submitted to company: 14th June
- Company verifies and forwards to EPFO: 21st June (7 days delay)
- EPFO processes withdrawal: 15th August 2025 (60 days from submission)

**Employee's Frustration**: Needs EPF funds (₹4.8 lakh) urgently for new house down payment, cannot wait 60 days

**Resolution**:
- **Online UAN Portal**: Employee can withdraw directly via EPF portal (if UAN activated, Aadhaar linked, bank account seeded) - 3-7 days processing
- **Advance Against EPF**: Some employers offer interest-free advance of ₹1-2L against pending EPF withdrawal (to be repaid when EPF received)

## Tax Implications

### 1. VRS Compensation (Section 10(10C))

**Tax Treatment**:
```
VRS Compensation: ₹25,00,000
Tax-Exempt: ₹5,00,000 (Section 10(10C))
Taxable: ₹20,00,000 (taxed as salary income in year of receipt)

TAX CALCULATION (FY 2024-25):
Taxable VRS: ₹20,00,000
Income Tax Slab (New Regime):
- Up to ₹3 lakh: Nil
- ₹3-7 lakh: 5% × ₹4L = ₹20,000
- ₹7-10 lakh: 10% × ₹3L = ₹30,000
- ₹10-12 lakh: 15% × ₹2L = ₹30,000
- ₹12-15 lakh: 20% × ₹3L = ₹60,000
- ₹15-20 lakh: 30% × ₹5L = ₹1,50,000
Total Tax: ₹2,90,000
Add: 4% Health & Education Cess = ₹11,600
TOTAL TAX LIABILITY: ₹3,01,600

NET VRS (After Tax): ₹25,00,000 - ₹3,01,600 = ₹21,98,400
```

### 2. Leave Encashment (Section 10(10AA))

**Tax Exemption** (For Non-Government Employees):
```
Section 10(10AA): Leave encashment (on retirement/resignation) exempt up to ₹25,00,000
or [salary at time of retirement × (leave balance/30) × 10 months], whichever is LOWER.

EXAMPLE:
Leave Balance: 300 days (10 years accumulated)
Salary: ₹1,00,000/month (Basic ₹50,000 + HRA ₹25,000 + Special ₹25,000)
Leave Encashment: 300 days × (₹50,000/30) = ₹5,00,000 (calculated on Basic only)

Exempt Amount:
Option A: ₹25,00,000 (statutory limit)
Option B: ₹50,000 × (300/30) × 10 months = ₹50,000 × 10 × 10 = ₹50,00,000

Lesser of A and B: ₹25,00,000
Actual Leave Encashment: ₹5,00,000

TAX: Fully exempt (₹5L < ₹25L limit)
```

### 3. Gratuity (Section 10(10))

**Tax Exemption**:
```
Section 10(10): Gratuity received at retirement/resignation exempt up to ₹20,00,000
(for employees covered by Payment of Gratuity Act 1972).

EXAMPLE:
Service: 25 years
Gratuity: (15 × ₹50,000 × 25) ÷ 26 = ₹7,21,154

TAX: Fully exempt (₹7.21L < ₹20L limit)
```

## Compliance Checklist

### For Employers (Processing Voluntary Separations)

- [ ] VRS scheme complies with Section 10(10C) (open to all employees in category, no selective targeting)
- [ ] VRS compensation calculated correctly (formula disclosed, ₹5L tax exemption applied)
- [ ] Resignation accepted in writing (acknowledge resignation letter)
- [ ] Notice period verified (as per employment agreement, 30-90 days standard)
- [ ] F&F settlement calculated (salary, leave, bonus, gratuity, reimbursements)
- [ ] Asset return verified (laptop, phone, access card recovered)
- [ ] Access revoked (email, VPN, source code, CRM by last working day)
- [ ] Exit interview conducted (feedback collected, non-compete/confidentiality reminded)
- [ ] F&F payment made within 30-45 days of last working day
- [ ] Form 16 issued (TDS certificate for tax year, by 15th June following financial year)

### For Employees (Voluntary Separation)

- [ ] Resignation letter submitted (with required notice period)
- [ ] Handover completed (project documentation, knowledge transfer)
- [ ] Assets returned (laptop, phone, books, access card)
- [ ] Clearances obtained (IT, Finance, Admin departments)
- [ ] Exit interview completed (feedback provided)
- [ ] F&F settlement reviewed (verify all components: salary, leave, bonus, reimbursements)
- [ ] EPF withdrawal initiated (Form 19 submitted, UAN portal withdrawal)
- [ ] Form 16 obtained (for tax filing, needed for new employer's tax planning)
- [ ] Experience/Relieving letter obtained (original signed copy for future employment)
- [ ] Non-compete obligations understood (12-18 months restriction, specific industry/geography)

## Landmark Cases

### 1. State Bank of India v. Employees Union (SC 2001)

**Facts**:
- SBI announced VRS in 1998 (downsizing, 50,000 employees opted)
- VRS package: ₹3-10 lakh (depending on service), ₹5L tax-exempt per Section 10(10C)
- Employee union challenged: Argued VRS was "disguised retrenchment" (employees coerced, not voluntary)

**Union's Allegations**:
- Employees pressured to opt for VRS (managers threatened "accept VRS or face forced transfer")
- Employees not given full information (future pension impact not disclosed)
- Selective targeting (underperforming employees targeted for VRS)

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **VRS NOT Retrenchment if Voluntary**: VRS is NOT retrenchment under Section 25F Industrial Disputes Act if genuinely voluntary (no coercion, pressure)
2. **Coercion Vitiates VRS**: If employer pressures employees (threats, adverse consequences if VRS not accepted), VRS becomes involuntary → Treated as retrenchment (Section 25F notice/compensation required)
3. **Burden of Proof**: Employee alleging coercion must prove (employer's managerial advice/encouragement to opt for VRS ≠ coercion)
4. **SBI's VRS Valid**: No evidence of coercion (employees had 60-day window to decide, could decline without penalty, scheme transparent)

**Principle**: Employer can offer VRS, but must ensure voluntary acceptance (no threats, full disclosure, reasonable decision time)
