# EPF & ESIC - Provident Fund & Health Insurance Framework

## Protocol ID
**IL-LABOUR-EPF-ESIC** (v1.0)

---

## Purpose
Guide employers and HR professionals through statutory compliance requirements under the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act 1952 and the Employees' State Insurance Corporation Act 1948, covering applicability thresholds, contribution calculations, wage ceilings, EPF withdrawal procedures, ESIC medical and cash benefits, registration processes, monthly compliance (ECR/challan), UAN/IP number management, and penalties for non-compliance.

---

## Legal Framework

### 1. Dual Social Security System

**India's Social Security Coverage**:

| **Scheme** | **Governing Act** | **Coverage** | **Purpose** | **Contribution** |
|------------|-------------------|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| **EPF (Employees' Provident Fund)** | EPF Act 1952 | Retirement savings, pension | Long-term savings for post-retirement income | 12% employer + 12% employee |
| **ESIC (Employees' State Insurance)** | ESI Act 1948 | Health insurance, sickness/maternity benefits | Short-term cash + medical benefits during illness/injury | 3.25% employer + 0.75% employee |

**Overlap**: Many employees covered by BOTH EPF and ESIC (if establishment has ≥20 employees for EPF AND employee's wage ≤₹21,000/month for ESIC)

## EMPLOYEES' PROVIDENT FUND (EPF)

### 1. Applicability - Section 1(3)

**Mandatory Coverage**:
```
Section 1(3): This Act applies to every establishment which is a factory engaged
in any industry specified in Schedule I and in which twenty or more persons are
employed.

Once applicable, coverage continues EVEN IF employee count falls below 20.
```

**Coverage Criteria**:

| **Criteria** | **Threshold** | **Example** |
|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Employee Count** | ≥20 persons employed | Factory with 25 workers → EPF applies |
| **Industry Type** | Any industry in Schedule I (183 industries listed) | Manufacturing, construction, hotels, IT services (added 2010), cinemas, newspapers |
| **Wage Ceiling** | ₹15,000/month Basic + DA (for contribution calculation) | Employee earning ₹50,000 Basic → Contribution calculated ONLY on ₹15,000 |

**Schedule I Industries** (Major Categories):
1. Manufacturing (textiles, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, electronics)
2. Construction & civil engineering
3. Hotels, restaurants, eating houses
4. Cinemas, theatres, cultural entertainment
5. Newspapers, magazines, periodicals
6. Road motor transport
7. Wholesale/retail trade (if ≥50 employees)
8. IT services, software development (added via amendment)
9. Security services
10. Cleaning services

**Exemptions**:
- Establishments with <20 employees (unless voluntarily covered)
- Establishments granted exemption under Section 17 (if they have their own provident fund scheme with benefits ≥EPF)

### 2. Contribution Structure (Section 6)

**Total Contribution**: 24% of Basic + DA (12% employer + 12% employee)

**Bifurcation**:

**EMPLOYER'S 12% Contribution**:
```
A. EPF (Employees' Provident Fund): 3.67% of Basic + DA
   - Goes to employee's EPF account (retirement savings)

B. EPS (Employees' Pension Scheme): 8.33% of Basic + DA (capped at ₹15,000 Basic)
   - Goes to pension fund (managed by EPFO)
   - Provides monthly pension after age 58 (if ≥10 years membership)

C. EDLI (Employees' Deposit Linked Insurance): 0.5% of Basic + DA (max ₹15,000)
   - Life insurance cover (₹7 lakh max, payable to nominee upon employee's death)

Total Employer: 3.67% + 8.33% + 0.5% = 12.5% (employer pays 12% + 0.5% admin charges)
```

**EMPLOYEE'S 12% Contribution**:
```
100% goes to EPF account (employee's retirement savings)
```

**Calculation Example**:

```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Priya Sharma
Basic Salary: ₹30,000/month
Dearness Allowance (DA): ₹5,000/month
Total Basic + DA: ₹35,000/month

EPF WAGE CEILING: ₹15,000 (statutory ceiling)
Contribution calculated on: ₹15,000 (NOT ₹35,000)

MONTHLY CONTRIBUTIONS:

A. EMPLOYEE'S CONTRIBUTION:
   12% of ₹15,000 = ₹1,800/month
   Deducted from salary, goes to EPF account

B. EMPLOYER'S CONTRIBUTION:
   Total: 12% of ₹15,000 = ₹1,800/month

   Bifurcation:
   - EPF (3.67%): ₹551/month → Employee's EPF account
   - EPS (8.33%): ₹1,250/month → Pension fund
   - Total to EPF account: ₹1,800 (employee) + ₹551 (employer EPF) = ₹2,351/month

C. EDLI (0.5%): ₹75/month → Insurance fund
D. Admin charges (0.5%): ₹75/month → EPFO operational costs

TOTAL EMPLOYER OUTGO: ₹1,800 + ₹75 + ₹75 = ₹1,950/month

EMPLOYEE'S EPF BALANCE GROWTH (Annual):
Monthly addition: ₹2,351
Annual addition: ₹2,351 × 12 = ₹28,212
After 10 years (with 8.25% interest compounded annually): ~₹4.3 lakh
After 30 years: ~₹35 lakh (retirement corpus)
```

**Higher Contribution Option** (Voluntary):
- Employee can opt to contribute 12% on ACTUAL Basic (₹35,000) instead of capped ₹15,000
- Employer MUST match (if employee opts for higher contribution)
- Example: Employee contributes 12% of ₹35,000 = ₹4,200/month
           Employer contributes 12% of ₹35,000 = ₹4,200/month
           Total: ₹8,400/month (vs ₹2,351 under capped contribution)

### 3. EPF Withdrawal - Rules & Procedures

**Withdrawal Scenarios**:

**A. RETIREMENT (Age 58+)**:
```
Condition: Employee reaches age 58 OR retires (whichever earlier)
Withdrawal: 100% of EPF balance (employee's contribution + employer's EPF share
            + accumulated interest)
Tax: FULLY TAX-FREE (if service ≥5 years)
Process: Submit Form 19 (EPF withdrawal) + Form 10D (pension withdrawal)
Timeline: 15-30 days (if UAN, Aadhaar, bank account linked, online claim processed)
```

**B. UNEMPLOYMENT (>2 Months)**:
```
Condition: Resigned/terminated + unemployed for >2 months

Partial Withdrawal (After 1 Month Unemployment):
- Withdraw: Up to 75% of EPF balance
- Tax: Taxable (if service <5 years, employer's contribution taxed as salary)
- Process: Submit Form 19, unemployment declaration

Full Withdrawal (After 2 Months Unemployment):
- Withdraw: 100% of EPF balance
- Tax: Tax-free (if service ≥5 years), else employer's contribution taxable
- Process: Submit Form 19, unemployment certificate (from previous employer or
  self-declaration if <1 year service)
```

**C. TRANSFER TO NEW EMPLOYER**:
```
Condition: Joined new employer (also EPF-covered)
Action: Transfer EPF from previous employer to new employer (via UAN)
Process: AUTOMATIC (if both employers use same UAN, no withdrawal needed)
         New employer updates new employment details in UAN portal

Benefits of Transfer:
- Continuity of service (for pension eligibility, need ≥10 years continuous membership)
- No tax liability (funds remain in EPF, not withdrawn)
- Accumulated corpus continues earning interest
```

**D. ADVANCE FOR SPECIFIC PURPOSES** (Partial Withdrawal):

**Home Loan (Form 31)**:
```
Condition: Employee purchasing/constructing house (own name or joint with spouse)
Withdrawal: Lesser of:
            (a) 36 months Basic + DA, OR
            (b) Actual cost of house, OR
            (c) 90% of EPF balance

Example:
EPF Balance: ₹10 lakh
Basic + DA: ₹30,000/month
Home Purchase Cost: ₹50 lakh

Option (a): 36 × ₹30,000 = ₹10.8 lakh
Option (b): ₹50 lakh
Option (c): 90% of ₹10L = ₹9 lakh

Withdrawal Allowed: ₹9 lakh (LOWEST of three options)

Documents: Home loan sanction letter, sale deed/builder agreement, Form 31
```

**Medical Emergency (Form 31)**:
```
Condition: Hospitalization of self, spouse, children, or parents (serious illness)
Withdrawal: Up to 6 months Basic + DA OR cost of treatment, whichever LOWER

Example:
Medical bill: ₹5 lakh (cancer treatment)
Basic + DA: ₹30,000/month
6 months: ₹1.8 lakh

Withdrawal Allowed: ₹1.8 lakh (lower of ₹5L vs ₹1.8L)

Documents: Medical bills, hospitalization certificate, doctor's prescription
```

**Marriage/Education (Form 31)**:
```
Condition: Marriage of self, children, or siblings / Education of children
Withdrawal: Up to 50% of employee's contribution (NOT employer's share)
Eligibility: After 7 years membership

Example:
EPF Balance: ₹10 lakh (Employee ₹5L + Employer ₹5L)
Withdrawal: 50% of ₹5L = ₹2.5 lakh (only employee's share)

Documents: Marriage invitation, admission letter (college/school)
```

### 4. UAN (Universal Account Number)

**What is UAN**:
- 12-digit unique number (e.g., 101234567890) assigned to EACH employee
- Remains SAME throughout career (even when changing jobs, employer, city)
- Links all EPF accounts (from different employers) under one UAN

**Benefits**:
1. **Single Account**: All EPF contributions (from multiple employers) visible in one UAN portal
2. **Online Withdrawal**: Can withdraw EPF online (if UAN activated, Aadhaar linked, bank account seeded)
3. **Auto Transfer**: When changing jobs, EPF auto-transfers to new employer (no manual intervention)
4. **Transparency**: Check EPF balance, passbook, claim status anytime via UAN portal

**UAN Activation Process**:
```
STEP 1: Employer generates UAN (for new employee, via Unified Portal)

STEP 2: Employee activates UAN
- Visit: https://unifiedportal-mem.epfindia.gov.in/memberinterface/
- Click "Activate UAN"
- Enter: UAN, Mobile Number, Member ID (format: KN/12345/1234567), Aadhaar
- Set password

STEP 3: Link Aadhaar
- Login to UAN portal → Manage → Link Aadhaar
- Enter Aadhaar number → OTP sent to Aadhaar-registered mobile → Verify

STEP 4: Seed Bank Account
- Login → Manage → KYC → Bank
- Enter: Bank name, IFSC, Account number, upload cancelled cheque
- Bank account seeded (for direct EPF credit during withdrawal)

STEP 5: Ready for Online Claims
- Can now submit Form 19 (EPF withdrawal), Form 31 (advance) online
- No need to visit EPFO office, employer's signature not required
```

## EMPLOYEES' STATE INSURANCE (ESIC)

### 1. Applicability - Section 1(4)

**Mandatory Coverage**:
```
Section 1(4): This Act applies to factories or establishments wherein ten or more
persons are employed or were employed on any day of the preceding twelve months.

Note: Some states have higher threshold (≥20 employees in certain states)
```

**Coverage Criteria**:

| **Criteria** | **Threshold** | **Details** |
|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Employee Count** | ≥10 persons (some states ≥20) | Once applicable, continues even if count falls below threshold |
| **Wage Ceiling** | ≤₹21,000/month gross wages | Employees earning >₹21,000/month NOT covered |
| **Industry Type** | Non-seasonal factories/establishments | Manufacturing, shops, hotels, cinemas, medical institutions |

**Wage Ceiling Enforcement**:
```
EXAMPLE:
Employee A: Gross salary ₹18,000/month → COVERED (≤₹21,000)
Employee B: Gross salary ₹25,000/month → NOT COVERED (>₹21,000)

If Employee B's salary increases from ₹20,000 to ₹22,000:
- Coverage continues (once covered, remains covered even if wage exceeds ₹21,000)
- Contributions calculated on ₹21,000 ceiling (not ₹22,000)
```

### 2. Contribution Structure (Section 40)

**Total Contribution**: 4% of gross wages (3.25% employer + 0.75% employee)

**Calculation Example**:
```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Suresh Kumar
Gross Salary: ₹18,000/month
- Basic: ₹10,000
- HRA: ₹5,000
- Conveyance: ₹1,500
- Special Allowance: ₹1,500

ESIC WAGE: ₹18,000 (entire gross, unlike EPF which uses only Basic + DA)

MONTHLY CONTRIBUTIONS:

A. EMPLOYER'S CONTRIBUTION:
   3.25% of ₹18,000 = ₹585/month

B. EMPLOYEE'S CONTRIBUTION:
   0.75% of ₹18,000 = ₹135/month

TOTAL: ₹720/month (₹8,640/year)

EMPLOYEE'S TAKE-HOME:
Gross: ₹18,000
Less: ESIC (0.75%): ₹135
Less: EPF (12% of ₹15,000): ₹1,800
Less: Professional Tax: ₹200
NET: ₹18,000 - ₹135 - ₹1,800 - ₹200 = ₹15,865/month
```

### 3. ESIC Benefits

**A. MEDICAL BENEFIT** (Section 46):
```
Coverage: Free medical treatment for employee + family (spouse, children up to
          age 25, dependent parents)

Facilities:
- ESIC hospitals/dispensaries (OPD + IPD)
- Empaneled private hospitals (if ESIC hospital not available within 8 km radius)
- Medicines, diagnostics, surgeries (all free, no charges)

Procedure:
1. Employee gets ESIC IP (Insured Person) number upon enrollment
2. Family members added to IP card
3. Visit ESIC dispensary/hospital (show IP card)
4. Consultation, medicines, tests FREE

Limitations:
- Limited to ESIC network (cannot claim reimbursement for non-ESIC hospital treatment
  unless emergency + prior approval)
- Quality of ESIC hospitals variable (some well-equipped, others basic)
```

**B. SICKNESS BENEFIT** (Section 48):
```
Condition: Employee falls sick (non-occupational illness), unable to work
Benefit: 70% of average daily wages for up to 91 days in a year
Eligibility: Must have contributed for ≥78 days in preceding 6 months

Calculation Example:
Average Daily Wage: ₹18,000/26 = ₹692/day
Sickness Benefit: 70% of ₹692 = ₹484/day

Employee sick for 15 days (hospitalized for viral fever):
Total Benefit: ₹484 × 15 days = ₹7,260 (paid by ESIC, employer doesn't deduct leave)

Procedure:
1. Employee gets medical certificate from ESIC doctor (certifying sick leave)
2. Submits certificate to employer + ESIC
3. ESIC pays benefit directly to employee's bank account (within 30 days)
```

**C. MATERNITY BENEFIT** (Section 50):
```
Condition: Female employee pregnant, delivery, miscarriage
Benefit: 100% of average daily wages for 26 weeks (amended 2017, earlier 12 weeks)
Eligibility: Must have contributed for ≥70 days in preceding 2 contribution periods

Payment:
- 26 weeks = 182 days
- Daily wage: ₹18,000/26 = ₹692
- Total Maternity Benefit: ₹692 × 182 days = ₹1,25,944

Additional Benefits:
- Free prenatal care (ESIC hospitals provide checkups, sonography, delivery)
- Free postnatal care (6 weeks, mother + newborn)
- Medical bonus: ₹2,500 (if delivery at non-ESIC hospital, reimbursement)

Procedure:
1. Employee submits Form 102 (maternity benefit claim) to employer
2. Attach: Doctor's certificate (expected delivery date), birth certificate (post-delivery)
3. ESIC disburses benefit in 2 installments (first at 8 months pregnancy, second post-delivery)
```

**D. DISABLEMENT BENEFIT** (Section 52-54):

**Temporary Disablement**:
```
Condition: Work-related injury/accident, employee temporarily unable to work
Benefit: 90% of average daily wages for duration of disablement
Example: Factory worker injures hand (machine accident), 3 months recovery
         Daily wage: ₹692, Benefit: 90% × ₹692 = ₹623/day × 90 days = ₹56,070
```

**Permanent Disablement**:
```
Condition: Work-related injury causing permanent disability (loss of limb, blindness,
          permanent incapacity)
Benefit: 90% of average daily wages AS MONTHLY PENSION (for life)
Example: Construction worker falls from height, loses leg (permanent disability)
         Monthly pension: 90% × ₹18,000 = ₹16,200/month (lifelong)

Dependents' Benefit (if employee dies):
If employee dies due to work-related injury, dependents (spouse, children) receive
90% of wages as monthly pension (till spouse remarries, children reach age 25)
```

### 4. ESIC IP Number & Registration

**IP (Insured Person) Number**:
- 17-digit number (e.g., 1234567891234567-0) assigned to each employee
- Linked to employee's Aadhaar, family details
- Used for availing medical benefits, claiming cash benefits

**Registration Process**:
```
EMPLOYER'S ROLE:

STEP 1: Establishment Registration
- Register establishment on ESIC portal (within 15 days of applicability)
- Obtain 17-digit Employer Code

STEP 2: Employee Enrollment
- Add employee details (name, Aadhaar, date of joining, wages) on ESIC portal
- Generate IP number for each employee

STEP 3: IP Card Issuance
- ESIC issues physical IP card (to employee's address, 30-45 days)
- Temporary IP number usable immediately for medical benefits

EMPLOYEE'S ROLE:

STEP 4: Family Declaration
- Employee submits Form 1A (declaration of family members)
- Attach: Spouse's Aadhaar, children's birth certificates, parents' age proof

STEP 5: Aadhaar Seeding
- Link Aadhaar to IP number (via ESIC portal or at ESIC branch)
- Enables direct benefit transfer (DBT) to bank account
```

## Compliance Requirements

### 1. Monthly Compliance - ECR (Electronic Challan cum Return)

**EPF Compliance**:

```
MONTHLY ECR FILING (by 15th of next month):

STEP 1: Prepare wage data
- Employee-wise Basic + DA, EPF/EPS contribution
- Joiners, leavers, UAN details

STEP 2: Upload ECR on Unified Portal
- Login: https://unifiedportal-emp.epfindia.gov.in/epfo/
- Upload: ECR file (Excel format with employee details, contributions)

STEP 3: Generate Challan
- System auto-generates challan (total EPF + EPS + EDLI + admin charges)

STEP 4: Payment
- Pay via NEFT/RTGS to EPFO account (by 15th)
- Payment reference number captured in portal

STEP 5: Verification
- EPFO verifies payment within 2-3 days
- EPF credits to individual employees' accounts (visible in passbook within 7 days)

PENALTY FOR DELAY:
- 5% p.a. damages (Section 7Q) if payment delayed beyond 15th
- 12% p.a. damages if delay >60 days
- Criminal prosecution under Section 14 (imprisonment up to 3 years + fine)
```

**ESIC Compliance**:

```
MONTHLY ESIC RETURN (by 11th of next month):

STEP 1: Generate Monthly Contribution
- Employee-wise gross wages, ESIC contribution (3.25% + 0.75%)

STEP 2: File Return on ESIC Portal
- Login: https://www.esic.in/
- Upload: Monthly contribution details

STEP 3: Generate Challan & Pay
- Pay via online banking (by 15th of next month)

STEP 4: Reconciliation
- ESIC matches payment with return, updates employee accounts

PENALTY FOR DELAY:
- 12% p.a. damages on delayed contribution
- Criminal prosecution (imprisonment up to 2 years + fine ₹10,000 per Section 85)
```

### 2. Annual Compliance

**EPF Annual Return**:
```
Form 3A (Annual Return): Due by 30th April each year
- Total employees covered during year (average, max, min headcount)
- Total contributions paid (employer + employee)
- Any exemptions claimed, disputes pending

Form 6A (Annual Statement): Due by 30th April
- Employee-wise contribution statement for entire year
- Verifies monthly ECRs aggregated correctly
```

**ESIC Annual Return**:
```
Form 5 (Annual Return): Due by 30th April each year
- Total employees covered, total wages paid
- Total contributions paid (employer + employee)
- Medical benefits availed (number of employees treated, cash benefits paid)
```

## Penalties & Prosecution

### 1. EPF Penalties (Section 14)

**Non-Remittance of Contributions**:
```
Section 14: Punishment for non-payment of contributions
If employer:
(a) Deducts EPF from employee's salary BUT doesn't deposit with EPFO, OR
(b) Fails to pay employer's contribution

Penalty:
- Imprisonment: 1-3 years (extendable to 5 years if repeat offender)
- Fine: No limit (at court's discretion, typically ₹10-50 lakh)
- Damages: 12% p.a. on delayed amount (Section 7Q)

Example Case:
ABC Pvt Ltd (100 employees) deducted ₹5 lakh EPF from salaries (12 months)
BUT deposited only ₹2 lakh (₹3 lakh misappropriated)

Criminal Case:
- Employer's MD arrested, charged under Section 14
- Fine: ₹25 lakh + Imprisonment: 2 years (sentenced)
- Civil Recovery: ₹3 lakh principal + 12% p.a. damages (₹36,000) = ₹3.36 lakh
- Total Liability: ₹28.36 lakh + 2 years jail term
```

### 2. ESIC Penalties (Section 85)

**Non-Remittance of Contributions**:
```
Section 85: Penalty for non-payment of contribution
If employer fails to pay ESIC contributions:

Penalty:
- Imprisonment: Up to 2 years
- Fine: Up to ₹10,000
- Damages: 12% p.a. on delayed contribution

Recovery: ESIC can recover as land revenue (attachment of property, bank accounts)
```

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Startup Compliance (First-Time EPF/ESIC)

```
COMPANY: XYZ Tech Solutions Pvt Ltd (IT startup, Bengaluru)
EMPLOYEE COUNT: 25 employees (crossed 20-employee threshold in Month 6)

EPF APPLICABILITY:
Month 6: 25 employees (≥20 threshold) → EPF applicable from Day 1 of Month 7

ESIC APPLICABILITY:
Karnataka threshold: ≥10 employees → ESIC applicable from Day 1 of Month 4
(when employee count reached 10)

COMPLIANCE ACTIONS:

MONTH 7 (EPF Kicks In):
1. Register establishment on EPF Unified Portal (within 30 days)
2. Obtain EPFO Establishment Code (KN/12345/000001)
3. Generate UAN for all 25 employees
4. File first ECR (Month 7 contributions by 15th of Month 8)

MONTH 4 (ESIC Kicks In):
1. Register establishment on ESIC portal (within 15 days)
2. Obtain ESIC Employer Code (12-34-567890-000-0000)
3. Enroll all employees (wage ≤₹21,000) → 15 employees eligible (10 earn >₹21K)
4. Generate IP numbers for 15 employees
5. File first ESIC return (Month 4 contributions by 15th of Month 5)

MONTHLY PAYROLL (Ongoing):
Month 7 onwards: Deduct EPF (12%) + ESIC (0.75% if applicable) from all employees
Month 8, 15th: File ECR, pay EPF contributions (employer + employee)
Month 8, 15th: File ESIC return, pay ESIC contributions

EMPLOYEE COMMUNICATION:
- Share UAN numbers via email (employees activate UAN, link Aadhaar)
- Share IP numbers (employees collect IP cards from ESIC branch)
- Conduct session on EPF withdrawal, ESIC medical benefits (HR orientation)
```

### Example 2: EPF Withdrawal - Unemployment Scenario

```
EMPLOYEE: Priya Sharma (resigned from XYZ Tech, unemployed)
EPF BALANCE: ₹5,00,000 (accumulated over 4 years)
SERVICE: 4 years (less than 5 years, so withdrawal taxable)

WITHDRAWAL OPTIONS:

OPTION A: Full Withdrawal (After 2 Months Unemployment)
- Wait 2 months post-resignation
- Submit Form 19 online (via UAN portal)
- Withdrawal: ₹5,00,000 (100%)
- Tax: Employer's share (₹2,50,000) taxable as salary (added to FY income)
       Assuming 30% tax bracket: Tax = ₹75,000
- Net Receipt: ₹5,00,000 (tax deducted during FY tax filing, not at withdrawal)
- Timeline: 7-10 days (direct credit to bank account)

OPTION B: Partial Withdrawal (After 1 Month)
- Submit Form 19 after 1 month unemployment
- Withdrawal: 75% of ₹5L = ₹3,75,000
- Tax: Proportionate (₹1,87,500 employer's share taxable)
- Remaining Balance: ₹1,25,000 continues in EPF (if rejoins EPF-covered job,
  gets transferred automatically via UAN)

OPTION C: Transfer to New Employer (No Withdrawal)
- Joined new employer (also EPF-covered) in Month 3
- No withdrawal, UAN auto-transfers EPF to new employer
- Benefits: Service continuity (if completes 5 years total, withdrawal tax-free),
           compound interest continues on ₹5L corpus
- BEST OPTION if new employment within 2 months (avoid tax, maximize retirement corpus)
```

## Compliance Checklist

### For Employers (EPF & ESIC)

**EPF Compliance**:
- [ ] Register establishment on Unified Portal (within 30 days of crossing 20 employees)
- [ ] Generate UAN for all employees (within 1 month of joining)
- [ ] File ECR by 15th of every month (wage data + contributions)
- [ ] Pay EPF contributions by 15th (employer + employee share)
- [ ] File Form 3A, 6A by 30th April each year (annual returns)
- [ ] Respond to EPFO notices within 30 days (clarifications, audits)
- [ ] Maintain EPF register (employee-wise contribution records, 3 years)

**ESIC Compliance**:
- [ ] Register establishment on ESIC portal (within 15 days of crossing 10 employees threshold)
- [ ] Enroll eligible employees (wage ≤₹21,000/month) within 10 days of joining
- [ ] Generate IP numbers, issue IP cards (coordinate with ESIC branch)
- [ ] File monthly return by 11th of next month (contribution details)
- [ ] Pay ESIC contributions by 15th of next month
- [ ] File Form 5 by 30th April each year (annual return)
- [ ] Display ESIC notice at workplace (benefits, nearest dispensary details)

### For Employees (EPF & ESIC)

**EPF**:
- [ ] Activate UAN within 1 month of joining (set password, link Aadhaar, seed bank account)
- [ ] Check EPF passbook quarterly (verify contributions credited)
- [ ] Transfer EPF via UAN when changing jobs (no withdrawal needed if new employer EPF-covered)
- [ ] Withdraw EPF after retirement OR unemployment >2 months (Form 19 online)
- [ ] Claim advances for home loan, medical emergency (Form 31 online)
- [ ] Update KYC (Aadhaar, PAN, bank account) to avoid claim rejections

**ESIC**:
- [ ] Collect IP card from ESIC branch (within 2 months of enrollment)
- [ ] Add family members to IP card (Form 1A, submit within 3 months)
- [ ] Avail medical benefits (visit ESIC dispensary, show IP card, free treatment)
- [ ] Claim sickness benefit if hospitalized (submit medical certificate from ESIC doctor)
- [ ] Female employees: Claim maternity benefit (Form 102, 8 months into pregnancy)

## Landmark Cases

### 1. Regional Provident Fund Commissioner v. Shiv Kumar Joshi (SC 2005)

**Facts**:
- Employer deducted ₹10 lakh EPF from 100 employees over 12 months
- Deposited only ₹4 lakh with EPFO (₹6 lakh misappropriated)
- EPFO filed criminal case under Section 14 (non-remittance of contributions)

**Employer's Defense**:
- Company in financial crisis (losses for 3 years)
- Used EPF money to pay salaries (prevented closure, saved 100 jobs)
- Pleaded for leniency (no criminal intent, only business compulsion)

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **EPF is Trust Money**: Employee's PF contribution is held in TRUST by employer (not employer's money)
2. **Misappropriation = Criminal Breach of Trust**: Using EPF for business purposes (even to pay salaries) is criminal offense (Section 406 IPC + Section 14 EPF Act)
3. **No Defense of Financial Crisis**: Financial hardship NOT a valid defense for EPF misappropriation
4. **Conviction Upheld**: Employer's MD sentenced to 2 years imprisonment + ₹10 lakh fine

**Principle**: EPF contributions are sacrosanct, employer CANNOT use for any purpose other than depositing with EPFO
