# IL-LABOUR-FACTORIES-ACT

## Protocol ID
IL-LABOUR-FACTORIES-ACT

## Classification
- **Domain**: Labour Law
- **Sub-domain**: Statutory Compliance
- **Type**: Operational Protocol
- **Complexity**: High
- **Version**: 1.0.0

---

## Purpose
This protocol provides comprehensive guidance on compliance with the Factories Act 1948, covering registration, licensing, health, safety, welfare provisions, working hours, leave entitlements, employment of women and children, inspections, and penalties for violations.

---

## Core Framework

### 1. APPLICABILITY & DEFINITIONS (Section 2)

#### 1.1 What is a Factory
```
FACTORY (Section 2(m)):
Any premises where:
- 10 OR MORE workers are employed (if using power), OR
- 20 OR MORE workers are employed (without power)
AND
- Manufacturing process is carried out

MANUFACTURING PROCESS (Section 2(k)):
Includes:
✓ Making, altering, repairing, ornamenting, finishing any article or substance
✓ Assembling, packing, oiling, washing, cleaning, breaking up, demolishing
✓ Pumping oil, water, sewage
✓ Generating, transforming, transmitting electricity or other power
✓ Printing, bookbinding, lithography

EXCLUDES:
✗ Mines (covered by Mines Act 1952)
✗ Mobile units (belonging to armed forces)
✗ Hotels, restaurants, shops (covered by S&E Acts)
✗ Construction sites (covered by Building & Other Construction Workers Act)
```

#### 1.2 Threshold Calculation
```
COUNTING WORKERS:

EXAMPLE 1 - Power Factory:
ABC Manufacturing uses electric motors (power)
Workers employed:
- Permanent: 8
- Contract: 3 (through contractor, but working in factory)
- Part-time: 1 (counts as 1, not 0.5)
TOTAL: 12 workers

Since >10 workers + power used → FACTORY (Factories Act applies)

EXAMPLE 2 - Non-Power Factory:
XYZ Handicrafts (manual labor, no power)
Workers: 22 (all manual workers)
TOTAL: 22 workers

Since >20 workers (even without power) → FACTORY (Factories Act applies)

EXAMPLE 3 - Below Threshold:
Small Workshop: 9 workers, uses power
NOT a factory (need ≥10 for power factory)
Covered by: Shops & Establishments Act (state-specific)
```

### 2. REGISTRATION & LICENSING (Section 6, 7)

#### 2.1 Registration (Section 7)
```
MANDATORY:
Every factory must be registered with Chief Inspector of Factories (State)

PROCEDURE:

STEP 1: APPLICATION (Form 1)
Submit to Chief Inspector at least 60 days before commencement of production
Details:
- Name and address of factory
- Name of occupier (owner/manager)
- Name of factory manager
- Nature of manufacturing process
- Number of workers (estimated)
- Power installed (HP/KW)
- Working hours, shifts

STEP 2: INSPECTION
Inspector may visit factory (verify infrastructure, safety measures)

STEP 3: REGISTRATION CERTIFICATE
If satisfied: Certificate of Registration issued (permanent, unless revoked)

TIMELINE:
60 days notice required before starting operations

AMENDMENTS:
Any change (increase in workers, new process, change of occupier) → Notify Inspector (Form 3)
```

#### 2.2 License (Section 6)
```
ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENT (for certain processes):
Factories engaged in hazardous processes require LICENSE (beyond registration)

HAZARDOUS PROCESSES (Schedule I):
- Manufacture of explosives, fireworks
- Petroleum refining, LPG handling
- Chemical manufacturing (acids, alkalis, pesticides)
- Asbestos processing
- Radiation-related work
- Any process notified by State Government as hazardous

PROCEDURE:

STEP 1: APPLICATION (Form 7)
Submit to Chief Inspector
Attachments:
- Layout plan (factory premises, machinery locations)
- Safety plan (emergency exits, fire equipment, first aid)
- Environmental clearances (pollution control board)
- Proof of trained personnel (safety officers, first-aiders)

STEP 2: RIGOROUS INSPECTION
Inspector examines:
- Safety equipment (fire extinguishers, sprinklers, alarms)
- Ventilation systems (for toxic fumes)
- Emergency exits, escape routes
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) for workers
- Trained staff (safety officer mandatory)

STEP 3: LICENSE ISSUANCE
Valid for: 1 year (renewable annually)
Conditions: Compliance with safety norms, periodic inspections

RENEWAL:
Apply 30 days before expiry (Form 7A)
Inspector verifies continued compliance
Renewal for 1 year

REVOCATION:
If serious violations: License suspended/cancelled
→ Factory must stop operations (hazardous process)
```

### 3. HEALTH PROVISIONS (Chapter III, Section 11-20)

#### 3.1 Cleanliness (Section 11)
```
MANDATORY MEASURES:

1. DAILY CLEANING:
   - Floors cleaned daily (swept, mopped)
   - Machinery cleaned (oil, grease residue removed)
   - Walls, ceilings whitewashed/painted annually

2. WASTE DISPOSAL:
   - Separate bins for dry, wet, hazardous waste
   - Daily disposal (no accumulation)
   - Drainage system (wastewater, sewage)

3. SPITTOONS:
   - Provided at convenient places (if workers chew tobacco)
   - Cleaned daily

PENALTY (Section 92):
Non-compliance: Fine up to ₹1,00,000 (occupier)
```

#### 3.2 Ventilation & Temperature (Section 13, 14)
```
VENTILATION (Section 13):
- Adequate fresh air circulation (natural or mechanical)
- Enclosed workrooms: Fans, exhaust systems mandatory
- Toxic fumes/dust: Separate exhaust ducts (not to mix with general air)

TEMPERATURE (Section 14):
- Reasonable temperature (not oppressively hot/cold)
- Hot processes: Fans, air coolers provided
- No specific temperature range (unlike OSHA in US), but "comfortable for workers"
- Thermometers installed (to monitor)

LIGHTING (Section 17):
- Sufficient natural or artificial lighting
- Minimum: 200 lux (factory floor), 500 lux (precision work)
- Shadows, glare minimized (lamp shades, diffusers)
```

#### 3.3 Dust & Fume Control (Section 14)
```
DUSTY PROCESSES (textile, cement, flour mills):
- Dust extractors at source (machines)
- Workers provided masks (N95 or equivalent)
- Periodic medical checkup (lung function tests for workers)

TOXIC FUMES (chemical factories, welding):
- Exhaust hoods above workstations
- Ventilation ducts (fumes expelled outside factory, above roof level)
- Gas detectors (for toxic gas leaks: CO, ammonia, chlorine)
- Emergency alarms, evacuation plan

PERIODIC TESTING:
Air quality testing (annually by certified agency)
Dust concentration, toxic gas levels measured
Report submitted to Inspector
```

#### 3.4 Overcrowding (Section 16)
```
MAXIMUM DENSITY:
- Each worker must have at least 14.2 cubic meters (500 cubic feet) space
- Height of room included in calculation

CALCULATION EXAMPLE:
Factory floor area: 1,000 sq meters
Ceiling height: 5 meters
Cubic space: 1,000 × 5 = 5,000 cubic meters

Maximum workers allowed: 5,000 ÷ 14.2 = 352 workers

If 400 workers → OVERCROWDING (violation)

REMEDY:
- Reduce workforce to 352, OR
- Expand factory premises, OR
- Implement shift system (only 176 workers per shift, if 2 shifts)
```

### 4. SAFETY PROVISIONS (Chapter IV, Section 21-41)

#### 4.1 Fencing of Machinery (Section 21)
```
MANDATORY FENCING:
All moving parts of machinery must be securely fenced:
- Flywheels
- Gears, pulleys, belts
- Rotating shafts
- Cutting blades, grinding wheels

FENCING REQUIREMENTS:
- Strong construction (metal grill, polycarbonate sheets)
- Height: Sufficient to prevent accidental contact
- Access panels: Only trained workers can open (lockout/tagout system)

EXCEPTIONS:
- Prime movers (engines) in separate rooms (room itself is fence)
- Machinery inaccessible (height >2.5 meters, no ladder/platform)

PENALTY (Section 92):
Non-compliance: Imprisonment up to 2 years + Fine (if accident occurs due to non-fencing)
```

#### 4.2 Safe Operating Procedures (Section 23-28)
```
EMPLOYMENT ON DANGEROUS MACHINES (Section 23):
- Only trained workers (minimum 6 months training or certified)
- Certification by factory manager (worker competent to operate)

STRIKING GEAR & DEVICES (Section 24):
- Emergency stop buttons at each machine (easily accessible, red color)
- Functional testing (monthly, documented)

CASING OF NEW MACHINERY (Section 25):
- All new machinery must have built-in safety guards (manufacturer's responsibility)
- Factory accepts only if safety-compliant

PROHIBITED WORK (Section 26):
- Women, young persons (14-18 years) NOT allowed to clean, oil moving machinery
- Cleaning only when machine is stopped and locked out

PRESSURE VESSELS (Section 31):
- Boilers, air receivers (compressed air tanks) require:
  - Annual inspection by competent person (boiler inspector, certified engineer)
  - Pressure relief valves (automatic, tested quarterly)
  - Logbook (pressure readings, maintenance)

LIFTING MACHINERY (Section 28):
- Cranes, hoists, lifts:
  - Load capacity clearly marked (e.g., "Max Load 5 Tonnes")
  - Overloading prohibited
  - Annual inspection (certificate of fitness)
  - Operator trained and certified
```

#### 4.3 Fire Safety (Section 38)
```
FIRE PREVENTION:

1. FIRE EXITS:
   - Minimum 2 exits per floor (opposite sides)
   - Width: ≥1.5 meters (for evacuation)
   - Unobstructed, unlocked during working hours
   - Glow-in-dark signage ("EXIT")

2. FIRE EXTINGUISHERS:
   - Type A (water, for wood/paper fires): 1 per 100 sq meters
   - Type BC (CO2, for electrical/oil fires): 1 per electrical panel
   - Annual maintenance contract (AMC) with certified agency
   - Monthly inspection (pressure check, pin intact)

3. FIRE ALARM:
   - Manual call points (break glass alarms) at exits
   - Automatic smoke detectors (in storage areas, electrical rooms)
   - Audible alarm (loud enough to hear over machinery noise, >90 dB)

4. FIRE DRILL:
   - Conducted twice per year (simulate fire evacuation)
   - All workers participate
   - Timed (evacuation must complete in <5 minutes)
   - Documented (date, time, observations, lessons learned)

5. FIRE FIGHTING EQUIPMENT (large factories, >500 workers):
   - Fire hydrant system (water pipes, hoses)
   - Sprinkler system (automatic, heat-activated)
   - Fire brigade contract (if municipal fire station >5 km away)

NO-SMOKING POLICY:
- Prohibited in factory premises (except designated areas, away from flammable materials)
- Signage, enforcement by security
```

### 5. WELFARE PROVISIONS (Chapter V, Section 42-50)

#### 5.1 Drinking Water (Section 42)
```
REQUIREMENTS:
- Wholesome drinking water at convenient places
- Marked "DRINKING WATER" (in local language + English)
- Within 6 meters of every worker (no long walks)
- Coolers in summer (if ambient temperature >35°C)
- Water quality testing: Annually by certified lab

QUANTITY:
Minimum 5 liters per worker per day (for 8-hour shift)

EXAMPLE:
Factory with 200 workers, 2 shifts:
Water required: 200 × 2 shifts × 5 liters = 2,000 liters/day
Install: 4 water coolers (500 liter capacity each) + RO purifiers
```

#### 5.2 Latrines & Urinals (Section 42)
```
MINIMUM REQUIREMENT:

TOILETS:
- 1 toilet per 25 workers (separate for men and women)
- Sanitary, clean (cleaned at least twice per shift)
- Adequate lighting, water supply
- Flush toilets (not dry latrines in urban areas)

URINALS (for men):
- 1 urinal per 50 male workers
- Partition walls (privacy)
- Constant water flow (flushing)

EXAMPLE:
Factory with 100 male workers, 50 female workers

Men:
- Toilets: 100 ÷ 25 = 4 toilets
- Urinals: 100 ÷ 50 = 2 urinals

Women:
- Toilets: 50 ÷ 25 = 2 toilets

TOTAL: 6 toilets (4 male, 2 female) + 2 urinals (male)

CLEANLINESS:
- Cleaned twice per 8-hour shift (morning, afternoon)
- Soap, hand wash provided
- Sanitary napkin vending machines (for women, mandatory if >30 female workers)
```

#### 5.3 Canteen (Section 46)
```
MANDATORY IF:
≥250 workers employed

REQUIREMENTS:

1. LOCATION:
   - Separate building or partitioned area (not in production zone)
   - Adequate seating (chairs, benches for 30% workers at a time, rotating batches)
   - Ventilation, lighting

2. FOOD:
   - Wholesome, nutritious, affordable
   - Subsidized (employer bears ≥50% cost)
   - Example: Meal costs ₹80 to prepare → Worker pays ₹40, employer subsidizes ₹40

3. HYGIENE:
   - Food handlers: Medical fitness certificates (annually)
   - Kitchen: Clean, pest-free, separate storage for dry/wet/cold items
   - Utensils washed with hot water (sanitized)

4. MANAGEMENT:
   - Canteen Committee: 50% employer reps, 50% worker reps (elected)
   - Monthly meetings (menu, pricing, hygiene complaints)
   - Accounts audited (subsidy verification)

5. TIMINGS:
   - Open during all shifts (24/7 if factory runs 3 shifts)
   - Lunch break: 30-45 minutes (as per shift timings)
```

#### 5.4 Crèche (Section 48)
```
MANDATORY IF:
≥30 female workers employed

REQUIREMENTS:

1. LOCATION:
   - Within factory premises or nearby (max 500 meters)
   - Separate room (not in production area)
   - Area: ≥15 sq meters (for up to 10 children), +1 sq meter per additional child

2. FACILITIES:
   - Clean, ventilated, well-lit
   - Drinking water, washing facilities
   - Suitable toys, play materials
   - First aid kit

3. STAFFING:
   - Trained attendants (1 per 10 children)
   - Qualification: Montessori training or child-care certification

4. TIMINGS:
   - Open during all working hours (including overtime)
   - Mothers allowed 2 breaks per day (15 minutes each) for nursing (paid)

5. FREE SERVICE:
   - No charge to workers for crèche use

EXAMPLE:
Factory employs 80 female workers, 25 have children <6 years

Crèche capacity: Design for 30 children (buffer for future)
Area: 15 + (30-10) = 35 sq meters
Attendants: 30 ÷ 10 = 3 attendants
Employer's annual cost: ₹3 lakh (attendant salaries ₹60,000/year × 3, infrastructure, supplies)
```

#### 5.5 First Aid (Section 45)
```
MANDATORY (all factories):
- First aid boxes: 1 per 150 workers
- Contents:
  - Antiseptic solution (Dettol, Savlon)
  - Bandages (various sizes)
  - Cotton wool, gauze
  - Adhesive plasters (Band-Aid)
  - Burn ointment
  - Scissors, tweezers, safety pins
  - Pain relievers (Paracetamol tablets)
  - Snake bite serum (if factory in rural/forested area)

- First-aiders: 1 trained person per shift (St. John Ambulance certification or equivalent)

ADDITIONAL (for factories with >500 workers):
- Ambulance room (separate, equipped with stretcher, examination table)
- Qualified nurse (full-time, nursing degree)
- Medical Officer (part-time visit, 2 hours/day minimum)

ACCIDENTS:
- Serious accidents (fractures, burns, electric shock): Rush to hospital (ambulance or company vehicle)
- First-aider provides immediate care (stop bleeding, CPR if needed)
- Accident register maintained (Form 36)
```

### 6. WORKING HOURS & OVERTIME (Chapter VI, Section 51-65)

#### 6.1 Maximum Working Hours (Section 51, 54)
```
DAILY LIMIT:
- 9 hours per day (for adults)
- Cannot exceed 10 hours including overtime (rare exceptions)

WEEKLY LIMIT:
- 48 hours per week (regular)
- Cannot exceed 60 hours including overtime

INTERVALS (Section 55):
- Minimum 30 minutes rest after 5 hours of continuous work
- Meal break (unpaid, workers can leave workstation)

WEEKLY OFF (Section 52):
- One full day (24 hours) per week
- Usually Sunday (or any fixed day)
- Compensatory off within 30 days if weekly off worked (for emergency production)

SPREAD OVER (Section 56):
- Total span from start to end of shift: Max 10.5 hours (including breaks)
- Example: Shift 8 AM to 6 PM (10 hours) → Work 9 hours, break 1 hour → Compliant
- If shift exceeds 10.5 hours → Violation (even if actual work is 9 hours)
```

#### 6.2 Overtime (Section 59)
```
CALCULATION:
Overtime = Hours worked beyond 9 hours/day OR 48 hours/week

OVERTIME WAGES:
Double the ordinary rate (Factories Act mandates 2x, unlike ID Act which allows 2x)

EXAMPLE:
Worker's monthly salary: ₹26,000 (for 26 working days, 8 hours/day)
Hourly rate: ₹26,000 ÷ (26 days × 8 hours) = ₹125/hour
Overtime rate: ₹125 × 2 = ₹250/hour

In a month, worker does:
- Regular: 208 hours (26 days × 8 hours)
- Overtime: 20 hours (some days worked 9-10 hours)

Overtime pay: 20 hours × ₹250 = ₹5,000
Total salary: ₹26,000 + ₹5,000 = ₹31,000

ANNUAL LIMIT (Section 64):
No worker shall work >60 hours overtime in any quarter (3 months)
If urgent: State government may allow exemption (by notification, temporary)
```

#### 6.3 Night Shifts (Section 66)
```
PROHIBITION FOR WOMEN (Section 66):
- Women cannot work between 7 PM and 6 AM (general rule)
- Exception: State government may allow (with safeguards)
  - Adequate lighting, safe transport home
  - Crèche operational during night shift
  - Written consent of women workers

RECENT AMENDMENT (2019):
- Many states now allow women night shifts (after Supreme Court judgment, gender equality)
- Conditions:
  - Employer provides transport (drop at doorstep, not bus stop)
  - Security guard in factory during night
  - Separate rest room for women
  - CCTV surveillance (factory premises)

CHILDREN (14-18 years):
- Prohibited from night work (7 PM to 6 AM) - NO EXCEPTIONS
- Maximum 4.5 hours/day work allowed
- Not during school hours (if enrolled in school)
```

### 7. LEAVE PROVISIONS (Section 79)

#### 7.1 Earned Leave (Annual Leave with Wages)
```
ELIGIBILITY:
Worker who has worked ≥240 days in a calendar year

ACCRUAL FORMULA:
1 day of earned leave for every 20 days worked

CALCULATION:
Worker worked 280 days in 2024
Earned leave accrued: 280 ÷ 20 = 14 days

ACCUMULATION:
- Maximum: 30 days (some states allow 45 days, check state rules)
- Carry forward to next year (till maximum reached)

ENCASHMENT:
- On resignation, retirement, termination: All accumulated leave must be encashed
- Payment: Leave days × (Basic salary ÷ 30 days)

CANNOT BE DENIED:
- If worker applies for earned leave (min 7 days notice), employer must grant
- Exception: Production emergency (employer can request postponement by 2 weeks max)

EXAMPLE:
Worker has 20 days earned leave balance
Applies for 10 days leave (for family function)
Employer must grant (cannot deny citing work pressure)

Leave encashment on resignation:
Remaining 10 days + accrued leave in final year
If Basic = ₹30,000/month
Encashment = 10 × (₹30,000 ÷ 30) = ₹10,000
```

### 8. INSPECTIONS & PENALTIES

#### 8.1 Factory Inspector Powers (Section 9-10)
```
INSPECTION AUTHORITY:
- State Factory Inspectors (appointed by State Government)
- Chief Inspector of Factories (state-level, heads the department)

POWERS (Section 9):
1. ENTRY AT ANY TIME:
   - Unannounced visits (daytime or night shift, if factory operates 24/7)
   - No prior notice required

2. EXAMINATION:
   - Inspect machinery, safety guards, fire equipment
   - Check registers (wage, attendance, leave, accidents)
   - Measure air quality, noise levels, lighting

3. SAMPLES:
   - Take samples (raw materials, finished products, air/water)
   - Test for hazardous substances (in certified labs)

4. INTERVIEW WORKERS:
   - Privately (without employer present)
   - Ask about wages, working hours, safety concerns

5. SEIZE DOCUMENTS:
   - If violations suspected (false registers, non-payment of wages)
   - Issue receipt, return after investigation

OBSTRUCTION OF INSPECTOR (Section 96):
CRIMINAL OFFENSE:
- Refusing entry → Imprisonment up to 6 months + Fine
- Destroying evidence → Imprisonment up to 2 years + Fine

COMPLIANCE:
Employer must cooperate (provide requested documents, access, answers)
```

#### 8.2 Penalties (Section 92-97)
```
MINOR VIOLATIONS (Section 92):
Examples: Dirty floors, insufficient drinking water, broken toilet flush
Penalty: Fine up to ₹1,00,000 (first offense)
        Fine up to ₹2,00,000 (repeat offense within 2 years)

MAJOR VIOLATIONS (Section 92):
Examples: No fencing on machinery, inadequate fire exits, overworked employees
Penalty: Imprisonment up to 2 years + Fine up to ₹2,00,000
        (If violation causes accident: Imprisonment up to 5 years)

ACCIDENT CAUSING DEATH (Section 88, 89):
If non-compliance with Act causes worker's death:
Penalty: Imprisonment up to 2 years (simple or rigorous) + Fine
        Criminal case (besides civil compensation to family)

FAILURE TO SEND ACCIDENT NOTICE (Section 88):
If serious accident (death, serious bodily injury) occurs:
- Employer must notify Inspector within 24 hours
- Failure to notify: Fine up to ₹50,000

CONTINUING OFFENSE (Section 95):
If violation continues despite notice from Inspector:
- Additional fine: ₹1,000 per day for each day violation continues
- Example: Unfenced machinery (Inspector notices, issues 30-day rectification notice)
  If not rectified in 30 days: Base fine ₹2L + (₹1,000 × days of delay)
```

## Compliance Checklist

### For Factory Owners/Managers

**Registration & Licensing:**
- [ ] Register factory (60 days before start, Form 1)
- [ ] Obtain license (if hazardous process, Form 7, annual renewal)
- [ ] Display registration certificate (at factory entrance)
- [ ] Notify changes (new process, increased workers, change of manager - Form 3)

**Health Provisions:**
- [ ] Daily cleaning (floors, machinery, whitewash walls annually)
- [ ] Adequate ventilation (fans, exhaust for toxic fumes)
- [ ] Temperature control (air coolers if hot processes)
- [ ] Dust/fume extractors (at source machines)
- [ ] Lighting: ≥200 lux factory floor, ≥500 lux precision work
- [ ] Overcrowding: ≥14.2 cubic meters per worker

**Safety Provisions:**
- [ ] Fence all moving machinery (gears, belts, shafts, blades)
- [ ] Emergency stop buttons (tested monthly)
- [ ] Trained operators only (for dangerous machines)
- [ ] Annual inspection (pressure vessels, lifting equipment)
- [ ] Fire safety: 2 exits/floor, extinguishers (1 per 100 sq m), smoke detectors
- [ ] Fire drill: Twice per year, documented

**Welfare Provisions:**
- [ ] Drinking water: Marked points, 5 liters/worker/day, annual water testing
- [ ] Toilets: 1 per 25 workers (separate male/female), cleaned twice per shift
- [ ] Urinals (men): 1 per 50 male workers
- [ ] Canteen (if ≥250 workers): Subsidized food, canteen committee, hygiene
- [ ] Crèche (if ≥30 female workers): 15+ sq meters, trained attendants, free
- [ ] First aid: 1 box per 150 workers, trained first-aider per shift

**Working Hours:**
- [ ] Max 9 hours/day, 48 hours/week (regular)
- [ ] Overtime: Max 60 hours/quarter, paid at 2x rate
- [ ] Breaks: 30 min after 5 hours work
- [ ] Weekly off: 1 day (24 hours) per week, compensatory if worked
- [ ] Women night shifts: Only if state permits + transport + safety measures
- [ ] Children (14-18): No night work, max 4.5 hours/day

**Leave:**
- [ ] Earned leave: 1 day per 20 worked, max 30 days accumulation
- [ ] Grant leave when applied (min 7 days notice)
- [ ] Encash on exit (all accumulated leave)

**Registers & Records (maintain for 5 years):**
- [ ] Muster roll (daily attendance)
- [ ] Wage register
- [ ] Leave register (Form 12)
- [ ] Overtime register
- [ ] Accident register (Form 36)
- [ ] Inspection book (Inspector's visit notes)

**Annual Submissions:**
- [ ] Annual return to Inspector (by 31st January, covering previous year)
- [ ] License renewal application (30 days before expiry, if hazardous)

## Common Pitfalls

### For Employers
1. **Operating without registration**: Criminal offense. Register 60 days before start (Form 1).

2. **Inadequate fire safety**: Major violation. Ensure 2 exits/floor, extinguishers, fire drill twice/year.

3. **Overtime exceeding limits**: 60 hours/quarter maximum. Track monthly, enforce limits (or seek govt exemption for emergencies).

4. **No crèche despite ≥30 female workers**: Mandatory. Cannot claim "no demand from workers" (legal obligation, not preference).

5. **Unfenced machinery**: If accident occurs, criminal liability (imprisonment up to 5 years). Fence ALL moving parts.

### For Workers
1. **Ignoring safety gear**: Refusing to wear PPE (helmets, goggles, gloves) = misconduct. Employer can dismiss after warning.

2. **Not reporting accidents**: Serious accident → Inform supervisor immediately. First aid, hospital, compensation claim.

3. **Accepting less overtime pay**: Factories Act mandates 2x rate. If paid 1.5x or 1x, file complaint with Inspector.

4. **Not utilizing leave**: Earned leave lapses after maximum accumulation (30 days). Use leave periodically (health, rest).

## Conclusion

The Factories Act 1948 is the cornerstone of industrial safety and welfare in India, protecting lakhs of factory workers from hazardous conditions. Key takeaways:

1. **Applicability**: ≥10 workers (with power) or ≥20 workers (without power) + manufacturing process.

2. **Dual Compliance**: Registration (all factories) + License (hazardous processes like chemicals, explosives).

3. **Health**: Cleanliness, ventilation, temperature, dust control, no overcrowding (14.2 cubic meters/worker).

4. **Safety**: Fencing of machinery, fire safety (2 exits, extinguishers, drills), emergency stop buttons, trained operators.

5. **Welfare**: Drinking water, toilets (1 per 25), canteen (≥250 workers), crèche (≥30 women), first aid (1 per 150).

6. **Working Hours**: 48 hours/week, 9 hours/day max, overtime at 2x rate, weekly off, earned leave (1 day per 20 worked).

7. **Inspections**: Unannounced, anytime, wide powers (entry, examination, samples, interviews, seizure).

8. **Penalties**: ₹1-2 lakh fines (minor), imprisonment up to 2-5 years (major, if causes accident/death).

Compliance is not optional—it is legally mandated and strictly enforced. The cost of non-compliance (fines, imprisonment, license revocation, worker injuries, death) far outweighs the cost of safety equipment, welfare facilities, and regulatory compliance. Progressive employers view Factories Act compliance not as burden, but as investment in worker safety, productivity, and long-term business sustainability.
