# Bonus - Statutory Payment Framework

## Protocol ID
**IL-LABOUR-BONUS** (v1.0)

---

## Purpose
Guide employers and HR professionals through the legal framework, calculation methodology, and compliance requirements under the Payment of Bonus Act 1965, covering applicability thresholds (≥20 employees, wage ≤₹21,000/month), minimum bonus (8.33%), maximum bonus (20%), allocable surplus calculation for companies, direct formula for non-corporate establishments, set-off/set-on provisions, payment timeline (within 8 months of accounting year end), and penalties for non-payment.

---

## Legal Framework

### 1. Payment of Bonus Act 1965 - Overview

**Objective** (Preamble):
```
"An Act to provide for the payment of bonus to persons employed in certain
establishments on the basis of profits or on the basis of production or
productivity and for matters connected therewith."

Purpose: Share profits with workers who contributed to profit generation,
         promote industrial harmony, incentivize productivity.
```

**Applicability** (Section 1):
```
This Act applies to:
(a) Every factory employing ≥20 persons (on any day during accounting year)
(b) Every establishment (non-factory) employing ≥20 persons (on any day during
    accounting year)

Establishments: Manufacturing units, plantations, ports, railway companies, motor
               transport undertakings, construction works, shops, hotels, restaurants
```

**Key Definitions**:

1. **Employee** (Section 2(13)):
   ```
   "Employee" means any person (other than apprentice) employed on a salary or
   wage not exceeding ₹21,000 per month in any industry to do any skilled,
   semi-skilled, or unskilled, manual, supervisory, managerial, administrative,
   technical, or clerical work for hire or reward.

   WAGE CEILING: ₹21,000/month (gross salary)
   - If salary >₹21,000/month → NOT covered (no statutory bonus entitlement)
   - Bonus calculation capped at ₹7,000/month (even if actual salary ₹21,000)
   ```

2. **Salary/Wage** (Section 2(21)):
   ```
   "Salary or wage" means all remuneration (excluding bonus) payable in cash to
   an employee, if the terms of employment were fulfilled.

   INCLUDES: Basic salary + Dearness Allowance (DA)
   EXCLUDES: HRA, overtime, commission, any other allowance, gratuity, employer's
            PF contribution, retrenchment compensation
   ```

3. **Accounting Year** (Section 2(1)):
   ```
   "Accounting year" means:
   - For companies: Financial year (April to March, as per Companies Act)
   - For non-companies: Year ending on any day, as employer may adopt
   ```

### 2. Eligibility - Section 8

**Minimum Work Requirement**:
```
Section 8: Every employee shall be entitled to be paid bonus who has worked for
at least thirty working days in an accounting year.

30 Working Days: Actual days worked (excludes leave days, holidays)
- If employee works 29 days → NOT eligible for bonus
- If employee works 30 days → Eligible (even if resigned/terminated after 30 days)

Pro-Rata Bonus: If employee works <full year, bonus calculated proportionately
               (e.g., worked 6 months out of 12 → 50% of annual bonus)
```

## Bonus Calculation - Minimum & Maximum

### 1. Minimum Bonus - Section 10

**8.33% Statutory Minimum**:
```
Section 10: Where in respect of any accounting year, the allocable surplus
exceeds the amount of minimum bonus payable to the employees, the employer shall
be bound to pay to every employee in that year bonus at the minimum rate of
8.33% of the salary or wage earned by the employee during that accounting year
OR ₹100, whichever is higher.

Formula:
Minimum Bonus = 8.33% of (Basic + DA earned during accounting year)
              OR ₹100, whichever HIGHER

Note: ₹100 minimum is nominal (applies only if 8.33% calculation results in <₹100,
      which is rare - would require annual salary <₹1,200)
```

**Calculation Basis**:
```
Bonus calculated on: Basic + DA (NOT entire gross salary)
Calculation Ceiling: ₹7,000/month (even if employee earns ₹21,000/month)

Example 1:
Employee earns ₹15,000/month (Basic ₹10,000 + HRA ₹5,000)
Bonus calculation basis: ₹10,000/month (Basic only, no DA)
Annual basis: ₹10,000 × 12 = ₹1,20,000
Minimum Bonus: 8.33% of ₹1,20,000 = ₹9,996

Example 2:
Employee earns ₹25,000/month (Basic ₹18,000 + HRA ₹7,000)
Eligibility: NOT COVERED (salary ₹25,000 > ₹21,000 ceiling)
Bonus: Nil (unless employer pays ex-gratia bonus voluntarily)

Example 3:
Employee earns ₹21,000/month (Basic ₹15,000 + HRA ₹6,000)
Eligibility: COVERED (salary ≤₹21,000)
Calculation Ceiling: ₹7,000/month (capped, NOT ₹15,000)
Annual basis: ₹7,000 × 12 = ₹84,000
Minimum Bonus: 8.33% of ₹84,000 = ₹6,997 (NOT 8.33% of ₹15,000×12 = ₹14,994)
```

### 2. Maximum Bonus - Section 11

**20% Statutory Maximum**:
```
Section 11: The maximum bonus payable to an employee under this Act in any
accounting year shall not exceed 20% of his salary or wage earned during that
accounting year.

Formula:
Maximum Bonus = 20% of (Basic + DA earned during accounting year, capped at
                ₹7,000/month)

Example:
Employee earns ₹20,000/month (Basic ₹14,000 + HRA ₹6,000)
Calculation Ceiling: ₹7,000/month (capped)
Annual basis: ₹7,000 × 12 = ₹84,000
Maximum Bonus: 20% of ₹84,000 = ₹16,800

If employer wants to pay MORE than ₹16,800:
- Statutory bonus: ₹16,800 (20% of ₹84,000)
- Ex-gratia bonus: ₹10,000 (voluntary, employer's discretion, NOT covered by Act)
- Total: ₹26,800 (₹16,800 statutory + ₹10,000 ex-gratia)
```

### 3. Bonus Range Summary

| **Scenario** | **Minimum (8.33%)** | **Maximum (20%)** | **Actual Bonus** |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **High Profits** | ₹6,997 | ₹16,800 | Depends on allocable surplus (8.33% to 20%) |
| **Low Profits** | ₹6,997 | ₹16,800 | 8.33% minimum (even if profits low/losses) |
| **No Profits (Losses)** | ₹6,997 | ₹16,800 | 8.33% minimum STILL PAYABLE (unless set-off applies) |

**Key Principle**: Employer MUST pay minimum 8.33% bonus regardless of profits/losses
(unless set-off provisions apply - see Section 15-16 below)

## Allocable Surplus Calculation

### 1. For Companies - Section 4

**Formula**:
```
Allocable Surplus = Gross Profits - Deductions

Where:
Gross Profits = Profit before tax (as per P&L statement)

DEDUCTIONS (Section 4):
1. Depreciation as per Income Tax Act (straight-line OR WDV method)
2. Development Allowance/Development Rebate (if claimed for tax purposes)
3. Direct Taxes (income tax, super tax payable for the year)
4. Sums specified under Section 36(1)(ii) of IT Act (donations to approved institutions)
5. Prior period losses (brought forward losses from earlier years, max 4 years)

Allocable Surplus = (Gross Profits - Above Deductions) × 67% (or 60% for certain industries)

67% Formula: Allocable surplus = Available surplus × 67%
            (67% shared with employees as bonus, 33% retained by employer)

60% Formula: For banking companies, applicable percentage is 60% (not 67%)
```

**Detailed Calculation Example**:
```
COMPANY: ABC Manufacturing Ltd (FY 2023-24)

STEP 1: GROSS PROFITS
Profit Before Tax (as per audited P&L): ₹10,00,00,000 (₹10 crore)

STEP 2: DEDUCTIONS (Section 4)

A. Depreciation (as per Income Tax Act):
   - Plant & Machinery: ₹1,50,00,000
   - Buildings: ₹30,00,000
   - Total Depreciation: ₹1,80,00,000

B. Direct Taxes:
   - Income Tax payable: ₹2,50,00,000 (25% of ₹10cr PBT)

C. Prior Period Losses (Brought Forward):
   - FY 2022-23 Loss: ₹50,00,000
   - FY 2021-22 Loss: ₹30,00,000
   - Total Prior Losses: ₹80,00,000 (max 4 years allowed)

AVAILABLE SURPLUS:
= Gross Profits - Depreciation - Direct Tax - Prior Losses
= ₹10,00,00,000 - ₹1,80,00,000 - ₹2,50,00,000 - ₹80,00,000
= ₹4,90,00,000 (₹4.9 crore)

STEP 3: ALLOCABLE SURPLUS (67% of Available Surplus)
= ₹4,90,00,000 × 67%
= ₹3,28,30,000 (₹3.28 crore available for bonus distribution)

STEP 4: BONUS CALCULATION

Total Employees: 500
Salary Bill (Basic + DA, annual): ₹12,00,00,000 (₹12 crore)

Minimum Bonus Liability (8.33%):
= 8.33% of ₹12 crore
= ₹99,96,000 (₹1 crore)

Maximum Bonus Possible (20%):
= 20% of ₹12 crore
= ₹2,40,00,000 (₹2.4 crore)

Allocable Surplus: ₹3,28,30,000
Since ₹3.28cr > ₹2.4cr (max bonus), employer pays 20% (maximum ceiling applies)

ACTUAL BONUS PAYABLE: ₹2,40,00,000 (20% of salary bill)

Per Employee (Average):
₹2,40,00,000 ÷ 500 employees = ₹48,000 per employee (average)

For individual employee earning ₹7,000/month (calculation ceiling):
Annual: ₹7,000 × 12 = ₹84,000
Bonus: 20% × ₹84,000 = ₹16,800 (maximum)
```

### 2. For Non-Corporate Establishments - Section 5

**Direct Formula** (If profits cannot be ascertained):
```
For establishments where allocable surplus cannot be calculated (e.g., partnerships,
proprietorships without proper accounts):

Bonus = 8.33% of (Basic + DA) - Minimum bonus payable regardless of profits

Example:
Partnership firm (no profit calculation available)
Employees: 50
Salary Bill: ₹60,00,000/year

Minimum Bonus: 8.33% of ₹60,00,000 = ₹4,99,800 (must be paid)
```

## Payment Timeline - Section 19

**8-Month Payment Deadline**:
```
Section 19(2): All amounts payable to an employee by way of bonus shall be paid
in cash by the employer within a period of eight months from the close of the
accounting year.

Accounting Year Ends: 31st March 2024
Payment Deadline: 30th November 2024 (within 8 months)

Typical Payment Month: October (for March year-end companies)
- Companies finalize accounts by June (3 months)
- Bonus calculation by August (5 months)
- Board approval in September (6 months)
- Payment in October (7 months, within deadline)
```

**Penalty for Delayed Payment**:
```
Section 19(3): If payment delayed beyond 8 months WITHOUT reasonable cause:

Penalty: Compensation @ 8% p.a. on bonus amount (from 9th month onwards till
         actual payment)

Example:
Bonus: ₹10,000
Due Date: 30th November 2024 (8 months from 31st March)
Actual Payment: 15th January 2025 (45 days delay)

Compensation: ₹10,000 × 8% × (45/365) = ₹98.63
Total Payable: ₹10,000 + ₹98.63 = ₹10,098.63
```

## Set-Off & Set-On Provisions

### 1. Set-Off - Section 15

**Carry Forward of Excess Bonus Paid**:
```
Section 15: If in any accounting year, the employer has paid bonus EXCEEDING
minimum bonus (8.33%), the excess can be SET-OFF against future years' bonus
liability.

Conditions:
(a) Excess must be carried forward in an account called "Bonus Equalization Reserve"
(b) Maximum 4 years carry forward (excess beyond 4 years lapses)
(c) Set-off only against minimum bonus (8.33%), NOT against allocable surplus

Example:
FY 2020-21: Paid 15% bonus (excess over 8.33% minimum = 6.67%)
FY 2021-22: Paid 12% bonus (excess = 3.67%)
FY 2022-23: Paid 10% bonus (excess = 1.67%)
FY 2023-24: Company makes LOSS (no allocable surplus)

Set-Off Calculation (FY 2023-24):
Minimum Bonus Liability: 8.33% of ₹10 crore salary = ₹83.3 lakh

Set-Off Available:
- FY 2020-21 excess: 6.67% of ₹8cr = ₹53.36 lakh (within 4 years)
- FY 2021-22 excess: 3.67% of ₹9cr = ₹33.03 lakh
- FY 2022-23 excess: 1.67% of ₹9.5cr = ₹15.87 lakh
Total Set-Off: ₹1,02.26 lakh

Adjustment:
Minimum Bonus: ₹83.3 lakh
Set-Off Applied: ₹83.3 lakh (capped at minimum liability)
NET BONUS PAYABLE (FY 2023-24): Nil (fully set-off)

Remaining Set-Off: ₹1,02.26L - ₹83.3L = ₹18.96L (carried forward to FY 2024-25)
```

### 2. Set-On - Section 16

**Carry Forward of Unpaid Bonus**:
```
Section 16: If in any accounting year, the employer pays LESS than maximum bonus
(20%) due to insufficient allocable surplus, the shortfall can be SET-ON to
future years if higher allocable surplus is available.

Conditions:
(a) Set-on only if allocable surplus insufficient (not if employer voluntarily
    paid less)
(b) Maximum 4 years carry forward
(c) Set-on added to future year's bonus (if allocable surplus permits)

Example:
FY 2020-21: Allocable surplus ₹50L (salary bill ₹10cr, max bonus 20% = ₹2cr)
           Actual bonus paid: 5% (₹50L, limited by allocable surplus)
           Shortfall: 20% - 5% = 15% (₹1.5cr set-on balance)

FY 2021-22: Allocable surplus ₹80L (salary bill ₹11cr, max bonus 20% = ₹2.2cr)
           Actual bonus: 7.27% (₹80L limited by allocable surplus)
           Shortfall: 20% - 7.27% = 12.73% (₹1.4cr set-on balance)

FY 2022-23: Allocable surplus ₹1.2cr (salary bill ₹12cr, max bonus 20% = ₹2.4cr)
           Actual bonus: 10% (₹1.2cr limited by allocable surplus)
           Shortfall: 20% - 10% = 10% (₹1.2cr set-on balance)

FY 2023-24: PROFITABLE YEAR - Allocable surplus ₹5cr (salary bill ₹13cr)
           Maximum bonus: 20% of ₹13cr = ₹2.6cr

Set-On Adjustment:
Current year max: ₹2.6cr
Add Set-On (from FY 2020-21): ₹1.5cr (proportionate to current salary ₹13cr/₹10cr = ₹1.95cr)
Total Payable: ₹2.6cr + ₹1.95cr = ₹4.55cr

Check Allocable Surplus: ₹5cr (sufficient to pay ₹4.55cr)
BONUS PAYABLE (FY 2023-24): ₹4.55cr (35% of current salary bill)

Remaining Set-On: FY 2021-22 (₹1.4cr), FY 2022-23 (₹1.2cr) carried forward
```

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Standard Bonus Calculation (Profitable Year)

```
COMPANY: XYZ Textiles Ltd (FY 2023-24)

EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Suresh Kumar
Designation: Machine Operator
Monthly Salary: ₹18,000 (Basic ₹12,000 + HRA ₹6,000)
Service: Full year (worked all 12 months)

BONUS CALCULATION:

Step 1: Eligibility Check
Salary: ₹18,000/month ≤ ₹21,000 → ELIGIBLE
Days Worked: 300 days > 30 days → ELIGIBLE

Step 2: Calculation Basis
Basic: ₹12,000/month (HRA excluded)
Calculation Ceiling: ₹7,000/month (capped, even though Basic = ₹12,000)
Annual Basis: ₹7,000 × 12 = ₹84,000

Step 3: Company's Allocable Surplus
Total Salary Bill (all employees, Basic only): ₹20 crore
Allocable Surplus: ₹5 crore
Minimum Bonus (8.33%): ₹1.666 crore
Maximum Bonus (20%): ₹4 crore

Allocable Surplus (₹5cr) > Max Bonus (₹4cr)
→ Company pays 20% (maximum)

Step 4: Suresh's Bonus
20% of ₹84,000 = ₹16,800

PAYMENT:
Accounting Year End: 31st March 2024
Payment Deadline: 30th November 2024
Actual Payment: 25th October 2024 (within deadline)

NET BONUS: ₹16,800 (paid in October 2024 salary)

TAX:
Bonus taxed as "Salary Income" in FY 2024-25
Added to Suresh's gross salary, taxed at applicable slab rate
```

### Example 2: Pro-Rata Bonus (Employee Worked Part Year)

```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Priya Sharma
Designation: Junior Accountant
Monthly Salary: ₹19,000 (Basic ₹13,000 + HRA ₹6,000)
Service: Joined 1st October 2023, worked till 31st March 2024 = 6 months

BONUS CALCULATION:

Step 1: Eligibility Check
Salary: ₹19,000 ≤ ₹21,000 → ELIGIBLE
Days Worked: 150 days (6 months × 25 days/month) > 30 days → ELIGIBLE

Step 2: Pro-Rata Calculation
Calculation Ceiling: ₹7,000/month
Months Worked: 6 months (Oct to March)
Basis: ₹7,000 × 6 = ₹42,000 (NOT ₹84,000 for full year)

Step 3: Bonus (Assuming 15% Bonus Rate)
15% of ₹42,000 = ₹6,300

PAYMENT: ₹6,300 (50% of full-year employee's bonus)

NOTE: If Priya had joined on 15th September (instead of 1st October), she would
      have worked only 29 days in September → NOT eligible (30-day threshold not met)
```

### Example 3: Loss-Making Company (Set-Off Applied)

```
COMPANY: ABC Steel Ltd (FY 2023-24 - Loss Year)

PROFIT & LOSS:
Net Loss: ₹2 crore (after tax)
Allocable Surplus: Nil (negative, cannot calculate)

BONUS OBLIGATION:
Minimum Bonus: 8.33% of ₹15 crore (salary bill) = ₹1.25 crore (MUST PAY)

SET-OFF AVAILABLE (From Previous Years):
FY 2020-21: Paid 18% (excess 9.67% over minimum = ₹1.2cr set-off balance)
FY 2021-22: Paid 14% (excess 5.67% = ₹0.8cr)
Total Set-Off: ₹2 crore

ADJUSTMENT:
Minimum Bonus (FY 2023-24): ₹1.25 crore
Set-Off Applied: ₹1.25 crore
NET BONUS PAYABLE: Nil (fully adjusted against set-off)

Remaining Set-Off: ₹2cr - ₹1.25cr = ₹0.75cr (carried forward to FY 2024-25)

EMPLOYEE COMMUNICATION:
"Dear Employees,
Due to the company's financial losses in FY 2023-24 (₹2 crore net loss), we are
unable to pay cash bonus this year. However, your statutory minimum bonus of
8.33% (₹1.25 crore) has been set-off against excess bonus paid in prior years
(FY 2020-21 and 2021-22 when we paid 18% and 14% respectively).

We remain committed to sharing profits with employees and will resume cash bonus
payments when the company returns to profitability."
```

## Penalties for Non-Payment - Section 28

**Criminal Penalties**:
```
Section 28: If employer fails to pay bonus:

Punishment:
- Imprisonment: Up to 6 months, OR
- Fine: Up to ₹1,000, OR
- Both

Continuing Offense: If bonus remains unpaid, additional fine of ₹100/day for
                   each day of default (capped at total bonus amount)

Practical Reality: Courts rarely impose imprisonment, prefer monetary penalties
                  (₹10,000-₹50,000 fine typical for first-time offenders)
```

**Civil Recovery**:
```
Section 21: Employee can file claim with Labour Court/Inspector for recovery of
           unpaid bonus.

Process:
1. Employee files application (within 1 year of bonus becoming due)
2. Labour Inspector investigates (inspects company accounts, wage registers)
3. If non-payment proven, Inspector issues recovery certificate
4. Recovery as arrears of land revenue (property attachment, bank account seizure)

Compensation: Employer liable to pay unpaid bonus + 8% p.a. interest (from due
             date till payment date)
```

## Compliance Checklist

### For Employers

- [ ] Verify applicability (≥20 employees on any day during accounting year)
- [ ] Identify eligible employees (wage ≤₹21,000/month, worked ≥30 days)
- [ ] Calculate allocable surplus (Section 4 formula for companies, direct 8.33% for others)
- [ ] Determine bonus percentage (minimum 8.33%, maximum 20%, based on allocable surplus)
- [ ] Apply calculation ceiling (₹7,000/month max, even if employee earns ₹21,000)
- [ ] Consider set-off/set-on from previous years (maintain Bonus Equalization Reserve)
- [ ] Pay within 8 months of accounting year end (by 30th Nov for March year-end)
- [ ] Issue bonus payment slip (showing calculation: Basic×months×bonus%, amount)
- [ ] Maintain bonus register (employee-wise payments, 3 years)
- [ ] Display annual statement (total employees, bonus paid, at workplace notice board)

### For Employees

- [ ] Check eligibility (salary ≤₹21,000/month, worked ≥30 days in year)
- [ ] Verify bonus calculation (8.33% to 20% of Basic + DA, capped at ₹7,000/month)
- [ ] Ensure payment within 8 months (by 30th Nov for March year-end companies)
- [ ] If underpaid/not paid, raise grievance with employer first (written complaint)
- [ ] If employer refuses, file claim with Labour Inspector (within 1 year of due date)
- [ ] Maintain evidence (salary slips, attendance records, appointment letter)
- [ ] Join union for collective bargaining (if employer systematically underpays bonus)

## Landmark Cases

### 1. Muir Mills Co. Ltd. v. Suti Mills Mazdoor Union (SC 1955)

**Facts**:
- Muir Mills (textile company) refused to pay bonus for FY 1952-53 (company made losses)
- Workers' union demanded minimum bonus (8.33%) despite losses
- Company argued: Bonus is profit-sharing (no profit = no bonus)

**Union's Argument**:
- Bonus is deferred wage (not charity), workers entitled regardless of profits

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **Bonus as Deferred Wage**: Bonus has evolved from ex-gratia payment (employer's discretion) to DEFERRED WAGE (worker's entitlement)
2. **Profit-Sharing Principle**: While bonus linked to profits, workers have FIRST CHARGE on profits (before shareholders get dividends)
3. **Minimum Bonus During Losses**: Even in loss years, employer MUST pay minimum bonus (unless set-off available from prior years)

**Principle**: Payment of Bonus Act codified this judgment - minimum 8.33% bonus mandatory regardless of profits (subject to set-off provisions)

### 2. Associated Cement Companies Ltd. v. Workmen (SC 1960)

**Facts**:
- ACC (cement manufacturer) calculated bonus on Basic salary only
- Workers demanded bonus on Basic + DA + HRA
- Dispute: Which salary components should be included in bonus calculation?

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **Basic + DA Only**: Bonus calculated on Basic + Dearness Allowance (DA represents cost of living adjustment, part of wage)
2. **HRA Excluded**: House Rent Allowance is reimbursement (not wage), excluded from bonus calculation
3. **Allowances Excluded**: All other allowances (conveyance, special allowance) excluded

**Principle**: Payment of Bonus Act Section 2(21) codified this - "Salary or wage" = Basic + DA (excludes all allowances, overtime, HRA)
