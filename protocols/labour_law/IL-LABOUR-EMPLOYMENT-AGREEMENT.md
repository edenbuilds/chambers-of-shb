# Employment Agreement - Comprehensive Framework & Restrictive Covenants

## Protocol ID
**IL-LABOUR-EMPLOYMENT-AGREEMENT** (v1.0)

---

## Purpose
Guide employers and HR professionals through the legal requirements, essential clauses, and best practices for drafting enforceable employment agreements under Indian labour law, covering fixed-term vs permanent employment, probation terms, restrictive covenants (non-compete, non-solicitation, confidentiality), intellectual property assignment, termination provisions, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

---

## Legal Framework

### 1. Employment Agreement vs Offer Letter

**Key Distinctions**:

| **Aspect** | **Offer Letter** | **Employment Agreement** |
|------------|------------------|--------------------------|
| **Timing** | Pre-joining (issued before joining date) | Post-joining (executed on Day 1) |
| **Purpose** | Propose employment terms, await acceptance | Comprehensive contract governing employment relationship |
| **Length** | 2-3 pages (basic terms) | 10-20 pages (detailed T&Cs, restrictive covenants) |
| **Legal Binding** | Binding upon acceptance (contract formed) | Binding from date of execution (employee signs on joining) |
| **Content** | CTC, designation, joining date, probation | All employment terms, IP assignment, confidentiality, non-compete, grievance procedures, standing orders |

**Execution Process**:
```
Offer Letter Issued → Candidate Accepts → Background Verification → Joining Date
→ Employee Signs Employment Agreement (Day 1) → Probation Begins → Confirmation
```

### 2. Legal Framework

**Applicable Laws**:
1. **Indian Contract Act 1872**: Employment agreement is a contract (offer, acceptance, consideration, lawful object)
2. **Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946**: Employers with ≥100 workers must have certified standing orders (employment terms standardized)
3. **Shops & Establishments Act** (State-specific): Governs employment terms for commercial establishments (working hours, leave, termination)
4. **Industrial Disputes Act 1947**: Governs termination, layoff, retrenchment (Section 25F - notice, compensation)
5. **Factories Act 1948**: For manufacturing establishments (working hours, overtime, leave)

**Standing Orders** (For Industrial Establishments):
- Section 3: Employers with ≥100 workmen must submit draft standing orders to Certifying Officer
- Model Standing Orders (1946): Standard employment conditions (appointment, termination, misconduct, disciplinary action)
- Certified Standing Orders binding on employer + workmen (cannot unilaterally modify without Certifying Officer approval)

## Types of Employment

### 1. Permanent Employment (Indefinite Tenure)

**Characteristics**:
- No fixed end date (employment continues until resignation, retirement, or termination for cause)
- Full statutory benefits (PF, gratuity, bonus, leave)
- Greater job security (termination requires notice period + compliance with Industrial Disputes Act)

**Standard Clause**:
```
EMPLOYMENT TENURE:

You are appointed as a permanent employee with effect from [Joining Date]. Your
employment shall continue unless terminated by either party in accordance with
the notice period provisions outlined in Clause 12.

As a permanent employee, you are entitled to all statutory benefits including
provident fund, gratuity (upon completion of 5 years), earned leave, bonus
(subject to Payment of Bonus Act applicability), and other benefits as per
company policy.
```

**When to Use**: Standard for most roles (especially in India where "at-will employment" not recognized)

### 2. Fixed-Term Employment (Definite Tenure)

**Legal Basis**: Industrial Disputes Act Section 2(oo) amended 2018
```
"Fixed term employment" means the engagement of a workman on the basis of a
written contract of employment for a fixed period.

Provided that (i) his hours of work, wages, allowances and other benefits shall
not be less than those of a permanent workman doing the same or similar kind of work.
```

**Key Features**:
1. **Written Contract Mandatory**: Must specify start and end date
2. **Equal Benefits**: Fixed-term employees entitled to same wages, benefits as permanent employees doing similar work (no discrimination)
3. **No Retrenchment Compensation**: Upon contract expiry, no Section 25F retrenchment notice/compensation required (contract naturally expires)
4. **Gratuity Eligibility**: Entitled to gratuity upon contract completion (prorated for contract duration)

**Standard Clause**:
```
EMPLOYMENT TENURE:

You are appointed on a fixed-term basis for a period of 24 months commencing
from [Start Date] and ending on [End Date].

Your employment will automatically terminate upon expiry of the contract period
without any further notice or compensation beyond statutory gratuity (prorated
for 24 months service).

The Company may, at its sole discretion, extend the contract period by written
amendment. Such extension shall not convert your employment to permanent status
unless expressly stated in writing.

You are entitled to the same wages, allowances, and benefits as permanent
employees performing similar work (as per Section 2(oo) Industrial Disputes Act).
```

**When to Use**:
- Project-based work (18-24 month project duration)
- Seasonal work (agriculture, tourism industries)
- Temporary capacity augmentation (hiring freeze but urgent need)
- Probationary senior hires (offer 12-month fixed-term, then convert to permanent)

**Example - Fixed-Term Contract**:
```
EXAMPLE: IT Project - Client Delivery

Client: Global Bank (2-year digital transformation project, ₹50 crore contract)
Requirement: 50 software engineers for project duration (24 months)

Fixed-Term Employment Terms:
- Duration: 24 months (1st April 2024 to 31st March 2026)
- CTC: ₹12 lakh (same as permanent employees with similar experience)
- Benefits: EPF, ESIC, leave, bonus (all same as permanent)
- Gratuity: Prorated for 24 months (if contract completes, ₹92,307 gratuity
  for ₹5.4L annual basic × 2 years ÷ 26 × 15 = ₹92,307)
- Termination: Either party can terminate with 30 days notice (before contract
  expiry), OR contract expires automatically on 31st March 2026 (no notice/
  compensation required)

Upon project completion (31st March 2026):
- Contract expires automatically
- Full & final settlement (last salary + leave encashment + prorated gratuity)
- No retrenchment compensation (Section 25F not applicable)
- If project extends: New fixed-term contract OR conversion to permanent
```

## Essential Clauses in Employment Agreement

### 1. Appointment & Designation

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 1: APPOINTMENT

The Company hereby appoints you as [Designation] in the [Department] department,
reporting to [Manager Name, Designation], with effect from [Joining Date].

Your primary place of work shall be [City, State]. However, the Company reserves
the right to transfer you to any other location in India or abroad based on
business requirements, with [30/60/90] days prior notice. Relocation assistance
will be provided as per company's Relocation Policy.

Your role and responsibilities are outlined in the attached Job Description
(Annexure A), which may be modified from time to time based on business needs.
```

### 2. Probation & Confirmation

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 2: PROBATION

You will be on probation for a period of [6/12] months from the date of joining.
The probation period may be extended by a further [3/6] months at the Company's
discretion if your performance is deemed unsatisfactory.

During probation:
a) Your performance will be evaluated at [3-month/quarterly] intervals
b) Either party may terminate employment by giving [14/30] days written notice
   or payment of salary in lieu
c) You will be entitled to all statutory benefits (PF, leave, etc.)
d) Confirmation is not automatic; it is subject to satisfactory performance and
   conduct during probation

Upon satisfactory completion of probation, you will be issued a confirmation
letter and your employment will continue as a permanent employee subject to the
terms of this Agreement.

The Company reserves the right to terminate your employment during or at the end
of probation without assigning any reasons, subject to [14/30] days notice or
payment in lieu.
```

**Legal Limits**:
- **Workmen** (Industrial Employment Standing Orders): Max 6 months probation, extendable to 12 months ONLY with written consent
- **Managers/Supervisors**: 12 months probation permissible (not covered by Standing Orders Act)

### 3. Remuneration & Benefits

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 3: REMUNERATION

Your annual Cost to Company (CTC) is ₹[Amount] comprising the following components
as detailed in Annexure B:

a) Fixed Components: Basic Salary, HRA, Special Allowance, Employer's PF
b) Variable Components: Performance Bonus, Retention Bonus (subject to performance
   and company policy)
c) Retiral Benefits: Gratuity, Leave Encashment (as per statutory provisions)

Your salary will be paid monthly in arrears, on or before the [7th/10th] day of
the succeeding month, by electronic transfer to your designated bank account.

The Company reserves the right to review and revise your compensation annually
based on performance, market conditions, and company's financial position. No
salary increase is guaranteed or automatic.

DEDUCTIONS:
The following deductions will be made from your gross salary:
a) Employee's PF contribution (12% of Basic + DA)
b) Income Tax (TDS as per Income Tax Act 1961)
c) Professional Tax (as applicable per state law)
d) Any other statutory deductions or recoveries (loans, advances, notice pay)
```

### 4. Working Hours & Leave

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 4: WORKING HOURS

Your normal working hours are [9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday to Friday], totaling
[40/45/48] hours per week. You are entitled to a lunch break of [1 hour] which
is unpaid.

The Company operates [5-day/6-day] work week. [Sunday/Saturday & Sunday] is a
weekly off.

You may be required to work beyond normal hours or on weekends/holidays to meet
business requirements, without additional compensation (as managerial staff are
exempt from overtime provisions under Factories Act/Shops & Establishments Act).

LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS:

a) Earned Leave (Privilege Leave): [18/21/24] days per calendar year, accumulating
   to a maximum of [45] days. Unutilized leave may be encashed upon resignation
   or retirement.

b) Casual Leave: [7/10] days per calendar year (non-accumulating, non-encashable).

c) Sick Leave: [7/10] days per calendar year (non-accumulating, subject to
   medical certificate for absence >2 consecutive days).

d) Maternity Leave: 26 weeks paid leave for female employees (as per Maternity
   Benefit Act 1961, applicable if you have worked ≥80 days in preceding 12 months).

e) Paternity Leave: [5/7/10] days paid leave (company policy, not statutory).

All leave is subject to prior approval from your reporting manager. Unauthorized
absence may result in disciplinary action including termination.
```

## Restrictive Covenants

### 1. Confidentiality & Non-Disclosure

**Legal Basis**: Common law duty of confidentiality + contractual obligation

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 5: CONFIDENTIALITY

During and after your employment, you shall:

a) CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION:
   Maintain strict confidentiality of all proprietary information including but
   not limited to:
   - Business strategies, financial data, pricing, profit margins
   - Customer lists, supplier details, vendor agreements
   - Technical information, product roadmaps, R&D data
   - Trade secrets, know-how, processes, methodologies
   - Employee data, compensation information
   - Any information marked "Confidential" or reasonably understood to be confidential

b) NON-DISCLOSURE OBLIGATION:
   Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party (competitors, media,
   family members, friends) without prior written authorization from the Company.

c) NON-USE OBLIGATION:
   Not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing your
   duties for the Company.

d) RETURN OF MATERIALS:
   Upon termination, immediately return all documents, data, devices (laptop,
   phone, access cards) containing Confidential Information. Delete all company
   data from personal devices.

e) PERPETUAL OBLIGATION:
   This confidentiality obligation survives termination of employment indefinitely
   (for trade secrets) or for [3/5/7] years post-termination (for other confidential
   information).

f) EXCEPTIONS:
   This clause does not apply to information that:
   - Is publicly available (not due to your breach)
   - Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority
     (but you must notify the Company immediately before disclosure)

BREACH: Breach of this clause will result in immediate termination for cause,
forfeiture of all pending payments (bonus, severance), and legal action for
injunction and damages.
```

### 2. Intellectual Property Assignment

**Legal Basis**: Copyright Act 1972 Section 17 (works created during employment belong to employer)

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 6: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

a) WORKS CREATED DURING EMPLOYMENT:
   All inventions, discoveries, designs, copyrights, patents, trademarks, software
   code, documentation, and other intellectual property ("IP") created, developed,
   or conceived by you during employment, whether during working hours or otherwise,
   and whether using Company resources or not, shall be the exclusive property of
   the Company.

b) AUTOMATIC ASSIGNMENT:
   You hereby assign and transfer all rights, title, and interest in such IP to
   the Company, effective immediately upon creation. No further assignment deed
   or documentation is required.

c) DISCLOSURE OBLIGATION:
   You shall promptly disclose all inventions/IP to the Company and cooperate in
   securing patents, copyrights, or other IP protection (execution of assignment
   deeds, inventor declarations, patent applications).

d) MORAL RIGHTS WAIVER:
   To the extent permitted by law, you waive all moral rights (right to be
   identified as author, right to object to derogatory treatment) in favor of
   the Company.

e) ASSISTANCE POST-TERMINATION:
   Even after termination, you shall assist the Company (at Company's expense)
   in securing IP rights (providing testimony, signing documents, attending patent
   hearings).

f) PRIOR IP:
   If you own any IP created before joining the Company and relevant to your role,
   you must disclose it in writing within 30 days of joining (Annexure C). Such
   prior IP remains your property but you grant the Company a non-exclusive,
   royalty-free license to use it during employment.

g) MOONLIGHTING PROHIBITION:
   You shall not engage in any freelance work, consulting, or part-time employment
   without prior written approval from the Company, as such activities may create
   conflicting IP ownership claims.
```

**Example - Software IP Assignment**:
```
SCENARIO: Software Engineer develops mobile app during employment

Situation A - During Working Hours, Using Company Laptop:
- Engineer develops e-commerce app feature (shopping cart functionality)
- Uses company-issued MacBook, during 9am-6pm office hours
- RESULT: 100% Company IP (Section 17 Copyright Act + Employment Agreement)

Situation B - After Hours, Personal Laptop, Unrelated to Company Business:
- Engineer develops personal finance app (budgeting tool) at home on weekends
- Uses personal laptop, no company resources
- App unrelated to company's business (company does B2B SaaS, not fintech)
- RESULT: Potentially Employee's IP (courts may limit employer's IP claim if:
  (a) created entirely outside working hours, (b) no company resources used,
  (c) unrelated to employer's business)
- BEST PRACTICE: Employee must disclose to employer, seek written waiver

Situation C - After Hours, Personal Laptop, Competing with Company:
- Engineer develops competing e-commerce platform while employed at e-commerce company
- Uses personal laptop, weekends
- RESULT: Company IP + Breach of confidentiality + Conflict of interest
  → Immediate termination for cause + Injunction preventing launch
```

### 3. Non-Compete Covenant

**Legal Basis**: Section 27 Indian Contract Act 1872 voids restraint of trade, BUT reasonable restrictions protecting legitimate business interests are enforceable (per Percept D'Mark v. Zaheer Khan precedent)

**Standard Clause** (Must Be Reasonable):
```
CLAUSE 7: NON-COMPETE

a) RESTRICTION PERIOD:
   For a period of [6/12/18] months immediately following termination of employment
   (for any reason), you shall not:

b) COMPETING BUSINESS:
   Directly or indirectly engage in, be employed by, consult for, or have any
   financial interest in any business that competes with the Company's business
   in the following areas:
   - [Specific Product Lines/Services: e.g., "Enterprise SaaS solutions for
     supply chain management"]
   - [Geographical Scope: e.g., "Within India, Singapore, and UAE" (markets
     where Company operates)]

c) LEGITIMATE BUSINESS INTEREST:
   This restriction is necessary to protect the Company's:
   - Confidential information and trade secrets (customer lists, pricing strategies,
     product roadmaps) which you have access to
   - Customer relationships and goodwill
   - Investments in your training and development

d) REASONABLE SCOPE:
   This restriction applies only to:
   - Duration: [12] months (limited, not perpetual)
   - Geography: [India] (markets where Company operates, not worldwide)
   - Scope: [Enterprise SaaS for supply chain] (specific niche, not all software/IT)

e) EXCEPTIONS:
   This clause does not prevent you from:
   - Owning <5% shares in publicly traded competing companies (passive investment)
   - Joining a large conglomerate that has a competing division (if you don't
     work in competing division)

f) ENFORCEMENT:
   Breach of this covenant will entitle the Company to:
   - Injunctive relief (restrain you from joining competitor)
   - Liquidated damages of [₹5 lakh/₹10 lakh/6 months gross salary]
   - Recovery of training costs, sign-on bonus, retention bonus paid to you

g) GARDEN LEAVE (Optional):
   If the Company wishes to enforce this non-compete, it may place you on "garden
   leave" during notice period (you remain on payroll but don't work, ensuring
   you don't join competitor for [notice period + non-compete period] total).
```

**Enforceability Factors** (Per Niranjan Shankar Golikari v. Century Spinning, 1967):

✅ **LIKELY ENFORCEABLE**:
- Duration: 6-12 months (up to 18 months for senior roles with critical confidential info)
- Geography: City/state where employee worked OR markets where employer operates (not entire India/world)
- Scope: Specific product line/industry segment (not "all IT jobs" or "any employment")
- Compensation: Employer pays salary during non-compete period (garden leave) OR provides consideration (sign-on bonus, stock options)

❌ **LIKELY UNENFORCEABLE**:
- Duration: >24 months (unreasonably long, Section 27 violation)
- Geography: Worldwide (overly broad, especially if employer operates only in India)
- Scope: "Any employment in IT industry" (too broad, violates right to livelihood)
- No consideration: Employer doesn't pay during non-compete + no additional compensation for restriction (courts view as one-sided, unconscionable)

**Example - Enforceable Non-Compete**:
```
SCENARIO: Sales Director at Pharmaceutical Company

Role: Sales Director - Oncology Division
Access to: Customer lists (500 oncologists across India), pricing strategies,
          upcoming drug launch plans, distributor agreements
Employment: 5 years (joined 2019, resigned 2024)
Non-Compete Terms:
- Duration: 12 months post-exit
- Geography: India (where company operates)
- Scope: "Oncology pharmaceutical sales" (specific segment, not all pharma)
- Consideration: ₹50 lakh retention bonus paid in 2022 (with clause: "Forfeited
  if non-compete breached")

OUTCOME: ENFORCEABLE
- Reasonable duration (12 months, not lifetime)
- Reasonable geography (India, not global)
- Narrow scope (oncology pharma only, not all sales jobs)
- Protects legitimate interest (customer relationships, confidential pricing)
- Consideration provided (₹50L retention bonus)

If Sales Director joins competitor (oncology pharma company in India within 12 months):
→ Employer can obtain injunction (restrain from working for competitor)
→ Recover ₹50 lakh retention bonus
```

**Example - Unenforceable Non-Compete**:
```
SCENARIO: Junior Software Engineer at Startup

Role: Junior Software Engineer (2 years experience)
Access to: Standard coding practices, open-source tools (no unique trade secrets)
Employment: 18 months (joined June 2023, resigned December 2024)
Non-Compete Terms:
- Duration: 24 months post-exit
- Geography: Worldwide
- Scope: "Any software development role"
- Consideration: None (no sign-on bonus, no garden leave pay)

OUTCOME: UNENFORCEABLE
- Excessive duration (24 months too long for junior role with limited confidential access)
- Overly broad geography (worldwide restriction unreasonable)
- Overly broad scope ("any software role" violates right to livelihood, Section 27)
- No consideration (employee gets nothing in exchange for restriction)

If Junior Engineer joins competitor:
→ Court likely to strike down non-compete clause
→ Engineer can work for competitor without penalty
→ Employer's only remedy: Confidentiality breach (if engineer discloses specific
  trade secrets, but generic coding skills not protectable)
```

### 4. Non-Solicitation Covenant

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 8: NON-SOLICITATION

For a period of [12/18/24] months following termination, you shall not:

a) CLIENT/CUSTOMER NON-SOLICITATION:
   Directly or indirectly solicit, contact, or do business with any client,
   customer, or prospective customer of the Company with whom you had material
   contact or about whom you obtained confidential information during the last
   [12/24] months of employment, for the purpose of:
   - Offering competing products/services
   - Diverting business away from the Company
   - Inducing them to reduce business with the Company

b) EMPLOYEE NON-SOLICITATION:
   Directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, or induce any employee, consultant,
   or contractor of the Company to:
   - Leave employment with the Company
   - Join your new employer or any competing business
   - Reduce their engagement with the Company

c) SUPPLIER/VENDOR NON-SOLICITATION:
   Interfere with the Company's relationships with suppliers, vendors, or
   business partners to the detriment of the Company.

d) LEGITIMATE BUSINESS INTEREST:
   This restriction protects the Company's investments in customer relationships,
   team building, and supplier networks.

e) EXCEPTIONS:
   - General LinkedIn posts about job openings (not targeted at Company employees)
   - Unsolicited approaches by Company clients/employees (you didn't initiate contact)

f) ENFORCEMENT:
   Breach entitles Company to injunction + liquidated damages of ₹[X] per employee
   solicited or ₹[Y] per customer diverted.
```

**Non-Solicitation vs Non-Compete**:
- **Non-Compete**: Broader (cannot work for competitor at all)
- **Non-Solicitation**: Narrower (can work for competitor, but cannot poach Company's clients/employees)
- Courts more willing to enforce non-solicitation (less restrictive on employee's right to livelihood)

## Termination Provisions

### 1. Termination by Employee (Resignation)

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 9: TERMINATION BY EMPLOYEE

You may resign from employment by giving the Company [30/60/90] days written
notice.

NOTICE PERIOD:
- During Probation: [14/30] days
- Post-Confirmation: [60/90] days

NOTICE PAY IN LIEU:
The Company may, at its discretion, waive the notice period and accept immediate
resignation, provided you pay the Company [Basic Salary/Gross Salary] for the
unserved notice period.

Alternatively, the Company may place you on "garden leave" (you remain on payroll
but do not work) or relieve you immediately without requiring notice pay.

FULL & FINAL SETTLEMENT:
Upon resignation, you are entitled to:
a) Salary for days worked in final month (prorated)
b) Leave encashment (all accumulated earned leave at last drawn Basic)
c) Gratuity (if ≥5 years service, as per Payment of Gratuity Act 1972)
d) Reimbursements (pending expense claims, if any)

The Company will deduct the following from your final settlement:
a) Notice pay shortfall (if you don't serve full notice)
b) Outstanding loans/advances
c) Cost of unreturned company assets (laptop, phone, access card)
d) Any other amounts due to the Company

Full & final settlement will be paid within [30/45] days of your last working day.
```

### 2. Termination by Employer (With/Without Cause)

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 10: TERMINATION BY COMPANY

A. TERMINATION WITHOUT CAUSE (CONVENIENCE):

The Company may terminate your employment without cause by giving you [60/90] days
written notice or payment of [Basic/Gross] salary in lieu of notice.

In such termination, you will receive:
- Notice pay (if not required to serve notice period)
- Leave encashment
- Gratuity (if ≥5 years service)
- Severance pay: [1 month gross salary per year of service, max 6 months] (company policy, not statutory)

B. TERMINATION FOR CAUSE (MISCONDUCT):

The Company may terminate your employment immediately without notice or payment
in lieu if you are guilty of misconduct including:
- Theft, fraud, embezzlement, or criminal activity
- Willful insubordination or refusal to obey lawful orders
- Habitual absence or lateness without authorization
- Intoxication at workplace (alcohol/drugs)
- Sexual harassment, violence, or threatening behavior
- Gross negligence causing material loss to the Company
- Breach of confidentiality or non-compete covenants
- Acceptance of bribes or kickbacks
- Falsification of records or documents
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Engaging in competing business during employment

DOMESTIC INQUIRY (PRINCIPLES OF NATURAL JUSTICE):
Before termination for cause, the Company will conduct a domestic inquiry:
1. Charge sheet issued (specific allegations, not vague)
2. Your written explanation sought (7 days to respond)
3. Inquiry notice issued (date, time, venue, inquiry officer, witness list)
4. Inquiry hearing conducted (you may cross-examine witnesses, present defense,
   engage representative)
5. Inquiry report prepared (findings on whether charges proven)
6. Management's final order (based on inquiry report, proportionate punishment)

If charges are proven, you may be terminated without notice, and you will NOT
receive:
- Notice pay
- Severance pay
- Gratuity (forfeited if dismissed for moral turpitude per Section 4(6) Gratuity Act)

You will still receive:
- Salary for days worked
- Leave encashment (for earned leave already accrued, minus any disciplinary deductions)
```

## Dispute Resolution

**Standard Clause**:
```
CLAUSE 11: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

a) INTERNAL GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE:
   Any grievance or dispute arising out of this Agreement shall first be raised
   through the Company's internal grievance redressal mechanism:
   - Step 1: Written complaint to immediate manager
   - Step 2: Escalation to HR within 7 days if not resolved
   - Step 3: Escalation to Grievance Committee within 14 days

b) CONCILIATION:
   If the dispute is not resolved internally within 30 days, either party may
   refer the matter to conciliation under the Industrial Disputes Act 1947.

c) ARBITRATION:
   If conciliation fails, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration under the
   Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996:
   - Single arbitrator mutually appointed by parties
   - If parties cannot agree, arbitrator appointed by [Arbitration body/Court]
   - Seat of arbitration: [City]
   - Language: English
   - Arbitration costs borne equally by parties

d) JURISDICTION:
   Courts at [City] shall have exclusive jurisdiction for any legal proceedings
   arising from this Agreement.

e) WAIVER OF CLASS ACTION:
   You agree to resolve disputes individually and waive any right to participate
   in class action lawsuits or collective arbitration.
```

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Senior Executive Employment Agreement

```
EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Agreement is entered into on 1st February 2025 between:

XYZ Technologies Pvt Ltd ("Company")
AND
Mr. Vikram Singh ("Employee")

RECITALS:
The Company has offered employment to Employee as Chief Technology Officer, and
Employee has accepted such offer subject to the terms herein.

1. APPOINTMENT:
   Designation: Chief Technology Officer (CTO)
   Department: Technology & Engineering
   Location: Bengaluru (with travel to Mumbai, Delhi offices as required)
   Reporting: Chief Executive Officer
   Start Date: 1st February 2025

2. PROBATION:
   12 months (senior management role)
   Evaluation: Monthly reviews with CEO + Board presentation at Month 6
   Termination during probation: 30 days notice by either party

3. REMUNERATION (Annual CTC: ₹1,20,00,000):
   Fixed: ₹80,00,000
   - Basic: ₹36,00,000 (30% of CTC)
   - HRA: ₹18,00,000 (15% of CTC, 50% of Basic)
   - Special Allowance: ₹26,00,000 (21.67%)

   Variable: ₹30,00,000
   - Performance Bonus: ₹24,00,000 (20% of CTC, target 100%, range 0-200%)
   - Retention Bonus: ₹6,00,000 (5% of CTC, paid after 12 months, forfeited if
     resignation within 24 months)

   Sign-On Bonus: ₹25,00,000 (paid Month 1, clawback if resignation within 36 months:
   - Resign within 12 months: Refund 100% (₹25L)
   - Resign within 24 months: Refund 67% (₹16.7L)
   - Resign within 36 months: Refund 33% (₹8.3L)

   ESOPs: 50,000 stock options (vesting 25% per year over 4 years, exercise price ₹200,
   current valuation ₹800 = ₹3 crore potential gain)

4. BENEFITS:
   - Company car: BMW 5 Series / Mercedes E-Class (chauffeur-driven)
   - Club membership: ₹5 lakh annual (Bangalore Club / ITC Gardenia)
   - Executive health check: Annual (Apollo Hospitals, ₹50,000 package)
   - Relocation: ₹10 lakh (if relocating from another city)

5. NON-COMPETE:
   Duration: 18 months post-exit
   Geography: India + Southeast Asia (markets where Company operates)
   Scope: Enterprise SaaS, specifically HR Tech and Payroll software
```

## Compliance Checklist

### For Employers (Drafting Employment Agreement)

- [ ] Probation period within legal limits (6 months for workmen, 12 months for managers)
- [ ] Notice period specified for both probation and confirmed employment (14-90 days standard)
- [ ] CTC breakdown matches offer letter (avoid discrepancies causing disputes)
- [ ] Restrictive covenants reasonable (non-compete 6-18 months, specific geography/scope)
- [ ] IP assignment clause covers works created during employment (Section 17 Copyright Act)
- [ ] Confidentiality perpetual for trade secrets, time-bound for other information (3-7 years)
- [ ] Termination clause includes domestic inquiry process (Principles of Natural Justice)
- [ ] Gratuity forfeiture clause complies with Section 4(6) Gratuity Act (only for moral turpitude)
- [ ] Dispute resolution clause specifies arbitration + exclusive jurisdiction (avoid forum shopping)
- [ ] Employment agreement executed on joining date (Day 1, before employee starts work)

### For Employees (Reviewing Employment Agreement)

- [ ] Cross-check CTC components with offer letter (ensure consistency)
- [ ] Understand notice period implications (90 days = 3 months lockout before next job)
- [ ] Review non-compete carefully (12 months post-exit = 1 year where you cannot join competitors)
- [ ] Assess non-compete scope (if too broad, negotiate narrower scope or removal)
- [ ] Check IP assignment clause (especially if you do personal projects/freelance on side)
- [ ] Understand confidentiality obligations (what information you cannot disclose post-exit)
- [ ] Review termination for cause grounds (ensure not overly broad or vague)
- [ ] Verify probation evaluation criteria (what metrics will be used to assess performance)
- [ ] Seek legal advice if uncertain (especially for senior roles with significant restrictive covenants)

## Landmark Cases

### 1. Niranjan Shankar Golikari v. Century Spinning & Manufacturing Co (SC 1967)

**Facts**:
- Golikari (Chief Chemist, Century Spinning, ₹1,000/month salary - very senior role in 1960s)
- Employment agreement had non-compete: "Cannot join any textile company in India for 5 years post-exit"
- Resigned, joined competing textile mill 6 months later
- Century Spinning sued for injunction (restrain Golikari from working)

**Golikari's Defense**: Section 27 Indian Contract Act voids restraint of trade → Non-compete unenforceable

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **Section 27 Voids Restraint of Trade**: General rule - restraints on employment are void
2. **Exception for Reasonable Restrictions**: If restriction is:
   - **Reasonable in duration** (5 years too long, 6-12 months reasonable)
   - **Reasonable in geography** (entire India too broad, state where worked reasonable)
   - **Protects legitimate business interest** (trade secrets, customer relationships)
   - **Not unduly harsh on employee** (doesn't deprive livelihood completely)
3. **Golikari's Non-Compete Unenforceable**: 5 years too long + entire India too broad
4. **Test Established**: Reasonableness test (duration, geography, scope, legitimate interest)

**Principle**: Non-compete clauses enforceable if reasonable, but courts scrutinize heavily to protect employee's right to livelihood (Article 19(1)(g) Constitution - right to practice profession)

**Modern Application**: Courts generally enforce 6-18 month non-competes with narrow geography/scope, reject >24 months or nationwide bans

### 2. Desiccant Rotors International Pvt Ltd v. Bappaditya Sarkar (SC 2022)

**Facts**:
- Bappaditya Sarkar (employee, manufacturing company, signed employment agreement with non-compete: 12 months post-exit, cannot join competitors in "desiccant rotor industry in India")
- Resigned, joined direct competitor within 3 months
- Employer obtained injunction from High Court (restrained Bappaditya from working)
- Bappaditya appealed to Supreme Court (argued: Non-compete violates right to livelihood)

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **Non-Compete Prima Facie Valid**: 12 months + India + specific industry (desiccant rotors, niche segment) = reasonable restrictions
2. **Balance of Convenience**: Employer will suffer irreparable harm if confidential information (customer lists, pricing, manufacturing processes) disclosed to competitor
3. **Injunction Upheld**: Employee restrained from working for competitor for remaining 9 months (of 12-month non-compete)
4. **Garden Leave Ordered**: Employer must continue paying 50% salary to employee during injunction period (compensate for loss of livelihood)

**Principle**:
- Courts will enforce reasonable non-competes (12 months, specific industry, specific geography)
- BUT may require employer to compensate employee during restriction period (garden leave pay)
- Balance: Protect employer's trade secrets vs employee's right to livelihood
