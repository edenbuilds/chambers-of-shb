# Probation & Confirmation - Performance Evaluation Framework

## Protocol ID
**IL-LABOUR-PROBATION-CONFIRMATION** (v1.0)

---

## Purpose
Guide employers and HR professionals through the legal framework, performance evaluation process, and best practices for managing employee probation periods and confirmation under Indian labour law, covering maximum probation duration, evaluation criteria, extension procedures, confirmation process, termination rights during probation, and employee entitlements.

---

## Legal Framework

### 1. Statutory Provisions on Probation

**Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946 - Rule 2(vi)**:
```
"A workman shall be deemed to be on probation during the period of his original
employment in the industrial establishment and such period shall not ordinarily
exceed six months."
```

**Key Statutory Requirements**:

| **Employee Category** | **Maximum Probation** | **Extension Allowed?** | **Legal Basis** |
|-----------------------|-----------------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **Workmen** (Industrial Employment Standing Orders Act) | 6 months | Yes, up to 12 months TOTAL (with written consent + justification) | Model Standing Orders Rule 2(vi) |
| **Managers/Supervisors** (Not covered by Standing Orders) | 12 months | Yes, employer discretion (common to extend to 18 months for senior roles) | Employment contract (no statutory limit) |
| **Apprentices** (Apprentices Act 1961) | Duration of apprenticeship (6 months to 4 years depending on trade) | N/A (apprenticeship, not probation) | Apprentices Act Section 3 |

**Applicable Establishments**:
- **Standing Orders Act**: Applies to industrial establishments with ≥100 workmen (manufacturing, mining, plantations)
- **Shops & Establishments Act**: State-specific (e.g., Maharashtra allows 6-month probation for all employees, Karnataka allows 3-6 months)

### 2. Probation vs Apprenticeship vs Fixed-Term

**Key Distinctions**:

| **Aspect** | **Probation** | **Apprenticeship** | **Fixed-Term Employment** |
|------------|---------------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Purpose** | Evaluate suitability for permanent role | Skill training (government-regulated) | Project-based work (definite tenure) |
| **Duration** | 6-12 months | 6 months to 4 years (trade-specific) | 12-36 months typically |
| **Wages** | Full contractual salary (per offer letter) | Stipend (prescribed rates, lower than market wages) | Full wages (equal to permanent employees) |
| **Statutory Benefits** | Full (PF, ESIC, leave, gratuity accrual) | Limited (no PF if stipend <₹15,000) | Full (PF, leave, prorated gratuity) |
| **Termination** | 14-30 days notice | Apprenticeship can be terminated only for misconduct (not performance) | Contract expires automatically (no notice required) |
| **Outcome** | Confirmation as permanent OR termination | Certificate of completion (no automatic employment) | Fixed-term ends OR converts to permanent (if extended) |

## Probation Period - Rights & Obligations

### 1. Employee Rights During Probation

**Full Statutory Entitlements** (Misconception: Probation employees have reduced rights):

✅ **ENTITLED TO**:
1. **Full Wages**: As per offer letter/employment agreement (no reduction allowed during probation)
2. **Provident Fund**: 12% employer + 12% employee contribution (if establishment covered by EPF Act)
3. **ESIC**: 3.25% employer + 0.75% employee (if wage ≤₹21,000/month + establishment covered)
4. **Earned Leave**: Accrues during probation (1.5 days per month for Factories Act, prorated)
   - Example: 6-month probation → 9 days earned leave accrued (can be encashed if terminated during probation or carried forward if confirmed)
5. **Casual/Sick Leave**: As per company policy (typically 7 days casual + 7 days sick per year, prorated for probation)
6. **Gratuity Accrual**: Begins from Day 1 (payable only if ≥5 years service, but accrues during probation)
7. **Bonus**: Statutory bonus (Payment of Bonus Act) applies if wage ≤₹21,000/month (prorated for probation period)
8. **Maternity Benefit**: Female employees entitled to 26 weeks paid leave (if worked ≥80 days in preceding 12 months, probation counted)

❌ **NOT ENTITLED TO**:
1. **Long Notice Period**: Shorter notice period during probation (14-30 days vs 60-90 days post-confirmation)
2. **Severance Pay**: No compensation if terminated during probation (beyond notice pay + leave encashment)
3. **Automatic Confirmation**: Confirmation is at employer's discretion based on performance (not automatic after 6 months)

### 2. Employer's Rights During Probation

**Termination Easier** (vs Post-Confirmation):
1. **Shorter Notice**: 14-30 days notice vs 60-90 days for confirmed employees
2. **No Retrenchment Compensation**: Section 25F Industrial Disputes Act (notice + 15 days wages per year of service) does NOT apply to probation termination
3. **Lower Bar for Termination**: Can terminate for unsatisfactory performance (vs post-confirmation where employer needs to prove misconduct through domestic inquiry for cause-based termination)

**Evaluation Flexibility**:
- Can extend probation if performance borderline (from 6 months to 12 months max for workmen, requires written consent)
- Can terminate without citing specific reasons (as long as notice period given)

**Example - Probation Termination**:
```
SCENARIO: Software Engineer on 6-Month Probation

Month 3 Review:
- Performance: Below expectations (code quality issues, missed 2 sprint deadlines)
- Manager feedback: Needs improvement in coding standards, time management
- Action: Verbal warning, additional training provided

Month 5 Review:
- Performance: Still below expectations (continued delays, peer feedback negative)
- HR Decision: Terminate during probation

Termination Process:
- Notice: 30 days notice (as per employment agreement)
- Payment: Full salary for 30 days + leave encashment (5 months × 1.75 days =
  8.75 days leave accrued, encashed at Basic salary)
- No severance: No additional compensation (not entitled during probation)
- No domestic inquiry: Not required for probation termination (unless Standing
  Orders mandates inquiry even for probation)

Employee Cannot Challenge:
- Courts/labour tribunals generally do NOT interfere with probation terminations
  (employer's subjective assessment of "unsatisfactory performance" respected)
- Exception: If termination violates Standing Orders (e.g., no notice given) OR
  discriminatory (terminated for pregnancy, caste, gender - violation of
  Constitution Article 15-16)
```

## Performance Evaluation Process

### 1. Evaluation Frequency

**Best Practice Timeline**:

```
MONTH 1: Induction & Onboarding
- Day 1-7: HR induction (policies, benefits, IT setup)
- Day 8-30: Department induction (team introductions, product training, role shadowing)
- Evaluation: Informal check-in with manager (settle in, questions answered)

MONTH 2-3: Initial Performance Assessment
- KPIs set: Specific, measurable goals for probation period
- Mid-probation review (Month 3):
  - Manager evaluation (performance vs KPIs, behavioral competencies)
  - 360-degree feedback (peers, cross-functional teams)
  - Self-assessment (employee's view on progress, challenges)
- Outcome:
  - On track: Continue probation, no intervention
  - Needs improvement: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) for remaining 3 months
  - Unsatisfactory: Consider termination (with 30 days notice)

MONTH 4-5: Performance Improvement (if PIP issued)
- Weekly check-ins with manager (progress tracking)
- Additional training/mentoring provided
- Month 5 review: Assess improvement

MONTH 6: Final Evaluation & Confirmation Decision
- Comprehensive evaluation:
  - Performance vs KPIs (quantitative metrics)
  - Behavioral competencies (teamwork, communication, initiative)
  - Cultural fit (alignment with company values)
  - Potential for growth (can take on increased responsibilities)
- Decision:
  - Option A: CONFIRM (performance satisfactory, issue confirmation letter)
  - Option B: EXTEND probation (borderline performance, extend to Month 9/12)
  - Option C: TERMINATE (performance unsatisfactory, 30 days notice)
```

### 2. Evaluation Criteria

**Quantitative Metrics (Role-Specific)**:

**Software Engineer**:
- Code quality: <5 bugs per 1,000 lines of code (industry standard)
- Sprint velocity: Complete assigned story points (target: 20 points per 2-week sprint)
- Code review feedback: Avg rating ≥3.5/5 from peer reviewers
- On-time delivery: ≥80% of tasks delivered by committed deadline

**Sales Executive**:
- Revenue: ₹30 lakh sales in 6 months (₹5 lakh/month avg)
- Lead conversion: 20% conversion rate (20 deals closed out of 100 leads)
- Customer acquisition: 15 new customers onboarded
- CRM hygiene: 100% leads updated in CRM within 24 hours

**HR Executive**:
- Recruitment: 12 positions filled in 6 months (2 hires/month avg)
- Time to hire: <45 days (from requisition to offer acceptance)
- Offer acceptance rate: ≥70% (7 out of 10 candidates accept offer)
- Employee engagement: Conduct 2 engagement activities (team outings, wellness sessions)

**Qualitative Metrics (Behavioral Competencies)**:

All Roles:
- **Teamwork**: Collaborates effectively, shares knowledge, supports colleagues
- **Communication**: Clear written/verbal communication, active listening
- **Initiative**: Proactively identifies problems, suggests solutions
- **Learning Agility**: Quickly learns new skills, adapts to feedback
- **Cultural Fit**: Aligns with company values (integrity, customer-focus, innovation)

**Evaluation Matrix** (Sample):

```
PROBATION EVALUATION FORM

Employee Name: Priya Sharma
Designation: Software Engineer
Department: Product Engineering
Probation Period: 1st Jan 2025 to 30th Jun 2025
Evaluation Date: 30th Jun 2025 (Month 6 Review)

A. QUANTITATIVE METRICS (Weight 60%):

1. Code Quality (20%)
   - Bugs per 1,000 lines: 4 bugs (Target: <5) → Rating: 4/5
   - Peer code review rating: 4.2/5 (Target: ≥3.5) → Rating: 5/5
   - Weighted Score: (4+5)/2 = 4.5 × 20% = 0.9

2. Sprint Velocity (20%)
   - Story points delivered: 18 avg (Target: 20) → Rating: 3.5/5
   - On-time delivery: 75% (Target: ≥80%) → Rating: 3/5
   - Weighted Score: (3.5+3)/2 = 3.25 × 20% = 0.65

3. Productivity (20%)
   - Tasks completed: 48 tasks in 6 months (Target: 50) → Rating: 4/5
   - Rework rate: 10% (Target: <15%) → Rating: 4/5
   - Weighted Score: (4+4)/2 = 4 × 20% = 0.8

QUANTITATIVE SCORE: (0.9 + 0.65 + 0.8) = 2.35/3.0 (78%)

B. QUALITATIVE METRICS (Weight 40%):

1. Teamwork (10%): Actively participates in team meetings, helps junior developers
   with code reviews → Rating: 4/5 → Score: 4 × 10% = 0.4

2. Communication (10%): Clear documentation, responsive on Slack, good stakeholder
   updates → Rating: 4.5/5 → Score: 4.5 × 10% = 0.45

3. Initiative (10%): Identified 3 process improvements (automated testing scripts,
   refactored legacy code) → Rating: 5/5 → Score: 5 × 10% = 0.5

4. Learning Agility (10%): Completed 2 certifications (AWS, React Advanced),
   quickly adapted to new tech stack → Rating: 5/5 → Score: 5 × 10% = 0.5

QUALITATIVE SCORE: (0.4 + 0.45 + 0.5 + 0.5) = 1.85/2.0 (92.5%)

C. OVERALL SCORE:
   Quantitative (60%): 2.35/3.0 = 78%
   Qualitative (40%): 1.85/2.0 = 92.5%
   TOTAL: (2.35 + 1.85) / 5.0 = 4.2/5.0 = 84%

D. RATING SCALE:
   - 4.5-5.0 (90-100%): Exceptional → CONFIRM + 10% salary hike
   - 4.0-4.49 (80-89%): Exceeds Expectations → CONFIRM + 5% salary hike
   - 3.5-3.99 (70-79%): Meets Expectations → CONFIRM
   - 3.0-3.49 (60-69%): Below Expectations → EXTEND probation by 3 months
   - <3.0 (<60%): Unsatisfactory → TERMINATE

E. DECISION: CONFIRM
   Employee has demonstrated solid performance (84% score), strong qualitative
   skills (initiative, learning agility), and good cultural fit. Sprint velocity
   slightly below target (18 vs 20), but improving trend observed in Month 5-6
   (20 points achieved in last sprint).

   RECOMMENDATION: Confirm as permanent employee with 5% salary hike (₹12 LPA
   to ₹12.6 LPA effective 1st July 2025).

Manager Signature: _______________
HR Signature: _______________
Date: 30th June 2025
```

## Probation Extension

### 1. Legal Requirements for Extension

**For Workmen** (Industrial Employment Standing Orders Act):
- **Original Probation**: 6 months
- **Extension**: Up to 6 additional months (12 months TOTAL maximum)
- **Requirements**:
  1. **Written Consent**: Employee must consent in writing to extension
  2. **Justification**: Clear performance gaps documented (not arbitrary)
  3. **Communicated Before Original Expiry**: Extension notice BEFORE Month 6 ends (if communicated after Month 6, extension invalid → employee auto-confirmed)

**For Managers/Supervisors** (Not Covered by Standing Orders):
- **Employer Discretion**: No statutory limit (common to extend 12 months to 18 months for senior roles)
- **Contractual Terms Govern**: Employment agreement specifies extension rights

**Standard Extension Clause** (Employment Agreement):
```
"The probation period may be extended by a further [3/6] months at the Company's
discretion if your performance is deemed unsatisfactory. Such extension will be
communicated in writing at least 7 days before the end of the original probation
period. Extension beyond [12] months total requires your written consent."
```

### 2. Extension Process

**Timeline**:
```
MONTH 5 (Week 3-4): Mid-Probation Review
- Manager identifies performance gaps (sprint velocity 15 vs target 20)
- Informal discussion with employee (areas for improvement)
- Decision: Consider extension if performance doesn't improve by Month 6

MONTH 6 (Week 1-2): Final Evaluation
- Performance still below target (18 vs 20 story points)
- Manager recommends extension (needs more time to assess improvement)
- HR drafts extension letter

MONTH 6 (Week 3, Day 15): Extension Letter Issued
- Timing: At least 7-14 days BEFORE original probation ends (30th June)
- Employee signs acknowledgment (written consent)

1st JULY: Extended Probation Begins
- Additional 3 months (till 30th September)
- Clear KPIs set for extended period (must achieve 20 story points/sprint consistently)
- Monthly reviews during extension (closer monitoring)

30th SEPTEMBER: Final Decision
- Performance improved: CONFIRM
- Performance still below: TERMINATE (with 30 days notice)
```

**Extension Letter Format**:
```
[Company Letterhead]

Date: 15th June 2025

Mr. Rohan Verma
Software Engineer
Product Engineering Team

Dear Rohan,

Subject: Extension of Probation Period

This is with reference to your probation period which is scheduled to end on
30th June 2025 (6 months from your joining date of 1st January 2025).

Based on your performance evaluation conducted on 10th June 2025, we have observed
that while you have demonstrated satisfactory progress in code quality and teamwork,
your sprint velocity (average 18 story points per sprint) has been below the
expected target of 20 story points.

In light of this, the Company has decided to extend your probation period by an
additional 3 months (from 1st July 2025 to 30th September 2025) to allow you
more time to demonstrate consistent performance at the required level.

EXTENDED PROBATION TERMS:
- Duration: 3 months (1st July 2025 to 30th September 2025)
- Performance Goals:
  a) Achieve 20 story points per sprint consistently (in 6 consecutive sprints)
  b) Maintain code quality (≤5 bugs per 1,000 lines)
  c) Improve on-time delivery to ≥85%
- Evaluation: Monthly reviews (end of July, August, September)
- Final Confirmation Decision: 30th September 2025

During the extended probation:
- Your salary and benefits remain unchanged
- All statutory entitlements (PF, leave, ESIC) continue
- Notice period remains 30 days (by either party)

Please sign and return the duplicate copy of this letter as acknowledgment and
consent for the extension.

We believe this additional time will help you achieve the required performance
standards and look forward to your continued improvement.

Yours sincerely,

[Signature]
Priya Mehta
HR Manager

EMPLOYEE ACKNOWLEDGMENT:
I acknowledge receipt of this letter and consent to the extension of my probation
period from 1st July 2025 to 30th September 2025.

Employee Signature: _______________
```

## Confirmation Process

**Issued After**: Satisfactory completion of probation (6/12 months)

### 2. Changes Upon Confirmation

**Rights Enhanced**:
1. **Longer Notice Period**: 60-90 days (vs 14-30 days during probation)
2. **Severance Eligibility**: If terminated without cause, eligible for severance pay (typically 1-3 months salary, company policy)
3. **Greater Job Security**: Termination for cause requires domestic inquiry (Principles of Natural Justice)
4. **Bonus/ESOP Eligibility**: Performance bonus, stock options (if applicable)
5. **Promotion Eligibility**: Enters annual promotion cycle

**Obligations Remain**:
1. **Confidentiality**: Continues (perpetual for trade secrets)
2. **Non-Compete**: Enforceable post-confirmation (12-18 months post-exit)
3. **IP Assignment**: All IP created belongs to employer
4. **Notice Period**: Must serve or pay in lieu

## Termination During Probation

### 1. Employer-Initiated Termination

**Grounds**:
- Unsatisfactory performance (most common)
- Misconduct (theft, insubordination, absenteeism)
- Background verification failure (post-joining discovery of false information)
- Position no longer required (business reasons)

**Process**:
1. **Notice**: 14-30 days written notice OR payment in lieu (as per employment agreement)
2. **No Domestic Inquiry**: Not required for probation termination (unless Standing Orders mandates)
3. **Payment**: Salary for notice period (if not served) + leave encashment
4. **No Severance**: No retrenchment compensation under Section 25F Industrial Disputes Act

**Termination Letter Format**:
```
[Company Letterhead]

Date: 15th May 2025

Mr. Karan Malhotra
Junior Executive - Sales
Sales Department

Dear Karan,

Subject: Termination of Employment During Probation Period

This is with reference to your employment with the Company as Junior Executive -
Sales, which commenced on 1st January 2025 on a probationary basis for 6 months.

Based on your performance evaluation conducted during the probation period, we
regret to inform you that your performance has not met the expected standards
for the role. Specifically:

1. Sales Target: You achieved ₹15 lakh sales against a target of ₹30 lakh
   (50% achievement) during the 4.5-month period.

2. Lead Conversion: Your conversion rate was 8% (8 deals closed out of 100 leads)
   against the target of 20%.

3. Customer Feedback: Received negative feedback from 3 customers regarding
   follow-up and responsiveness.

Despite coaching and support provided by your manager, the required improvement
has not been observed.

Accordingly, the Company has decided to terminate your employment during the
probation period, effective 14th June 2025 (30 days from the date of this letter,
as per your Employment Agreement Clause 9).

LAST WORKING DAY: 14th June 2025

FULL & FINAL SETTLEMENT:
You will receive the following in your final settlement (payable by 30th June 2025):

1. Salary for notice period (15th May to 14th June): ₹25,000 (pro-rated for
   30 days)
2. Leave encashment: 7 days earned leave accrued (4.5 months × 1.5 days/month)
   encashed at Basic salary (₹14,000 × 7/26 = ₹3,769)
3. Reimbursements: Pending expense claims (if any, submit by 20th May)

DEDUCTIONS:
- Company laptop (HP ProBook) not returned: ₹45,000 (will be deducted from
  final settlement, please return by 14th June to avoid deduction)

You are requested to:
a) Hand over all company property (laptop, access card, documents)
b) Complete the exit formalities with HR (exit interview, asset return form)
c) Provide your updated bank account details for final settlement credit

We wish you the best in your future endeavors.

Yours sincerely,

[Signature]

Sales Manager

[Signature]
HR Department
```

### 2. Employee-Initiated Resignation During Probation

**Process**:
- **Notice**: 14-30 days (as per employment agreement, shorter than post-confirmation)
- **Payment**: No buyback clause typically enforced during probation (vs post-confirmation where notice buyout common)

## Common Issues & Resolutions

### Issue 1: Automatic Confirmation After Probation?

**Misconception**: "If employer doesn't issue extension/termination letter by end of Month 6, employee is auto-confirmed"

**Reality**:
- **Standing Orders Establishments**: YES, if employer doesn't communicate extension/termination before probation ends, employee deemed confirmed (per Model Standing Orders Rule 2)
- **Non-Standing Orders Establishments**: Depends on employment agreement
  - If agreement states "confirmation subject to satisfactory performance" → Not automatic
  - If agreement silent → Courts may interpret as automatic confirmation after probation period

**Best Practice**: Employer should issue confirmation/extension/termination letter AT LEAST 7-14 days before probation ends (avoid disputes)

### Issue 2: Probation Extension Without Written Consent

**Scenario**: Employer verbally tells employee "Your probation is extended by 3 months" but no written extension letter issued

**Legal Position**:
- **Workmen**: Extension invalid (Standing Orders require written communication + employee consent)
- **Result**: Employee deemed confirmed from original probation end date (6 months)
- **Employer Cannot Terminate**: Must now give 60-90 days notice (post-confirmation notice period) + may need domestic inquiry if terminating for cause

**Landmark Case**: Management of RSRTC v. Shyam Sunder (SC 2001)
- **Facts**: Shyam Sunder's probation was to end on 31st March. On 5th April (AFTER probation ended), employer issued extension letter (extending probation by 3 months)
- **Held**: Extension invalid (communicated after original probation expired). Shyam Sunder deemed confirmed from 1st April. Employer cannot now treat him as probationer.
- **Principle**: Extension must be communicated BEFORE original probation ends, else employee auto-confirmed

## Compliance Checklist

### For Employers

- [ ] Probation period specified in offer letter + employment agreement (6-12 months depending on role)
- [ ] Performance evaluation criteria defined upfront (KPIs, behavioral competencies)
- [ ] Evaluation frequency scheduled (Month 3, Month 6 reviews mandatory)
- [ ] Extension letter issued 7-14 days BEFORE probation ends (if extending)
- [ ] Written consent obtained from employee for extension (for workmen covered by Standing Orders)
- [ ] Confirmation letter issued promptly after probation completion (within 7 days of end date)
- [ ] Salary revision (if any) effective from confirmation date
- [ ] Notice period updated in HR systems (14-30 days probation → 60-90 days post-confirmation)
- [ ] Statutory benefits continued during probation (PF, ESIC, leave accrual)

### For Employees

- [ ] Understand probation duration (6/12 months) and evaluation criteria
- [ ] Review performance goals set at Month 1 (ensure specific, measurable, achievable)
- [ ] Prepare for mid-probation review (Month 3, document achievements)
- [ ] Seek feedback proactively (don't wait for formal reviews, monthly check-ins with manager)
- [ ] If extension letter received, review carefully (reasons for extension, goals for extended period)
- [ ] Provide written consent for extension (if required)
- [ ] If terminated during probation, check notice period compliance (employer must give 14-30 days or pay in lieu)
- [ ] Claim full & final settlement (salary + leave encashment, typically paid within 30-45 days of last working day)
