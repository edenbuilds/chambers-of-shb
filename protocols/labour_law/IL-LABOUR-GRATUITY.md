# Gratuity - Payment Framework & Calculation

## Protocol ID
**IL-LABOUR-GRATUITY** (v1.0)

---

## Purpose
Guide employers and HR professionals through the legal framework, calculation methodology, and compliance requirements under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972, covering eligibility criteria (5 years service), formula for calculation (15 days wages × years ÷ 26), maximum ceiling (₹20 lakh), forfeiture provisions (Section 4(6) moral turpitude), payment timeline (30 days), nomination procedures, death benefits, and tax implications under Section 10(10) Income Tax Act 1961.

---

## Legal Framework

### 1. Payment of Gratuity Act 1972 - Overview

**Objective** (Preamble):
```
"An Act to provide for a scheme for the payment of gratuity to employees engaged
in factories, mines, oilfields, plantations, ports, railway companies, shops or
other establishments and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto."

Gratuity is a DEFERRED WAGE (not employer's bounty), payable as a token of
appreciation for long service rendered by employee.
```

**Applicability** (Section 1(3)):
```
This Act applies to:
(a) Every factory, mine, oilfield, plantation, port, and railway company
(b) Every shop or establishment employing ≥10 persons

Once applicable (≥10 employees), coverage continues EVEN IF employee count falls below 10.
```

**Key Definitions**:

1. **Completed Year of Service** (Section 2(e)):
   ```
   "Continuous service of one year" means:
   - For monthly-wage employees: Service of 12 months
   - For seasonal employees: 190 days of actual work in 12 months

   Breaks in service (due to sickness, accident, leave, layoff, strike, lockout)
   do NOT break continuity if employee rejoins within permissible period.
   ```

2. **Wages** (Section 2(s)):
   ```
   "Wages" means all emoluments earned by employee:
   - Basic salary + Dearness Allowance (DA)

   EXCLUDES: Bonus, commission, HRA, overtime, any allowances, traveling allowance,
            retrenchment compensation
   ```

### 2. Eligibility - Section 4

**Five-Year Service Requirement**:
```
Section 4(1): Gratuity shall be payable to an employee on the termination of his
employment after he has rendered continuous service for not less than five years.

EXCEPTIONS (5-year rule waived):
(a) Death of employee (gratuity payable to nominee/legal heir even if <5 years service)
(b) Disablement due to accident/disease (gratuity payable even if <5 years service)
```

**Continuous Service** (Section 2A):
```
Employee deemed to be in continuous service if:
(a) Worked for ≥240 days in preceding 12 months (for establishments working ≥6 days/week)
(b) Worked for ≥190 days in preceding 12 months (for seasonal establishments,
    mines where work <6 days/week)

Days include: Weekly offs, paid leave, maternity leave, temporary disablement
             leave (all counted towards 240-day threshold)
```

**Example - Continuity Calculation**:
```
EMPLOYEE: Priya Sharma (joined 1st Jan 2020, resigned 31st Dec 2024 = 5 years)

YEAR 1 (2020):
- Days worked: 280 days (260 working days + 20 days leave)
- Breaks: 10 days unauthorized absence (without leave approval)
- Continuous service: YES (280 days > 240 days threshold)

YEAR 2 (2021):
- Days worked: 230 days (employee on maternity leave 26 weeks = 182 days)
- Maternity leave counted: YES (182 days + 48 days actual work = 230 days)
- Continuous service: NO (230 days < 240 days, BUT maternity leave protects continuity)

YEAR 3 (2022):
- Days worked: 290 days
- Continuous service: YES (290 > 240)

YEAR 4 (2023):
- Days worked: 270 days
- Continuous service: YES (270 > 240)

YEAR 5 (2024):
- Days worked: 300 days
- Continuous service: YES (300 > 240)

RESULT: 5 completed years of continuous service → Eligible for gratuity (even
        though Year 2 had only 230 days, maternity leave exception applies)
```

## Gratuity Calculation Formula

### 1. Formula - Section 4(2)

**For Monthly-Wage Employees** (Majority):
```
Gratuity = (15 × Last Drawn Wages × Years of Service) ÷ 26

Where:
- 15 = Days of wages payable per year of service (statutory entitlement)
- Last Drawn Wages = Basic + DA (monthly, as on date of termination)
- Years of Service = Completed years (fraction ≥6 months rounded up to 1 year)
- 26 = Working days per month (statutory average, excluding Sundays)
```

**For Piece-Rate/Daily-Wage Employees** (Less Common):
```
Gratuity = (15 × Average Wages × Years of Service) ÷ 30

Where:
- Average Wages = Total wages in 3 months immediately preceding termination ÷ 3
- 30 = Days in a month (for daily-wage workers)
```

### 2. Maximum Ceiling - Section 4(3)

**Statutory Maximum** (Amended 2018):
```
Section 4(3): The amount of gratuity payable to an employee shall not exceed
₹20,00,000 (Twenty Lakh Rupees).

Earlier Ceiling: ₹10 lakh (2010-2018)
Current Ceiling: ₹20 lakh (effective 29th March 2018)

IMPORTANT: Ceiling applies to EACH termination, not lifetime cumulative.
If employee works with 2 employers (5 years each), gets ₹20L max from each.
```

### 3. Calculation Examples

**Example 1 - Standard Calculation**:
```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Rajesh Kumar
Service: 10 years 8 months (1st Jan 2014 to 31st Aug 2024)
Last Drawn Salary: ₹80,000/month
- Basic: ₹50,000
- HRA: ₹20,000 (NOT counted in gratuity)
- Special Allowance: ₹10,000 (NOT counted)

Last Drawn Wages (for Gratuity): ₹50,000 (Basic only, no DA component in salary)

Years of Service:
10 years 8 months = 10 + 8/12 years
Since 8 months ≥ 6 months, round up to 1 year
Total: 11 years

GRATUITY CALCULATION:
Gratuity = (15 × ₹50,000 × 11) ÷ 26
         = (₹82,50,000) ÷ 26
         = ₹3,17,307.69
         = ₹3,17,308 (rounded to nearest rupee)

TAX TREATMENT:
Exempt up to ₹20 lakh (Section 10(10) Income Tax Act)
Taxable: Nil (₹3.17L < ₹20L limit)

NET GRATUITY: ₹3,17,308 (tax-free)
```

**Example 2 - High Earner (Ceiling Applies)**:
```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Vikram Singh (CEO)
Service: 25 years 3 months (1st Apr 1999 to 30th Jun 2024)
Last Drawn Salary: ₹5,00,000/month
- Basic: ₹2,50,000
- HRA: ₹1,25,000 (NOT counted)
- Performance Bonus: ₹1,25,000 (NOT counted)

Last Drawn Wages: ₹2,50,000 (Basic only)

Years of Service:
25 years 3 months = 25 + 3/12 years
Since 3 months < 6 months, DO NOT round up
Total: 25 years

GRATUITY CALCULATION (Formula):
Gratuity = (15 × ₹2,50,000 × 25) ÷ 26
         = (₹9,37,50,000) ÷ 26
         = ₹36,05,769

CEILING APPLIES:
Maximum payable: ₹20,00,000 (Section 4(3) ceiling)

ACTUAL GRATUITY PAYABLE: ₹20,00,000 (capped, even though formula = ₹36L)

TAX TREATMENT:
Exempt up to ₹20 lakh (Section 10(10))
Taxable: Nil (exactly at ₹20L limit)

NET GRATUITY: ₹20,00,000 (tax-free)
```

**Example 3 - Partial Year Service**:
```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Suresh Kumar
Service: 5 years 4 months (1st Feb 2019 to 31st May 2024)
Last Drawn Wages: ₹40,000/month (Basic + DA)

Years of Service:
5 years 4 months = 5 + 4/12 years
Since 4 months < 6 months, DO NOT round up
Total: 5 years (minimum eligibility met)

GRATUITY CALCULATION:
Gratuity = (15 × ₹40,000 × 5) ÷ 26
         = (₹30,00,000) ÷ 26
         = ₹1,15,384.61
         = ₹1,15,385 (rounded)

If service was 5 years 6 months (exactly 6 months):
Total: 6 years (rounded up, as ≥6 months)
Gratuity = (15 × ₹40,000 × 6) ÷ 26 = ₹1,38,461

DIFFERENCE: ₹23,076 additional for 2 extra months of service (due to rounding rule)
```

**Example 4 - Death Case (Service <5 Years)**:
```
EMPLOYEE PROFILE:
Name: Amit Sharma (died in service due to heart attack)
Service: 3 years 2 months (1st Jun 2021 to 31st Jul 2024)
Last Drawn Wages: ₹35,000/month

Eligibility: Service <5 years, BUT Section 4(1) exception applies (death case)
           → Gratuity payable to nominee/legal heir

Years of Service:
3 years 2 months = 3 + 2/12 years
Since 2 months < 6 months, DO NOT round up
Total: 3 years

GRATUITY CALCULATION:
Gratuity = (15 × ₹35,000 × 3) ÷ 26
         = (₹15,75,000) ÷ 26
         = ₹60,576.92
         = ₹60,577 (rounded)

PAYABLE TO: Nominee (as per Form F submitted during employment)
            OR Legal heirs (if no nomination, distributed per succession laws)

TAX TREATMENT:
Fully exempt (death case, Section 10(10) exemption applies regardless of amount)
```

## Forfeiture of Gratuity - Section 4(6)

### 1. Grounds for Forfeiture

**Section 4(6) - Moral Turpitude**:
```
The gratuity of an employee, whose services have been terminated for any act,
willful omission or negligence causing any damage or loss to, or destruction of,
property belonging to the employer, OR for riotous or disorderly conduct or any
other act of violence, OR for any act constituting an offence involving moral
turpitude, provided such act was committed by him in the course of his employment,
may be forfeited to the extent of the damage or loss so caused.
```

**Forfeiture Categories**:

**A. DAMAGE TO PROPERTY**:
- Willful destruction, negligence causing loss
- Examples: Machine operator damages equipment through negligence (₹5 lakh loss),
           warehouse fire due to employee's smoking (₹50 lakh damage)
- Forfeiture: To the extent of damage/loss (not full gratuity, proportionate)

**B. RIOTOUS/DISORDERLY CONDUCT**:
- Violence, assault, rioting in workplace
- Examples: Employee assaults supervisor, instigates mob violence during strike

**C. MORAL TURPITUDE** (Most Common):
- Theft, fraud, embezzlement, sexual harassment, bribery
- Examples: Cashier embezzles ₹10 lakh, Manager accepts bribes, Security guard
           steals goods

### 2. Extent of Forfeiture

**Full Forfeiture**:
```
If misconduct involves moral turpitude (theft, fraud, sexual harassment), employer
can forfeit ENTIRE gratuity (not limited to actual damage amount).

Example:
Employee: 15 years service, gratuity ₹8 lakh
Misconduct: Theft of ₹2 lakh (proved through domestic inquiry)
Forfeiture: ₹8 lakh FULL gratuity forfeited (not just ₹2L)
Reason: Moral turpitude (theft) permits full forfeiture per Section 4(6)(a)
```

**Partial Forfeiture**:
```
If misconduct involves property damage/negligence (without moral turpitude),
forfeiture limited to extent of damage.

Example:
Employee: 20 years service, gratuity ₹12 lakh
Misconduct: Negligence causing machine damage (₹3 lakh loss)
Forfeiture: ₹3 lakh ONLY (proportionate to damage)
Gratuity Payable: ₹12L - ₹3L = ₹9 lakh
```

### 3. Procedural Requirements for Forfeiture

**Domestic Inquiry Mandatory**:
```
Before forfeiting gratuity, employer MUST:
1. Conduct domestic inquiry (Principles of Natural Justice)
2. Prove misconduct through inquiry (charge sheet, evidence, employee's defense)
3. Issue termination order (specifying grounds: "dismissed for theft")
4. Issue gratuity forfeiture order (separate from termination order, stating:
   "Gratuity of ₹X forfeited under Section 4(6)(a) due to moral turpitude - theft")

If employer fails to conduct proper inquiry OR forfeits gratuity without proving
misconduct, employee can challenge in Labour Court/Controlling Authority.
```

**Example - Forfeiture Process**:
```
CASE: Warehouse Manager Theft

Facts:
- Employee: Ramesh Kumar (10 years service, gratuity ₹4.5 lakh)
- Allegation: Theft of 50 cartons goods (₹1 lakh value) on 10th Jan 2024

Domestic Inquiry (Jan-Feb 2024):
- Charge sheet issued (15th Jan)
- Employee's explanation sought (48 hours)
- Inquiry conducted (25th Jan) - CCTV footage, witness testimony
- Inquiry report (5th Feb): Charges PROVEN (theft established)

Termination Order (10th Feb):
"You are dismissed from service with immediate effect for misconduct - theft of
company property (₹1 lakh value) on 10th Jan 2024, proven through domestic inquiry."

Gratuity Forfeiture Order (10th Feb):
"Your gratuity of ₹4,50,000 (for 10 years service) stands FORFEITED in full under
Section 4(6)(a) of the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972, as your dismissal is for an
act constituting moral turpitude (theft of company property)."

Employee's Remedy:
If employee believes:
(a) Inquiry was unfair (no opportunity to defend), OR
(b) Charges not proven (evidence insufficient), OR
(c) Theft not involving moral turpitude (e.g., took goods with intent to return)

Employee can file application with Controlling Authority (Assistant Labour
Commissioner) under Section 7 within 90 days of forfeiture order.

Controlling Authority will review inquiry proceedings, may order:
- Full gratuity payment (if forfeiture unjustified)
- Partial forfeiture (if damage proven but not moral turpitude)
- Uphold full forfeiture (if moral turpitude established)
```

## Payment Timeline & Process

### 1. Payment Deadline - Section 7(3)

**30-Day Payment Rule**:
```
Section 7(3): Gratuity shall be paid within 30 days from the date it becomes payable.

"Date it becomes payable" means:
(a) Resignation: Date of resignation acceptance + last working day
(b) Retirement: Date employee attains retirement age (58/60 years)
(c) Death: Date of death
(d) Disablement: Date of disablement certificate issued

If employer delays beyond 30 days WITHOUT valid reason:
Penalty: Simple interest @ 10% p.a. from 31st day till actual payment date
```

**Example - Delay Penalty**:
```
CASE: Delayed Gratuity Payment

Employee: Priya Sharma (resigned 30th Jun 2024)
Last Working Day: 30th Jun 2024
Gratuity: ₹5,00,000
Payment Due: 30th Jul 2024 (30 days from 30th Jun)

Actual Payment: 15th Sep 2024 (46 days delay = 15th Aug to 15th Sep)

DELAY INTEREST:
Principal: ₹5,00,000
Interest Rate: 10% p.a.
Delay Period: 46 days
Interest = ₹5,00,000 × 10% × (46/365) = ₹6,301

TOTAL PAYABLE: ₹5,00,000 + ₹6,301 = ₹5,06,301

Employee can claim ₹6,301 interest by filing application with Controlling Authority
(Section 7(4) - recovery of delayed gratuity + interest).
```

### 2. Nomination - Section 6

**Form F - Nomination**:
```
Every employee must nominate one or more persons (within family) to receive
gratuity in case of employee's death.

Eligible Nominees (Section 6):
- Spouse
- Children (sons, daughters, including adopted children)
- Dependent parents
- In absence of above: Any other dependent family member

Nomination Process:
1. Employee fills Form F (nomination form) at time of joining OR any time during service
2. Specifies nominee(s) name, relationship, share (if multiple nominees: 50% to
   spouse, 25% each to 2 children)
3. Submits to employer (employer maintains in personal file)
4. Can change nomination anytime (by submitting fresh Form F)

If NO Nomination:
Gratuity paid to legal heirs per Hindu Succession Act / Muslim Personal Law /
Indian Succession Act (depending on employee's religion)
Distribution: Equal share to all Class I legal heirs (spouse + children)
```

**Example - Nomination Scenario**:
```
EMPLOYEE: Rajesh Kumar (died in service after 12 years)
GRATUITY: ₹6,00,000

NOMINATION (Form F submitted in 2015):
- Nominee 1: Wife (Sunita Kumar) - 60% share = ₹3,60,000
- Nominee 2: Son (Arjun Kumar, age 15) - 20% share = ₹1,20,000
- Nominee 3: Daughter (Priya Kumar, age 12) - 20% share = ₹1,20,000

PAYMENT:
Employer pays:
- ₹3,60,000 to Sunita Kumar (wife's account)
- ₹1,20,000 to Arjun Kumar (minor, paid to guardian - mother Sunita)
- ₹1,20,000 to Priya Kumar (minor, paid to guardian - mother Sunita)

Practically: Employer may pay entire ₹6L to Sunita Kumar (as guardian of minor
children) with undertaking to distribute as per nomination.

TAX: Fully exempt (death case, no tax on gratuity received by nominees)
```

## Controlling Authority & Disputes

### 1. Controlling Authority - Section 3

**Appointment**:
```
State Government appoints Controlling Authority (typically Assistant Labour
Commissioner or Labour Commissioner) for each area.

Powers of Controlling Authority:
(a) Determine gratuity amount (if disputed between employer & employee)
(b) Order payment of gratuity + interest (if employer refuses/delays payment)
(c) Determine forfeiture validity (if employee challenges forfeiture)
(d) Recover gratuity as arrears of land revenue (if employer doesn't comply with order)
```

### 2. Filing Application - Section 7

**Employee's Application** (Within 90 Days):
```
If employer:
(a) Refuses to pay gratuity (disputes eligibility, service years, calculation), OR
(b) Delays payment beyond 30 days, OR
(c) Forfeits gratuity (employee believes unjustified)

Employee can file application with Controlling Authority (in Form I) within 90
days of:
- Date gratuity became payable (for non-payment cases)
- Date of forfeiture order (for forfeiture dispute cases)

LIMITATION: 90 days is STRICT (courts rarely condone delay beyond 90 days, employee
            loses right to claim if application filed late)
```

**Example - Dispute Case**:
```
CASE: Gratuity Calculation Dispute

Employee: Suresh Kumar (resigned 30th Jun 2024, last working day 30th Jun)
Service: 15 years 7 months (1st Dec 2008 to 30th Jun 2024)
Last Drawn Wages: ₹60,000/month (Basic ₹40,000 + HRA ₹20,000)

EMPLOYEE'S CLAIM:
Years: 16 years (15 years 7 months, rounded up as 7 months ≥ 6 months)
Wages: ₹60,000 (Basic + HRA)
Gratuity = (15 × ₹60,000 × 16) ÷ 26 = ₹5,53,846

EMPLOYER'S CALCULATION:
Years: 15 years (employer claims 7 months < 6 months, no rounding - WRONG)
Wages: ₹40,000 (Basic only, HRA excluded - CORRECT)
Gratuity = (15 × ₹40,000 × 15) ÷ 26 = ₹3,46,153

EMPLOYER PAID: ₹3,46,153 (on 15th Jul 2024, within 30 days)

EMPLOYEE'S GRIEVANCE:
1. Years wrongly calculated (should be 16, not 15)
2. Wages should include HRA (WRONG - gratuity on Basic only per Act)

CORRECT CALCULATION:
Years: 16 years (7 months ≥ 6 months, round up - EMPLOYEE CORRECT)
Wages: ₹40,000 (Basic only, HRA excluded - EMPLOYER CORRECT)
Gratuity = (15 × ₹40,000 × 16) ÷ 26 = ₹3,69,230

UNDERPAYMENT: ₹3,69,230 - ₹3,46,153 = ₹23,077

APPLICATION TO CONTROLLING AUTHORITY (Filed 20th Aug 2024):
Employee files Form I (within 90 days of 30th Jun = deadline 28th Sep)
Claims: ₹23,077 shortfall + 10% p.a. interest (from 31st Jul to payment date)

CONTROLLING AUTHORITY'S ORDER (15th Oct 2024):
1. Employee's service: 16 years (rounding up correct, employer's calculation wrong)
2. Wages: ₹40,000 Basic (employee's claim for HRA inclusion REJECTED, HRA not
   part of "wages" per Section 2(s))
3. Correct gratuity: ₹3,69,230
4. Shortfall: ₹23,077
5. Interest: ₹23,077 × 10% × (76 days / 365) = ₹480
6. TOTAL PAYABLE: ₹23,077 + ₹480 = ₹23,557

ORDER: Employer to pay ₹23,557 within 30 days (by 14th Nov 2024), failing which
       recovery as land revenue (property attachment, bank account seizure)
```

## Tax Implications - Section 10(10) Income Tax Act 1961

**Tax Exemption**:
```
Section 10(10): Gratuity received by an employee is exempt from income tax to
the extent of:

(a) For Government Employees: FULLY EXEMPT (no limit)

(b) For Private Sector Employees (covered by Payment of Gratuity Act 1972):
    Exempt up to ₹20,00,000

(c) For Employees NOT covered by Gratuity Act (e.g., <5 years service, <10
    employee establishment):
    Exempt up to LOWEST of:
    (i) ₹20 lakh, OR
    (ii) Last drawn salary × Years of service × 15/26, OR
    (iii) Actual gratuity received

Taxable Amount: Gratuity EXCEEDING exempt limit is taxed as "Salary Income" in
               the year of receipt.
```

**Example - Taxable Gratuity**:
```
EMPLOYEE: CEO (25 years service)
GRATUITY RECEIVED: ₹35,00,000 (employer paid voluntarily, exceeds ₹20L statutory ceiling)

TAX CALCULATION (FY 2024-25):
Exempt: ₹20,00,000 (Section 10(10) limit)
Taxable: ₹15,00,000 (₹35L - ₹20L)

Income Tax:
Assuming 30% slab (income >₹10 lakh):
Tax on ₹15L = 30% × ₹15L = ₹4,50,000
Add: 4% Health & Education Cess = ₹18,000
TOTAL TAX: ₹4,68,000

NET GRATUITY (After Tax): ₹35,00,000 - ₹4,68,000 = ₹30,32,000
```

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Standard Retirement Case

```
EMPLOYEE: Mr. Ramesh Iyer (Retirement at age 60)
JOINING DATE: 1st April 1994
RETIREMENT DATE: 31st March 2024
SERVICE: 30 years (exactly)
LAST DRAWN SALARY: ₹1,20,000/month
- Basic: ₹60,000
- HRA: ₹30,000
- Special Allowance: ₹30,000

GRATUITY CALCULATION:
Last Drawn Wages: ₹60,000 (Basic only)
Years of Service: 30 years
Gratuity = (15 × ₹60,000 × 30) ÷ 26
         = (₹2,70,00,000) ÷ 26
         = ₹10,38,461.54
         = ₹10,38,462 (rounded)

CEILING CHECK: ₹10,38,462 < ₹20 lakh limit → No capping

TAX: Fully exempt (₹10.38L < ₹20L limit)

PAYMENT:
Due Date: 30th April 2024 (30 days from 31st March)
Paid On: 25th April 2024 (within deadline, no interest)
Mode: NEFT to Mr. Ramesh Iyer's bank account

NET RECEIPT: ₹10,38,462 (tax-free)

ADDITIONAL BENEFITS (Separate from Gratuity):
- EPF: ₹45 lakh (accumulated over 30 years)
- Leave Encashment: 240 days × ₹60,000/26 = ₹5,53,846
- Pension (if covered by EPS): ₹25,000/month (50% of average salary for last 60 months)

TOTAL RETIREMENT CORPUS: ₹10.38L (gratuity) + ₹45L (EPF) + ₹5.54L (leave) = ₹60.92 lakh
```

### Example 2: Resignation After 7 Years

```
EMPLOYEE: Ms. Anjali Verma (Resignation - better opportunity)
JOINING DATE: 1st August 2017
RESIGNATION DATE: 31st October 2024 (Last working day)
SERVICE: 7 years 3 months
LAST DRAWN SALARY: ₹85,000/month
- Basic: ₹45,000
- HRA: ₹22,500
- Special Allowance: ₹17,500

GRATUITY CALCULATION:
Years of Service: 7 years 3 months
Rounding: 3 months < 6 months → DO NOT round up → 7 years

Last Drawn Wages: ₹45,000 (Basic only)
Gratuity = (15 × ₹45,000 × 7) ÷ 26
         = (₹47,25,000) ÷ 26
         = ₹1,81,730.77
         = ₹1,81,731 (rounded)

TAX: Fully exempt (₹1.82L < ₹20L limit)

PAYMENT:
Due Date: 30th November 2024 (30 days from 31st October)
Paid On: 28th November 2024 (within deadline)

NET RECEIPT: ₹1,81,731 (tax-free)

NOTE: If Ms. Anjali had resigned at exactly 7 years 6 months (instead of 7 years
      3 months), gratuity would be:
      (15 × ₹45,000 × 8) ÷ 26 = ₹2,07,692 (₹25,961 more due to rounding up to 8 years)

ADVICE: Employees close to 0.5 year mark (6 months) should consider delaying
        resignation by 1-2 months to benefit from rounding (significant gratuity increase)
```

## Compliance Checklist

### For Employers

- [ ] Verify applicability (establishment with ≥10 employees covered by Act)
- [ ] Maintain service records (date of joining, continuous service calculation)
- [ ] Collect Form F (nomination) from all employees at joining
- [ ] Update nominations periodically (especially after marriage, childbirth)
- [ ] Calculate gratuity correctly (15 days wages × years ÷ 26, Basic only)
- [ ] Pay within 30 days of due date (avoid 10% p.a. interest penalty)
- [ ] If forfeiting gratuity, conduct proper domestic inquiry (prove moral turpitude/damage)
- [ ] Issue gratuity payment statement (showing calculation: wages, years, amount)
- [ ] Maintain gratuity register (employee-wise payments, 3 years record)
- [ ] File annual return if required by state (some states mandate annual gratuity return)

### For Employees

- [ ] Submit Form F (nomination) within 1 month of joining (protect family in case of death)
- [ ] Update nomination after life events (marriage, divorce, childbirth)
- [ ] Maintain service records (offer letter, confirmation letter, salary slips proving continuous service)
- [ ] Verify gratuity calculation (use formula: 15 × Basic × Years ÷ 26)
- [ ] Check payment within 30 days of last working day (claim interest if delayed)
- [ ] If gratuity denied/short-paid, file application with Controlling Authority within 90 days (strict deadline)
- [ ] Retain gratuity payment advice for tax filing (claim Section 10(10) exemption in ITR)

## Landmark Cases

### 1. Excel Wear v. Union of India (SC 1978)

**Facts**:
- Excel Wear (garment manufacturer) challenged Payment of Gratuity Act 1972 as unconstitutional
- Argued: Gratuity is employer's voluntary payment (gift/bounty), government cannot mandate it

**Constitutional Challenge**:
- Violates Article 19(1)(g) (freedom to carry on business) by imposing statutory gratuity obligation
- Violates Article 14 (equality) by applying only to establishments with ≥10 employees (discriminatory)

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **Gratuity is Deferred Wage** (Not Employer's Bounty): Gratuity is payment for services rendered over long period, employee has earned it through years of service (not employer's gift)
2. **Statutory Obligation Valid**: Government can mandate gratuity as social security measure (reasonable restriction under Article 19(6))
3. **10-Employee Threshold Reasonable**: Classification based on establishment size is reasonable (small employers <10 exempted due to capacity constraints)

**Principle**: Gratuity is employee's RIGHT (not employer's charity), statutory obligation to pay gratuity is constitutionally valid

### 2. Vishwanath v. Sanjay Spinners (SC 1992)

**Facts**:
- Employee (15 years service, gratuity ₹3 lakh) dismissed for theft (₹50,000 stolen)
- Employer forfeited FULL gratuity (₹3L) under Section 4(6)(a) - moral turpitude
- Employee challenged: Forfeiture disproportionate (theft ₹50K, forfeiture ₹3L)

**Employee's Argument**:
- Section 4(6)(a) allows forfeiture "to the extent of damage" (₹50K), not full gratuity
- Full forfeiture unreasonable, violates proportionality

**Supreme Court Held**:
1. **Moral Turpitude Permits Full Forfeiture**: Section 4(6)(a) has two categories:
   - Property damage/negligence: Forfeiture limited to extent of damage
   - Moral turpitude (theft, fraud): Full forfeiture permitted (not limited to damage amount)
2. **Theft = Moral Turpitude**: Theft involves dishonesty, breach of trust (moral turpitude), permits full gratuity forfeiture regardless of stolen amount
3. **Forfeiture Upheld**: ₹3 lakh gratuity forfeiture valid (theft justifies full forfeiture)

**Principle**: Misconduct involving moral turpitude (theft, fraud, sexual harassment) permits FULL gratuity forfeiture, not limited to actual damage amount
