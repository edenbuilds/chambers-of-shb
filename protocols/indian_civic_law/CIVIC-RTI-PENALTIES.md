# RTI Penalties and Enforcement

## Purpose
Understand penalty provisions under RTI Act for non-compliance by PIOs, procedure for seeking penalties, and protection of whistleblowers/RTI activists.

## Activation Triggers
- PIO deliberately refuses valid information
- PIO fails to respond within 30 days
- PIO provides false/misleading information
- Need to seek compensation for RTI violation
- Understanding protection for RTI activists

---

## Section 20: Penalties

### Penalty Framework

**Section 20(1) - Penalty on PIO**:
> "Where the Central Information Commission or the State Information Commission... comes to a conclusion that the Central Public Information Officer or the State Public Information Officer... has, without any reasonable cause, refused to receive an application for information or has not furnished information within the time specified under sub-section (1) of section 7 or malafidel denied the request for information or knowingly given incorrect, incomplete or misleading information or destroyed information which was the subject of the request or obstructed in any manner in furnishing the information, it shall impose a penalty of two hundred and fifty rupees each day till application is received or information is furnished... not exceeding twenty-five thousand rupees."

**Conditions for Penalty**:
1. **PIO's default** (refused/delayed/gave false information)
2. **Without reasonable cause** (not genuine error)
3. **Malafide denial** OR **knowingly gave incorrect information**

**Penalty Amount**:
- **₹250 per day** of delay
- **Maximum**: ₹25,000

**Authority**: Only CIC/SIC can impose penalty (not FAA).

---

### Grounds for Penalty

**1. Refusal to Accept Application**
PIO refuses to accept RTI application citing frivolous grounds.

**Example**: PIO says "This is not maintainable" and refuses to accept.

**2. Non-Furnishing Within Time (Section 7(1))**
PIO does not provide information within 30 days without reasonable cause.

**Reasonable Cause (Exemptions)**:
- Genuine difficulty in compilation
- Third-party information (Section 11 procedure delays)
- Natural calamity affecting office functioning

**Not Reasonable**:
- Staff shortage (unless acute)
- Volume of work
- "I forgot"

**3. Malafide Denial**
PIO refuses information citing exemption when:
- Exemption clearly not applicable
- Public interest override (Section 8(2)) ignored
- Deliberate misapplication of law

**Example**: Denying FIR copy citing "investigation impeded" when FIR is public document.

**4. Knowingly Incorrect Information**
PIO provides false information knowing it's false.

**Example**: PIO says "Tender awarded to X" when actually awarded to Y (and PIO knows).

**5. Destroyed Information**
PIO destroys documents that were subject of RTI request.

**Example**: RTI filed on Jan 1 seeking file. PIO burns file on Jan 15.

**Criminal Liability**: May also attract charges under IPC (destruction of evidence).

**6. Obstruction**
PIO creates hurdles in providing information.

**Examples**:
- Demands excessive fees
- Keeps changing excuses
- Provides information in unreadable format deliberately
- Denies inspection without reason

## Procedure for Seeking Penalty

### Step 1: Exhaust First Appeal
File first appeal to FAA if PIO violates RTI.

**Note**: FAA **cannot impose penalty**. Only CIC/SIC can.

**Why File FAA First**:
- Procedural requirement (exhaust lower remedy)
- FAA may direct PIO compliance
- Creates record of violation

### Step 2: File Second Appeal/Complaint to CIC/SIC

**In Second Appeal**, include:
- Request for penalty on PIO
- Specify grounds (Section 20 violation)
- Request compensation (if applicable)

**Application Format**:
```
To,
The Central/State Information Commission
[Address]

Subject: Second Appeal + Complaint for Penalty under Section 20, RTI Act 2005

RTI Application No: [Number]
Date of RTI: [Date]
PIO Name: [Name]
Public Authority: [Name]

FACTS:
1. I filed RTI on [date] seeking [information].
2. PIO failed to respond within 30 days (deadline: [date]).
3. I filed first appeal on [date]. FAA disposed appeal on [date] directing PIO to provide information.
4. Despite FAA order, PIO has still not provided information (as of [date]).

VIOLATIONS BY PIO:
1. Non-furnishing information within time (Section 7(1)) - violation since [date]
2. Non-compliance with FAA order - deliberate obstruction

RELIEF SOUGHT:
1. Direct PIO to immediately provide information sought
2. Impose penalty of ₹250/day from [start date] to [end date] = ₹[amount] on PIO under Section 20(1)
3. Award compensation of ₹[amount] for mental agony caused by PIO's willful denial

[If PIO gave false information]:
4. Initiate departmental proceedings against PIO for giving knowingly false information

GROUNDS FOR PENALTY:
The delay/refusal is:
- Without reasonable cause (no evidence of natural calamity, genuine difficulty)
- Malafide (PIO deliberately ignored FAA order)
- Deliberate obstruction of citizen's fundamental right

Documents Enclosed:
1. Copy of RTI application
2. Copy of first appeal
3. Copy of FAA order
4. Correspondence with PIO (if any)

[Signature]
[Name & Address]
```

### Step 3: CIC/SIC Hearing

**Notice**: CIC/SIC issues notice to PIO, FAA, applicant

**Hearing**:
- Personal appearance or written submission
- PIO must explain reasons for default
- Burden on PIO to prove "reasonable cause"

**CIC/SIC Decision**:
- If satisfied PIO violated without reasonable cause → **Impose penalty**
- If PIO proves reasonable cause → **No penalty**

**Factors CIC Considers**:
1. Was delay/refusal deliberate?
2. Did PIO have reasonable cause?
3. Was information subsequently provided (after appeal)?
4. PIO's explanation credible?
5. Past record of PIO (repeat offender?)

## Landmark CIC Orders on Penalties

**1. Heavy Penalties for Willful Denial**

*Khanapuram Gandaiah v. CPIO, DGP Office* (CIC Decision):
CIC imposed ₹25,000 penalty (maximum) on PIO for:
- Deliberate non-response to RTI
- Ignoring FAA order
- Pattern of non-compliance

**2. No Penalty for Genuine Errors**

*Shri Ram v. CPIO, Ministry of Finance*:
CIC refused penalty where:
- PIO genuinely believed exemption applied
- Information provided on appeal
- No malafide intent

**3. Penalty for False Information**

*Bhim Singh v. CPIO, PWD*:
PIO said "Tender awarded to X for ₹10 lakhs." Actually awarded to Y for ₹15 lakhs.
CIC: Knowingly false information → ₹15,000 penalty.

**4. Repeat Offenders**

*Multiple complaints against same PIO*:
CIC may:
- Impose higher penalty (₹25,000)
- Recommend departmental action
- Direct authority to replace PIO

## Penalty Calculation

**Formula**:
```
Penalty = ₹250 × Number of days of delay
Maximum = ₹25,000 (i.e., capped at 100 days)
```

**Example 1: Simple Delay**
- RTI filed: January 1, 2024
- Response due: January 31, 2024
- PIO responded: March 15, 2024
- Delay: January 31 - March 15 = 44 days
- Penalty: ₹250 × 44 = **₹11,000**

**Example 2: Capped at Maximum**
- Delay: 150 days
- Theoretical penalty: ₹250 × 150 = ₹37,500
- **Actual penalty**: ₹25,000 (statutory cap)

**Example 3: Partial Compliance**
- RTI sought 5 documents
- PIO provided 3 on time, delayed 2 by 30 days
- CIC may impose proportionate penalty: ₹250 × 30 × (2/5) = ₹3,000

## Compensation to Applicant (Discretionary)

**CIC Power**: Beyond penalty on PIO, CIC may award **compensation** to applicant for:
- Mental agony
- Loss of opportunity
- Harassment

**Not Statutory**: RTI Act doesn't explicitly provide compensation. CIC derives power from general remedial jurisdiction.

**Rare**: CICs rarely award compensation. More common: Recommend departmental action.

**Example Cases**:
*Some SICs* have awarded ₹500 - ₹5,000 compensation in extreme cases (applicant had to make multiple visits, incurred costs, lost job opportunity due to information denial).

---

## Enforcement of Penalty

**Collection**:
- Penalty treated as **arrears of land revenue**
- Recovered from PIO's salary
- Public authority (employer) responsible for recovery

**Non-Payment**:
- CIC may issue recovery certificate to District Collector
- Coercive recovery (attachment, auction) possible

**Departmental Action**:
- Beyond penalty, CIC may recommend:
  - Warning to PIO
  - Adverse entry in service record
  - Suspension
  - Initiation of disciplinary proceedings

**Practical Reality**: Enforcement weak. Many PIOs don't pay penalties. Follow-up by applicant required.

## Protection of Action (Section 21)

### Whistleblower/RTI Activist Protection

**Section 21**:
> "No suit, prosecution or other legal proceeding shall lie against any person for anything which is in good faith done or intended to be done under this Act or any rule made thereunder."

**Protection For**:
1. **PIOs/FAAs**: Acting in good faith (cannot be sued for disclosing information)
2. **Information Commissioners**: For decisions in good faith
3. **Applicants**: Using RTI for legitimate purpose

**"Good Faith"**: Honest intention, no malafide

**Examples**:

**PIO Protected**:
PIO discloses information believing Section 8 exemption not applicable. Third party sues PIO for breach of confidence.
→ **PIO protected** under Section 21 (acted in good faith).

**Applicant Protected**:
RTI applicant files RTI exposing corruption. Official files defamation suit.
→ **Applicant protected** (if information truthful, used for public interest).

### Attacks on RTI Activists

**Ground Reality**: RTI activists face:
- Threats
- Physical attacks
- False criminal cases
- Social boycott

**Legal Protection**:
- **Section 21**: Limited (only for legal proceedings, not physical attacks)
- **IPC Sections**: Assault, criminal intimidation (if reported)
- **State Protection**: Some states have RTI activist protection schemes

**Landmark Case**:
*Satendra Kumar v. CIC* (2010):
Supreme Court held RTI is fundamental right. Any obstruction violates Article 19(1)(a). State must protect RTI users.

**Practical Steps** for RTI Activists:
1. Inform local police of RTI filing (if sensitive matter)
2. Use registered post (creates evidence trail)
3. Share RTI copies with NGOs/media
4. File FIR immediately if threatened
5. Use CIC platform to report threats

## When Penalty NOT Imposed

**Reasonable Cause Examples**:

**1. Third-Party Consultation (Section 11)**:
Information involves third party. PIO followed Section 11 procedure (notice, representation). Delay justified.

**2. Voluminous, Scattered Information**:
RTI seeks 10 years of records from multiple departments. PIO took 60 days (reasonable given volume). No penalty if PIO showed diligence.

**3. Legal Complexity**:
Information involves complex legal interpretation (e.g., whether exemption applies). PIO sought legal opinion. Delay justified.

**4. Natural Calamity/Emergency**:
Office flooded, records damaged. PIO couldn't respond within 30 days. Force majeure - no penalty.

**5. Genuine Error (Non-Malafide)**:
PIO mistakenly believed information exempt. First-time offender, corrected on appeal. CIC may warn, not penalize.

## Strategic Use of Penalty Provision

### 1. Deterrent Effect
**Mention penalty in RTI application** itself:
"Under Section 20, failure to respond within 30 days may attract penalty of ₹250/day up to ₹25,000."

**Effect**: PIOs often respond promptly when aware of penalty risk.

### 2. Seek Penalty Proactively
In second appeal, **always seek penalty** if PIO violated without cause.

**Why**:
- Deters future violations by same PIO
- Creates precedent (other PIOs in department become cautious)
- Justice for applicant's harassment

### 3. Document Violations Meticulously
**Evidence for CIC**:
- Certified postal receipts (proof of RTI filing)
- Acknowledgments
- Reminder letters sent to PIO
- Unanswered emails
- Visit logs (if visited office personally)

**Better evidence = Higher penalty likelihood**

### 4. Highlight Patterns
If **same PIO** violated RTI multiple times:
- File consolidated complaint to CIC
- Cite past violations
- Seek maximum penalty + departmental action

**CIC takes repeat offenders seriously.**

---

## Limitations

**1. Weak Enforcement**:
Even if penalty imposed, recovery is poor. PIOs often don't pay.

**2. No Personal Accountability for Officials**:
Senior officials directing PIO to refuse information face no penalty (only PIO penalized).

**3. No Compensation**:
Applicant suffers loss (time, opportunity) but rarely gets compensation.

**4. Delays in CIC**:
CIC backlog means penalty order comes years later (loses deterrent effect).

**5. No Criminal Prosecution**:
Section 20 is civil penalty. Even for deliberate destruction of information, no automatic criminal case (requires separate FIR under IPC).
