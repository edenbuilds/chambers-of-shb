# Local Self-Government - Panchayats and Municipalities

## Purpose
Understand local self-government structure (Panchayats and Municipalities), their powers, functions, and how citizens can engage with/seek redressal from local authorities.

---

## Constitutional Framework

### 73rd Amendment (1992): Panchayati Raj
**Articles 243-243O**: Established Panchayats (rural local self-government).

**Three-Tier Structure** (Article 243C):
1. **Gram Panchayat** (Village level)
2. **Panchayat Samiti / Block Panchayat** (Intermediate level)
3. **Zila Parishad** (District level)

**Key Features**:
- **Direct elections** every 5 years (Article 243E)
- **Reservations**: 1/3 seats for women, SC/ST proportional reservation (Article 243D)
- **State Election Commission** conducts elections (Article 243K)
- **State Finance Commission** recommends financial devolution (Article 243I)

**Functions** (11th Schedule - 29 items):
1. Agriculture, land improvement
2. Minor irrigation, water management
3. Animal husbandry, dairy, poultry
4. Fisheries
5. Social forestry, farm forestry
6. Minor forest produce
7. Small scale industries
8. Khadi, village, cottage industries
9. Rural housing
10. Drinking water
11. Fuel and fodder
12. Roads, culverts, bridges, ferries
13. Rural electrification
14. Non-conventional energy sources
15. Poverty alleviation programmes
16. Education (primary, secondary)
17. Technical training, vocational education
18. Adult and non-formal education
19. Libraries
20. Cultural activities
21. Markets and fairs
22. Health and sanitation
23. Family welfare
24. Women and child development
25. Social welfare
26. Welfare of weaker sections (SC/ST/OBC)
27. Public distribution system
28. Maintenance of community assets
29. **Any other subject** assigned by state law

---

### 74th Amendment (1992): Municipalities
**Articles 243P-243ZG**: Established municipalities (urban local self-government).

**Types of Municipalities** (Article 243Q):
1. **Nagar Panchayat** (Transitional area - village → town)
2. **Municipal Council** (Smaller urban area)
3. **Municipal Corporation** (Larger urban area)

**Key Features**:
- Direct elections every 5 years (Article 243U)
- Reservations for SC/ST, women (1/3), OBC (Article 243T)
- State Election Commission (Article 243K)
- Wards committees in cities >3 lakh population (Article 243S)

**Functions** (12th Schedule - 18 items):
1. Urban planning, town planning
2. Regulation of land use, construction of buildings
3. Planning for economic and social development
4. Roads and bridges
5. Water supply for domestic, industrial, commercial purposes
6. Public health, sanitation, solid waste management
7. Fire services
8. Urban forestry, environmental protection
9. Safeguarding interests of weaker sections
10. Slum improvement and upgradation
11. Urban poverty alleviation
12. Provision of urban amenities and facilities
13. Promotion of cultural, educational, aesthetic aspects
14. Burials and burial grounds, cremations, crematoria, electric crematori
15. Cattle pounds, prevention of cruelty to animals
16. Vital statistics (births, deaths registration)
17. Public amenities (street lighting, parking lots, bus stops, public conveniences)
18. Regulation of slaughter houses and tanneries

## Powers of Local Bodies

### Revenue Sources

**Panchayats** (Article 243H):
- Land revenue
- Taxes on professions, trades, callings
- Taxes on animals, boats
- Toll taxes
- Market fees
- Grants from state government
- Assigned revenues

**Municipalities** (Article 243X):
- **Property tax** (major source)
- Octroi (entry tax on goods)
- Advertisement tax
- Water/sewerage charges
- Building plan approval fees
- Trade license fees
- Market fees
- Parking fees
- Grants from state/central government

**Reality**: Most local bodies financially weak, depend heavily on state grants.

---

### Regulatory Powers

**1. Building Permissions** (Municipal Corporation Act):
- Citizen must obtain permission before construction
- Submit building plan
- Compliance with zoning regulations, setback rules, FAR (Floor Area Ratio)
- Timeline: 30-60 days for approval

**Citizen's Right**:
- If plan complies with rules, approval **mandatory** (not discretionary)
- If denied, appeal to municipal authority OR file writ (mandamus)

**2. Trade Licenses**:
- Shops, businesses must obtain trade license
- Health, safety conditions compliance
- Annual renewal

**3. Water/Electricity Connections**:
- Municipality supplies water (in some areas)
- Must provide connection if area has supply line

**4. Sanitation, Garbage Collection**:
- Municipality's **mandatory duty**
- Citizen can file complaint if not collecting garbage
- Writ petition possible if persistent violation

## Citizen Engagement with Local Government

### 1. Gram Sabha (Village Assembly)

**Article 243A**: Gram Sabha = Body consisting of all registered voters in village.

**Powers**:
- Approve plans, programmes, projects
- Identify beneficiaries for welfare schemes
- Monitor implementation
- Financial audit of Gram Panchayat accounts

**Meetings**: Minimum 2 per year (varies by state law)

**Citizen Participation**:
- **Any voter** can attend Gram Sabha
- Voice concerns, suggestions
- Hold Panchayat accountable

**Example**: Gram Sabha can question Panchayat on MGNREGA (rural employment scheme) implementation, fund utilization.

---

### 2. Ward Committees (Urban)

**Article 243S**: Cities >3 lakh population must have ward committees.

**Composition**:
- 1 or more wards
- Councillor of ward (chairperson)
- Community representatives

**Functions**:
- Local development planning
- Feedback on municipal services
- Grievance redressal

**Citizen Participation**: Attend ward committee meetings, raise local issues (roads, water supply, garbage).

---

### 3. RTI to Local Bodies

**Local bodies are "Public Authorities"** under RTI Act.

**Information You Can Seek**:
- Property tax assessment records
- Building plan approvals granted in your area
- Water supply timings, quality test reports
- Road repair budgets and contractors
- Waste management contracts
- Official's attendance, salaries

**Example RTI**: "Provide copy of building plan approval granted to plot no. [X] in [locality] on [date]."

## Common Grievances and Remedies

### Problem 1: Property Tax Dispute

**Issue**: Municipality levies excessive property tax / wrongly calculates.

**Remedy**:
1. **Appeal to Assessment Officer** (within 30 days of notice)
2. **Revision to Municipal Commissioner**
3. **Writ Petition** (if tax levy illegal or arbitrary - Article 226)

**Legal Ground**: Article 265 - "No tax shall be levied except by authority of law." If tax levy violates municipal act, unconstitutional.

---

### Problem 2: Building Permission Denied

**Issue**: Municipality refuses building permission despite plan complying with rules.

**Remedy**:
1. **Appeal within municipal hierarchy** (if provided in state municipal act)
2. **Writ Petition (Mandamus)**: Compel municipality to grant permission

**Legal Ground**: If plan complies with all rules, municipality has **mandatory duty** to approve (not discretionary).

**Case Law**: *Hardeep Singh v. Municipal Corporation* - If all statutory conditions met, permission cannot be arbitrarily denied.

---

### Problem 3: Water Supply Disruption

**Issue**: Municipality not providing water supply for weeks in your area.

**Remedy**:
1. **Written complaint to municipal office** (Executive Engineer, Water Supply Dept)
2. **RTI**: Ask for reasons for disruption, action plan to restore
3. **Public Grievance Portal** (state/central)
4. **Writ Petition (Mandamus)**: If municipality fails to act, compel water supply

**Legal Ground**: Right to water is part of Article 21 (right to life). Municipality has constitutional duty.

**Case Law**: *Subhash Kumar v. State of Bihar* (1991) - Right to potable water flows from Article 21.

---

### Problem 4: Garbage Not Collected

**Issue**: Municipal garbage van not coming to your area.

**Remedy**:
1. **Complaint to Sanitation Department**
2. **Social Media / Local Councillor**: Often effective
3. **Swachh Bharat Helpline** (1969 - national)
4. **Writ Petition** (if persistent non-compliance)

**Legal Ground**: Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016 (under Environment Protection Act) mandate municipal garbage collection.

---

### Problem 5: Road Repair Delays

**Issue**: Roads in your ward in poor condition, potholes not repaired.

**Remedy**:
1. **Ward Councillor**: Raise in ward committee / council meeting
2. **RTI**: Ask for road repair budget allocation, work orders issued
3. **Public Complaint on Municipal App/Portal**
4. **Writ Petition**: If deliberate negligence causes accidents (Article 21 - right to safe roads)

**Case Law**: *Hindustan Construction v. Union of India* - Government has duty to maintain public infrastructure.

## Practical Tips

### 1. Know Your Local Representatives

- **Gram Panchayat**: Sarpanch, Ward Members
- **Municipality**: Councillor (ward), Mayor, Municipal Commissioner

**Contact Details**: Available on municipal website, notice boards.

**Approach**: Before legal remedies, approach elected representative (often quickest resolution).

---

### 2. Use Mobile Apps

**Central Government**:
- **MyGov** (mygov.in): Citizen engagement, grievances
- **CPGRAMS** (pgportal.gov.in): Public grievance portal

**State/Municipal Apps**:
Most cities have mobile apps (e.g., MyBMC for Mumbai, MCD app for Delhi):
- Register complaints (garbage, streetlight, potholes)
- Track status
- Pay property tax online

---

### 3. Attend Meetings

**Gram Sabha** (rural): Participate actively, hold Panchayat accountable.

**Ward Committee** (urban): Raise local issues, demand action.

**Open House** (Mayor/Commissioner): Some cities have monthly open houses - citizens can directly meet officials.

---

### 4. RTI for Transparency

**Strategic RTI Queries**:
- "Provide list of contractors awarded contracts in [ward] in last 6 months with contract amounts."
  → Exposes corruption if same contractor gets all work.

- "Provide quality test reports of drinking water supplied in [area] in last 3 months."
  → Forces municipality to test water if not testing.

- "Provide attendance records of sanitation workers assigned to [ward] for last month."
  → Holds workers accountable.
