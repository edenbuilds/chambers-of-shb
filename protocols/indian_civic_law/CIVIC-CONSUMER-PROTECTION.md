# Consumer Protection - Rights and Remedies

## Purpose
Understand consumer rights under Consumer Protection Act 2019, three-tier consumer forum structure (District/State/National Commissions), procedure for filing consumer complaints, deficiency in service vs. defective goods, and strategic use of consumer forums for quick, cost-effective relief.

## Activation Triggers
- Defective product purchased (manufacturing defect, not as advertised)
- Deficiency in service (bank, telecom, hospital, restaurant, e-commerce, courier, etc.)
- Unfair trade practice (misleading advertisement, false representation)
- Overcharging, billing errors
- Non-delivery of goods/services despite payment
- Medical negligence (private hospital)
- Real estate builder delays, deficiencies

---

## Who is a Consumer?

### Section 2(7): Consumer Definition

**Consumer** means any person who:

**1. Buys Goods** for consideration (paid money):
> "buys any goods for a consideration which has been paid or promised or partly paid and partly promised, or under any system of deferred payment..."

**AND uses such goods** (can be buyer OR any person using goods with buyer's permission).

**2. Hires Services** for consideration:
> "hires or avails of any services for a consideration which has been paid or promised or partly paid and partly promised, or under any system of deferred payment..."

**Key Requirements**:
- **Consideration paid**: Free goods/services NOT covered (except free sample if defective causes injury)
- **For personal use**: NOT for commercial resale (but small traders covered if purchase for self-use, not resale)

---

### Who is NOT a Consumer?

**Exclusions**:

**1. Goods for Resale**:
If you buy goods to resell (commercial purpose), NOT a consumer.

**Example**:
- ✗ Trader buys 100 mobile phones for selling in shop → NOT consumer
- ✓ Trader buys 1 mobile phone for personal use → Consumer

**2. Goods for Commercial Use**:
If goods used for earning livelihood (commercial use), generally NOT consumer (BUT exceptions exist - see below).

**Example**:
- ✗ Taxi driver buys car for taxi business → Commercial use → NOT consumer (earlier position)
- ✓ **NEW**: Under 2019 Act, self-employed persons can be consumers if not large-scale commercial use

**3. Free Goods/Services** (unless defective causes injury):
- ✗ Free medical camp → Generally NOT consumer
- ✓ If free goods cause injury → Can claim compensation

---

### Self-Employed / Small Traders

**2019 Act Clarification** (Section 2(7) Explanation):
> "For the purposes of this clause, 'commercial purpose' does not include use by a person of goods bought and used by him exclusively for the purpose of earning his livelihood, by means of self-employment."

**Implication**:
- ✓ Auto-rickshaw driver buying auto for self-driving → Consumer
- ✓ Tailor buying sewing machine for tailoring → Consumer
- ✗ Company buying 50 vehicles for fleet → NOT consumer (large-scale commercial)

## What Complaints Can Be Filed?

### Section 2(9): Deficiency in Service

**Deficiency** means:
> "any fault, imperfection, shortcoming or inadequacy in the quality, nature and manner of performance which is required to be maintained by or under any law... or has been undertaken to be performed by a person in pursuance of a contract..."

**Examples**:
- Bank wrongly debits your account
- Telecom operator bills for services not used
- Airline cancels flight without notice/refund
- Hospital provides negligent treatment
- Builder delivers flat with defects, delays possession
- E-commerce site delivers damaged product
- Restaurant serves contaminated food

---

### Defective Goods

**Manufacturing Defect**:
- Product doesn't function as warranted
- Product causes injury due to defect

**Examples**:
- Mobile phone stops working within warranty
- Pressure cooker explodes due to manufacturing defect
- Electrical appliance causes fire

---

### Unfair Trade Practice (Section 2(47))

Includes:
1. **False/Misleading Representation**:
   - Advertisement claims product does X, but doesn't
   - "Ayurvedic cure for diabetes" (false claim)
   - "Swiss made" watch, actually made in China

2. **False Offer of Bargain Price**:
   - "50% off" but price increased before discount

3. **Non-Compliance with Standards**:
   - Product doesn't meet ISI/BIS standards claimed

4. **Hoarding/Destruction** of goods to raise prices

5. **Adulteration**: Selling adulterated food, drugs

---

### Restrictive Trade Practice

**Limiting supply** to raise prices OR impose unjustified conditions.

**Example**: Manufacturer restricts spare parts supply to force purchase of new product.

---

## Consumer Rights (Section 2(9))

**Six Consumer Rights**:

1. **Right to Safety**: Protection against hazardous goods/services
2. **Right to Information**: Complete information about goods/services
3. **Right to Choose**: Access to variety of goods/services at competitive prices
4. **Right to be Heard**: Consumer interests represented in policy-making
5. **Right to Seek Redressal**: Compensation for defective goods/deficient services
6. **Right to Consumer Education**: Awareness of rights

---

## Three-Tier Consumer Forum Structure

### District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission

**Jurisdiction** (Section 34):
- **Value**: Up to ₹1 crore
- **Territorial**: Where complainant resides OR where opposite party's office (complainant's choice)

**Composition**: President (judicial) + 2 members

**Location**: Every district (600+ commissions across India)

---

### State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission

**Jurisdiction** (Section 47):
- **Original**: Claims **above ₹1 crore, up to ₹10 crore**
- **Appellate**: Appeals from District Commission orders

**Composition**: President (judicial) + minimum 4 members

**Location**: Every state capital (35 commissions)

---

### National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC)

**Jurisdiction** (Section 58):
- **Original**: Claims **above ₹10 crore**
- **Appellate**: Appeals from State Commission orders

**Composition**: President (SC/HC judge) + minimum 4 members

**Location**: New Delhi

---

### Determining Which Forum

**Value of Claim**:
- **Up to ₹1 crore**: District Commission
- **₹1 crore - ₹10 crore**: State Commission
- **Above ₹10 crore**: National Commission

**Appeals**:
- District Commission → **State Commission** (within 45 days)
- State Commission → **National Commission** (within 30 days)
- National Commission → **Supreme Court** (within 30 days)

## Procedure for Filing Consumer Complaint

### Step 1: Determine Jurisdiction

**Check**:
1. **Am I a consumer?** (bought for personal use, paid consideration)
2. **Is there deficiency/defect?** (service deficient OR goods defective)
3. **Value of claim?** (determines District/State/National forum)
4. **Within limitation?** (2 years from cause of action - when deficiency/defect occurred OR when complainant knew)

**Cause of Action**:
- Goods: Date of purchase OR date defect discovered
- Services: Date of deficiency OR when complainant became aware

**Example**: Mobile purchased Jan 2023, defect discovered March 2023 → Complaint can be filed till March 2025 (2 years from defect discovered).

---

### Step 2: Send Legal Notice (Optional but Recommended)

**Before filing complaint**, send legal notice to opposite party:

**Format**:
```
To,
[Company Name / Service Provider]
[Address]

Subject: Legal Notice - Deficiency in Service / Defective Goods

Dear Sir/Madam,

I purchased / availed service [description] on [date] for ₹[amount].

The goods/service suffered from following defect/deficiency:
[Describe: product stopped working / service not provided as promised / billing error, etc.]

I contacted your customer care on [dates], but no resolution provided.

This constitutes deficiency in service / defective goods under Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

You are hereby called upon to:
1. Replace the defective product / rectify deficiency
2. Refund the amount paid: ₹[amount]
3. Compensate for mental agony and harassment: ₹[amount]

Failing which, I shall be constrained to file consumer complaint without further notice. All my rights, remedies, and contentions are expressly reserved.

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
[Address]
Date: [Date]
```

**Send by**: Registered Post / Speed Post (with Ack Due) OR Email (for record)

**If No Response in 15-30 Days**: Proceed to file complaint.

---

### Step 3: Draft Consumer Complaint

**Format** (Consumer Protection Rules, 2020):

```
BEFORE THE DISTRICT / STATE / NATIONAL CONSUMER DISPUTES REDRESSAL COMMISSION
[LOCATION]

CONSUMER COMPLAINT NO. _____ OF 2024

IN THE MATTER OF:
Consumer Protection Act, 2019

[Your Name, Address] ... Complainant

Versus

[Company / Service Provider Name, Address] ... Opposite Party

COMPLAINT UNDER SECTION 35 / 48 / 59 OF THE CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT, 2019

MOST RESPECTFULLY SUBMITTED AS UNDER:

1. Complainant's Details:
   Name: [Name]
   Address: [Complete Address]
   Occupation: [Occupation]
   Email: [Email]
   Mobile: [Mobile]

2. Opposite Party's Details:
   Name: [Company Name]
   Address: [Registered Office / Branch Address]

3. Jurisdiction:
   This Hon'ble Commission has territorial and pecuniary jurisdiction as:
   - Value of claim: ₹[amount] (within jurisdiction of District/State/National Commission)
   - Complainant resides within jurisdiction / Opposite Party's office within jurisdiction

4. Facts:
   [Chronological numbered paragraphs]

   (a) On [date], complainant purchased [product] / availed [service] from Opposite Party for ₹[amount].

   (b) [Receipt/Agreement/Invoice No.: ___, dated ___] (Annexure A)

   (c) The Opposite Party represented/warranted that [product would have X features / service would be provided as per Y terms].

   (d) However, on [date], complainant discovered defect/deficiency: [describe - product stopped working / service not provided / billing error / delay / negligence / etc.]

   (e) Complainant contacted Opposite Party's customer service on [dates] via [phone/email/visit] (Annexure B - correspondence)

   (f) Despite multiple follow-ups, Opposite Party failed to resolve the issue / provided unsatisfactory response / denied liability.

   (g) Complainant issued legal notice dated [date] (Annexure C), but Opposite Party did not respond / responded inadequately.

5. Deficiency in Service / Defect in Goods:
   The Opposite Party's act/omission constitutes:
   - Deficiency in service as per Section 2(9) of Consumer Protection Act, 2019
   [OR]
   - Sale of defective goods
   [OR]
   - Unfair trade practice as per Section 2(47)

   [Elaborate why it's deficiency - breach of contract / breach of warranty / negligence / false representation / statutory violation]

6. Relief Claimed:
   Complainant is entitled to relief under Section 35(1) / 48(1) / 59(1):

   (a) Replacement of defective product [OR] Rectification of deficiency
   (b) Refund of amount paid: ₹[amount]
   (c) Compensation for mental agony and harassment: ₹[amount]
   (d) Compensation for loss of time, expenses: ₹[amount]
   (e) Interest @ 9% p.a. from date of purchase till realization
   (f) Costs of complaint: ₹[amount]

7. Cause of Action:
   Cause of action arose on [date when deficiency occurred / when complainant became aware].

8. Limitation:
   Complaint filed within 2 years from cause of action (within limitation as per Section 69).

9. Valuation:
   For court fee and jurisdiction: ₹[total claim amount]

PRAYER:
In light of above facts and circumstances, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Commission may be pleased to:

(a) Direct Opposite Party to replace defective product / rectify deficiency
(b) Direct Opposite Party to refund ₹[amount] with interest @ 9% p.a.
(c) Award compensation of ₹[amount] for mental agony and harassment
(d) Award costs of complaint: ₹[amount]
(e) Pass any other order as this Hon'ble Commission deems fit

COMPLAINANT

Through
[Advocate Name] (if filing through lawyer)

VERIFICATION:
I, [Name], complainant, do hereby verify that the contents of above complaint are true to my knowledge and belief, and nothing material has been concealed.

Place: [Place]
Date: [Date]

Signature of Complainant

LIST OF ANNEXURES:
Annexure A: Invoice/Receipt/Agreement
Annexure B: Correspondence with Opposite Party
Annexure C: Legal Notice (if sent)
Annexure D: [Any other relevant documents - photos of defect, medical reports (if injury), etc.]
```

---

### Step 4: Filing Fee

**Nil** for claims up to ₹5 lakh (consumer-friendly!)

**Above ₹5 lakh**:
- District Commission: ₹200 - ₹5,000 (varies by value)
- State Commission: ₹2,500 - ₹5,000
- National Commission: ₹5,000

**Much lower than civil courts.**

---

### Step 5: File Complaint

**Mode**:
- **In person**: Visit consumer forum registry
- **By post**: Speed Post to Registrar, [Consumer Forum]
- **Online**: EDAAKHIL portal (https://edaakhil.nic.in) - E-filing facility

**Documents to Attach**:
- Complaint (4 copies)
- Affidavit/Verification
- Documents (invoices, agreements, correspondence, photos) - 4 sets
- Demand draft for court fee (if applicable)

**Acknowledgment**: Registry issues acknowledgment with complaint number.

---

### Step 6: Admission & Notice

**Commission scrutinizes**:
- Is complainant a consumer?
- Is there prima facie deficiency/defect?
- Within jurisdiction?
- Within limitation?

**If Admitted**:
- Notice issued to Opposite Party (within 21 days)
- Opposite Party must file written version/reply (within 30-45 days)

**If Defective**:
- Return complaint OR give time to rectify defects

---

### Step 7: Hearing

**Commission Schedule**:
Usually monthly hearings (varies by forum's workload).

**Simplified Procedure**:
- **No strict CPC/Evidence Act** rules (informal)
- **Can represent yourself** (no lawyer mandatory)
- **Proceeds on affidavits** (usually no oral evidence/cross-examination unless necessary)
- Both sides present arguments

**Settlement**:
Commission encourages settlement. Can refer to mediation.

---

### Step 8: Order

**Commission can** (Section 35(1) / 48(1) / 59(1)):
1. **Remove defect** in goods
2. **Replace** goods
3. **Refund** price paid
4. **Pay compensation** for loss/injury suffered
5. **Pay punitive damages** (if gross negligence / deliberate deficiency)
6. **Discontinue unfair trade practice** (+ not to repeat)
7. **Withdraw hazardous goods** from market
8. **Cease manufacture** of hazardous goods
9. **Pay costs** to complainant

**Compensation Includes**:
- Actual loss (refund, medical expenses, repair cost, etc.)
- **Mental agony and harassment**: ₹5,000 - ₹1,00,000+ (varies by gravity)
- Loss of time, inconvenience
- Interest (usually 9% p.a. from date of purchase/deficiency till payment)

---

### Step 9: Execution

**If Opposite Party doesn't comply** with Commission's order:
- File **Execution Application** (EA)
- Commission can order **attachment and sale** of opposite party's property
- **Imprisonment** of responsible officer (up to 3 years if willful non-compliance - Section 71)

## Common Consumer Complaints

### 1. Banking Services

**Deficiencies**:
- Wrong debits from account
- Non-credit of amount
- Unauthorized transactions
- Forced insurance/products
- Harassment by recovery agents
- ATM card cloning

**Remedy**: Complaint to District/State Commission (depending on value) + Complaint to Banking Ombudsman (parallel remedy).

**Case Law**: *Syndicate Bank v. Channaveerappa* - Bank liable for unauthorized debit.

---

### 2. Telecom Services

**Deficiencies**:
- Billing errors (charged for calls/data not used)
- Poor network quality (despite paying)
- Unauthorized activation of services
- Non-portability despite request

**Remedy**: Complaint to Consumer Forum + TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority)

---

### 3. Medical Negligence

**Deficiency**:
- Wrong treatment
- Negligent surgery
- Delay in treatment causing harm
- Wrong diagnosis

**Who Can Complain**: Only patients of **private hospitals** (Government hospitals' medical negligence NOT under Consumer Act - remedy is tort suit OR National/State Human Rights Commission).

**Case Law**:
- *Indian Medical Association v. V.P. Shantha* (1996) - Medical services by private doctors/hospitals covered under Consumer Act
- *Spring Meadows Hospital v. Harjol Ahluwalia* (1998) - Hospital liable for negligent surgery

**Compensation**: Can be substantial (₹10 lakh - ₹1 crore+ depending on injury/death).

---

### 4. Real Estate / Builder Delays

**Deficiencies**:
- Delay in possession beyond agreed date
- Defects in construction (seepage, cracks, etc.)
- Non-delivery of amenities promised (clubhouse, swimming pool, etc.)
- Unilateral increase in price/charges

**Remedy**: Complaint to Consumer Forum (very common cases, success rate high).

**Relief**:
- Refund of amount with interest
- Compensation for delay (₹5-10 per sq ft per month of delay)
- Compensation for mental agony

**Case Law**: *Lucknow Development Authority v. M.K. Gupta* (1994) - Housing authority is service provider under Consumer Act.

---

### 5. E-Commerce Deficiencies

**Issues**:
- Product received different from what was ordered
- Delayed delivery
- Non-delivery despite payment
- Damaged product
- Non-refund after return

**Who to Sue**: E-commerce platform (Amazon, Flipkart) OR seller OR both (as opposite parties).

**Remedy**: Usually faster resolution via e-commerce grievance redressal (escalate to senior, email to grievance officer). If unresolved → Consumer complaint.

---

### 6. Insurance Claim Denial

**Deficiency**: Insurance company rejects claim citing vague/unjustified reasons.

**Remedy**: Complaint to Consumer Forum + Complaint to Insurance Ombudsman (IRDA).

**Common Claims**:
- Health insurance claim rejected (pre-existing condition clause misapplied)
- Motor insurance claim denied
- Life insurance maturity benefit not paid

**Success Rate**: High if policy terms clear and claim valid.

---

### 7. Airlines / Travel

**Deficiencies**:
- Flight cancellation without notice/refund
- Denied boarding despite confirmed ticket
- Lost baggage
- Travel agent fraud (package not as promised)

**Remedy**: Complaint to Consumer Forum + Complaint to Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA).

## Strategic Tips

### 1. Preserve Evidence

**From Day 1**:
- Keep invoice, receipt, agreement
- Take photos/videos (defective product, deficient service)
- Save emails, SMS, call recordings (customer service interactions)
- Medical reports (if injury)

**Better Evidence = Higher Success**

---

### 2. Exhaust Internal Grievance First (Optional)

**Most companies have**:
- Customer service → Senior escalation → Nodal officer → Grievance officer

**Escalate to highest level** (CEO email if needed). Document all interactions.

**Why**:
- May get resolution without complaint (saves time)
- Creates strong evidence for complaint (company's refusal on record)

**But**: Don't delay beyond limitation (2 years). If company is stonewalling, file complaint.

---

### 3. Quantify Loss Precisely

**In Complaint, Specify**:
- Actual loss (price paid, medical expenses, repair cost): ₹[exact amount with bills]
- Mental agony: ₹[reasonable - ₹10,000 to ₹50,000 for moderate cases, higher for severe]
- Loss of time: ₹[for visits, follow-ups]
- Interest @ 9% p.a. from [date] till payment

**Higher compensation if loss quantified with evidence.**

---

### 4. File Yourself (No Lawyer Needed)

**Consumer forums are consumer-friendly**:
- Can file complaint yourself (use format above)
- Can argue yourself (informal procedure)
- No need to engage advocate (saves cost)

**When to Engage Advocate**:
- High-value claim (>₹10 lakh)
- Complex legal issue (medical negligence, construction defects)
- Company engages senior lawyer (level playing field)

---

### 5. Mediation / Settlement

**Consumer forums encourage settlement**:
- If company offers reasonable settlement during proceedings, consider accepting (saves time)
- Can be recorded in consent order (legally binding)

**But**: Don't settle for inadequate amount under pressure.

---

## Limitation & Appeals

### Limitation Period

**2 Years** from:
- Date of cause of action (when deficiency occurred / defect discovered) OR
- Date when complainant could have known with due diligence

**Extension**: If sufficient cause shown, Commission may condone delay (but difficult beyond 2 years).

**Continuing Cause of Action**: If deficiency is continuing (e.g., bank wrongly deducting charges every month), fresh cause of action each month.

---

### Appeals

**From District Commission**:
- Appeal to **State Commission** (within **45 days** of order)

**From State Commission**:
- Appeal to **National Commission** (within **30 days** of order)

**From National Commission**:
- Appeal to **Supreme Court** (within **30 days** of order)

**Grounds for Appeal**:
- Error of law
- Perversity (findings contrary to evidence)
- Inadequate compensation

**Stay of Lower Order**: Appellate Commission can stay lower Commission's order if prima facie case (usually on deposit of part amount).

## Disclaimer

**This protocol provides information about consumer protection law. It does NOT constitute legal advice.**

**Key Points**:
- Consumer forums are consumer-friendly (low fee, can self-represent)
- File complaint within 2 years from deficiency/defect
- Preserve all evidence (invoices, correspondence)
- Compensation includes actual loss + mental agony + interest + costs
- Appeals within strict timelines (45/30/30 days)

**Consumer protection is powerful tool for citizen empowerment. Use it for genuine deficiencies, not frivolous complaints.**
