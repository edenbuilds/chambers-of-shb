# Right to Information Act 2005 - Fundamentals

## Purpose
Understand the basic framework of RTI Act 2005, who can file RTI applications, what information can be requested, and fundamental principles of transparency and accountability in government.

## Activation Triggers
Use this protocol when:
- Filing first RTI application
- Understanding scope of RTI Act
- Determining if information qualifies under RTI
- Assessing if body is "public authority"
- Educating about citizen's right to information

---

## Legal Framework

### Constitutional Foundation

**Article 19(1)(a) - Freedom of Speech and Expression**:
Supreme Court in *S.P. Gupta v. Union of India* (1982) held right to information is fundamental right flowing from Article 19(1)(a).

**Rationale**: Informed citizenry is prerequisite for participative democracy. Citizens cannot make informed choices without access to government information.

### Right to Information Act, 2005

**Enactment**: October 12, 2005 (effective)
**Objective**: Promote transparency and accountability in government functioning by providing citizens access to information.

---

## Key Definitions

### 1. Information (Section 2(f))

**Statutory Definition**:
> "Information means any material in any form, including records, documents, memos, e-mails, opinions, advises, press releases, circulars, orders, logbooks, contracts, reports, papers, samples, models, data material held in any electronic form and information relating to any private body which can be accessed by a public authority under any other law for the time being in force."

**Key Elements**:
- **Any material**: Physical or digital
- **Any form**: Documents, emails, data, models, samples
- **Electronic form included**: Databases, digital files
- **Private body information**: If accessible by public authority under law

**Examples of "Information"**:
- Government files, notings, minutes
- Tender documents, contracts
- Environmental clearance applications
- Police FIRs, investigation reports
- Municipal building plans, NOCs
- CCTV footage (if held by public authority)
- Emails between government officials
- Consultant reports commissioned by government

**NOT "Information"** (Exemptions - Section 8):
- Covered separately in CIVIC-RTI-PROCEDURE (exemptions)

---

### 2. Public Authority (Section 2(h))

**Statutory Definition**:
> "Public authority means any authority or body or institution of self-government established or constituted—
> (a) by or under the Constitution;
> (b) by any other law made by Parliament;
> (c) by any other law made by State Legislature;
> (d) by notification issued or order made by the appropriate Government,
> and includes any—
> (i) body owned, controlled or substantially financed;
> (ii) non-Government organisation substantially financed, directly or indirectly by funds provided by the appropriate Government."

**Categories**:

**Category A: Constitutional Bodies**
- Supreme Court, High Courts
- Election Commission
- Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG)
- Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)
- State Public Service Commissions

**Category B: Statutory Bodies**
- Central/State Government Departments
- Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs): ONGC, BHEL, Indian Railways
- Regulatory Authorities: SEBI, TRAI, RBI
- Universities (established by statute)

**Category C: Funded Bodies**
- NGOs receiving substantial government funding (>95% threshold per CIC rulings)
- Private schools receiving government grants
- Private hospitals empaneled under government schemes

**Category D: Self-Government Institutions**
- Gram Panchayats
- Municipal Corporations
- Cantonment Boards

**Important Rulings**:

*CBSE v. Aditya Bandopadhyay* (2011) 8 SCC 497:
CBSE is public authority. However, answer sheets are fiduciary relationship with student - not disclosed under RTI.

*Thalappalam Service Cooperative Bank v. State of Kerala* (2013) 16 SCC 82:
Cooperative societies NOT public authorities unless substantially funded by government.

---

### 3. Right to Information (Section 2(j))

**Statutory Definition**:
> "Right to information means the right to information accessible under this Act which is held by or under the control of any public authority and includes the right to—
> (i) inspection of work, documents, records;
> (ii) taking notes, extracts or certified copies of documents or records;
> (iii) taking certified samples of material;
> (iv) obtaining information in the form of diskettes, floppies, tapes, video cassettes or in any other electronic mode or through printouts where such information is stored in a computer or in any other device."

**Rights Included**:
1. **Inspection**: Visit office, view files physically
2. **Copies**: Certified/plain copies
3. **Notes/Extracts**: Handwritten notes from files
4. **Samples**: If information in material form (e.g., water quality sample)
5. **Electronic Format**: Data in digital form (CSV, PDF, etc.)

## Who Can File RTI?

### Section 3: Right to Information

**Statutory Text**:
> "Subject to the provisions of this Act, all citizens shall have the right to information."

**Eligibility**:
- **ANY citizen of India**
- No educational qualification required
- No age limit (minor can file through guardian)
- No need to show locus standi (personal interest)
- No need to give reasons for seeking information

**Case Law**:
*Institute of Chartered Accountants of India v. Shaunak H. Satya* (2011):
Applicant need NOT give reasons for seeking information. RTI Act does not require applicant to prove "need to know."

**Who CANNOT File RTI**:
- Non-citizens (foreigners, NRIs who renounced citizenship)
- Companies, LLPs, societies (only natural persons - citizens)

**Practical Note**: NRIs who retain Indian citizenship CAN file RTI.

## What Information Can Be Requested?

### Section 4: Obligations of Public Authorities

**Mandatory Disclosure** (Suo Moto):
Every public authority must publish 17 categories of information (Section 4(1)(b)):

1. **Particulars of organization, functions, duties**
2. **Powers and duties of officers and employees**
3. **Procedure for decision-making**
4. **Norms for discharge of functions**
5. **Rules, regulations, instructions, manuals, records for public**
6. **Documents held or under control**
7. **Policy formation process**
8. **Directory of officers**
9. **Monthly remuneration of officers**
10. **Budget allocation, plans, proposed expenditure**
11. **Subsidy programs (beneficiaries)**
12. **Concessions, permits, authorizations**
13. **Grant recipients**
14. **Tender information**
15. **Land/property allotment**
16. **Board, council, committee, body members**
17. **Any other information prescribed**

**Compliance**: Must be published on website, updated regularly.

**Benefit**: If already published (Section 4 compliance), citizen gets information without RTI application. If not published, can file RTI.

---

### Types of Information Requestable

**A. Government Decisions and Files**
- Cabinet decisions
- File notings (opinions of officers)
- Meeting minutes
- Policy drafts

**B. Financial Information**
- Budget documents
- Expenditure statements
- Audit reports
- Contractor payments
- Subsidy beneficiaries

**C. Project Information**
- Infrastructure project DPRs (Detailed Project Reports)
- Environmental clearances
- Land acquisition proceedings
- Tender evaluation reports

**D. Personal Information** (About Self):
- Service records (government employees)
- Medical records (government hospitals)
- Exam answer sheets (with caveats per CBSE case)

**E. Third-Party Information**:
- Contractor performance
- NGO utilization certificates
- University accreditation reports

---

### Exemptions (Section 8 - Brief Overview)

Information **CANNOT** be obtained if falls under exemptions:
1. National security, sovereignty
2. Cabinet papers, cabinet decisions (except post-decision)
3. Trade secrets, commercial confidence
4. Fiduciary relationship (e.g., banker-customer, doctor-patient)
5. Information from foreign governments in confidence
6. Endangers life or safety
7. Impedes investigation or prosecution
8. Legislature privilege
9. Contempt of court
10. Personal information (unrelated to public activity)

**Detailed Analysis**: CIVIC-RTI-PROCEDURE protocol

**Public Interest Override (Section 8(2))**:
Even if information falls under exemptions (except (1)), it MUST be disclosed if **public interest in disclosure outweighs harm** from disclosure.

**Example**: Corruption allegations against minister - personal information exemption overridden by public interest.

## Fundamental Principles

### 1. Maximum Disclosure, Minimum Exemptions

RTI Act embodies principle: **Government information is PUBLIC information**. Secrecy is exception, transparency is rule.

### 2. No Need for Reasons

Applicant need not:
- Justify why information is needed
- Show personal interest
- Prove public interest
- Explain use of information

**Rationale**: Right to information is fundamental right. Citizen does not need to justify exercising fundamental right.

### 3. Time-Bound Disposal

- **30 days**: Normal cases (Section 7(1))
- **48 hours**: Life or liberty matters (Section 7(1) proviso)
- **Additional 5 days**: If third party involved (Section 11)

Failure to respond within time = deemed refusal (Section 7(1))

### 4. Low Cost Access

- **Application fee**: ₹10 (Central), varies by state
- **Information fee**: ₹2 per page (A4/A3 size)
- **Inspection**: First hour free, ₹5 per additional 15 minutes
- **Samples**: Actual cost

**Below Poverty Line (BPL) applicants**: NO fees

### 5. Decentralized Mechanism

- **Public Information Officer (PIO)**: Receives applications, provides information
- **First Appellate Authority (FAA)**: Reviews PIO decisions
- **Central/State Information Commission (CIC/SIC)**: Final appellate authority

Every public authority must designate PIOs and FAAs (Section 5).

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Municipal Building Permission

**Question**: Can I get information about my neighbor's building plan approval?

**Analysis**:
- **Information**: Building plan, NOC, approval order (Section 2(f) - documents) ✓
- **Public Authority**: Municipal Corporation (Section 2(h) - self-government institution) ✓
- **Right**: Certified copy of building approval (Section 2(j)) ✓
- **Exemption Check**: No exemption (not personal information - relates to public activity of construction) ✓
- **Conclusion**: **YES**, RTI can be filed to Municipal Corporation PIO

### Example 2: Private School Fee Structure

**Question**: Can I get fee structure of private school (not aided)?

**Analysis**:
- **Public Authority Check**: Private unaided school - NOT public authority (no substantial government funding) ✗
- **Conclusion**: **NO**, RTI Act does not apply to private unaided schools

**Alternative**: If school affiliated to CBSE/ICSE, file RTI to Board asking for fee structure (as Board has regulatory power over affiliated schools).

### Example 3: Police Investigation Details

**Question**: Can I get copy of FIR filed by me?

**Analysis**:
- **Information**: FIR (Section 2(f) - document) ✓
- **Public Authority**: Police (Section 2(h) - government department) ✓
- **Right**: Certified copy (Section 2(j)) ✓
- **Exemption Check**: FIR is NOT exempt (it's public document). However, investigation details (chargesheet before filing) may be exempt under Section 8(1)(h) (impede investigation) ⚠
- **Conclusion**: **YES** for FIR copy. Investigation status MAY be provided if not impeding investigation.

**Practical Note**: Supreme Court has held FIR must be provided to complainant. RTI is alternative mechanism if police delay.

### Example 4: Government Tender Information

**Question**: Can I get details of tenders floated by PWD?

**Analysis**:
- **Information**: Tender documents, bidder names, evaluation reports (Section 2(f)) ✓
- **Public Authority**: Public Works Department (Section 2(h)) ✓
- **Exemption Check**:
  - **Pre-award**: Bidder identities, commercial bids MAY be exempt (Section 8(1)(d) - commercial confidence) ⚠
  - **Post-award**: Winning bid, contract terms, payment details - NOT exempt (public money involved) ✓
- **Conclusion**:
  - **Pre-award**: Partial disclosure (technical specs - yes, bidder details - may be refused)
  - **Post-award**: **FULL disclosure** (except trade secrets in contract)

## Strategic Tips for RTI Success

### 1. Be Specific

**Poor RTI**: "Give me all information about XYZ project"
**Good RTI**: "Provide copy of (1) DPR dated [date], (2) Environmental clearance letter, (3) Tender evaluation report for contract awarded to [contractor name]"

**Why**: Vague requests → PIO confusion → delay/rejection. Specific requests → clear response.

### 2. Identify Correct Public Authority

**Wrong**: File RTI about land records to Municipal Corporation
**Right**: File to Revenue Department (land records maintained by Tehsildar/Sub-Registrar)

**Research**: Check which department holds the information (visit office or check website)

### 3. Pay Correct Fee

**Fee varies**: Central (₹10), State-wise variation (Maharashtra ₹5, Delhi ₹10, etc.)
**Payment**: Cash/DD/IPO/Online (as per authority)
**BPL**: Attach BPL card copy, write "BPL applicant - no fee"

### 4. Use Section 6(3) Transfer Provision

If file RTI to **wrong** public authority:
- **PIO must transfer** to correct authority within 5 days (Section 6(3))
- Must inform applicant of transfer

**Benefit**: Even if wrong authority, application not rejected - transferred.

### 5. Cite Section 7(1) Proviso for Urgency

If information concerns **life or liberty**:
- **48-hour response** mandatory
- Mention in application: "This concerns life/liberty - response required within 48 hours u/s 7(1) proviso"

**Examples**: Medical emergency, illegal detention, food contamination

## Limitations and Disclaimers

**This protocol provides information about RTI Act 2005. It does NOT constitute legal advice.**

**Key Points**:
1. **State Variations**: Some states have specific RTI rules (fee, procedure variations)
2. **Evolving Jurisprudence**: Courts continuously interpret RTI provisions
3. **PIO Discretion**: PIOs may refuse information citing exemptions - appeal available
4. **Third-Party Rights**: Some information may involve third-party privacy/commercial interests
5. **Professional Verification**: For complex RTI matters, consult RTI activists or lawyers

**RTI is citizen's tool for transparency. Use it responsibly.**
