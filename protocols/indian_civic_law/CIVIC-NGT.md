# National Green Tribunal (NGT) - Environmental Justice Forum

## Purpose
Understand NGT as specialized environmental tribunal, its jurisdiction, procedure for filing applications, types of cases, compensation principles, and strategic use for environmental enforcement.

## Activation Triggers
- Pollution violation (air, water, noise, soil)
- Illegal construction in eco-sensitive zone
- Forest clearance violation
- Wildlife protection violation
- Challenge to environment clearance
- Seeking compensation for environmental damage
- Appeal from Pollution Control Board order

---

## What is National Green Tribunal (NGT)?

### Establishment
**National Green Tribunal Act, 2010** - Enacted by Parliament.

**Operational**: October 18, 2010

**Purpose** (Preamble):
> "An Act to provide for the establishment of a National Green Tribunal for the effective and expeditious disposal of cases relating to environmental protection and conservation of forests and other natural resources..."

---

### Need for Specialized Tribunal

**Problems with Regular Courts**:
1. **Delays**: Environmental cases in HC/SC take 5-10 years
2. **Lack of Technical Expertise**: Judges not trained in environmental science
3. **Procedural Complexity**: CPC/Evidence Act formalities slow down proceedings

**NGT Solution**:
- **Faster Disposal**: Target 6 months (Section 18(3))
- **Expert Members**: Judicial + Environmental Scientists/Engineers
- **Simplified Procedure**: Less formalistic than regular courts
- **Specialized Focus**: Only environmental matters

---

### International Inspiration
**Similar Tribunals Globally**:
- Land and Environment Court (Australia)
- Environmental Court (New Zealand)
- Environmental Tribunal (South Africa)

**India's NGT**: One of the most comprehensive environmental tribunals globally.

---

## Jurisdiction of NGT

### Section 14: Civil Cases Relating to Environment

**NGT has jurisdiction over**:

**1. Substantial Questions Relating to Environment**:
Arising under 7 specified Acts:
1. Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974
2. Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977
3. Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980
4. Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981
5. **Environment (Protection) Act, 1986** (Umbrella law)
6. Public Liability Insurance Act, 1991
7. Biological Diversity Act, 2002

**Plus**: Any rules, notifications, regulations under these Acts (e.g., EIA Notification 2006, Solid Waste Rules 2016, etc.)

**2. Enforcement of Legal Rights Relating to Environment**

**3. Compensation and Relief**:
For damages to persons, property, environment.

**What NGT CANNOT Decide**:
- Pure criminal cases (criminal prosecution under EPA - goes to criminal courts)
- Wildlife offense prosecutions (goes to criminal courts)
- Cases NOT arising under the 7 specified Acts
- Disputes between private parties (unless involving environmental violation)

---

### Section 16: Appeals from Regulatory Authorities

**NGT is Appellate Authority** for orders/decisions/directions by:
- **Pollution Control Boards** (CPCB, SPCBs)
- **State Environment Impact Assessment Authority (SEIAA)**
- **District Environment Impact Assessment Authority (DEIAA)**
- **Any other authority** under the 7 Acts

**Example**:
- SPCB issues closure direction to factory → Factory appeals to NGT
- SEIAA denies Environment Clearance → Applicant appeals to NGT
- SPCB grants consent to polluting industry → Citizen appeals to NGT

**Timeline**: Appeal must be filed **within 30 days** of order (extendable by 60 days if sufficient cause).

---

## Who Can File in NGT?

### Section 15: Locus Standi

**Two Categories**:

**1. Aggrieved Person**:
Person whose legal right violated OR who suffered loss due to environmental violation.

**Example**:
- Farmer whose crops damaged by industrial pollution
- Resident suffering health issues from air pollution
- Landowner whose land encroached for illegal mining

**2. Any Person (PIL-Type Jurisdiction)**:
> "Any person... for a **substantial question** relating to environment..."

**Similar to PIL in HC/SC**: Public-spirited person, NGO, can file even if not personally affected.

**Example**:
- Environmental NGO files application on river pollution (even if NGO members not personally affected)
- Activist files on illegal mining affecting forest (even if activist lives elsewhere)

**Gatekeeping** (Section 19):
NGT may reject frivolous/vexatious applications + impose costs up to ₹10,000.

## NGT Bench Structure

### Principal Bench
**Location**: Delhi

**Jurisdiction**: All India (can hear cases from any state)

**Officers**:
- Chairperson (Judicial Member - retired SC/HC judge)
- Expert Members (Environmental scientists/engineers)

---

### Regional Benches

**1. Central Zonal Bench**: Bhopal
- Jurisdiction: Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh

**2. Eastern Zonal Bench**: Kolkata
- Jurisdiction: West Bengal, Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, Sikkim, Andaman & Nicobar Islands

**3. Southern Zonal Bench**: Chennai
- Jurisdiction: Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Puducherry, Lakshadweep

**4. Western Zonal Bench**: Pune
- Jurisdiction: Maharashtra, Goa, Gujarat, Daman & Diu, Dadra & Nagar Haveli

**Northern States**: No dedicated zonal bench → File in **Principal Bench, Delhi**

---

### Bench Composition

**Each Bench** has:
- **Judicial Members**: Retired HC/SC judges (minimum 2)
- **Expert Members**: Environmental scientists, engineers (minimum 2)

**Quorum**: 1 Judicial Member + 1 Expert Member (minimum for hearing).

**Advantage**: Technical environmental issues decided by experts, not just lawyers/judges.

---

## Types of Cases in NGT

### 1. Original Applications (OA)

**Fresh cases** filed directly in NGT for:
- **Environmental Violations**: Pollution, illegal construction in protected area, etc.
- **Compensation**: For environmental damage
- **Enforcement**: Compelling authority to enforce environmental laws

**Example**:
- OA seeking closure of polluting industry
- OA for compensation to villagers affected by industrial effluent
- OA challenging unauthorized construction in wetland

**Section 14(1)**: Original jurisdiction.

---

### 2. Appeals

**Against Orders of**:
- SPCBs (consent denial, closure direction, penalty)
- SEIAA/DEIAA (Environment Clearance grant/denial)
- Forest Clearance Authority
- Any authority under 7 Acts

**Example**:
- Factory appeals SPCB's closure order
- Citizen appeals SEIAA's grant of EC to project (claiming improper procedure)

**Section 16**: Appellate jurisdiction.

**Timeline**: Within **30 days** (extendable by 60 days).

---

### 3. Suo Moto Cases

**NGT can take cognizance** on its own based on:
- Newspaper reports
- Letters/complaints
- Media coverage

**Example**: NGT took suo moto cognizance of Delhi air pollution based on media reports, issued directions to government.

---

## Procedure for Filing in NGT

### Step 1: Determine Jurisdiction

**Check**:
- Does case arise under 7 specified Acts? (If not, NGT has no jurisdiction - approach HC)
- Is it within 30 days of impugned order (if appeal)?
- Which bench has territorial jurisdiction?

---

### Step 2: Draft Application

**Format** (NGT Practice & Procedure Rules, 2011):

```
BEFORE THE NATIONAL GREEN TRIBUNAL
[PRINCIPAL BENCH / ZONAL BENCH NAME]

ORIGINAL APPLICATION NO. _____ OF 2024
(OR: APPEAL NO. _____ OF 2024)

IN THE MATTER OF:
[Brief description - e.g., "Pollution of River X by Industrial Effluents"]

AND

IN THE MATTER OF:
Environment (Protection) Act, 1986
Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974

[Applicant Name/Organization] ... Applicant

Versus

[Respondent Names - Polluter, SPCB, etc.] ... Respondents
```

**Contents**:
1. **Parties**: Applicant, Respondents
2. **Facts**: Chronological, numbered paragraphs
3. **Legal Violations**: Which Act/Rule violated
4. **Locus Standi**: Why applicant has right to file (aggrieved / substantial question)
5. **Relief Sought**: Prayers (closure, compensation, directions)
6. **Documents**: List of annexures
7. **Verification**: Applicant verifies facts on oath

---

### Step 3: Filing Fee

**Fee Structure** (NGT Rules):
- **Individuals**: ₹1,000
- **Association of Individuals**: ₹5,000
- **Other Applicants** (companies, etc.): ₹10,000

**Payment Mode**: Demand draft in favor of "Registrar, National Green Tribunal"

**Fee Waiver**: NGT may waive fee if applicant proves poverty (Section 18(2)).

---

### Step 4: Required Documents

**Mandatory**:
1. **Application** (typed, on A4 paper, double-spaced)
2. **Affidavit** verifying application
3. **Index** of annexures
4. **Vakalatnama** (if filing through advocate - optional, can file yourself)
5. **Annexures** (all supporting documents):
   - Pollution Control Board's order (if appeal)
   - RTI responses
   - Photos, videos (in CD/USB)
   - Expert reports (if available)
   - News clippings
6. **Demand Draft** (filing fee)
7. **Certified Copies**: 5 copies for respondents + 1 for NGT file

---

### Step 5: Filing

**Mode**:
- **In Person**: Visit NGT Registry (during office hours)
- **By Post**: Speed Post / Registered Post to Registrar, NGT (relevant bench)
- **Email**: ngt.filing@gov.in (some benches accept email filing)

**Acknowledgment**: Registry issues acknowledgment with diary number.

---

### Step 6: Scrutiny

**Registry Scrutiny** (3-5 days):
- Check if application complete
- Check if fee paid
- Check if jurisdiction exists

**If Defect**: Return application OR give time to rectify.

**If Complete**: Register application, allocate number.

---

### Step 7: Listing & Notice

**NGT issues notice** to respondents (SPCB, polluter, government).

**Respondents file reply** within specified time (usually 30 days).

**Listing**: Case listed for hearing.

---

### Step 8: Interim Relief (If Sought)

**If Urgent Relief Needed**:
File **application for interim directions** along with main application.

**Relief Sought**:
- Stay operation of impugned order
- Stop polluting activity immediately
- Prevent irreversible damage

**Test**:
- Prima facie case?
- Balance of convenience?
- Irreparable injury if relief not granted?
- Environmental emergency?

**Example**: River pollution causing health hazard → NGT may order immediate stoppage of effluent discharge pending final hearing.

---

### Step 9: Hearing

**Simplified Procedure**:
- **No CPC/Evidence Act formalities** (NGT not bound by these - Section 19(2))
- **Proceeds on affidavits** (usually no oral evidence)
- **Arguments by parties** (lawyers OR self-representation)
- **Questions by Expert Members** (on technical aspects)

**Timeline Target**: Dispose within **6 months** (Section 18(3)) - Often not met due to backlog, but faster than HC.

---

### Step 10: Judgment/Order

**NGT Passes**:
- **Reasoned Order**: Facts, legal analysis, findings
- **Directions**: To respondents (comply, pay compensation, restore, etc.)
- **Compensation** (if awarded)
- **Costs** (if frivolous application, costs imposed on applicant; if violation proved, costs on respondent)

**Execution**: NGT order is executable as decree of civil court (Section 26).

---

## Reliefs & Remedies in NGT

### 1. Closure / Stoppage Directions

**NGT can order**:
- Close polluting industry
- Stop illegal construction/mining
- Cease operations till compliance

**Example**: NGT ordered closure of 500+ illegal stone crushers in NCR for air pollution.

---

### 2. Compensation

**Section 17**: NGT can award compensation to victims of environmental damage.

**Principles Applied**:
- **Polluter Pays Principle**: Polluter bears cost of restoration + compensation
- **Restitution**: Restore environment to original state
- **Compensation to Affected Persons**: For health damage, property loss, livelihood loss

**Quantum**:
- Can be substantial (₹crores in some cases)
- Based on extent of damage, restoration cost, number of affected persons

**Example**: NGT ordered mining company to pay ₹100 crore for illegal mining + ecological damage in Goa.

---

### 3. Restoration Directions

**NGT can direct**:
- Afforestation
- Removal of encroachments
- Cleaning of rivers/water bodies
- Restoration of wetlands

**Example**: NGT directed Delhi government to remove all encroachments on Yamuna floodplain.

---

### 4. Compliance Monitoring

**NGT appoints**:
- **Monitoring Committees**: To oversee implementation (comprising government officials, experts, NGO representatives)
- **Expert Committees**: To study technical issues, submit reports

**Continuing Jurisdiction**: NGT monitors compliance till complete execution.

---

### 5. Costs

**On Frivolous Applicants**: Up to ₹10,000 (Section 19)

**On Violators**: Costs for wasting court time, causing environmental harm (no cap).

---

## Strategic Use of NGT

### Advantages Over HC/SC

**1. Speed**: Target 6 months (vs. 3-10 years in HC/SC)

**2. Lower Cost**:
- Filing fee: ₹1,000 (vs. ₹5,000+ in HC)
- No mandatory lawyer (can file yourself)

**3. Technical Expertise**: Expert members understand environmental science (don't need to educate judges on technicalities)

**4. Simplified Procedure**: Less formalistic

**5. Compensation**: NGT readily awards compensation on Polluter Pays Principle

---

### Disadvantages

**1. Limited Jurisdiction**: Only 7 Acts (doesn't cover all environmental issues)

**2. No Writ Powers**: Unlike HC (Article 226), NGT cannot issue writs against government for non-environmental matters

**3. Cannot Declare Law Unconstitutional**: Only SC/HC can declare law unconstitutional (if challenging validity of environmental law itself, must go to HC)

**4. Backlog**: Despite 6-month target, many cases take 1-3 years

**5. Appeal to SC**: If NGT decision adverse, must appeal to SC (expensive, time-consuming)

---

### When to Choose NGT vs. HC

**NGT**:
- Pure environmental violation (pollution, clearance violation)
- Seek compensation for environmental damage
- Appeal from SPCB/SEIAA order
- Cost-effective litigation
- Technical issue (need expert members)

**HC**:
- Constitutional question (Articles 14, 21)
- Challenge law itself (not just its application)
- Mixed issues (environmental + non-environmental)
- Need writ powers (mandamus, certiorari)
- Urgency (HC may grant stay faster in some cases)

**Both Allowed** (not mutually exclusive): Can file in both fora if grounds different. But usually, exhaust NGT first, then appeal to SC.

## Common Cases in NGT

### 1. Industrial Pollution

**Application**: Seek closure of polluting factory, compensation to affected residents.

**Evidence**: SPCB inspection reports (via RTI), pollution readings, medical reports of affected persons, photos/videos.

**Outcome**: NGT often orders closure till compliance + compensation.

---

### 2. Challenge to Environment Clearance

**Application** (Appeal under Section 16): SEIAA granted EC without proper public consultation OR based on flawed EIA.

**Grounds**: Violation of EIA Notification procedure, suppression of facts, non-consideration of environmental impact.

**Outcome**: NGT may quash EC, remand for fresh consideration, or uphold with additional conditions.

---

### 3. Illegal Construction in Eco-Sensitive Zone

**Application**: Seek demolition of illegal construction in forest/wetland/coastal regulation zone.

**Legal Basis**: Violation of Forest Act, Coastal Regulation Zone Notification, Wetland Rules.

**Outcome**: NGT orders demolition + restoration + penalty.

---

### 4. Waste Management Violations

**Application**: Municipality not collecting garbage / illegal landfill / hazardous waste dumping.

**Relief**: Directions to municipality to comply with Solid Waste Rules, appoint monitoring committee.

---

### 5. Wildlife Protection

**Application** (if civil relief sought, not criminal prosecution): Illegal clearing of habitat, encroachment in sanctuary.

**Outcome**: Restoration, compensation to Wildlife Department, penalty.

---

## Appeal from NGT

### Appeal to Supreme Court

**Section 22**: Any person aggrieved by NGT decision can appeal to SC.

**Timeline**: Within **90 days** (no extension provision - strictly 90 days).

**Grounds**: Legal error, perversity, non-consideration of material evidence.

**Note**: Appeal on facts difficult (SC generally doesn't reappreciate facts found by NGT's expert members).

## Practical Tips

### 1. Gather Strong Evidence

**NGT Proceeds on Evidence**:
- Pollution readings (from SPCB or independent testing)
- Photos/videos (timestamped, geotagged)
- Expert reports (environmental scientists, doctors)
- RTI responses (SPCB consent status, inspection reports)
- Affidavits of affected persons

**Weak Evidence = Weak Case.**

---

### 2. Use RTI Before Filing

**File RTI to**:
- SPCB: Industry's consent status, violations, inspection dates, actions taken
- SEIAA: EC application, EIA report, public hearing records
- Forest Department: Clearances, encroachment details

**RTI = Ammunition for NGT Application.**

---

### 3. Quantify Damage (For Compensation)

**For Compensation Claims**:
- Calculate restoration cost (afforestation, cleaning, etc.)
- Medical expenses of affected persons
- Loss of livelihood (if crops damaged, fish killed, etc.)
- Property damage

**Higher Compensation if Quantified Precisely.**

---

### 4. Seek Interim Relief

**If Environmental Emergency**:
File interim application immediately (stoppage of pollution, prevent irreversible damage).

**NGT Grants Interim Relief** if:
- Serious environmental degradation ongoing
- Irreversible damage likely
- Prima facie violation

---

### 5. Follow Up on Implementation

**NGT Order ≠ Automatic Compliance**:
- Monitor whether respondent complying
- If not, file **execution application** (Section 26)
- NGT can issue contempt proceedings for non-compliance
