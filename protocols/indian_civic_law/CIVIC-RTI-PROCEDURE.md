# RTI Procedure - Application, Appeals, and Exemptions

## Purpose
Step-by-step procedure for filing RTI applications, understanding timelines, navigating exemptions, and filing appeals to First Appellate Authority and Information Commissions.

## Activation Triggers
- Filing RTI application for first time
- RTI application rejected or delayed
- Information partially provided
- Need to file first appeal or second appeal
- Understanding Section 8 exemptions

---

## RTI Application Procedure

### Step 1: Draft RTI Application (Section 6)

**Section 6(1) Requirements**:
```
Application must be:
1. In writing (handwritten or typed)
2. In English/Hindi/official language of area
3. Accompanied by application fee
4. Addressed to Public Information Officer (PIO)
```

**No Prescribed Format**: RTI Act does not mandate specific format. Simple letter suffices.

**Essential Elements**:
1. Applicant name and address (for response)
2. Public authority name
3. PIO designation (if known) or "PIO, [Department Name]"
4. Information sought (specific, itemized)
5. Preferred mode of delivery (post, email, inspection, certified copy)
6. Mobile number, email (optional but recommended)
7. Date
8. Signature

---

### Standard RTI Application Format

```
To,
The Public Information Officer,
[Name of Public Authority/Department],
[Address]

Date: [DD/MM/YYYY]

Subject: Application under Right to Information Act, 2005

Dear Sir/Madam,

Under the Right to Information Act, 2005, I request the following information from your office:

1. [Specific information request - be precise]
   Example: "Copy of tender document for [project name] floated on [date]"

2. [Second information point]
   Example: "Names and addresses of all bidders who submitted bids for above tender"

3. [Third information point]
   Example: "Copy of contract awarded to successful bidder including payment terms"

I am enclosing application fee of ₹[10 for Central, varies for State] in the form of [Cash/DD/IPO/Online payment receipt].

[If BPL: I am a Below Poverty Line cardholder (BPL card No: _____). I am exempted from payment of fee under RTI Act. Copy of BPL card enclosed.]

[If urgent u/s 7(1) proviso: This information concerns life/liberty of [name]. I request response within 48 hours as mandated by Section 7(1) proviso.]

Please provide the information in the form of:
☐ Certified copies by post
☐ Inspection at your office (preferred date: [date])
☐ Email to: [email]
☐ Soft copy (CD/pen drive)

My contact details:
Name: [Full Name]
Address: [Complete Postal Address]
Mobile: [10-digit number]
Email: [email address]

Thanking you,

[Signature]
[Name in BLOCK LETTERS]
```

---

### Step 2: Pay Application Fee

**Fee Structure** (as of 2024):

**Central Government**:
- Application fee: ₹10
- Information fee: ₹2 per page (A4/A3)
- Inspection: First hour free, ₹5 per 15 minutes thereafter
- Samples: Actual cost
- Soft copy: ₹50 per CD/floppy

**State Governments**: Varies (₹5 to ₹10 typically)

**BPL Applicants**: NO fee (attach BPL card copy)

**Payment Modes**:
- **Cash**: At PIO office (get receipt)
- **Demand Draft**: Favoring PIO/Department
- **Indian Postal Order**: Payable to PIO
- **Online**: If public authority has online RTI portal
- **Court fee stamps**: Some states accept

**IMPORTANT**: Keep payment receipt - needed for appeals.

---

### Step 3: Submit Application

**Submission Methods**:

**A. In Person**:
- Visit PIO office
- Submit application + fee
- Get acknowledgment with **unique application number** and date
- Keep acknowledgment safely (needed for tracking, appeals)

**B. By Post**:
- Speed post/Registered post (for proof of delivery)
- Enclose DD/IPO for fee
- PIO must respond by post to given address

**C. Online** (if available):
- Central Government: rtionline.gov.in
- State Governments: Check state RTI portal
- Upload application, pay fee online
- Download acknowledgment

**D. Email**:
- Some public authorities accept (check website)
- Scan application + fee receipt, email to designated email
- Request acknowledgment by return email

**CRITICAL**: Obtain acknowledgment with application number. Without this, cannot track or appeal.

---

### Step 4: PIO Actions (Section 6-7)

**PIO's Duties**:

**Within 48 Hours** (Section 6(3)):
If application filed to **wrong** public authority:
- PIO must **transfer** to correct authority
- Must inform applicant of transfer

**Within 30 Days** (Section 7(1)):
PIO must:
1. Provide information requested, OR
2. Reject request (with reasons citing Section 8 exemptions), OR
3. Transfer to correct PIO (if wrong department within same authority)

**Within 48 Hours** (Section 7(1) proviso):
If information concerns **life or liberty** - immediate response mandatory.

**Failure to Respond**:
If PIO does not respond within 30 days → **Deemed refusal** (Section 7(1))
Applicant can file **First Appeal** treating non-response as rejection.

## Exemptions (Section 8)

### Section 8(1): Absolute and Qualified Exemptions

**Clause (a): National Security, Sovereignty**
```
Information prejudicially affecting:
- Sovereignty and integrity of India
- Security, strategic, scientific or economic interests of State
- Relation with foreign State
- Incitement to offense
```

**Example**: Military deployment details, defense technology, intelligence operations.

**Clause (b): Parliament/Legislature Privilege**
```
Information expressly forbidden to be published by Parliament/Legislature.
```

**Example**: In-camera proceedings of Parliamentary committee.

**Clause (c): Court Contempt**
```
Information disclosure would constitute contempt of court.
```

**Clause (d): Trade Secrets, Commercial Confidence**
```
Information including commercial confidence, trade secrets, or IP:
- Harm competitive position of third party
- Available to person under fiduciary relationship (unless competent authority satisfied)
```

**Examples**:
- ❌ Coca-Cola formula (trade secret)
- ❌ Bidder's commercial bid before award (competitive position)
- ✓ Awarded contract value (public money - no exemption)

**Public Interest Override (Section 8(2))**: Larger public interest can override this exemption.

**Case Law**:
*Reliance Industries v. CIC* (2015):
Commercial confidence not absolute. If public money involved, disclosure required unless demonstrable harm.

**Clause (e): Fiduciary Relationship**
```
Information available to person in fiduciary relationship.
```

**Examples**:
- Bank account details (banker-client fiduciary)
- Medical records (doctor-patient) - unless patient consents
- Tax returns (Income Tax dept-taxpayer)

**Exception**: If competent authority satisfied public interest outweighs privacy.

**Clause (f): Foreign Government Information**
```
Information received in confidence from foreign government.
```

**Clause (g): Life/Safety Endangerment**
```
Information endangering life or physical safety of any person.
```

**Examples**:
- Witness protection program details
- Location of protected persons
- Security arrangements for VVIPs

**Clause (h): Investigation/Prosecution Impediment**
```
Information impeding investigation or apprehension/prosecution of offenders.
```

**Examples**:
- ❌ Ongoing investigation details (before chargesheet)
- ❌ Informer identity
- ✓ FIR (public document - not exempt)
- ✓ Chargesheet (after filing - becomes public)

**Timeline**: Once investigation concluded, information typically disclosable.

**Clause (i): Cabinet Papers**
```
Cabinet papers including records of deliberations of Council of Ministers, Secretaries and other officers.

EXCEPTION: Decisions of Cabinet/Council of Ministers (reasons thereof) MUST be disclosed after decision taken + implemented or 5 years elapsed.
```

**Important**:
- **Cabinet discussions**: Exempt (to enable free deliberation)
- **Cabinet decision**: Disclosable (after decision + implementation OR 5 years)

**Example**:
- ❌ File noting: "Finance Minister objected to proposal" (deliberation - exempt)
- ✓ Cabinet decision: "GST rate on item XYZ increased to 18%" (decision - disclosable)

**Clause (j): Personal Information**
```
Information unrelated to any public activity or interest, or invasion of privacy of individual.

EXCEPTION: Information which can be made available to Parliament/State Legislature (then disclosable).
```

**Examples**:
- ❌ Minister's medical records (personal)
- ✓ Minister's educational qualifications (public activity - election affidavit)
- ❌ Government employee's caste certificate (personal, unless linked to reservation claim)
- ✓ Government employee's salary, TA/DA (public money - public activity)

**Test**: Does information relate to public activity? If yes, not personal.

**Case Law**:
*Central Board of Secondary Education v. Aditya Bandopadhyay* (2011) 8 SCC 497:
Student answer sheets involve fiduciary relationship (examiner-examinee). Not disclosed via RTI to third party. Student himself can get copy.

### Section 8(2): Public Interest Override

**Statutory Text**:
> "Notwithstanding anything in the Official Secrets Act, 1923 (19 of 1923) nor any of the exemptions permissible in accordance with sub-section (1), a public authority may allow access to information, if public interest in disclosure outweighs the harm to the protected interests."

**Application**:
- Applies to ALL Section 8(1) exemptions **except** clause (a) (national security)
- Balancing test: **Harm from disclosure vs. Public benefit from disclosure**

**Examples**:

**Corruption Allegation**:
- Information: File noting showing minister's direction to favor contractor
- Exemption: Section 8(1)(i) - cabinet papers
- Public Interest: Exposing corruption
- **Result**: Public interest outweighs - **DISCLOSE**

**Environmental Hazard**:
- Information: Factory emission test report (private company)
- Exemption: Section 8(1)(d) - commercial confidence
- Public Interest: Public health (pollution)
- **Result**: Public interest outweighs - **DISCLOSE**

**PIO's Duty**: When information falls under exemption, PIO must actively consider Section 8(2) public interest override.

## Third Party Information (Section 11)

**Procedure** when RTI seeks information about **third party**:

### Step 1: PIO Receives Application
RTI application seeks information that may affect commercial confidence or personal information of third party.

**Example**: RTI asking for ABC Company's contract with government.

### Step 2: PIO Issues Notice (Section 11(1))
PIO must give **written notice** to third party **within 5 days** of receiving RTI.

**Notice Contents**:
- RTI application details
- Information sought
- Third party's right to make representation (within 10 days)

### Step 3: Third Party Representation
Third party has **10 days** to submit written representation opposing disclosure.

**Grounds**: Must cite Section 8(1)(d) - commercial confidence, trade secret, etc.

### Step 4: PIO Decision
After considering third party representation:
- **Decide within 40 days total** (30 days + 10 days for third party representation)
- If disclose: Inform third party, give reasons why Section 8(1)(d) not applicable
- If refuse: Inform applicant with reasons

### Step 5: Third Party Appeal Right (Section 19(3))
If PIO decides to **disclose** over third party's objection:
- Third party can file **appeal** to FAA within 30 days
- Information NOT disclosed till appeal decided

**Timeline Extension**: If third party involved, total timeline = 40 days (30 + 10) + any appeal delay.

## Appeals Mechanism

### First Appeal (Section 19(1))

**When to File**:
1. PIO refused information (rejection order)
2. PIO failed to respond within 30 days (**deemed refusal**)
3. Partial information provided (rest refused)
4. Information fee excessive
5. Unsatisfied with information quality

**To Whom**: **First Appellate Authority (FAA)**
- Officer senior to PIO in same department
- Designated by public authority under Section 19(1)

**Timeline**: **Within 30 days** from:
- Date of PIO's refusal order, OR
- Expiry of 30-day response period (for deemed refusal)

**Condonation**: FAA may condone delay if **sufficient cause** shown (max delay condoned: reasonable period, no statutory limit).

**Format**:
```
To,
The First Appellate Authority,
[Name of Public Authority],
[Address]

Date: [DD/MM/YYYY]

Subject: First Appeal under Section 19(1), RTI Act 2005

RTI Application Number: [Unique Number]
Date of Application: [DD/MM/YYYY]
PIO Name: [Name]

Dear Sir/Madam,

I had filed RTI application on [date] to PIO, [Department Name] seeking the following information:
1. [Information point 1]
2. [Information point 2]

[Choose one]:
A. The PIO vide order dated [date] refused the information citing [exemption cited]. This is incorrect because [reasons - e.g., "Information does not fall under Section 8(1)(d) as it relates to public money and no commercial confidence involved"].

OR

B. The PIO has failed to respond within the 30-day period prescribed under Section 7(1). This constitutes deemed refusal.

OR

C. The PIO provided partial information but refused [specific points] citing [exemption]. This exemption is not applicable because [reasons].

I request the First Appellate Authority to:
1. Direct the PIO to provide complete information sought
2. Impose penalty on PIO for violation of RTI Act [if deliberate denial]

Copies of the following documents are enclosed:
1. Copy of original RTI application
2. Copy of PIO's refusal order [if applicable]
3. Fee payment receipt

Thanking you,

[Signature]
[Name]
[Address]
[Contact Details]
```

**FAA Powers** (Section 19(5)):
- Require PIO to take decision (if PIO failed to decide)
- Require disclosure
- Appoint new PIO
- Impose penalty (refer to CIVIC-RTI-PENALTIES)

**FAA Timeline**: Must dispose appeal **within 30 days** (extendable to 45 days with recorded reasons).

---

### Second Appeal (Section 19(3))

**When to File**:
1. FAA rejected first appeal
2. FAA failed to decide within 30/45 days (deemed refusal)
3. Dissatisfied with FAA's order

**To Whom**:
- **Central Information Commission (CIC)** - for Central Government/PSUs
- **State Information Commission (SIC)** - for State Government

**Timeline**: **Within 90 days** from:
- Date of FAA's order, OR
- Expiry of 30/45-day FAA response period

**Condonation**: CIC/SIC may condone delay if sufficient cause (no maximum limit specified).

**Online Filing**:
- CIC: https://cic.gov.in (e-filing available)
- SIC: Check respective state SIC website

**Format**: Similar to first appeal, but addressed to CIC/SIC.

**CIC/SIC Powers** (Section 18):
- Same as FAA powers
- Additionally: Direct public authority to appoint PIOs
- Direct compliance with Section 4 (suo moto disclosure)
- Impose penalty up to ₹25,000 (Section 20)
- Award compensation to complainant (in some cases)

**CIC/SIC Hearing**:
- Quasi-judicial proceedings
- Personal hearing (can appear or send written submission)
- PIO, FAA, applicant - all can be heard
- Reasoned order passed

**Timeline**: CIC typically takes 6-18 months to decide (heavy backlog). SICs vary by state.

**No Further Appeal**: CIC/SIC decision is final (Section 19(7)).

**Judicial Review**: Can challenge CIC/SIC order in High Court under Article 226 (writ jurisdiction) on limited grounds (jurisdictional error, perversity).

## Strategic Tips

### 1. Craft Precise Questions

**Poor**:
"Give me all information about ABC project."

**Problems**:
- Vague ("all information" = thousands of pages?)
- PIO may refuse citing "fishing expedition"
- Unmanageable volume

**Good**:
"Provide following specific documents regarding ABC project:
1. Copy of DPR dated [date]
2. Environmental clearance letter no. [ref] dated [date]
3. Minutes of meeting held on [date] where project approval discussed
4. Tender evaluation report dated [date]"

**Benefits**:
- Specific, measurable
- PIO knows exactly what to provide
- Harder to refuse

---

### 2. Anticipate Exemptions

**Before Filing**, consider:
- Does information fall under Section 8(1)?
- If yes, is there Section 8(2) public interest override?

**Example**:
Seeking corruption investigation details against officer.

**Exemption Risk**: Section 8(1)(h) - impedes investigation

**Counter-Strategy**:
- Request already **closed** cases (investigation completed)
- Cite public interest in exposing corruption (Section 8(2) override)
- Specifically request **FIR copy, final report** (not ongoing investigation details)

---

### 3. Use Section 6(3) Transfers

If unsure of correct public authority:
- File to most likely authority
- PIO **must transfer** to correct authority within 5 days (Section 6(3))

**Benefit**: No time lost - transferred application timeline continues from original date.

---

### 4. File Appeals Promptly

**30-day appeal deadline** is strict. Don't delay.

**Calculate Timeline**:
- RTI filed: January 1
- 30-day response deadline: January 31
- No response = Deemed refusal on: January 31
- First appeal deadline: March 2 (30 days from January 31)

**Track Dates**: Maintain Excel sheet with RTI no., filing date, expected response date, appeal deadlines.

---

### 5. Escalate Strategically

**Escalation Ladder**:
1. **Informal reminder**: Email/letter to PIO before 30 days elapse
2. **First Appeal**: If PIO doesn't respond or rejects
3. **Second Appeal to CIC**: If FAA rejects
4. **Writ Petition (Article 226)**: If CIC unreasonably delays/rejects

**When to Skip FAA**:
- If PIO and FAA are clearly colluding (small department)
- Consider direct second appeal to CIC (though procedurally should exhaust FAA first)

---

### 6. Document Everything

**Maintain File**:
- Original RTI application copy
- Fee payment receipt
- Acknowledgment
- All correspondence (PIO, FAA, CIC)
- Refusal orders
- Appeal applications

**Why**: Essential for appeals, potential court case, RTI penalty proceedings.

## Common PIO Tactics and Counters

### Tactic 1: "Information Not Available"

**PIO Response**: "Information sought is not available with this office."

**Counter**:
1. **First Appeal**: Argue information MUST be maintained per Section 4 obligations
2. Cite **Bhagat Singh v. CIC** (2015): Public authority cannot refuse citing non-maintenance of records - that itself is violation
3. Request FAA to direct search for information + penalty for non-maintenance

---

### Tactic 2: "Voluminous Information"

**PIO Response**: "Information is voluminous, cannot be provided in RTI."

**Counter**:
1. RTI Act has NO volume limit
2. Offer to pay higher information fee (₹2/page)
3. Request inspection instead of copies (cheaper)
4. First Appeal: Cite **Gujarat High Court in Harisudan v. FAA**: Volume cannot be reason for refusal

---

### Tactic 3: "Diversion of Resources"

**PIO Response**: "Providing this information would disproportionately divert resources."

**Counter**:
1. Section 7(9): PIO may refuse only if "disproportionately divert resources of public authority"
2. **Burden on PIO**: PIO must prove with evidence (time estimates, staff shortage)
3. Offer to narrow request or accept phased disclosure
4. Appeal: Argue resources cannot be excuse without concrete evidence

---

### Tactic 4: "Third Party Confidentiality" (Misapplied)

**PIO Response**: "Information relates to third party, cannot disclose."

**Counter**:
1. Third party exemption (Section 8(1)(d), (j)) not absolute
2. If public money involved, no exemption (e.g., contractor payments)
3. Cite **Section 8(2)**: Public interest in disclosure
4. Appeal: Demand PIO prove commercial confidence harm

**This protocol provides procedural guidance for RTI applications. It does NOT constitute legal advice.**
